    "<S> we consider a server with large capacity delivering video files encoded in various resolutions . </S>",
    "<S> we assume that the system is under saturation in the sense that the total demand exceeds the server capacity @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> in such case , requests may be rejected . </S>",
    "<S> for the policies considered in this paper , instead of rejecting a video request , it is downgraded . </S>",
    "<S> when the occupancy of the server is above some value @xmath1 , the server delivers the video at a minimal bit rate . </S>",
    "<S> the quantity @xmath2 is the bit rate adaptation threshold . </S>",
    "<S> for these policies , request blocking is thus replaced with bit rate adaptation . under the assumptions of poisson request arrivals and exponential service times , </S>",
    "<S> we show that , by rescaling the system , a process associated with the occupancy of the server converges to some limiting process whose invariant distribution is computed explicitly . </S>",
    "<S> this allows us to derive an asymptotic expression of the key performance measure of such a policy , namely the equilibrium probability that a request is transmitted at requested bitrate . </S>",
    "<S> numerical applications of these results are presented .    ' '' ''    ' '' '' </S>"
