    "the notion of sequentially cohen  macaulay complexes first arose in combinatorics : motivated by questions concerning subspace arrangements , bjrner & wachs introduced the notion of nonpure shellability  @xcite .",
    "stanley then introduced the sequentially cohen  macaulay property in order to have a ring - theoretic analogue of nonpure shellability  @xcite .",
    "schenzel independently defined the notion of sequentially cohen  macaulay modules ( called by him cohen  macaulay filtered modules @xcite ) , inspired by earlier work of goto .",
    "in essence , a simplicial complex is sequentially cohen ",
    "macaulay if and only if it is naturally composed of a sequence of cohen  macaulay subcomplexes , namely the pure skeleta of the complex , graded by dimension .",
    "they come with an associated numerical invariant , the so - called @xmath3-triangle , which measures the face - numbers of each component according to a doubly - indexed grading .",
    "just as the classical @xmath0-vector determines the numbers of faces of various dimensions of a simplicial complex , the @xmath3-triangle determines the numbers of faces in each component of the complex .    motivated by the macaulay - stanley theorem for cohen - macaulay complexes , which are always pure ) bjrner & wachs  @xcite posed the problem to characterize the possible @xmath0-triangles of sequentially cohen ",

    "after some significant initial progress , due to duval @xcite and aravoma , herzog & hibi  @xcite , which reduced these two questions to combinatorial settings , some partial results on the second question were obtained by crupi & utano  @xcite and herzog , sharifan & varbaro   @xcite .",
    "part of the difficulty of this nonpure `` macaulay problem '' is that , in contrast to the classical situation , it does not suffice to use a criterion that makes a decision by only pairwise `` macaulay type '' comparisons of entries in the @xmath0-triangle .",
    "our main objective in this paper is to give a numerical characterization of the possible @xmath0-triangles of sequentially cohen  macaulay complexes .",
    "the method that we use is based on a modification of a correspondence between shifted multicomplexes and pure shifted simplicial complexes , provided by bjrner , frankl & stanley  @xcite .",
    "finally , we also provide a characteristic - independent characterization of the possible betti tables of componentwise linear ideals using our main result and an observation made by herzog , sharifan & varbaro  @xcite .",
