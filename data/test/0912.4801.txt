    "the string theory plays a significant role in the study of physical situation at the very early stages of the formation of the universe .",
    "it is generally assumed that after the big bang , the universe may have undergone a series of phase transitions as its temperature lowered down below some critical temperature as predicted by grand unified theories @xcite@xmath0@xcite . at the very early stages of evolution of the universe",
    ", it is believed that during phase transition the symmetry of the universe is broken spontaneously .",
    "it can give rise to topologically stable defects such as domain walls , strings and monopoles @xcite .",
    "in all these three cosmological structures , only cosmic strings have excited the most interesting consequence @xcite because they are believed to give rise to density perturbations which lead to formation of galaxies @xcite .",
    "these cosmic strings can be closed like loops or open like a hair which move through time and trace out a tube or a sheet , according to whether it is closed or open .",
    "the string is free to vibrate and its different vibrational modes present different types of particles carrying the force of gravitation .",
    "this is why much interesting to study the gravitational effect that arises from strings by using einstein s field equations .",
    "the general relativistic treatment of strings has been initially given by letelier @xcite and stachel @xcite .",

    "<S> bianchi type v string cosmological models with bulk viscosity for massive string are investigated . </S>",
    "<S> the bulk viscosity is assumed to vary with time in such a manner that it is related to simple power function of the energy density . using generation technique ( camci  et  al . , 2001 ) , it is shown that einstein s field equations are solvable for any arbitrary cosmic scale function . </S>",
    "<S> solution for particular form of cosmic scale functions are also obtained . </S>",
    "<S> it is found that solutions based on generation technique are relevant to the observational results . </S>",
    "<S> some physical and geometrical aspect of the models are also discussed . </S>",
