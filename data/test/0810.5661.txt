    "<S> there have been attempts to fit the abundance patterns of extremely metal - poor stars with supernova nucleosynthesis models for the lighter elements than zn . on the other hand </S>",
    "<S> , observations have revealed that the presence of emp stars with peculiarly high ratio of `` weak r - process elements '' sr , y and zr . </S>",
    "<S> although several possible processes were suggested for the origin of these elements , the complete solution for reproducing those ratios is not found yet . in order to reproduce the abundance patterns of such stars , we investigate a model with neutron rich matter ejection from the inner region of the conventional mass - cut . </S>",
    "<S> we find that explosive nucleosynthesis in a high energy supernova ( or `` hypernova '' ) can reproduce the high abundances of sr , y and zr but that the enhancements of sr , y and zr are not achieved by nucleosynthesis in a normal supernova . </S>",
    "<S> our results imply that , if these elements are ejected from a normal supernova , nucleosynthesis in higher entropy flow than that of the supernova shock is required . </S>"
