    "<S> there is strong evidence that the period - luminosity ( pl ) relation for the large magellanic cloud ( lmc ) cepheids shows a break at a period around 10 days . since the lmc pl relation </S>",
    "<S> is extensively used in distance scale studies , the non - linearity of the lmc pl relation may affect the results based on this lmc calibrated relation . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we show that this problem can be remedied by using the wesenheit function in obtaining cepheid distances . </S>",
    "<S> this is because the wesenheit function is linear although recent data suggests that the pl and the period - colour ( pc ) relations that make up the wesenheit function are not . </S>",
    "<S> we test the linearity of the wesenheit function and find strong evidence that the lmc wesenheit function is indeed linear . </S>",
    "<S> this is because the non - linearity of the pl and pc relations cancel out when the wesenheit function is constructed . </S>",
    "<S> we discuss this result in the context of distance scale applications . </S>",
    "<S> we also compare the distance moduli obtained from @xmath0 ( equivalent to wesenheit functions ) constructed with the linear and the broken lmc pl relations , and find that the typical difference in distance moduli is @xmath1mag . </S>",
    "<S> hence , the broken lmc pl relation does not seriously affect current distance scale applications . </S>",
