    "during the last few years , a two - dimensional hexagonal lattice of carbon atoms known as graphene has attracted widespread attention owing to its unusual physical properties and promising applications @xcite . unlike conventional condensed matter systems , the quasiparticles in graphene are massless or very light particles which at low energies obey the relativistic dirac equation , where the speed of light @xmath0 is replaced with the fermi velocity @xmath1 ( see the monograph @xcite and review papers @xcite ) .",
    "this makes the electronic properties of graphene highly nontrivial , and , specifically , results in the existence @xcite of a universal ( up to small nonlocal corrections ) frequency - independent conductivity @xmath2 in the limit of zero temperature .    the electrical conductivity of graphene was investigated in many theoretical papers using the current - current correlation functions in the random phase approximation , the two - dimensional drude model , the quasiclassical approach , the kubo formula , and boltzmann s transport theory ( see , e.g. , refs .",
    "@xcite ) . at first , a few distinct values for the universal conductivity @xmath2 have been obtained by different authors ( see refs .",

    "<S> the electrical conductivity of graphene with a nonzero mass - gap parameter is investigated starting from the first principles of quantum electrodynamics in ( 2 + 1)-dimensional space - time at any temperature . </S>",
    "<S> the formalism of the polarization tensor defined over the entire plane of complex frequency is used . at zero temperature </S>",
    "<S> we reproduce the results for both real and imaginary parts of the conductivity , obtained previously in the local approximation , and generalize them taking into account the effects of nonlocality . at nonzero temperature </S>",
    "<S> the exact analytic expressions for real and imaginary parts of the longitudinal and transverse conductivities of gapped graphene are derived , as well as their local limits and approximate expressions in several asymptotic regimes . </S>",
    "<S> specifically , a simple local result for the real part of conductivity of gapped graphene valid at any temperature is obtained . according to our results , </S>",
    "<S> the real part of the conductivity is not equal to zero for frequencies exceeding the width of the gap and goes to the universal conductivity with increasing frequency . </S>",
    "<S> the imaginary part of conductivity of gapped graphene varies from infinity at zero frequency to minus infinity at the frequency defined by the gap parameter and then goes to zero with further increase of frequency . </S>",
    "<S> the analytic expressions are accompanied by the results of numerical computations . </S>",
    "<S> possible future generalization of the used formalism is discussed . </S>"
