    "<S> in this paper we study multivariate polynomial functions in complex variables and their corresponding symmetric tensor representations . the focus is to find conditions under which such complex polynomials always take real values . </S>",
    "<S> we introduce the notion of symmetric conjugate forms and general conjugate forms , characterize the conditions for such complex polynomials to be real - valued , and present their corresponding tensor representations . </S>",
    "<S> new notions of eigenvalues / eigenvectors for complex tensors are introduced , extending similar properties from the hermitian matrices . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , we study a property of the symmetric tensors , namely the largest eigenvalue ( in the absolute value sense ) of a symmetric real tensor is equal to its largest singular value ; the result is also known as banach s theorem . </S>",
    "<S> we show that a similar result holds for the complex case as well . finally , we discuss some applications of the new notion of eigenvalues / eigenvectors for the complex tensors .    </S>",
    "<S> * keywords : * symmetric complex tensor ; conjugate complex polynomial ; tensor eigenvalue ; tensor eigenvector ; nonnegative polynomial .    </S>",
