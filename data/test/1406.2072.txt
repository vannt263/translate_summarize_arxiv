    "graphene plasmonics has recently emerged as a promising platform for studying strong light - matter interactions.@xcite graphene is a novel two - dimensional ( 2d ) material @xcite with unique electronic and optical properties @xcite and a wide range of applications such as biosensors,@xcite ultrafast lasers , @xcite optical modulators , @xcite and photodetectors .",
    "@xcite clean graphene samples are characterized by long electron scattering times and much lower , compared to metals , ohmic losses due to relatively weak electron - phonon interaction .",
    "@xcite charge - doped graphene with fermi energy , @xmath0 , in the range 0.2 - 0.6 ev exhibits a stable in - plane plasmon in the infrared frequency range @xcite with gate - tunable wavelength , @xmath1 , well below radiation wavelength at the same frequency .",
    "@xcite    a large local density of states ( ldos ) of graphene plasmons as compared to that of surface plasmon polaritons ( spp ) on metal surfaces ensures very efficient plasmon excitation by a local probe such as an excited dye molecule or an exciton in semiconductor quantum dot ( qd ) situated at a close distance to the graphene sheet .",
    "@xcite recent measurements @xcite of nonradiative energy transfer from a dye molecule to graphene pointed to a strong coulomb coupling between electronic excitations in these two systems with transfer rates exceeding the radiative decay rate by a factor of @xmath210@xmath3 .",
    "plasmons can also provide efficient coupling between several emitters situated near a graphene sheet , e.g. , by enhancing superradiance from two excited qds @xcite or facilitating long - distance energy transfer between a donor and an acceptor .",
    "@xcite these studies explored weak coupling regime between graphene plasmons and excitons , i.e. , the energy spectra of these excitations are not significantly altered by the optical interactions between them .    here",
    "we demonstrate that a _ strong _ coupling between excitons and graphene plasmons can be realized in a hybrid graphene - semiconductor system giving rise to a propagating exciton - plasmon state with a mixed 2d dispersion ( hereafter referred to as exciton - plasmariton@xcite ) .",
    "strong exciton - plasmon coupling effects have been recently studied between surface plasmon - polaritons ( spp ) or localized surface plasmons in metal structures and excitons in dye j - aggregates , @xcite individual dye molecules , @xcite semiconductor quantum wells ( qw ) , @xcite or qds and nanoscrystals .",
    "@xcite in these systems , very large rabi splittings ( relative to vacuum rabi splitting @xcite ) were reported in the range of 100 - 200 mev for dispersion of spp coupled to molecular excitons @xcite and in the range of 200 - 450 mev for absorption / transmission spectra of excitons coupled to localized plasmons ( plexcitons ) , @xcite while relatively small splittings ( @xmath4 mev ) were observed for spp coupled to qw excitons.@xcite the optical excitation energies in the above experiments were in the range 1 - 3 ev to match those of plasmon resonances in metal nanostructures .",
    "in contrast , stable graphene plasmons have significantly lower energies ( below @xmath5 ev in highly doped samples ) and , to the best of our knowledge , strong coupling regime in graphene has not yet been explored . here we show that a strong exciton - graphene plasmon coupling can be achieved in a hybrid structure comprised of doped graphene monolayer separated by a thin spacer from a narrow gap semiconductor qw .",

    "<S> we study strong coupling between plasmons in monolayer charge - doped graphene and excitons in a narrow gap semiconductor quantum well separated from graphene by a potential barrier . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the coulomb interaction between excitons and plasmons result in mixed states described by a hamiltonian similar to that for exciton - polaritons and derive the exciton - plasmon coupling constant that depends on system parameters . </S>",
    "<S> we calculate numerically the rabi splitting of exciton - plasmariton dispersion branches for several semiconductor materials and find that it can reach values of up to 50 - 100 mev . </S>"
