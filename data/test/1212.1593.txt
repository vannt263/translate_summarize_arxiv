    "@xcite it has become clear that the stress fluctuation correction to the leading born term does not necessarily vanish in the zero - temperature limit .",
    "this is due to the fact that when a system is subjected to a homogeneous deformation , the ensuing particle displacements need not follow the macroscopic strain affinely @xcite .",
    "the stress fluctuation term quantifies the extent of these non - affine displacements , whereas the born term reflects the affine part of the particle displacements .",
    "how important the non - affine motions are , depends on the system under consideration @xcite . while the elastic properties of crystals with one atom per unit cell are given by the born term only , stress fluctuations are significant for crystals with more complex unit cells @xcite .",
    "they become particularly pronounced for polymer like soft materials @xcite and amorphous solids @xcite .",
    "this is revealed by recent simulation studies of various glass formers @xcite , like lennard - jones ( lj ) mixtures @xcite , polymer glasses @xcite or silica melts @xcite .",
    "many of these simulation studies concentrate on the mechanical properties deep in the glassy state , exploring for instance correlations between the non - affine displacement field and vibrational anomalies of the glass (  boson peak \" ) @xcite , the mechanical heterogeneity at the nanoscale @xcite or the onset of molecular plasticity in the regime where the macroscopic deformation is still elastic @xcite .",
