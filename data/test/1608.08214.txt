    "the global evolution of the observable universe can be separated on four epochs : inflation , radiation domination era , matter domination era and dark energy domination era .",
    "all these epochs can be described by general relativity models with minimally coupled scalar fields , because the assumption that the hubble parameter is a monotonically decreasing function does not contradict with the observation data . in such type of models the initial period of the universe evolution with energies above the planck scale",
    "should be described by quantum gravity because the classical evolution includes the initial singularity .",
    "important question of theoretical cosmology is whether the entire universe evolution can remain classical and has no singularity .",
    "it is possible to avoid this singularity considering modified gravity .",
    "bouncing universe smoothly transits from a period of contraction to a period of expansion and its evolution remains classical .",

    "in this paper we have considered a class of spatially flat flrw models with bounce solutions , generalizing those found in @xcite .",
    "we show that despite the model with the potential @xmath323 , studied in   @xcite is the unique model with the curvature scalar @xmath5 being the integral of motion , a much wider class of potentials admits bounce solutions followed by smooth future asymptotic behavior .",
    "one class of models studied in the present paper generalizes the above mentioned potential by adding a term quadratic in @xmath8 .",
    "this generalization allows us to find bounce solutions with non - monotonic behavior of the hubble parameter , in particular we found solutions with essential decreasing hubble parameter , including the solutions with the hubble parameter tending to zero .",
    "moreover , we present a list of possible types of the bounce solutions and show that bounce behavior is still present even in the case when @xmath324 being negative , though in this case the nice future asymptotic is evidently lost .    another generalization replaces the quartic term in the potential by other even index .",
    "we studied the quartic and sixth - power cases , changing also the coupling constant @xmath18 .",
    "we show that despite the model becomes non - integrable , the qualitative features of solution found in  @xcite are still valid for a not so restricted conditions on the potential . on the other hand , some new interesting features like possible branch changing during the cosmological evolution appears , which can decrease the measure of initial conditions good for bounce , though",
    "does not eliminate the possibility for the bounce completely . on the other hand , for sufficient small positive @xmath18",

    "note that inflationary models with the ricci scalar multiplied by a function of the scalar field are very popular  @xcite .",
    "they not only do not contradict the recent observational data  @xcite , but also connect cosmology and particle physics .",
    "note that models with non - minimally coupled scalar fields are quite natural because quantum corrections to the effective action with minimal coupling include induced gravity terms  @xcite .",
    "also , the einstein frame model that corresponds to the bouncing model  @xcite describes inflation  @xcite ( see also  @xcite ) . note that non - local model  @xcite and its generalization  @xcite not only has bounce solution , but also ( at other values of parameters ) can describe inflation  @xcite .",
