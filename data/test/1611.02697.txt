    "the spin structure of the proton is one of the most challenging problems in modern physics .",
    "the experimental results of the european muon collaboration showed that only a small fraction of the proton spin is carried by the quark spin @xcite . at the moment we know that : ( a ) about 1/3 of the nucleon spin comes from the quark spin , see for example @xcite-@xcite ; ( b ) the gluon spin seems to make negligible contribution @xcite-@xcite ; ( c ) some portion of the proton spin can come from the orbital angular momentum of quarks and anti - quarks @xcite , see also the reviews @xcite-@xcite .",
    "theoretical investigations of the proton spin structure are also performed on the lattice , see for example @xcite .    in this letter",
    "we use the non - perturbative methods  la heisenberg @xcite to investigate the gluon field contribution to the proton spin .",
    "the main idea of our approach is that flux tubes between quarks in a proton have the angular momentum density coming from colour electric and magnetic fields .",
    "the flux tube has longitudinal electric field and transversal electric and magnetic fields .",
    "the transversal fields have different colour indices and , at first glance , can not give us the angular momentum density .",
    "but if we consider the quantum superposition of states with different transversal fields in the flux tube we will have transversal electric and magnetic fields with the same colour index . in this case",
    "we can calculate the angular momentum coming from the gluon fields which are in the flux tube . in this connection we have to note that in ref .",
    "@xcite the authors have shown that some properties of a glueball may explain the gluonic contribution to the proton spin .",
    "let us note that the mechanism presented here is non - perturbative , and it can not be explained on the perturbative level .",
