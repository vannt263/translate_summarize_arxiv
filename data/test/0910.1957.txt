    "<S> we explore the advantages offered by twin light beams produced in parametric down - conversion for precision measurement . </S>",
    "<S> the symmetry of these bipartite quantum states , even under losses , suggests that monitoring correlations between the divergent beams permits a high - precision inference of any symmetry - breaking effect , e.g. fiber birefringence . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the quantity of entanglement is not the key feature for such an instrument . in a lossless </S>",
    "<S> setting , scaling of precision at the ultimate ` heisenberg ' limit is possible with photon counting alone . </S>",
    "<S> even as photon losses approach 100% the precision is shot - noise limited , and we identify the crossover point between quantum and classical precision as a function of detected flux . </S>",
    "<S> the predicted hypersensitivity is demonstrated with a bayesian simulation . </S>"
