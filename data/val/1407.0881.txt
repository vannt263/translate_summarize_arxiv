    "<S> the presence of multiple fields during inflation might seed a detectable amount of non - gaussianity in the curvature perturbations , which in turn becomes observable in present data sets like the cosmic microwave background ( cmb ) or the large scale structure ( lss ) . within this proceeding </S>",
    "<S> we present a fully analytic method to infer inflationary parameters from observations by exploiting higher - order statistics of the curvature perturbations . to keep this analyticity , and thereby to dispense with numerically expensive sampling techniques , </S>",
    "<S> a saddle - point approximation is introduced whose precision has been validated for a numerical toy example . applied to real data , this approach might enable to discriminate among the still viable models of inflation . </S>"
