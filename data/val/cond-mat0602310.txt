    "the mott  hubbard metal - insulator transition in the one - band hubbard model at half band - filling continues to pose an intriguing problem  @xcite , even in the limit of infinite dimensions  @xcite .",
    "starting from the limit of large interactions , the mott ",
    "hubbard gap characterizing the insulating phase diminishes as @xmath0 is lowered and eventually closes at a finite @xmath1 .",
    "the determination of the precise value of the latter is an unsolved problem in infinite dimensions : the equations from dynamical mean - field theory need to be solved numerically and the best available treatments using the dynamical density - matrix renormalization group method lead to conflicting results  @xcite .",
    "other attempts to determine @xmath1 involve extrapolations of finite - order @xmath2-expansions of the mott ",
    "hubbard gap  @xcite and the ground - state energy  @xcite , respectively .",
    "these expansions can be carried out because the spin background is completely disordered and one is left with finding the description of the effective motion of the charge degrees of freedom . however , the extrapolated values for  @xmath1 obtained by such methods",
    "vary significantly , depending on the details of the procedure .    in light of these issues",
    "it is desirable to have an example of a hubbard - type model with a disordered spin background which can be solved exactly .",
    "one such example is the falicov  kimball model at half band - filling in the disordered phase  @xcite .",
    "however , its ground - state energy turns out to have a trivial @xmath2-expansion and hence is of limited utility .",
    "curiously , the exactly solvable one - dimensional hubbard model  @xcite at half band - filling features a spin - disordered regime at a small but finite temperature in the limit of strong coulomb repulsion , see refs .",
    "it is then an interesting question to analyze the physics of this regime in view of the above issues raised by the studies of the mott ",
    "hubbard insulator in infinite dimensions .",

    "<S> we analyze the thermodynamic bethe ansatz equations of the one - dimensional half - filled hubbard model in the `` spin - disordered regime '' , which is characterized by the temperature being much larger than the magnetic energy scale but small compared to the mott  hubbard gap . in this regime </S>",
    "<S> the thermodynamics of the hubbard model can be thought of in terms of gapped charged excitations with an effective dispersion and spin degrees of freedom that only contribute entropically . in particular , the internal energy and the effective dispersion become essentially independent of temperature . </S>",
    "<S> an interpretation of this regime in terms of a putative interacting - electron system at zero temperature leads to a metal - insulator transition at a finite interaction strength above which the gap opens linearly . </S>",
    "<S> we relate these observations to studies of the mott  </S>",
    "<S> hubbard transition in the limit of infinite dimensions .    </S>",
    "<S> _ keywords _ : hubbard model , thermodynamic bethe ansatz , mott transition </S>"
