    "quantum dot spin qubits are promising candidates for quantum computing because of their long decoherence times and their potential to leverage existing semiconductor technologies  @xcite .",
    "the exchange coupling is a desirable tool for mediating interactions between spin qubits , because it can be controlled electrostatically and it is typically very fast  @xcite . in combination with arbitrary single - qubit operations ,",
    "the exchange coupling enables universal quantum computation  @xcite .",
    "when logical qubits consist of two  @xcite or three  @xcite physical qubits in a decoherence - free subsystem , the exchange coupling alone is universal for quantum computation . on the other hand ,",
    "the intrinsic short - range nature of the exchange coupling ( typically tens of nanometers ) imposes strong constraints on the physical architecture of the spin qubits .",
    "these constraints present a significant challenge to scalability during quantum error correction , particularly for linear qubit architectures , which are typical for quantum dot spin qubits  @xcite .",
    "indeed , large - scale quantum computing is challenging in any qubit implementation , and the complexity of a given quantum circuit could , to a large extent , determine its success .    in a number of qubit systems , such as nuclear magnetic resonance ( nmr ) ,",

    "<S> we develop a class of  mediated \" gates for spin qubits , which act on nonproximal spins via intermediate ancilla qubits . at the end of the operation , the ancillae return to their initial states . </S>",
    "<S> we show how these mediated gates can be used ( 1 ) to generate arbitrary quantum states and ( 2 ) to construct arbitrary quantum gates . </S>",
    "<S> we provide some explicit examples of circuits that generate common states [ _ e.g. _ , bell , greenberger - horne - zeilinger ( ghz ) , @xmath0 , and cluster states ] and gates ( _ e.g. _ , @xmath1 , swap , cnot , and toffoli gates ) . </S>",
