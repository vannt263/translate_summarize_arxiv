    "in the previous sections , we have seen that position fluctuations of a reflecting boundary are capable of removing divergences in the renormalized values of local observables , such as @xmath20 and @xmath175 . in the case of the massless ,",
    "minimally coupled scalar field , such fluctuations also remove the discrepancy between the spatial integral of the renormalized energy density , @xmath176 , and the renormalized expectation value of the hamiltonian , @xmath177 .",
    "position fluctuations are necessary if one is to treat the mirror as a quantum mechanical object .",
    "of course , for real mirrors the mass is likely to be so large that the position uncertainty @xmath65 is very small . in this case",
    ", the cutoff in reflectivity due to dispersion will normally be the dominant effect .",
    "dielectric materials become transparent to electromagnetic radiation at wavelengths shorter than about the plasma wavelength , @xmath178 .",
    "so long as the position uncertainty is small compared to this length , @xmath179 , dispersive effects are dominant , and the position fluctuations may be ignored . however ,",
    "if one could arrange to prepare a mirror in a quantum state in which @xmath180 , the position fluctuation effects discussed in this paper would become dominant .    in the area of gravitational physics ,",
    "the situation is rather different .",
    "here it is also possible to have horizons which act as boundaries for quantized fields , and for the renormalized expectation value of the stress tensor to diverge on the horizon . as discussed in the introduction ,",
    "examples include the event horizon of schwarzschild spacetime in the boulware vacuum , the cauchy horizon in misner space , and possibly the chronology horizon in a spacetime containing closed timelike curves .",
    "now there is no natural cutoff at high frequencies , and in fact higher frequency modes tend to couple more strongly to gravity by virtue of their larger energy .",
    "it is of course possible that a more complete quantum theory of gravity will introduce an effective cutoff at the planck scale . at the present , any discussion of planck scale physics",
    "must be regarded as highly speculative .",
    "nonetheless , position fluctuations of the horizon would seem to provide a possible way to avoid divergent stress tensors .",
    "it is plausible that the location of a spacetime horizon undergoes position fluctuations due either to the quantum nature of gravity ( `` active fluctuations '' ) @xcite , or to fluctuations of the stress tensor of quantum matter fields ( `` passive fluctuations '' ) @xcite .",
    "this is a topic requiring further study .",
