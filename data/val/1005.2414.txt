    "quantum mechanics determines the behavior of physical systems at atomic and subatomic scales .",
    "the search for quantum effects at macroscopic scales started soon after the development of quantum mechanics .",
    "a number of quantum manifestations at such scales have been found including quantum tunneling of the phase in josephson junctions  @xcite or resonant tunneling of magnetization in spin cluster systems  @xcite .",
    "dynamic issues in isolated quantum many - body systems are the focus of active research .",
    "some of the problems that are currently being studied theoretically are : the time evolution of the entropy of entanglement in spin systems  @xcite , the nature of non - equilibrium steady states in small quantum systems driven out of equilibrium  @xcite due to their relevance for nano - devices , quantum annealing techniques  @xcite , and the density of defects left over after a gradual change in a parameter  @xcite .",
    "the influence of an environment on the dynamics of quantum systems was also dealt with in a number of cases such as the spin - boson model  @xcite , disordered spin chains coupled to bosonic baths  @xcite , or an electronic ring coupled to leads and further driven by a time - dependent field  @xcite .    once the interest is set upon macroscopic systems , the question as to whether these undergo",
    "phase transitions naturally arises .",

    "these are realized when a system is forced in a non equilibrium steady state ( by a shear rate , an external current flowing through it , etc . )",

    "in contrast , the effect of a drive on a _ macroscopic _ system close to a quantum phase transition is a rather unexplored subject .",

    "in this paper we presented a detailed study of the quantum fully - connected rotor model driven out of equilibrium by a fermionic drive .",
    "we determined analytically the phase diagram of the model and we showed that a critical manifold , controlled by the value of the disorder strength , separates a qness with zero order parameter from an ordering phase with non - zero order parameter .",
    "we solved the equations that describe the dynamics in the different phases with a numerical integration and analytically by using various approximation schemes that give valuable physical insights .",
    "in particular , we showed that this ( quasi ) quadratic model maps to a set of langevin equations with additive colored noise that describes the dynamics of the rotors .",
    "the nature of the noise is determined by the type of electron baths used and , in the driven case , the friction kernel and noise - noise correlation are not linked by any fluctuation - dissipation relation . by using this effective langevin description",
    "we established the connection with the 3@xmath540 coarsening dynamics of the @xmath541 model and we showed that the long - time ordering dynamics are in the class of the classical limit of our model without a drive , _",

    "<S> we solve the dynamics of an ensemble of interacting rotors coupled to two leads at different chemical potential letting a current flow through the system and driving it out of equilibrium . </S>",
    "<S> we show that at low temperature the coarsening phase persists under the voltage drop up to a critical value of the applied potential that depends on the characteristics of the electron reservoirs . </S>",
    "<S> we discuss the properties of the critical surface in the temperature , voltage , strength of quantum fluctuations , and coupling to the bath phase diagram . </S>",
    "<S> we analyze the coarsening regime finding , in particular , which features are essentially quantum mechanical and which are basically classical in nature . </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate that the system evolves via the growth of a coherence length with the same time dependence as in the classical limit , @xmath0  the scalar curvature driven universality class . </S>",
    "<S> we obtain the scaling function of the correlation function at late epochs in the coarsening regime and we prove that it coincides with the classical one once a prefactor that encodes the dependence on all the parameters is factorized . </S>",
    "<S> we derive a generic formula for the current flowing through the system and we show that , for this model , it rapidly approaches a constant that we compute . </S>"
