    "models with extra dimensions have been used many times in the physics of elementary particles and cosmology .",
    "the first attempt has been in 1922 by kaluza and klein in an attempt to unify all the known interactions at this time , i.e. electromagnetism and gravity @xcite .",
    "string theories and m - theory , that also incorporate quantum gravity effects , consider space times with extra spatial dimensions to be consistently defined . in these theories",
    "ten ( string theory ) or eleven ( m - theory ) space time dimensions are required .",
    "one of the main problems with the extra dimensional models is their connection with the four dimensional physics . on the other hand gauge theories are non - renormalizable in higher dimensions and we have to define them with a cutoff @xmath1 .",
    "we can interpret these models as a low - energy effective description of an underlying more fundamental theory .",
    "the effective theory is then valid for energies lower than the cutoff @xmath1 .",
    "after this energy scale the underlying theory becomes relevant .",
    "the lattice regularization gives a gauge invariant cutoff for these models and provides a natural setup for a non - perturbative study .",
    "+ today the main approach to the models with higher dimensions is through the `` brane '' models .",
    "we suppose that our 4d world ( brane ) is embedded in a higher dimensional space with n extra dimensions , d=4+n .",
    "it is possible to consider flat extra dimensions ( add model ) @xcite or curved ones like the randall - sundrum model @xcite .",
    "gravity is supposed to propagate freely in the higher dimensional manifold ( bulk ) and the standard model particles are pinned on the brane . in this respect",
    "we need a mechanism for localization of them on the 4d brane see for example @xcite and references there in .",
    "+ on the lattice such a mechanism has been proposed by fu and nielsen @xcite in 1984 . they suggested that if in a d dimensional lattice we define a set of different nearest - neighbor couplings for the gauge fields in a d dimensional sublattice , this may lead to the formation of a d - dimensional layer phase ( @xmath2 ) . within a d - dimensional layer phase ,",
    "gauge particles and charged particles can travel as if they were massless . in any attempt to probe the remaining n dimensions , @xmath3 , the above particles propagate as if they were confined . actually , it is this confinement which forbids the interaction with neighboring d dimensional layers and implies the effective detection of a four - dimensional world .",
    "+ in a pure anisotropic d=5 u(1 ) gauge theory , with different couplings @xmath4 and @xmath0 in a 4d subspace and the extra fifth transverse direction correspondingly we observe three phases : the strong - confining phase , the layer phase with coulomb interaction in the four dimensional layers and a five dimensional coulomb deconfined phase @xcite .",
    "note that the transition line between the strong coupling ( @xmath5 ) phase and the layer phase ( @xmath6 ) is weakly first order .",
    "the phase transition between the layer phase and the five - dimensional coulomb phase , @xmath7 , is probably of second order @xcite , while the transition from the strong coupling to 5d coulomb phase is a strong first order phase transition .",
    "for the abelian higgs model and the su(2 ) adjoint higgs model in five dimensions lattice monte carlo results give serious evidence for a layered structure @xcite .",
    "finally we would like to mention that the layer phase disappears at high temperature @xcite .",
    "note that the layer structure in the previous models is based on the fact that when @xmath0 in the extra dimension is zero then there is a non - trivial phase transition .",
    "+ in this paper we study the phase diagram for the anisotropic five dimensional pure su(2 ) gauge theory .",
    "the interest in studying five dimensional pure gauge theories is related to a particular extension of the standard model which is called gauge - higgs unification .",
    "the idea behind this model is the identification of the higgs boson with the extra dimensional components of the gauge field as the system undergoes a dimensional reduction from five to four dimensions @xcite .",
    "a first step in this study for the dimensional reduction is to explore the phase diagram of the theory .",

    "<S> in this paper we attempt a non - perturbative study of the five dimensional , anisotropic su(2 ) gauge theory on the lattice using monte - carlo techniques . </S>",
    "<S> our goal is the exploration of the phase diagram , define the various phases and the critical boundary lines . </S>",
    "<S> three phases appear , two of them are continuations of the strong and the weak coupling phases of pure 4d su(2 ) to non - zero coupling @xmath0 in the fifth transverse direction and they are separated by a crossover transition , while the third phase is a 5d coulombic phase . </S>",
    "<S> we provide evidence that the phase transition between the 5d coulomb phase and the weak coupling phase is a second order phase transition . assuming that this result is not altered </S>",
    "<S> when increasing the lattice volume we give a first estimate of the associated critical exponents . </S>",
    "<S> this opens the possibility for a continuum effective five dimensional field theory .    </S>",
