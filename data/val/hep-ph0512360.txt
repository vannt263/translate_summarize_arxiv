    "in the present paper we constructed an effective lagrangian describing the photonic and non - photonic @xmath0 conversion in the context of the inverse seesaw model and specified the parameters characterizing this process .",
    "we focused in the simplest inverse seesaw model .",
    "the interest in the model is that it accounts for the observed masses and mixings indicated by current oscillation data in such a way that the underlying physics `` does not decouple '' and can be phenomenologically probed experimentally .",
    "the model provides a framework for enhanced rates with a rather simple , almost minimalistic , particle content .",
    "in contrast to the conventional seesaw , this is achieved without need of supersymmetrization .",
    "we derived a general formula for the coherent @xmath0 conversion branching ratio in terms of the parameters of the above quark level effective lagrangian and we calculated the corresponding nuclear matrix elements of currently interesting nuclear targets like @xmath5au ( the current sindrum ii target ) , @xmath7al ( the target of the ongoing meco experiment ) and @xmath9ti ( the target for the upcoming prism experiment ) .",
    "these results are given in table  [ tab : q_bounds ] and can be used to obtain sensitivity limits from existing or planned experiments on parameters in a variety of particle physics models .",
    "we have considered in detail the new important contributions to conversion present in the inverse seesaw model that come from the exchange of the relatively light 1 singlet neutral leptons .",
