    "domain coarsening of uniform phases in systems locally in thermal equilibrium has received a lot of attention over the past decades @xcite .",
    "after quenching a system below a transition point , ordered regions of the lower symmetry phase form and their characteristic length @xmath5 grows slowly , usually as a power law at large times , @xmath6 .",
    "numbers of theoretical schemes based on the dynamical scaling hypothesis and on the study of the relaxation of topological defects have been proposed to infer coarsening exponents and address their universality @xcite .    less understood is the phase ordering kinetics in systems driven out - of - equilibrium , in particular those forming dissipative structures like regular patterns .",
    "basic non - equilibrium structures are the modulated phases ( stripes ) of well defined periodicity that appear above an instability threshold in thermal convection of fluids , in driven chemical reactors or in electroconvection of nematic liquid crystals @xcite .",
    "a body of numerical results @xcite suggests that the ordering kinetics of stripe patterns following a quench is not universal but model - dependent , and even , parameter - dependent .",
    "modulated phases in isotropic systems actually exhibit a rich variety of topological defects ( dislocations , disclinations , grain boundaries ) not observed in uniform phases .",
    "there might exist many mechanisms controlling the coarsening of stripe patterns : for instance , the annihilation of disclination quadrupoles , as observed experimentally @xcite in block - copolymer melts with cylindrical mesophases ( leading to @xmath7 ) , or grain boundary motion in the swift - hohenberg model close to onset ( @xmath8 ) @xcite .    for a given system , the growing length scale may even be not unique and the dynamical scaling hypothesis not fulfilled .",
    "different defect - based definitions of the size @xmath5 of the ordered striped domains can lead to different coarsening exponents @xcite .",
    "this effect seems to be more pronounced far from the onset of stripe formation . in this regime , the growth of order often becomes very slow or arrested @xcite , in particular due to pattern - induced pinning effects acting on defects @xcite .",
    "in addition , unlike equilibrium phases , the dynamics of nonequilibrium patterns is _ a priori _ not driven by the minimization of a free - energy functional but better modelled by nonpotential ( nonrelaxational ) equations @xcite .",
    "recent simulations of the boussinesq equations for rayleigh - bnard convection @xcite suggest that multiple length scales must be defined to characterize the coarsening of nonpotential stripe patterns . adding nonpotential terms to potential models @xcite ( or , similarly , taking into account hydrodynamics in the order parameter description of",
    "block - copolymer melts @xcite ) can noticeably increase some coarsening rates and lead to qualitative changes in the structure of the defected patterns .    in this context ,",
    "systems forming anisotropic patterns have recently attracted a particular attention @xcite .",

    "domain coarsening in these systems have been studied in details experimentally in electroconvection of nematics @xcite .",
