    "<S> we propose a method of visualizing superpositions of macroscopically distinct states in many - body pure states . </S>",
    "<S> we introduce a visualization function , which is a coarse - grained quasi joint probability density for two or more hermitian additive operators . </S>",
    "<S> if a state contains superpositions of macroscopically distinct states , one can visualize them by plotting the visualization function for appropriately taken operators . </S>",
    "<S> we also explain how to efficiently find appropriate operators for a given state . as examples , we visualize four states containing superpositions of macroscopically distinct states : the ground state of the xy model , that of the heisenberg antiferromagnet , a state in shor s factoring algorithm , and a state in grover s quantum search algorithm . </S>",
    "<S> although the visualization function can take negative values , it becomes non - negative ( hence becomes a coarse - grained joint probability density ) if the characteristic width of the coarse - graining function used in the visualization function is sufficiently large . </S>"
