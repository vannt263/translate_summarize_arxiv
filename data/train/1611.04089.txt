    "<S> extracting energy from fluctuations has been an everlasting endeavor . </S>",
    "<S> an important strategy is to use information - based operation , which allows energy extraction even from thermal fluctuations . </S>",
    "<S> however , it remains unclear whether a _ blind _ external operation , ignorant of such fluctuations , will also work . </S>",
    "<S> assuming such a small operation , we find that energy extraction requires a negative response spectrum in a certain frequency region , which inevitably requires an internal power source to maintain . </S>",
    "<S> energy is then extracted by operating the system at these frequencies . </S>",
    "<S> negative response is intrinsic to any system that adapts to a slow external operation , which could be realized through integral feedback control . </S>",
    "<S> these are demonstrated through a solvable model .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction. _ extracting energy from fluctuations challenges our fundamental understanding of thermodynamics . </S>",
    "<S> maxwell first showed that energy could be extracted from thermal fluctuations by an intelligent demon that can detect the velocity of a single particle  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the apparent violation of the second law of thermodynamics was clarified later by noting that the energy source is information , and its erasure is inevitably dissipative  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> recently , such an information engine has been demonstrated experimentally  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> remarkable theoretical progress has also been achieved along this line  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> another strategy of energy extraction is to exploit the temperature difference between two baths , by using a single particle . </S>",
    "<S> such a stochastic heat engine has also been achieved in recent experiments  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> its energetics and efficiency is under active investigation  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> finally , chemical engines could also be constructed , as achieved by various molecular motors within the cell  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> let us classify these engines based on their operation . </S>",
