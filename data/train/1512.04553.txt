    "<S> we devise local lattice models whose ground states are _ model fractional chern insulators_abelian and non - abelian topologically ordered states characterized by exact ground state degeneracies at any finite size and infinite entanglement gaps . </S>",
    "<S> most saliently , we construct exact parent hamiltonians for two distinct families of bosonic lattice generalizations of the @xmath0 parafermion quantum hall states : ( i ) color - entangled fractional chern insulators at band filling fractions @xmath1 and ( ii ) nematic states at @xmath2 , where @xmath3 is the chern number of the lowest band . in spite of a fluctuating berry curvature , </S>",
    "<S> our construction is partially frustration free : the ground states reside entirely within the lowest band and exactly minimize a local @xmath4-body repulsion term by term . </S>",
    "<S> in addition to providing the first known models hosting intriguing states such as higher chern number generalizations of the fibonacci anyon quantum hall states , the remarkable stability and finite - size properties make our models particularly well - suited for the study of novel phenomena involving e.g.  twist defects and proximity induced superconductivity , as well as being a guide for designing experiments .    [ [ introduction . ] ] introduction . </S>",
    "<S> + + + + + + + + + + + + +    the prospect of lattice - scale fractional quantum hall ( fqh ) phenomena at high temperatures , without the need for a strong magnetic field , has attracted ample recent attention to the theory of fractional chern insulators ( fcis )  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> while experimental realizations of fcis are becoming increasingly realistic in the light of the recent realizations of integer chern insulators with unit chern number in solid state materials  @xcite and cold atom systems  @xcite , the theoretical frontier has turned towards strongly correlated states in bands with higher chern numbers  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this is due to the fact that , although notable differences compared to the continuum setting have been established  @xcite , all fcis discovered in chern number @xmath5 bands have direct continuum fqh analogs to which the adiabatic continuity has been explicitly established in several important cases  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> of special value is the early work by kapit and mueller who provided a natural lattice discretization of the continuum lowest landau level and showed that an two - body on - site interaction leads to a perfect lattice version of the bosonic @xmath6 laughlin state  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> related work has established the existence lattice parent hamiltonians of non - abelian states @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> there is however a glaring lack of similar models describing @xmath7 systems , despite intriguing progress with long - ranged lattice models  @xcite and approximative mappings to continuum models with unusual boundary conditions  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> given the importance of solvable models in the theory of topological and strongly correlated states of matter  ranging from the aklt model for the haldane spin chain  @xcite and the kitaev chain describing a one - dimensional @xmath8-wave superconductor  @xcite to the model wave functions for the continuum fqh effect  @xcite and their concomitant pseudopotential parent hamiltonians  @xcite  finding such has remained an outstanding challenge in the theory of fcis . </S>",
    "<S> this is particularly pressing given the accumulating numerical evidence that @xmath9 systems feature an even richer phenomenology than continuum landau levels .    </S>",
    "<S> in the present work , we bridge this divide and provide exact lattice parent hamiltonians for a large class of abelian as well as non - abelian _ model _ fcis in bands carrying any chern number @xmath10 . </S>",
    "<S> we explicitly verify that the ground state multiplets are exactly degenerate at any finite size , that the gap to excited states remains finite in the thermodynamic limit , and that there is an infinite gap in the particle entanglement spectrum  @xcite .        </S>",
