    "<S> strongly - correlated quantum many - body systems exhibits a variety of exotic phases with long - range quantum correlations , such as spin liquids and supersolids . despite the rapid increase in computational power of modern computers , </S>",
    "<S> the numerical simulation of these complex systems becomes intractable even for a few dozens of particles . </S>",
    "<S> feynman s idea of quantum simulators offers an innovative way to bypass this computational barrier . </S>",
    "<S> however , the proposed realizations of such devices either require very low temperatures ( ultracold gases in optical lattices , trapped ions , superconducting devices ) and considerable technological effort , or are extremely hard to scale in practice ( nmr , linear optics ) . in this work , </S>",
    "<S> we propose a new architecture for a scalable quantum simulator that can operate at room temperature . </S>",
    "<S> it consists of strongly - interacting nuclear spins attached to the diamond surface by its direct chemical treatment , or by means of a functionalized graphene sheet . </S>",
    "<S> the initialization , control and read - out of this quantum simulator can be accomplished with nitrogen - vacancy centers implanted in diamond . </S>",
    "<S> the system can be engineered to simulate a wide variety of interesting strongly - correlated models with long - range dipole - dipole interactions . due to the superior coherence time of nuclear spins and nitrogen - vacancy centers in diamond , </S>",
    "<S> our proposal offers new opportunities towards large - scale quantum simulation at room temperatures .    </S>",
    "<S> many intriguing phenomena in condensed - matter systems originate from the interplay of strong interactions and frustrations . </S>",
    "<S> a representative example is frustrated quantum magnetism , where the spins can not order to minimize all local interactions , and the ground state is highly degenerate @xcite . together with long - range interactions between non - nearest neighbors , </S>",
    "<S> the frustrated quantum models give rise to intriguing quantum phases , e.g. supersolid @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , they can also stabilize the long - sought quantum spin liquid @xcite , which has connections with high - temperature superconductivity @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , the properties of these systems have proven to be very hard to understand from numerical calculations , partly due to the combination of long - range quantum correlations and the superposition principle of quantum mechanics @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this principle implies that the required computational resources grow exponentially with the number of particles , making numerical approaches inefficient . </S>",
    "<S> richard feynman s idea @xcite of quantum simulation provides a powerful solution to this problem : one could gain deep insight into complex states of matter by experimentally simulating them with another well - controlled quantum system @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> large scale quantum simulations is expected to be a powerful tool @xcite for the investigation of fundamental problems in condensed - matter physics .    </S>",
    "<S> quantum simulation has attracted extensive research interest in the last decade . </S>",
    "<S> various architectures for quantum simulations have been constructed based on different systems , ranging from ultracold neutral atoms @xcite , trapped ions @xcite , and photonic systems @xcite to superconducting circuits in solid - state devices @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the still challenging goal is to realize a large - scale quantum simulator that can not be efficiently solved numerically with classical computers , which will probably require going beyond one - dimensional systems . in this work , </S>",
    "<S> we propose a scalable architecture that is of practical interest for large - scale quantum simulation . </S>",
    "<S> our quantum simulator is based on lattices of interacting nuclear spins , which can be fabricated chemically on the diamond surface @xcite or by depositing functionalized graphene films @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> we propose to implant the nitrogen - vacancy ( nv ) centers @xcite beneath the diamond surface , and exploit them as an efficient control element for cooling , spin - spin interaction engineering , and read - out of the nuclear - spin quantum simulator .    </S>",
    "<S> we explain how this simulator is constructed , and establish schemes for its initialization , control and read - out . </S>",
