    "1 .   in high - dimensional data processing such as image / video denoising , inpainting ,",
    "etc . , in order to avoid the boundary effect when performing convolution with a filter , one usually employs symmetric extension and periodization techniques on the data .",
    "in such a case , the data can be regarded as samples on the torus @xmath505 for which the convolution in time domain can be implemented by the classical discrete fourier transforms in the frequency domain . when data are sampled from the regular integer grid ,",
    "the framelet transforms in this case correspond to the classical framelet filter bank transforms , see e.g. @xcite , which employs the fast discrete fourier transform ( dft ) for the fast implementation of framelet transforms .",
    "tremendous numerical examples exist for such types of regular data in inpainting , denoising , debluring , segmentation , etc .",
    "when data are sampled from irregular grids , the nonequispaced fast fourier transforms @xcite provide an implementation of fast framelet transforms in our setting for framelet systems on @xmath506{2}$ ] , which is also an active area of `` irregular sampling '' or `` non - uniform sampling '' .",
    "it is also possible and promising to use component - by - component ( cbc ) points and qmc points with low - discrepancy , see e.g. @xcite to construct framelets in a high - dimensional torus .",
    "2 .   the polynomial - exact quadrature rule simplify the requirement and implementation of tight framelets .",
    "however , we shoud point out that the choice of quadrature rules for tight framelets in theorem  [ thm : framelet.tightness ] is rather general",
    ". one can also consider non - polynomial - exact quadrature rules as suggested by the equivalent conditions ( iv ) ",
    "( v ) of theorem  [ thm : framelet.tightness ] .",
    "these bring flexibility when designing tight framelet systems on manifolds , as there are many quadrature rules with good geometric property and good approximation for numerical integration without the requirement of polynomial exactness , see e.g. qmc designs on the two - dimensional sphere @xcite , minimal energy points on a compact manifold @xcite .",
    "investigation into the construction of tight @xmath0-framelets and tight @xmath0-framelet filter banks for tight framelets on a manifold with such quadrature rules ( not exact for polynomials ) is an important topic .",
