    "<S> we describe the system in terms of local areas ( @xmath0 ) , which can be uniquely assigned to each contact . </S>",
    "<S> these microscopic degrees of freedom serve to define _ local _ order parameters , and allow us to probe the origin of the widely noted scaling of _ global _ physical properties near the unjamming transition . </S>",
    "<S> we find that the scaling with energy @xmath1 near the transition can be attributed to divergences in the distributions of these local areas , which can be clearly separated into contributions arising from locally ordered structures and a disordered piece . </S>",

    "<S> introduction : _ the jamming of soft particles has been of continued interest in the field granular physics @xcite in addition , it is also used as a paradigm to study glassy systems @xcite , active matter @xcite and biological tissues @xcite amongst others . </S>",
    "<S> frictionless soft disks and spheres serve as a first approximation to many theoretical models and have been extensively investigated over the last decade @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the unjamming transition of soft spheres exhibits properties reminiscent of critical points in equilibrium systems . </S>",
    "<S> observations include power laws @xcite , a scaling form for the energy analogous to free energy and resulting relationships between scaling exponents @xcite , scaling collapse of dynamical quantities such as viscosity @xcite , and indications of diverging length scales @xcite . many scaling properties of soft particles near the jamming transition have been analysed in detail @xcite , and finite - size scaling studies seem to suggest a mixed order transition with two critical exponents @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , despite considerable effort towards a unifying theory , a clear description is still lacking , and the origin of various power laws in this system have remained somewhat mysterious . </S>",
    "<S> theories so far have focussed on the behavior of global quantities such as energy , packing fraction , pressure , stresses , and the total contact numbers . </S>",
    "<S> this is in contrast to the norm in studying critical points where a local order parameter and its distribution within the system is of primary importance .     </S>",
