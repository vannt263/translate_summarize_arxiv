    "open system dynamics is of uppermost importance in the quantum regime where non classical phenomena turn out to be very fragile with respect to any noise source .",
    "the noise effects are often modeled as the result of an interaction of the system with a large number of uncontrollable degrees of freedom , i.e.  an _ environment _ @xcite .",
    "environments can be assumed as to be composed by different kinds of particles , for instance oscillators or spin-@xmath1 .",
    "these objects come , under the mathematical point of view , from the realizations of two different algebras ( the heisenberg - weyl algebra and the lie algebra su@xmath2 ) corresponding to fermionic and bosonic commutation relations .",
    "these latter can be seen as two limit cases of more general commutation relations involving deformed algebras parameterized by one continuous parameter @xcite .",
    "our aim is to analyze the qubit dynamics in an environment of oscillators satisfying suitable @xmath0-deformed commutation relations , such that it permits to continuously interpolate between oscillators and spin-@xmath1 .",
    "actually , we investigate how quantum decoherence phenomena changes in passing from bosonic to fermionic environments .",
    "we find a slowing down of decoherence in the fermionic case .",
    "however , this effect only manifests at finite temperature .",
    "the paper is organized as follows . in section [ model ]",

    "we then study the dynamics of two qubits and we evaluate the entanglement decay by distinguishing the case of the two qubits in the same environment ( section [ twoqubit_same ] ) , from that of the two qubits in separate environments ( section [ twoqubit_separate ] ) .",
    "finally , section [ conclude ] is for concluding remarks .",
