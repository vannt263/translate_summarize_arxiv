    "<S> magnetoencephalographic ( meg ) measurements record magnetic fields generated from neurons while information is being processed in the brain . </S>",
    "<S> the inverse problem of identifying sources of biomagnetic fields and deducing their intensities from meg measurements is ill - posed when the number of field detectors is far less than the number of sources . </S>",
    "<S> this problem is less severe if there is already a reasonable prior knowledge in the form of a distribution in the intensity of source activation . in this case </S>",
    "<S> the problem of identifying and deducing source intensities may be transformed to one of using the meg data to update a prior distribution to a posterior distribution . here </S>",
    "<S> we report on some work done using the maximum entropy method ( me ) as an updating tool . </S>",
    "<S> specifically , we propose an implementation of the me method in cases when the prior contain almost no knowledge of source activation . </S>",
    "<S> two examples are studied , in which part of motor cortex is activated with uniform and varying intensities , respectively .     </S>",
