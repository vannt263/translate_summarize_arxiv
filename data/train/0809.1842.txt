    "<S> in numerical experiments involving nonlinear solitary waves propagating through nonhomogeneous media one observes `` breathing '' in the sense of the amplitude of the wave going up and down on a much faster scale than the motion of the wave  see fig . </S>",
    "<S> [ f : fastslow1 ] below . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we investigate this phenomenon in the simplest case of stationary waves in which the evolution corresponds to relaxation to a nonlinear ground state . </S>",
    "<S> the particular model is the popular @xmath0 impurity in the cubic nonlinear schrdinger equation on the line . </S>",
    "<S> we give asymptotics of the amplitude on a finite but relevant time interval and show their remarkable agreement with numerical experiments , see fig . </S>",
    "<S> [ f:1 ] . </S>",
    "<S> we stress the nonlinear origin of the `` breathing patterns '' caused by the selection of the ground state depending on the initial data , and by the non - normality of the linearized operator . </S>"
