    "one immediate result of our mapping onto the kondo problem is the entropy loss in the flow from the ultraviolet to the infrared . in ref .",

    "<S> we study quasiparticle tunneling between the edges of a non - abelian topological state . </S>",
    "<S> the simplest examples are a @xmath0 superconductor and the moore - read pfaffian non - abelian fractional quantum hall state ; the latter state may have been observed at landau level filling fraction @xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> formulating the problem is conceptually and technically non - trivial : edge quasiparticle correlation functions are elements of a vector space , and transform into each other as the quasiparticle coordinates are braided . </S>",
    "<S> we show in general how to resolve this difficulty and uniquely define the quasiparticle tunneling hamiltonian . </S>",
    "<S> the tunneling operators in the simplest examples can then be rewritten in terms of a free boson . </S>",
    "<S> one key consequence of this bosonization is an emergent spin-@xmath2 degree of freedom . </S>",
    "<S> we show that vortex tunneling across a @xmath0 superconductor is equivalent to the single - channel kondo problem , while quasiparticle tunneling across the moore - read state is analogous to the two - channel kondo effect . </S>",
    "<S> temperature and voltage dependences of the tunneling conductivity are given in the low- and high - temperature limits . </S>"
