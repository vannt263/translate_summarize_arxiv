    "discrete tomography is concerned with problems such as reconstructing binary images on a lattice from given projections in lattice directions @xcite .",
    "each point of a binary image has a value equal to zero or one .",
    "the line sum of a line through the image is the sum of the values of the points on this line .",
    "the projection of the image in a certain lattice direction consists of all the line sums of the lines through the image in this direction .",
    "several problems related to the reconstruction of binary images from two or more projections have been described in the literature @xcite . already in 1957 , ryser gave an algorithm to reconstruct binary images from their horizontal and vertical projections and characterised the set of projections that correspond to a unique binary image @xcite .",

    "an interesting problem in discrete tomography is the stability of reconstructions .",
    "even if an image is uniquely determined by its projections , a very small error in the projections may lead to a completely different reconstruction @xcite .",

    "<S> in this paper we prove several new stability results for the reconstruction of binary images from two projections . </S>",
    "<S> we consider an original image that is uniquely determined by its projections and possible reconstructions from slightly different projections . </S>",
    "<S> we show that for a given difference in the projections , the reconstruction can only be disjoint from the original image if the size of the image is not too large . </S>",
    "<S> we also prove an upper bound for the size of the image given the error in the projections and the size of the intersection between the image and the reconstruction .    </S>",
