    "<S> in this paper we derive the equations of motion for two - layer point vortex motion on the upper half plane . </S>",
    "<S> we study the invariants using symmetry , including the hamiltonian and show that the two vortex problem is integrable . </S>",
    "<S> we characterize all two vortex motions for the cases where the vortex strengths are both equal , @xmath0 and when they are opposite @xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> we also prove that there are no equilibria for the two vortex problem when @xmath1 .  </S>",
    "<S> we show that there is only one relative equilibrium configuration when @xmath0 and the vortices are in different layers . </S>",
    "<S> we also make observations concerning the finite - time collapse of two vortices in the half plane . </S>",
    "<S> we then compare the regimes of motion for both cases ( motion on the half plane ) with the case of the two - layer vortex problem on the entire plane . </S>",
    "<S> we also study several classes of streamline topologies for two vortices in different layers . </S>",
    "<S> we conclude with a hamiltonian study of integrable two - layer 3 vortex motion on the half plane by studying integrable symmetrical configurations and provide a rich class of new relative equilibria . </S>"
