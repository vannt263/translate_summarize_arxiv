    "<S> symmetrical multilevel diversity coding ( smdc ) is a network compression problem introduced by roche ( 1992 ) and yeung ( 1995 ) . in this </S>",
    "<S> setting , a simple separate coding strategy known as superposition coding was shown to be optimal in terms of achieving the minimum sum rate ( roche , yeung , and hau , 1997 ) and the entire admissible rate region ( yeung and zhang , 1999 ) of the problem . </S>",
    "<S> this paper considers a natural generalization of smdc to the secure communication setting with an additional eavesdropper . </S>",
    "<S> it is required that all sources need to be kept perfectly secret from the eavesdropper as long as the number of encoder outputs available at the eavesdropper is no more than a given threshold . </S>",
    "<S> first , the problem of encoding individual sources is studied . a precise characterization of the entire admissible rate region is established via a connection to the problem of secure coding over a three - layer wiretap network and utilizing some basic polyhedral structure of the admissible rate region . </S>",
    "<S> building on this result , it is then shown that superposition coding remains optimal in terms of achieving the minimum sum rate for the general secure smdc problem . </S>"
