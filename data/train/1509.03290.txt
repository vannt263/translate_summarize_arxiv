    "<S> a nondissipative supercurrent state of a josephson junction is metastable with respect to the formation of a finite - resistance state . </S>",
    "<S> this transition is driven by fluctuations , thermal at high temperatures and quantum at low temperatures . </S>",
    "<S> we evaluate the life time of such a state due to quantum fluctuations in the limit when the supercurrent is approaching the critical current . </S>",
    "<S> the decay probability is determined by the instanton action for the superconducting phase difference across the junction . at low temperatures , dynamics of the phase </S>",
    "<S> is massive and is determined by the effective capacitance , which is a sum of the geometric and intrinsic capacitance of the junction . </S>",
    "<S> we model the central part of the josephson junction either by an arbitrary short mesoscopic conductor described by the set of its transmission coefficients , or by a diffusive wire of an arbitrary length . </S>",
    "<S> the intrinsic capacitance can generally be estimated as @xmath0 , where @xmath1 is the normal - state conductance of the junction and @xmath2 is the proximity minigap in its normal part . </S>",
    "<S> the obtained capacitance is sufficiently large to qualitatively explain hysteretic behavior of the current - voltage characteristic even in the absence of overheating . </S>"
