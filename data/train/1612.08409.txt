    "<S> memristive systems are generalisations of memristors , which are resistors with memory . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> , we present a quantum description of memristive systems . using this model we propose and </S>",
    "<S> experimentally demonstrate a simple and practical scheme for realising memristive systems with quantum dots . </S>",
    "<S> the approach harnesses a phenomenon that is commonly seen as a bane of nanoelectronics , i.e. switching of a trapped charge in the vicinity of the device . </S>",
    "<S> we show that quantum - dot memristive systems have hysteresis current - voltage characteristics and quantum jump induced stochastic behaviour . </S>",
    "<S> realising such a quantum memristor completes the menu of components for quantum circuit design .    </S>",
    "<S> resistors , capacitors and inductors are the three fundamental circuit elements that are familiar from high school level electronics . in 1971 leon chua postulated a fourth two - terminal circuit element characterized by a relationship between charge and flux linkage  @xcite . predicting that conductance would depend on the history of current flow through it </S>",
    "<S> , he dubbed it _ </S>",
    "<S> memristor _ for memory and resistor . in 2008 </S>",
    "<S> , a theoretical article is published in nature showing that memristive behaviour existed in previously observed resistance switching behaviour when  electronic and ionic transport are coupled \" , especially in nanoscale films  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> it remains controversial whether indeed this class of system implements the memristor as envisaged by chua  @xcite but the term has come to be used by many authors as a generic reference to 2-terminal non - volatile memory devices based on resistance switching , blurring the line with resistive random - access memory rram  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> subsequent research has realised memristive behaviour in a wide variety of systems , and has identified applications where the memory effect may lead to dramatic advantages in simplicity and power versus conventional electronics . these range from ultra dense , </S>",
    "<S> low power memory  @xcite envisaged as a successor to today s ram and flash , through novel stateful logic processes  @xcite , and even neuromorphic computing where the memristor is seen as a synthetic analog of a neuron  @xcite .    as with many other electronic devices , the behaviour of memristive systems is increasingly affected by quantum phenomena as the device size shrinks to ever smaller dimensions . </S>",
    "<S> memristors based on ionic motion can already be scaled down to less than 10 nm  @xcite , and other candidates of memristive systems include even smaller objects , e.g.  molecules  @xcite . at the nanoscale </S>",
    "<S> , electron transport through devices is strongly influenced by the discrete energy spectrum and interference effects  @xcite , which may even emerge at room temperature in certain molecules  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> it is therefore crucial to understand the non - classical behaviour that emerges in nanoscale memristive systems . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> , we will present a quantum - mechanical model of a memristive system . </S>",
    "<S> based on this generic model we propose and demonstrate a specific scheme of a memristive device using two capacitively - coupled quantum dots . </S>",
    "<S> capacitive links between two quantum dots  @xcite or between a quantum dot and random  @xcite or intentionally placed impurities  @xcite are often used for the read - out of charge- and spin - states in semiconductor nanodevices  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the effect we now wish to exploit is often observed in nanoelectronic experiments , i.e. the device characteristics alter dramatically when a charge state in its immediate environment changes . </S>",
    "<S> this is frequently a frustration in experimental work , but we now propose that it may be usefully harnessed as the basis of memristive function </S>",
    "<S> . room temperature operation of these devices could potentially be achieved in nano - scale semiconductor devices  @xcite or by using single - molecule transistors  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> while hysteresis effect have previously been studied in single - electron devices@xcite , the use of a double quantum dot system as a memristive device has not been explored to date .    in quantum mechanics , </S>",
    "<S> a memristive device can be considered as an open quantum system : the internal state of the device interacts with the environment , typically the electrodes  @xcite , such that the internal state affects the electron transport ( the resistance ) , and the evolution of the internal state depends on external signals ( current and voltage ) . </S>",
    "<S> an example of such an open quantum system is a pair of capacitively - coupled quantum dots , where only one dot participates in the electron transport while the other controls the transport via the capacitive coupling . </S>",
    "<S> the environment ( electrodes ) is configured such that the evolution of the control dot depends on the applied voltage . </S>",
    "<S> we will show that such a double - dot device has the characteristic property of memristive systems , i.e.  current - voltage hysteresis curves  @xcite . following the theoretical description of this open quantum system </S>",
    "<S> , we demonstrate an experimental realization of the hysteresis in a capacitively coupled pair of silicon metal - oxide - semiconductor ( mos ) quantum dots . </S>",
    "<S> unlike classical memristive systems , the current in quantum memristive systems shows stochastic behaviour and may not converge to a periodic curve under periodic driving due to quantum jumps between different values of the quantised conductance .     </S>",
