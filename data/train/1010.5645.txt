    "there is a number of quantum many - body problems that are of paramount importance for condensed matter physics and , at the same time , possess an exact solution .",
    "these are the anderson orthogonality catastrophe@xcite , fermi edge singularity@xcite ( fes ) , luttinger liquid@xcite ( ll ) zero - bias anomaly@xcite , and kondo problems@xcite .",
    "it has been realized long ago that these problems are , in fact , inter - connected , both vis - a - vis the underlying physics and the mathematics involved .",
    "such connections have been used , e.g. , for the representation of the dynamics of the kondo problem as an infinite sequence of fermi - edge - singularity events@xcite .    in our recent works@xcite",
    "we have addressed some of these problems away from equilibrium . to achieve this goal ,",
    "we have developed a non - equilibrium bosonization technique generalizing the conventional bosonization @xcite onto problems with non - equilibrium distribution functions .",
    "we have shown that the relevant correlation functions can be expressed through fredholm determinants of a `` counting '' operator .",
    "the information on the specific type of the problem , as well as on different aspects of the interaction , is encoded in the time - dependent scattering phase of the counting operator .",
    "the results of refs .   have demonstrated that those classic many - body problems are more closely connected than has been previously understood , extending the interrelations into the non - equilibrium regime .",
    "the purpose of the present paper is twofold .",
    "first , we present the relation between the ll tunneling spectroscopy , the full counting statistics , and the fermi - edge singularity problems in a systematic way .",
    "we also include in this scheme the generalization of the ll problem to a non - equilibrium gas of strongly repulsive bosons ( tonks - girardeau gas ) .",
    "these results are summarized in table  [ table1 ] .",
    "second , we apply the general theory of asymptotic behavior of toeplitz determinants to the present problems .",
    "this allows us to extract important information on the many - body physics of these models , including non - equilibrium dephasing rates and the modification of power - law exponents due to non - equilibrium conditions .",

    "the operators are controlled by the ( non - equilibrium ) distribution function , as well as by the value of the scattering phase depending on the interaction strength .",
    "our non - equilibrium bosonization approach allows to solve these problems and to establish connections among them .",

    "<S> non - equilibrium bosonization technique facilitates the solution of a number of important many - body problems out of equilibrium , including the fermi - edge singularity , the tunneling spectroscopy and full counting statistics of interacting fermions forming a luttinger liquid . </S>",
    "<S> we generalize the method to non - equilibrium hard - core bosons ( tonks - girardeau gas ) and establish interrelations between all these problems . </S>",
    "<S> the results can be expressed in terms of fredholm determinants of toeplitz type . </S>",
    "<S> we analyze the long time asymptotics of such determinants , using szeg and fisher - hartwig theorems . </S>",
    "<S> our analysis yields dephasing rates as well as power - law scaling behavior , with exponents depending not only on the interaction strength but also on the non - equilibrium state of the system .    </S>",
