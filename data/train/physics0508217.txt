    "<S> we extend an isothermal thermal model of ultracold neutral plasma expansion to systems without spherical symmetry , and use this model to interpret new fluorescence measurements on these plasmas . by assuming a self - similar expansion , it is possible to solve the fluid equations analytically and to include velocity effects to predict the fluorescence signals . in spite of the simplicity of this approach </S>",
    "<S> , the model reproduces the major features of the experimental data .    </S>",
    "<S> ultracold plasmas are produced from photo - ionized laser - cooled gases @xcite . in these laboratory plasmas </S>",
    "<S> , it is possible to study the kinetics and thermodynamics of multi - component , strongly - interacting coulomb systems . </S>",
    "<S> these systems are characterized by the ratio of the nearest - neighbor coulomb energy to the average kinetic energy , denoted as @xmath0 , with @xmath1 being the interparticle spacing .    </S>",
    "<S> a new class of ultracold plasma experiments has recently become available in which it is possible to spectroscopically study the plasma ions @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> these plasmas are made using alkaline - earth atoms , because the resonance transition wavelengths of the ions are readily generated using standard laser methods . </S>",
    "<S> the spatially - resolved time evolution of the plasma ion temperature and density can be measured using absorption and fluorescence techniques .    </S>",
    "<S> these ultracold neutral plasmas are not trapped , although efforts are underway in a few laboratories to trap them . </S>",
    "<S> the untrapped plasmas freely expand , and as they expand the density and temperature change radically . </S>",
    "<S> processes of recombination , collisional and thermal ionization , radiative cascade , and adiabatic and evaporative cooling all play important roles in how the system evolves and equilibrates .    a variety of models have been used to investigate the properties of these plasmas @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> one particularly simple isothermal fluid model @xcite has been surprisingly successful in predicting the general features of these plasmas @xcite . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we extend this model from the spherically - symmetric gaussian plasma distributions to gaussian distributions with elliptical symmetry .    </S>",
    "<S> the elliptical symmetry has important experimental advantages . in such systems </S>",
    "<S> the plasma expands primarily in two dimensions . </S>",
    "<S> the practical advantage is that the density falls more slowly than in the three dimensional case , making it possible to study the plasma for longer times . </S>",
    "<S> the doppler - shift due to the directed expansion of the plasma is also suppressed . </S>",
    "<S> it should therefore be possible to study plasma oscillations and heating effects for greater time periods before these oscillations are masked by the directed expansion of the plasma . finally , </S>",
    "<S> if the plasmas are generated from a density - limited neutral atom trap , the elongated symmetry allows a greater number of atoms to be trapped initially , corresponding to a greater column density of plasma ions . </S>",
    "<S> this directly increases the visibility of fluorescence and absorption signals . </S>"
