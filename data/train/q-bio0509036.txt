    "we have extended the kuramoto model to frequencies which can drift in time following ornstein - uhlenbeck dynamics .",
    "the net effect is that the white noise source in eq .",
    "[ kuramoto ] is replaced by colored noise ( with a cauchy distributed power spectrum ) , hereby adding a new time scale describing memory or frequency stiffness to the problem .",
    "apart from mean field coupling among the phases which introduces a non - linearity , the stochastic phase and frequency dynamics lead to a linear fokker - planck operator which can be solved .",
    "consequently the linear stability of the incoherent state can be addressed analytically using spectral calculus .",
    "the expression for the critical coupling above which macroscopic phase coherence emerges can be resummed and expressed in terms of incomplete @xmath106-functions .",
    "asymptotic expansion for small and large @xmath35 shows that the full model continuously interpolates between two limits of the original kuramoto model : one dominated by noise ( large @xmath35 ) and the other by the frequency dispersion ( small @xmath35 ) .",
    "therefore , the coupling force must counteract different sources of fluctuations to induce collective synchrony in drifting frequency oscillators , depending on the regime set by @xmath35 . specifically , for slowly drifting ( small @xmath35 ) frequencies the model approaches the noiseless model ( eq .",
    "[ kuramoto ] with @xmath25 and @xmath165 ) where the coupling splits the population into distinct locked and incoherent sub - populations , depending on the proximity of individual frequencies to the population mode . as @xmath35 increases , ( while @xmath45 is held fixed ) the frequencies lose their stiffness which results in a reduction in the critical coupling @xmath3 needed for synchrony .",
    "finally for very rapidly drifting oscillators ( large @xmath35 ) cancel out the frequency distribution and generate an effective white noise source acting on the phases of otherwise equal frequency oscillators . at the same time",
    "the locked and incoherent subgroups become indistinguishable .",
