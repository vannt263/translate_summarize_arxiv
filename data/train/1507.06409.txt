    "<S> we scrutinize the real - frequency structure of the self - energy in the superconducting state of the attractive hubbard model within the dynamical mean - field theory . within the strong - coupling superconducting phase which has been understood in terms of the bose - einstein condensation in the literature , we find two qualitatively different regions crossing over each other . in one region close to zero temperature </S>",
    "<S> , the self - energy depends on the frequency only weakly at low energy . on the other hand , in the region close to the critical temperature , the self - energy shows a pole structure . </S>",
    "<S> the latter region becomes more dominant as the interaction becomes stronger . </S>",
    "<S> we reveal that the self - energy pole in the latter region is generated by a coupling to a hidden _ fermionic _ excitation . </S>",
    "<S> the hidden fermion persists in the normal state , where it yields a pseudogap . </S>",
    "<S> we compare these properties with those of the repulsive hubbard model relevant for high - temperature cuprate superconductors , showing that hidden fermions are a key common ingredient in strongly correlated superconductivity .    </S>",
    "<S> a range of metals show superconductivity below a critical temperature ( @xmath0 ) , at which paired electrons acquire a spatial coherence . in conventional superconductors , </S>",
    "<S> the pairing mechanism is well described by the bardeen - cooper - schrieffer ( bcs ) theory @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , the bcs theory does not straightforwardly apply when the attractive interaction between electrons is strong . in this case , the electron pairing occurs at a temperature higher than @xmath0 and superconductivity arises when the electron pairs ( regarded as composite bosons ) go through the bose - einstein condensation ( bec ) at a lower temperature @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> such superconductivity in the bec regime has been explored for ultracold fermionic atom systems @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in fact , tightly - bound pairs @xcite and an associated pseudogap behavior @xcite have been observed for @xmath1k gas in the strongly - interacting region . </S>",
    "<S> a recent experiment also suggested that the superconductivity in fese is in the bcs - bec crossover regime @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the preformed pairing has also been proposed in the context of cuprate high-@xmath0 superconductors @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> although it looks unlikely that the preformed pairing solely can explain the whole pseudogap behavior in the cuprates @xcite , it may be relevant in a region close to @xmath0 around the optimal doping @xcite .    on the theoretical side , the crossover from bcs to bec @xcite has been intensively studied with continuous @xcite or lattice @xcite fermion models , both of which give a similar phase diagram . </S>",
    "<S> the latter is , however , more tractable with numerical simulations and allows us to employ nonperturbative methods to study this problem . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , the dynamical mean - field theory ( dmft ) @xcite , which becomes exact in infinite spatial dimensions , is a suitable tool to study dynamical properties of the lattice models . </S>",
    "<S> in fact , the dmft and its extensions have been extensively applied to the attractive hubbard model @xcite . among the results , for strong coupling , </S>",
    "<S> a pseudogap in the spectral function has been found above @xmath0 @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> further , the pseudogap state was found to be separated from a fermi - liquid metal at weak coupling by a first - order pairing transition @xcite . in light of an electron - hole transformation </S>",
    "<S> @xcite , this first - order transition is mapped onto a well - known mott metal - insulator transition in the repulsive half - filled hubbard model under an external magnetic field . since the mott gap in the repulsive model </S>",
    "<S> is characterized by an emergent pole in the self - energy , a self - energy pole emerges equivalently in the pseudogap state of the attractive model @xcite , signaling the formation of the preformed pairs above @xmath0 .    </S>",
    "<S> it is a nontrivial open problem to determine the fate of the self - energy pole below @xmath0 since the sudden appearance of the pole in the normal phase appears incompatible with a smooth crossover of superconducting properties from weak to strong coupling . at weak coupling in the bcs region , the dynamics of quasiparticles , described by the low - frequency dependence of the self - energies , </S>",
    "<S> is well understood . on the other hand , at strong coupling in the bec region , the superconducting transition </S>",
    "<S> is well described by the boson condensation of tightly - bound cooper pairs @xcite , while the dynamics of the original fermions , which is directly relevant to the single - particle spectroscopies such as the radiofrequency @xcite / photoemission spectroscopy , has not been fully addressed .    in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we propose an alternative (  fermionic \" ) viewpoint to the bec of tightly - bound cooper pairs . </S>",
    "<S> this decription also applies to the intermediate - coupling region where the bosonic picture does not hold anymore , bridging the bec limit with the well known bcs region . </S>",
    "<S> our main purpose is to reveal that the low - energy quasiparticle dynamics in the bec region is governed by a coupling to a _ </S>",
    "<S> fermionic _ excitation ( hidden fermion ) arising from the strong interaction . </S>",
    "<S> the hidden fermion generates the self - energy pole in the normal states and persists below @xmath0 , similarly to what has been found in the superconducting state of the repulsive hubbard model @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> as the temperature @xmath2 lowers further , however , the hidden fermion loses the weight and eventually vanishes , in marked contrast with the repulsive case . </S>",
    "<S> these results set out for a unified view of the superconducting transition of tightly - bound cooper pairs and its relation with the mott physics .    </S>",
