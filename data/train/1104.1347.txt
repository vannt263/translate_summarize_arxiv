    "<S> we demonstrate a simple pulse shaping technique designed to improve the fidelity of spin - dependent force operations commonly used to implement entangling gates in trapped - ion systems . </S>",
    "<S> this extension of the mlmer - srensen gate can theoretically suppress the effects of certain frequency and timing errors to any desired order and is demonstrated through walsh modulation of a two - qubit entangling gate on trapped atomic ions . </S>",
    "<S> the technique is applicable to any system of qubits coupled through collective harmonic oscillator modes .    </S>",
    "<S> the use of spin - dependent forces to create entangled quantum systems has become widespread @xcite and is currently the technique used for the highest fidelity multi - qubit operations @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this powerful technique , first proposed in @xcite , has been used to implement quantum algorithms @xcite , create large entangled states @xcite , test quantum fundamentals @xcite , and perform simulations of quantum magnetism @xcite and quantum field theory @xcite . as these types of experiments are scaled to larger numbers of qubits and more complex algorithms , the accumulation of gate errors </S>",
    "<S> will eventually require quantum error correction . because of the large overhead required for quantum error correction , it is important that qubit operations be optimized passively in terms of speed and robustness to non - ideal control environments . in this paper </S>",
