    "nonequilibrium behaviors of physical systems are one of the most important theme of the statistical physics .",
    "although the general theory is partially absent , a lot of efforts have been made to study several kinds of simplified models that possess nonequilibrium properties .",
    "one of the most studied model is the asymmetric simple exclusion process ( asep ) .",
    "this is a model of many particles on a one - dimensional system . due to an exclusion interaction",
    ", the model essentially belongs to a many - body system , however , the model is solvable and deep analysis can be made by means of the matrix product method .",
    "there are many variants for the asep .",
    "the two - species version of the asep exhibits remarkable behaviors in many ways , such as construction of a shock profile @xcite and spontaneous translation breaking @xcite .",
    "one of the most interesting findings for the asep is the relation with the theory of the @xmath0-orthogonal polynomials @xcite . in this paper",
    ", we extend the relation for the two - species version of the asep with open boundary conditions .",
    "we will see that the continuous big @xmath0-hermite polynomials play a central role for this model . by the help of the @xmath0-calculus",
    ", we calculate the bulk quantities and the @xmath1-point functions for the steady state and obtain integral formulae for them .",
    "the integral formulae are useful for evaluation in the thermodynamic limit .",
    "we clarify the existence of the boundary - induced phase transitions .",
    "this paper is organized as follows . in sec .",

    "<S> we study the steady state of the two - species asymmetric simple exclusion process ( asep ) with open boundary conditions . </S>",
    "<S> the matrix product method works for the determination of the stationary probability distribution . </S>",
    "<S> several physical quantities are calculated through an explicit representation for the matrix products . by making full use of the relation with the continuous big @xmath0-hermite polynomials , </S>",
    "<S> we arrive at integral formulae for the partition function and the @xmath1-point functions . </S>",
    "<S> we examine the thermodynamic limit and find three phases : the low - density phase , the high - density phase and the maximal current phase . </S>"
