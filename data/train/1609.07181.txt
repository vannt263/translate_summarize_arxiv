    "<S> we have conducted a search for l subdwarf candidates within the photometric catalogues of the ukirt infrared deep sky survey and sloan digital sky survey . </S>",
    "<S> six of our candidates are confirmed as l subdwarfs spectroscopically at optical and/or near infrared wavelengths . </S>",
    "<S> we also present new optical spectra of three previously known l subdwarfs ( wisea j001450.17 - 083823.4 , 2mass j00412179 + 3547133 , ulas j124425.75 + 102439.3 ) . </S>",
    "<S> we examined the spectral types and metallicity subclasses classification of known l subdwarfs . </S>",
    "<S> we summarised the spectroscopic properties of l subdwarfs with different spectral types and subclasses . </S>",
    "<S> we classify these new l subdwarfs by comparing their spectra to known l subdwarfs and l dwarf standards . </S>",
    "<S> we estimate temperatures and metallicities of 22 late type m and l subdwarfs by comparing their spectra to bt - settl models . </S>",
    "<S> we find that l subdwarfs have temperatures between 1500 k and 2700 k , which are higher than similarly - typed l dwarfs by around 100 - 400 k depending on different subclasses and subtypes . </S>",
    "<S> we constrained the metallicity ranges of subclasses of m , l and t subdwarfs . </S>",
    "<S> we also discussed the spectral type and absolute magnitude relationships for l and t subdwarfs .    </S>",
