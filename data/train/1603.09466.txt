    "the monte carlo simulations described in this paper are based on the metropolis algorithm with parallel tempering  @xcite and over - relaxation  @xcite .",
    "the spins are modelled as classical vectors of length @xmath97 and locally updated using the standard marsaglia method  @xcite .",
    "we consider cubic clusters of linear dimension @xmath98 , based on the 16-site cubic unit cell of the pyrochlore lattice , and containing @xmath99 sites . a monte carlo step ( mcs )",
    "is defined as @xmath100 attempts to locally update a randomly chosen spin , and @xmath101 ( measured in mcs ) is the total monte carlo time over which data are collected .",
    "equilibration is performed for each temperature in two successive steps .",
    "first the system is slowly cooled down from high temperature ( random initial spin configuration ) to the temperature of measurement @xmath102 during @xmath103 mcs .",
    "then , the system is equilibrated at temperature @xmath102 during additional @xmath103 mcs .",
    "after equilibration , monte carlo time is set to zero and measurements start and go on for @xmath104 mcs .",
    "all thermodynamical observables have been averaged over monte carlo time every 10 mcs , except for calculations of the equal - time structure factor @xmath105 , where data points were taken every 100 mcs for efficiency .",
    "the parallel tempering method implies simultaneously simulating a large number of replicas of the system in parallel , with each replica held at a different temperature .",
    "the program then regularly attempts to swap the spin configurations of replicas with neighbouring temperatures , in such a way as to maintain detailed balance  @xcite . simulating @xmath106 replicas , with",
    "swaps attempted every 100 mcs appears to offer a good compromise between efficiency and decorrelation for @xmath107 .    in the case of the over - relaxation method , after each monte carlo step , two further sweeps are made of the entire lattice .",
    "each spin feels an effective field due to the interaction with its six nearest neighbours ; any rotation around this axis conserves the energy and is thus an acceptable move respecting detailed balance . to avoid rotating successive neighbouring spins",
    ", we first update all spins of sublattice 0 , then sublattice 1 , 2 and finally 3 .",
    "the first iteration of all @xmath100 spins is deterministic , _",
    "i.e. _ we rotate them by the maximum allowed angle ; while for the second iteration , a random angle of rotation is chosen for each spin .",
    "the generation of so many random numbers is of course time consuming but is recommended for better equilibration  @xcite .",
