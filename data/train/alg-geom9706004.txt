    "<S> we develop numerical homotopy algorithms for solving systems of polynomial equations arising from the classical schubert calculus . </S>",
    "<S> these homotopies are optimal in that generically no paths diverge . for problems defined by hypersurface schubert conditions we give two algorithms based on extrinsic deformations of the grassmannian : </S>",
    "<S> one is derived from a grbner basis for the plcker ideal of the grassmannian and the other from a sagbi basis for its projective coordinate ring . </S>",
    "<S> the more general case of special schubert conditions is solved by delicate intrinsic deformations , called pieri homotopies , which first arose in the study of enumerative geometry over the real numbers . </S>",
    "<S> computational results are presented and applications to control theory are discussed . </S>"
