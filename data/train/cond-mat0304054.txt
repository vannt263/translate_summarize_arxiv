    "scattering of quasiparticles by these objects leads to the suppression of the order parameter ( pairbreaking ) , suppression of the superfluid transition as well as more subtle physical effects associated with the interplay of scattering and particle - hole coherence in the superfluid state .",

    "the free surface is modelled as a specular surface , and the film resides on a substrate that we assume is atomically rough .",
    "we model the scattering of quasiparticles by the substrate by introducing a thin layer of atomic - size impurities randomly distributed on the surface ( tdl model ) .",
    "the width , @xmath3 , of the impurity layer is assumed to be much less than the superfluid coherence length , while the mean free path @xmath4 for quasiparticles propagating in the layer is much smaller than the width @xmath3 .",
    "thus , quasiparticles are strongly scattered inside the layer , but eventually scatter out of the layer at an angle uncorrelated with the incident trajectory .",

    "<S> the pairing correlations in superfluid  are strongly modified by quasiparticle scattering off a surface or an interface . </S>",
    "<S> we present theoretical results and predictions for the order parameter , the quasiparticle excitation spectrum and the free energy for thin films of superfluid . </S>",
    "<S> both specular and diffuse scattering by a substrate are considered , while the free surface is assumed to be a perfectly reflecting specular boundary . </S>",
    "<S> the results are based on self - consistent calculations of the order parameter and quasiparticle excitation spectrum at zero pressure . </S>",
    "<S> we obtain new results for the phase diagram , free energy , entropy and specific heat of thin films of superfluid . </S>"
