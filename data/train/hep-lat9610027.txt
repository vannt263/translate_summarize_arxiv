    "numerical simulations of qcd can be performed by discretizing euclidean space - time onto a lattice and working within a finite volume .",
    "physical measurements are then obtained by extrapolating to zero lattice spacing and infinite lattice volume .",
    "there are many lattice actions which correspond to continuum qcd in the appropriate limit , and a lot of recent work has focussed on finding actions which reduce the errors due to discrete lattice spacing .",
    "the goal is to find actions which provide accurate results on coarsely - spaced lattices and so allow lattice sizes ( and therefore computing resources ) to be reduced .",
    "a progress report on improving actions may be found in ref .",
    "@xcite .    to demonstrate the value of an improved action",
    ", one would like to show that accurate results can be obtained from coarse lattices for a wide variety of quantities . as a first step beyond the mass spectrum , where significant improvements on coarse lattices have already been demonstrated , we consider the decay constants of light vector mesons and the associated vector current renormalization factor .",
    "( a preliminary discussion was given in ref .",
    "an advantage of studying vector currents is the existence of a conserved vector current even at nonzero lattice spacing , which does not get renormalized .",
    "neglecting lattice spacing errors , the matrix element of the conserved current between any initial and final states is identical to the matrix element of the local vector current between those same states , except for the multiplicative `` renormalization factor '' , @xmath4 . in this way",
    ", @xmath4 can be easily computed . in practice",
    ", it has been known for a long time@xcite that the determination of @xmath4 is quite sensitive to lattice spacing errors , which is another reason that the vector current is valuable for the present work , since it is precisely these errors that should be reduced by an improved action .",
    "the action@xcite that will be used here is improved at the classical level up to and including @xmath3 , and is also improved at the quantum level via the `` tadpole factor '' of lepage and mackenzie@xcite .",
    "the action has the added feature of being quite simple , since the gauge sector contains only @xmath5 and @xmath6 plaquettes , and the fermion sector does not contain the so - called `` clover '' term and has no need of the associated field transformations .",
    "the mass spectrum of this improved action has been studied numerically in ref .",
    "@xcite , where it was concluded that data from the original wilson action@xcite at a lattice spacing of @xmath7fm can be reproduced with the improved action on lattices with @xmath8fm , representing a very substantial saving of computing resources .",
    "other studies of the mass spectrum with the same improved action have produced similar results@xcite .    in this paper",
    "it is shown that a comparable degree of improvement is also obtained in the computation of the vector meson decay constants , @xmath9 , @xmath10 and @xmath11 , and the renormalization factor , @xmath4 .",

    "in this paper we have calculated vector current matrix elements and the renormalization factor in the light quark sector for an improved action on lattices with spacings of about 0.3 to 0.4fm .",
    "this is the first step in going beyond the mass spectrum with improved actions on coarse lattices .",
    "the action used here is particularly simple .",
    "it has next - nearest - neighbour couplings to remove @xmath18 and @xmath3 errors at the classical level and also incorporates tadpole improvement .",
    "no special tuning of parameters and no field transformations are required with this action .",
    "vector meson decay constants and the vector current renormalization factor calculated with the improved action were found to be comparable to those with the wilson action on lattices with about 1/3 the spacing .",
    "this is the same as the improvement found previously in mass calculations and it represents a very substantial saving in computer resources .",
