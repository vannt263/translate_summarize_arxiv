    "<S> it is a well - known procedure for constructing a torus knot or link that first we prepare an unknotted torus and meridian disks in the complementary solid tori of it , and second smooth the intersections of the boundary of meridian disks uniformly . </S>",
    "<S> then we obtain a torus knot or link on the unknotted torus and its seifert surface made of meridian disks . in the present paper </S>",
    "<S> , we generalize this procedure by a closed fake surface and show that the resultant two surfaces obtained by smoothing triple points uniformly are essential . </S>",
    "<S> we also show that a knot obtained by this procedure satisfies the neuwirth conjecture , and the distance of two boundary slopes for the knot is equal to the number of triple points of the closed fake surface . </S>"
