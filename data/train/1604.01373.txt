    "<S> the free - space transfer of high - fidelity optical signals between remote locations has many applications , including both classical and quantum communication , precision navigation , clock synchronization , etc . </S>",
    "<S> the physical processes that contribute to signal fading and loss need to be carefully analyzed in the theory of light propagation through the atmospheric turbulence . here </S>",
    "<S> we derive the probability distribution for the atmospheric transmittance including beam wandering , beam shape deformation , and beam - broadening effects . </S>",
    "<S> our model , referred to as the elliptic - beam approximation , applies to weak , weak - to - moderate , and strong turbulence and hence to the most important regimes in atmospheric communication scenarios .    </S>",
    "<S> [ [ introduction . ] ] introduction. + + + + + + + + + + + + + +    the transmission of quantum light to remote receivers recently attracted great interest in connection with the implementation of quantum communication protocols over large distances . </S>",
    "<S> experimental advances in this field allowed one to demonstrate the successful quantum - light transmission over horizontal communication links  @xcite and paved the way for the realization of ground - to - satellite quantum communication  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the main obstacle for the transmission of quantum light in free space is the atmospheric turbulence , which leads to spatial and temporal variations of the refractive index of the channel . </S>",
    "<S> the transmitted signal is usually measured by detectors with a finite aperture . </S>",
    "<S> typically , the recorded data are contaminated by fluctuating losses due to beam wandering , beam broadening , scintillation , and degradation of coherence .    </S>",
    "<S> the theory of classical light propagation through the atmosphere is well developed  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> some progress was also achieved in the theory of free - space propagation of quantum light @xcite . </S>",
