    "the simplest model to describe light - matter interaction is the quantum rabi model , in which a two - level system ( two states of a single atom in the early applications ) interacts with a single mode of the radiation field @xcite .",
    "a seminal step in its analytical treatment has been taken by jaynes and cummings through the invention of the `` rotating - wave approximation '' ( rwa ) , valid close to resonance and for coupling strengths typical for atom optics @xcite .",
    "the ensuing model can be solved analytically in a very simple way , because the rwa introduces a strong continuous symmetry @xcite , rendering it superintegrable @xcite .",
    "a natural generalization of the rabi model is the dicke model , in which the radiation mode couples simultaneously to @xmath2 two - level systems ( qubits ) @xcite .",
    "it was first studied in the limit of large @xmath2 , because it exhibits a phase transition to a `` super - radiant '' state for strong coupling @xcite .",
    "although the transition can not be observed within atom optics @xcite , it should be realizable within circuit qed @xcite and its equivalent has been experimentally observed in a bose - einstein condensate coupled to an optical cavity @xcite .",
    "while these developments concern the dicke model for large @xmath2 , applications to quantum information technology have renewed the interest in the case of small @xmath2 @xcite .",
    "the model with three qubits allows in principle the dynamical generation of greenberger - horne - zeilinger ( ghz ) states @xcite which could be of importance for future applications e.g. in quantum cryptography @xcite .",
    "possible realizations of the @xmath0 dicke model within circuit qed will be able to explore the strong coupling region @xcite where the rwa is not feasible and one has to consider the full model .",
