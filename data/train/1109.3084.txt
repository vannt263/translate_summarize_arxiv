    "<S> we study the fibration of augmented link complements . given the diagram of an augmented link we associate a spanning surface and a graph . </S>",
    "<S> we then show that this surface is a fiber for the link complement if and only if the associated graph is a tree . </S>",
    "<S> we further show that fibration is preserved under dehn filling on certain components of these links . </S>",
    "<S> this last result is then used to prove that within a very large class of links , called locally alternating augmented links , every link is fibered . </S>"
