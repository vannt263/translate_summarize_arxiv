    "<S> in two previous publications  , we have demonstrated that stationary rotation of magnetized plasma about a compact central object permits an enormous number of different mhd instabilities , with the well - known magneto - rotational instability   as just one of them . </S>",
    "<S> we here concentrate on the new instabilities found that are driven by transonic transitions of the poloidal flow . a particularly promising class of instabilities , from the point of view of mhd turbulence in accretion disks , </S>",
    "<S> is the class of _ trans - slow alfvn continuum modes _ , that occur when the poloidal flow exceeds a critical value of the slow magnetosonic speed . </S>",
    "<S> when this happens , virtually every magnetic / flow surface of the disk becomes unstable with respect to highly localized modes of the continuous spectrum . </S>",
    "<S> the mode structures rotate , in turn , about the rotating disk . </S>",
    "<S> these structures lock and become explosively unstable when the mass of the central object is increased beyond a certain critical value . </S>",
    "<S> their growth rates then become huge , of the order of the alfvn transit time . </S>",
    "<S> these instabilities appear to have all requisite properties to facilitate accretion flows across magnetic surfaces and jet formation . </S>"
