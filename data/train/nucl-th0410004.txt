    "<S> extending the concepts of light - front field theory to quantum statistics provides a novel approach towards nuclear matter under extreme conditions . </S>",
    "<S> such conditions exist , e.g. , in neutron stars or in the early stage of our universe . </S>",
    "<S> they are experimentally expected to occur in heavy ion collisions , e.g. , at rhic and accelerators to be build at gsi and cern . </S>",
    "<S> light - front field theory is particularly suited , since it is based on a relativistic hamiltonian approach . </S>",
    "<S> it allows us to treat the perturbative as well as the nonperturbative regime of qcd and also correlations that emerge as a field of few - body physics and is important for hadronization . </S>",
    "<S> last but not least the hamiltonian approach is useful for nonequilibrium processes by utilizing , e.g. , the formalism of nonequilibrium statistical operators . </S>"
