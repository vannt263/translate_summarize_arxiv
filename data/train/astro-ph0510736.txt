    ", we used large silicate grains and/or carbonaceous dust .",

    "this spherical model in the optically - thin regime can nicely fit the sed ( as shown in fig .",
    "[ fig : dusty_silicates ] ) , but it significantly underestimates the mid - ir visibilities , even when the model parameters are varied considerably .",
    "this means that the measured dust - emitting region is too compact ( @xmath69 or @xmath70 ; see table  [ ta : models ] ) to be reproduced by a spherical model ( even though the sed fit is acceptable ) .",
    "only a disk - like structure seems to allow the dust to survive within @xmath71  au from the star ( see kraus & lamers 2003 ) .",
    "another argument against a spherical cse comes from polarization measurements of yudin & evans ( 1998 ) .",
    "after correction for the interstellar polarization , yudin ( private communication ) estimated an intrinsic polarization position angle @xmath72 .",
    "interestingly , within the error bars this angle is perpendicular to the major - axis pa we derived from the midi data ( @xmath73 ; see table  [ ta : models ] ) , as is expected from a disk - like dusty cse .",

    "<S> we present the first spectro - interferometric observations of the circumstellar envelope ( cse ) of a b[e ] supergiant ( cpd@xmath0 ) , performed with the very large telescope interferometer ( vlti ) using the beam - combiner instruments amber ( near - ir interferometry with three 8.3  m unit telescopes or uts ) and midi ( mid - ir interferometry with two uts ) . </S>",
    "<S> our observations of the cse are well fitted by an elliptical gaussian model with fwhm diameters varying linearly with wavelength . </S>",
    "<S> typical diameters measured are @xmath1  mas or @xmath2  au ( adopting a distance of @xmath3  kpc ) at @xmath4 , and @xmath5  mas or @xmath6  au at @xmath7 . we show that a spherical dust model reproduces the sed but it underestimates the midi visibilities , suggesting that a dense equatorial disk is required to account for the compact dust - emitting region observed . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , the derived major - axis position angle in the mid - ir ( @xmath8 ) agrees well with previous polarimetric data , hinting that the hot - dust emission originates in a disk - like structure . </S>",
    "<S> our results support the non - spherical cse paradigm for b[e ] supergiants . </S>"
