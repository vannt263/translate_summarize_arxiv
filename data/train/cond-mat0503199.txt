    "<S> the wormlike chain model of stiff polymers is a nonlinear @xmath0-model in one spacetime dimension in which the ends are fluctuating freely . </S>",
    "<S> this causes important differences with respect to the presently available theory which exists only for periodic and dirichlet boundary conditions . </S>",
    "<S> we modify this theory appropriately and show how to perform a systematic large - stiffness expansions for all physically interesting quantities in powers of @xmath1 , where @xmath2 is the length and @xmath3 the persistence length of the polymer . </S>",
    "<S> this requires special procedures for regularizing highly divergent feynman integrals which we have developed in previous work . </S>",
    "<S> we show that by adding to the unperturbed action a correction term @xmath4 , we can calculate all feynman diagrams with green functions satisfying neumann boundary conditions . </S>",
    "<S> our expansions yield , order by order , properly normalized end - to - end distribution function in arbitrary dimensions @xmath5 , its even and odd moments , and the two - point correlation function . </S>"
