    "the quantum entanglement problem is regarded as a central problem in quantum information @xcite , and the geometric measure is one of the most important measures of quantum entanglement @xcite .",
    "it was first proposed by shimony @xcite and generalized to multipartite systems by wei and goldbart @xcite , and has become one of the widely used entanglement measures for multiparticle cases @xcite .",
    "the geometric measure is based on the geometric distance between a given pure state and the set of separable pure states . from the definition ,",
    "the quantum eigenvalue problem is derived to characterize the nearest separable pure state with respect to this measure @xcite .",
    "this characterization is significant : * the eigenvalues are always real numbers and the largest one corresponds to the maximal overlap of the given pure state and the separable pure states*.    based on the convex roof construction , this geometric measure is extended to multipartite mixed states @xcite .",
    "although the extension is standard , analogue characterizations for disentangled mixed states are not clear @xcite . instead of the convex roof extension ,",
    "we propose in this paper a natural extension of the geometric measure from pure states to mixed states .",
    "most interestingly , a characterization for the nearest disentangled mixed state still holds .",
    "we show that there is a system of equations associated to the proposed geometric measure for mixed states .",
    "the entanglement eigenvalue for a mixed state is introduced and it is proven to be an indicator of the proposed geometric measure . moreover ,",
    "the disentangled mixed state corresponding to the entanglement eigenvalue is shown to be the nearest disentangled mixed state to the given mixed state with respect to this measure .",
