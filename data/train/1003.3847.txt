    "the kondo effect has been experimentally tested in several devices , ranging from atoms and molecules in contact with metallic reservoirs to quantum dots embedded in complex mesoscopic structures .",
    "@xcite this many body effect occurs when the ground state of the atom , molecule or quantum dot ( from hereon ` the impurity ' ) is degenerated typically due to spin degeneracy .",
    "while at high temperature the impurity behaves as a free spin , correlations build up as the temperature @xmath0 is lowered . as a consequence , below a characteristic temperature @xmath11 ,",
    "called the kondo temperature , the impurity s spin is screened by the spins of the electrons in the host structure . as a result",
    ", the fermi liquid picture is recovered as @xmath12 . for a system in equilibrium ,",
    "a characteristic feature of the kondo regime is the emergence of a sharp resonance in the impurity spectral density at the fermi level.@xcite in the absence of external magnetic fields , @xmath11 is the only low energy scale and all the ( zero bias ) transport properties obey a universal scaling as a function of @xmath13 .",
    "this effect , which is theoretically well understood , has been reported in a variety of experiments .",
    "@xcite    external magnetic fields @xmath14 or bias voltages @xmath15 introduce new energy scales that could be of the order of @xmath11 . in these conditions , several recent works focused on the search of scaling laws to test whether the non - equilibrium kondo physics remains universal .",

    "most of the approaches consider the single impurity anderson model , in which a single correlated electronic state is connected to two leads .",
    "for this configuration , some theoretical considerations predict an universal scaling of @xmath1 in temperature and applied bias but disagree about the coefficients and parameters that determine the scaling function.@xcite other studies that employ non - equilibrium renormalized perturbation theory,@xcite need to include valence fluctuations in order to reproduce the simple scaling formula for @xmath1 .",
