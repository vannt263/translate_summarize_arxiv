    "<S> gapless electronic systems containing topologically nontrivial fermi points are sources of various topological insulators . </S>",
    "<S> whereas most of these special band - crossing points are built in the electronic structure of the non - interacting lattice models , we show that a quadratic fermi point characterized by a non - zero winding number emerges with a collinear triple-@xmath0 spin - density - wave state that arises from a perfectly nested but topologically trivial fermi surface . </S>",
    "<S> we obtain a universal low - energy hamiltonian for the quadratic fermi point and show that such collinear orderings are unstable against the onset of scalar spin chirality that opens a gap and induces a spontaneous quantum hall insulator as the temperature tends to zero .    </S>",
    "<S> fermi points with nontrivial berry connection have recently been the focus of intense theoretical and experimental effort . </S>",
    "<S> these singular points are the momentum - space counterpart of topological defects in ordered media ; both are characterized by a topological invariant  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> topological fermi points are usually robust against small perturbations which preserve certain discrete symmetries of the system . on the other hand , </S>",
    "<S> when a gap opens at a singular fermi point , its topological nature can be transferred to the resultant insulating state , giving rise to intriguing phases such as spontaneous quantum hall or quantum spin - hall states @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> the most famous fermi point is the dirac point . </S>",
    "<S> the fermi `` surface '' of a half - filled honeycomb lattice , like graphene , consists of a pair of these points . </S>",
    "<S> each dirac point is similar to a vortex in the brillouin zone ( bz ) and is characterized by a topological winding number  @xmath1  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> haldane showed that the inclusion of complex hoppings in a honeycomb lattice opens up a gap at both dirac points , giving rise to a quantum hall state in absence of external magnetic field  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> similar ideas for realizing quantum hall insulators through gapped dirac points have been explored in double - exchange models on square , kagome , and checkerboard lattices  @xcite . by creating and manipulating dirac points with the aid of artificial gauge fields , it is also possible to control topological phase transitions in optical lattices  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> quantum hall effect can also be induced by opening a gap at a topological fermi point with a quadratic band dispersion  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> these points are vortices carrying multiple topological charges @xmath2 in momentum space  @xcite . because of its higher winding numbers </S>",
    "<S> , a quadratic fermi point can decay into several elementary dirac points while preserving the total topological charge  @xcite . unlike dirac points where interaction effects are suppressed due to a vanishing density of states ( dos ) , </S>",
    "<S> quadratic fermi points are unstable against arbitrarily weak short - range interactions due to their finite dos  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> these fermi points are usually protected in lattice models by @xmath3 or @xmath4 point group symmetries . </S>",
