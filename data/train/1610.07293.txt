    "<S> generating and detecting a prescribed single - electron state is an important step towards solid - state fermion optics . </S>",
    "<S> we propose how to generate an electron in a gaussian state , using a quantum - dot pump with gigahertz operation and realistic parameters . with the help of a strong magnetic field , the electron occupies a coherent state in the pump , insensitive to the details of nonadiabatic evolution . </S>",
    "<S> the state changes during the emission from the pump , governed by competition between the landauer - buttiker traversal time and the passage time . </S>",
    "<S> when the former is much shorter than the latter , the emitted state is a gaussian wave packet . </S>",
    "<S> the gaussian packet can be identified by using a dynamical potential barrier , with a resolution reaching the heisenberg minimal uncertainty @xmath0 .    on - demand </S>",
    "<S> single - electron sources have been developed , opening a road towards a fermion version of quantum optics and related quantum processing  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> they are formed in a two - dimensional electron gas ( 2deg ) , and can be classified according to emission energy . </S>",
    "<S> a mesoscopic capacitor  @xcite and a leviton pump  @xcite emit electrons at fermi energy , constituting one class . </S>",
    "<S> significant steps , including the generation of a prescribed state  @xcite , hanbury brown - twiss effects  @xcite , and single - electron quantum state tomography  @xcite , have been experimentally and theoretically done for this class  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> a quantum - dot ( qd ) pump  @xcite belongs to another class . </S>",
    "<S> it emits hot electrons of @xmath1 mev above the fermi energy and has been studied for metrology  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> its applications to fermion optics are complementary to those of the former class , and plausible as scattering of the hot electrons by phonons and other electrons rarely occurs  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> controllability of its emission energy is an important merit absent in the first class . this direction to fermion optics has been considered only recently  @xcite .    for various purposes of the direction , it is crucial to realize a qd pump emitting a prescribed electron wave packet of a useful form . </S>",
    "<S> how to pump such states is a nontrivial question that requires an understanding of the latter half ( emission process ) of one pump cycle . </S>",
    "<S> this process has been little considered theoretically , while the first half ( capturing ) was analyzed  @xcite for pump accuracy . </S>",
    "<S> state evolution in the emission involves complications from nonadiabatic operations and tunneling through a qd barrier ; how the shape of a wave packet changes by tunneling is a basic question that has not been addressed . </S>",
    "<S> as we show below , the change is governed by competition between landauer - buttiker traversal time  @xcite @xmath2 , a characteristic scale of tunneling , and passage time  @xcite @xmath3 for a packet to evolve into an orthogonal state . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , a detector for measuring an emitted packet is crucial for fermion optics . </S>",
    "<S> it has not been analyzed , although it was addressed  @xcite .    in this letter , we show , analytically and numerically with realistic parameters , that a single - electron gaussian packet can be emitted , without fine - tuning , from a qd pump under a strong magnetic field . </S>",
