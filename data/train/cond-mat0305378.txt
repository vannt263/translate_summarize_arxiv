    "<S> the effects of dispersed aerosil nanoparticles on two of the phase transitions of the thermotropic liquid crystal material 4-@xmath0-pentylphenylthiol-4-@xmath0-octyloxybenzoate ( @xmath1 ) have been studied using high - resolution x - ray diffraction techniques . </S>",
    "<S> the aerosils hydrogen bond together to form a gel which imposes a weak quenched disorder on the liquid crystal . </S>",
    "<S> the smectic - a fluctuations are well characterized by a two - component line shape representing thermal and random - field contributions . </S>",
    "<S> an elaboration on this line shape is required to describe the fluctuations in the smectic - c phase ; specifically the effect of the tilt on the wave - vector dependence of the thermal fluctuations must be explicitly taken into account . </S>",
    "<S> both the magnitude and the temperature dependence of the smectic - c tilt order parameter are observed to be unaffected by the disorder . </S>",
    "<S> this may be a consequence of the large bare smectic correlation length in the direction of modulation for this transition . </S>",
    "<S> these results show that the understanding developed for the nematic to smectic - a transition for octylcyanobiphenyl ( 8cb ) and octyloxycyanobiphenyl ( 8ocb ) liquid crystals with quenched disorder can be extended to quite different materials and transitions . </S>"
