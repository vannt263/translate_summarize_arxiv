    "<S> we demonstrate a two - qubit logic gate driven by near - field microwaves in a room - temperature microfabricated ion trap . </S>",
    "<S> we measure a gate fidelity of 99.7(1)% , which is above the minimum threshold required for fault - tolerant quantum computing . </S>",
    "<S> the gate is applied directly to `` atomic clock '' qubits ( coherence time @xmath0 ) using the microwave magnetic field gradient produced by a trap electrode . </S>",
    "<S> we introduce a dynamically - decoupled gate method , which stabilizes the qubits against fluctuating a.c .  </S>",

    "<S> laser - cooled trapped atomic ions are a promising platform for the development of a general - purpose quantum computer  @xcite . in common with other technologies </S>",
    "<S> , the present challenge is performing all elementary logic operations with the fidelity necessary for quantum error correction , whilst using techniques which can be scaled to the number of qubits required to perform a useful computation . </S>",
    "<S> trapped - ion qubits are based on either optical  @xcite or hyperfine  @xcite atomic transitions . </S>",
    "<S> hyperfine qubits lie in the convenient microwave domain , and have exhibited minute - long memory coherence times  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> nevertheless , they are usually manipulated via optical ( raman ) transitions , firstly because of the convenience of addressing individual ions with tightly - focussed laser beams  @xcite , and secondly because the short optical wavelength allows efficient multi - qubit logic gates based on coupling the ions spin and motional degrees of freedom  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> microwave methods have been proposed  @xcite , and recently demonstrated , both for individual qubit addressing  @xcite , and for multi - qubit logic gates  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this offers the prospect of performing all coherent operations using purely electronic methods , making phase control significantly easier , and replacing lasers with cheaper , smaller , more stable microwave devices . </S>",
    "<S> microwave elements can also be integrated into trapping structures more easily than their optical counterparts for improved scalability  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , microwave gates can theoretically attain higher fidelities as they are not fundamentally limited by photon scattering  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> two distinct microwave methods are being pursued : using far - field microwaves in combination with a local static magnetic field gradient ; and , using a local near - field microwave magnetic field gradient . </S>",
    "<S> microwave - driven two - qubit gates have previously been reported in a single experiment using the near - field method ( with 76% fidelity  @xcite ) , and in two experiments using the far - field method ( with 70% fidelity in a 3-ion chain and , very recently , 98.5% for a pair of ions  @xcite ) . </S>",
    "<S> beyond quantum information processing , microwave quantum logic techniques are also applicable to metrology and high - resolution spectroscopy , for example for the study of systems without accessible optical transitions  @xcite .    in this letter , we report a near - field microwave two - qubit gate with fidelity exceeding the @xmath1 minimum threshold required for fault - tolerant quantum error correction  @xcite , and comparable to that of the best reported fidelities achieved with lasers or other qubit technologies  @xcite . </S>",
