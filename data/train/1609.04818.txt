    "<S> we consider the dynamics of strongly localized systems subject to dephasing noise with arbitrary correlation time . </S>",
    "<S> although noise inevitably induces delocalization , transport in the noise - induced delocalized phase is subdiffusive in a parametrically large intermediate - time window . </S>",
    "<S> we argue for this intermediate - time subdiffusive regime both analytically and using numerical simulations on single - particle localized systems . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , we show that normal diffusion is restored in the long - time limit , through processes analogous to variable - range hopping . </S>",
    "<S> our qualitative conclusions are also valid for interacting systems in the many - body localized phase .    </S>",
    "<S> the effects of disorder on quantum transport and dynamics have been a topic of longstanding interest  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> both noninteracting  @xcite and interacting  @xcite systems of electrons in a random potential can get `` localized '' by the disorder , causing their d.c .  </S>",

    "<S> _ conclusions_*.we have studied noise - induced transport in strongly disordered quantum systems . </S>",
    "<S> we have argued that for slowly fluctuating noise , transport is governed by an incoherent hopping model with an _ emergent _ broad distribution of hopping rates , giving rise to anomalous diffusion on intermediate timescales , and regular diffusion ( albeit with a strongly suppressed diffusion constant ) at late times .    </S>",
    "<S> our approach paves the way for developing a self - consistent theory for the metallic phase in disordered and interacting quantum systems where interactions can be treated by a self - consistent hartree - fock decoupling . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , such a self - consistent theory can also be developed with the prospect of studying the response of a many - body localized system coupled to a bath . </S>",
