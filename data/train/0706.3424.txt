    "we derive the following galaxy parameters according to the approach of @xcite : the v - band dust attenuations , a@xmath69 in magnitudes , stellar metallicity z , the current star formation rate , sfr , averaged over the past 100 myr in m@xmath9 yr@xmath10 , the present - day stellar mass , m@xmath70 , of the galaxy in m@xmath9 , the fraction of stellar mass formed in bursts over the last 100 myr , f@xmath71 , and the @xcite b parameter , defined as the ratio of the current sfr to the past time - averaged sfr ( averaged over the estimated age , not hubble time ) .",
    "the galaxy parameters are derived from model libraries of galaxies at redshifts between 0.1 and 1.6 at redshift increments of 0.1 for the deimos sample , and at redshifts of 0.05,0.1,0.15,0.2 , and 0.25 for the ir1.1/dr2 sample .",
    "each library consists of up to @xmath410@xmath72 models .",
    "each model is parameterized according to galaxy age , optical depth , star formation history ( sfh ) , and metallicity .",
    "the sfh of each model is parameterized according to @xcite , with an underlying , continuous , exponentially declining sfr upon which bursts of star formation , random in time and amplitude , are superimposed .",
    "dust attenuation in each model is parameterized using the prescription of @xcite using an effective @xmath73-band optical depth @xmath74@xmath69 and absorption curve , @xmath75 resulting from both giant molecular clouds and the diffuse ism , with the fraction @xmath21 of @xmath74@xmath69 contributed only by the diffuse ism .",
    "the @xmath73-band optical depth from giant molecular clouds is taken to only affect stars younger than 10 myr .",
    "a description of the prior distributions of the model parameters is discussed in @xcite .",
    "spectral energy distributions ( seds ) are created for each galaxy in the library using the population synthesis code of @xcite .",
    "the model seds are convolved with the _ galex _ and sdss filter response curves .",
    "statistical estimates of physical galaxy parameters are derived by comparing the observed 7 band galex@xmath30sdss fluxes of each galaxy to all the convolved model seds in the nearest redshift library .",
    "probability density functions ( pdfs ) for each physical parameter are created by assigning weights to the parameters .",
    "the @xmath76 goodness of fit of the model determines the weight ( @xmath77 $ ] ) that is assigned to the parameters of that model . the median ( or most typical parameter value ) of the pdf",
    "is taken as the estimate of the galaxy parameter .",
