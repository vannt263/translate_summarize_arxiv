    "for hyperbolic systems of conservation laws in one space dimension , a satisfactory existence - uniqueness theory is currently available for entropy weak solutions with small total variation @xcite .",
    "the well - posedness of the cauchy problem holds also in the case of large data , as long as the total variation remains bounded @xcite .",
    "a major remaining open problem is whether , for large bv initial data , the total variation remains uniformly bounded or can blow up in finite time .",
    "examples of finite time blow up have been constructed in @xcite .",
    "however , these systems do not come from physical models and do not admit a strictly convex entropy .    for initial data having small total variation , regardless of the order in which wave fronts cross each other , the glimm interaction estimates @xcite",
    "show that the total strength of waves remains small for all times .",
    "there are few examples of hyperbolic systems where uniform bv estimates hold also for solutions with large data @xcite . in the present paper",

    "more precisely , we study the following question :    _ consider a front tracking approximation for ( [ 1 ] ) with large bv initial data .",
    "assume that , at each interaction time , the outgoing wave fronts have the same strength as in the exact solution of the riemann problem but some error is allowed in their speed .",
    "can the total variation of such approximate solution become arbitrarily large ? _    in this paper , some examples will be constructed , showing that the total strength of waves in a front tracking approximation can indeed approach infinity in finite time .",

    "<S> we consider front tracking approximate solutions to the p - system of isentropic gas dynamics . at interaction times , the outgoing wave fronts have the same strength as in the exact solution of the riemann problem , but some error is allowed in their speed . for large bv initial data , we construct examples showing that the total variation of these approximate solutions can become arbitrarily large , or even blow up in finite time . </S>",
    "<S> this happens even if the density of the gas remains uniformly positive . </S>"
