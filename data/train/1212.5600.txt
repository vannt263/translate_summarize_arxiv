    "the unique properties of graphene , the first truly two - dimensional material , have spurred a flood of research on fundamental physics and on technological possibilities .",
    "@xcite monolayer graphene ( mlg ) has a non - bravais honeycomb lattice structure with two triangular sublattices .",
    "the physical consequences of its linear @xmath2-band crossing at the fermi level , described at low energies by a chiral massless dirac @xmath3 hamiltonian , have been discussed at length .",
    "bilayer graphene ( blg ) consists of two bernal - stacked coupled graphene monolayers , resulting in four inequivalent sites and four corresponding @xmath2-bands .",
    "its carriers are chiral but massive and characterized at low energies by a gapless parabolic spectrum displaying sublattice pseudospin - momentum locking . in blg a band gap may be induced by a top gate @xcite or by dual gates which can vary the carrier density in the two layers independently .",
    "@xcite the theory of electronic transport in this unique and tunable @xmath2-band system has been investigated extensively .",
    "@xcite experimental studies have focused on transport , @xcite magnetotransport , @xcite and optics .",
    "@xcite recent efforts that have succeeded in manufacturing and manipulating quantum dots , @xcite have been motivated by potential applications in quantum computing .",
    "an extensive body of research has been devoted to electron - electron interactions in blg .",
    "@xcite interactions in all forms of graphene are expected to be strong when the two - dimensional material is surrounded by low-@xmath4 dielectrics .",
    "progress in studying interaction effects has been improved by achieving samples with higher mobility , @xcite by studying suspended blg samples that are not influenced by a substrate @xcite and by breakthroughs in fabricating samples with top and back gates .",
    "@xcite interactions are expected to become more important as one approaches the charge neutrality point .",
    "@xcite in equilibrium interactions in blg lead to competing ground states , @xcite including a host of exotic states .",
    "@xcite theoretical studies of electron - electron interactions in equilibrium blg@xcite have demonstrated , among other properties , that screening and friedel oscillations have different functional forms from mlg and 2degs .",
    "@xcite    the role of electron - electron interactions out of equilibrium has not yet received attention . given the wealth of research on transport , it is timely to address the influence of electron - electron interactions on the charge conductivity of blg .",
    "as in single - layer graphene , charge currents in blg lead to a net pseudospin polarization .",
    "interactions are therefore expected to renormalize the charge current and with it the pseudospin polarization .",
    "the question naturally arises of whether this polarization may be enhanced by interactions and produce observable effects .",
    "it is important to understand whether the effect on the conductivity of non - equilibrium contributions to the interaction self - energy can be substantial , and whether it can be controlled using various tuning parameters such as the carrier density @xmath5 .",
    "this paper is therefore concerned with the effect of electron - electron interactions in bilayer graphene transport in the metallic regime @xmath6 .",
    "we begin with the quantum liouville equation for the density matrix , working in the first born approximation with respect to momentum scattering .",
    "electron - electron interactions are taken into account self - consistently using the non - equilibrium hartree - fock approximation , with screening treated in the random phase approximation .",
    "we determine an exact expression for the conductivity in the presence of interactions within our framework .",
    "this work is distinct from recent papers discussing other interactions in blg transport .",

    "<S> conducting steady - states of doped bilayer graphene have a non - zero sublattice pseudospin polarization . </S>",
    "<S> electron - electron interactions renormalize this polarization even at zero temperature , when the phase space for electron - electron scattering vanishes . </S>",
    "<S> we show that because of the strength of interlayer tunneling , electron - electron interactions nevertheless have a negligible influence on the conductivity which vanishes as the carrier number density goes to zero . </S>",
    "<S> the influence of interactions is qualitatively weaker than in the comparable cases of single - layer graphene or topological insulators , because the momentum - space layer pseudo spin vorticity is @xmath0 rather than @xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> our study relies on the quantum liouville equation in the first born approximation with respect to the scattering potential , with electron - electron interactions taken into account self - consistently in the hartree - fock approximation and screening in the random phase approximation . within this framework </S>",
    "<S> the result we obtain is exact . </S>"
