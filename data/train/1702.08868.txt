    "graphene is an allotrope of carbon in the form of an atomic monolayer , arranged as a honeycomb lattice with @xmath0 symmetry .",
    "it is then mathematically described as a superposition of two bravais lattices with @xmath1 symmetry , usually denoted as a and b sub lattices , respectively @xcite . as a consequence tight - binding , as well as ab - initio band structure calculations , show that the energy spectrum possesses linear dispersion in the vicinity of two non - equivalent , so called dirac points ( or valleys ) in reciprocal space @xcite .",
    "this particular feature allows for the description of graphene electronic properties in terms of an effective dirac hamiltonian , whose eigenstates are given by two - component spinors , where a pseudo - spin property emerges as a consequence of the two sub - lattice structure @xcite . those states exhibit pseudo - relativistic properties , such as the emergence of relativistic landau levels in the presence of an external magnetic field @xcite . even in the presence of a physical magnetic field ,",
    "the two dirac points are connected by time - reversal symmetry , so the two valleys are degenerate @xcite .",
    "perhaps an even more interesting feature arises under the presence of mechanical strain . within the effective dirac theory",
    ", strain constitutes a gauge field whose curl represents a pseudo - magnetic field that reverses sign at each dirac point , thus breaking the valley symmetry @xcite .",
    "conductance experiments have shown the emergence of pseudo - relativistic landau levels in the presence of strain solely , thus suggesting that the magnitude of the associated pseudo - magnetic fields can reach over 100 tesla @xcite . from the theoretical perspective",
    ", strain - induced gauge fields have been incorporated into extended dirac hamiltonians that involve the simultaneous description of both non - equivalent dirac cones @xcite .",
    "other physical effects , such as charge density waves , can also be included in the form of generalized su(2 ) gauge fields@xcite .",
    "arbitrary strain patterns generate inhomogeneous pseudo - magnetic fields , and in an experimental sample it is difficult to characterize with manometric resolution the precise geometry of a strain pattern in order to correlate it with the magnitude of the corresponding pseudo - magnetic field . on the other hand ,",
    "electronic conductance is relatively straightforward to measure , and here we show that it can be directly correlated with the magnitude of the strain field imposed , thus providing a proof - of - principle for the development of a piezoelectric sensor with manometric resolution . in what follows , we shall present a theoretical model to represent elastic scattering of conduction electrons in graphene through a disk - shaped region submitted to mechanical strain and an external magnetic field normal to the plane , as depicted in fig .",

    "<S> we consider the scattering of dirac particles in graphene due to the superposition of an external magnetic field and mechanical strain . </S>",
    "<S> since graphene can be described by a dirac hamiltonian in the low energy limit , we find exact analytical solutions for single - particle states inside and outside the region submitted to the fields . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we obtain analytical expressions for the scattering cross - section , as well as for the landauer current through the region . </S>",
    "<S> our results suggest that measurements of the electric conductance , combined with a controlled magnetic field , may be used for the construction of a piezoelectric sensor based on this principle . </S>"
