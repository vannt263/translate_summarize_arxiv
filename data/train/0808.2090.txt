    "<S> we explore large - scale hydrodynamics of h slowromancap2@ regions for various self - similar shock flows of a polytropic gas cloud under self - gravity and with quasi - spherical symmetry . </S>",
    "<S> we formulate cloud dynamics by invoking specific entropy conservation along streamlines and obtain global self - similar  champagne flows \" for a conventional polytropic gas with shocks as a subclass . </S>",
    "<S> molecular cloud cores are ionized and heated to high temperatures after the onset of nuclear burning of a central protostar . </S>",
    "<S> we model subsequent evolutionary processes in several ways and construct possible self - similar shock flow solutions . </S>",
    "<S> we may neglect the mass and gravity of the central protostar . </S>",
    "<S> the ionization and heating of the surrounding medium drive outflows in the inner cloud core and a shock travels outwards , leading to the so - called  champagne phase \" with an expanding outer cloud envelope . </S>",
    "<S> complementarily , we also consider the expansion of a central cavity around the centre . as </S>",
    "<S> the inner cloud expands plausibly due to powerful stellar winds , a cavity ( i.e. , ` void ' or ` bubble ' ) can be created around the centre , and when the cavity becomes sufficiently large , one may neglect the gravity of the central protostar . </S>",
    "<S> we thus present self - similar shock solutions for  champagne flows \" with an expanding central void . </S>",
    "<S> we compare our solutions with isothermal solutions and find that the generalization to the polytropic regime brings about significant differences of the gas dynamics , especially for cases of @xmath0 , where @xmath1 is a key scaling index in the self - similar transformation . </S>",
    "<S> we also compare our global polytropic self - similar solutions with numerical simulations on the expansion of h slowromancap2@ regions . </S>",
    "<S> we further explore other possible dynamic evolutions of h slowromancap2@ regions after the initiation of nuclear burning of the central protostar , for example asymptotic inflows or contractions far from the cloud centre and the ongoing infall around a central protostar . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , it is possible to use the downstream free - fall solution with shocks to describe the dynamic evolution of h slowromancap2@ regions shortly after the nascence of the central protostar . </S>",
    "<S> we also give an analysis on the invariant form of self - similar polytropic flows by ignoring self - gravity .    </S>",
