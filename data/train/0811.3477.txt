    "<S> we introduce estimation and test procedures through divergence minimization for models satisfying linear constraints with unknown parameter . </S>",
    "<S> several statistical examples and motivations are given . </S>",
    "<S> these procedures extend the empirical likelihood ( el ) method and share common features with generalized empirical likelihood ( gel ) . </S>",
    "<S> we treat the problems of existence and characterization of the divergence projections of probability measures on sets of signed finite measures . </S>",
    "<S> our approach allows for a study of the estimates under misspecification . </S>",
    "<S> the asymptotic behavior of the proposed estimates are studied using the dual representation of the divergences and the explicit forms of the divergence projections . </S>",
    "<S> we discuss the problem of the choice of the divergence under various respects . </S>",
    "<S> also we handle efficiency and robustness properties of minimum divergence estimates . </S>",
    "<S> a simulation study shows that the hellinger divergence enjoys good efficiency and robustness properties . </S>",
    "<S> + key words : empirical likelihood ; generalized empirical likelihood ; minimum divergence ; efficiency ; robustness ; duality ; divergence projection . </S>"
