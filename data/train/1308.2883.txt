    "<S> we consider interacting particle systems and their mean - field limits , which are frequently used to model collective aggregation and are known to demonstrate a rich variety of pattern formations . </S>",
    "<S> the interaction is based on a pairwise potential combining short - range repulsion and long - range attraction . </S>",
    "<S> we study particular solutions , that are referred to as flocks in the second - order models , for the specific choice of the quasi - morse interaction potential . </S>",
    "<S> our main result is a rigorous analysis of continuous , compactly supported flock profiles for the biologically relevant parameter regime . existence and uniqueness are proven for three space dimensions , whilst existence is shown for the two - dimensional case . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , we numerically investigate additional morse - like interactions to complete the understanding of this class of potentials . </S>"
