    "in this article , we discuss how the moir superlattice in graphene ( g ) heterostructures with hexagonal boron nitride ( _ h_bn ) would be reflected in angle - resolved photoemission spectroscopy ( arpes ) measurements .",
    "arpes @xcite is a powerful method of exploring the electronic band structure of solids , in particular two - dimensional materials . @xcite it was used to probe electronic states in graphene , @xcite a honeycomb layer of carbon and the first of atomically thin two - dimensional atomic crystals .",
    "@xcite the high resolution state - of - the - art arpes enables one to observe the modifications of the electronic dispersion in graphene due to the underlying substrate @xcite or in graphene grown on metal surfaces , @xcite as well as to distinguish between bernal stacking and twisted arrangement of layers in bilayer graphene .",
    "@xcite    in g/_h_bn van der waals heterostructures , a difference @xmath0 @xcite between the lattice constants of the two crystals and a misalignment angle @xmath1 between their crystalline axes produce a quasi - periodic structure , @xcite known as moir pattern with the principal period @xmath2 , where @xmath3 is the lattice constant of graphene .",
    "the moir perturbation leads to the formation of minibands in the graphene electronic spectrum , revealed by stm spectra , @xcite capacitance spectroscopy @xcite and transport measurements .",
    "@xcite here , we show how arpes can help in characterising the specific details of the moir superlattice affecting electrons in graphene , specifically , in the perfectly oriented heterostructures ( @xmath4 ) which can be grown using cvd @xcite or mbe @xcite deposition of graphene on _ h_bn .",
    "we also analyze how the visibility of the distinctive miniband features is affected by the inelastic broadening of holes , which is substantial for the valence band energies where the moir miniband structure would be most sensitive to the moire superlattice details and find that second order energy derivative of arpes signal allows to recover the characteristic features of the arpes maps .",
