    "<S> this result suggests that the @xmath2-@xmath3 hybridization , as well as the heavy fermions , exists even in the afm phase of cein@xmath0 .    </S>",
    "<S> recently , physics at the quantum critical point ( qcp ) , which is the border between local magnetism and itinerant paramagnetism at zero temperature , has become one of the main topics in the condensed - matter field because new quantum properties such as non - bcs superconductivity appear in the vicinity of the qcp . </S>",
    "<S> the ground state of rare - earth intermetallic compounds , namely , heavy - fermion ( hf ) materials , changes between the local magnetic and itinerant nonmagnetic states through external perturbation by such factors as pressure and magnetic field  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the qcp appears owing to the energy balance between the local magnetic state based on the ruderman - kittel - kasuya - yoshida ( rkky ) interaction and the itinerant hf state due to the kondo effect . in the itinerant hf regime , the conduction band hybridizes with the nearly local @xmath1 state , so that a large fermi surface as well as the hybridization band between them , namely , the @xmath2-@xmath3 hybridization band , is realized . in the case of a magnetic regime , on the other hand , </S>",
    "<S> two theoretical scenarios have been proposed . </S>",
    "<S> one is the spin - density wave ( sdw ) scenario based on spin fluctuation , in which large fermi surfaces due to @xmath2-@xmath3 hybridization remain even in magnetically ordered states . </S>",
    "<S> the other is the kondo breakdown ( kbd ) scenario , in which the @xmath2-@xmath3 hybridization state disappears in the magnetic state and only small fermi surfaces due to conduction electrons appear  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> many controversies for these scenarios have been performed so far , but the conclusion has not been obtained yet .    </S>",
    "<S> experimental studies have supported both scenarios as follows . </S>",
    "<S> a neutron scattering experiment on ce(ru@xmath4rh@xmath5)@xmath6si@xmath6 has found itinerant antiferromagnetic ( afm ) behavior at the qcp , supporting the sdw scenario  @xcite . on the other hand , </S>",
    "<S> hall effect measurements of ybrh@xmath6si@xmath6 have shown a marked increase in the carrier density at the qcp , supporting the kbd scenario  @xcite , while pressure - dependent de haas - van alphen ( dhva ) effect measurements of cerhin@xmath7 and cein@xmath0 have also indicated a discontinuous change in the fermi surface across the critical pressure  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the drastic fermi surface reconstruction seems to be explained by the kbd scenario . </S>",
    "<S> recent theoretical works , however , have shown that the valence transition is the main origin of these discontinuous changes of dhva  @xcite . to clarify the change in the electronic structure across the critical pressure , it is therefore necessary to investigate the electronic structure at low temperatures as a function of @xmath2-@xmath3 hybridization intensity .    </S>",
