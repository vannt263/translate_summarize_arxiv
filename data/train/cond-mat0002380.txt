    "the transport properties of a two - dimensional electron gas ( 2deg ) subjected to a nonhomogeneous perpendicular magnetic field ( periodically modulated or not ) have been the focus of a great deal of research in recent years .",
    "@xcite  current fabrication technologies permit to create nonhomogeneous magnetic fields on a nanometer scale by deliberately shaping or curving the 2deg,@xcite or by integration of superconducting@xcite or ferromagnetic materials@xcite on top of the 2deg .",
    "this will add a new functional dimension to the present semiconductor technology and will open avenues for new physics and possible applications.@xcite    theoretically , the effect of nonhomogeneous magnetic fields on a 2deg have been studied both in the ballistic and the diffusive regime .",
    "the resulting perpendicular magnetic field can act as a scattering centre,@xcite but can also bind electrons,@xcite and so influence the transport properties of the 2deg . in transport calculations one needs the electron states , which are obtained by solving the schrdinger equation .",
    "mller@xcite studied theoretically the single particle electron states of a 2deg in a wide quantum waveguide under the application of a nonuniform magnetic field and showed that in the case of a magnetic field modulation in one direction , transport properties also become one dimensional and electron states propagate perpendicularly to the field gradient.@xmath0    making use of this decoupling , the electron states for different nonhomogeneous magnetic field profiles along one dimension were investigated , i.e. for a periodically modulated magnetic field @xcite , for magnetic quantum steps , barriers and wells in an infinite 2deg @xcite and in a narrow waveguide.@xcite    in this paper we consider an infinite 2deg subjected to a step - like magnetic field , i.e abruptly changing in magnitude or polarity at @xmath1 , in one dimension ( taken to be the @xmath2-direction ) .",

    "<S> we study the electron motion near magnetic field steps at which the strength and/or sign of the magnetic field changes . </S>",
    "<S> the energy spectrum for such systems is found and the electron states ( bound and scattered ) are compared with their corresponding classical paths . </S>",
    "<S> several classical properties as the velocity parallel to the edge , the oscillation frequency perpendicular to the edge and the extent of the states are compared with their quantum mechanical counterpart . </S>",
    "<S> a class of magnetic edge states is found which do not have a classical counterpart . </S>"
