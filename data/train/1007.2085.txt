    "the study of speckle patterns , irregular interference structures produced by light propagating through an inhomogeneous medium , is of fundamental importance in the understanding of coherent wave propagation in disordered media @xcite . while , at first sight , a speckle pattern looks perfectly random , closer analysis reveals a more complex picture . in the backscattering direction , for instance",
    ", the light intensity is enhanced , due to the constructive interference of pairs of counter - propagating multiple scattering paths .",
    "this is known as the coherent backscattering effect @xcite . in transmission ,",
    "speckle patterns exhibit long- and infinite - range correlations between distant spots .",
    "the latter are , in particular , at the origin of universal conductance fluctuations in electron transmission across mesoscopic conductors @xcite . once again",
    ", these highly nontrivial correlations originate from the interference between pairs of scattering paths which persists under disorder average , and have been studied extensively during the last decades @xcite .    while our knowledge of the statistics of _ classical _ waves in disordered media is today fairly well developed , little",
    "is known about the statistical properties of _ nonclassical _ light propagating in such media .",
    "recently , an important step forward was made with the investigation of the interplay between quantum fluctuations of light , on the one hand , and the classical fluctuations induced by a scattering medium with static disorder @xcite or moving scatterers @xcite , on the other hand . in this scenario ,",
    "a given quantum state of light enters a disordered medium , and signatures of the quantum statistics are found in the photon number variance analyzed upon transmission .",
    "later , the probability distribution of the coincidence counting rate , which defines a  two - photon speckle \" , was analyzed on output of a disordered medium @xcite .",
    "two - photon speckles are relevant for studying entangled states of light , and were recently observed experimentally @xcite , but interferences between different scattering paths were assumed to vanish under disorder average .    interference phenomena with nonclassical light in disordered media have so far only been addressed in the framework of one - dimensional tight - binding models @xcite or in the transport of quantum states with independent photons @xcite . in our present contribution , we consider the propagation of a pair of _ spectrally entangled photons _ in an open , disordered medium with three - dimensional disorder .",
