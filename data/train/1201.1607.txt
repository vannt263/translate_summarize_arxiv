    "recent experimental progress of ultracold electric dipolar heteronuclear molecules has become a major focus of ultracold atom physics @xcite .",
    "electric dipole moments are essentially classic polarization vectors induced by the external electric field .",
    "when they are aligned along the @xmath5 axis , the electric dipolar interaction becomes anisotropic exhibiting the @xmath6-type anisotropy . in fermi systems , this anisotropy has important effects on many - body physics including both single - particle and collective properties @xcite .",
    "fermi surfaces of polarized electric dipolar fermions exhibit quadrupolar distortion elongated along the @xmath5 axis @xcite .",
    "various fermi surface instabilities have been investigated including the pomeranchuk type nematic distortions @xcite and stripelike orderings @xcite .",

    "<S> we investigate fermi liquid states of the ultra - cold magnetic dipolar fermi gases in the simplest two - component case including both thermodynamic instabilities and collective excitations . </S>",
    "<S> the magnetic dipolar interaction is invariant under the simultaneous spin - orbit rotation , but not under either the spin or the orbit one . </S>",
    "<S> therefore , the corresponding fermi liquid theory is intrinsically spin - orbit coupled . </S>",
    "<S> this is a fundamental feature of magnetic dipolar fermi gases different from electric dipolar ones . </S>",
    "<S> the landau interaction matrix is calculated and is diagonalized in terms of the spin - orbit coupled partial - wave channels of the total angular momentum @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> the leading thermodynamic instabilities lie in the channels of ferromagnetism hybridized with the ferronematic order with @xmath1 and the spin - current mode with @xmath2 , where @xmath3 and @xmath4 represent even and odd parities , respectively . </S>",
    "<S> an exotic propagating collective mode is identified as spin - orbit coupled fermi surface oscillations in which spin distribution on the fermi surface exhibits a topologically nontrivial hedgehog configuration . </S>"
