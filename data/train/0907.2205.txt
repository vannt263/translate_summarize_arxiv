    "traversable lorentzian wormholes @xcite are solutions of the equations of gravitation representing geometries which have a throat that connects two regions of the same universe or two separate universes @xcite . for static wormholes ,",
    "the throat is a minimal area surface satisfying a flare - out condition @xcite . in the context of the general relativity theory of gravitation",
    ", wormholes must be threaded by exotic matter that violates the null energy condition @xcite .",
    "the amount of exotic matter needed around the throat can be made arbitrarily small by an appropriate choice of the geometry of the wormhole @xcite , although it may be at the expense of large stresses at the throat @xcite . +",
    "thin  shell wormholes are mathematically constructed by cutting and pasting two manifolds @xcite to form a new one , which is geodesically complete and it has a shell placed in the joining surface . the exotic matter required for their existence can be located at the shell , with normal matter outside this surface .",

    "in this paper , spherically symmetric thin  shell wormholes supported by a generalized chaplygin gas were theoretically constructed by using the usual cut and paste procedure for a general class of metrics .",
    "such kind of fluid has received great attention in cosmology in the last few years , because it provides a possible explanation for the accelerated expansion of the universe , and it has also been considered in previous wormhole studies . for a general class of metrics ,",
    "the equation that determines the possible radii of the throat for static wormholes was obtained and the stability of the static configurations under radial perturbations was analyzed using the standard potential method .",
    "the energy density and the pressure at the throat were obtained as functions of the throat radius .",
    "examples of wormholes made from reissner  nordstrm and schwarzschild with a cosmological constant metrics were analyzed in detail and the results were compared with those obtained in a previous work @xcite for the original chaplygin gas .",
    "it was found that for properly chosen values of the parameters , stable solutions are also possible when the gas exponent @xmath106 is smaller than @xmath111 .",
    "the main difference when @xmath108 is the presence of an extra unstable solution which appears for some values of the parameters in both charged and anti  de sitter wormholes . in the charged case it happens when the charge @xmath110 is slightly smaller than the mass @xmath91 , while in the anti ",
