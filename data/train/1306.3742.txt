    "<S> the merger of binary neutron stars ( nss ) is among the most promising gravitational wave ( gw ) sources . </S>",
    "<S> next - generation gw detectors are expected to detect signals from the ns merger within 200 mpc . </S>",
    "<S> detection of electromagnetic wave ( em ) counterpart is crucial to understand the nature of gw sources . among possible em emission from the ns merger , emission powered by radioactive r - process nuclei </S>",
    "<S> is one of the best targets for follow - up observations . </S>",
    "<S> however , prediction so far does not take into account detailed r - process element abundances in the ejecta . </S>",
    "<S> we perform radiative transfer simulations for the ns merger ejecta including all the r - process elements from ga to u for the first time . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the opacity in the ns merger ejecta is about @xmath0 , which is higher than that of fe - rich type ia supernova ejecta by a factor of @xmath1 100 . as a result , </S>",
    "<S> the emission is fainter and longer than previously expected . </S>",
    "<S> the spectra are almost featureless due to the high expansion velocity and bound - bound transitions of many different r - process elements . </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate that the emission is brighter for a higher mass ratio of two nss and a softer equation of states adopted in the merger simulations . because of the red color of the emission , follow - up observations in red optical and near - infrared ( nir ) wavelengths will be the most efficient . at 200 mpc , </S>",
    "<S> expected brightness of the emission is @xmath222 - 25 ab mag , @xmath321 - 23 ab mag , and 21 - 24 ab mag in nir @xmath4 bands . </S>",
    "<S> thus , observations with wide - field 4m- and 8m - class optical telescopes and wide - field nir space telescopes are necessary . </S>",
    "<S> we also argue that the emission powered by radioactive energy can be detected in the afterglow of nearby short gamma - ray bursts . </S>"
