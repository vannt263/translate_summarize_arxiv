    "<S> a complete degradability analysis of one - mode gaussian bosonic channels is presented . </S>",
    "<S> we show that apart from the class of channels which are unitarily equivalent to the channels with additive classical noise , these maps can be characterized in terms of weak- and/or anti - degradability . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore a new set of channels which have null quantum capacity is identified . </S>",
    "<S> this is done by exploiting the composition rules of one - mode gaussian maps and the fact that anti - degradable channels can not be used to transfer quantum information .    within the context of quantum information theory  @xcite bosonic gaussian channels  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite play a fundamental role . </S>",
    "<S> they include all the physical transformations which preserve `` gaussian character '' of the transmitted signals and can be seen are the quantum counterpart of the gaussian channels in the classical information theory  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> bosonic gaussian channels describe most of the noise sources which are routinely encountered in optics , including those responsible for the attenuation and/or the amplification of signals along optical fibers . moreover , due to their relatively simple structure </S>",
    "<S> , these channels provide an ideal theoretical playground for the study of continuous variable  @xcite quantum communication protocols .    </S>",
    "<S> not surprisingly in the recent years an impressive effort has been put forward to characterize the properties of bosonic gaussian channels . </S>",
    "<S> most of the efforts focused on the evaluation of the optimal transmission rates of these maps under the constraint on the input average energy both in the multi - mode scenario ( where the channel acts on a collection of many input bosonic mode ) and in the one - mode scenario ( where , instead , it operates on a single input bosonic mode ) . in few cases  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite the exact values of the communication capacities  @xcite of the channels have been computed . in the general case </S>",
    "<S> however only certain bounds are available ( see  @xcite ) . </S>",
    "<S> finally various additivity issues has been analyzed in refs .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> recently the notions of anti - degradability and weak - degradability were proposed as an useful tool for studying the quantum capacity properties of one - mode gaussian channels  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this suggested the possibility of classifying these maps in terms of a simple canonical form which was achieved in ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , proceeding along similar lines , the exact solution of the quantum capacity of an important subset of those channels was obtained in ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> in this paper we provide a complete degradability classification of one - mode gaussian channels and exhibit a new set of channels which have null quantum capacity extending a previous result in ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> the definition of weak- and anti - degradability of a quantum channel is similar to the definition of degradability introduced by devetak and shor in ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> it is based on replacing the stinespring dilation  @xcite of the channel with a representation where the ancillary system ( environment ) is not necessarily in a _ pure </S>",
    "<S> _ state  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this yields a generalization of the notion of _ complementary _ channel from ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite which is named _ weakly complementary _  @xcite . in this context </S>",
    "<S> weakly degradable are those channels where the modified state of the ancillary system  described by the action of the weakly complementary channel  can be recovered from the output state of the channel through the action of a third channel . </S>",
    "<S> vice - versa anti - degradable channels obey the opposite rule ( i.e. the output state of the channel can be obtained from the modified state of the ancilla through the action of another suitable channel ) . </S>",
    "<S> exploiting the canonical form  @xcite one can show that , apart from the class @xmath0 consisting of the maps which are unitarily equivalent to the channels with additive classical gaussian noise  @xcite , all one - mode bosonic gaussian channels are either weakly degradable or anti - degradable . as discussed in ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite the anti - degradability property allows one to simplify the analysis of the quantum capacity  @xcite of these channels . </S>",
    "<S> indeed those maps which are anti - degradable can be shown to have null quantum capacity . </S>",
    "<S> on the other hand , those channels which are weakly degradable with pure ancillas ( i.e. those which are degradable in the sense of ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite ) have quantum capacity which can be expressed in terms of a single - letter expression . here </S>",
    "<S> we will focus mostly on the anti - degradability property , and , additionally , we will show that by exploiting the composition rules of one - mode bosonic gaussian channels , one can extend the set the maps with null quantum capacity well beyond the set of anti - degradable maps .    </S>",
