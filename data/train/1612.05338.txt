    "the rapid development of nanotechnology has opened the possibility to realize quantum information tasks at an atomic scale in experiment  @xcite . on the other hand , due to the unavoidable coupling with the surrounding bath",
    ", the microscopic quantum device severely undergoes decoherence which is the main difficulty in fulfilling reliable quantum computation and quantum communication tasks .",
    "the dissipation - induced decoherence in a quantum microscopic system can be effectively modeled by a spin - boson model  @xcite which describes the interaction between a quantum system and a bosonic bath .",
    "the spin - boson model has attracted considerable attention in past decades because it provides a universal model for numerous physical and chemical processes .",
    "the reduced system dynamics of spin - boson model has been studied by various analytical and numerical methods , for example , the polaron  @xcite or generalized silbey - harris  @xcite transformation approach , the time - dependent numerical renormalization group method  @xcite , the quasi adiabatic propagator path integral ,  @xcite and hierarchical equations of motion ( heom )  @xcite .",
    "each method has its own regimes of validity depending on the system - bath coupling strength , the bath temperature and the bath spectral density function .",
    "heom is a set of time - local equations for the reduced system , which was originally proposed by tanimura and his co - workers  @xcite as a nonperturbative numerical method . in recent years",
    ", heom was successfully used to study the dynamics of chemical and biophysical systems , such as optical line shapes of molecular aggregates  @xcite and electron energy transfer dynamics in the fenna - matthews - olson complex  @xcite .",
    "thermal noise is one of the most familiar reasons for decoherence .",
    "it is a common recognition that the bosonic bath temperature always plays a negative role in preserving the coherence , i.e. , a higher bosonic bath temperature induces a more severe decoherence  @xcite .",
    "however , it was pointed out in refs .  @xcite that the bosonic bath temperature can enhance the coherence in a two - qubit spin - boson model , where a bare qubit interacts with the other qubit which is coupled to a thermal bosonic bath .",
    "nevertheless , their conclusions are based on the born - markov or weak coupling approximations .",
    "a very interesting question arises : does this interesting decoherence phenomenon ( decoherence reduced by increasing bath temperature ) still exist without any of these approximations ? to answer this question , in this paper , we reexamine the decoherence dynamics of this two - qubit spin - boson model by making use of a stochastic decoupling scheme along with heom  @xcite which is beyond the usual born - markov or weak - coupling approximations .",
    "it is found that the decoherence can be reduced by increasing the bath temperature in strong - coupling regimes ; for weak coupling , the bath temperature may enhance the decoherence .",
    "our study is the generalization of previous studies  @xcite and suggests a decoherence dynamical transition in this two - qubit spin - boson model .",

    "<S> we report an anomalous decoherence phenomenon of a quantum dissipative system in the framework of a stochastic decoupling scheme along with a hierarchical equations - of - motion formalism without the usual born - markov or weak coupling approximations . </S>",
    "<S> it is found that the decoherence of a two - qubit spin - boson model can be reduced by increasing the bath temperature in strong - coupling regimes . </S>",
    "<S> for the weak - coupling situation , we find that the bath temperature may enhance the decoherence . </S>",
    "<S> this result is contrary to the common recognition that a higher bath temperature always induces a more severe decoherence and suggests that a decoherence dynamical transition occurs in this two - qubit spin - boson model . </S>",
    "<S> we also demonstrate that the critical transition point can be characterized by the behavior of the frequency spectrum of the quantum coherence indicator . </S>"
