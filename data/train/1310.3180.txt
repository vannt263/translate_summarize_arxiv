    "<S> enzymes within biochemical pathways are often colocalized , yet the consequences of specific spatial enzyme arrangements remain poorly understood . </S>",
    "<S> we study the impact of enzyme arrangement on reaction efficiency within a reaction - diffusion model . </S>",
    "<S> the optimal arrangement transitions from a cluster to a distributed profile as a single parameter , which controls the probability of reaction versus diffusive loss of pathway intermediates , is varied . </S>",
    "<S> we introduce the concept of enzyme exposure to explain how this transition arises from the stochastic nature of molecular reactions and diffusion .            </S>",
    "<S> to efficiently catalyze multi - step biochemical reactions , sets of enzymes have evolved to function synergistically . </S>",
    "<S> cells not only keep concerted control over the concentrations and activities of enzymes in the same pathway , but often also arrange them in self - assembled multi - enzyme complexes @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> apart from the large molecular machines ( polymerases , ribosomes , spliceosomes ) , one of the best - studied natural multi - enzyme complexes is the cellulosome , a complex where up to 11 different enzymes are arranged on a non - catalytic scaffolding protein @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this complex is assembled extracellularly by anaerobic bacteria to efficiently break down cellulose , the most abundant organic material on the planet . </S>",
    "<S> similarly , enzyme complexes are used for intracellular metabolism @xcite . however , neither the precise consequences of putting enzymes together into complexes are well understood , nor the degree to which complex formation confers a functional advantage in each case @xcite .    it has long been thought that physical association between collaborating enzymes might increase the effective reaction flux , minimize the pool of unwanted intermediate products , allow coordinate regulation by a single effector , and reduce transient timescales @xcite . however , while enzymatic activity has been studied for over a century , suitable techniques to characterize such effects quantitatively have become available only recently . on the one hand , </S>",
    "<S> single - molecule enzymology allows to monitor @xcite and manipulate @xcite the activity of individual enzyme molecules . on the other hand , </S>",
    "<S> enzyme molecules can be positioned with nanometer precision in artificial systems using `` single - molecule cut - and - paste '' @xcite on 2d surfaces or along 1d channels , and with dna origami structures even in 3d @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> these experimental developments call for a theoretical analysis of the effects of spatial proximity and arrangement of enzymes , to uncover the principles for the design and optimization of multi - enzyme systems . </S>",
    "<S> such principles could be applied to bio - engineer systems that control biochemical reactions at will , such as for the production of drugs or biofuels @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> related issues also arise in the context of signaling proteins @xcite , however the functional criteria for the optimization of signaling systems are likely different @xcite .    here </S>",
    "<S> , we ask under which conditions it is beneficial to localize enzymes rather than to distribute them . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , what is the optimal arrangement and how does it depend on the system parameters ? </S>",
    "<S> we base this study on simple - reaction diffusion models , which permit rigorous quantitative analysis , and assume the steady - state reaction flux is the single critical system property . </S>",
