    "<S> we study the josephson effect in ballistic sinis and sifis double - barrier junctions , consisting of superconductors ( s ) , a clean normal ( n ) or ferromagnetic ( f ) metal , and insulating interfaces ( i ) . for short sinis double - tunnel junctions with one channel open for quasiparticle propagation , the critical josephson current as a function of the junction width shows sharp peaks because of a resonant amplification of the andreev process : when the quasibound states of the normal interlayer enter the superconducting gap the andreev bound states are lowered down to the fermi level . for corresponding sifis junctions the quasibound states are spin - split ; they amplify the supercurrent less efficiently , and trigger transitions between @xmath0 and @xmath1 states of the junction . </S>",
    "<S> in contrast to sinis junctions , a narrow dip related to the @xmath0  </S>",
    "<S> @xmath1 transition opens up exactly at the peak due to a compensation of partial currents flowing in opposite directions . with an increased barrier transparency </S>",
    "<S> these features are gradually lost , due to the broadening and overlap of quasibound states . </S>",
    "<S> temperature - induced transitions both from @xmath0 to @xmath1 and from @xmath1 to @xmath0 states are studied by computing the phase diagram ( with temperature and junction width as the variables ) for different interfacial transparencies varying from metallic to the tunnel limit . </S>"
