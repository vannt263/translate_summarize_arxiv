    "<S> the single - mode dicke model is well - known to undergo a quantum phase transition from the so - called normal phase to the superradiant phase ( hereinafter called the  superradiant quantum phase transition \" ) </S>",
    "<S> . normally , quantum phase transitions are closely related to the critical behavior of quantities such as entanglement , quantum fluctuations , and fidelity . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> , we study the quantum fisher information ( qfi ) of the field mode and that of the atoms in the ground state of the dicke hamiltonian . for finite and large enough number of atoms , </S>",
    "<S> our numerical results show that near the critical atom - field coupling , the qfis of the atomic and the field subsystems can surpass the classical limits , due to the appearance of nonclassical squeezed states . </S>",
    "<S> as the coupling increases far beyond the critical point , the two subsystems are in highly mixed states , which degrade the qfi and hence the ultimate phase sensitivity . in the thermodynamic limit , we present analytical results of the qfis and their relationships with the reduced variances . for each subsystem , we find that there is a singularity in the derivative of the qfi at the critical point , a clear signature of quantum criticality in the dicke model . </S>"
