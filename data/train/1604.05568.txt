    "<S> we develop fluctuational electrodynamics for media with nonlinear optical response . in a perturbative manner , </S>",
    "<S> we amend the stochastic helmholtz equation to describe fluctuations in a nonlinear setting , in agreement with the fluctuation dissipation theorem , and identify the local ( rytov ) current fluctuations . </S>",
    "<S> we show how the linear response ( the solution of the scattering problem ) of a collection of objects is found from the individual responses , as measured in isolation . as an example , we compute the casimir force acting between nonlinear objects which approaches the result for linear optics for large separations , and deviates for small distances .    the fluctuating electromagnetic field has become a research area of increasing interest and importance , giving rise to phenomena like casimir or van der waals interactions @xcite and thermal radiation at far and near fields @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> also , the rapid development concerning experimental detection and manipulation , including the framework of mems , allows exploration of effects down to the nanoscale @xcite . in general , </S>",
    "<S> fluctuational electrodynamics has been successfully applied to situations in thermal equilibrium , but also to objects in relative motion or at different temperatures @xcite . however , </S>",
    "<S> such setups have mostly been considered for media described by linear electric and magnetic responses .    </S>",
    "<S> the field of nonlinear optics is by itself a growing and fundamentally interesting field , comprising , among others , frequency mixing processes , the optical kerr effect , brillouin scattering and raman effects @xcite . especially considering recent developments concerning metamaterials , where large nonlinear response functions are observed , promising novel materials with interesting and useful properties . </S>",
    "<S> examples include media infused with nanoparticles @xcite , organic materials @xcite or polymers @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> fluctuations in nonlinear systems have been investigated for more than 50 years ( mostly for classical systems ) @xcite , also in interacting field theories ( see e.g. @xcite ) and applied to critical casimir forces @xcite . regarding the fluctuating electromagnetic field , short - range enhancement of van der waals forces have been predicted @xcite and casimir forces for systems with nonlinear boundary conditions @xcite and nonlinear coupling functions @xcite have also been studied . </S>",
    "<S> fluctuations have also been considered in nonlinear optical cavities @xcite , with intriguing effects regarding heat radiation , studied in the langevin framework @xcite . yet , the direct combination of nonlinear optics and fluctuational electrodynamics is missing in the literature .    in this letter </S>",
    "<S> , we develop fluctuational electrodynamics for systems with nonlinear optical response . starting from the stochastic nonlinear helmholtz equation </S>",
    "<S> , we develop a perturbative scheme to amend response functions and fluctuations according to the fluctuation dissipation theorem ( fdt ) . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the linear response of a system of several objects is not a simple combination of the response functions of the individual objects . </S>",
    "<S> last , we derive and discuss the casimir force between nonlinear media in equilibrium . we find that an object which is invisible  in the sense that its linear response is zero  still feels a casimir force when brought to a second object due to nonlinear response .    </S>",
