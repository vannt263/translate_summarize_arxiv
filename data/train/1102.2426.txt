    "<S> the main source of decoherence for an electron spin confined to a quantum dot is the hyperfine interaction with nuclear spins . to analyze this process </S>",
    "<S> theoretically we diagonalize the central spin hamiltonian in the high magnetic @xmath0-field limit </S>",
    "<S> . then we project the eigenstates onto an unpolarized state of the nuclear bath and find that the resulting density of states has gaussian tails . </S>",
    "<S> the level spacing of the nuclear sublevels is exponentially small in the middle of each of the two electron zeeman levels but increases super - exponentially away from the center . </S>",
    "<S> this suggests to select states from the wings of the distribution when the system is projected on a single eigenstate by a measurement to reduce the noise of the nuclear spin bath . </S>",
    "<S> this theory is valid when the external magnetic field is larger than a typical overhauser field at high nuclear spin temperature .    </S>",
    "<S> spin dynamics in semiconductor nanostructures has recently become a topic of great interest due to the possibility of using the spin degree of freedom instead of charge in electronic circuits @xcite and equally important due to the proposal of using electron spin in a semiconductor quantum dot as a fundamental building block of the quantum computing device @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> gaas quantum dots are the main candidates in practical realizations of these proposals due to the well developed manufacturing technology . however , unavoidable inhomogeneous hyperfine interaction of electron spin with many nuclear spins of the host crystal acts as a noisy environment that is the main source of dephasing for the electron spin at low temperature when relaxation due to the phonons is ineffective .    </S>",
    "<S> the limit of fully polarized nuclear spin bath was analyzed exactly in @xcite , including spectral properties . </S>",
    "<S> however , it is rather hard to achieve a significant polarization dynamically , and thermodynamic polarization , requiring sub - milli kelvin temperatures @xcite , is still out of reach for semiconductors . </S>",
    "<S> currently , a more promising route is to actively reduce the distribution width of the nuclear overhauser field by projective measurements @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this has been partially achieved in experiments leading to significantly longer decoherence times @xcite . to further optimize projective measurement techniques </S>",
    "<S> it is essential to gain a better understanding of the spectral properties of the unpolarized system which , so far , have only been understood qualitatively .    in this paper </S>",
