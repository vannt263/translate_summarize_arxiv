    "the prediction of an attractive force between two uncharged , perfectly reflecting plane - parallel plates embedded in vacuum by h.  b.  g.  casimir back in 1948 turned out to be a milestone on the way to modern quantum physics @xcite .",
    "the equilibrium casimir effect can be equivalently ascribed to quantum zero - point fluctuations of the electromagnetic field , or to charge and current fluctuations in the plates @xcite . by introducing objects to the quantum vacuum , forces appear due to topological constraints .",
    "casimir s famous formula for the equilibrium energy @xmath0 @xmath1 was rederived and verified many times ( e.g. see ref .",
    "@xcite ) . in the present notation @xmath2 denotes the separation between the plates , @xmath3 their surface area , @xmath4 the speed of light , and @xmath5 the reduced planck constant , indicating the quantum nature of the casimir effect .",
    "the casimir effect is relevant on small length scales ( e.g. on the submicron scale ) . shortly after casimir",
    "s breakthrough , the formalism was further developed to be applicable to any kind of dielectric media at finite temperature @xcite .",
    "the rapid development of casimir physics culminated in the birth of fluctuational electrodynamics in the 1950s @xcite .    on the experimental side",
    ", scientists were able to quantitatively verify the existence of the theoretically predicted forces in high - precision measurements for the first time around the turn of the millennium @xcite .",
    "consequently , many sources of imprecision in force measurements between objects at close proximity were identified and remedied . a few years ago , the attractivity of the casimir force was reversed by a suitable choice of interacting materials immersed in a fluid @xcite .",
    "the reversal of the algebraic sign of the force verified the theoretical predictions made by lifshitz over 50 years ago @xcite .",
    "recently , situations out of equilibrium have entered the limelight of theory . in this context ,",
    "phenomena such as vacuum friction or objects at different temperatures have been investigated @xcite .",
    "casimir forces in thermal non - equilibrium have been computed for a variety of different set - ups , e.g. for parallel plates @xcite , deformed plates @xcite , between dielectric gratings @xcite , between cylinders @xcite , between a sphere and a plate @xcite , between atoms and surfaces @xcite , between three bodies @xcite , and for inhomogeneous media @xcite . also interactions between brownian charges at different temperatures",
    "have been studied @xcite . moreover ,",
    "non - equilibrium casimir forces have been computed in fluid  or other classical systems @xcite .    generally , in thermal non - equilibrium",
    ", forces can be repulsive @xcite , exhibit different power laws @xcite , show stable points @xcite or levitation @xcite . for two spheres with different temperatures , points of self - propelled pairs",
    "have been observed @xcite , where the two identical spheres feel equal forces in the same direction for a specific choice of parameters .",
    "the subject of self - propulsion has become a very popular topic also in fluid systems , where small particles are propelled through different means @xcite .    in this paper",

    "<S> we theoretically study the phenomenon of self - propulsion through casimir forces in thermal non - equilibrium . </S>",
    "<S> using fluctuational electrodynamics , we derive a formula for the self - propulsion force for an arbitrary small object in two scenarios , i ) for the object being isolated , and ii ) for the object being close to a planar surface . in the latter case , </S>",
    "<S> the self - propulsion force ( i.e. , the force parallel to the surface ) increases with decreasing distance , i.e. , it couples to the near - field . </S>",
    "<S> we numerically calculate the lateral force acting on a hot spheroid near a surface and show that it can be as large as the gravitational force , thus being potentially measurable in fly - by experiments . </S>",
    "<S> we close by linking our results to well - known relations of linear response theory in fluctuational electrodynamics : looking at the friction of the anisotropic object for constant velocity , we identify a correction term that is additional to the typically used approach . </S>"
