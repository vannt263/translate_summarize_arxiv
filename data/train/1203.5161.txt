    "<S> a dynamical system is controllable if by imposing appropriate external signals on a subset of its nodes , it can be driven from any initial state to any desired state in finite time . </S>",
    "<S> here we study the impact of various network characteristics on the minimal number of driver nodes required to control a network . </S>",
    "<S> we find that clustering and modularity have no discernible impact , but the symmetries of the underlying matching problem can produce linear , quadratic or no dependence on degree correlation coefficients , depending on the nature of the underlying correlations . </S>",
    "<S> the results are supported by numerical simulations and help narrow the observed gap between the predicted and the observed number of driver nodes in real networks .    while during the past decade significant efforts have been devoted to understanding the structure , evolution and dynamics of complex networks @xcite , only recently has attention turned to an equally important problem : our ability to control them . </S>",
    "<S> given the problem s importance , recent work has extended the concept of pinning control @xcite and structural controllability @xcite to complex networks . here </S>",
    "<S> we focus on the latter approach . </S>",
    "<S> a networked system is considered controllable if by imposing appropriate external signals on a subset of its components , called driver nodes , the system can be driven from any initial state to any final state in finite time @xcite . as the control of a system requires a quantitative description of the governing dynamical rules , progress in this area </S>",
    "<S> was limited to small engineered systems . yet , recently liu et al . </S>",
    "<S> @xcite showed that the identification of the minimal number of driver nodes required to control a network , @xmath0 , can be derived from the network topology by mapping controllability @xcite to the maximum matching in directed networks @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the mapping indicated that @xmath0 is mainly determined by the degree distribution @xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> we know , however , that a series of characteristics , from degree correlations @xcite to local clustering @xcite and communities @xcite , can not be accounted for by @xmath1 alone , prompting us to ask : which network characteristics affect the system s controllability ?    </S>",
