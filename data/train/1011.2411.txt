    "<S> theories of planet formation predict the birth of giant planets in the inner , dense , and gas - rich regions of the circumstellar disks around young stars . </S>",
    "<S> these are the regions from which strong co emission is expected . </S>",
    "<S> observations have so far been unable to confirm the presence of planets caught in formation . </S>",
    "<S> we have developed a novel method to detect a giant planet still embedded in a circumstellar disk by the distortions of the co molecular line profiles emerging from the protoplanetary disk s surface . </S>",
    "<S> the method is based on the fact that a giant planet significantly perturbs the gas velocity flow in addition to distorting the disk surface density . </S>",
    "<S> we have calculated the emerging molecular line profiles by combining hydrodynamical models with semianalytic radiative transfer calculations . </S>",
    "<S> our results have shown that a giant jupiter - like planet can be detected using contemporary or future high - resolution near - ir spectrographs such as vlt / crires or elt / metis . </S>",
    "<S> we have also studied the effects of binarity on disk perturbations . </S>",
    "<S> the most interesting results have been found for eccentric circumprimary disks in mid - separation binaries , for which the disk eccentricity - detectable from the asymmetric line profiles - arises from the gravitational effects of the companion star . </S>",
    "<S> our detailed simulations shed new light on how to constrain the disk kinematical state as well as its eccentricity profile . </S>",
    "<S> recent findings by independent groups have shown that core - accretion is severely affected by disk eccentricity , hence detection of an eccentric protoplanetary disk in a young binary system would further constrain planet formation theories . </S>"
