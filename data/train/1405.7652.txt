    "the concept of scaling has been of tremendous interest since the days of galilei to whom the insight is attributed that the effects of physical laws may change considerably under a mere rescaling of object sizes @xcite .",
    "scale transformations became important in studies of turbulent flow , beginning with reynolds work and culminating in kolmogorov s seminal papers of 1941 @xcite . in the context of phase transitions between equilibrium states of matter ,",
    "scaling behaviour is a hallmark of criticality and the appearance of macroscopic structure independent of microscopic details .",
    "when a system near a phase transition is driven away from equilibrium , critical properties also show up in the ensuing dynamical evolution @xcite .",
    "the correlation length grows large , and relaxation time scales diverge .",
    "critical dynamics away from thermal equilibrium has been studied extensively in the physics of non - linear systems , in particular in the context of pattern formation , phase - ordering kinetics , and turbulence , but the full structure of non - thermal criticality is still far from being satisfactorily understood .    here ,",
    "we study , by means of functional renormalisation - group methods , non - thermal critical states of driven and dissipative hydrodynamics in view of possible scaling .",
    "we consider classical burgers turbulence @xcite , and relate our results to scaling solutions of the kardar - parisi - zhang ( kpz ) equation @xcite as well as to quantum turbulence described by the gross - pitaevskii ( gp ) model @xcite .",
    "we study , specifically , possible scalings at fixed points of the driven - dissipative burgers equation and discuss their relevance in the context of turbulent cascades . by comparing with semi - classical simulations of the gp equation ,",
    "we show that critical exponents known for the kpz equation can be used to quantify anomalous scaling of acoustic turbulence in a superfluid .",
    "the dynamics of many - body systems driven out of equilibrium can become stationary by means of dissipation and be characterised by a local flux of energy , both in position and momentum space .",
    "such dynamics has many realisations in nature since it is virtually impossible to fully suppress contact to the environment in any realistic setting .",
    "if the driving , the flux of energy in momentum space , and the dissipation have suitable characteristics , the stationary dynamics can exhibit scale invariance and universality distinct from any thermal equilibrium state .",
    "examples of such driven - dissipative stationary systems are realised in a wide range of systems , from exciton - polariton condensates @xcite , through pattern formation in non - linear media @xcite all the way to classical hydrodynamic turbulence @xcite .    in this article",
    ", we study scaling solutions of the stochastic burgers equation @xcite , which is a model for fully compressible fluid dynamics .",
    "we compute stationary correlation functions of burgers turbulence driven by a random gaussian forcing .",
    "we set up functional renormalisation group ( rg ) flow equations to look for non - perturbative fixed points , applying ideas from refs .",
    "@xcite in the context of classical hydrodynamic turbulence @xcite . for this , we take momentum dependence of low - order correlations into account .",

    "fixed points outside this range , which require a uv regulator to be implemented in the integrals , can , to a certain extent , be related to direct cascades of energy .",

    "in this article , we have investigated the scaling properties of the stochastic burgers equation within the functional renormalisation group ( rg ) approach . for this purpose ,",
    "a set of fixed - point equations has been established for the two - point correlators , including , in particular , the velocity cumulants .",
    "they are solved in both , the ultraviolet and infrared asymptotic regimes .",
    "a regime of uv - convergent fixed points was identified .",
    "it was found that direct cascades of energy require a uv regulator as it is set by a dissipation term . on the other hand ,",
    "the ir asymptotic form of the integral equations was found to allow for a continuous set of solutions .",
    "in one spatial dimension , the continuous set of fixed points was shown to coincide with perturbative results for the kpz equation @xcite .",
    "moreover , we found interesting implications for strong wave and quantum turbulence , as well as acoustic turbulence in a dilute bose gas .",
    "in particular , we have shown that the canonical kolmogorov - type @xmath0 exponent can be derived for the direct energy cascade in a quantum system from previously studied strong - wave - turbulence solutions .",
    "on the basis of existing numerical studies we hence conjecture the corresponding anomalous exponents of superfluid turbulence to be close to zero .",
    "using literature averages for kpz critical exponents we furthermore obtained first quantitative estimates for the anomalous scaling of acoustic turbulence in a superfluid bose gas , corresponding to ensembles of grey solitons in one - dimensional condensates and to density depressions and sound wave turbulence in two- and three - dimensional systems .",
    "these anomalous exponents are corroborated by independent existing numerical simulations .",

    "<S> scaling in the dynamical properties of complex many - body systems has been of strong interest since turbulence phenomena became the subject of systematic mathematical studies . in this article , </S>",
    "<S> dynamical critical phenomena far from equilibrium are investigated with functional renormalisation group equations . </S>",
    "<S> the focus is set on scaling solutions of the stochastic driven - dissipative burgers equation and their relation to solutions known in the literature for burgers and kardar - parisi - zhang dynamics . </S>",
    "<S> we furthermore relate superfluid as well as acoustic turbulence described by the gross - pitaevskii model to known analytic and numerical results for scaling solutions . in this way </S>",
    "<S> , the canonical kolmogorov exponent @xmath0 for the energy cascade in superfluid turbulence is obtained analytically . </S>",
    "<S> we also get first results for anomalous exponents of acoustic and quantum turbulence . these are consistent with existing experimental data . </S>",
    "<S> we suggest experimental investigations in , e.g. , exciton - polariton condensates in solid - state systems as well as in ultra cold atomic gases . </S>"
