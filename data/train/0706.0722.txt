    "<S> sequences that are defined by multisums of hypergeometric terms with compact support occur frequently in enumeration problems of combinatorics , algebraic geometry and perturbative quantum field theory . the standard recipe to study the asymptotic expansion of such sequences is to find a recurrence satisfied by them , convert it into a differential equation satisfied by their generating series , and analyze the singulatiries in the complex plane . </S>",
    "<S> we propose a shortcut by constructing directly from the structure of the hypergeometric term a finite set , for which we conjecture ( and in some cases prove ) that it contains all the singularities of the generating series . </S>",
    "<S> our construction of this finite set is given by the solution set of a balanced system of polynomial equations of a rather special form , reminiscent of the bethe ansatz . </S>",
    "<S> the finite set can also be identified with the set of critical values of a potential function , as well as with the evaluation of elements of an additive @xmath0-theory group by a regulator function . </S>",
    "<S> we give a proof of our conjecture in some special cases , and we illustrate our results with numerous examples . </S>"
