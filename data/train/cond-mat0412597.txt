    "<S> we compute the free energy for two rows of localized adsorption sites embedded in a two dimensional one - component plasma with neutralizing background density @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> the interaction energy between the adsorption sites is repulsive . </S>",
    "<S> we also compute the average occupation number of the adsorption sites and compare it to the result for a single row of sites . </S>",
    "<S> the exact result indicates that the discretization does not induce charge asymmetry and no attractive forces occur . </S>",
    "<S> one - component plasma , localized adsorption , dna attraction .    </S>",
    "<S> the subtleties of electrostatics in condensed matter theory represent a formidable and never ending challenge . one topic of much recent activity , has been the attraction between two macromolecules of the same charge  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> one mechanism that has been proposed invokes charge asymmetry related to the formation of lattices or wigner crystals @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> one problem with this picture is that it will create a dipole that is inconsistent with the perfect screening sum rules ( blum _ _ et al.__@xcite ) . </S>",
    "<S> while the formation of wigner crystals under special conditions is an experimental fact , the question of the large asymmetry in the charge distribution needs clarification . </S>",
    "<S> as has happened in the past the exact solution of the two dimensional jancovici model  @xcite can provide an unambiguous answer to the puzzle . </S>",
    "<S> the interaction between two equally charged lines ( which are charged surfaces in 2 dimensions ! ) has been discussed using the exact solution , and is always repulsive @xcite . here , we study a discretized version of this problem , namely two lines of discrete adsorption sites , where the adsorption potential is given by the baxter  @xcite sticky potential . to do this we extend the localized adsorption model of a single line  @xcite to the case of two lines of discrete adsorption sites . </S>",
    "<S> this extension is non - trivial , and as in a similar case discussed in the past , has a simple solution for what we would call a commensurate lattice @xcite , namely the spacing of the adsorption sites is such that the background charge of the enclosed area corresponds to an entire number of discrete charges . </S>"
