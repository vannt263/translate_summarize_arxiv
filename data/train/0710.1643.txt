    "<S> the reflection of a three - dimensional vectorial maxwell - gaussian beam by a planar surface is studied . </S>",
    "<S> the surface is characterized by its complex reflection coefficients @xmath0 and @xmath1 for te and tm electromagnetic plane waves of wavevector @xmath2 , respectively . </S>",
    "<S> the field impinging upon the reflecting surface is modeled as a quasi - monochromatic fundamental gaussian beam suitably modified in order to satisfy maxwell equations ( maxwell - gaussian beam ) . </S>",
    "<S> analytical expressions , correct up to the second order in a perturbation expansion , are given for the reflected electric and magnetic field , respectively . </S>",
    "<S> we found that first order terms in the perturbation expansion account for a longitudinal shift ( goos - hnchen effect ) of the whole reflected beam , while second order terms modifies the transverse shape of the beam which is , at this order , no longer cylindrically symmetric . </S>"
