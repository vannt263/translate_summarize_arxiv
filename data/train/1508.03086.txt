    "<S> a major direction in the theory of cluster algebras is to construct ( quantum ) cluster algebra structures on the ( quantized ) coordinate rings of various families of varieties arising in lie theory . </S>",
    "<S> we prove that all algebras in a very large axiomatically defined class of noncommutative algebras possess canonical quantum cluster algebra structures . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , they coincide with the corresponding upper quantum cluster algebras . </S>",
    "<S> we also establish analogs of these results for a large class of poisson nilpotent algebras . </S>",
    "<S> many important families of coordinate rings are subsumed in the class we are covering , which leads to a broad range of application of the general results to the above mentioned types of problems . as a consequence </S>",
    "<S> , we prove the berenstein  </S>",
    "<S> zelevinsky conjecture for the quantized coordinate rings of double bruhat cells and construct quantum cluster algebra structures on all quantum unipotent groups , extending the theorem of gei , leclerc and schrer for the case of symmetric kac  moody groups . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , we prove that the upper cluster algebras of berenstein , fomin and zelevinsky associated to double bruhat cells coincide with the corresponding cluster algebras .    </S>",
    "<S> he theory of cluster algebras provides a unified framework for treating a number of problems in diverse areas of mathematics such as combinatorics , representation theory , topology , mathematical physics , algebraic and poisson geometry , and dynamical systems @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the construction of cluster algebras was invented by fomin and zelevinsky @xcite who also obtained a number of fundamental results on them . </S>",
    "<S> this construction builds algebras in a novel way by producing infinite generating sets via a process of mutation rather than the classical approach using generators and relations .    </S>",
    "<S> the main algebraic approach to cluster algebras relies on representations of finite dimensional algebras and derived categories @xcite . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we describe a different algebraic approach based on noncommutative ring theory .    </S>",
    "<S> an important range of problems in the theory of cluster algebras is to prove that the coordinate rings of certain algebraic varieties coming from lie theory admit cluster algebra structures . </S>",
    "<S> the idea is that , once this is done , one can use cluster algebras to study canonical bases in such coordinate rings . </S>",
    "<S> analogous problems deal with the corresponding quantizations . </S>",
    "<S> the approach via representation theory to this type of problem needs combinatorial data for quivers as a starting point . </S>",
    "<S> such might not be available a priori . </S>",
    "<S> this approach also differs from one family of varieties to another , which means that in each case one needs to design an appropriate categorification process .    </S>",
    "<S> we prove that all algebras in a very general , axiomatically defined class of quantum nilpotent algebras are quantum cluster algebras . the proof is based on constructing quantum clusters by considering sequences of prime elements in chains of subalgebras which are noncommutative unique factorization domains . </S>",
    "<S> these clusters are canonical relative to the mentioned chains of subalgebras , which are determined by the presentation of the quantum nilpotent algebra </S>",
    "<S> . the construction does not rely on any initial combinatorics of the algebras . on the contrary </S>",
    "<S> , the construction itself produces intricate combinatorial data for prime elements in chains of subalgebras . </S>",
    "<S> when this is applied to special cases , we recover the weyl group combinatorics which played a key role in categorification earlier @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> because of this , we expect that our construction will be helpful in building a unified categorification of quantum nilpotent algebras . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we also prove similar results for ( commutative ) cluster algebras using poisson prime elements .    </S>",
    "<S> the results of the paper have many applications since a number of important families of algebras arise as special cases in our axiomatics . </S>",
    "<S> berenstein , fomin , and zelevinsky proved @xcite that the coordinate rings of all double bruhat cells in every complex simple lie group are upper cluster algebras . </S>",
    "<S> it was an important problem to decide whether the latter coincide with the corresponding cluster algebras . </S>",
    "<S> we resolve this problem positively . on the quantum side , </S>",
    "<S> we prove the berenstein  zelevinsky conjecture @xcite on cluster algebra structures for all quantum double bruhat cells . </S>",
