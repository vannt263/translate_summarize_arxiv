    "the possibility of inducing an electronic non - thermal phase transition in high - temperature superconductors ( htsc ) , by means of ultra - short laser pulses , will set a new path for studying the origin of the superconductivity in these materials . in facts , under such non - equilibrium conditions ,",
    "the homogeneous superconducting phase becomes unstable as its free energy increases during the pulse duration@xcite , while the superconducting order parameter can coexist with the pseudogap or the normal - state . for many years",
    "the exploration of the physics of this process has been a difficult task because of experimental limitations , mainly arising from laser induced heating of the samples@xcite . only recent all - optical pump - probe experiments on underdoped and optimally doped htsc@xcite have achieved control of the impulsive vaporization of the superconducting condensate in the high - intensity regime .",
    "this phenomenon has been observed as the saturation of the transient reflectivity variation ( @xmath5r / r ) signal associated with the superconducting phase@xcite , in contrast to its linear fluence dependence in the low intensity regime@xcite .",
    "a similar photoinduced phase transition ( pipt ) to the metallic phase has been recently reported on charge - density - wave@xcite ( cdw ) and spin - density wave@xcite ( sdw ) compounds .",
    "while in such systems the pipt exhibits quasi - thermal character , as it is accompanied by the same critical slowing down observed in quasi - equilibrium conditions at t@xmath4@xcite , the origin of the pipt in htsc remains unclear .",
    "a picture of the photoinduced non - equilibrium state is still lacking due to intrinsic difficulties to disentangle pseudogap and normal - phase signals in optimally and underdoped samples@xcite , the fingerprint of the pseudogap phase being the @xmath6r / r sign change observed above t@xmath4 in pump - probe experiments when probing at 800 nm wavelength@xcite .    here",
    "we report pump - probe optical reflectivity measurements at 800 nm in the high - excitation regime on an overdoped bi@xmath0sr@xmath0ca@xmath7y@xmath8cu@xmath0o@xmath3 ( y - bi2212 ) single crystal ( t@xmath4=@xmath9k ) . at this doping regime",
    "the underlying normal phase is fermi - liquid - like@xcite and no @xmath6r / r sign change above t@xmath4 is measured , at variance with optimally and underdoped samples@xcite .",
    "these characteristics are fundamental to quantitatively interpret the data within a rothwarf - taylor ( rt ) model with time - dependent parameters .",
    "we show the lack of the quasiparticles decay time increase at the fluence required to photoinduce the transition , at variance with previously reported pipts on cdw@xcite and sdw@xcite systems and in contrast to the decay time divergence observed at t@xmath4 on cuprates@xcite .",
    "this finding demonstrates the non - thermal character of the superconducting - to - normal - state pipt , opening the question on the nature of this phase transition . to address this question",
