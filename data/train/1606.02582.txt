    "<S> the existence of localized electromagnetic structures is discussed in the framework of the 1-dimensional relativistic maxwell - fluid model for a cold plasma with immobile ions . </S>",
    "<S> new partially localized solutions are found with a finite - difference algorithm designed to locate numerically exact solutions of the maxwell - fluid system . </S>",
    "<S> these solutions are called quasi - solitons and consist of a localized electromagnetic wave trapped in a spatially extended electron plasma wave . </S>",
    "<S> they are organized in families characterized by the number of nodes @xmath0 of the vector potential and exist in a continuous range of parameters in the @xmath1 plane , where @xmath2 is the velocity of propagation and @xmath3 is the vector potential angular frequency . </S>",
    "<S> a parametric study shows that the familiar fully localized relativistic solitons are special members of the families of partially localized quasi - solitons . </S>",
    "<S> soliton solution branches with @xmath4 are therefore parametrically embedded in the continuum of quasi - solitons . on the other hand , geometric arguments and numerical simulations </S>",
    "<S> indicate that @xmath5 solitons exist only in the limit of either small amplitude or vanishing velocity . </S>",
    "<S> direct numerical simulations of the maxwell - fluid model indicate that the @xmath6 quasi - solitons ( and embedded solitons ) are unstable and lead to wake excitation , while @xmath5 quasi - solitons appear stable . </S>",
    "<S> this helps explain the ubiquitous observation of structures that resemble @xmath5 solitons in numerical simulations of laser - plasma interaction . </S>"
