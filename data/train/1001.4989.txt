    "<S> we examine the recently proposed imaginary - time formulation for strongly correlated steady - state nonequilibrium for its range of validity and discuss significant improvements in the analytic continuation of the matsubara voltage as well as the fermionic matsubara frequency . </S>",
    "<S> the discretization error in the conventional hirsch - fye algorithm has been compensated in the fourier transformation with reliable small frequency behavior of self - energy . here </S>",
    "<S> we give detailed discussions for generalized spectral representation ansatz by including high order vertex corrections and its numerical analytic continuation procedures . </S>",
    "<S> the differential conductance calculations agree accurately with existing data from other nonequilibrium transport theories . </S>",
    "<S> it is verified that , at finite source - drain voltage , the kondo resonance is destroyed at bias comparable to the kondo temperature . </S>",
    "<S> calculated coefficients in the scaling relation of the zero bias anomaly fall within the range of experimental estimates .    </S>",
    "<S> progress in nanoscale fabrication techniques has recently generated a great deal of interest in electron transport out of equilibrium . </S>",
    "<S> well - controlled quantum dot ( qd ) structures in semiconductor devices have enabled thorough investigation of quantum many - body effects in confined geometry . </S>",
    "<S> one of the established phenomena is the zero bias anomaly ( zba ) due to the kondo effect in coulomb blockade devices  @xcite , where the scaling behavior of nonlinear conductance has been extensively verified . </S>",
    "<S> recently , more complex quantum dot systems such as molecular nano - junctions  @xcite and multi - channel kondo dots  @xcite with intricate device design have fueled intense research for electron transport mechanism in strongly correlated regime . </S>",
    "<S> nanoscale single - electron devices hold great promise not only in applications for quantum devices but also in development of general quantum many - body theory out of equilibrium .    in the past few years </S>",
    "<S> , the strong correlation community has embraced the challenge of developing quantum many - body formulations out of equilibrium , which has lead to significant advances at various levels of theories . </S>",
    "<S> unlike equilibrium quantum many - body theory where the analytic and numerical theories play complementary roles , the nonequilibrium theory has only recently had full - fledged numerical tools which could support or disprove the diagrammatic approximations , known as keldysh technique  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> so far , numerous algorithms have been proposed . </S>",
    "<S> however , most of the theories have yet to be fully established to have reliable predictive power in a wide range of strongly correlated transport .    </S>",
    "<S> the main focus of the theoretical efforts has been the description of the transient behavior toward a nonequilibrium steady - state or the properties of an established steady - state . here </S>",
    "<S> the steady - state concerns the dc current - carrying state driven by a time - independent bias in quantum dot geometry , as sketched in fig .  </S>",
    "<S> [ fig : scheme ] . quantum simulations of real - time behavior of nonequilibrium steady - state have been performed using time - dependent numerical renormalization group ( tnrg  @xcite ) , time - dependent density - matrix rg ( tdmrg  @xcite ) , perturbative rg ( prg  @xcite ) , functional renormalization group ( frg  @xcite ) , iterative summation of real - time path - integral method ( ispi  @xcite ) , diagrammatic monte carlo  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> analytic methods of nonequilibrium bethe ansatz  @xcite and perturbative steady - state expansion  @xcite have been developed .    </S>",
    "<S> the imaginary - time formulation for steady - state nonequilibrium proposed by han and heary  @xcite takes quite a different approach from the above real - time techniques . </S>",
    "<S> main motivation has been to extend the equilibrium quantum many - body theory within a similar mathematical framework of the imaginary - time formalism and therefore to easily adopt existing equilibrium numerical techniques for complex quantum interactions such as molecular dots  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the method combines the equilibrium many - body theory and steady - state nonequilibrium quantum statistics by extending the chemical potentials into complex variables , the _ matsubara voltage_. it has been shown that , upon analytic continuation of complex chemical potential back to physical chemical potential the theory recovers the nonequilibrium dynamics , and the imaginary - time green s functions map to real - time green s functions .    in this review </S>",
    "<S> , we show how the previously introduced spectral ansatz can be extended to include vertex corrections and give comprehensive discussions detailing the justification and the range of validity of the analytic continuation . </S>",
    "<S> we further enhance the computational method by modifying the conventional hirsch - fye  @xcite quantum monte carlo ( qmc ) algorithm by compensating the discretization error and obtain electron self - energy with much improved reliability . </S>",
    "<S> these improvements lead to reliable differential conductance curves in accurate agreement with other existing methods . </S>",
    "<S> we confirm that the kondo resonance is destroyed at the bias of kondo scale and the resulting scaling behavior of conductance is consistent with experiments .    </S>",
    "<S> the paper is organized as follows . in the following section  [ sec : imag ] </S>",
    "<S> , we define the imaginary - time hamiltonian for nonequilibrium . </S>",
    "<S> we then show the equivalence of imaginary - time and real - time green s functions in the perturbation expansion with arbitrary interaction through the analytic continuation . </S>",
    "<S> we give examples of different quantum dot geometry where this formalism can be extended . in the following section  [ sec : algorithm ] , </S>",
    "<S> we briefly introduce the qmc procedure and propose an algorithm which fixes discretization errors in the nonequilibrium qmc . in section  [ sec : analytic ] </S>",
    "<S> we extend the previously introduced analytic continuation ansatz via pad approximants and describe detailed numerical procedures to fit spectral functions . </S>",
    "<S> we make a direct comparison of conductance in the small to intermediate interaction regimes with other methods . in section  [ sec : results ] computational results are presented and compare them with existing theories and experiments . in the appendix , </S>",
    "<S> analytic structure of high order corrections to self - energy is discussed . </S>"
