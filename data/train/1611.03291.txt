    "<S> introduction_ due to strong one - dimensional feature of their electronic states , 3d metals and semimetals under high magnetic field have generic instabilities toward various spontaneous symmetry breaking ( ssb ) phases on lowering temperature  @xcite . in expectation of this , </S>",
    "<S> high - magnetic - field experiments have been carried out in a large number of semimetallic materials during the past decades , which lead to discoveries of unusual metal - insulator transitions in graphite above the quantum limit  @xcite , first - order phase transition into a state with larger magnetic anisotropy in bismuth  @xcite , and an abrupt field - induced quantum phase transition in weyl semimetals such as tantalumn phosphide  @xcite . despite considerable studies in past years  @xcite , </S>",
    "<S> theory is far from deciphering these experiments and the nature of these phase transitions as well as identities of respective low - temperature phases are veiled in mystery . to this end , it is vital to know canonical behaviours of transport properties in the low temperature ssb phases .    in this letter , we present a theory of transport property of density wave ( dw ) phases in a 3d electron gas under the magnetic field . </S>",
    "<S> the dw phases considered in this letter break the translational symmetry along the field direction , and can be regarded as a canonical example of ssb phases in 3d metal under the high field  @xcite . using the standard one - dimensional bosonization scheme  @xcite , we first bosonize an electron hamiltonian with a repulsive electron - electron interaction . </S>",
    "<S> the boson hamiltonian comprises of a pair of two conjugate phase variables ; an electron displacement field ( along the field direction ) and electron current field . </S>",
    "<S> the dw order manifests itself as a long range ordering of the displacement field . </S>",
    "<S> we calculate the conductivity in the dw phases , by taking into account effects of charge fluctuation around the dw orders and disorder potentials . </S>",
    "<S> the optical conductivity thus calculated shows characteristic features in finite frequency regime , which can be regarded as _ </S>",
    "<S> fingerprints _ of low - energy collective excitations in the dw phases . our heuristic argument as well as </S>",
    "<S> the bosonization scheme also relate single - particle ( imaginary - time ) electron green function with a partition function of a three - dimensional xy model in the presence of a pair of magnetic monopole and antimonopole . using this relation , we calculate an electron spectral function above and below the dw ordering . </S>",
    "<S> the result clearly shows that no well - defined single - particle excitations exist in the dw / normal phases near the phase transition . </S>",
    "<S> based on this observation , we discuss a temperature - dependence of an in - plane conductance due to surface chiral fermi arc states .    </S>",
