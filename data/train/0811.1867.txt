    "<S> we explore the extinction properties of the dust in the distant universe through the afterglows of high - redshifted grbs based on the `` drude '' model which , unlike previous studies , does not require a prior assumption of template extinction laws . </S>",
    "<S> we select grb070802 at @xmath0 ( which shows clear evidence for the 2175  extinction bump ) and grb050904 at @xmath1 , the 2nd most distant grb observed to date . </S>",
    "<S> we fit their afterglow spectra to determine the extinction of their host galaxies . </S>",
    "<S> we find that ( 1 ) their extinction curves differ substantially from that of the milky way , the small and large magellanic clouds ( which were widely adopted as template extinction laws in literature ) ; ( 2 ) the 2175  extinction feature appears to be also present in grb050904 at @xmath1 ; and ( 3 ) there does not appear to show strong evidence for a dependence of dust extinction on redshifts . </S>",
    "<S> the inferred extinction curves are closely reproduced in terms of a mixture of amorphous silicate and graphite , both of which are expected supernova condensates and have been identified in primitive meteorites as presolar grains originating from supernovae ( which are considered as the main source of dust at high-@xmath2 ) . </S>"
