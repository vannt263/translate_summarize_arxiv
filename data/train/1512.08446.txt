    "bose - einstein condensation ( bec ) occurs when low - energy states are macroscopically occupied by bosons below a critical temperature due to bose - einstein statistics , and the advent of ultracold atoms allows detailed analyses of bec and its related phenomena @xcite .",

    "<S> ultracold atoms provide clues to an important many - body problem regarding the dependence of bose - einstein condensation ( bec ) transition temperature @xmath0 on interactions . </S>",
    "<S> however , cold atoms are trapped in harmonic potentials and theoretical evaluations of the @xmath0 shift of trapped interacting bose gases are challenging . </S>",
    "<S> while previous predictions of the leading - order shift have been confirmed , more recent experiments exhibit higher - order corrections beyond available mean - field theories . by implementing two large-@xmath1 based theories with the local density approximation ( lda ) , </S>",
    "<S> we extract next - order corrections of the @xmath0 shift . </S>",
    "<S> the leading - order large-@xmath1 theory produces results quantitatively different from the latest experimental data . </S>",
    "<S> the leading - order auxiliary field ( loaf ) theory containing both normal and anomalous density fields captures the @xmath0 shift accurately in the weak interaction regime . </S>",
    "<S> however , the loaf theory shows incompatible behavior with the lda and forcing the lda leads to density discontinuities in the trap profiles . </S>",
    "<S> we present a phenomenological model based on the loaf theory , which repairs the incompatibility and provides a prediction of the @xmath0 shift in stronger interaction regime . </S>"
