    "frustrated magnets have been attracting much interest for many decades.@xcite the ordinary magnetic order is destroyed and the ground state may remain disordered or turn into an exotic state .",
    "the ground state of a frustrated system is usually unstable against a small perturbation .",
    "the system manages to find a way to relax frustration and change the state .",
    "we may design and control material functions using the frustration effects .",
    "the abx@xmath0-type compounds are well - known frustrated magnets .",
    "the lattice structure is the stacked triangular lattice .",
    "there is frustration when the nearest - neighbor interactions are antiferromagnetic .",
    "successive magnetic phase transitions occur because of the strong frustration when the spins have the ising anisotropy.@xcite the low - temperature magnetic structure is the ferrimagnetic state .",
    "there exists a partially - disordered ( pd ) phase between the paramagnetic phase and the ferrimagnetic phase . in the pd phase ,",
    "one of the three sublattices is completely disordered , while the other two sublattices take antiferromagnetic configurations .",
    "there is no structural phase transition in most compounds .",
    "the system remains fully frustrated down to the lowest temperature .",
    "the knicl@xmath0-family compounds are exceptional in that they exhibit structural phase transitions.@xcite we can observe the structural phase transitions by the dielectric measurements because each bx@xmath0 chain has a negative charge .",
    "these compounds have both magnetic and dielectric characteristics : we call them the magneto - dielectric compounds . the magnetic phase transitions and the structural ( dielectric ) phase transitions usually occur at different temperatures",
    ". however , morishita _ et al . _ found that both transitions occur at the same temperature in rbcobr@xmath0.@xcite it is a very rare case among the knicl@xmath0-family compounds .",
    "magnetic and dielectric measurements@xcite found that the phase transitions in rbcobr@xmath0 are quite unusual in the following points compared with other compounds :    * the dielectric transition temperature 37  k of rbcobr@xmath0 is very low compared with other compounds , for which the transition takes place around the room temperatures .",
    "the energy scale of the structural ( dielectric ) system in rbcobr@xmath0 seems to be suppressed somehow .",
    "* the temperature dependence of the dielectric constant does not exhibit a diverging behavior .",
    "this is clearly different from another knicl@xmath0-family compound rbfebr@xmath0 , which exhibits sharp divergence at the transition temperature , 34.4 k.@xcite * the increase of the spontaneous polarization below the dielectric transition temperature is very slow , while that of rbfebr@xmath0 is very sharp .",
    "* the magnetic pd phase appears in a very narrow temperature region .",
    "the first neutron measurement suggested that it might disappear.@xcite a recent improved neutron experiment @xcite made it clear that it exists between 31 k and 37 k. this is also a clear difference from other compounds such as cscobr@xmath0@xcite and cscocl@xmath0.@xcite * the growth of the ferrimagnetic order is very slow .",
    "the neutron count increases linearly with the temperature decrease in the low - temperature phase .",
    "these characteristic behaviors suggest that there is an unknown mechanism of interplay between the magnetic system and the dielectric system in rbcobr@xmath0 .",
    "an aim of this paper is to propose a proper theoretical model that _ quantitatively _ explains the experimental results of rbcobr@xmath0 .",
    "a well - known theoretical model for the abx@xmath0 compounds is the antiferromagnetic spin system on the stacked triangular lattice.@xcite however , the ordinary spin model without a coupling to the dielectric system is not sufficient to explain rbcobr@xmath0 .",
    "the chain - mean - field theory@xcite gives the magnitude of the second - nearest - neighbor magnetic interactions on the @xmath1-plain @xmath2 k , which is comparable to that of the nearest - neighbor interactions @xmath3 k.@xcite this is not acceptable from the experimental point of view .    in the present paper ,",
    "we use the spin - lattice model proposed by shirahata and nakamura.@xcite this model showed that the pd phase may disappear because of the relaxation of frustration by the lattice distortion ; each of the spin system and the lattice system relaxes frustration of the other .",
    "a single transition may occur from a paramagnetic and paraelectric phase to the ground state phase without experiencing the intermediate pd phase .",
    "shirahata and nakamura also noticed that the cooperation between the spin system and the lattice system works only when the energy scale of the lattice system is comparable with that of the spin system .    in this paper , we refine the above spin - lattice model .",
    "we find that a soft lattice system coupled with a spin system explains the interesting material rbcobr@xmath0 .",
    "we thereby clarify the origin of the characteristic behaviors of this compound .",

    "<S> extensive monte carlo simulations are performed to analyze a recent neutron diffraction experiment on a distorted triangular lattice compound rbcobr@xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> we consider a spin - lattice model , where both spin and lattice are ising variables . </S>",
    "<S> this model explains well successive magnetic and dielectric transitions observed in the experiment . </S>",
    "<S> the exchange interaction parameters and the spin - lattice coupling are estimated . </S>",
    "<S> it is found that the spin - lattice coupling is important to explain the slow growth of a ferrimagnetic order . </S>",
    "<S> the present simulations were made possible by developing a new monte carlo algorithm , which accelerates slow monte carlo dynamics of quasi - one - dimensional frustrated systems . </S>"
