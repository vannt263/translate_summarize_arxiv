    "<S> we show that , when graphene is subjected to an appropriate one - dimensional external periodic potential , additional branches of massless fermions are generated with nearly the same electron - hole crossing energy as that at the original dirac point of graphene . </S>",
    "<S> because of these new zero - energy branches , the landau levels at charge neutral filling becomes @xmath0-fold degenerate ( with _ n_=0,1,2 , ... , tunable by the potential strength and periodicity ) with the corresponding hall conductivity @xmath1 showing a step of size @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> these theoretical findings are robust against variations in the details of the external potential and provide measurable signatures of the unusual electronic structure of graphene superlattices .    </S>",
    "<S> the physical properties of graphene  @xcite are currently among the most actively investigated topics in condensed matter physics . </S>",
    "<S> graphene has the unique feature that the low - energy charge carriers are well described by the two - dimensional ( 2d ) massless dirac equation , used for massless neutrinos , rather than by the schrdinger equation  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , graphene is considered to be a promising candidate for electronics and spintronics applications  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> it has been shown that , because of their gapless energy spectrum and chiral nature , the charge carriers in graphene are not hindered by a slowly varying electrostatic potential barrier at normal incidence  @xcite , analogous to the klein tunneling effect predicted in high - energy physics . </S>",
    "<S> direct evidences of klein tunneling through a single barrier in graphene  @xcite have been observed in recent experiments  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> application of multiple barriers or periodic potentials , either electrostatic  @xcite or magnetic  @xcite , to graphene has been shown to modulate its electronic structure in unique ways and lead to fascinating new phenomena and possible applications . </S>",
    "<S> periodic arrays of corrugations  @xcite have also been proposed as graphene superlattices ( gss ) .    </S>",
    "<S> experimentally , different classes of gss have been fabricated recently . </S>",
    "<S> patterns with periodicity as small as 5  nm have been imprinted on graphene through electron - beam induced deposition of adsorbates  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> epitaxially grown graphene on the ( 0001 ) surface of ruthenium  @xcite and that on the ( 111 ) surface of iridium  @xcite also show superlattice patterns with @xmath33  nm lattice period . </S>",
    "<S> the amplitude of the periodic potential applied to graphene in these surface systems has been estimated to be in the range of a few tenths of an electron volt  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> fabrication of periodically patterned gate electrodes is another possible way of making gss with periodicity close to or larger than  @xmath320  nm .    </S>",
