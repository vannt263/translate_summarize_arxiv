    "<S> the investigation of galaxies hosting long - duration grbs ( whose progenitor is a massive star ) demonstrated their connection to star formation . </S>",
    "<S> still , the link to the total galaxy population is controversial , mainly because of the small - number statistics : @xmath0 are the grbs detected so far , @xmath1 those with measured redshift , and @xmath2 the hosts studied in detail . </S>",
    "<S> these are typically low - redshift ( @xmath3 ) , low luminosity , metal poor , and star - forming galaxies . on the other hand , at @xmath4 , massive , metal rich and dusty , interacting galaxies are not uncommon . </S>",
    "<S> the most distant population ( @xmath5 ) is poorly explored , but the deep limits reached point towards very small and star - forming objects , similar to the low-@xmath6 population . </S>",
    "<S> this ` back to the future ' behavior is a natural consequence of the connection of long grbs to star formation in young regions of the universe . </S>"
