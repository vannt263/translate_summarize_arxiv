    "the dipolar interaction endows magnetostatic ( long - wavelength ) spin waves with a very peculiar dynamics . in an in - plane magnetized ferromagnetic film ,",
    "their dispersion shows a strong anisotropy originating from the magnetization vector @xmath5 @xcite .",
    "spin waves propagating through the volume of the film appear to move backwards as their group velocity is opposite to their phase velocity ( backward volume magnetostatic spin - wave modes , bvmsw ) @xcite .",
    "conversely , spin waves with a wavevector @xmath6 tend to localize near the surface of the film in damon ",
    "eshbach ( de ) modes @xcite , which are forward modes .",
    "the surface localization of de modes is exponential , with a decay length inversely proportional to the perpendicular component of @xmath4 @xcite .    while the backward volume propagation of parallel spin waves @xmath7 is well known , in the case of perpendicular propagation @xmath6 usually only the de modes are considered @xcite .",
    "the de modes are the most likely to be excited by a microstrip antenna in the damon  eshbach geometry @xcite and show unusual features such as nonreciprocal propagation @xcite . however , in a film of finite thickness , bvmsws are not restricted to the case @xmath7 and can exist with any ( in - plane ) wavevector @xmath4 ; in particular , perpendicularly propagating volume modes also exist and have frequencies below the de branch @xcite .",
    "the bvmsw modes are , in fact , the dominant modes in optomagnetic @xcite experiments as in ref .",

    "<S> the dipolar ( magnetostatic ) interaction dominates the behavior of spin waves in magnetic films in the long - wavelength regime . in an in - plane magnetized film , volume modes exist with a negative group velocity ( backward volume magnetostatic spin waves ) , in addition to the forward surface - localized mode ( damon  eshbach ) . inside the film of finite thickness @xmath0 , </S>",
    "<S> the volume modes have a nontrivial spatial dependence , and their two - dimensional dispersion relations @xmath1 can be calculated only numerically . </S>",
    "<S> we present explicit perturbative expressions for the profiles and frequencies of the volume modes , taking into account an in - plane applied field and uniaxial anisotropy , for the regimes @xmath2 and @xmath3 , which together provide a good indication of the behavior of the modes for arbitrary wavevector @xmath4 . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , we derive a very accurate semianalytical expression for the dispersion relation @xmath1 of the lowest - frequency mode that is straightforward to evaluate using standard numerical routines . </S>",
    "<S> our results are useful to quickly interpret and control the excitation and propagation of spin waves in ( opto-)magnetic experiments . </S>"
