    "the kitaev honeycomb model , with several variations , has attracted a lot of attention over the last years . @xcite",
    "many different interesting aspects of it have been studied in detail in the original work of kitaev .",
    "@xcite there , an exact method of solution of the model based on the mapping to majorana fermion operators is discussed . although alternative mappings and approximation techniques exist,@xcite kitaev s method is widely applied , being ideally suited to this class of spin models .",
    "further , the presence of an abelian and a non - abelian phase ( in the presence of an external magnetic perturbation ) was demonstrated .",
    "@xcite the kitaev honeycomb model has a wide spectrum of physical applications , ranging from the description of strongly correlated materials @xcite to the analytical study of critical quantum spin liquids .",
    "@xcite it is also of central importance in the context of quantum information theory since its gapped phase provides a perturbative realization of the toric code .",
    "@xcite extensions of the honeycomb model have been lately proposed as promising candidates for the realization of a topological quantum memory .",
    "@xcite although very challenging , its physical realization has become closer to reality thanks to recent proposals .",
    "@xcite    in this paper we examine the projection to the physical subspace of the exact mapping to majorana fermions proposed by kitaev .",
    "@xcite as briefly discussed in refs .",
    ", unprojected and projected models have different physical properties , especially the parity of fermions .",
    "we derive here for the first time an explicit and immediately applicable representation of the projector in terms of the parity of physical fermions .",
    "as it turns out , the physical fermion parity depends in a nontrivial way on the configuration of vortices and on the lattice topology . applying the projection to specific cases",
    ", we find large differences between projected and unprojected physical quantities ( e.g. ground state and vortex energies , or spin - spin correlation functions ) .",
    "such discrepancies exist both in the gapped and gapless phase and can also survive the thermodynamic limit .",
    "our analysis is consequently essential for the exact numerical study via kitaev s exact mapping of large spin systems , especially if one wants to go beyond the small system sizes of about @xmath0 spins currently accessible to various numerical approaches such as direct diagonalization @xcite or density matrix renormalization group ( dmrg ) .",

    "<S> we investigate the exact solution of the honeycomb model proposed by kitaev and derive an explicit formula for the projector onto the physical subspace . </S>",
    "<S> the physical states are simply characterized by the parity of the total occupation of the fermionic eigenmodes . </S>",
    "<S> we consider a general lattice on a torus and show that the physical fermion parity depends in a nontrivial way on the vortex configuration and the choice of boundary conditions . in the vortex - free case with a constant gauge field we are able to obtain an analytical expression of the parity . </S>",
    "<S> for a general configuration of the gauge field the parity can be easily evaluated numerically , which allows the exact diagonalization of large spin models . </S>",
    "<S> we consider physically relevant quantities , as in particular the vortex energies , and show that their true value and associated states can be substantially different from the one calculated in the unprojected space , even in the thermodynamic limit . </S>"
