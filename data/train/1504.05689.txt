    "<S> we identify a new class of topologically driven phase transitions when calculating the hall conductance of two - band chern insulators in the long - time limit after a global quench of the hamiltonian . </S>",
    "<S> the hall conductance is expressed as the integral of the berry curvature in the diagonal ensemble . </S>",
    "<S> even if the topological invariant of the wave function is conserved under unitary evolution , the hall conductance as a function of the energy gap in the post - quench hamiltonian displays a continuous but nonanalytic behavior , that is it has a logarithmically divergent derivative as the gap closes . </S>",
    "<S> the coefficient of this logarithmic function is the ratio of the change of chern number in the ground state of the post - quench hamiltonian to the energy gap in the initial state . </S>",
    "<S> this nonanalytic behavior is universal in two - band chern insulators .    _ introduction_. the discovery of the quantum hall effect  @xcite , </S>",
    "<S> i.e. a quantized hall conductance in the ground state which jumps from one plateau to another , inspired the study of topological order  @xcite to characterize different topological phases outside the conventional framework of spontaneous symmetry breaking . considerable effort has been devoted to understanding topological order or symmetry protected topological ( spt ) order in the ground state . </S>",
    "<S> more recently , a lot of attention was devoted to the nature of topological order and spt order for a state driven out of equilibrium , in particular for quantum quenches of the hamiltonian  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> consider an isolated system initially in the ground state of a hamiltonian @xmath0 and suddenly changing the hamiltonian to @xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> the wave function follows a unitary time evolution , while the local observables in the long time limit settle to the prediction of the diagonal ensemble  @xcite , which in some cases can be reduced to a thermal ensemble or a generalized gibbs ensemble  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> topological order or spt order can not be expressed as a local observable . </S>",
    "<S> therefore , its identification in a nonequilibrium state is far from trivial . in the toric code model , the topological entropy in the long time limit </S>",
    "<S> is found to be the same as its initial value independent from whether the ground states of @xmath0 or @xmath1 are topologically trivial or not  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this result agrees with a universal argument for gapped spin liquids  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> similarly , for the fermi gas on a honeycomb lattice which essentially simulates the haldane model , the chern number is proved to be conserved under unitary evolution  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , in the two - dimensional topological superfluid , the winding number of the retarded green s function after a quench shows a strong dependence on the post - quench hamiltonian @xmath1  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite , even if the winding of the anderson pseudo spin texture is conserved  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> also in the one - dimensional case , an analysis of tunneling spectroscopy by coupling the system to an auxiliary thermal bath shows that the spt order is mostly determined by @xmath1  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> but in topological superconductors with proximity - induced superconductivity , the majorana order parameter  @xcite or the entanglement spectrum  @xcite indicate that the quenched state is topologically trivial if @xmath0 and @xmath1 are in different topological phases .    </S>",
    "<S> to clarify the issue of spt order far from equilibrium , we appeal to a measurable physical quantity , namely the hall conductance in chern insulators . </S>",
    "<S> we first study a paradigmatic model , i.e. the dirac model  @xcite , and then extend our results to a general two - band chern insulator . </S>",
