    "rapid experimental development in the field of nanotechnology facilitates us to fabricate the single - molecule junctions @xcite , of which the goal is to extend the lifetime of moore s law .",
    "the charging of the molecule leads to elastic mechanical deformations , and hence causes an interaction between electronic and the quantized mechanical degrees of freedom , which is the electron - phonon coupling .",
    "molecular junctions in which a strong electron - phonon coupling can lead to polaronic regime @xcite could reveal to us a variety of intriguing transport properties , such as phonon - assisted current steps and franck - condon blockade @xcite .",
    "such a junction could be modeled as a quantum dot described by the anderson - holstein model @xcite coupled to two electrodes .",
    "efforts have been made both on theoretical and computational methods , such as master equations @xcite , diagrammatic quantum monte carlo @xcite , and numerical renormalization group @xcite , on transport problems involving electron - phonon interactions .",
    "however , lots of numerical calculations are involved in these methodologies .",
    "nonequilibrium green s function ( negf ) technique is a powerful tool to deal with a wide range of physical problems of nonequilibrium systems and in principle the most appropriate method to study interaction effects in the time - dependent properties . in order to decouple the phonon cloud operator in the polaronic regime , dressed",
    "tunneling approximation ( dta ) , in which the leads self - energies are dressed with the polaronic cloud , has been proposed to eliminate the noticeable pathological features of the single particle approximation at low frequencies and polaron tunneling approximation at high frequencies @xcite .",
    "quantum transport is in nature a stochastic process and a physical quantity of a stochastic process could be characterized by the corresponding distribution function @xcite .",
    "full - counting statistics ( fcs ) which was initiated by levitov and lesovik @xcite could give us a full scenery of probability distribution of electron and energy transport @xcite .",
    "the key in fcs is to obtain the generating function ( gf ) which is actually the fourier transform of probability distribution of related physical quantity . using the negf technique @xcite and path integral method under the two - time quantum measurement scheme @xcite , gf",
    "is formulated as a fredholm determinant in the time domain both in the phonon @xcite and electron @xcite transport systems .",
    "this formalism enables us to study transient regime which could help us have a better understanding in the short time dynamics @xcite .",

    "<S> we investigate the full - counting statistics ( fcs ) of energy transport carried by electrons in molecular junctions for the anderson - holstein model in the polaronic regime . using two - time quantum measurement scheme , generating function ( gf ) for the energy transport </S>",
    "<S> is derived and expressed as a fredholm determinant in terms of keldysh nonequilibrium green s function in the time domain . </S>",
    "<S> dressed tunneling approximation is used in decoupling the phonon cloud operator in the polaronic regime . </S>",
    "<S> this formalism facilitates us to analyze the time evolution of energy transport dynamics after a sudden switch - on of the coupling between the dot and the leads towards the stationary state . </S>",
    "<S> transient dynamics of energy current cumulants is numerically calculated and analyzed . </S>",
    "<S> the universal scaling of normalized transient energy cumulants is found under the external bias . </S>",
    "<S> the steady state energy current cumulant gf in the long time limit is obtained in the energy domain as well . </S>",
    "<S> universal relations for steady state energy current fcs is derived under finite temperature gradient with zero bias and this enables us to express the equilibrium energy current cumulant by a linear combination of lower order cumulants . </S>",
    "<S> behaviors of energy current cumulants under temperature gradient and external bias are numerically shown and explained . </S>"
