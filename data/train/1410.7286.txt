    "<S> a mathematical model is introduced for thermoelectrochemical phenomena in an electrolysis cell , and its qualitative analysis is focused on existence of solutions . </S>",
    "<S> the model consists of a system of nonlinear parabolic pdes in conservation form expressing conservation of energy , mass and charge . on the other hand , </S>",
    "<S> an integral form of newton s law is used to describe heat exchange at the electrolyte / electrode interface , a nonlinear radiation condition is enforced on the heat flux at the wall and a nonlinear boundary condition is considered for the electrochemical flux in order to account for butler - volmer kinetics . </S>",
    "<S> the main objective is the nonconstant character of each parameter , that is , the coefficients are assumed to be dependent on the spatial variable and the temperature . making recourse of known estimates of solutions for some auxiliary elliptic and parabolic problems , which are explicitly determined by the gehring - giaquinta - modica theory </S>",
    "<S> , we find sufficient smallness conditions on the data to guarantee the existence of the original solutions via the schauder fixed point argument . these conditions may provide useful informations for numerical as well as real applications . </S>",
    "<S> we conclude with an example of application , namely the electrolysis of molten sodium chloride . </S>"
