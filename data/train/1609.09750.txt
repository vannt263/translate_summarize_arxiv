    "<S> we show that the two impurity anderson model exhibit an additional quantum critical point at infinitely many specific distances between both impurities for an inversion symmetric 1d dispersion . unlike the quantum critical point previously established by jones and varma , it is robust against particle - hole or parity symmetry breaking . </S>",
    "<S> the quantum critical point separates a spin doublet from a spin singlet ground state and is , therefore , topological protected . </S>",
    "<S> a finite single particle tunneling @xmath0 or an applied uniform gate voltages will drive the system across the quantum critical point . </S>",
    "<S> the discriminative magnetic properties of the different phases cause a jump in the spectral functions at low temperature which might be useful for future spintronics devices . </S>",
    "<S> a local parity conservation will prevent the spin - spin correlation function to decay to its equilibrium value after spin - manipulations .    </S>",
    "<S> [ [ introduction . ] ] introduction. + + + + + + + + + + + + + +    the promising perspective of combining traditional electronics with novel spintronics devices leads to an intense research of controlling and switching magnetic properties of such nano - devices . </S>",
    "<S> experimentally magnetic properties of adatoms one surfaces @xcite or magnetic molecules @xcite might serve as smallest building blocks for spintronic devices . from a theoretical perspective </S>",
    "<S> , the two impurity anderson model ( tiam ) @xcite constitute an important but simple system which embodies the competition of interactions between two localized magnetic moments with those between the impurities and the conduction band .    </S>",
    "<S> a well known quantum critical point ( qcp ) was predicted for the tiam @xcite separating a singlet phase where both impurity spins are bound into a local singlet for strong antiferromagnetic interactions between the impurities and another singlet phase where both impurity spins are completely screened by the conduction band . </S>",
    "<S> this qcp @xcite turned out to be unstable against particle - hole ( ph ) symmetry breaking @xcite and is a consequence of unphysical approximations @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> it is generically replaced by a crossover regime @xcite that can be broad .    in this letter </S>",
    "<S> we establish that the model exhibit another stable qcp for any inversion symmetric 1d dispersion depending only on the absolute value of the wave vector . </S>",
    "<S> the existence of this different qcp relies only on the fact that for specific distances @xmath1 between both impurities either the even or odd parity contributions to the conduction band decouples from the impurities at low energy scales leading to an underscreened kondo effect . </S>",
    "<S> this underscreened kondo fixed point ( usk fp ) has a doublet ground state which is topological different to the singlet ground state for large antiferromagnetic interactions between both impurities excluding a smooth crossover between both phases . </S>",
    "<S> this qcp also exist for the limit @xmath2 in all dimensions @xcite : for this special case the qcp has been recently observed in molecular dimers @xcite where the different phases can clearly be detected in the scanning tunneling spectra .    here , we present the generalization to finite distances , its robustness against particle - hole symmetry as well as parity breaking and </S>",
    "<S> demonstrate that the quantum phase transition ( qpt ) can also be evoked by applying a gate voltage to the impurities . </S>",
    "<S> since the entanglement between the impurity spins is protected by a dynamical symmetry in the parity - symmetric case , the spin - spin correlation function can not completely decay to its equilibrium value , and , therefore , might be useful for future qubit implementations </S>",
