    "linear optics is important in quantum information processing .",
    "the problem of sampling the output coincidence distribution of a linear optical interferometer , i.e. , the bosonsampling problem , is hard to simulate on a classical computer  @xcite .",
    "linear optics enables the efficient simulation of quantum walks  @xcite .",
    "single - photon detectors and linear optics allow for efficient universal quantum computation  @xcite .",
    "arbitrary linear optical transformations can be realized on various degrees of freedom ( dofs ) of light .",
    "for instance , any @xmath4 unitary transformations on the polarization dof can be decomposed into elementary operations that are implemented using quarter- and half - wave plates  @xcite . any unitary transformation on an arbitrary number of spatial modes",
    "can be realized as an arrangement of beamsplitters , phase shifters and mirrors  @xcite and of temporal modes using nested fiber loops or dispersion  @xcite .",
    "finally , unitary transformations on orbital - angular - momentum modes of light can be realized using beamsplitters , phase shifters , holograms and extraction gates  @xcite .",
    "experimental implementations employ spatial modes of light to perform quantum walks  @xcite , bosonsampling  @xcite , bosonic transport simulations  @xcite and photonic quantum gates  @xcite . implementing linear optical transformations on @xmath5 spatial modes requires aligning @xmath6 beamsplitters  @xcite ; this requirement poses the key challenge to the scalability of linear optical implementation of unitary transformations .",
    "one approach to overcoming the challenge of realizing a higher number of modes is to use internal dofs , such as polarization , arrival time and orbital angular momentum , in addition to the spatial dof .",
    "in particular , any lossless transformation on @xmath0  spatial and @xmath1  internal modes can be described by an @xmath2  unitary transformation .",
    "however , there is no known method to effect an arbitrary @xmath2  unitary transformation on the state of light in @xmath0 spatial and @xmath1 internal modes .",
    "here we aim to devise an efficient realization of an arbitrary unitary transformation using spatial and internal dofs . by efficient",
    "we mean that the cost of realizing the transformation , as quantified by the number of required spatial and internal optical elements , scales no faster than a polynomial in the dimension of the transformation .",
    "specifically , we construct an algorithm to decompose an arbitrary @xmath2 unitary transformation into a sequence of @xmath7 beamsplitters and @xmath7 internal transformations , each of which acts only on the internal modes of light in one spatial mode .",

    "<S> any lossless transformation on @xmath0  spatial and @xmath1  internal modes of light can be described by an @xmath2  unitary matrix , but there is no known procedure to effect an arbitrary @xmath2  unitary matrix on light in @xmath0  spatial and @xmath1  internal modes . </S>",
    "<S> we devise an algorithm to realize an arbitrary discrete unitary transformation on the combined spatial and internal degrees of freedom of light . </S>",
    "<S> our realization uses beamsplitters and operations on internal modes to effect arbitrary linear transformations . </S>",
    "<S> the number of beamsplitters required to realize a unitary transformation is reduced as compared to existing realization by a factor @xmath3 at the cost of increasing the number of internal optical elements by a factor of two . </S>",
    "<S> our algorithm thus enables the optical implementation of higher dimensional unitary transformations . </S>"
