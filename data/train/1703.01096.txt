    "<S> we report on a novel method for photo - association of strongly polar trilobite rydberg molecules . </S>",
    "<S> this exotic ultralong - range dimer , consisting of a ground - state atom bound to the rydberg electron via electron - neutral scattering , inherits its polar character from the admixture of high angular momentum electronic orbitals . </S>",
    "<S> the absence of low-@xmath0 character hinders standard photo - association techniques . here </S>",
    "<S> , we show that for suitable principal quantum numbers resonant coupling of the orbital motion with the nuclear spin of the perturber , mediated by electron - neutral scattering , hybridizes the trilobite molecular potential with the more conventional @xmath1-type molecular state . </S>",
    "<S> this provides a general path to associate trilobite molecules with large electric dipole moments , as demonstrated via high - resolution spectroscopy . </S>",
    "<S> we find a dipole moment of 135(45)d for the trilobite state . </S>",
    "<S> our results are compared to theoretical predictions based on a fermi - model .    </S>",
    "<S> controlling dipolar molecules at ultralow temperatures is of central interest for a plethora of developments and applications in ultracold chemistry @xcite , precision spectroscopy , quantum information processing @xcite , or quantum many - body physics @xcite . in the case of ultracold rydberg gases , </S>",
    "<S> the observation of a novel type of molecule formed by a neutral ground - state atom trapped within the giant rydberg electron wavefunction @xcite has recently sparked intense interest in their few- and many - body properties @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> astonishingly , these ultralong - range rydberg molecules can possess large permanent electric dipole moments , which arise from the coupling of many high - angular momentum rydberg states @xcite by electron - neutral scattering . </S>",
    "<S> while dimers correlated with @xmath1- @xcite , @xmath2- @xcite , and @xmath3- @xcite rydberg states have been efficiently photo - associated from atomic ground - state ensembles via single- or two - photon excitation pathways , they typically inherit relatively weak high-@xmath0 admixture , owing to the large non - integer parts of their quantum defects , and consequently exhibit comparatively small dipole moments , e.g. @xmath4 debye for rb @xcite . </S>",
