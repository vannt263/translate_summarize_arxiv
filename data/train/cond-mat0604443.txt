    "<S> we investigate how the conductance @xmath0 through a clean interacting quantum wire is affected by the presence of contacts and noninteracting leads . </S>",
    "<S> the contacts are defined by a vanishing two - particle interaction to the left and a finite repulsive interaction to the right or vice versa . </S>",
    "<S> no additional single - particle scattering terms ( impurities ) are added . </S>",
    "<S> we first use bosonization and the local luttinger liquid picture and show that within this approach @xmath0 is determined by the properties of the leads regardless of the details of the spatial variation of the luttinger liquid parameters . </S>",
    "<S> this generalizes earlier results obtained for step - like variations . in particular , no single - particle backscattering is generated at the contacts . </S>",
    "<S> we then study a microscopic model applying the functional renormalization group and show that the spatial variation of the interaction produces single - particle backscattering , which in turn leads to a reduced conductance . </S>",
    "<S> we investigate how the smoothness of the contacts affects @xmath0 and show that for decreasing energy scale its deviation from the unitary limit follows a power law with the same exponent as obtained for a system with a weak single - particle impurity placed in the contact region of the interacting wire and the leads . </S>"
