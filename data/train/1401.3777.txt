    "<S> we show that a single dark soliton can exist in a spin - orbit - coupled fermi gas with a high spin imbalance , where spin - orbit coupling favors uniform superfluids over non - uniform fulde - ferrell - larkin - ovchinnikov states , leading to dark soliton excitations in highly imbalanced gases . above a critical spin imbalance , two topological majorana fermions ( mfs ) without interactions </S>",
    "<S> can coexist inside a dark soliton , paving a way for manipulating mfs through controlling solitons . at the topological transition point </S>",
    "<S> , the atom density contrast across the soliton suddenly vanishes , suggesting a signature for identifying topological solitons .                </S>",
    "<S> solitons , topological defects arising from the interplay between dispersion and nonlinearity of underlying systems , are significant for many different physical branches  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the realization of cold atomic superfluids provides a clean and controllable platform for exploring soliton physics . in cold atomic gases , </S>",
    "<S> dark solitons represent quantum excitations of a superfluid with the superfluid order parameter vanishing at the soliton center in conjunction with a phase jump across the soliton . </S>",
    "<S> dark solitons have been extensively investigated in cold atoms @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , dark solitons have recently been experimentally observed in strongly interacting spin balanced fermi gases  @xcite , where the cooper pairing wave - function has a phase jump across the soliton  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , dark solitons in the presence of a large spin imbalance have not been well explored .    with a large spin imbalance </S>",
    "<S> , the ground state of the superfluid is theoretically predicted to be the spatially non - uniform fulde - ferrell - larkin - ovchinnikov ( fflo ) phase  @xcite with finite momentum pairing in 1d and quasi-1d  yoshida2007pra , huhui2007pra , parish2007prl , roman2011pra , kuei2012pra , xiwen2013rmp , which is partially verified by the experiment  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this spatially non - uniform phase does not support dark soliton excitations that usually occur in bcs - type uniform superfluids . on the other hand </S>",
    "<S> , uniform superfluids can exist in large spin imbalanced fermi gases  @xcite in the presence of spin - orbit ( so ) coupling . since so coupling for cold atoms has been experimentally generated recently for both bosons and fermions lin2011nature , jing2012prl , zwierlen2012prl , panjian2012prl , qu2013pra , spilman2013prl </S>",
    "<S> , a natural question is whether such so coupled superfluids with large spin imbalances can also support dark solitons .    </S>",
    "<S> more interestingly , it is well known that defects ( vortices , edges , etc . ) in so coupled fermionic superfluids with large spin imbalances can accommodate majorana fermions ( mfs ) @xcite , topological excitations that satisfy exotic non - abelian exchange statistics  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> recently mfs have attracted tremendous attention in various physical systems mfsystem because of their fundamental importance as well as potential applications in fault - tolerant quantum computation  @xcite . in this context , so coupled fermionic superfluids have their intrinsic advantages for mfs because of their disorder free  @xcite and highly controllable characteristics . </S>",
    "<S> therefore another important question is whether topological majorana excitations can exist inside dark solitons if such topological defects do exist .    in this letter , we address these two important questions by studying dark solitons in degenerate fermi gases ( dfgs ) trapped in 1d harmonic potentials with the experimentally already realized so coupling and spin imbalances . here </S>",
    "<S> the spin imbalance is equivalent to a zeeman field . in the absence of so coupling , the fflo state  @xcite with an oscillating order parameter amplitude </S>",
    "<S> is the ground state @xcite with a large zeeman field , which can not support dark solitons . with so coupling , </S>",
    "<S> we find    \\(i ) so coupling suppresses the fflo state , leading to uniform bcs superfluids that support dark solitons . </S>",
    "<S> the parameter region for dark solitons and their spatial properties are obtained .    </S>",
    "<S> \\(ii ) for substantially large spin imbalances , we find remarkably that two mfs can coexist inside a dark soliton without any interaction , beyond the general expectation that two mfs with overlapping wave - functions interact , leading to energy splitting that destroys mfs . </S>",
    "<S> such solitons are topological solitons to be distinguished from solitons without mfs .    </S>",
    "<S> \\(iii ) the experimental signature of mfs inside the dark soliton in the local density of states ( ldos ) is characterized , which show an isolated zero energy peak at the center of the soliton . moreover , </S>",
    "<S> the density contrast across the soliton suddenly decreases to zero at the topological transition point , which may be used to experimentally detect topological solitons .    </S>",
