    "<S> we investigate optimal discrimination between two projective single - qubit measurements in a scenario where the measurement can be performed only once . </S>",
    "<S> we consider general setting involving a tunable fraction of inconclusive outcomes and we prove that the optimal discrimination strategy requires an entangled probe state for any nonzero rate of inconclusive outcomes . </S>",
    "<S> we experimentally implement this optimal discrimination strategy for projective measurements on polarization states of single photons . </S>",
    "<S> our setup involves a real - time electrooptical feed - forward loop which allows us to fully harness the benefits of entanglement in discrimination of quantum measurements . </S>",
    "<S> the experimental data clearly demonstrate the advantage of entanglement - based discrimination strategy as compared to unentangled single - qubit probes . </S>"
