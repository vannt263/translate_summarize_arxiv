    "<S> we study a nanofabricated silicon rod levitated in an optical trap . by manipulating the polarization of the light we gain full control over the ro - translational dynamics of the rod . </S>",
    "<S> we are able to trap both its centre - of - mass and align it along the linear polarization of the laser field . </S>",
    "<S> the rod can be set into rotation at a tuned frequency by exploiting the radiation pressure exerted by elliptically polarized light . </S>",
    "<S> the rotational motion of the rod dynamically modifies the optical potential , which allows tuning of the rotational frequency over hundreds of kilohertz . </S>",
    "<S> this ability to trap and control the motion and alignment of nanoparticles opens up the field of rotational optomechanics , rotational ground state cooling and the study of rotational thermodynamics in the underdamped regime .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction _ nanofabrication has advanced all areas of science , technology and medicine @xcite , including the field of optomechanics , where the motion of a mechanical oscillator is controlled by light . </S>",
    "<S> the quantum ground state of motion has been reached in optomechanical crystal devices @xcite , and superconducting microwave circuits @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> ground - state cooling enables the coherent transduction of signals @xcite , the production of non - classical states of light and matter @xcite , and the ultra - sensitive detection of motion @xcite and forces @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> coherent optomechanical technology is limited by the coupling between the mechanical device and its environment , which leads to decoherence of quantum states , and by a reduction in mechanical quality factor due to clamping forces on the oscillator .    </S>",
    "<S> these limitations can be overcome by optically levitating the mechanical system , such that it oscillates in a harmonic trapping potential . </S>",
    "<S> optical trapping is applicable from atoms in vacuum @xcite , to complex organisms in liquid @xcite . by optically levitating nanoscale objects in vacuum , ultra - high mechanical quality factors ( @xmath0 ) </S>",
    "<S> are predicted @xcite , and it may be possible to generate macroscopic quantum superpositions @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> such massive quantum systems could test the limits of quantum physics @xcite , looking for the existence of new mechanisms of wave - function collapse such as spontaneous localisation @xcite or gravitational effects @xcite .     </S>",

    "<S> conclusions _ in summary , we present a method to capture and levitate nanofabricated silicon nanorods at low pressures , working with telecom wavelengths in a fiber - based setup . </S>",
    "<S> we can precisely control the length and diameter of our nanorods , meaning we can tailor rods to attain particular trapping and rotational frequencies . </S>",
