    "<S> hlder - brascamp - lieb inequalities provide upper bounds for a class of multilinear expressions , in terms of @xmath0 norms of the functions involved . </S>",
    "<S> they have been extensively studied for functions defined on euclidean spaces . </S>",
    "<S> bennett - carbery - christ - tao have initiated the study of these inequalities for discrete abelian groups and , in terms of suitable data , have characterized the set of all tuples of exponents for which such an inequality holds for specified data , as the convex polyhedron defined by a particular finite set of affine inequalities .    </S>",
    "<S> in this paper we advance the theory of such inequalities for torsion - free discrete abelian groups in three respects . </S>",
    "<S> the optimal constant in any such inequality is shown to equal @xmath1 whenever it is finite . </S>",
    "<S> an algorithm that computes the admissible polyhedron of exponents is developed . </S>",
    "<S> it is shown that nonetheless , existence of an algorithm that computes the full list of inequalities in the bennett - carbery - christ - tao description of the admissible polyhedron for all data , is equivalent to an affirmative solution of hilbert s tenth problem over the rationals . </S>",
    "<S> that problem remains open . </S>"
