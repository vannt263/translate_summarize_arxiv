    "<S> we investigate , in the framework of perturbation theory at finite @xmath0 , the effectiveness of improved gauge actions in suppressing the chiral violations of domain - wall fermions . </S>",
    "<S> our calculations show substantial reductions of the residual mass when it is compared at the same value of the gauge coupling , the largest suppression being obtained when the dbw2 action is used . </S>",
    "<S> similar effects can also be observed for a power - divergent mixing coefficient which is chirally suppressed . </S>",
    "<S> no significant reduction instead can be seen in the case of the difference between the vector and axial - vector renormalization constants when improved gauge actions are used in place of the plaquette action . </S>",
    "<S> we also find that one - loop perturbation theory is not an adequate tool to carry out comparisons at the same energy scale ( of about 2 gev ) , and in fact in this case even an enhancement of the chiral violations is frequently obtained . </S>"
