    "<S> we propose and demonstrate a new approach for realizing spin - orbit coupling with ultracold atoms . </S>",
    "<S> we use orbital levels in a double - well potential as pseudospin states . </S>",
    "<S> two - photon raman transitions between left and right wells induce spin - orbit coupling . </S>",
    "<S> this scheme does not require near resonant light , features adjustable interactions by shaping the double - well potential , and does not depend on special properties of the atoms . </S>",
    "<S> a pseudospinor bose - einstein condensate spontaneously acquires an antiferromagnetic pseudospin texture which breaks the lattice symmetry similar to a supersolid .            </S>",
    "<S> spin - orbit coupling is the mechanism for many intriguing phenomena , including @xmath0 topological insulators , the spin quantum hall effect  @xcite , majorana fermions  @xcite , and spintronics devices  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> realizing controllable spin - orbit coupling with ultracold atoms should make it feasible to explore fundamental aspects of topology in physics and applications in quantum computing  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> spin - orbit coupling requires the atom s motion to be dependent on its spin state . </S>",
    "<S> spin - orbit coupling without spin flips is possible for schemes which are diagonal in the spin component @xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> such spin - dependent vector potentials , which are sufficient for realizing quantum spin hall physics and topological insulators , can be engineered using far - detuned laser beams to completely suppress spontaneous emission  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> however , spin flips ( i.e. , spin - orbit coupling terms involving @xmath2 or @xmath3 operators ) are necessary for rashba  @xcite and dresselhaus  @xcite spin - orbit coupling  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> experiments with ultracold atoms couple pseudospin states using optical dipole transitions , which couple only to orbital angular momentum of the atom . </S>",
    "<S> most realizations , including the first demonstration  @xcite , use hyperfine states of an alkali atom as pseudospins . in this case </S>",
