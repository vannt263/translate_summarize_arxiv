    "<S> linear optical circuits of growing complexity are playing an increasing role in emerging photonic quantum technologies . </S>",
    "<S> individual photonic devices are typically described by a unitary matrix containing amplitude and phase information , the characterisation of which is a key task . </S>",
    "<S> we present a constructive scheme to retrieve the unitary matrix describing an arbitrary linear optical device using data obtained from one - photon and two - photon ensembles . </S>",
    "<S> the scheme is stable on the arbitrarily increasable length scale of the photon packet and independent of photon loss at input and output ports of the device . </S>",
    "<S> we find a one - to - one correspondence between ideal data and unitary matrix , and identify the class of non - unitary matrices capable of reproducing the data . </S>",
    "<S> the method is extended for coherent state probes , which can simulate two - photon statistics with a reduced visibility . </S>",
    "<S> we analyse the performance of reconstruction to simulated noise .    </S>",
    "<S> photonic circuitry is set to play an important role in emerging quantum technologies @xcite , connecting or implementing quantum logic gates @xcite , enabling photonic simulators @xcite , and coding states for quantum communication @xcite , with integrated optics @xcite enabling large reconfigurable circuits @xcite . in these contexts , </S>",
    "<S> each lossless linear optical network is ideally described by a unitary matrix @xcite , the determination of which is a key task . </S>",
    "<S> while a general quantum optical process may be fully characterised with coherent light and homodyne detection @xcite , the device to be determined and the probing set - up must be stable with respect to one another on a sub - wavelength scale .    </S>",
    "<S> since the phases imparted to each photon of a two - photon state combine to a single global phase , circuit probing via two - photon interferometry @xcite is stable on the scale of the photon packet , which is typically orders of magnitude greater than wavelength . </S>",
    "<S> photons generated from spontaneous parametric down conversion ( spdc ) can be lengthened through spectral filtering or with a cavity in narrowband spdc @xcite , while photons generated in an atom - cavity system can be many meters long @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> two - photon sources are commonplace in quantum optics labs , with quantum interference visibilities approaching unity @xcite , and future many - photon experiments are likely to be easily configured to produce subsets of data from one - photon and two - photon ensembles for characterisation purposes . </S>",
