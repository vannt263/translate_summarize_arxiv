    "<S> introduction_protected surface states are the key characteristic of topological phases of matter . </S>",
    "<S> time reversal invariant topological insulators ( ti ) host at their surface two - dimensional ( 2d ) massless dirac quasiparticles protected by time reversal symmetry ( @xmath0 )  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> weyl semimetals ( wsm ) are 3d gapless materials described at low energy by weyl fermions . </S>",
    "<S> they host topologically robust surface states referred to as fermi arcs since they form an open fermi surface  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> their emergence can be understood in terms of charge conservation ( gauge invariance ) of the effective field theory describing the wsm s response to external electromagnetic fields , in analogy with the quantum hall effect @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> such an effective response predicts a number of striking physical properties , such as a finite hall conductivity  @xcite , a current parallel to an external magnetic field ( chiral magnetic effect )  @xcite , and a finite angular momentum induced by a thermal gradient ( axial magnetic effect ) @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the band structure of a wsm is characterized by a linear dispersion around a set of non - degenerate band touchings points called weyl nodes . </S>",
    "<S> their existence requires the breaking of either time reversal @xmath0 or inversion @xmath1 symmetry  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> each weyl node is chiral and has an associated momentum space berry flux that gives it the character of a berry flux monopole . </S>",
    "<S> since the total flux in momentum space is required to be zero by gauge invariance  @xcite , the weyl nodes must appear in pairs with opposite monopole charge  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> they are therefore topologically stable as they can only be annihilated by bringing together a pair with opposite chirality  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the simplest realization of a topological semimetal , one exhibiting only a single pair of nodes , necessarily breaks time - reversal symmetry . </S>",
    "<S> indeed , @xmath0 symmetry connects two weyl nodes with the _ same _ monopole charge  @xcite , implying the existence of at least another pair with opposite chirality  @xcite . from this symmetry perspective , the coexistence of 2d dirac ti surface states and pairs of fermi arcs is in principled allowed if @xmath0 symmetry is respected , as inferred from ab - initio calculations  @xcite . </S>",
