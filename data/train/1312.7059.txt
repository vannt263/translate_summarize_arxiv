    "the meissner effect is a fundamental property of superconductors as shown in standard textbooks  @xcite .",
    "the response of superconductors is usually diamagnetic because a superconductor excludes weak enough magnetic fields from its interior .",
    "the anomalous paramagnetic response , however , has been observed in small disks of metallic superconductor  @xcite , small high-@xmath0 compounds  @xcite , and mesoscopic proximity structures  @xcite . the spatial inhomogeneity of the magnetic property is a key feature to realize the paramagnetic phase . in metallic superconductors ,",
    "the inhomogeneous distribution of magnetic fields  @xcite and the formation of giant vortex are responsible for the paramagnetic meissner effect pme  @xcite .",
    "the presence of the @xmath1 junctions is also pointed out as an origin of pme in a network of josephson junction  @xcite . in unconventional superconductors ( uss ) , on the other hand , an experiment  @xcite has shown the decrease of the pair density with decreasing temperature , which suggests a peculiar mechanism of the pme unique to the uss . as a result of the topological nature in the wave function ,",
    "the uss have the topologically protected surface andreev bound states ( abss ) at the zero - energy  @xcite .",
    "so far theoretical studies have shown that the magnetic response at the ( 110 ) surface of high-@xmath0 superconductor is nonlinear  @xcite and paramagnetic  @xcite due to the abss .",
    "the paramagnetic response has been mainly explained in terms of the energetics of the abss .",
    "weak magnetic fields shift the energy of the surface abss away from the fermi level and decrease the total energy of superconductor , which leads to the paramagnetic response or the paramagnetic instability",
    ". however , there is an important open question : what carries the large paramagnetic supercurrent ? by addressing this issue , we will conclude that the magnetic properties of uss are intrinsically inhomogeneous and that small uss can be paramagnetic at low temperature .",
