    "<S> we derive spin - dominated waveforms ( sdw ) for binary systems composed of spinning black holes with unequal masses ( less than @xmath0 ) . </S>",
    "<S> such systems could be formed by an astrophysical black hole with a smaller black hole or a neutron star companion ; and typically arise for supermassive black hole encounters . </S>",
    "<S> sdw characterize the last stages of the inspiral , when the larger spin dominates over the orbital angular momentum ( while the spin of the smaller companion can be neglected ) . </S>",
    "<S> they emerge as a double expansion in the post - newtonian parameter @xmath1 and the ratio @xmath2 of the orbital angular momentum and dominant spin . </S>",
    "<S> the sdw amplitudes are presented to ( @xmath3 ) orders , while the phase of the gravitational waves to ( @xmath4 ) orders ( omitting the highest order mixed terms ) . to this accuracy </S>",
    "<S> the amplitude includes the ( leading order ) spin - orbit contributions , while the phase the ( leading order ) spin - orbit , self - spin and mass quadrupole - monopole contributions . </S>",
    "<S> while the sdw hold for any mass ratio smaller than @xmath0 , lower bounds for the mass ratios are derived from the best sensitivity frequency range expected for advanced ligo ( giving @xmath5 ) , the einstein telescope ( @xmath6 ) , the lagrange ( @xmath7 ) and lisa missions ( @xmath8 ) , respectively . </S>"
