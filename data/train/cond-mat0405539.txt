    "<S> we calculate the x - ray diffraction spectrum produced by a pinned charge density wave ( cdw ) . </S>",
    "<S> the signature of the presence of a cdw consists of two satellite peaks , asymmetric as a consequence of disorder . </S>",
    "<S> the shape and the intensity of these peaks are determined in the case of a collective weak pinning using the variational method . </S>",
    "<S> we predict divergent asymmetric peaks , revealing the presence of a bragg glass phase . </S>",
    "<S> we deal also with the long range coulomb interactions , concluding that both peak divergence and anisotropy are enhanced . </S>",
    "<S> finally we discuss how to detect experimentally the bragg glass phase in the view of the role played by the finite resolution of measurements .    </S>",
    "<S> the study of disordered elastic objects sheds light on the physics of a wide range of systems . a first class of systems is accurately modeled by elastic manifolds in the presence of randomness ; significant examples include domain walls in magnetic @xcite or ferroelectric @xcite materials , contact lines of liquid menisci on a rough substrate @xcite and propagating cracks in solids @xcite . a second class of disordered elastic systems is given by periodic structures such as charge density waves ( cdws ) @xcite , vortex flux lines in type - ii superconductors @xcite and wigner crystals @xcite </S>",
    "<S> it was recently shown that periodic systems have unique properties , quite different from the ones of the interfaces . </S>",
    "<S> in fact , if topological defects ( i.e. dislocations etc . ) in the crystal are excluded , displacements can grow only logarithmically @xcite , in contrast with the power - law growth of interfaces . </S>",
    "<S> the positional order is only algebraically destroyed @xcite , leading to divergent bragg peaks and a nearly perfect crystal state . </S>",
    "<S> quite remarkably , it was shown that for weak disorder this solution is _ stable _ to the proliferation of topological defects , and thus that a thermodynamically stable phase having both glassy properties and quasi - long range positional order exists @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this phase , nicknamed _ </S>",
    "<S> bragg glass _ , has prompted many further analytical and experimental studies ( see e.g. ref .  </S>",
    "<S> [ ] for reviews and further references ) . </S>",
    "<S> although its existence can be tested indirectly by the consequences on the phase diagram of vortex flux lines , the most direct proof is to measure the predicted algebraic decay of the positional order . </S>",
    "<S> such a measurement can be done by means of diffraction experiments , using either neutrons or x - rays . </S>",
    "<S> neutron diffraction experiments have recently provided unambiguous evidences @xcite of the existence of the bragg glass phase for vortex lattices .    </S>",
    "<S> another periodic system in which one can expect a bragg glass to occur are cdws @xcite , where the electronic density shows a sinusoidal modulation . as a consequence of the electron - phonon interaction , this modulations generates a permanent distortion of the underlying lattice . </S>",
    "<S> this distortion can be revealed thanks to x - ray measurements : in fact , the corresponding x - ray spectrum presents satellite peaks around each principal bragg peak . </S>",
    "<S> these satellites contain information concerning the positional order of the cdw . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , we are interested in the detection of effects due to disorder @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the x - ray experimental resolution is in principle much higher than the one that can be achieved by neutrons for vortex lattices , consequently cdw systems should be prime candidates to check for the existence of a bragg glass state @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , compared to the case of vortex lattices the interpretation of the spectrum is much more complicated for two main reasons : ( i ) the phase of the cdw is the object described by an elastic energy , whereas the x - rays probe the displacements of the atoms in the crystal lattice ( essentially a cosine of the phase ) ; ( ii ) since the impurities substitute for some atoms of the crystal , the very presence of the impurities changes the x - ray spectrum . </S>",
    "<S> this gives rise to non - trivial terms of interference between disorder and atomic displacements @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> it is thus necessary to make a detailed theoretical analysis of the diffraction due to a pinned cdw .    in the past , </S>",
    "<S> the study of the spectrum has been carried out only either for strong pinning or at high temperatures @xcite . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we focus on the low temperature limit , where a well formed cdw exists , and on weak disorder , for which one expects to be in the bragg glass regime . </S>",
    "<S> both the short and long range screening of the coulomb interactions are considered . </S>",
    "<S> we show that in both cases the diffraction spectrum consists in two asymmetric peaks . </S>",
    "<S> the peaks are power - law divergent , with a stronger anisotropic shape in the case of unscreened long - range coulomb interaction . </S>",
    "<S> this finding is consistent with the bragg glass behavior @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the asymmetry divergence follows a subdominant power - law as well , with an exponent that we determine . </S>",
