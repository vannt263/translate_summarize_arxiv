    "it has been widely known that quantum computation enormously promotes computational efficiency by using several basic and purely physical features of quantum mechanics , such as coherent superposition , quantum entanglement , measurement collapse etc . @xcite .",
    "the accelerant ability of quantum computation is related to quantum entanglement and tensor product structure , which are essential to allow growing exponentially the computation resource with the number of qubits @xcite .",
    "yet the practical quantum computations are difficult to be realized for restrictions of quantum system controllability , decoherence property and measurement randomness knill , nielsen2,browne , kok .",
    "the classical simulation of quantum systems , especially of quantum entanglement has been under investigation for a long time  cerf , massar , spreeuw .",
    "in addition to easy implementations , the researches on the classical simulations can help understand some fundamental concepts in quantum mechanics .",
    "however , it has been pointed out by several researchers that the classical simulation of quantum systems requires exponentially scaling of physical resources with the number of quantum particles @xcite . in ref .",
    "@xcite , an optical analogy to quantum systems was introduced , in which the number of light beams and optical components required grows exponentially with the number of qubits . in ref .",
    "@xcite , a classical protocol to efficiently simulate any pure - state quantum computation is presented , yet the amount of entanglement involved is restricted . in ref .",
    "@xcite , it is elucidated that in classical theory , the state space of a composite system is the direct product of subsystems , whereas in quantum theory it is the tensor product .",
    "it is generally accepted that the essential distinction between direct and tensor products is precise the phenomenon of quantum entanglement , and regarded as the origin of the limitation of any classical systems . recently",
    ", several researches have proposed a new concept of realization of classical entanglement based on classical optical fields by introducing a new degree of freedom , such as orbital angular momentum , to realize tensor product in quantum entanglement @xcite .",
    "however , the method can not provide enough orthogonal degrees of freedom , which the scalability might be doubtful .    in this paper , we propose an optical parallel computation similar to quantum computation that can be realized by introducing pseudorandom phase sequences into optical fields with two orthogonal modes @xcite .",
    "the two orthogonal modes ( polarization or transverse ) of optical field are encoded as optical analogies to quantum bits @xmath2  and  @xmath3 @xcite . in wireless and optical communications ,",
    "orthogonal pseudorandom sequences have been widely applied to code division multiple access ( cdma ) communication technology as a way to distinguish different users @xcite .",

    "in this paper , we have discussed a new approach to imitate quantum states using the optical fields modulated with ppss .",
    "we demonstrated that @xmath1 optical fields modulated with @xmath1  different ppss can span a @xmath0 dimensional hilbert space that contains tensor product structure similar to quantum systems .",
    "it is noteworthy that a classical optical field  is the most similar to a quantum state , especially for coherent superposition state .",
    "this is why the space spanned by optical fields can imitate a quantum system yet not the space of probability distributions of classical coins that also contains a tensor product .    in this paper",

    "it is particularly interesting to simulate higher - dimensional real quantum systems applying this approach , such as qutrits , higher - dimensional hilbert spaces , even quantum fields .",

    "<S> we propose an optical parallel computation similar to quantum computation that can be realized by introducing pseudorandom phase sequences into classical optical fields with two orthogonal modes . based on the pseudorandom phase sequences , </S>",
    "<S> we first propose a theoretical framework of phase ensemble model  referring from the concept of quantum ensemble . </S>",
    "<S> using the ensemble model , we further demonstrate the inseparability of the fields similar to quantum entanglement . </S>",
    "<S> it is interesting that a @xmath0 dimensional hilbert space spanned by @xmath1 optical fields is larger than that spanned by @xmath1 quantum particles . </S>",
    "<S> this leads a problem for our scheme that is not the lack of resources but the redundancy of resources . in order to reduce the redundancy , </S>",
    "<S> we propose a special sequence permutation mechanism to efficiently imitate certain quantum states , including the product state , bell states , ghz state and w state . for better fault tolerance </S>",
    "<S> , we further devise each orthogonal mode of optical fields is measured to assign discrete values . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we propose a generalized gate array model to imitate some quantum algorithms , such as shor s algorithm , grover s algorithm and quantum fourier algorithm . </S>",
    "<S> the research on the optical parallel computation might be important , for it not only has the potential beyond quantum computation , but also provides useful insights into fundamental concepts of quantum mechanics . </S>"
