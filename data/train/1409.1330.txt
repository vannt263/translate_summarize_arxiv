    "<S> we identify the existence of various symmetry - protected topological states in one - dimensional superlattices with periodically modulated hopping amplitudes or on - site potentials , which can be characterized by the quantized berry phase @xmath0 or the emergence of a pair of degenerate boundary states . </S>",
    "<S> it is shown that there may exist three types of topological phases , which are protected by the inversion symmetry , the chiral symmetry , and both of them , respectively , depending on the modulations , the odd or even modulation period . </S>",
    "<S> the connection between the hopping and potential modulations is also discussed . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , we demonstrate that the topological phase protected by the inversion symmetry can be realized in the interacting boson systems trapped in the same superlattices . </S>",
    "<S> the results are very possibly studied experimentally in the superlattice systems engineered with state - of - art technologies .            _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction.- _ the one - dimensional ( 1d ) topological phases have attracted intense recent studies due to the experimental progress in hybrid superconductor - semiconductor wires @xcite , photonic crystals @xcite and cold atomic gases @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the classification of 1d free fermion systems covering the time - reversal symmetry , particle - hole symmetry and chiral symmetry has been established and five out of ten symmetry classes are topological @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the inversion symmetry has also been included and the classification is considerably modified , such as : the ai class becomes topological ; the topological invariant of the bdi class is replaced by a @xmath1 number @xcite . a generalization of the free fermion result to interacting cases has also been obtained for 1d systems @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> many efforts are devoted to construct models belonging to different classes in order to study different kinds of topological properties @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> while the theory of topological classifications indicates permitted types of topological states , existing topological orders in realistic physical systems are rare . due to their good tunability </S>",
    "<S> , the 1d optical and photonic superlattice systems provide an ideal toolbox for exploring topologically nontrivial states @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> particularly , recent studies of 1d superlattice and quasiperiodic systems from the topological viewpoint @xcite have unveiled the relation between these systems and two - dimensional ( 2d ) topological insulators @xcite , which has been experimentally confirmed by using optical waveguides @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> these studies stimulated the exploration of topological phases in 1d superlattice systems @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> nevertheless , it is worth indicating that most previous studied topological phases in 1d superlattice systems do not belong to standard topological classification of the tenfold way , i.e. , they are generally not symmetry - protected topological ( spt ) states as these states are characterized by topological invariants of the 2d parameter space through dimensional extension @xcite .    in this work , </S>",
    "<S> we explore spt states in 1d superlattices with periodically modulated hopping amplitudes or on - site potentials , and identify a series of topological phases protected by the inversion symmetry , the chiral symmetry , and both of them , respectively . </S>",
    "<S> all these spt states are characterized by the presence of a pair of degenerate in - gap boundary states or nontrivial quantized berry phase . </S>",
    "<S> for the systems with periodically modulated hopping amplitudes , the odd modulations only generate the inverse - symmetry - protected topological states , while all three types of spt states can exist in the even modulation systems . </S>",
    "<S> the systems with periodical potentials only support the topological states protected by the inversion symmetry . </S>",
    "<S> it is interesting that topological states protected solely by the inversion symmetry corresponds to the crossings of edge modes in the parameter space of the modulation phase , whereas spt states with the chiral symmetry exists in a much wider regime of the parameter space . </S>",
    "<S> it is also found that 1d bosonic topological phases protected by the inversion symmetry can be directly realized by loading the interacting bosons into the above lattices . due to the simplicity of our models and the sophisticated technologies of manipulating optical lattices and photonic crystals , </S>",
    "<S> the 1d superlattice systems are thus expected to be an experimentally accessible platform demonstrating rich spt phases .    _ model with </S>",
