    "<S> cohomology and cohomology ring of three - dimensional ( 3d ) objects are topological invariants that characterize holes and their relations . </S>",
    "<S> cohomology ring has been traditionally computed on simplicial complexes . </S>",
    "<S> nevertheless , cubical complexes deal directly with the voxels in 3d images , no additional triangulation is necessary , facilitating efficient algorithms for the computation of topological invariants in the image context . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> , we present formulas to directly compute the cohomology ring of 3d cubical complexes without making use of any additional triangulation . </S>",
    "<S> starting from a cubical complex @xmath0 that represents a 3d binary - valued digital picture whose foreground has one connected component , we compute first the cohomological information on the boundary of the object , @xmath1 by an incremental technique ; then , using a face reduction algorithm , we compute it on the whole object ; finally , applying the mentioned formulas , the cohomology ring is computed from such information .    </S>",
