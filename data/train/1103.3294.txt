    "<S> we present a general framework to study the thermodynamic denaturation of double - stranded dna under superhelical stress . </S>",
    "<S> we report calculations of position- and size - dependent opening probabilities for bubbles along the sequence . </S>",
    "<S> our results are obtained from transfer - matrix solutions of the zimm - bragg model for unconstrained dna and of a self - consistent linearization of the benham model for superhelical dna . </S>",
    "<S> the numerical efficiency of our method allows for the analysis of entire genomes and of random sequences of corresponding length ( @xmath0 base pairs ) . </S>",
    "<S> we show that , at physiological conditions , opening in superhelical dna is strongly cooperative with average bubble sizes of @xmath1 base pairs ( bp ) , and orders of magnitude higher than in unconstrained dna . in heterogeneous sequences , </S>",
    "<S> the average degree of base - pair opening is self - averaging , while bubble localization and statistics are dominated by sequence disorder . </S>",
    "<S> compared to random sequences with identical gc - content , genomic dna has a significantly increased probability to open large bubbles under superhelical stress . </S>",
    "<S> these bubbles are frequently located directly upstream of transcription start sites . </S>"
