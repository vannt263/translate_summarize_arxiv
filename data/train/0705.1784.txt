    "<S> a self - contained approach to studying the unitary evolution of coupled qubits is introduced , capable of addressing a variety of physical systems described by exchange hamiltonians containing rabi terms . </S>",
    "<S> the method automatically determines both the weyl chamber steering trajectory and the accompanying local rotations . </S>",
    "<S> particular attention is paid to the case of anisotropic exchange with tracking controls , which is solved analytically . </S>",
    "<S> it is shown that , if computational subspace is well isolated , any exchange interaction can always generate high - fidelity , single - step controlled - not ( cnot ) logic , provided that both qubits can be individually manipulated . </S>",
    "<S> the results are then applied to superconducting qubit architectures , for which several cnot gate implementations are identified . </S>",
    "<S> the paper concludes with consideration of two cnot gate designs having high efficiency and operating with no significant leakage to higher - lying non - computational states . </S>"
