    "superconductivity expels magnetic flux from the interior of a solid , while ferromagnetism generates it , thus both phenomena are antagonistic . moreover , ferromagnetic order is usually detrimental to superconductivity because strong internal fields from aligned moments break cooper pairs",
    ". nevertheless , both phenomena are not mutually exclusive in all cases .",
    "after early investigations of alloys with magnetic rare earth atoms diluted in superconducting lanthanum metal @xcite , the first superconductors with spatially ordered arrays of magnetic atoms were the metallic molybdenum sulphides @xmath11mo@xmath12s@xmath13 ( @xmath11 = rare earth element ) , referred to as the chevrel phases @xcite . among them , compounds with the strongly magnetic rare earth elements tb - er have superconducting critical temperatures ( @xmath14 ) around 2 k , and enter magnetically ordered states between 15 mk and 5 mk @xcite .",
    "a further example is errh@xmath15b@xmath15 where ferromagnetism destroys superconductivity at 1 k , while co - existence with antiferromagnetic ordering has been found in the borocarbides @xmath11ni@xmath16b@xmath16c @xcite and the ruthenate rusr@xmath16gdcu@xmath16o@xmath13 @xcite .",
    "recently , the co - existence of superconductivity with ferromagnetic ordering of eu@xmath17 ions in the iron arsenides eufe@xmath16(as@xmath0p@xmath1)@xmath16 and eu(fe@xmath0ru@xmath1)@xmath16as@xmath16 has been reported @xcite .",
    "such materials where the ferromagnetic ordering temperature @xmath18 is below @xmath14 are called ferromagnetic superconductors .",
    "therein , both phenomena are usually spatially decoupled , and do not interact directly in the sense that the same electrons are responsible for both .",
    "the latter is discussed in superconducting ferromagnets with @xmath19 . here",
    "the superconducting state emerges in a ferromagnetic metal ( usually at mk temperatures ) , which gives evidence for exotic mechanisms like spin triplet pairing , for example in uge@xmath16 or urhge which have been intensively studied @xcite .",
    "so far the extremely low temperatures , as well as the inertness of the rare earth 4@xmath20 shell hardly allowed chemical manipulation of these quite fascinating phenomena .",
    "this would be different if the ferromagnetic ordering emerges from @xmath10-elements , where the magnetic state is much more susceptible to the chemical environment .",
