    "<S> we study generalised additive models , with shape restrictions ( e.g. monotonicity , convexity , concavity ) imposed on each component of the additive prediction function . </S>",
    "<S> we show that this framework facilitates a nonparametric estimator of each additive component , obtained by maximising the likelihood . </S>",
    "<S> the procedure is free of tuning parameters and under mild conditions is proved to be uniformly consistent on compact intervals . </S>",
    "<S> more generally , our methodology can be applied to generalised additive index models . here again , the procedure can be justified on theoretical grounds and , like the original algorithm , possesses highly competitive finite - sample performance . </S>",
    "<S> practical utility is illustrated through the use of these methods in the analysis of two real datasets . </S>",
    "<S> our algorithms are publicly available in the ` r ` package ` scar ` , short for * * s**hape-**c**onstrained * * a**dditive * * r**egression .    * </S>",
    "<S> keywords * : generalised additive models , index models , nonparametric maximum likelihood estimation , shape constraints . </S>"
