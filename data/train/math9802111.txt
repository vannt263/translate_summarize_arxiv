    "<S> motivated by these works and by kashiwara s theory of crystal bases we define a statistic on paths yielding two novel classes of polynomials . </S>",
    "<S> one of these provides a generalization of the kostka polynomials while the other , which we name the a@xmath0 supernomial , is a @xmath1-deformation of the expansion coefficients of products of schur polynomials . </S>",
    "<S> many well - known results for kostka polynomials are extended leading to representations of our polynomials in terms of a charge statistic on littlewood  </S>",
    "<S> richardson tableaux and in terms of fermionic configuration sums . </S>",
    "<S> several identities for the generalized kostka polynomials and the a@xmath0 supernomials are proven or conjectured . finally , a connection between the supernomials and bailey s lemma is made . </S>"
