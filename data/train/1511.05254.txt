    "<S> we introduce a statistical model for the problem of finding a subgraph with specified topology in an otherwise random graph . </S>",
    "<S> this task plays an important role in the analysis of social and biological networks . in these type of networks , </S>",
    "<S> small subgraphs with a specific structure have important functional roles .    within our model , a single copy of a subgraph is added ( ` planted ' ) in an erds - renyi random graph with @xmath0 vertices and edge probability @xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> we ask whether the resulting graph can be distinguished reliably from a pure erds - renyi random graph , and present two types of result . </S>",
    "<S> first we investigate the question from a purely statistical perspective , and ask whether there is _ any _ test that can distinguish between the two graph models . </S>",
    "<S> we provide necessary and sufficient conditions that are essentially tight for subgraphs of size asymptotically smaller than @xmath2 .    </S>",
    "<S> next we study two polynomial - time algorithms for solving the same problem : a spectral algorithm , and a semidefinite programming ( sdp ) relaxation . for the spectral algorithm , we establish sufficient conditions under which it distinguishes the two graph models with high probability . under the same conditions the spectral algorithm indeed identifies the hidden subgraph .    </S>",
    "<S> the spectral algorithm is substantially sub - optimal with respect to the optimal test . </S>",
    "<S> we show that a similar gap is present for the sdp approach . </S>",
    "<S> this points at a large gap between statistical and computational limits for this problem . </S>"
