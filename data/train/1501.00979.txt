    "<S> we explore the phase diagram of interacting spin-@xmath0 systems in the presence of anisotropic interactions , spontaneous decay and driving . </S>",
    "<S> we find a rich phase diagram featuring a limit cycle phase in which the magnetization oscillates in time . </S>",
    "<S> we analyze the spatio - temporal fluctuations of this limit cycle phase at the gaussian level , and show that spatial fluctuations lead to quasi - long - range limit cycle ordering for dimension @xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> this result can be interpreted in terms of a spatio - temporal goldstone mode corresponding to phase fluctuations of the limit cycle . </S>",
    "<S> we also demonstrate that the limit - cycle phase exhibits an asymmetric power spectrum measurable in fluorescence experiments .    </S>",
    "<S> a quantum system that is coherently driven and connected to a heat bath eventually reaches a steady state ; when the system is macroscopic , this steady state can be ordered in the sense of spontaneously breaking a symmetry @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the patterns of steady - state ordering are , in general , qualitatively different from those of the equilibrium phase diagrams : for instance , the interplay between coherent and dissipative dynamics can stabilize staggered phases that are absent in equilibrium @xcite , and by engineering the dissipative terms one can optically pump a many - body system into a pure state @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the forms of steady - state order @xcite that have been investigated to date mostly fit into equilibrium _ paradigms _ </S>",
    "<S> , such as the landau paradigm of spontaneous symmetry breaking or the paradigm of topological order </S>",
    "<S> . however , the departure from equilibrium allows one to realize novel types of order  specifically , limit cycles ( lc ) @xcite which break time - translation invariance  that have no obvious equilibrium counterpart . </S>",
    "<S> although previous works have predicted a lc phase within mean - field theory @xcite , it is an open question whether such a phase really exists or is an artifact of mean - field theory .    in this work , </S>",
    "<S> we show that the lc phase exists with long - range order in three and higher dimensions and quasi - long - range order in two dimensions . </S>",
    "<S> we study a paradigm model of interacting spins  the anisotropic spin-1/2 heisenberg model in a transverse field  and find a regime where it exhibits a limit - cycle phase . </S>",
    "<S> we discuss the origin of the lc and study the effects of quantum fluctuations to go beyond previous mean - field works . </S>",
    "<S> we show by explicit , microscopic calculation that the spontaneous breaking of the continuous time - translation symmetry is reflected in the presence of a gapless `` goldstone '' mode , and that this gapless mode prevents global time - translation - symmetry - breaking in one or two dimensions . </S>",
    "<S> we then discuss this lc at a more phenomenological level , noting its unusual implications , e.g. , that the temporal ordering of the lc phase gives rise to an _ asymmetric _ dynamical power spectrum of emitted photons . </S>",
    "<S> these predictions are straightforward to test in experiments with trapped ions @xcite or rydberg atoms @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> we want to contrast our paper with recent works on time crystals which also spontaneously break time - translational symmetry @xcite . </S>",
