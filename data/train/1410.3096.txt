    "rule 54 is a discrete analog of an active , nonlinear one - dimensional medium .",
    "assume each cell is a micro - volume , which takes two states : resting ( 0 ) and excited ( 1 ) .",
    "when a single micro - volume is perturbed , its corresponding cell takes state 1 .",
    "the perturbation / activation spreads to neighbors of the initially excited micro - volume : @xmath23 .",
    "for example , the transition @xmath24 encodes the following activation mechanism : if the left neighbor of a resting cell is excited , the resting cell excites .",
    "transition @xmath25 indicates the simple fact that the medium could not activate itself ; that is , excitation can not develop from a totally resting medium .",
    "the three most interesting transitions are @xmath26 .",
    "they encode the following fact : if an excited micro - volume has at least one excited neighboring micro - volume , then this micro - volume returns to a resting state .",
    "this can be interpreted as a mutual inhibition .",

    "<S> the dynamics of rule 54 one - dimensional two - state cellular automaton ( ca ) are a discrete analog of a space - time dynamics of excitations in nonlinear active medium with mutual inhibition . </S>",
    "<S> a cell switches its state 0 to state 1 if one of its two neighbors is in state 1 ( propagation of a perturbation ) and a cell remains in state 1 only if its two neighbors are in state 0 . </S>",
    "<S> a lateral inhibition is because a 1-state neighbor causes a 1-state cell to switch to state 0 . </S>",
    "<S> the rule produces a rich spectrum of space - time dynamics , including gliders and glider guns just from four primitive gliders . </S>",
    "<S> we construct a catalogue of gliders and describe them by tiles . </S>",
    "<S> we calculate a subset of regular expressions @xmath0 to encode gliders . </S>",
    "<S> the regular expressions are derived from de bruijn diagrams , tile - based representation of gliders , and cycle diagrams sometimes . </S>",
    "<S> we construct an abstract machine that recognizes regular expressions of gliders in rule 54 and validate @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> we also propose a way to code initial configurations of gliders to depict any type of collision between the gliders and explore self - organization of gliders , formation of larger tiles , and soliton - like interactions of gliders and computable devices .     + paper published in _ complex systems _ , october , 2014 . </S>",
