    "<S> we consider a glauber - fock oscillator and show that diffraction can be managed . </S>",
    "<S> we show how to design arrays of waveguides where light beams experience zero diffraction . </S>",
    "<S> we find an exact analytical family of nondiffracting localized solution . </S>",
    "<S> we predict discrete parametric oscillation in the glauber - fock oscillator .    </S>",
    "<S> diffraction is a fundamental process in physics . </S>",
    "<S> it is well known that a wave is diffracted if it passes through an opening or if there is an obstacle in its path . </S>",
    "<S> diffraction leads to the broadening of an initial intensity profile in space . </S>",
    "<S> propagation - invariant waves have attracted a considerable interest over the years . </S>",
    "<S> the most familiar example of nondiffracting wave is that of a plane wave propagating in free space . </S>",
    "<S> other nontrivial nondiffracting waves for various physical systems have been intensively investigated by many authors . </S>",
    "<S> durnin introduced diffractionless ( nonspreading ) solution of the free - space scalar wave equation almost three decades ago @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> his solution is in the form of a bessel function in the transverse direction and the corresponding intensity profile remains invariant during propagation unlike other beams that spread during propagation . in the framework of quantum mechanics , only the plane wave had been known as a nondiffracting wave in 1-d before berry and balazs . </S>",
    "<S> they theoretically showed another nonspreading solution was available for the schrodinger equation describing a free particle @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> their solution is unique in the sense that it self - accelerates although no external potential exists . </S>",
    "<S> the accelerating behavior is not consistent with the ehrenfest theorem , which describes the motion of the center of mass of the wave packet . </S>",
    "<S> the reason why the ehrenfest theorem does nt work is because of the non - integrability of the airy function . + the physics of photon propagation is discrete lattices is very rich and has been extensively studied by many authors . nondiffracting waves in media such as waveguide or nonlinear materials are interesting to study . </S>",
    "<S> diffraction in such an array of waveguides is governed by hopping light from site to site through optical tunneling . </S>",
    "<S> discrete diffraction is different from continuous diffraction . as an example , if light is initially excited at only one waveguide of a 1-d periodic array of waveguide , then light spreads into two main lobes with several secondary peaks between them . </S>",
    "<S> the idea to control discrete diffraction has attracted a special attention . </S>",
    "<S> the case of optical field propagation in a linearly coupled , infinite array of one dimensional waveguides was considered in @xcite and anomalous diffraction ( negative discrete diffraction ) and diffraction - free cases were theoretically discussed and experimentally realized . </S>",
    "<S> discrete diffraction was shown to be controlled in size and sign by the input conditions and diffractionless beams and focusing of normally diverging beams were discussed in homogeneous waveguide arrays @xcite . the self - collimation effect where the spatial width of a light beam does not change over hundreds of free - space diffraction lengths </S>",
    "<S> was realized in a macroscobic photonic lattice @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> periodic photonic structures where the strength of diffraction can be made normal , anomalous or zero in a very broad frequency range was introduced @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> + one special system where discrete diffraction can be studied is the semi - infinite and asymmetric glauber fock lattice that has recently been introduced into optics community @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the system is composed of an array of evanescently coupled waveguides with a square - root distribution of the coupling between adjacent guides @xcite . the first experimental realization with a direct observation of the classical analogue of fock state displacements </S>",
    "<S> was presented in @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the glauber - fock photonic lattice is interesting in the sense that every excited waveguide represents a fock state and the system admits an exact analytical solution . in @xcite , the ermakov - lewis invariant is constructed for the glauber - fock oscillator with propagation distance dependent tunneling amplitude and refractive index gradients . </S>",
    "<S> some interesting effects can be observed in a glauber - fock system in the presence of the refractive index gradient , which was shown to be accomplished by varying the waveguide writing velocity of the femtosecond laser @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> for example both periodic collapses and revivals in a discrete glauber - fock oscillator were observed in the intensity evolution . </S>",
    "<S> it was shown that periodically changing tunneling amplitude leads to bloch - like oscillations and dynamic delocalization depending on the oscillation frequency and strength of refractive index gradient @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> it is interesting to observe the bloch - like oscillations in spite of the fact that the evanescently coupled waveguide array has nonuniform coupling and semi - infinite . </S>",
    "<S> dynamic localization and quantum self - imaging are other interesting effects that occur in periodical lattice . </S>",
    "<S> these two effects were theoretically discussed and shown to be possible in a glauber - fock oscillator @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> glauber - fock oscillator was shown to be engineered by the method of shortcuts to adiabaticity @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> recently , geometric phase for a glauber - fock oscillator lattice was measured @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> quantum rabi model based on light transport in two decoupled semi infinite binary tight binding photonic lattice with a square - root distribution of the coupling between neighboring sites was experimentally realized in @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the standard glauber - fock oscillator is semi - infinite and all the experiments mentioned above were realized in an effectively semi infinite system ( the dimension in the transverse direction is long enough ) . </S>",
    "<S> the effect of truncation in a finite glauber - fock oscillator was discussed in @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the glauber - fock oscillator was generalized to include the nonlinear interaction and the corresponding system was theoretically explored in @xcite . in this paper , we consider glauber - fock oscillator with propagation distance dependent tunneling amplitude and refractive index gradient . </S>",
