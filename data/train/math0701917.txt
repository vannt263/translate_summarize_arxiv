    "<S> we consider a branching model introduced by kimmel for cell division with parasite infection . </S>",
    "<S> cells contain proliferating parasites which are shared randomly between the two daughter cells when they divide . </S>",
    "<S> we determine the probability that the organism recovers , meaning that the asymptotic proportion of contaminated cells vanishes . </S>",
    "<S> we study the tree of contaminated cells , give the asymptotic number of contaminated cells and the asymptotic proportions of contaminated cells with a given number of parasites . </S>",
    "<S> this depends on domains inherited from the behavior of branching processes in random environment ( bpre ) and given by the bivariate value of the means of parasite offsprings . in one of these domains , </S>",
    "<S> the convergence of proportions holds in probability , the limit is deterministic and given by the yaglom quasistationary distribution . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , we get an interpretation of the limit of the q - process as the size - biased quasistationary distribution .    .    </S>"
