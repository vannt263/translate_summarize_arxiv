    "systems of piecewise - smooth , discontinuous odes are utilised in many areas to model phenomena involving switching events . except in special cases , the dynamical behaviour local to a point on a discontinuity surface conforms to one of three scenarios : crossing , stable sliding , or repelling .",
    "these are illustrated in fig .",
    "[ fig : schemfilippov ] . in the first scenario trajectories simply cross the discontinuity surface . in the second scenario",
    "the discontinuity surface attracts nearby trajectories .",
    "here filippov s solution @xcite may be used to define _ sliding motion _ on the discontinuity surface .",
    "sliding motion has been described in models of diverse phenomena , including oscillators subject to dry friction @xcite , relay control @xcite , and population dynamics @xcite . in the third scenario trajectories",
    "head away from the discontinuity surface from both sides . in this case",
    "forward evolution from a point on the discontinuity surface is ambiguous or non - unique and a differential inclusion may be used to define a set - valued solution @xcite .",
    "however , from an applied viewpoint this is typically not an important issue because there are no initial conditions for which forward evolution leads to the discontinuity surface .",

    "evolution from two - folds is generally ambiguous .",
    "however , unlike for repelling discontinuity surfaces , the ambiguity of forward evolution from a two - fold is a serious issue if many trajectories go into the two - fold .",
    "this is the case for the two - fold depicted in fig .",

    "dynamics local to generic two - folds in three dimensions have been systematically classified @xcite . in more than three dimensions , two - folds have similar properties @xcite .",
    "also , it has recently been shown that two - folds arise in models of simple circuit systems @xcite , and have deep connections to _ folded nodes _ of slow - fast systems @xcite .",
    "however , dynamical behaviour near two - folds should only be understood with the knowledge that as mathematical models nonsmooth equations only represent an approximation to reality .",
    "discontinuities are used to model events , such as impacts , that on an appropriately fine scale are not instantaneous and perhaps actually smooth , although highly nonlinear .",
    "model inaccuracies are crucial at sensitive regions of phase space ; indeed two - folds are points of extreme sensitivity .    to address this issue , in this paper physically motivated perturbations of a discontinuous ode system are analysed with the aim of resolving the ambiguity of forward evolution at two - folds . in order to obtain analytical results the scenario depicted in fig .",

    "the addition of noise to a system of nonsmooth odes with multiple solutions gives a stochastic system with a unique solution @xcite .",
    "in the zero - noise limit , this solution may represent a useful weighted selection of all possible solutions @xcite .",
    "recently such ideas have been extended and applied to transport equations for fluid dynamics @xcite .",
