    "the magnetism of conventional metal is composed of two different contributions , the spin component known as the pauli paramagnetism and the orbital component as the landau diamagnetism . in condensed matter systems ,",
    "the orbital magnetism sensitively depends on the detail of the electronic band structure , and sometimes largely deviates from the landau diamagnetism .",
    "particularly , narrow gap materials such as graphite@xcite or bismuth @xcite exhibit a singular behavior in the orbital susceptibility near the energy gap . in this paper , we show that the anomalous orbital magnetism in narrow gap systems can be understood in terms of pseudo - spin paramagnetism , which arises from the extra degree of freedom in the orbital motion of electrons .",
