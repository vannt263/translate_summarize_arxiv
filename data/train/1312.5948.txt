    "<S> we present experimental evidence for scale invariant behaviour of the excitation spectrum in phase - fluctuating quasi-1d bose gases after a rapid change of the external trapping potential . probing density correlations in free expansion , we find that the temperature of an initial thermal state scales with the spatial extension of the cloud as predicted by a model based on adiabatic rescaling of initial eigenmodes with conserved quasiparticle occupation numbers . based on this result </S>",
    "<S> , we demonstrate that shortcuts to adiabaticity for the rapid expansion or compression of the gas do not induce additional heating .    </S>",
    "<S> a systematic understanding of non - equilibrium dynamics in many - body quantum systems is a longstanding goal , with far - reaching applicability for many different fields of physics . </S>",
    "<S> ultracold atom experiments offer clean implementations of systems that are tunable , well isolated from the environment and theoretically tractable @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , the profound understanding available for the one - dimensional ( 1d ) bose gas makes it an ideal test bed for quantum many - body dynamics @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> tunable parameters in the system s hamiltonian allow the controlled preparation of non - equilibrium states @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the identification of characteristic scaling laws is an important step for the concise description of the subsequent dynamical processes . </S>",
    "<S> of particular importance are laws governing not only global parameters @xcite but ideally the full spectrum of excitations , as studied in recent experiments with 2d bose @xcite or tonks - girardeau gases @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> recent work @xcite has shown that a general scaling property of many - body wavefunctions holds exactly for a broad class of systems , including the weakly interacting 1d bose gas addressed in this letter . </S>",
    "<S> the existence of such a scaling solution is a consequence of a dynamical symmetry of the underlying hamiltonian . for an ultracold gas , fast changes of control parameters in the hamiltonian </S>",
    "<S> generally lead to quasiparticle production and heating @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the existence of a scaling solution for the full spectrum of quasiparticle modes implies that so - called shortcuts to adiabaticity ( sta ) @xcite can be engineered not only for the mean density profile of a 1d gas , but also for correlation properties of the system in certain regimes of interaction strength @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> we show in this work that the scaling solutions for a true many - body wavefunction have their counterpart in the hydrodynamic regime of our experimental system . </S>",
    "<S> we bring our system out of equilibrium by rapidly changing its longitudinal confinement . </S>",
    "<S> the subsequent system evolution gives insight into the scaling properties of the gas . </S>",
    "<S> this allows us to study the regimes and limits of such a manipulation , with an emphasis on sta schemes . </S>",
    "<S> we furthermore demonstrate for the first time that sta schemes are valid for the second - order correlation , and thereby the temperature , of weakly interacting 1d bose gases . </S>"
