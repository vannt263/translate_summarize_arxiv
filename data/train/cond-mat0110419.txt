    "we have shown that the interplay of phase - conjugation and multiple scattering by disorder leads to a drastic slowing down of the decay in time @xmath7 of the average power spectrum @xmath146 of frequency components @xmath8 of a reflected pulse .",
    "the slowing down exists in a narrow frequency range around the characteristic frequency @xmath36 of the phase - conjugating mirror ( degenerate regime ) . if @xmath8 is outside this frequency range ( non - degenerate regime ) , the power spectrum decays as rapidly as for a normal mirror .",
    "the slowing down can be interpreted in terms of a long - lived resonance at @xmath0 , that is induced in the random medium by the phase - conjugating mirror .",
    "this resonance is known from investigations of the static scattering properties @xcite .",
    "the resonance is exponentially narrow @xmath147 in the presence of localization ( with @xmath4 the scattering time , @xmath2 the length of the disordered region , and @xmath3 the mean free path ) .",
    "the resonance leads to the exponentially large differences in time scales for the decay of the power spectrum in the degenerate regime and the non - degenerate regime .",
    "we have restricted the calculation in this paper to the case of a single propagating mode , when a complete analytical theory could be provided .",
    "we expect that the @xmath9-mode case is qualitatively similar : an exponentially large difference in time scales @xmath148 for the decay in the degenerate and non - degenerate regimes provided the medium is localized [ @xmath2 large compared to the localization length @xmath14 ] . in the diffusive regime we expect @xmath150 to decay on the time scale of the diffusion time @xmath151 .",
    "the difference with the non - degenerate regime ( or a normal mirror ) is then a factor @xmath152 instead of exponentially large .    in final analysis",
    "we see that phase conjugation greatly magnifies the difference in the dynamics with and without localization . indeed , if there is no phase - conjugating mirror the main difference is a decay @xmath153 in the diffusive regime versus @xmath154 in the localized regime @xcite , but the characteristic time scale remains the same ( set by the scattering time @xmath4 ) .",
    "we therefore suggest that phase conjugation might be a promising tool in the ongoing experimental search for dynamical features of localization @xcite .",
