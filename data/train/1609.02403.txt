    "in recent years , the notion of parity  time ( @xmath0 ) symmetry has attracted considerable interest due to its potential applications in the fields of quantum optics and quantum information processing ( qip ) @xcite .",
    "since bender and boettcher proved that a @xmath0symmetric non  hermitian hamiltonian ( @xmath1=0 $ ] ) can also have real eigenvalue spectrum @xcite , realizing @xmath0symmetric complex quantum systems has become a rapidly developing issue in both theoretical and experimental researches @xcite . up to now ,",
    "non  hermitian  based complex quantum mechanics is still debated @xcite , however , attempting to test the @xmath0symmetry in open quantum systems or optical systems is effective .",
    "recent experiments have already demonstrated @xmath0symmetry behaviors in a variety of physical systems @xcite . among them , a simple and intuitive scheme is to link two quantum open systems with gain and loss respectively @xcite . as reported in refs .",
    "@xcite , experimentalists observed that a mode splitting between two supermodes will occur with degenerate effective dissipations once the coupling intensity passes through the exceptional point ( ep ) .",
    "ideally , the balanced gain and loss make the eigenvalues end up on the real axis .",
    "the system supermodes perform like a closed quantum system in this case , and some environmental damage effects can be suppressed by quantum gain .",
    "so far , similar mechanism has already been applied in lots of quantum investigations , including enhancing optics nonlinearity @xcite , enhancing photon blockade @xcite and realizing quantum chaos @xcite by an extra optical gain .",
    "although @xmath0symmetry has made progress in optimizing qip scheme , it is regrettable that common non  hermitian hamiltonian is not a complete quantum description for open gain / loss quantum systems @xcite . in previous works ,",
    "the gains were generally introduced by classical amplification effects ( e.g. parametric amplification and doped erbium ions for waveguides and microcavities ) @xcite . correspondingly , a dissipation non  hermitian hamiltonian can be deduced by adopting markovian quantum master equation after neglecting its the jump term or by utilizing quantum langevin equations without input operators .",
