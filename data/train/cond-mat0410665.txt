    "<S> for a fast rotating condensate in a harmonic trap , we investigate the structure of the vortex lattice using wave functions minimizing the gross pitaveskii energy in the lowest landau level . </S>",
    "<S> we find that the minimizer of the energy in the rotating frame has a distorted vortex lattice for which we plot the typical distribution . </S>",
    "<S> we compute analytically the energy of an infinite regular lattice and of a class of distorted lattices . </S>",
    "<S> we find the optimal distortion and relate it to the decay of the wave function . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we generalize our method to other trapping potentials .    </S>",
    "<S> the rotation of a macroscopic quantum fluid is a source of fascinating problems . </S>",
    "<S> by contrast with a classical fluid , for which the equilibrium velocity field corresponds to rigid body rotation , a quantum fluid described by a macroscopic wave function rotates through the nucleation of quantized vortices @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> a vortex is a singular point ( in 2 dimensions ) or line ( in 3 dimensions ) where the density vanishes . along a contour encircling a vortex </S>",
    "<S> , the circulation of the velocity is quantized in units of @xmath0 , where @xmath1 is the mass of a particle of the fluid .    </S>",
    "<S> vortices are universal features which appear in many macroscopic quantum systems , such as superconductors or superfluid liquid helium . </S>",
    "<S> recently , detailed investigations have been performed on rotating atomic gaseous bose - einstein condensates . </S>",
    "<S> these condensates are usually confined in a harmonic potential , with cylindrical symmetry around the rotation axis @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> two limiting regimes occur depending on the ratio of the rotation frequency @xmath3 and the trap frequency @xmath4 in the @xmath5 plane . </S>",
    "<S> when @xmath3 is notably smaller than @xmath4 , only one or a few vortices are present at equilibrium @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> when @xmath3 approaches @xmath4 , since the centrifugal force nearly balances the trapping force , the radius of the rotating gas increases and tends to infinity , and the number of vortices in the condensate diverges @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> as pointed out by several authors , the fast rotation regime presents a strong analogy with quantum hall physics . indeed </S>",
    "<S> the one - body hamiltonian written in the rotating frame is similar to that of a charged particle in a uniform magnetic field . </S>",
    "<S> therefore the ground energy level is macroscopically degenerate , as the celebrated landau levels obtained for the quantum motion of a charge in a magnetic field . </S>",
    "<S> there are two aspects in this connection with quantum hall physics . </S>",
    "<S> firstly , when the number of vortices inside the fluid remains small compared to the number @xmath6 of atoms , we expect that the ground state of the system will correspond to a bose - einstein condensate , described by a macroscopic wave function @xmath7 . </S>",
    "<S> this situation has been referred to as ` mean field quantum hall regime ' @xcite . secondly , when @xmath3 approaches @xmath4 even closer , the number of vortices reaches values comparable to the total number of atoms @xmath6 . </S>",
    "<S> the description by a single macroscopic wave function then breaks down , and one expects a strongly correlated ground state , such as that of an electron gas in the fractional quantum hall regime @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> we do not address the second situation in this paper and we rather focus on the first regime . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore we restrict our analysis to the case of a two - dimensional gas in the @xmath5 plane , assuming that a strong confinement along the @xmath2 direction so that the corresponding degree of freedom is frozen .    </S>",
    "<S> the main features of the vortex assembly equilibrium in the fast rotation regime are well known . </S>",
    "<S> the vortices form a triangular abrikosov lattice in the @xmath5 plane and the area of the elementary cell is @xmath8 @xcite . the atomic velocity field obtained by a coarse - grained average over a few elementary cells is equal to the rigid body rotation result @xmath9 , where @xmath10 ( @xmath11 is the unit vector along the @xmath2 axis ) .    beyond this approximation , the physics is very rich and many points are still debated . in a seminal paper @xcite , </S>",
    "<S> ho introduced the description of the macroscopic state of the rotating gas in the @xmath5 plane by a wave function belonging to the lowest landau level ( lll ) . </S>",
    "<S> an lll wave function is entirely determined ( up to a global phase factor ) by the location of vortices . </S>",
    "<S> ho considered the case of a uniform infinite vortex lattice and inferred that the ground state of the system corresponds to a gaussian shape for the coarse - grained atom density profile . using both analytical @xcite and numerical @xcite investigations , </S>",
    "<S> it was subsequently pointed out that the atom distribution may have the shape of an inverted parabola , instead of ho s gaussian result . </S>",
    "<S> two paths have been proposed to explain the emergence of such non gaussian profiles . </S>",
    "<S> the first one assumes that the restriction to the lll is not sufficient and the contamination of the ground state wave function by other landau levels is responsible for the transition from a gaussian to an inverted parabola @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the second path explores the influence of distortions of the vortex lattice ( within the lll ) to account for the deviation of the equilibrium profile from a gaussian @xcite .    . </S>",
