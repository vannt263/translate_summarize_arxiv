    "<S> a simplified susceptible - infected - recovered ( sir ) epidemic model and a small - world model are applied to analyse the spread and control of severe acute respiratory syndrome ( sars ) for hong kong in early 2003 . from data available in mid april 2003 </S>",
    "<S> , we predict that sars would be controlled by june and nearly 1700 persons would be infected based on the sir model . </S>",
    "<S> this is consistent with the known data . a simple way to evaluate the development and efficacy of control </S>",
    "<S> is described and shown to provide a useful measure for the future evolution of an epidemic . </S>",
    "<S> this may contribute to improve strategic response from the government . </S>",
    "<S> the evaluation process here is universal and therefore applicable to many similar homogeneous epidemic diseases within a fixed population . </S>",
    "<S> a novel model consisting of map systems involving the small - world network principle is also described . </S>",
    "<S> we find that this model reproduces qualitative features of the random disease propagation observed in the true data . </S>",
    "<S> unlike traditional deterministic models , scale - free phenomena are observed in the epidemic network . </S>",
    "<S> the numerical simulations provide theoretical support for current strategies and achieve more efficient control of some epidemic diseases , including sars .    during 2003 </S>",
    "<S> sars killed 916 and infected 8422 globally@xcite . in hong kong ( hk ) , one of the most severely affected regions , 1755 individuals were infected and 299 died@xcite . </S>",
    "<S> sars is caused by a coronavirus , which is more dangerous and tenacious than the aids virus because of its strong ability to survive in moist air and considerable potential to infect through close personal contact@xcite . </S>",
    "<S> unlike other well - known epidemic diseases , such as aids , sars spreads quickly . although significant , its mortality rate is , fortunately , relatively low ( approximately 11%)@xcite . </S>",
    "<S> researchers have decoded the genome of sars coronavirus and developed prompt diagnostic tests and some medicines , a vaccine is still far from being developed and widely usedmarra1,rota1,stadler1 . </S>",
    "<S> the danger of a recurrence of sars remains .    </S>",
    "<S> irrespective of pharmacological research , the epidemiology study of sars will help to prevent possible spreading of similar future contagions . </S>",
    "<S> generally , current epidemiological models are of two types . </S>",
    "<S> first , the well - known susceptible - infected - recovered ( sir ) model proposed in 1927kermack1,daley1 . </S>",
    "<S> second , the concept of small - world ( sw )  networkswatts1 . arousing a new wave of epidemiological research </S>",
    "<S> , the sw model has made some progress recently@xcite . </S>",
    "<S> our work aims to model sars data for hk . </S>",

    "<S> in particular , a generalized method to evaluate control of an epidemic is promoted here based on the sir model with fixed population . </S>",
