    "<S> the formation of electron pairs is a prerequisite of superconductivity . </S>",
    "<S> the fermionic nature of electrons yields four classes of superconducting correlations with definite symmetry in spin , space and time . here , we suggest double quantum dots coupled to conventional @xmath0-wave superconductors in the presence of inhomogeneous magnetic fields as a model system exhibiting unconventional pairing . due to their small number of degrees of freedom , tunable by gate voltages , </S>",
    "<S> quantum - dot systems are ideal to gain fundamental insight in unconventional pairing . </S>",
    "<S> we propose two detection schemes for unconventional superconductivity , based on either josephson or andreev spectroscopy .    </S>",
    "<S> [ [ introduction . ] ] introduction. + + + + + + + + + + + + + +    conventional superconductivity is well understood in terms of bardeen - cooper - schrieffer ( bcs ) theory by the formation of spin - singlet cooper pairs of electrons  @xcite . in certain unconventional superconductors , </S>",
    "<S> spin - triplet pairs are involved  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> a further generalization is the notion of pairing of electrons at different times . </S>",
    "<S> there are four different classes of superconducting correlations with definite symmetries in spin , space ( momentum ) , and time ( frequency ) under exchange of two electrons forming a cooper pair . </S>",
    "<S> even - frequency spin - singlet ( odd in spin ) cooper pairs appear in conventional @xmath0-wave but also in high-@xmath1 @xmath2wave  @xcite superconductors ( even in space ) while @xmath3- and @xmath4-wave pairing ( odd in space ) supports even - frequency spin triplets ( even in spin ) . </S>",
    "<S> there is experimental evidence for triplet @xmath3-wave pairing ( similar to superfluid @xmath5he  @xcite ) in sr@xmath6ruo@xmath7  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> recent proposals to induce triplet correlations in nanowires with strong spin - orbit interaction in proximity to @xmath0-wave superconductors were motivated by the prospect of creating majorana fermions at the ends of the wire  @xcite . by a similar mechanism , majorana fermions </S>",
    "<S> can also be generated in double quantum dots  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the idea of odd - frequency pairing has first been brought up by berezinskii  @xcite as a possible explanation of superfluid @xmath5he but has experienced a revival in the context of superconductor - ferromagnet heterostructures  @xcite . for noncollinear magnetizations , e.g. , due to domain walls  @xcite , spin - active interfaces  @xcite , multiple noncollinear magnetized ferromagnetic layers  @xcite , helical magnets  @xcite , or spin - orbit coupling @xcite , odd - frequency correlations with finite spin polarization can penetrate deeply into ferromagnets , as confirmed in several experiments  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> odd - frequency triplet pairing also appears in diffusive normal metals contacted by an even - frequency triplet superconductor @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> finally , odd - frequency singlet superconductivity has only been theoretically predicted  @xcite without experimental confirmation so far .    </S>",
    "<S> quantum dots coupled to conventional superconductors show an interesting interplay of proximity effect and coulomb interaction  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> they have also been suggested as a tool to _ detect _ unconventional pairing @xcite . in this letter , </S>",
    "<S> we propose double quantum dots as an ideal system to _ generate all four types _ of superconducting correlations _ in a single device _ and to _ control _ them via gate and bias voltages and inhomogeneous magnetic fields . </S>",
    "<S> this is a substantial advance over proposals which are specific to one type of correlations only , in which tuning is limited , or which describe equilibrium scenarios only .    </S>",
