    "<S> simulations are used to examine the microscopic origins of strain hardening in polymer glasses . </S>",
    "<S> while stress - strain curves for a wide range of temperature can be fit to the functional form predicted by entropic network models , many other results are fundamentally inconsistent with the physical picture underlying these models . </S>",
    "<S> stresses are too large to be entropic and have the wrong trend with temperature . </S>",
    "<S> the most dramatic hardening at large strains reflects increases in energy as chains are pulled taut between entanglements rather than a change in entropy . </S>",
    "<S> a weak entropic stress is only observed in shape recovery of deformed samples when heated above the glass transition . </S>",
    "<S> while short chains do not form an entangled network , they exhibit partial shape recovery , orientation , and strain hardening . </S>",
    "<S> stresses for all chain lengths collapse when plotted against a microscopic measure of chain stretching rather than the macroscopic stretch . </S>",
    "<S> the thermal contribution to the stress is directly proportional to the rate of plasticity as measured by breaking and reforming of interchain bonds . </S>",
    "<S> these observations suggest that the correct microscopic theory of strain hardening should be based on glassy state physics rather than rubber elasticity . </S>"
