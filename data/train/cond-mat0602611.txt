    "in this paper we have explained the nature of the @xmath0-core percolation transition in uncorrelated networks . to obtain our results , we used heuristic arguments and developed an exact theory .",
    "let us list the main features of the quite unusual @xmath0-core percolation transition : ( i ) a jump emergence of the @xmath0-core , ( ii ) the critical singularity in the phase with the @xmath0-core , ( iii ) the absence of any critical effects  strong correlations , divergent `` susceptibilities '' , etc .  on the `` normal phase '' side .",
    "we had to reveal the meaning of the order parameter in this problem , to explain the nature of the jump , to find the origin of the singularity , to indicate a `` physical '' quantity , which diverges at the critical point , to indicate long - range correlations , specific for this transition .",
    "we have shown that the order parameter in this problem is simply expressed in terms of the relative number of edges in the @xmath0-core , see relation  ( [ lt ] ) .",
    "the tree ansatz has allowed us to find the @xmath0-core order parameter and other @xmath0-core characteristics of various uncorrelated networks .",
    "we have found that the unique properties of the @xmath0-core percolation transition are essentially determined by the corona subset of the @xmath0-core , that is , by vertices with exactly @xmath0 connections to the @xmath0-core .",
    "these are the `` weakest '' vertices in the @xmath0-core .",
    "the critical correlations in the @xmath0-core are due to the correlations in the system of the corona clusters .    in the `` @xmath0-core phase '' ,",
    "the corona clusters are finite , but their sizes and long - range correlations grow as the network approaches the @xmath0-core percolation threshold . the mean size of a corona cluster to which a randomly chosen corona vertex belong diverges at the @xmath0-core percolation threshold .",
    "this quantity plays the role of a critical susceptibility in this problem .",
    "so , the @xmath0-core percolation threshold coincides with the percolation threshold for corona clusters , and the @xmath0-core phase is the `` normal '' phase for the corona .",
    "the dramatic difference from the ordinary percolation is that the corona disappears on the other side of the threshold , and so critical fluctuations in the phase without the @xmath0-core are absent .    for understanding the nature of this transition",
    ", we have studied the process of the destruction of the @xmath0-core due to the random deletion of vertices .",
    "the deletion of a vertex in the @xmath0-core results in the clipping out the entire adjacent corona clusters from the @xmath0-core due to the domino principle .",
    "this effect is enormously increased when corona clusters become large  near the @xmath0-core percolation threshold . in the threshold , the removal of a tiny fraction of vertices results in the complete collapse of the corona and the @xmath0-core . in this respect",
    ", the @xmath0-core percolation problem can be mapped to a model of cooperative relaxation , which undergoes critical relaxation with a divergent rate at some critical moment .    to conclude ,",
