    "<S> we study the interplay between disorder and interaction in one - dimensional topological superconductors which carry localized majorana zero - energy states . using abelian bosonization and the perturbative renormalization group ( rg ) approach </S>",
    "<S> , we obtain the rg - flow and the associated scaling dimensions of the parameters and identify the critical points of the low - energy theory . </S>",
    "<S> we predict a quantum phase transition from a topological superconducting phase to a non - topological localized phase , and obtain the phase boundary between these two phases as a function of the electron - electron interaction and the disorder strength in the nanowire . based on an instanton analysis which incorporates the effect of disorder </S>",
    "<S> , we also identify a large regime of stability of the majorana - carrying topological phase in the parameter space of the model .    </S>",
    "<S> _ introduction . _ </S>",
    "<S> the search for topological phases of matter has become an active and exciting pursuit in condensed matter physics  @xcite . among the many important examples of such phases </S>",
    "<S> are topological superconductors ( sc ) supporting zero - energy majorana bound states ( mbs )  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> a particularly promising realization of topological superconductivity is one - dimensional ( 1d ) semiconductor / sc heterostructures  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in addition to being one of the simplest examples of fractionalization , zero - energy mbs quasiparticles have ising - like non - abelian braiding properties  @xcite and can be used for topological quantum computation  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> the distinct feature of topological scs is the ground - state degeneracy due to the fermion parity encoded in the exponentially localized zero - energy mbs  @xcite . in a finite - length 1d wire , this degeneracy is approximate and there is an exponentially small energy splitting @xmath0 due to a finite overlap of mbs . here </S>",
    "<S> @xmath1 and @xmath2 are the length of the wire and superconducting coherence length , respectively . </S>",
    "<S> the presence of impurities in 1d p - wave scs with broken time - reversal and spin @xmath3 symmetry ( class d )  @xcite adversely affects the stability of the topological phase and drives a transition to a non - topological insulator phase  @xcite . the aforementioned qpt transition between topological and non - topological ( localized ) thermal insulator phases is accompanied by the change of the ground - state degeneracy splitting from exponential to algebraic in @xmath1  @xcite . in other words , increasing the disorder strength leads to a topological quantum phase transition ( qpt ) from the majorana - carrying topological sc phase with quantum degeneracy to a trivial phase with no end - mbs in the wire . </S>",
    "<S> the effect of electron - electron interactions in the disordered sc wires have _ not _ been taken account before . </S>",
    "<S> the latter may have important implications for the topological phase , and there may be qpts associated with the tuning of the interaction strength . </S>",
    "<S> indeed , it is well known that the low - energy properties of 1d conductors are strongly affected by both electron - electron interactions and disorder @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> clarification of their combined effect is crucial for our complete understanding of the topological phase diagram of the system and ultimately for the experimental realization of majorana quantum wires in the laboratory  @xcite , where both disorder and interactions would be inevitably present .    in this letter </S>",
    "<S> , we investigate an important question concerning the effect of both disorder and interaction on the stability of the topological phase and go beyond the non - interacting results of refs.@xcite , and of refs .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite , where the effects of interaction have been studied in clean nanowires . </S>",
    "<S> we consider a generic 1d p - wave sc and include the effects of both quenched disorder and interaction using abelian bosonization and the replica method  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> we derive a set of coupled renormalization - group ( rg ) equations for the parameters of the model , obtaining in the process the quantum phase diagram of the system . </S>",
    "<S> using these results in combination with an instanton analysis allows us to analyze the topological stability of mbs under the influence of both interaction and disorder . in general , </S>",
    "<S> disorder and repulsive interactions reinforce their detrimental effects on the topological sc phase and tend to eliminate the exponentially - split ground state mbs degeneracy associated with different fermion parity  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , for a sufficiently strong initial induced pairing @xmath4 , we predict a stable topological phase at low temperatures , even in the presence of disorder and interaction . </S>",
    "<S> our results are relevant to recent experiments on semiconductor nanowires with strong spin - orbit and zeeman interactions , proximity - coupled to a s - wave bulk sc @xcite , whose low - energy hamiltonian was shown to reduce to an effective 1d spinless p - wave sc @xcite , and shed light on the question of the stability of mbs in realistic situations .    _ theoretical model . </S>",
