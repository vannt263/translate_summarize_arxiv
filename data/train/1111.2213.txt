    "<S> a chaotic network of size @xmath0 with delayed interactions which resembles a pseudo - inverse associative memory neural network is investigated . for a load @xmath1 , where @xmath2 stands for the number of stored patterns , the chaotic network functions as an associative memory of @xmath3 attractors with macroscopic basin of attractions which decrease with @xmath4 . at finite @xmath4 , a chaotic spin glass phase exists , where the number of distinct chaotic attractors scales exponentially with @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> each attractor is characterized by a coexistence of chaotic behavior and freezing of each one of the @xmath0 chaotic units or freezing with respect to the @xmath2 patterns . </S>",
    "<S> results are supported by large scale simulations of networks composed of bernoulli map units and mackey - glass time delay differential equations .    over two decades </S>",
    "<S> ago a link between statistical physics and neural network theory was established , where associative memory models were mapped onto magnetic hamiltonian systems with random and frustrated interactions @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the capacity , i.e. the number of stored patterns , was calculated using techniques borrowed from random frustrated magnetic systems and in particular advanced method to solve infinite - range spin - glass models @xcite . in parallel to the progress in interdisciplinary research on random magnetic systems , the theory of nonlinear dynamics developed and recently there have been important steps forward in the understanding of conditions to achieve chaotic networks synchronization @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> it is reasonable to assume that the two phenomena , spin - glasses and chaos , represent two conflicting trends . </S>",
    "<S> the spin - glass ( sg ) phenomenon is characterized by the existence of exponential number of meta - stable states , in which the local degrees of freedom ( spins ) are frozen @xcite . on the other hand , </S>",
    "<S> chaotic dynamics is characterized such that two nearby trajectories diverge from each other as a consequence of the positive lyapunov exponent @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> nevertheless , a network of coupled chaotic units can synchronize @xcite to an identical chaotic trajectory . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we show that chaotic networks can have a sg phase , characterized by an exponential number of chaotic attractors with the system size , @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> each one of the exponentially many attractors is characterized by a coexistence of chaotic behavior and substantial freezing of each one of the n chaotic units . </S>",
    "<S> we start in section [ sec : model ] with the definition of the model for a network of chaotic maps . </S>",
