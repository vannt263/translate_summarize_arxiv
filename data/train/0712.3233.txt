    "the massless conduction electrons in a two - dimensional carbon lattice respond differently to an electric field than ordinary massive electrons do .",
    "because the magnitude @xmath6 of the velocity of a massless particle is independent of its energy , a massless electron moving along the field lines can not be backscattered  since that would require @xmath7 at the turning point .",
    "the absence of backscattering was discovered in carbon nanotubes,@xcite where it is responsible for the high conductivity in the presence of disorder .",
    "a graphene nanoribbon is essentially a carbon nanotube that is cut open along the axis and flattened .",
    "one distinguishes armchair and zigzag nanotubes , depending on whether the cut runs parallel or perpendicular to the carbon - carbon bonds .",
    "the edges of the nanoribbon fundamentally modify the ability of an electric field to backscatter electrons . as discovered in computer simulations by wakabayashi and aoki,@xcite a potential step in a zigzag nanoribbon blocks",
    "the current when it crosses the fermi level , forming a _ p - n _ junction (= a junction of states in conduction and valence band ) .",
    "the current blocking was interpreted in ref .   by analogy with the spin - valve effect in ferromagnetic junctions.@xcite in this analogy the valley polarization in a zigzag nanoribbon plays the role of the spin polarization in a ferromagnet",
    " hence the name `` valley - valve '' effect .",

    "the current is blocked when @xmath5 is even ( zigzag configuration ) , while it is not blocked when @xmath5 is odd ( anti - zigzag configuration , see fig .  [ fig_zigzag ] ) . this even - odd difference ( first noticed in connection with the quantum hall effect@xcite )",
    "is puzzling since zigzag and anti - zigzag nanoribbons are indistinguishable at the level of the dirac equation,@xcite which is the wave equation that governs the low - energy dynamics in graphene .",
    "the applicability of the dirac equation rests on the assumption that a smooth potential step causes no intervalley scattering . as we now show , it is this assumption which fails in the _ p - n _ junction , breaking the analogy between spin valve and valley valve . in the spin valve , a spin - up electron incident on a ferromagnetic junction which only transmits spin - down",
    "is simply reflected as spin - up .",
    "the current blocking can therefore be understood without invoking spin - flip scattering . in the valley valve , however , an electron in valley @xmath17 incident on a _ p - n _ junction which only transmits valley @xmath18 can not be reflected in valley @xmath17 .",
    "both transmission and reflection require a switch of the valley from @xmath17 to @xmath18 ( see fig .",

    "we conclude that _ a _ p - n _ junction in a zigzag nanoribbon is an intrinsic source of intervalley scattering_. it does not matter how smooth the potential step might be , since the incoming and outgoing states are from different valleys , the scattering must switch valleys to preserve the current .",
