    "materials with potentially useful thermoelectric properties are currently under intense theoretical and experimental investigation , mainly due to the prospect of applications , e.g. , for conversion of waste heat into electricity in thermoelectric generators , for applications to refrigeration , or for on - chip cooling and energy efficiency in microelectronics applications@xcite . apart from possible applications",
    ", thermoelectric materials can also serve as an interesting testing ground for theoretical approaches to electrical and thermal transport in solids@xcite .",
    "as the scale of the individual components in semiconducting devices is approaching the nano - size , a description of thermal transport through quantum dots is also attracting a lot of experimental and theoretical attention@xcite .",
    "in this paper we address the thermoelectric properties of a nanoscale size quantum dot exhibiting the kondo effect , which we describe in terms of a single level anderson impurity model with two conduction electron leads at fixed chemical potentials .",
    "the quantum dots that we consider have sizes of @xmath23 and can be tuned from the kondo to the mixed valence and empty orbital regimes by a gate voltage @xcite .",
    "short segments of carbon nanotubes @xcite connected to leads exhibit similar physics , so our results could also be of relevance to such systems .",
    "very recent experiments on nanoscale quantum dots @xcite are beginning to probe the effect of kondo correlations on the thermopower , although as we shall argue in the conclusions a quantitative comparison with theory is still some way off .",
