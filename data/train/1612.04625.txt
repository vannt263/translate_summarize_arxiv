    "<S> calculating the degree of non - markovianity of a dissipative process is a difficult task , even for the dynamics of a single qubit , given the complex maximization problem . in this work , </S>",
    "<S> focusing on the entanglement - based quantifier of non - markovianity , we present an analytical solution for such an optimization problem . </S>",
    "<S> we then propose a computable non - markovianity measure based on generalized robustness of entanglement , an entanglement measure that can be readily calculated by a semidefinite programming method . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the non - markovianity , in a given interval of time , can be witnessed by calculating the expectation value of an observable , making it attractive for experimental investigations .    </S>",
    "<S> recently , non - markovian processes have been receiving special attention in the literature @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in fact , the understanding of non - markovian process is important for practical applications such as communication protocols , quantum algorithms , and quantum cryptography @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> much effort has been devoted to the characterization and quantification of non - markovianity in dissipative process . </S>",
    "<S> several measures have been presented , and among the most evident , we can mention the measure based on the trace distance defined by breuer , laine , and pillo @xcite , the measure based on entanglement dynamics defined by rivas , huelga , and plenio @xcite , and the measure based on the dynamics of mutual information defined by luo , fu , and song @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> it is known that , in general , such measures do not agree on the description of dissipative dynamics , differing even when defining a process such as markovian @xcite . moreover , besides these differences , they have a particular and unpleasant feature in common : all are computationally difficult to evaluate because the number of variables involved is hugely increased with the system size . </S>",
    "<S> this makes the study of non - markovian processes an extremely difficult task , and it is exactly in this direction that we develop our work by presenting an efficient new strategy .    </S>",
    "<S> focusing on the non - markovianity measure based on entanglement dynamics , we first solve the maximization problem analytically . </S>",
    "<S> we show that , for an arbitrary quantum process and independent of the system size , the state that maximizes the quantifier of non - markovianity is the bipartite maximally entangled state . with this result , bypassing the maximization problem , we define a quantifier of non - markovianity based on the dynamics of the generalized robustness of entanglement , a measure that can be written by means of the entanglement witness approach @xcite , which allows its evaluation in an efficient manner through robust semidefinite programming ( rsdp ) @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , we demonstrate how non - markovianity can be witnessed directly in the laboratory without the necessity of tomography of the whole state in each instant of time . </S>",
    "<S> these results open huge prospects , allowing the study of non - markovian dynamics for high - dimensional systems as theoretical and experimental points of view . _ </S>",

    "<S> _ conclusion _ - </S>",
    "<S> we have presented an analytical solution to the maximization problem involved in the calculation of the entanglement based measure of quantum non - markovianity . for an arbitrary quantum process </S>",
    "<S> , we have demonstrated that the optimal initial bipartite state of the open system and the ancilla is given by a bipartite maximal entangled state . </S>",
    "<S> based on this finding , we introduce a computable measure of quantum non - markovianity based on the generalized robustness of entanglement . </S>",
    "<S> we have shown how a non - markovian process can be detected by means of an expectation value of an observable , making it attractive for experimental investigations . </S>",
    "<S> as an example , we calculate exactly the degree of non - markovianity of two trapped cold atoms interacting with an ultracold bosonic rubidium gas in a bose - einstein condensate ( bec ) . </S>",
    "<S> this finding opens up new avenues in the investigation of quantum non - markovian dynamics , providing an efficient way to study dissipative processes on high dimensional systems .    </S>",
