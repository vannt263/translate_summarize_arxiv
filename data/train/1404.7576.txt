    "<S> we theoretically study transmission in nanowaveguide coupled to high - quality plasmon resonances for which the metal loss is overcompensated by gain . </S>",
    "<S> the on - resonance transmission can vary widely from lower than 20db to higher than 20db for a range of gain coefficient . </S>",
    "<S> a reversible transition between the high - quality amplification and the suppression can be induced by a quite small change of gain coefficient for a moderately increased distance between the waveguide and the resonator . </S>",
    "<S> it is expected that in practice a small change of gain coefficient can be made by flexibly controlling pumping rate or utilizing nonlinear gain . </S>",
    "<S> additionally , based on the frequency - dependant model for gain - transition susceptibility , it is shown that the wide variation of the on - resonance transmission can also be observed for defferent detuning of the gain - transition line - center . </S>",
    "<S> such a widely controllable on - resonance transmission is promising for applications such as well - controlled lumped amplification of surface plasmon - polariton as well as plasmonic switching . </S>"
