    "in the theoretical description of high energy ( semi-)inclusive processes involving hadrons , the cross - sections are usually expanded in powers of @xmath0 , where @xmath1 is the large momentum transfer of the collision .",
    "the contribution at the leading power can be expressed as a convolution of the leading - twist ( or twist - two ) distribution / fragmentation functions and the hard scattering coefficients . in the first subleading power of the @xmath0 expansion ,",
    "the twist-3 distribution and/or fragmentation functions contribute to the cross - section  @xcite .",
    "unlike the twist - two distribution functions that describe the parton densities inside the nucleon , there is no probability interpretation for the twist-3 distribution functions .",
    "however , they provide a wealth of information about the nucleon parton structure  @xcite , especially when the parton transverse momenta are present .",
    "the interest on the twist-3 contributions also comes from the fact that they are related to the multi - parton correlation inside the nucleon  @xcite .",
    "in this letter we apply a phenomenological model to study twist-3 quark distributions that are encoded in the quark - gluon - quark correlation .",
    "we pay special attention on the time - reversal - odd ( t - odd ) transverse momentum dependent ( tmd ) distributions . in the tmd factorization approach , the leading twist t - odd tmd  @xcite play important roles  @xcite in the single - spin asymmetries ( ssas ) measured in semi - inclusive deeply inelastic scattering ( sidis )  @xcite . at the twist-3 level ,",
    "much richer phenomena arise , i.e. there are eight t - odd distributions that can contribute to various azimuthal asymmetries in the sidis  @xcite and drell - yan  @xcite processes .",
    "although the twist-3 contributions are suppressed by @xmath0 , they are potential experimental observables and may be accessible in the kinematical regime where @xmath1 is not so large .",
    "the experiments at jefferson lab  @xcite and pax  @xcite are ideal for exploring this kinematical region .",
