    "the emission of the strong electromagnetic radiation by some astrophysical objects called anomalous x - ray pulsars ( axp ) and soft @xmath0-ray repeaters ( sgr ) is likely to be associated with the transformation of the magnetic energy in the star interiors to the energy of hard photons  @xcite .",
    "the estimates of the internal magnetic field strength necessary to explain the observed flashes give the value @xmath1 , making these compact stars one of the most powerful magnets in the universe , or magnetars  @xcite . despite the existence of the numerous models for the generation of such strong magnetic fields based on classical magnetohydrodynamics ,",
    "this issue is still an open problem for the modern astrophysics .",
    "note that the actual mechanism for the transfer of the internal magnetic energy to the stellar magnetosphere is unclear either .",
    "some of the popular models for the description of magnetars are reviewed in ref .",
    "@xcite .",
    "recently several attempts to explain the processes happening in magnetars with help of the elementary particles methods were undertaken .",
    "for example , the chiral magnetic effect ( cme )  @xcite , which leads to the magnetic field instability resulting in the enhancement of a seed field , potentially can be used for the explanation of magnetic fields in magnetars . note that cme can be also involved to account for the high linear velocities of pulsars  @xcite .",
    "these astrophysical applications of cme are reviewed in ref .",
    "@xcite .",
    "recently , in ref .",
    "@xcite , we proposed the new model for the generation of strong magnetic fields in magnetars based on the magnetic field instability driven by the parity violating electroweak interaction between background fermions . in this model the contribution of the electroweak interaction was a correction to cme .",
    "note that the idea about the magnetic field instability induced by the parity violating interaction was put forward earlier in ref .",
    "@xcite .    then , in ref .",
    "@xcite , the similar idea about the magnetic field amplification driven by the electroweak interaction was implemented in dense quark matter in a hybrid star ( hs ) or in a quark star ( qs ) . sometimes qs",
    "is also called a strange star because of the presence of the @xmath2 quark component .",
    "although these compact stars have not been observed yet , there is a quite strong theoretical motivation for their existence  @xcite .    in this work",
    "we continue the study of the magnetic field evolution in dense quark matter where the chiral symmetry is restored and , thus , both cme and the electroweak correction can take place . since the typical time for the magnetic field growth found in ref .",
    "@xcite appeared to be much shorter than that in ref .",
    "@xcite , the magnetic field amplification can happen in earlier stages of the stellar evolution when the effects of the turbulent motion of stellar matter are sizable .",
    "thus the main motivation of the present work will be the study of the magnetic field evolution accounting for both cme , and the electroweak interaction between quarks , and the effects of the matter turbulence .",
    "recently analogous problem in the early universe plasma was studied in ref .",

    "<S> we analyze the magnetic field evolution in dense quark matter with unbroken chiral symmetry . in our study </S>",
    "<S> we take into account the chiral magnetic effect , the electroweak interaction between quarks , and the turbulent motion of quark matter . </S>",
    "<S> the evolution equations for the spectra of the magnetic helicity density and the magnetic energy density as well as for the chiral imbalances are obtained . on the basis of the numerical solution of these equations </S>",
    "<S> , we find that turbulence effects are important for the behavior of relatively small scale magnetic fields . </S>",
    "<S> it is revealed that , under certain initial conditions , the time evolution of the magnetic field resembles the electromagnetic flashes of some magnetars . </S>",
    "<S> we suggest that these magnetic fields , created inside a magnetized compact star , can initiate magnetar bursts . </S>"
