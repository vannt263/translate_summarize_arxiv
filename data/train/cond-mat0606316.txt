    "<S> we describe microscopic theory for the quantum transport through finite interacting systems connected to noninteracting leads . it can be applied to small systems such as quantum dots , quantum wires , atomic chain , molecule , and so forth . </S>",
    "<S> the keldysh formalism is introduced to study the nonlinear current - voltage characteristics , and general properties of the nonequilibrium green s functions are provided . </S>",
    "<S> we apply the formulated to an out - of - equilibrium anderson model that has been used widely for the kondo effect in quantum dots . in the linear - response regime the kubo formalism still has an advantage , because it relates the transport coefficients directly to the correlation functions defined with respect to the thermal equilibrium , and it has no ambiguities about a profile of the inner electric field . we introduce a many - body transmission coefficient , by which the dc conductance can be expressed in a landauer - type form , quite generally . </S>",
    "<S> we also discuss transport properties of the tomonaga - luttinger liquid , which has also been an active field of research in this decade . </S>"
