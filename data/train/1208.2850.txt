    "fe - based materials are used for various technological applications such as springs in watches , car bodies , magnetic cores , and heads of hard disk drives . despite their ubiquity in everyday life",
    ", they exhibit intriguing phenomena that include , among others , high - temperature superconductivity in fe pnictides @xcite , fermi - liquid breakdown in fe - nb alloys @xcite , and the invar effect in transition - metal alloys @xcite . discovered more than 100 years ago , invar fe - based materials display anomalously small thermal expansion coefficients over broad temperature ranges .",
    "fe - ni alloys with a ni concentration of about 35 at.% were the first to be found @xcite . subsequently , other invar fe - based materials were reported , some showing ferromagnetism ( e.g. , fe@xmath5pd@xmath6 @xcite ) and some antiferromagnetism ( e.g. , fe@xmath8ti @xcite ) .    despite the general consensus that the invar effect in fe - based ferromagnets occurs as a result of magnetism ,",
    "the mechanism giving rise to the invar phenomenon remains controversial .",
    "two prominent questions raised by recent publications @xcite have yet to be answered before the invar effect is fully understood : ( i ) does the anomaly in fe - ni appear when changes in the magnitude of local magnetic moments with increasing temperature become anomalously large ? ( ii ) are the anomalies observed in fe - pt , fe - pd , and fe - ni governed by the same underlying physics ?",
