    "<S> we propose a qualitatively new mechanism for generating cosmological fluctuations from inflation . </S>",
    "<S> the non - equilibrium excitation of interacting scalar fields often evolves into infra - red ( ir ) and ultra - violet ( uv ) cascading , resulting in an intermediate scaling regime . </S>",
    "<S> we observe elements of this phenomenon in a simple model with inflaton @xmath0 and iso - inflaton @xmath1 fields interacting during inflation via the coupling @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> iso - inflaton particles are created during inflation when they become instantaneously massless at @xmath3 , with occupation numbers not exceeding unity . </S>",
    "<S> previous studies have focused on the momentary slowing down of the condensate @xmath4 by back - reaction effects . here , we point out that very quickly the produced @xmath1 particles become heavy and their multiple re - scatterings off the homogeneous condensate @xmath4 generates bremsstrahlung radiation of light inflaton ir fluctuations with high occupation numbers . </S>",
    "<S> the subsequent evolution of these ir fluctuations is qualitatively similar to that of the usual inflationary fluctuations , but their initial amplitude is different . </S>",
    "<S> the ir cascading generates a bump - shaped contribution to the cosmological curvature fluctuations , which can even dominate over the usual fluctuations for @xmath5 . the ir cascading curvature fluctuations </S>",
    "<S> are significantly non - gaussian and the strength and location of the bump are model - dependent , through @xmath6 and @xmath7 . </S>",
    "<S> the effect from ir cascading fluctuations is significantly larger than that from the momentary slowing - down of @xmath4 . with a sequence of such bursts of particle production </S>",
    "<S> , the superposition of the bumps can lead to a new broad band non - gaussian component of cosmological fluctuations added to the usual fluctuations . </S>",
    "<S> such a sequence of particle creation events can , but need not , lead to trapped inflation .    epsf </S>"
