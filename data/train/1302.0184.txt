    "<S> we use the recently proposed scattering states numerical renormalization group ( snrg ) approach to calculate @xmath0 and the differential conductance through a single molecular level coupled to a local molecular phonon . </S>",
    "<S> we also discuss the equilibrium physics of the model and demonstrate that the low - energy hamiltonian is given by an effective interacting resonant level model . from the nrg level flow , we directly extract the effective charge transfer scale @xmath1 and the dynamically induced capacitive coupling @xmath2 between the molecular level and the lead electrons which turns out to be proportional to the polaronic energy shift @xmath3 for the regimes investigated here . the equilibrium spectral functions for the different parameter regimes are discussed . </S>",
    "<S> the additional phonon peaks at multiples of the phonon frequency @xmath4 correspond to additional maxima in the differential conductance . </S>",
    "<S> non - equilibrium effects , however , lead to significant deviations between a symmetric junction and a junction in the tunnel regime . the suppression of the current for asymmetric junctions with increasing electron - phonon coupling , the hallmark of the franck - condon blockade , is discussed with a simple framework of a combination of ( i ) polaronic level shifts and ( ii ) the effective charge transfer scale @xmath5 . </S>"
