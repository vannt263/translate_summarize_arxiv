    "<S> we obtain the ultimate quantum limit for estimating the transverse separation of two thermal point sources using a given imaging system with limited spatial bandwidth . </S>",
    "<S> we show via the quantum cramr - rao bound that , contrary to the rayleigh limit in conventional direct imaging , quantum mechanics does not mandate any loss of precision in estimating even deep sub - rayleigh separations . </S>",
    "<S> we propose two coherent measurement techniques , easily implementable using current linear - optics technology , that approach the quantum limit over an arbitrarily large range of separations . </S>",
    "<S> our bound is valid for arbitrary source strengths , all regions of the electromagnetic spectrum , and for any imaging system with an inversion - symmetric point - spread function . </S>",
    "<S> the measurement schemes can be applied to microscopy , optical sensing , and astrometry at all wavelengths .    </S>",
    "<S> the rayleigh criterion for resolving two incoherent optical point sources @xcite is the most widely used benchmark for the resolving power of an imaging system . according to it </S>",
    "<S> , the sources can be resolved by direct imaging only if they are separated by at least the diffraction - limited spot size of the point - spread function of the imaging system . </S>",
    "<S> while the criterion is heuristic and does not take into account the intensity of the sources or the measurement shot noise , recent work @xcite has made it rigorous by taking as resolution measure the classical cramr - rao lower bound ( crb ) of estimation theory @xcite on the mean squared error ( mse ) of any unbiased estimate of the separation of the sources using spatially - resolved image - plane photon counting . </S>",
    "<S> these works showed that if the detected average photon number per mode @xmath0 , the mse of any unbiased estimator based on direct imaging diverges as the source separation decreases to zero over an interval comparable to the rayleigh limit . </S>",
    "<S> this phenomenon , dubbed rayleigh s curse in @xcite , stems from the indistinguishability between the photons coming from the two sources and imposes a fundamental limitation of direct imaging in resolving sources much closer than the spot size , even when the measured photon number is taken into account . </S>",
    "<S> recent developments in far - field microscopy @xcite sidestep rayleigh s curse by preventing multiple sources from emitting simultaneously , but control over the emission properties of sources is unavailable in target sensing or astronomical imaging .    </S>",
    "<S> while the development of novel quantum states of light and measurement techniques has given rise to the vast field of quantum imaging @xcite , fundamental quantum limits in resolving two _ incoherent _ sources have been largely neglected since the early days of quantum estimation theory @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> recently , the coherent @xcite and incoherent @xcite two - source resolution problems were revisited using the quantum cramr - rao bound ( qcrb ) @xcite that accounts for all ( unbiased ) measurement techniques allowed by quantum mechanics . under a weak - source assumption similar to that in @xcite , </S>",
    "<S> it was found in @xcite that the qcrb showed no dependence on the separation of the sources . </S>",
    "<S> linear optics - based measurements that approach the bound were also proposed @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> subsequent demonstrations of superresolution @xcite have substantiated the feasibility of these proposals . </S>",
    "<S> nevertheless , the classical treatments @xcite and the quantum treatment @xcite neglect multi - photon coincidences and bunching , phenomena that figure prominently in quantum optics @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> while such an approximation leads to correct conclusions for weak sources , e.g. , at optical frequencies @xcite , it is problematic for intense sources , e.g. , in the microwave to far - infrared regimes , for high - temperature astronomical sources , and for optical demonstrations using pseudothermal light generated from laser sources @xcite . as such </S>",
    "<S> , a quantum - optically rigorous derivation of the resolution limit is as yet unavailable .    in this paper , we solve these problems and obtain the qcrb for estimating the separation of two thermal point sources of arbitrary strength using rigorous quantum optics and estimation theory , and show that resolution is not fundamentally compromised at sub - rayleigh separations . </S>",
    "<S> we then propose two schemes that approach the qcrb . </S>",
    "<S> the finite spatial - mode demultiplexing ( fin - spade ) scheme performs photon counting in a finite number of suitably chosen transverse spatial modes of the field . </S>",
    "<S> the interferometric pixelated superlocalization by image inversion interferometry ( pix - sliver ) scheme uses pixelated detector arrays in the two interferometer outputs . </S>",
