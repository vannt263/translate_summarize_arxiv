    "lattice methods allow , in principle , an ` ab initio ' calculation of the fundamental parameters of qcd , among them the quark masses .",
    "quarks are not asymptotic states of qcd and so quark masses need to be defined by a renormalisation procedure , @xmath5 by giving the scheme @xmath6 and scale @xmath7 .    to convert the lattice results to continuum numbers , one needs control over the discretisation errors and the matching relations between the lattice scheme and the continuum renormalisation scheme @xmath8 .",
    "discretisation errors can be kept small and manageable by employing an improved fermion action .",
    "but , still , the lattice numbers may show considerable cut - off dependence at present couplings , which requires that the calculations are done over a range of sufficiently small lattice spacings @xmath9 , discretisation errors then being removed by an extrapolation to @xmath10 .",
    "the perturbative relations between renormalised quantities in the continuum and the bare lattice results are in almost all cases known to one - loop order only .",

    "we have presented an entirely non - perturbative ( np ) calculation of the light quark masses based on the vector ward identity ( vwi ) , using non - perturbatively @xmath1 improved wilson fermions with @xmath0 flavours of dynamical quarks .",
    "the calculation was done at four different lattice spacings @xmath18 , which allowed us to perform a continuum extrapolation .",
    "we found a strange quark mass of @xmath19 mev .",
    "a highlight and essential ingredient of the calculation was that we were able to compute the flavour singlet mass renormalisation constant , which is needed in the vwi approach .",
    "this result has been complemented recently by further studies in @xcite which also used np determinations of the renormalisation constant .",
    "in this paper we present an independent calculation of the strange quark mass using the axial vector ward identity ( awi ) , again for @xmath0 flavours of improved wilson fermions .",
