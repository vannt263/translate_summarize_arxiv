    "<S> we study theoretically and experimentally the modulational instability of broad optical beams in photorefractive nonlinear media . </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate the impact of the anisotropy of the nonlinearity on the growth rate of periodic perturbations . </S>",
    "<S> our findings are confirmed by experimental measurements in a strontium barium niobate photorefractive crystal .    </S>",
    "<S> a plane wave propagating in a medium with focusing nonlinearity is unstable with respect to the generation of small scale filaments @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this so called modulational instability ( mi ) phenomenon , has been extensively studied because of its importance as a factor limiting the propagation of high power beams . </S>",
    "<S> filamentation may also be identified as the first stage in the development of turbulent fluctuations in the transverse profile of a laser beam@xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , mi is often considered as a precursor for the formation of spatial and/or temporal optical solitons . as far as optics is concerned , </S>",
    "<S> mi has been studied in media with various mechanisms of nonlinear response including cubic @xcite , quadratic @xcite , nonlocal @xcite and inertial @xcite types of nonlinearity . </S>",
    "<S> importantly , mi is not restricted to nonlinear optics but has also been studied in many other nonlinear systems including fluids @xcite , plasmas @xcite and matter waves @xcite .    in the context of optical beam propagation in nonlinear materials </S>",
    "<S> mi has usually been considered in media with spatially isotropic nonlinear properties . recently </S>",
    "<S> a great deal of theoretical and experimental efforts have been devoted to studies of nonlinear optical effects and soliton formation in photorefractive crystals @xcite . while these media exhibit strong nonlinearity at very low optical power their nonlinear response is inherently anisotropic @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the anisotropy causes a number of observable effects including astigmatic self - focusing of optical beams @xcite , elliptically shaped solitary solutions@xcite , geometry - sensitive interactions of solitons @xcite , and fixed optical pattern orientation @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> several previous studies of mi in the context of photorefractive media were limited to a 1-dimensional geometry where the anisotropy is absent @xcite , and the physics is similar to the standard saturable nonlinearity @xcite . on the other hand , in a real physical situation where one deals with finite sized beams , the anisotropic aspects of the photorefractive nonlinear response are expected to play a significant role . </S>",
    "<S> some previous work @xcite already indicated the importance of anisotropy in the transversal break - up of broad beams propagating in biased photorefractive crystals . </S>",
    "<S> however , no detailed analysis of this phenomenon was carried out . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we study the mi of optical beams in photorefractive media taking into account the full 2-dimensional anisotropic model of the photorefractive nonlinearity .    </S>",
