    "the solar neutrino problem has its origin in the discrepancy between the observed terrestrial neutrino fluxes of the neutrinos produced by nuclear reactions in the solar core and the predicted neutrino fluxes by standard solar models ( ssm s )  @xcite .",
    "the observation of the solar neutrinos is of decisive importance , because it allows us contrary to the observed solar electromagnetic radiation to look into the nuclear active zone in the core of the sun , since the solar mantle is practically transparent to neutrinos .",
    "the observed neutrino fluxes are substantially less than predicted by ssm s . roughly speaking the homesteak chlorine - detector  @xcite , the neutrino - electron scattering detector kamiokande  @xcite , and the gallium detectors gallex  @xcite and sage  @xcite observe approximately the fractions 3/10 , 4/10 , and 5/10 of the predicted solar neutrino flux , respectively",
