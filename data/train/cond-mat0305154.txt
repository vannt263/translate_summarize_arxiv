    "a multitude of growth phenomena in physical processes are driven by exchange of particles between clusters .",
    "examples include droplet growth via evaporation and re - condensation @xcite , island growth in deposition processes @xcite , and phase ordering @xcite .",
    "exchange processes have been also used to model social and economical systems including segregation of heterogeneous populations @xcite , the distribution of wealth in a society @xcite , and growth of urban populations @xcite .    in exchange processes ,",
    "clusters are composed of ` atoms ' ( monomers ) .",
    "monomers detach from one cluster and re - attach to another cluster .",
    "we shall consider the detachment controlled limit where the time scale for transport between clusters is much faster then the time scale for detachment .",
    "exchange processes incorporate both reversible and irreversible features .",
    "clusters may grow or shrink , yet when a monomer attaches to another cluster , its respective cluster disappears .",
    "this irreversible step provides the mechanism for cluster growth .",
    "therefore , exchange - driven processes are fundamentally different from irreversible growth processes , particularly aggregation @xcite .",
    "such mass transfer processes are governed by an exchange kernel @xmath6 that represents the rate of transfer of monomers from a cluster of size @xmath7 to a cluster of size @xmath8 . generally , the rate of monomer exchange between two clusters depends on their sizes .",
    "moreover , we consider the case where there is _ no _ preferable direction for exchanges , i.e. , symmetric exchange kernels , @xmath9 .",
    "this is unlike migration processes where the exchange is preferential ( `` big gets bigger '' or `` rich gets richer '' ) .",
    "migration underlies certain physical processes ( e.g. , coarsening with conserved order parameter @xcite ) as well as social and economical processes @xcite .",
    "we investigate homogeneous exchange kernels , @xmath10 .",
    "in particular , we consider the product kernel @xmath11 and its generalization @xmath12 with @xmath13 and @xmath14 .",
    "we obtain a complete description of the problem in the asymptotic scaling regime .",
    "the overall range of possible behaviors and the emergence of self - similar size distributions are as in aggregation and migration processes . however , there are quantitative and qualitative differences . unlike aggregation , the gelation transition is complete , and unlike migration , the size distributions are extended rather than compact .",
    "the behavior falls into three categories .",
    "i ) growth : when @xmath15 and @xmath16 , clusters grow indefinitely .",
    "the typical cluster size grows algebraically with time , @xmath17 , and the cluster size distribution is given by a self - similar distribution with a stretched exponential tail .",
    "ii ) gelation : when @xmath15 and @xmath18 , the entire mass in the system is transformed into an infinite gel in a finite time .",
