    "it is an open question whether the quantum superposition principle holds at arbitrary length and mass scales .",
    "its validity has been confirmed for increasingly large particles and distances by interference experiments involving single neutrons and atoms @xcite , bose - einstein condensates @xcite , and massive molecules @xcite . by direct verification of quantum superpositions",
    "@xcite , these experiments establish our perception of the quantum - to - classical transition .",
    "spontaneous localization models @xcite and the gravitational localization discussed by disi and penrose @xcite provide a dynamical description of the wavefunction collapse through an objective modification of the schrdinger equation .",
    "one of the most prominent collapse theories is the continuous spontaneous localization ( csl ) model @xcite . by adding a nonlinear and stochastic term to the schrdinger equation it describes the random localization of the wave function of a delocalized particle .",
    "the modification involves two parameters , the rate @xmath0 and the localization length @xmath1 .",
    "the maximal decay rate @xmath2 grows quadratically with particle mass @xmath3 ( in units of the reference mass @xmath4 ) ; it is met if the spatial delocalization well exceeds the csl length @xmath1 , while spatial superpositions much smaller than @xmath1 remain practically undisturbed . in this way",
    "the csl model gives rise to classical behavior of massive particles , while the quantum dynamics of microscopic particles is preserved .",
    "a natural way to test objective collapse models is to observe matter - wave interference and thus exclude all parameters @xmath0 and @xmath1 that contradict the experiment @xcite .",
    "however , the csl modification implies that even a localized particle isolated from its environment experiences momentum diffusion and heating , which can be measured in principle @xcite .",
    "in fact , the most stringent up - to - date restrictions on the csl parameters result from the noise spectrum of lisa pathfinder @xcite and from x - ray emission from a germanium surface @xcite .",
    "levitated nanoparticles offer a particularly promising platform for testing collapse models because they can be well isolated from their environment @xcite .",
    "so far , studies and tests of csl involve only the center - of - mass degrees of freedom of the nanoparticle @xcite .",
    "motivated by recent advances in optical trapping and controlling the orientation of anisotropic nanoparticles @xcite and the prospect to achieve ro - translational ground state cooling @xcite , we study the effect of the csl model on the orientational coherences of an arbitrarily shaped rigid object .",
    "specifically , we present the master equation describing the impact of csl on the ro - translational quantum dynamics of a rigid nanoparticle . the associated localization rate for orientational superpositions is then evaluated for bodies with cylindrical and spheroidal shape .",
    "we determine the resulting linear momentum and angular momentum diffusion constants quantifying the rate of motional heating .",
    "their dependence on the csl parameters implies that the anisotropic shape of a nanoparticle can be exploited in future csl tests .",
    "finally , by solving the master equation for planar rotations we show how csl leads to an enhanced spread of the orientational wave packet dynamics .",

    "<S> we show how the ro - translational motion of anisotropic particles is affected by the model of continuous spontaneous localization ( csl ) , the most prominent hypothetical modification of the schrdinger equation restoring realism on the macroscale . </S>",
    "<S> we derive the master equation describing collapse - induced spatio - orientational decoherence , and demonstrate how it leads to linear- and angular - momentum diffusion . since the associated heating rates scale differently with the csl parameters , the latter can be determined individually by measuring the random motion of a single levitated nanorotor . </S>"
