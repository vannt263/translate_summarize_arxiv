    "we have presented a method to use large deviations from synchronization in order to determine the characteristics of the parameter mismatch in a collection of nearly identical chaotic dynamical systems .",
    "it has been noted that knowledge and manipulation of the mismatch patterns can be advantageous in order to improve the quality of the synchronization @xcite .",
    "the main advantage of our method is that it only requires direct knowledge of the synchronization error when it is large enough to be measured .",
    "furthermore , in principle , there are no limitations on the number of systems it can handle . on the other hand",
    ", the method only provides the relative deviations from the mean and has yet to be extended to systems with comparable mismatch in different parameters .",

    "<S> we present a method to determine the relative parameter mismatch in a collection of nearly identical chaotic oscillators by measuring large deviations from the synchronized state . </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate our method with an ensemble of slightly different circle maps . </S>",
    "<S> we discuss how to apply our method when there is noise , and show an example where the noise intensity is comparable to the mismatch .    </S>",
    "<S> the study of networks of coupled dynamical systems is an important area of research with applications in diverse fields , ranging from biology to laser physics @xcite-@xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the synchronization of coupled oscillators has been under extensive study in recent years and , in particular , the synchronization of identical oscillators has received considerable interest @xcite . since it is impossible in practice to obtain identical oscillators , the effect of the difference in the parameters of the oscillators , or _ parameter mismatch _ , might be relevant in some applications . </S>",
    "<S> it might be desired to have dynamical units as similar to each other as possible , or to know the characteristics of the parameter mismatch in a collection of nearly identical systems . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we propose a method to use deviations from synchronization to extract information on the parameter mismatch of the coupled dynamical units . </S>",
    "<S> existing methods for parameter estimation ( see , for example , refs .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite-@xcite ) usually rely on knowledge of the typically small synchronization error . </S>",
    "<S> our method depends on relatively large deviations from the synchronized state , and might be useful in cases in which the small synchronization error can not be measured accurately .    </S>",
    "<S> when a number of identical systems are appropriately coupled in a network , a solution exists in which the state of all oscillators at all times is the same . </S>",
    "<S> this is referred to as _ </S>",
    "<S> identical _ synchronization . </S>",
    "<S> this concept is useful only when the systems are identical . </S>",
    "<S> we will deal with systems that are nearly , but not exactly , identical . </S>",
    "<S> we will refer to a situation in which the states of the systems are very close to each other as _ nearly identical _ synchronization . </S>",
    "<S> a method to determine the stability of the synchronous state when the systems are identical , the _ master stability function _ , has been proposed by pecora and carroll @xcite .    in the case of </S>",
    "<S> nearly identical chaotic systems , the nearly synchronized state might be interrupted by relatively short periods of desynchronization ( _ desynchronization bursts _ ) . </S>",
    "<S> these bursts develop with spatial patterns on the network . </S>",
    "<S> these spatial patterns , and the parameters for which they can be expected , can be predicted from the laplacian matrix describing the network connections , the master stability function of the attractor , and the unstable periodic orbits embedded in it @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the spatial patterns of the bursts depend on the parameter mismatch of the different systems . </S>",
    "<S> we use this fact to infer the relative deviation of the parameters of the individual units with respect to their mean from the desynchronization bursts . </S>",
    "<S> the proposed method is as follows . </S>",
    "<S> the oscillators are connected in such a way that the parameter mismatch determines the spatial patterns of the desynchronization bursts . as we will see later </S>",
    "<S> , one such way is all - to - all coupling . </S>",
    "<S> the system is set up in a parameter region in which desynchronization bursts are expected . </S>",
    "<S> while a burst is developing , measurements are taken of the deviations of the different systems from the synchronous state . from these observations , </S>",
    "<S> the relative deviations of the parameters from the mean are deduced .    </S>",
    "<S> some limitations of this method are the following . </S>",
    "<S> it is assumed that measurements can be taken with enough precision such that the deviations from the synchronous state can be measured in the linear regime . </S>",
    "<S> also , the presence of noise affects the spatial patterns of the bursts . </S>",
    "<S> although we will describe how to deal with the noise , the effectiveness of the method decreases as the ratio of noise to mismatch increases . </S>",
    "<S> it is also assumed that unavoidable small differences in the way in which the systems are connected to each other does not introduce a difference between the systems which is of the same order of magnitude or larger than the parameter mismatch being measured .    in sec .  </S>",
