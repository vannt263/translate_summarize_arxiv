    "<S> the output spectrum of both gas and semiconductor lasers usually contains more than one frequency . </S>",
    "<S> multimode operation in gas versus semiconductor lasers arises from different physics . in gas lasers , slow equilibration of the electron populations at different energies makes each frequency an independent single - mode laser . </S>",
    "<S> the slow electron diffusion in semiconductor lasers , combined with the spatially varying optical intensity patterns of the modes , makes each region of space an independent single - mode laser . </S>",
    "<S> we develop a rate equation model for the photon number in each mode which captures all these effects . plotting the photon number versus pumping rate for the competing modes , in both subthreshold and above threshold operation , illustrates the changes in the laser output spectrum due to either slow equilibration or slow diffusion of electrons . </S>"
