    "<S> recent progress in optomechanical systems may soon allow the realization of optomechanical arrays , i.e. periodic arrangements of interacting optical and vibrational modes . </S>",
    "<S> we show that photons and phonons on a honeycomb lattice will produce an optically tunable dirac - type band structure . </S>",
    "<S> transport in such a system can exhibit transmission through an optically created barrier , similar to klein tunneling , but with interconversion between light and sound . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , edge states at the sample boundaries are dispersive and enable controlled propagation of photon - phonon polaritons .    _ </S>",
    "<S> _    rapid progress is being made in the field of optomechanics , which studies the interaction of light with nano - mechanical motion ( for a recent review , see @xcite ) . </S>",
    "<S> most current achievements are based on a single vibrational mode coupled to a single optical mode ( i.e. a single `` optomechanical cell '' ) . </S>",
    "<S> a logical next step is to couple many such modes , providing new functionality and generating new physical phenomena . </S>",
    "<S> first steps have been taken using setups with a few modes ( e.g. for synchronization @xcite , wavelength conversion @xcite , phonon lasing @xcite , or cooling @xcite ) . </S>",
    "<S> going beyond this , we can envisage a periodic arrangement of cells . in that case </S>",
    "<S> we will speak of an `` optomechanical array '' . </S>",
    "<S> optomechanical arrays might be realized on a number of experimental platforms : microdiscs @xcite and microtoroids @xcite could be coupled via evanescent optical fields @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> superconducting on - chip microwave cavity arrays ( of the type discussed in @xcite ) could be combined with nanobeams @xcite or membranes @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> * * currently the most promising platform are optomechanical crystals , i.e. photonic crystals engineered to contain localized vibrational and optical modes . </S>",
    "<S> single - mode optomechanical systems based on that concept have been demonstrated experimentally , with very favorable parameters @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> ab - initio simulations indicate the feasibility of arrays @xcite . given these developments it seems that optomechanical arrays are on the verge of realization . </S>",
    "<S> the existing theoretical work on optomechanical arrays deals with slow light @xcite , synchronization @xcite , quantum information processing @xcite and quantum many - body physics @xcite and photon transport @xcite . in this letter </S>",
    "<S> , we go beyond these works and illustrate the possibilities offered by engineering nontrivial optomechanical band structures of photons and phonons in such arrays . </S>",
    "<S> specifically , we will investigate an array with a honeycomb geometry . </S>",
    "<S> this lattice is the basis for modeling electrons in graphene @xcite , but it has recently also been studied for photonic crystals @xcite , exciton - photon polaritons @xcite and other systems @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> it is the simplest lattice with a band structure showing singular and robust features called dirac cones , mimicking the dispersion of relativistic massless particles . as we will be interested in the long - wavelength properties of the structure , on scales much larger than the lattice spacing </S>",
    "<S> , we may call this an `` optomechanical metamaterial '' . </S>",
    "<S> tunability would be the biggest advantage of optomechanical metamaterials , rivaling that of optical lattices : the band structure is easily tunable by the laser drive ( intensity , frequency , phases ) . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , it can be observed by monitoring the emitted light . using spatial intensity profiles for driving </S>",
    "<S> , one can even engineer arbitrary potentials and hence local changes in the band structure . </S>",
    "<S> we predict that these features could be used to observe photon - phonon dirac polaritons , an optomechanical klein tunneling effect , and edge state transport .    </S>",
