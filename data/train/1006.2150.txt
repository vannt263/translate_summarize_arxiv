    "electronic properties of single wall carbon nanotubes ( cnts ) have been studied intensively in the dc limit by means of electron transport  @xcite , by optical excitations  @xcite , and by angle - resolved electron energy loss spectroscopy in the uv region  @xcite .",
    "cnt properties in the intermediate ( thz ) band of frequencies are harder to access experimentally and consequently they attracted less attention in theory .",
    "the dominant excitations in that frequency range are plasmons .",
    "initial experiments probing them by means of time - resolved measurements were reported recently  @xcite .",
    "here we are interested in the intrinsic mechanisms of the decay of plasmons into other excitations within a metallic ( armchair ) cnt .",
    "the existing tunneling experiments provide support for the luttinger liquid description of the charge carriers in cnts  @xcite . in that description ,",
    "the collective excitation , plasmon , is just one of the eigenmodes with linear spectrum . as such",
    ", it does not decay .    in the conventional fermi liquid description applicable to higher - dimensional conductors , separation of excitations into plasmons and fermi - quasiparticles",
    "is not exact , leading to a plasmon decay by exciting quasiparticles  @xcite .",
    "in contrast , in one - dimensional conductors quasiparticles are ill - defined , and plasmon decays into other collective excitations of the electron liquid .    in armchair nanotubes in addition to the plasmon mode the spectrum of bosonic low energy excitations of electron liquid contains three neutral modes propagating with the fermi velocity .",
