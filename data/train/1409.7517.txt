    "<S> we study weyl nodes in materials with broken inversion symmetry . </S>",
    "<S> we find based on first - principles calculations that trigonal te and se have multiple weyl nodes near the fermi level . </S>",
    "<S> the conduction bands have a spin splitting similar to the rashba splitting around the h points , but unlike the rashba splitting the spin directions are radial , forming a hedgehog spin texture around the h points , with a nonzero pontryagin index for each spin - split conduction band . the weyl semimetal phase , which has never been observed in real materials without inversion symmetry , is realized under pressure . </S>",
    "<S> the evolution of the spin texture by varying the pressure can be explained by the evolution of the weyl nodes in @xmath0 space .    </S>",
    "<S> materials having a linear dispersion ( dirac cone ) such as graphene  @xcite have been under intensive investigation recently . among various dirac cones in band structures of solids , three - dimensional dirac cones without spin degeneracy </S>",
    "<S> are of particular interest because of their topological nature . a material having three - dimensional dirac dispersion without degeneracy near the fermi energy </S>",
    "<S> is called a weyl semimetal  @xcite . in weyl semimetals </S>",
    "<S> the dirac cones have no spin degeneracy because of the spin - orbit interaction ( soi ) . </S>",
    "<S> the gapless dirac points without degeneracy are called weyl nodes . </S>",
    "<S> interestingly , the weyl nodes are protected topologically against small perturbations because they have nontrivial topological invariants , namely a monopole charge associated with the berry curvature in @xmath0 space  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> each weyl node is either a monopole or an antimonopole , having a monopole charge of @xmath1 or @xmath2 , respectively . </S>",
    "<S> the effective hamiltonian around the weyl node is expressed by a @xmath3 matrix which has degrees of freedom of helicity reflecting the monopole charge . </S>",
    "<S> thus the system having weyl nodes naturally has nontrivial spin texture . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , if the lattice structure lacks mirror symmetry , the weyl node will bring unconventional spin texture and nontrivial phenomena , because mirror symmetry imposes strong restrictions on the spin direction . </S>",
    "<S> the weyl semimetal is realized in a system which breaks time - reversal or inversion symmetry . for weyl semimetals with broken time - reversal symmetry , pyrochlore iridates  @xcite , hgcr@xmath4se@xmath5  @xcite , and a superlattice with a normal insulator and a topological insulator with magnetic doping  @xcite </S>",
