    "the fluid interface _ splash singularity _ was introduced by castro , crdoba , fefferman , gancedo , & gmez - serrano in @xcite in the context of the one - phase water waves problem . as shown in figure [ fig1 ] , a _ splash singularity _ occurs when a fluid interface remains locally smooth but self - intersects in finite time .",
    "using methods from complex analysis together with a clever transformation of the equations , castro , crdoba , fefferman , gancedo , & gmez - serrano @xcite showed that a splash singularity occurs in finite time for the water waves equations . in coutand & shkoller @xcite",
    ", we showed the existence of a finite - time splash singularity for the one - phase incompressible euler equations with free - boundary using a very different approach , founded upon an approximation of the self - intersecting fluid domain by a sequence of smooth fluid domains , each with non self - intersecting boundary .",
    "for one - phase flow , it is the vacuum state on one side of the interface which permits this finite - time interface self - intersection , and neither surface tension nor magnetic fields nor other inviscid regularizations of the interface change this fact @xcite , and even stationary solutions , having a splash singularity , have been shown to exist ( see crdoba , enciso , & grubic @xcite ) .",
    "[ fig1 ]    on the other hand , for the two - phase incompressible euler equations , wherein the moving interface is a vortex sheet , it was proven by fefferman , ionescu , & lie @xcite and coutand & shkoller @xcite that a splash singularity can not occur in finite - time while the interface remains locally smooth . in particular , there is a fundamental difference in the behavior of the fluid interface when vacuum is replaced with fluid in the mathematical model .",
    "since these results have been established for inviscid flows , it is natural to ask if splash singularities can occur for viscous flows modeled by the incompressible navier - stokes equations with a moving free - surface .",
