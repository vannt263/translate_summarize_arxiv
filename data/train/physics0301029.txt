    "<S> the stability of a thermocapillary flow in an extended cylindrical geometry is analyzed . </S>",
    "<S> this flow occurs in a thin liquid layer with a disk shape when a radial temperature gradient is applied along the horizontal free surface . besides the aspect ratio </S>",
    "<S> , a second parameter related to the local curvature is introduced to describe completely the geometrical effects . </S>",
    "<S> we recover classical hydrothermal waves as predicted by smith and davis  @xcite but the properties of these waves are shown to evolve with the curvature parameter , thus leading to a non uniform pattern over the cell . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , it is shown that the problem is not invariant with respect to the exchange of the hot and cold sides .    </S>",
    "<S> keywords : hydrodynamic instabilities , hydrothermal waves , thermocapillary flow </S>"
