    "<S> the mapping between a classical length and inverse temperature as imaginary time provides a direct equivalence between the casimir force of a classical system in @xmath0 dimensions and internal energy of a quantum system in @xmath1@xmath2@xmath0@xmath3@xmath4 dimensions . </S>",

    "<S> we propose protocols for the experimental realization of critical casimir forces for periodic boundaries through state - of - the - art cold - atom and solid - state experiments .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction - _ the confinement of a fluctuating field generates forces on the confining surfaces . </S>",
    "<S> this concept , originally developed by casimir @xcite for the confinement of the quantum electromagnetic field between two perfectly conducting plates can be extended to classical fields and critical phenomena in which diverging order parameter fluctuations at a second - order phase transition are cut off by the confinement @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> being a critical phenomenon , this casimir effect takes a form characterized by a universal scaling function that depends on both the bulk and the surface universality classes @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> indeed , boundary conditions play a significant role in determining the scale and even the sign of the casimir force as shown in both numerical @xcite , and field theoretic studies . for the latter , </S>",

    "<S> , it typically fails in the ordered phase , and converges poorly at the critical point @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> on the experimental side , casimir forces have so far been measured in wetting films of liquid helium , driven through the lambda transition into the superfluid phase , or in binary mixtures showing a demixing transition in the dense fluid phase @xcite .    </S>",
