    "<S> we present a finite - temperature diagrammatic perturbation theory of third harmonic generation ( thg ) in doped graphene . </S>",
    "<S> we carry out calculations of the third - order conductivity in the scalar potential gauge , highlighting a subtle cancellation between a fermi surface contribution , which contains only power laws , and power - law contributions of inter - band nature . only logarithms survive in the final result . </S>",
    "<S> we conclude by presenting quantitative results for the up - conversion efficiency at zero and finite temperature . </S>",
    "<S> our results shed light on the on - going dispute over the dependence of thg on carrier concentration in graphene .    </S>",

    "<S> @xcite demonstrated experimentally that the third - order optical susceptibility of graphene is remarkably large ( @xmath0 ) and only weakly dependent on wavelength in the near - infrared frequency range . </S>",
    "<S> third harmonic generation ( thg ) from mechanically exfoliated graphene sheets has been measured by kumar et al .  </S>",

    "<S> finally , hong et al .  </S>",

    "<S> our approach has the advantage of being transparent and easily extendable to 2d materials with a more complex band structure like graphene derivatives ( e.g.  bilayer graphene ) , transition - metal dichalcogenides , and few - layer black - phosphorus . </S>",

    "<S> we carry out microscopic calculations of thg in a non - interacting 2d system of massless dirac fermions ( mdfs )  @xcite in the _ scalar potential gauge _ </S>",
    "<S> ( spg ) . in this gauge , </S>",
    "<S> light - matter interactions are described by utilizing an external scalar potential , which couples to the electronic density operator . as explained in ref .  </S>",
    "<S> , this gauge is free of the pathologies that one encounters when optical properties of 2d mdfs are calculated by employing the _ vector potential gauge _ and the mdf current operator , which lacks a diamagnetic contribution  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , in the vector potential gauge , light - matter interactions are described through the minimal coupling @xmath4 in the continuum - model hamiltonian . </S>",
    "<S> the vector potential is time - dependent but uniform , implying that in this gauge the momentum @xmath5 of incident photons is set to zero from the very beginning . </S>",
    "<S> although this choice tremendously simplifies analytical calculations , it is known to miss intra - band ( i.e.  fermi surface ) contributions .    </S>",
    "<S> in this work we report an _ a priori _ unexpected cancellation between the fermi surface contribution to the third - order conductivity , which contains only power laws , and power - law contributions of inter - band nature . only logarithms survive in the final result . </S>",
    "<S> this anomalous cancellation occurs for all values of the microscopic parameters and is not tied to the linear dispersion of mdfs in single - layer graphene . </S>",
    "<S> for example , we have checked ( not shown here ) that it also occurs for massive chiral fermions in bilayer graphene  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> also , we have checked ( not shown here ) that it occurs i ) in the presence of terms that break particle - hole symmetry ( e.g.  next - nearest - neighbor hopping in the tight - binding model ) and ii ) for anisotropic 2d mdfs ( e.g.  uniaxially strained graphene ) . </S>",
    "<S> we believe that this cancellation stems from the gapless nature of the dispersion relation , as power - law terms are present in the final result for thg in gapped graphene  @xcite . </S>",
