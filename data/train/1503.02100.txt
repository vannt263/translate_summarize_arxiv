    "<S> we present an analysis of long - term photometric variability for nearby red dwarf stars at optical wavelengths . </S>",
    "<S> the sample consists of 264 m dwarfs south of dec . </S>",
    "<S> = @xmath030 with @xmath1 = 3.969.16 and @xmath2 @xmath3 1020 , corresponding to spectral types m2v  m8v , most of which are within 25 pc . </S>",
    "<S> the stars have been observed in the @xmath4 filters for @xmath5414 years at the ctio / smarts 0.9 m telescope . </S>",
    "<S> of the 238 red dwarfs within 25 pc , we find that only @xmath58% are photometrically variable by at least 20 mmag ( @xmath52% ) in the @xmath4 bands . </S>",
    "<S> only four stars have been found to vary by more than 50 mmag , including gj 1207 at 8.6 pc that experienced a single extraordinary flare , and gj 2006a , twa 8a , and twa 8b , which are all young stars beyond 25 pc linked to moving groups . </S>",
    "<S> we find that high variability at optical wavelengths over the long - term can in fact be used to identify young stars . </S>",
    "<S> overall , however , the fluxes of most red dwarfs at optical wavelengths are steady to a few percent over the long term .    </S>",
    "<S> the low overall rate of photometric variability for red dwarfs is consistent with results found in previous work on similar stars on shorter timescales , with the body of work indicating that most red dwarfs are only mildly variable . as expected , we find that the degree of photometric variability is greater in the @xmath6 band than in the @xmath7 or @xmath8 bands , but we do not find any obvious trends in variability over the long term with red dwarf luminosity or temperature . </S>",
    "<S> we highlight 17 stars that show long - term changes in brightness , sometimes because of flaring activity or spots , and sometimes because of stellar cycles similar to our sun s solar cycle . </S>",
    "<S> remarkably , two targets show brightnesses that monotonically increase ( g 169 - 029 ) or decrease ( wt 460ab ) by several percent over a decade . </S>",
    "<S> we also provide long - term variability measurements for seven m dwarfs within 25 pc that host exoplanets , none of which vary by more than 20 mmag . both as a population , and for the specific red dwarfs with exoplanets observed here , photometric variability is therefore often not a concern for planetary environments , at least at the optical wavelengths where they emit much of their light . </S>"
