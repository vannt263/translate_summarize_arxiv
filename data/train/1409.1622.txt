    "<S> the dynamics of quantum phase transitions are inevitably accompanied by the formation of defects when crossing a quantum critical point . for a generic class of quantum critical systems , </S>",
    "<S> we solve the problem of minimizing the production of defects through the use of a gradient - based deterministic optimal control algorithm . by considering a finite size quantum ising model with a tunable global transverse field , </S>",
    "<S> we show that an optimal power law quench of the transverse field across the ising critical point works well at minimizing the number of defects , in spite of being drawn from a subset of quench profiles . </S>",
    "<S> these power law quenches are shown to be inherently robust against noise . </S>",
    "<S> the optimized defect density exhibits a transition at a critical ratio of the quench duration to the system size , which we argue coincides with the intrinsic speed limit for quantum evolution .    </S>",
    "<S> _ introduction._understanding the dynamics of quantum phase transitions  @xcite is a topic of more than academic interest , as the production of defects during the crossing of a quantum critical point ( qcp ) hinders the ability to accurately prepare initial many - body states for use in quantum simulators  @xcite and quantum computation  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , the exact preparation of a many - body ground state is a highly nontrivial problem due to the difficulty of evaluating the real time dynamics of interacting many - body systems . </S>",
    "<S> achieving this goal by naively tuning a parameter of the hamiltonian is only possible if the process is carried out on impractically long time scales .    </S>",
    "<S> recently , numerous analyses for achieving an optimal passage through a qcp have emerged  @xcite . in general </S>",
    "<S> , the methods used in these studies can be classified into two categories ( see ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite for a recent review ) : i. the transitionless quantum driving proposed in ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite and developed in refs .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this type of quantum control requires the addition of generally complex interaction terms to the system . </S>",
    "<S> although such protocols have been implemented experimentally in few - body systems  @xcite , their application in realistic many - body systems remains a challenge . </S>",
    "<S> ii . quantum optimal control methods , a natural idea for tuning terms already present in the original hamiltonian . based on the universal dynamics of phase transitions , refs .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite show that the use of power law protocols to traverse a qcp enables suppression of defect formation in the thermodynamic limit . </S>",
    "<S> however , only recently have there been a few attempts to apply optimal control techniques to many - body dynamics  @xcite . in spite of these developments , </S>",
    "<S> there are no systematic algorithms to deal with the dynamical control of general many - body systems .    in this work , </S>",
    "<S> we propose using a gradient - based optimal control algorithm  @xcite to minimize excitations when crossing a qcp of a generic class of quantum critical systems . </S>",

    "<S> we stress that we seek to tune only parameters present in the original hamiltonian . as an illustration </S>",
    "<S> , we show that the kink densities formed across a paramagnetic to ferromagnetic phase transition in the quantum ising model can be greatly minimized by an optimal power law resulting from our method .    furthermore , although it has been shown that optimal control can drive few - body systems at rates up to the quantum speed limit ( qsl )  @xcite , the relation between optimal control and the qsl remains to be understood in many - body dynamics . </S>",
