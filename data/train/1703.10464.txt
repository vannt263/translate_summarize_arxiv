    "hybrid perovskite photovoltaic ( hppv ) technology @xcite is the most recent rising star in the emerging solar - cell community due to its record increase in power - conversion efficiency ( pce ) during the last four years @xcite .",
    "the current state - of - the - art hppv architecture was proposed in 2012 achieving @xmath4 pce @xcite .",
    "now the pce of hppv cells has already reached @xmath5 , overtaking the best - performing inorganic - based single - junction thin - film cells such as @xmath6 and copper - indium - gallium - selenide ( cigs ) cells @xcite .",
    "the most common photoactive material in hppv cells is methylammonium ( @xmath7 ) lead triiodide ( @xmath0 , shortened as @xmath8 hereafter ) .",
    "this hybrid perovskite material exhibits several advantageous features for photovoltaic applications , such as a band gap close to the optimal value for single - junction solar - cell absorbers @xcite , excellent absorption strength in the visible part of the solar spectrum @xcite , and high mobilities for both electron and hole transport @xcite .",
    "it can be synthesized in solution at low temperature from common starting materials that have limited harm to the environment . therefore hppv cells are considered as promising candidates that can offer clean , affordable and sustainable energy .",
    "apart from the pce improvement , recent experimental and theoretical studies in hppv technology have focused on the origin of the high mobility and low recombination rate @xcite , the observed current - voltage hysteresis @xcite , and the stability of hybrid perovskites materials @xcite . to resolve open questions in hybrid",
    "perovskites it is imperative to develop a comprehensive understanding of their atomic structure , which is both fundamental and challenging due to the structure s complexity . taking the prototype hybrid perovskite @xmath8 as an example , the central cation @xmath9 is not spherical ( as , e.g. , @xmath10 in the conventional perovskite @xmath11 ) but exhibits polarity and an orientational preference in the lattice . at low temperatures",
    ", @xmath8 assumes a minimal - energy structure with regularly - aligned @xmath9 cations and thereby a regularly - deformed inorganic @xmath12 matrix , resulting in an orthorhombic phase .",
    "conversely , at room temperature or above , the @xmath9 cations are randomly oriented due to thermal fluctuations , forming ( dynamically-)disordered structures @xcite .",
    "experimental @xcite and theoretical @xcite studies suggest that the activation energy for the reorientation of the @xmath13@xmath14 bond is of the order of @xmath15 ( corresponding to a characteristic thermal energy of @xmath16 ) .",
    "the @xmath9 ions therefore do not rotate freely at @xmath17 , but are bound to the inorganic framework by hydrogen bonds @xcite .",
    "they form localized patterns @xcite on a small length scale , which makes @xmath8 appear effectively cubic .",
    "modeling the orientational disorder of @xmath9 ions in @xmath8 is a challenging task .",
    "quantum mechanical first principles techniques are required to correctly describe the hydrogen bonding of @xmath9 ions to the inorganic cage and the corresponding distortions of the cage . however , even density - functional theory ( dft ) , which in local or semi - local approximations is currently the most computationally efficient first principles technique , can not scale up to the required length scales or the large number of candidate structures .",
    "the simple primitive - cell model is not representative of @xmath8 s atomic structure , as it effectively describes a system of infinitely many aligned polar @xmath9 ions .",
    "the dipole introduced by each @xmath9 unit can be canceled by compensating alignments of @xmath9 ions in an appropriately chosen supercell model . for such compensated models , the atomic and electronic structure , especially in the low - temperature orthorhombic and tetragonal phases ,",
    "can then be calculated at the dft or beyond level by means of small supercell models such as @xmath18{2}\\!\\times\\!\\sqrt[]{2}\\!\\times\\!2 $ ] and @xmath2 @xcite .",
    "@xmath2 supercell models have also been adopted to study the distribution of @xmath9 orientations at a finite temperature using _ ab initio _ molecular dynamics ( md ) @xcite . however , to really model disorder we would need to know the structure of @xmath8 on a length scale of a few to a few tens of single ( primitive ) cells . @xmath2 supercell models do not suffice for this purpose because of the periodic boundary conditions , while dft calculations for larger supercell models become computationally very demanding . to our knowledge , the application of dft to large mapbi@xmath19 supercells has mostly been limited to defect modeling @xcite .",
    "disordered structures , such as the behavior of domains has so far only been investigated with classical monte carlo simulations @xcite .    in our previous work @xcite ,",
    "we have comprehensively analyzed the atomic structure of hybrid perovskites using the primitive - cell model .",
    "we found several stable locations of @xmath9 in the lattice .",
    "moreover , our analysis revealed that the stability of hybrid perovskites is closely related to the deformation of the inorganic cage , which acts synergetically with the organic ions analogous to a chicken - and - egg paradox . in this work",
    ", we performed dft calculations for a number of different @xmath8 supercell models and focus on the pairs of neighboring @xmath9 ions .",
    "we devised a pair - mode description that reduces each @xmath9 ion to a dipole @xcite with discrete orientations that were adopted from our previous primitive - cell results @xcite .",
    "we then defined the relative geometry of two nearest individual dipoles as a `` pair mode '' . with the pair - mode concept , we were then able to relate the dependence of certain @xmath8 properties ( e.g. , total energy , band structure ) to the distribution of @xmath9-orientations and to the dipoles alignment .",
    "this was done by studying a series of @xmath2 supercell models .",
    "we further investigated @xmath3 supercell models focusing on the distribution of pair modes .",
    "this distribution tells us for a given dipole which dipole - orientations are preferred in its surrounding , thus providing knowledge of the local structure beyond a single @xmath8 unit cell . the atomic structure of the pair modes , as the physical origin of this pair - mode distribution , will be discussed in detail in a forthcoming paper @xcite .",

    "the considered supercell models were initialized with different @xmath9 alignments .    in our previous study @xcite we demonstrated that the `` pbe+vdw '' exchange - correlation functional produces the lattice constants of hybrid perovskite systems in good agreement with experiment , and can properly describe the interaction between the organic cations and the inorganic framework .",
