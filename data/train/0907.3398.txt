    "<S> we consider a simple model of digital memory where each cell is a reflecting medium with two possible reflectivities ( used to store a bit of information ) . adopting this model , we show that a non - classical source of light , possessing einstein - podolsky - rosen correlations , can retrieve more information than any classical source . </S>",
    "<S> this improvement is shown in the regime of high reflectivities and few photons , where the gain of information can be dramatic . as a result </S>",
    "<S> , quantum light can be used to increase storage capacities and data - transfer rates of classical digital memories .    in recent years , non - classical states of radiation </S>",
    "<S> have been exploited to achieve marvellous results in quantum information and computation books , books2 . in the language of quantum optics , </S>",
    "<S> the bosonic states of the electromagnetic field are called classical  when they can be expressed as probabilistic mixtures of coherent states . </S>",
    "<S> classical states describe practically all the radiation sources which are used in today s technological applications . by contrast </S>",
    "<S> , a bosonic state is called non - classical  when its decomposition in coherent states is non - positive @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> one of the key properties which makes a state non - classical is the quantum entanglement . in the bosonic framework , this is usually present under the form of einstein - podolsky - rosen ( epr ) correlations @xcite , meaning that the position and momentum quadrature operators of two bosonic modes are so correlated to beat the standard quantum limit @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this is a well - known feature of the two - mode squeezed vacuum ( tmsv ) state @xcite , one of the most important states routinely produced in today quantum optics labs .    in this letter </S>",
    "<S> , we show how the use of non - classical light possessing eprcorrelations can _ widely _ improve the readout of information from classical digital memories . to our knowledge , this is the first study which proves and quantifies the advantages of using non - classical light for this fundamental task , being absolutely non - trivial to identify the physical conditions that can effectively disclose these advantages ( as an example , see the recent no - go theorems of ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite applied to quantum illumination @xcite ) . </S>",
    "<S> our model of digital memory is simple but can potentially be extended to realistic optical disks , like cds and dvds , or other kinds of memories , like the barcodes . </S>",
    "<S> in fact , we consider a memory where each cell is composed by a reflecting medium with two possible reflectivities , @xmath0 and @xmath1 , used to store a bit of information . </S>",
    "<S> this memory is irradiated by a source of light which is able to resolve every single cell . </S>",
    "<S> the light focussed on , and reflected from , a single  cell is then measured by a detector , whose outcome provides the value of the bit stored in that cell . besides the signal  modes irradiating the target cell </S>",
    "<S> , we also consider the possible presence of ancillary idler  modes which are directly sent to the detector . </S>",
    "<S> the general aim of these modes is to improve the performance of the output measurement by exploiting possible correlations with the signals . adopting this model and fixing the number of photons irradiated over the memory , </S>",
    "<S> we show that a _ non - classical _ source of light with epr correlations between signals and idlers can retrieve more information than any classical source of light . </S>",
