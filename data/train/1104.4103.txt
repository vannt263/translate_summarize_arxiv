    "<S> we derive conditions under which random sequences of polarizations ( two - point symmetrizations ) converge almost surely to the symmetric decreasing rearrangement . </S>",
    "<S> the parameters for the polarizations are independent random variables whose distributions need not be uniform . </S>",
    "<S> the proof of convergence hinges on an estimate for the expected distance from the limit that yields a bound on the rate of convergence . in the special case of i.i.d .  </S>",
    "<S> sequences , almost sure convergence holds even for polarizations chosen at random from suitable small sets . as corollaries , </S>",
    "<S> we find bounds on the rate of convergence of steiner symmetrizations that require no convexity assumptions , and show that full rotational symmetry can be achieved by randomly alternating steiner symmetrizations in a finite number of directions that satisfy an explicit non - degeneracy condition . </S>",
    "<S> we also present some negative results on the rate of convergence and give examples where convergence fails . </S>"
