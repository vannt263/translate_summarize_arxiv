    "<S> in this paper , we discover a new quantum monte carlo ( qmc ) method to solve the fermion sign problem in interacting fermion models by employing majorana representation of complex fermions . </S>",
    "<S> we call it `` majorana qmc '' ( mqmc ) . mqmc simulations can be performed efficiently both at finite and zero temperatures . </S>",
    "<S> especially , mqmc is fermion sign free in simulating a class of _ spinless _ fermion models on bipartite lattices at half filling and with arbitrary range of ( unfrustrated ) interactions . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , we find a class of @xmath0 fermionic models with _ odd _ </S>",
    "<S> @xmath1 , which are sign - free in mqmc but whose sign problem can not be in solved in other qmc methods such as continuous - time qmc . to the best of our knowledge , mqmc is the _ first _ auxiliary field qmc method to solve fermion sign problem in spinless ( more generally , odd number of species ) fermion models . </S>",
    "<S> we conjecture that mqmc could be applied to solve fermion sign problem in more generic fermionic models .    interacting fermionic quantum systems with strong correlations and/or topological properties </S>",
    "<S> have attracted increasing attentions@xcite . </S>",
    "<S> nonetheless , in two and higher spatial dimensions , strongly interacting quantum systems are generically beyond the reach of analytical methods in the sense of solving those quantum models in an unbiased way . as an intrinsically - unbiased numerical method </S>",
    "<S> , quantum monte carlo simulation plays a key role in understanding physics of strongly correlated many - body systems@xcite . </S>",
    "<S> unfortunately , in simulating fermionic many - body systems , qmc often encounters the notorious fermion minus - sign problem@xcite , which arises as a consequence of fermi statistics@xcite . </S>",
    "<S> undoubtedly , generic solutions of fermion sign problems would lead to a great leap forward in understanding correlated electronic systems@xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> many qmc algorithms are based on converting an interacting fermion model into a problem of free fermions interacting with background auxiliary classical fields ; the boltzmann weight is the determinant of free fermion matrix which is a function of auxiliary fields and which can be positive , negative , or even complex . in such determinant qmc ( dqmc ) , </S>",
    "<S> when the determinants are rendered to be positive definite , we say a solution to the fermion sign problem is found . for spinful electrons , conventional strategy of solving fermion sign problem </S>",
    "<S> is to find a symmetric treatment of both spin components of electrons such that the boltzmann weight can be written as the product of two real determinants with the same sign and is then positive definite@xcite . for spinless or spin - polarized fermion models , it is usually much more difficult to solve fermion sign problem because the boltzmann weight contains only a single determinant and the usual strategy used for even species of fermions can not be directly applied here .    in this paper , based on majorana representation of fermions , </S>",
    "<S> we propose a genuinely new auxiliary field qmc approach to solve fermion sign problem in spinless fermion models . </S>",
    "<S> we observe that each complex fermion can be represented as two majorana fermions . </S>",
    "<S> consequently , we can express spinless fermion hamiltonians in majorana representation and then perform hubbard - stratonovich ( hs ) transformations to decouple interactions by introducing background auxiliary fields . under certain conditions such as particle - hole symmetry </S>",
    "<S> , we can find a symmetric treatment of two species of majorana fermions , namely the free majorana fermion hamiltonian obtained after hs transformations is a sum of two symmetric parts each involving only one species of majorana fermions , such that the boltzmann weight is a product of two identical real quantities and is then positive definite . </S>",
    "<S> this is the basic idea of the majorana approach to solve fermion sign problem in spinless or spin - polarized fermion models which we call `` majorana qmc '' ( mqmc ) . </S>",
    "<S> note that the mqmc approach proposed here is qualitatively different from the meron - cluster method@xcite and fermion bags method@xcite developed previously , all of which are based on continuous - time qmc ( ctqmc)@xcite . as far as we know , mqmc is the first qmc approach based on auxiliary fields to solve fermion sign problem in a class of spinless ( more generally , odd number of species ) fermion models . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , mqmc has an important advantage : it is much more efficient than continuous - time qmc in simulating models at low and zero temperatures ; the computation - time cost in mqmc scales as @xmath2 while it scales as @xmath3 in continuous - time qmc@xcite ( also see more recent development@xcite ) .    as an application of the sign - free mqmc algorithm , </S>",
