    "magneto - optical effects not only provide a powerful tool in probing the magnetic properties of solids , @xcite but are also of direct technological interest as phenomena to be used for high  density magneto  optical recording .",
    "@xcite up to now , however , realistic theoretical investigations were lacking , because band  structure methods using supercells , @xcite can not provide an adequate description of layered systems , for which special computational techniques such as the spin  polarized relativistic screened korringa  kohn ",
    "rostoker ( skkr ) method have been designed .",
    "@xcite furthermore , the absorptive parts of the optical conductivity tensor as obtained from the inter  band contributions , @xcite are not sufficient for magneto  optical kerr spectra calculations , since also the dissipative parts have to be known .",
    "hence , in supercell type calculations , besides the necessity to use the kramers  kronig relations , one has to include also the inter  band contributions by means of a semi  empirical drude term . @xcite",
    "only recently a better scheme was developed by two of the present authors @xcite , in which a contour integration is used to obtain the complex optical conductivity tensor as based luttinger s formula , @xcite which in turn includes all inter and intra  band contributions .",
    "@xcite combining this contour integration technique with the skkr method , realistic inter and intra  layer complex optical conductivities can be obtained for layered systems .",
    "having evaluated the inter and intra  layer optical conductivities , the magneto  optical kerr spectra can then be calculated by using a macroscopical model such as , e.g. , the two  media approach .",
    "@xcite because the layered system contains more boundaries than just the interface between the vacuum and the surface layer , the two  media approach not fully includes the dynamics of the electromagnetic waves propagation in such systems . since the pioneering work of abels in 1950 , @xcite several methods",
