    "<S> statistical pattern classification methods based on data - random graphs were introduced recently . in this approach , a random directed graph is constructed from the data using the relative positions of the data points from various classes . </S>",
    "<S> different random graphs result from different definitions of the proximity region associated with each data point and different graph statistics can be employed for data reduction . the approach used in this article </S>",
    "<S> is based on a parameterized family of proximity maps determining an associated family of data - random digraphs . </S>",
    "<S> the relative arc density of the digraph is used as the summary statistic , providing an alternative to the domination number employed previously . </S>",
    "<S> an important advantage of the relative arc density is that , properly re - scaled , it is a @xmath0-statistic , facilitating analytic study of its asymptotic distribution using standard @xmath0-statistic central limit theory . </S>",
    "<S> the approach is illustrated with an application to the testing of spatial patterns of segregation and association . </S>",
    "<S> knowledge of the asymptotic distribution allows evaluation of the pitman and hodges - lehmann asymptotic efficacy , and selection of the proximity map parameter to optimize efficacy . </S>",
    "<S> notice that the approach presented here also has the advantage of validity for data in any dimension . </S>"
