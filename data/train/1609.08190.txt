    "<S> we derive the diffusion equation for the probability density function of the configuration of a general bead - rod - spring model in short - correlated gaussian random flows . under isotropic conditions </S>",

    "<S> the study of polymer solutions generally requires a coarse - grained description of a polymer molecule . </S>",
    "<S> a successful and well - established approach consists in using bead - rod - spring models , where a polymer is described as a sequence of beads connected by rigid or elastic links  @xcite . by selecting the number of beads and the nature of the links , it is possible to build flexible , semi - flexible , or rigid molecules with various internal structures . </S>",
    "<S> bead - rod - spring models play a central role across several fields , including rheology , non - newtonian fluid mechanics , chemical physics , soft matter  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> analytical solutions of these models represent an essential step towards the derivation of constitutive equations and hence the prediction of the non - newtonian properties of polymeric fluids  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , in spite of their conceptual simplicity , the internal dynamics of bead - rod - spring models may be exceedingly complex . </S>",
    "<S> for this reason bead - rod - spring models have been solved analytically only in simplified settings . in the case of laminar flows , </S>",
    "<S> analytical results are restricted to linear velocity fields with elementary time dependence , namely steady , start - up , or oscillatory extensional and shear flows  @xcite . in the case of randomly fluctuating flows , exact solutions </S>",
    "<S> are only available for dumbbells , which are simply composed of two beads and a single elastic or rigid link  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> nevertheless , in recent years there has been a growing interest in the lagrangian dynamics of complex - shaped objects in turbulent flows , such as elastic polymers @xcite , triaxial ellipsoids @xcite , flexible fibers @xcite , crosses and jacks @xcite , isotropic helicoids and four - bead particles @xcite , chiral dipoles @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> it is therefore important to advance the analytical tools needed for the study of this problem . </S>",
    "<S> here we first derive the diffusion equation for the probability distribution of the configuration of a general bead - rod - spring polymer in a gaussian random flow with short correlation time . </S>",
    "<S> short - correlated stochastic fields have been widely employed in the theoretical study of turbulent flows and have yielded fundamental results on passive - scalar mixing and the turbulent dynamo  @xcite ; moreover , they have been used to predict the coil  </S>",
    "<S> stretch transition of polymers in turbulent flows  @xcite and the associated critical slowing down  @xcite . by using the aforementioned diffusion equation </S>",
    "<S> , we then analytically calculate the stationary configuration of the elastic rhombus model in an isotropic random flow . </S>",
    "<S> the rhombus model was introduced by curtiss , bird , and hassager  @xcite and is a prototype of a finitely extensible multibead polymer that includes both elastic and rigid internal links .    </S>",
