    "<S> in general relativity , spacetime singularities raise a number of problems , both mathematical and physical .    one can identify a class of singularities  with smooth but degenerate metric  which , under a set of conditions , allow us to define proper geometric invariants , and to write field equations , including equations which are equivalent to einstein s at non - singular points , but remain well - defined and smooth at singularities . </S>",
    "<S> this class of singularities is large enough to contain isotropic singularities , warped - product singularities , including the friedmann - lematre - robertson - walker singularities , _ </S>",
    "<S> etc._also a big - bang singularity of this type automatically satisfies penrose s weyl curvature hypothesis .    the schwarzschild , reissner - nordstrm , and kerr - newman singularities apparently are not of this benign type , but we can pass to coordinates in which they become benign . </S>",
    "<S> the charged black hole solutions reissner - nordstrm and kerr - newman can be used to model classical charged particles in general relativity . </S>",
    "<S> their electromagnetic potential and electromagnetic field are analytic in the new coordinates  </S>",
    "<S> they have finite values at @xmath0 .    </S>",
    "<S> there are hints from quantum field theory and quantum gravity that a dimensional reduction is required at small scale . a possible explanation is provided by benign singularities , because some of their properties correspond to a reduction of dimensionality .    </S>",
