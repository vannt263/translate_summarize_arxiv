    "<S> we theoretically studied the quasiparticle transport in a 2d electron gas biased in the quantum hall regime and in the presence of a lateral potential barrier . </S>",
    "<S> the lateral junction hosts the specific magnetic field dependent quasiparticle states highly localized in the transverse direction . </S>",
    "<S> the quantum tunnelling across the barrier provides a complex bands structure of a one - dimensional energy spectrum of these bound states , @xmath0 , where @xmath1 is the electron momentum in the longitudinal direction @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> such a spectrum manifests itself by a large number of peaks and drops in the dependence of the magnetic edge states transmission coefficient @xmath3 on the electron energy @xmath4 . </S>",
    "<S> e.g. , the high value of @xmath5 occurs as soon as the electron energy @xmath4 reaches gaps in the spectrum . </S>",
    "<S> these peaks and drops of @xmath3 result in giant oscillations of the transverse conductance @xmath6 with the magnetic field and/or the transport voltage . </S>",
    "<S> our theoretical analysis based on the coherent macroscopic quantum superposition of the bound states and the magnetic edge states propagating along the system boundaries , is in a good accord with the experimental observations found in ref . </S>",
    "<S> @xcite    great interest has been devoted to theoretical and experimental studies of various low - dimensional systems such as tunnel junctions , quantum point contacts ( qpc ) , quantum nanowires , and 2d electron gas based nanostructures , just to name a few . </S>",
    "<S> these systems show a large variety of fascinating quantum - mechanical effects on the macroscopic scale , e.g. the weak localization @xcite , the quantum hall effect @xcite , the macroscopic quantum tunnelling @xcite , the conductance quantization in qpcs @xcite etc .    </S>",
    "<S> the quantum - mechanical dynamics of quasiparticles and the electronic transport become even more complex and intriguing when _ bound states _ </S>",
    "<S> are present in such systems . </S>",
    "<S> indeed , it was shown in ref . </S>",
    "<S> @xcite that the bound states manifest themselves by narrow drops ( the anti - resonance ) in the gate voltage dependent conductance of qpcs . </S>",
    "<S> it is well known also that the bound states naturally arising on the boundaries of various systems such as graphene nanoribbons @xcite , nanowires @xcite , 2d electron gas under magnetic field , greatly influence the transport properties .    </S>",
