    "<S> * topological physics in photonic systems have attracted great attentions in recent years . in this work , we theoretically study the one and two dimensional photonic quasicrystal resonator lattices characterized by the first and second chern number , which show exotic boundary states within the photonic energy band gap . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , the second chern number protected edge states has opened up new possibilities for realizing topological physics of dimensions higher than three in photonic systems , which is highly sought for . </S>",
    "<S> such photonic systems can be easily experimentally realized in regular photonic crystal with dielectric rods in air , by varying the radius of the rods , so we propose experiments realizing our predictions . </S>",
