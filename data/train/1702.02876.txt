    "<S> vector - borne diseases with reservoir cycles are complex to understand because new infections come from contacts of the vector with humans and different reservoirs . in this scenario , the basic reproductive number @xmath0 of the system where the reservoirs are not included could turn out to be less than one , yet , an endemic equilibrium be observed . </S>",
    "<S> indeed , when the reservoirs are taken back into account , the basic reproductive number @xmath1 , of only vectors and reservoirs , explains the endemic state . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , reservoirs cycles with a small basic reproductive number could contribute to reach an endemic state in the human cycle . </S>",
    "<S> therefore , when controlling for the spread of a disease , it could not be enough to focus on specific reservoir cycles or only on the vector . in this work </S>",
    "<S> , we created a simple epidemiological model with a network of reservoirs where @xmath1 is a bifurcation parameter of the system , explaining disease endemicity in the absence of a strong reservoir cycle . </S>",
    "<S> this simple model may help to explain transmission dynamics of diseases such as chagas , leishmaniasis and dengue . </S>"
