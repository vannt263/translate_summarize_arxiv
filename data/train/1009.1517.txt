    "<S> we point out that the multiple integral defining the sought quantity for a bipartition of sizes @xmath0 is formally identical ( upon simple algebraic manipulations ) to the one providing the probability density of landauer conductance in open chaotic cavities supporting @xmath1 and @xmath2 electronic channels in the two leads . </S>",
    "<S> known results about the latter can then be straightforwardly employed in the former problem for both systems with broken ( @xmath3 ) and preserved ( @xmath4 ) time reversal symmetry . </S>",
    "<S> the analytical results , yielding a continuous but not everywhere analytic distribution , are in excellent agreement with numerical simulations . </S>"
