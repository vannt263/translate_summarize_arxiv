    "in this paper we have established the ultimate limits of adaptive quantum metrology and adaptive quantum channel discrimination for a wide class of discrete - variable channels , which includes fundamental transformations for qubits and qudits . by exploiting the technique of teleportation stretching  @xcite ,",
    "we have been able to simplify the most general entanglement - based adaptive protocols for quantum parameter estimation and quantum channel discrimination into much simpler block versions , where the output states are simply expressed in terms of choi matrices of the encoding channels .    in this way",
    ", we have been able to compute the maximal value achievable by the quantum fisher information ( for the estimation problem ) and the minimum value achievable by the error probability ( for the discrimination problem ) .",
    "our formulas are surprisingly simple and pave the way for the exact quantification of the maximal improvement allowed by quantum mechanics beyond classical strategies . in turn",
    ", this contributes to explore the ultimate limits of several crucial applications , which includes quantum imaging and quantum sensing .",
    "further work should be directed to extend the current results to continuous - variable systems  @xcite , in order to establish the ultimate performances for adaptive estimation and discrimination of bosonic channels .",

    "<S> we consider the problem of estimating a classical parameter encoded in a quantum channel , assuming the most general strategy allowed by quantum mechanics . this strategy is based on the exploitation of an unlimited amount of pre - shared entanglement plus the use of adaptive probings , where the input of the channel is interactively updated during the protocol . </S>",
    "<S> we show that , for the wide class of teleportation - stretchable channels in finite dimension , including all pauli channels and erasure channels , the quantum fisher information can not exceed an ultimate bound given by the choi matrix of the encoding channel . </S>",
    "<S> we also extend our methods and results to quantum channel discrimination , finding a corresponding ultimate bound for the minimum error probability . </S>",
    "<S> thus , our findings establish the ultimate precision limits that are achievable in quantum metrology and quantum discrimination for the most basic models of discrete - variable quantum channels .    </S>",
    "<S> quantum information is today a mature science . </S>",
    "<S> developed for quantum systems of various nature  @xcite , it is already generating new quantum technologies with the potential of revolutionizing the classical state - of - the - art . </S>",
    "<S> a prominent filed is certainly that of quantum metrology  @xcite , which has recently attracted substantial investments  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> quantum metrology studies the optimal quantum strategies for the estimation of classical parameters which are encoded in quantum transformations ; it has many important applications , from enhancing the precision of large scale gravitational wave detectors  @xcite , to improving frequency standards  @xcite and clock synchronization  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> another notable application is quantum imaging , which non - trivial implications for microscopy and astronomical observations  @xcite .    from a theoretical point of view </S>",
    "<S> , it is of paramount importance to establish the ultimate limits that we can achieve by using the most powerful protocols of quantum metrology , with no restriction on the use of entanglement , classical side - channels and feedback assistance . assuming that a parameter - dependent transformation is stored in a black box , the most general estimation strategy allowed by quantum mechanics </S>",
    "<S> can always be formulated as a protocol involving two parties , alice preparing the inputs , and bob getting the outputs . </S>",
    "<S> these parties access an unlimited amount of pre - shared entanglement and they can exploit unlimited two - way classical communication , used to implement adaptive local operations . </S>",
    "<S> this means that alice may continuously update her input based on bob s feedback , while bob can optimize his quantum operations based on alice s feedforward . </S>",
    "<S> the two parties can also join together and apply coherent measurements on all their quantum systems .    due to the feedback , </S>",
    "<S> such kinds of adaptive protocols are very difficult to study , so that adaptive quantum metrology has only very limited literature  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , very recently , ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite has introduced a novel technique , dubbed teleportation stretching , which allows one to reduce an adaptive quantum protocol to a much simpler block ( i.e. , non - adaptive ) protocol , achieving the same task of the original one . </S>",
    "<S> this is possible as long the original protocol is implemented over suitable stretchable channels , which are covariant with respect to quantum teleportation . </S>",
    "<S> ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite identified this class for both discrete- and continuous - variable systems . by applying teleportation stretching to adaptive protocols for quantum ( and private ) communication , ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite derived exact formulas for the two - way assisted quantum ( and private ) capacities of a number of fundamental channels , including bosonic lossy channels , quantum - limited amplifiers , dephasing and erasure channels in arbitrary dimension .    in this paper , </S>",
    "<S> we show how teleportation stretching can simplify the most general entanglement - assisted adaptive protocols for parameter estimation into a simple non - adaptive form . </S>",
    "<S> this allows us to compute the ultimate achievable precision in the estimation of classical parameters encoded in discrete - variable stretchable channels , which include all pauli channels , but also non - pauli ones such as the erasure channels . </S>",
    "<S> we formulate the ultimate precision in terms of maximally - achievable quantum fisher information  @xcite , which can not violate a simple fidelity bound based on the choi matrix of the encoding channel . </S>",
    "<S> surprisingly , we also found that the availability of pre - shared entanglement , combined with the covariance properties of the stretchable channels , allows us to achieve the ultimate fidelity bound asymptotically in the limit of many probings , and without the need of real - time feedback - assisted adaptation of the input .    </S>",
    "<S> we then extend our results to quantum channel discrimination  @xcite , where the black - box contains two possible stretchable channels , selected with the same a priori probability . in this case </S>",
    "<S> , we consider the most general entanglement - assisted adaptive protocol for quantum discrimination , which can be completely reduced by teleportation stretching . by optimizing over all these protocols </S>",
