    "<S> recently there has been much effort in understanding topological phases of matter with gapless bulk excitations , which are characterized by topological invariants and protected intrinsic boundary states . here </S>",
    "<S> we show that topological semimetals of majorana fermions arise in exactly solvable kitaev spin models on a series of three dimensional lattices . </S>",
    "<S> the ground states of these models are quantum spin liquids with gapless nodal spectra of bulk majorana fermion excitations . </S>",
    "<S> it is shown that these phases are topologically stable as long as certain discrete symmetries are protected . </S>",
    "<S> the corresponding topological indices and the gapless boundary states are explicitly computed to support these results . </S>",
    "<S> in contrast to previous studies of non - interacting systems , the phases discussed in this work are novel examples of gapless topological phases in interacting spin systems .    </S>",
    "<S> [ [ introduction ] ] introduction + + + + + + + + + + + +    theoretical prediction and experimental realization of topological insulators@xcite ( tis ) pushes our understanding of topological phenomena in condensed matter physics to a new level . </S>",
    "<S> recently it was revealed that analogs of tis exist in a large class of interacting boson and spin systems , dubbed `` symmetry protected topological phases''@xcite . </S>",
    "<S> these topological phases are analogs of one another due to the existence of gapless surface states protected by symmetries , in spite of an energy gap for bulk excitations . similar to tis </S>",
    "<S> , a rich topology also exists in semimetals of weakly - interacting electrons featuring protected boundary excitations@xcite , such as weyl semimetals with surface fermi arcs@xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this raises a natural question : are there analogs of topological semimetals in interacting boson / spin systems , which harbor both gapless bulk excitations and protected surface states ? here we provide a positive answer to this question , in the ground states of the kitaev model@xcite on a series of three - dimensional trivalent lattices .    </S>",

    "<S> in addition , a three - dimensional @xmath2 spin liquid with a two - dimensional fermi surface was explored on the hyperoctagon lattice@xcite . in this work , </S>",
