    "<S> we propose to observe many - body localization in cold atomic gases by realizing a bose - hubbard chain with binary disorder and studying its non - equilibrium dynamics . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , we show that measuring the difference in occupation between even and odd sites , starting from a prepared density - wave state , provides clear signatures of localization . as hallmarks of the many - body localized phase </S>",
    "<S> we confirm , furthermore , a logarithmic increase of the entanglement entropy in time and poissonian level statistics . </S>",
    "<S> our numerical density - matrix renormalization group calculations for infinite system size are based on a purification approach which allows to perform the disorder average exactly , thus producing data without any statistical noise , and with maximal simulation times of up to a factor @xmath0 longer than in the clean case .    </S>",
    "<S> cold atomic gases with or without optical lattices are an ideal platform to realize model hamiltonians of strongly correlated quantum systems by offering an unprecedented control over the microscopic parameters @xcite . among the many achievements are the observation of the superfluid to mott insulator transition for a bose gas held in a three - dimensional optical lattice @xcite , the realization of the tonks - girardeau regime in a quasi one - dimensional ( 1d ) bose gas @xcite , and the simulation of the non - equilibrium dynamics in an almost integrable one - dimensional quantum system @xcite .    while these experiments have all been performed on very clean systems , </S>",
    "<S> there is also a tremendeous interest in building quantum simulators for models with disorder . </S>",
    "<S> this interest is sparked by the unavoidable presence of disorder and impurities in real materials which can lead to completely new physics , such as the kondo effect and anderson localization @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> for cold atomic gases there have been several different approaches to realize disorder . </S>",
    "<S> the first experiments have employed quasi - periodic lattices or laser speckles to study anderson localization in effectively non - interacting bose condensates in one and three dimensions @xcite . as an alternative </S>",
    "<S> it has been suggested to use the repulsive interactions between two different species of atoms  with one being effectively immobile  to obtain a binary disorder potential for the mobile species on the scale of the lattice constant @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> experimentally , the localization of bosonic atoms by fermionic impurities has been demonstrated @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> theoretically , it has been suggested by anderson @xcite that a localized phase might be stable against small interactions , a result which has been supported by a recent study @xcite leading to a renewed interest in many - body localization ( mbl ) . in interacting spin chains with a random magnetic field drawn from a box distribution , in particular </S>",
    "<S> , a transition is found between a delocalized , ergodic phase at weak disorder and a many - body localized ( mbl ) phase at strong disorder @xcite . in an mbl phase , where all many - body eigenstates are localized , a simple picture emerges : in this case one can separate a chain @xcite into segments of length @xmath1 where @xmath2 is the localization length . </S>",
