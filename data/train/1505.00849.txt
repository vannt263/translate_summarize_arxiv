    "massive stars are believed to have a decisive effect on the physical and chemical evolution of galaxies through their energetic feedbacks , including ultraviolet ( uv ) radiation , stellar winds , bipolar outflows , hii regions , and eventually supernova explosions .",
    "nevertheless , our understanding about how massive stars form is not yet well established  ( see @xcite for a review ) .",
    "it is hard to capture the early evolutionary phases of massive stars due to their fast evolution . in addition",
    ", observational information can be easily contaminated because the objects are distant and typically form in clusters .",
    "for these reasons , observational and theoretical works regarding massive star formation are still relatively limited .",
    "therefore , characterizing the various early phases of massive star formation in a coherent way is important to better understand massive star formation .",
    "hcn and its geometrical isomer hnc are two fundamental and ubiquitous molecules in the dense interstellar medium .",
    "they appear to be a tracer of high - density gas , and hcn has been used as a tracer of infall motion in star forming regions  @xcite .",
    "above all , since the @xmath5 abundance ratio  is known to strongly depend on the kinetic temperature  @xcite , it can be a useful tool to explore the physical and chemical conditions of star - forming regions . although both hcn and hnc are known to form in equal measure through the following dissociative recombination  @xcite , @xmath6 the @xmath5 abundance ratio  has been shown to vary by regions .",
    "for example , observational studies of the orion molecular cloud ( omc-1 ) showed that the abundance ratio increases from 5 at the cold edge to 80 in the warm core  @xcite .",
    "to explain such a variation in the @xmath5 abundance ratio , many mechanisms have been suggested that the neutral ",
    "neutral reaction below , which selectively consumes hnc , is likely the main cause of the temperature dependence of the hcn / hnc abundance ratio .",
    "@xmath7    @xcite hypothesized that the above reaction is only effective at high temperature , and empirically set the rate coefficient to reproduce the variance of the hcn / hnc abundance ratio in omc-1 .",
    "in addition , @xcite theoretically analyzed the neutral ",
    "neutral reaction using quantum  chemical calculation , and the resulting rate coefficient for the neutral ",
    "neutral reaction has been reflected in the umist  @xcite and kida  @xcite chemical reaction networks with the associated conventional chemical models .",
    "@xcite suggested that an activation energy barrier for the reaction is as high as 2000  k so that the reaction is not effective at a temperature lower than 100  k. however , this explanation does not fully account for the fluctuation of the @xmath5 abundance ratio  in observations . for example , @xcite and @xcite fitted the @xmath5 abundance ratio  for the dataset of @xcite by modifying the rate coefficient in a semi  quantitative way .",

    "such a discrepancy between the observational works and the theoretical works still remains unresolved  @xcite .",
    "there have been several attempts to measure the @xmath5 abundance ratio  toward massive star forming regions  @xcite .",
    "however , many of those studies dealt with limited phases of massive star formation or suffered from the optical depth effect in the calculation of abundances . in this study",
    ", we determine the @xmath5 abundance ratio  based on optically thin lines toward various evolutionary stages related to massive star formation ; infrared dark clouds ( irdcs ) , high - mass protostellar objects ( hmpos ) and ultracompact hii regions ( uchiis ) .",
    "irdcs were identified by the msx and spitzer surveys as dark extinction features against the bright galactic mid - infrared background  @xcite . since these objects contain cold ( @xmath1025  k ) and dense ( @xmath11 ) cores with strong ( sub)millimeter emissions ( e.g. , rathborne et al .",
    "2006 ) , they are thought to be the ideal birthplaces of massive stars .",
    "hmpos are luminous infrared ( @xmath12 ) point - like sources without associated radio continuum emission  @xcite .",
    "they have higher gas temperatures than irdcs and mostly show outflow activities ( e.g. , @xcite ) .",
    "thus they are believed to be massive protostars undergoing active accretion .",
    "uchiis are very small ( d@xmath130.1 pc ) , dense ( @xmath14 ) , and bright ( @xmath15 ) ionized regions  @xcite .",
    "the objects are considered to represent the childhood of hii regions .",
    "they are the most evolved among the three phases discussed here but are still relatively early phases of massive star formation .",
