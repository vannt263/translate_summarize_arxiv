    "<S> this paper is in the form of an essay . </S>",
    "<S> it defines fractal tops and code space structures associated with set - attractors of hyperbolic iterated function systems ( ifss ) . </S>",
    "<S> the fractal top of an ifs is associated with a certain shift invariant subspace of code space , whence the entropy of the ifs , and of its set - attractor , may be defined . given any ordered pair of hyperbolic ifss , each with the same number of maps , there is a natural transformation , constructed with the aid of fractal tops , whose domain is the attractor @xmath0of the first ifs and whose range is contained in the attractor @xmath1 of the second ifs . </S>",
    "<S> this transformation is continuous when the code space structure of the ifs is `` contained in '' the code space structure of the second ifs , and is a homeomorphism between @xmath0 and @xmath1 when the code space structures are the same . </S>",
    "<S> conversely , if two ifs are homeomorphic then they possess the same code space structure . </S>",
    "<S> hence we obtain that two ifs attractors are homeomorphic then they have the same entropy . </S>",
    "<S> several examples of fractal transformations and fractal homeomphisms are given . </S>"
