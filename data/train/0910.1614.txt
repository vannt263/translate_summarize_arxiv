    "<S> an analysis of archival mid - infrared ( mid - ir ) spectra of seyfert galaxies from the _ spitzer space telescope _ </S>",
    "<S> observations is presented . </S>",
    "<S> we characterize the nature of the mid - ir active nuclear continuum by subtracting a template starburst spectrum from the seyfert spectra . </S>",
    "<S> the long wavelength part of the spectrum contains a strong contribution from the starburst - heated cool dust ; this is used to effectively separate starburst - dominated seyferts from those dominated by the active nuclear continuum . within the latter category , the strength of the active nuclear continuum drops rapidly beyond @xmath0 . on average , type </S>",
    "<S> 2 seyferts have weaker short - wavelength active nuclear continua as compared to type 1 seyferts . </S>",
    "<S> type 2 seyferts can be divided into two types , those with strong poly - cyclic aromatic hydrocarbon ( pah ) bands and those without . </S>",
    "<S> the latter type show polarized broad emission lines in their optical spectra . </S>",
    "<S> the pah - dominated type 2 seyferts and seyfert 1.8/1.9s show very similar mid - ir spectra . </S>",
    "<S> however , after the subtraction of the starburst component , there is a striking similarity in the active nuclear continuum of all seyfert optical types . </S>",
    "<S> pah - dominated seyfert 2s and seyfert 1.8/1.9s tend to show weak active nuclear continua in general . </S>",
    "<S> a few type 2 seyferts with weak / absent pah bands show a bump in the spectrum between 15 and 20@xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> we suggest that this bump is the peak of a warm ( @xmath2200@xmath3 ) blackbody dust emission , which becomes clearly visible when the short - wavelength continuum is weaker . </S>",
    "<S> this warm blackbody emission is also observed in other seyfert optical sub - types , suggesting a common origin in these active galactic nuclei . </S>"
