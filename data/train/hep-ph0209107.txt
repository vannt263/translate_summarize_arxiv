    "we have developed and proved a method for the calculation of the coefficients proportional to @xmath30_+$ ] of the non - singlet parton splitting functions , whose knowledge , for example , is important for nnll resummations .",
    "the method is based on the factorization properties of the splitting functions , and on the exponentiation of eikonal cross sections .",
    "we illustrated the method with the rederivation of the 1- and 2-loop coefficients @xmath2 and @xmath3 , as well as the @xmath5 terms at order @xmath202 .",
    "we presented the result for the term proportional to @xmath6 at three loops , which coincides with the approximate result obtained by vogt @xcite .",
    "the full splitting functions at three loops are currently being computed by moch , vermaseren , and vogt @xcite with the help of the operator product expansion .",
    "their results for the @xmath6-term @xcite provide a further check of our calculations .    although a calculation via the ope provides the complete @xmath18 , or equivalently , @xmath12-dependence of the splitting functions , it involves a large number of diagrams and complex expressions at higher orders .",
    "a computation at three loops is a formidable task , and it seems unlikely that higher loop calculations will be completed in the near future .",
    "however , for certain observables , large logarithms due to soft and/or collinear radiation become numerically important , and need to be resummed to as high a level in logarithms as possible . our method ,",
    "although limited to only the computation of @xmath11 , has the advantage that higher - order computations are much less complex than within conventional methods , because the number of graphs is greatly reduced , and the expressions involved are relatively simple in lcopt .",
    "moreover , a fully computerized implementation of the algorithm should be straightforward .",
    "therefore , the computation of the coefficients @xmath11 at four or even higher loops may be within reach .",
