    "<S> various authors have shown that , near the onset of a period - doubling bifurcation , small perturbations in the control parameter may result in much larger disturbances in the response of the dynamical system . </S>",
    "<S> such amplification of small signals can be measured by a gain defined as the magnitude of the disturbance in the response divided by the perturbation amplitude . in this paper , </S>",
    "<S> the perturbed response is studied using normal forms based on the most general assumptions of iterated maps . </S>",
    "<S> such an analysis provides a theoretical footing for previous experimental and numerical observations , such as the failure of linear analysis and the saturation of the gain . </S>",
    "<S> qualitative as well as quantitative features of the gain are exhibited using selected models of cardiac dynamics . </S>"
