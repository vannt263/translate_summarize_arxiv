    "<S> the spin - gyromagnetic factor @xmath0 is an important physical quantity relating the magnetic dipole moment of a particle to its spin . </S>",
    "<S> the electron @xmath0-factor _ in vacuo _ is one of the best model - based theoretical predictions ever made , showing agreement with the measured value up to ten parts per trillion @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , for electrons in a material the @xmath0-factor is modified with respect to its value _ in vacuo _ because of environment interactions . here , </S>",
    "<S> we report a detailed calculation of the @xmath0-factor in graphene , taking into account the full electromagnetic interaction described by pseudo - qed @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> because the electrons in graphene have spin and valley internal degrees of freedom , one may associate a @xmath1-factor to both flavors . </S>",
    "<S> experiments indicate that the spin @xmath1-factor @xmath2 , depending on the graphene substrate @xcite , whereas the valley @xmath1-factor is colossal , @xmath3 . </S>",
    "<S> we determine the radiative corrections to the spin @xmath1-factor both in the high- and low - doping regimes , and find a very good agreement between theory and experiments @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , we show that the gigantic value measured recently for the valley @xmath1-factor @xcite can also be well explained within the pseudo - qed formalism .    </S>",
    "<S> the energy dispersion of the electrons in solid - state materials strongly depends on the lattice geometry . in the case of graphene </S>",
    "<S> , the honeycomb lattice leads to a zero - mass relativistic - like dispersion @xmath4 . </S>",
    "<S> this characteristic allows us to relate the electrons in graphene to free dirac massless particles in ( 2 + 1 ) dimensions ( d ) @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , the fact that these electrons propagate with a velocity @xmath5 three hundred times smaller than the speed of light @xmath6 @xcite has important implications upon the physical properties of the system .    for long </S>",
    "<S> , graphene was believed to be an effectively noninteracting system . </S>",
    "<S> the recent measurement of the fractional quantum hall effect @xcite , which is a typical feature of strongly correlated systems , has changed this paradigm . </S>",
    "<S> the importance of interactions in graphene was further attested by the experimental observation of the renormalization of the fermi velocity @xcite , as had been predicted by theories that accounted for _ static _ interactions @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , dynamical effects have proven to be relevant in some cases , by generating novel quantum topological states that would not emerge in the static approximation @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> although the electrons in graphene are confined to move on a plane , the electromagnetic field through which they interact extends throughout the 3d space . </S>",
    "<S> integrating away out - of - the - plane photons , one obtains an effective interaction that is non - local in space and time . in spite of being fully 2d </S>",
    "<S> , it conveys all properties of the genuine 3d electromagnetic interaction . </S>",
    "<S> this effective theory has been called pseudo - qed because it contains pseudo - differential operators . </S>",
    "<S> it has been shown to respect causality @xcite , scale invariance , the huygens principle , and unitarity @xcite , apart from exhibiting an @xmath7 static coulomb potential . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , the propagator in pseudo - qed in coordinate space coincides with the one of qed3 in momentum space @xcite , and these two theories are dual to each other @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> inspired by the relevance of electron - electron interactions in graphene , we investigate in this paper the _ anisotropic _ pseudo - qed by considering a term that breaks lorentz invariance in our quantum - field - theory formalism . </S>",
    "<S> we can easily include the lorentz violating term in the matter field , since the nonlocal gauge field produces the full electromagnetic interaction , independently on whether the matter is relativistic or not @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> we then determine the spin gyromagnetic factor ( @xmath8-factor ) in two regimes , at small and large carrier density @xmath9 , and show that it compares very well to the experimental data available in the literature @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , we compare our predictions for the valley gyromagnetic factor ( @xmath10-factor ) with experimental measurements obtained for epitaxial graphene grown on sic @xcite and graphene flakes on graphite @xcite , finding in both cases an excellent agreement . </S>",
    "<S> our results exploit the relevance of interactions in estimating the g - factor in graphene in particular , and 2d relativistic condensed - matter systems in general , besides establishing a solid theoretical ground that allows one to understand puzzling and disparate experimental results observed so far .    </S>",
