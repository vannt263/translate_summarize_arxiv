    "<S> hopf bifurcations in fast - slow systems of ordinary differential equations can be associated with surprising rapid growth of periodic orbits . </S>",
    "<S> this process is referred to as canard explosion . </S>",
    "<S> the key step in locating a canard explosion is to calculate the location of a special trajectory , called a maximal canard , in parameter space . </S>",
    "<S> a first - order asymptotic expansion of this location was found by krupa and szmolyan @xcite in the framework of a `` canard point''-normal - form for systems with one fast and one slow variable . </S>",
    "<S> we show how to compute the coefficient in this expansion using the first lyapunov coefficient at the hopf bifurcation thereby avoiding use of this normal form . </S>",
    "<S> our results connect the theory of canard explosions with existing numerical software , enabling easier calculations of where canard explosions occur . </S>"
