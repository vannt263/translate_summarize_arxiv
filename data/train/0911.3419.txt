    "<S> long - period transiting exoplanets provide an opportunity to study the mass - radius relation and internal structure of extrasolar planets . </S>",
    "<S> their studies grant insights into planetary evolution akin to the solar system planets , which , in contrast to hot jupiters , are not constantly exposed to the intense radiation of their parent stars . </S>",
    "<S> observations of secondary eclipses allow investigations of exoplanet temperatures and large - scale exo - atmospheric properties . in this short paper </S>",
    "<S> , we elaborate on , and calculate , probabilities of secondary eclipses for given orbital parameters , both in the presence and absence of detected primary transits , and tabulate these values for the forty planets with the highest primary transit probabilities . </S>"
