    "<S> we study the feynman integral for the three - banana graph defined as the scalar two - point self - energy at three - loop order . </S>",
    "<S> the feynman integral is evaluated for all identical internal masses in two space - time dimensions . </S>",
    "<S> two calculations are given for the feynman integral ; one based on an interpretation of the integral as an inhomogeneous solution of a classical picard - fuchs differential equation , and the other using arithmetic algebraic geometry , motivic cohomology , and eisenstein series . </S>",
    "<S> both methods use the rather special fact that the feynman integral is a family of regulator periods associated to a family of @xmath0 surfaces . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the integral is given by a sum of elliptic trilogarithms evaluated at sixth roots of unity . </S>",
    "<S> this elliptic trilogarithm value is related to the regulator of a class in the motivic cohomology of the @xmath0 family . </S>",
    "<S> we prove a conjecture by david broadhurst that at a special kinematical point the feynman integral is given by a critical value of the hasse - weil @xmath1-function of the @xmath0 surface . </S>",
    "<S> this result is shown to be a particular case of deligne s conjectures relating values of @xmath1-functions inside the critical strip to periods . </S>"
