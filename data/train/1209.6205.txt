    "+ branching processes ( and especially birth and death processes ) with mutations have many applications in biology . in carcinogenesis @xcite , they can model the evolution of cancerous cells . in @xcite ,",
    "kendall modeled carcinogenesis by a birth and death process where mutations occur during life according to an inhomogeneous poisson process . in @xcite , cancerous cells are modeled by a multitype branching process where a cell is of type @xmath6 if it has undergone @xmath6 mutations and where the more a cell has undergone mutations , the faster it grows .",

    "the authors study the arrival time of the first resistant cell and the number of resistant cells , in a model of cancerous cells undergoing a medical treatment and becoming resistant after having experienced a certain number of mutations .    branching processes with mutations are also used in epidemiology .",
    "epidemics , and especially their onset , can be modeled by birth and death processes , where particles are infected hosts , births are disease transmissions and deaths are recoveries or actual deaths . in @xcite",
    ", stadler provides a statistical method for the inference of transmission rates and of the reproductive value of epidemics in a birth and death model with mutations . in @xcite , lambert & trapman",

    "let us also mention the existence of models , e.g. @xcite , of phage reproduction within a bacterium by a ( possibly time - inhomogeneous ) birth and death process with poissonian mutations , where particles model phage in the vegetative phase ( dna strands in the bacterium without protein coating ) and death is interpreted as phage maturing ( reception of protein coating ) .    in ecology ,",
