    "<S> we study spatial symmetry in general @xmath0-stacked multilayer graphene to illustrate how electronic spectra at the two valleys are related in a magnetic field . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the lattice of multilayers with an even number of layers , as well as that of monolayer graphene , satisfy spatial inversion symmetry , which rigorously guarantees valley degeneracy in the absence of time - reversal symmetry . </S>",
    "<S> a multilayer with an odd number of layers ( three or more ) lacks inversion symmetry , but there is another transformation imposing an approximate valley degeneracy , which arises because the low - energy hamiltonian consists of separate monolayerlike and bilayerlike parts . </S>",
    "<S> we show that an external electrostatic potential generally breaks valley degeneracy in a magnetic field , in a markedly different manner in odd and even multilayers . </S>"
