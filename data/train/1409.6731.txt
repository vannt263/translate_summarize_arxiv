    "<S> in this paper , we consider an expanding construction of a distributed control system , which is obtained by adding a new subsystem one after the other , until all @xmath0 subsystems , where @xmath1 , are included in the distributed control system . </S>",
    "<S> it is assumed that a small random perturbation enters only into the first subsystem and is then subsequently transmitted to the other subsystems . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , for any @xmath2 , the distributed control system , compatible with the expanding construction , which is obtained from the first @xmath3 subsystems , satisfies an appropriate hrmander condition . as a result of this , </S>",
    "<S> the diffusion process is degenerate , i.e. , the backward operator associated with it is a degenerate parabolic equation . </S>",
    "<S> our main interest here is to prevent the diffusion process ( that corresponds to a particular subsystem ) from leaving a given bounded open domain . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , we consider a risk - sensitive version of the mean escape time criterion with respect to each of the subsystems . </S>",
    "<S> using a variational representation , we characterize the risk - sensitive escape control for the diffusion process as the lower and upper values of an associated stochastic differential game . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we comment on the implication of our results , where one is also interested in evaluating the performance of the risk - sensitive escape control , when there is some modeling error in the distributed control system . </S>"
