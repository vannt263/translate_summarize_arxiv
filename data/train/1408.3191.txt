    "<S> we study the possibility of generating non - zero reactor mixing angle @xmath0 by perturbing the @xmath1 symmetric neutrino mass matrix . </S>",
    "<S> the leading order @xmath1 symmetric neutrino mass matrix originates from type i seesaw mechanism whereas the perturbations to @xmath1 symmetry originate from type ii seesaw term . </S>",
    "<S> we consider four different realizations of @xmath1 symmetry : bimaximal mixing(bm ) , tri - bimaximal mixing ( tbm ) , hexagonal mixing ( hm ) and golden ratio mixing ( grm ) all giving rise to @xmath2 but different non - zero values of solar mixing angle @xmath3 . </S>",
    "<S> we assume a minimal @xmath1 symmetry breaking type ii seesaw mass matrix as a perturbation and calculate the neutrino oscillation parameters as a function of type ii seesaw strength . </S>",
    "<S> we then consider the origin of non - trivial leptonic cp phase in the charged lepton sector and calculate the lepton asymmetry arising from the lightest right handed neutrino decay by incorporating the presence of both type i and type ii seesaw . </S>",
    "<S> we constrain the type ii seesaw strength as well as leptonic cp phase ( and hence the charged lepton sector ) by comparing our results with experimental neutrino oscillation parameters as well as planck bound on baryon to photon ratio . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we extend our analysis on lepton flavour violating decays like @xmath4 and @xmath5 due to exchange of tev scale higgs triplet scalar within the low scale type ii seesaw framework . </S>",
    "<S> the branching ratios for these lepton flavour processes are examined with the small type ii perturbation term @xmath6 and the estimated values are very close to the experimental bound coming from current search experiments . </S>"
