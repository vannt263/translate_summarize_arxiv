    "<S> the kondo effect is the many - body screening of a local spin by a cloud of electrons at very low temperature . </S>",
    "<S> it has been proposed as an explanation of the zero - bias anomaly in quantum point contacts where interactions drive a spontaneous charge localization . </S>",
    "<S> however , the kondo origin of this anomaly remains under debate , and additional experimental evidence is necessary . </S>",
    "<S> here we report on the first phase - sensitive measurement of the zero - bias anomaly in quantum point contacts using a scanning gate microscope to create an electronic interferometer . </S>",
    "<S> we observe an abrupt shift of the interference fringes by half a period in the bias range of the zero - bias anomaly , a behavior which can not be reproduced by single - particle models . </S>",
    "<S> we instead relate it to the phase shift experienced by electrons scattering off a kondo system . </S>",
    "<S> our experiment therefore provides new evidence of this many - body effect in quantum point contacts .    </S>",
    "<S> quantum point contacts @xcite ( qpcs ) are small constrictions in high - mobility two - dimensional electron gases ( 2degs ) controlled by a metallic split gate at the surface of a semiconductor heterostructure . despite their apparent simplicity , </S>",
    "<S> they reveal complex many - body phenomena which defy our understanding . </S>",
    "<S> when these quasi - one - dimensional ballistic channels are sufficiently open , electrons are perfectly transmitted via each available transverse mode @xcite , and the conductance is quantized in units of the conductance quantum @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> below the first conductance plateau however , this single - particle picture fails due to the increasing importance of many - body effects . </S>",
    "<S> an additional shoulder shows up in the linear conductance curve around @xmath1 , called the 0.7 anomaly @xcite , and a narrow peak of enhanced conductance appears around zero bias in the non - linear conductance curves at low enough temperature , called the zero - bias anomaly @xcite ( zba ) . </S>",
    "<S> the peak behavior versus temperature and magnetic field was shown to share strong similarities with the kondo effect in quantum dots @xcite ( qds ) , i.e. the many - body screening of a local spin by conduction electrons below a characteristic temperature @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , deviations of the zba from the established kondo effect have been reported @xcite , and the occurrence of this effect in qpcs remains a debated issue @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> because of enhanced electron interactions at low density , a spontaneous charge localization is predicted in qpcs below the first plateau @xcite , showing similarities with the one - dimensional wigner crystallization @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this phenomenon is supported by two recent experiments where localized states with even and odd numbers of charges have been observed @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the development of a kondo effect is therefore expected at very low temperature , but its specific properties for a self - consistently localized state have not been calculated yet , due to the complexity of the problem . in this unsettled situation , the zba remains the subject of intensive investigations , and any new information pointing to a kondo origin is important .        </S>",
    "<S> here we use a scanning gate microscope @xcite ( sgm ) to create a fabry - prot ( fp ) cavity between the qpc and the tip @xcite , and measure the phase of the electron wave function scattered by the qpc in the zba regime . </S>",
    "<S> phase - sensitive experiments indeed provide unique information on quantum phenomena and , in the case of qpcs , will help us to clarify the microscopic origin of the zba . </S>",
    "<S> recently , a phase measurement on a qpc has been reported @xcite , but no significant deviation from the single - particle prediction has been found @xcite . in the past , the transmission phase of qds in the kondo regime was measured by embedding them in aharonov - bohm ( ab ) rings @xcite . here </S>",
    "<S> we measure instead the reflection phase of the system and observe a phase shift by @xmath2 of the interference fringes in the bias voltage range of the zba . </S>",
    "<S> this shift occurs via two phase jumps , and disappears with gate voltage and temperature in the same way as the zba . </S>",
    "<S> calculations of the reflection phase for a single - particle resonant level give a smooth shift across the resonance @xcite , in strong contrast with the two phase jumps observed in our experiment , thereby indicating a many - body origin . </S>",
    "<S> the observed behavior shows characteristic signatures of the kondo effect , where the transmission phase at the fermi energy is locked at @xmath3 in the kondo valleys @xcite , and where a `` sharp kondo double phase lapse '' is predicted as a function of source - drain bias @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> we therefore attribute the observed phase shift to the kondo effect , thus providing new evidence of this effect as the origin of the zero - bias anomaly in qpcs .    </S>",
