    "decoherence in quantum systems is a major obstacle for quantum computation and information processing , causing errors during the implementation of various gate operations .",
    "decoherence is well - studied in single qubit systems which are realized by pseudo two - level systems in superconducting quantum interference devices ( squids ) @xcite , nuclear magnetic sonance ( nmr ) @xcite , and quantum dot @xcite systems .",
    "although single qubits form the fundamental unit for quantum computing , multi - qubit systems are required to perform non - trivial gate operations and implementing quantum algorithms @xcite .",
    "the study of decoherence in multi - qubit systems is , therefore , quite important .",
    "it is a more involved task here , as one has to look at the effects of environment on both the loss of coherence and the entanglement of the initial state . as entanglement",
    "is believed to exponentially speed up the implementation of quantum algorithms @xcite , the time scales for the entanglement loss are as important as the decoherence time scales .    in this work",
    "we study the spin dynamics of two electrons confined in a quantum dot geometry .",
    "that is , we inject a pair of electrons ( in a definite spin state ) into a confined region of nuclear spin bath .",
    "the two electrons are coupled to each other by an exchange interaction , and to the nuclear spins through a contact hyperfine interaction .",
    "the exchange interaction strength @xmath0 is a measure of the overlap of the spatial wave functions of the two electrons ( qubits ) .",
    "there are two distinct physical cases we have to consider .",
    "the spatial wave functions of the electrons can have a strong overlap , indicating a large value of @xmath0 .",
    "also both electrons interact with the same set of neighboring nuclear spins here , which indicates a common nuclear bath for the two - qubit system . on the other hand ,",
    "when the two electrons are physically apart , the overlap is quite small indicating a smaller value of @xmath0 . here ,",
    "each electron interact with a different set of neighboring nuclear spins , and thus implying a different spin bath for each electron .",
    "since @xmath0 can be controlled by gate voltages one can tune from one regime to the other .",
    "cent experiments @xcite have shown a strong dependence of decoherence time scales on the exchange interaction between the qubits . in a semi - classical treatment given by taylor et al @xcite , the interaction of the qubits with the nuclear spins is replaced by an effective magnetic field .",
    "the decoherence time scales are found by performing an ensemble average of the spin expectation values over different configurations of the magnetic field . a recent review by hanson et al @xcite summarizes the results of experimental procedures employed in studying the spins in few - electron quantum dots .",
    "gywat et al @xcite have studied the evolution of qubits interacting with an off - resonant cavity ( instead of a nuclear spin bath ) .",
    "decoherence of two electrons , interacting with different nuclear baths , in the presence of a magnetic field has been studied by zhang et al @xcite .",
    "the decay of the singlet - triplet correlator in a double dot system was studied by coish and loss @xcite .",
    "the evolution of a central two - qubit system interacting with a spin bath is studied by several authors @xcite . a bath - induced entanglement between",
    "initially unentangled qubits has been studied earlier @xcite .",
    "it was shown by storcz et al @xcite that the errors during gate operations involving coupled solid state qubits can be reduced when both the qubits are coupled to a common bath at low temperatures .",

    "<S> the time evolution of spin states of two electrons interacting with a nuclear spin bath in a quantum dot system is studied . </S>",
    "<S> the hyperfine interaction between the electrons and the nuclear spins is modeled by an isotropic heisenberg interaction , and the interaction between the electron spins by heisenberg exchange . </S>",
    "<S> depending on the extent of the overlap between the spatial wave functions of the electrons , there are two physically different cases , namely the two qubits either interact with the same set of nuclear spins or they see different nuclear spin environment . in the two cases , </S>",
    "<S> the decoherence of the two - qubit state is studied analytically . </S>",
    "<S> we have identified a class of two - qubit states which have a rich dynamics when the exchange interaction between the qubits becomes large in comparison to the hyperfine interaction strengths . </S>",
    "<S> the decoherence time scale is determined as a function of the bath - spin distribution and the polarizations of the initial two - qubit state . </S>",
    "<S> states with large decoherence times are identified by performing a minimization over all the two - qubit pure states . </S>"
