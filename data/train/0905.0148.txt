    "<S> cooling atoms , molecules , and even macroscopic objects to the quantum mechanical ground state of their motion represents an important step towards comprehensive control of large or complex quantum mechanical systems @xcite . of particular interest are cooling methods that do not destroy the quantum mechanical coherences in other degrees of freedom . </S>",
    "<S> for example , in trapped ion quantum information processing @xcite , it is necessary to cool the motion of the ions to the ground state without affecting the internal state where the quantum information is stored . </S>",
    "<S> this has been accomplished by sympathetic cooling of the processing ion using a laser cooled ion of another species @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> an all optical method that may allow one to cool a particle without causing decoherence of the internal states is cavity cooling . </S>",
    "<S> when a particle is illuminated with monochromatic laser light , the spectrum of the scattered light is distributed around the incident frequency due to the particle s motion . </S>",
    "<S> if an optical resonator is used to enhance selected portions of the emission spectrum , such that the average emission frequency is larger than that of the incident light , energy conservation implies that the particle is cooled in the scattering process ( cavity cooling ) @xcite . unlike conventional doppler cooling @xcite </S>",
    "<S> , cavity cooling does not require the incident light to be matched to an atomic resonance , and can in principle be performed with non - resonant light . </S>",
    "<S> as light scattered far off resonance carries no information about the atom s internal state @xcite , such a process can potentially cool an atom without destroying a quantum superposition of internal states .    </S>",
