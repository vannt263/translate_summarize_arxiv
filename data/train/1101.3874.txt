    "<S> as an image of the many - to - one map of loop - erasing operation @xmath0 of random walks , a self - avoiding walk ( saw ) is obtained . </S>",
    "<S> the loop - erased random walk ( lerw ) model is the statistical ensemble of saws such that the weight of each saw @xmath1 is given by the total weight of all random walks @xmath2 which are inverse images of @xmath1 , @xmath3 . </S>",
    "<S> we regard the brownian paths as the continuum limits of random walks and consider the statistical ensemble of loop - erased brownian paths ( lebps ) as the continuum limits of the lerw model . </S>",
    "<S> following the theory of fomin on nonintersecting lerws , we introduce a nonintersecting system of @xmath4-tuples of lebps in a domain @xmath5 in the complex plane , where the total weight of nonintersecting lebps is given by fomin s determinant of an @xmath6 matrix whose entries are boundary poisson kernels in @xmath5 . </S>",
    "<S> we set a sequence of chambers in a planar domain and observe the first passage points at which @xmath4 brownian paths @xmath7 first enter each chamber , under the condition that the loop - erased parts @xmath8 make a system of nonintersecting lebps in the domain in the sense of fomin . </S>",
    "<S> we prove that the correlation functions of first passage points of the brownian paths of the present system are generally given by determinants specified by a continuous function called the correlation kernel . </S>",
    "<S> the correlation kernel is of eynard - mehta type , which has appeared in two - matrix models and time - dependent matrix models studied in random matrix theory . </S>",
