    "<S> they have emerged as a major tool for the study of lattice hamiltonians that either model low-@xmath3 thermodynamic properties of interesting strongly correlated materials  @xcite , or provide concrete realizations of novel phases in such condensed matter systems  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , models of _ geometrically frustrated magnets _  @xcite , in which antiferromagnetic interactions compete with each other due to the geometry of the exchange pathways , have typically remained beyond the reach of qmc methods . </S>",

    "<S> the average sign decreases exponentially with system size and inverse temperature , leading to unmanageably large statistical errors in the estimation of physical quantities . </S>",
    "<S> a similar sign - problem crops up in diverse settings ranging from qcd to strongly - correlated metals , and a general solution is considered unlikely  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> limited progress has been possible in a few cases , for instance in anisotropic systems in which the frustration only affects @xmath4 ( thereby allowing sign - free simulation in the @xmath5 basis  @xcite ) , or when the sign problem of the full theory can be finessed at low t by working with a low - energy effective hamiltonian which has no sign problem  @xcite . for some models , sign - free simulations </S>",
    "<S> are possible by virtue of specific symmetries of the hamiltonian  @xcite . in other strongly correlated systems with a full - fledged sign problem </S>",
