    "returning to our initial motivation , we seek a computationally tractable way to include pairing effects in a global model of nuclear binding energies , going beyond the bcs theory .",
    "two attractive possibilities are the lipkin - nogami method and the rpa , in particular if the pairing interaction has a separable form . in this paper",
    ", we used a solvable two - level pairing hamiltonian to show that the rpa formula for the correlation energy reproduces well the exact solutions both in a normal - fluid and a superfluid phases . on the contrary ,",
    "the lipkin - nogami method is considerably less accurate for weak pairing , and therefore is not suitable in transition regions . as a consequence ,",
    "the lipkin - nogami method fails to reproduce the correct mass number dependence of the binding energy around a shell closure .",
    "the correlation energy associated with the number fluctuation for the neutron mode in o isotopes was shown to be order of 2 mev and has a relatively strong mass dependence .",
    "although the correlation energy is small compared with the absolute value of the ground state energy , this suggests that including the correlation energy in the rpa provides a promising way to develop a better microscopic systematic theory for nuclear binding energies .",

    "<S> we study the correlation energy associated with the pair fluctuations in bcs theory . </S>",
    "<S> we use a schematic two - level pairing model and discuss the behavior of the correlation energy across shell closures , including the even - odd differences . </S>",
    "<S> it is shown that the random phase approximation ( rpa ) to the ground state energy reproduces the exact solutions quite well both in the normal fluid and in the superfluid phases . </S>",
    "<S> in marked contrast , other methods to improve the bcs energies , such as lipkin - nogami method , only work when the strength of a pairing interaction is large . </S>",
    "<S> we thus conclude that the rpa approach is better for a systematic theory of nuclear binding energies . </S>"
