    "type ia supernovae ( sne  ia ) are among the most luminous of astrophysical events , making them useful probes of the distant universe .",
    "sne  ia gave the first concrete evidence that the expansion of the universe is accelerating @xcite , and they have been used to constrain cosmological parameters such as @xmath10 , @xmath11 , and @xmath12 @xcite and the nature and evolution of dark energy @xcite .",
    "this is possible because sne  ia have a well - established relationship between their optical peak luminosity and rate of brightness decline , making them excellent standardizable candles ( see @xcite , @xcite , @xcite , and @xcite for reviews of the subject ) .",
    "this standardizing is done by calibrating the peak luminosity with distance - independent observables such as the light - curve shape",
    ". one common measure of this shape is the number of magnitudes the @xmath13 band declines in the first 15 days after maximum light , @xmath14   @xcite .",
    "another is how much the light curve must be stretched to match a template @xcite , or by fitting the light curves to templates @xcite .",
    "the importance of distance - independent luminosity indicators has led to the development of a large number of photometric and spectroscopic methods ( see also @xcite ) .    the observed trend that more luminous sne have broader light curves",
    "will be referred to here generically as the luminosity - width relation .",
    "the underlying cause of this relation is believed to be the amount of @xmath15ni formed in the sn explosion and the resulting change in the temperature and ionization evolution @xcite .",
    "the exact shape of the relation is determined by the interplay between @xmath15ni , which contributes to the sn luminosity and affects the shape of the light curve via its effect on the opacity , and the total amount of fe - group elements produced , including stable isotopes which only affect the opacity @xcite .",
    "there is scatter about this relation , which may be due to differences in the progenitor mass or metallicity @xcite or asymmetries @xcite .",
    "a few peculiar outliers to the luminosity - width relation have also been found , including sne 2000cx @xcite , 2002cx @xcite , and 2003fg @xcite .",
    "more recent studies have expanded the standard - candle utility beyond optical wavelengths . @xcite and @xcite have reported that sne  ia might be standard candles in the near - infrared ( ir ; @xmath16 bands ) at the 0.2 mag level without a clear dependence on the decay slope .",
    "recent observations by @xcite strengthen this claim , although @xcite and @xcite have shown that some sne that are subluminous in the optical are also subluminous in the near - ir , possibly indicating a continuous or bimodal distribution of the near - ir absolute magnitudes of rapidly declining sne .    at shorter wavelengths , @xcite has recently shown that the @xmath17-band light curves of sne  ia are standardizable , similar to the optical , and can be used to determine the extinction and distances .",
    "the scatter is larger than in the optical @xmath13 and @xmath18 bands , and @xcite have found discrepancies in determinations of cosmological parameters when including rest - frame @xmath19-band measurements .",
    "the source of the discrepancy is still not certain , underlying the importance of understanding sn  ia behavior in this regime .",
    "here we take the next step to shorter wavelengths , reaching beyond the atmospheric cutoff to compare the absolute magnitudes of sne in the uv .",
    "the redshifting of the light from distant sne leads to two approaches .",
    "one can either follow the well - understood rest - frame optical light by observing at longer wavelengths , or continue to observe in the optical and study the evolution of the rest - frame uv . for moderate - redshift sne",
    ", both approaches can be taken , and the information can be combined to place strong constraints on the reddening and distances of the objects . at higher redshifts ,",
    "however , fewer rest - frame optical bands are observable , especially from the ground .",
    "thus , rest - frame uv observations will be crucial for future studies of high - redshift sne .",
