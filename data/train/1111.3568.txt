    "<S> i propose quantum versions of the ziv - zakai bounds as alternatives to the widely used quantum cramr - rao bounds for quantum parameter estimation . from a simple form of the proposed bounds , </S>",
    "<S> i derive both a `` heisenberg '' error limit that scales with the average energy and a limit similar to the quantum cramr - rao bound that scales with the energy variance . </S>",
    "<S> these results are further illustrated by applying the bound to a few examples of optical phase estimation , which show that a quantum ziv - zakai bound can be much higher and thus tighter than a quantum cramr - rao bound for states with highly non - gaussian photon - number statistics in certain regimes and also stay close to the latter where the latter is expected to be tight .    in statistics , </S>",
    "<S> one often has to resort to analytic bounds on the error to assess the performance of a parameter estimation technique . for the mean - square error criterion , </S>",
    "<S> the cramr - rao bounds ( crbs ) are the most well known @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> although the crbs are asymptotically tight in the limit of infinitely many trials , it is well known that the bounds can grossly underestimate the achievable error when the likelihood function is highly non - gaussian and the number of trials is limited @xcite . for such situations , </S>",
    "<S> the ziv - zakai bounds ( zzbs ) , which relate the mean - square error to the error probability in a binary hypothesis testing problem , have been found to be superior alternatives in many cases @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> these bounds are often much tighter in the highly non - gaussian regime and can also follow the crbs closely for large numbers of trials @xcite . in physics , the zzbs have also been applied to gravitational - wave astronomy @xcite </S>",
    "<S> .    the crbs can be generalized for quantum parameter estimation , where one estimates an unknown parameter such as phase shift , mirror position , time , or magnetic field by measuring a quantum system such as an optical beam , an atomic clock , or a spin ensemble @xcite . given a quantum state to be measured , the quantum crbs ( qcrbs ) </S>",
    "<S> give error bounds that hold for any measurement , but since they are always less tight to the error than the corresponding classical crbs @xcite , the qcrbs share all the shortcomings of their classical counterparts . </S>",
    "<S> this is an outstanding problem in quantum metrology , as there have been many claims based on the qcrbs or other similarly rudimentary arguments about the parameter - estimation capabilities of certain exotic quantum states @xcite , but such claims can not be justified if the bounds are not tight . </S>",
    "<S> similar to the classical case , one expects the qcrbs to be tight when many copies of the quantum object are available @xcite ; the question is how many . </S>",
    "<S> for example , braunstein _ et al . _  found numerically that the crb for phase estimation using the quantum state proposed by @xcite is tight only when the number of copies exceeds a threshold @xcite , while genoni _ </S>",
    "<S> et al . _  found experimentally that the qcrb for a phase - diffused coherent state is tight only after @xmath0 copies have been measured @xcite .    in this letter , </S>",
    "<S> i propose quantum ziv - zakai bounds ( qzzbs ) as alternatives to the qcrbs for quantum parameter estimation . </S>",
    "<S> the qzzbs relate the mean - square error in a quantum parameter estimation problem to the error probability in a quantum hypothesis testing problem , and should be contrasted with previous studies that consider quantum interferometry as a binary decision problem only @xcite . to demonstrate the versatility of the proposed bounds , i show that a simple form of the bounds can produce both a `` heisenberg '' error limit ( h limit @xcite ) that scales with the average energy @xcite and another limit similar to the qcrb that scales with the energy variance . </S>",
    "<S> i then illustrate these results by applying the bound to a few examples of optical phase estimation . </S>",
    "<S> an especially illuminating example is the state proposed by rivas and luis , the qcrb of which can be arbitrarily low @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> i show that a qzzb can be used to rule out any actual error scaling that is better than the h - limit scaling for multiple copies of this state . beyond a certain number of copies , </S>",
    "<S> the qzzb starts to follow the qcrb closely , thus revealing the regime where the qcrb must be overly optimistic and indicating more precisely the asymptotic regime where the qcrb is tight . </S>",
    "<S> although the qzzbs are also lower error bounds and not guaranteed to be tight either , the study here and the usefulness of their classical counterparts suggest that they should be similarly useful for quantum parameter estimation in general , whenever one is suspicious about the tightness of the qcrbs .    </S>",
