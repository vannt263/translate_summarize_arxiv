    "<S> the fermionic condensate is investigated in a ( 2 + 1)-dimensional conical spacetime in the presence of a circular boundary and a magnetic flux . </S>",
    "<S> it is assumed that on the boundary the fermionic field obeys the mit bag boundary condition . for irregular modes , </S>",
    "<S> we consider a special case of boundary conditions at the cone apex , when the mit bag boundary condition is imposed at a finite radius , which is then taken to zero . </S>",
    "<S> the fermionic condensate is a periodic function of the magnetic flux with the period equal to the flux quantum . for both exterior and interior regions , </S>",
    "<S> the fermionic condensate is decomposed into boundary - free and boundary - induced parts . </S>",
    "<S> two integral representations are given for the boundary - free part for arbitrary values of the opening angle of the cone and magnetic flux . at distances from the boundary larger than the compton wavelength of the fermion particle </S>",
    "<S> , the condensate decays exponentially with the decay rate depending on the opening angle of the cone . if the ratio of the magnetic flux to the flux quantum is not a half - integer number , for a massless field the boundary - free part in the fermionic condensate vanishes , whereas the boundary - induced part is negative . for half - integer values of the ratio of the magnetic flux to the flux quantum , the irregular mode gives non - zero contribution to the fermionic condensate in the boundary - free conical space .    </S>",
