    "relativistic spherical shells are important in two context , in astrophysics as models of elliptical galaxies , bulges of disc galaxies and dark matter haloes , and general relativity as sources of vacuum gravitational fields , cosmological models , gravitational collapse , among others @xcite .",
    "the pioneering work on relativistic shells is due to israel @xcite , who also specialized his general framework to spherical symmetry @xcite .",
    "relativistic spherical shells with varying thickness were studied in @xcite for a schwarzschild type conformastatic spacetime .",
    "thin shells representing the field of a disk are also important to model galaxies , accretion disks , and the superposition of a black hole and a galaxy .",
    "static thin disks without radial pressure were first studied by bonnor and sackfield @xcite , and morgan and morgan @xcite , and with radial pressure also by morgan and morgan @xcite .",
    "several classes of exact solutions of the einstein field equations corresponding to static thin disks with or without radial pressure have been obtained by different authors @xcite .",
    "rotating thin disks that can be considered as a source of a kerr metric were presented by bick and ledvinka @xcite , while rotating disks with heat flow were were studied by gonzlez and letelier @xcite .",

    "we consider models of spherical thick shells for a majumdar - papapetrou type conformastatic spacetime .",
    "the models are constructed from a seed newtonian potential - density pair .",
    "the formalism used allows to build different spherically symmetric sources for this spacetime such as spheres , spherical shells , disks and haloes . as simple application",
    ", we construct a family of plummer type relativistic spherical shells .",
    "these objects are then used to construct dust disk models surrounded of haloes of matter .",
    "the structures considered are essentially of infinite extension . however , since the energy density decreases rapidly one can define a cut off radius and , in principle , to consider these objects as finite .",
