    "<S> the first to emerge , as simple function singularities , were the weyl groups @xmath1 and @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> they were followed by the @xmath3 , @xmath4 and @xmath5 as simple boundary singularities or , equivalently , functions invariant under the involution @xcite . since then appearance of weyl groups in a singularity problem became a kind of criterion of naturalness of the problem @xcite .    the next to receive a singularity realisation was the list of all coxeter groups , in the classification of stable lagrangian maps @xcite . some of the shephard - todd groups appeared in @xcite in the context of simple functions equivariant with respect to a cyclic group action . and </S>",
    "<S> finally , the first examples of complex crystallographic groups came out in @xcite in connection with the symmetries of parabolic functions @xmath6 and @xmath7 . </S>",
    "<S> the affine reflection groups appeared there as monodromy groups , which is similar to the first realisations of other classes of reflection groups . </S>",
    "<S> this time it was the equivariant monodromy corresponding to the symmetry eigenspaces @xmath8 in the vanishing second homology on which the intersection form @xmath9 has corank 1 .    </S>",
    "<S> this paper completes the study of cyclically equivariant parabolic functions started in @xcite and considers the @xmath0 singularities . </S>",
