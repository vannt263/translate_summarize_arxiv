    "<S> chimera states are complex spatiotemporal patterns in networks of identical oscillators , characterized by the coexistence of synchronized and desynchronized dynamics . here we propose to extend the phenomenon of chimera states to the quantum regime , and uncover intriguing quantum signatures of these states . </S>",
    "<S> we calculate the quantum fluctuations about semiclassical trajectories and demonstrate that chimera states in the quantum regime can be characterized by bosonic squeezing , weighted quantum correlations , and measures of mutual information . </S>",
    "<S> our findings reveal the relation of chimera states to quantum information theory , and give promising directions for experimental realization of chimera states in quantum systems .    in classical systems of </S>",
    "<S> coupled nonlinear oscillators , the phenomenon of chimera states , which describes the spontaneous emergence of coexisting synchronized and desynchronized dynamics in networks of identical elements , has recently aroused much interest  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> these intriguing spatio - temporal patterns were originally discovered in models of coupled phase oscillators . in this case , they exist due to nonlocal coupling between identical elements of the ensemble  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> there has been extensive work on the theoretical investigation of chimera states  @xcite , followed by spectacular experimental observations of chimera states in optical  @xcite , chemical  @xcite , mechanical  @xcite , electronic  @xcite , optoelectronic @xcite , and electrochemical  @xcite setups .    </S>",
    "<S> while synchronization of classical oscillators has been well studied since the early observations of huygens in the @xmath0th century  @xcite , synchronization in quantum mechanics has only very recently become a focus of interest . </S>",
    "<S> for example , quantum signatures of synchronization in a network of globally coupled van der pol oscillators have been investigated  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> related works focus on the dynamical phase transitions of a network of nanomechanical oscillators with arbitrary topologies characterized by a coordination number  @xcite , and the semiclassical quantization of the kuramoto model by using path integral methods  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> contrary to classical mechanics , in quantum mechanics the notion of phase - space trajectory is not well defined . as a consequence </S>",
    "<S> , one has to define new measures of synchronization for continuous variable systems like optomechanical arrays  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> these measures are based on quadratures of the coupled systems and allow one to extend the notion of phase synchronization to the quantum regime  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> additional measures of synchronization open intriguing connections to concepts of quantum information theory  @xcite , such as decoherence - free subspaces  @xcite , quantum discord  @xcite , entanglement  @xcite , and mutual information  @xcite . despite the intensive theoretical investigation of quantum signatures of synchronized states , to date </S>",
    "<S> , studies of the quantum manifestations of chimera states are still lacking .    in this letter </S>",
    "<S> we study the emergence of chimera states in a network of coupled quantum van der pol oscillators . unlike in previous work  @xcite </S>",
    "<S> , we address here the fundamental issue of the dynamical properties of chimera states in a continuous variable system . considering the chaotic nature of chimera states  @xcite </S>",
    "<S> , we study the short - time evolution of the quantum fluctuations at the gaussian level . </S>",
    "<S> this approach allows us to use powerful tools of quantum information theory to describe the correlations in a nonequilibrium state of the system . </S>",
    "<S> we show that quantum manifestations of the chimera state appear in the covariance matrix and are related to bosonic squeezing , thus bringing these signatures into the realm of observability in trapped ions  @xcite , optomechanical arrays  @xcite , and driven - dissipative bose - einstein condensates  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> we find that the chimera states can be characterized in terms of rnyi quantum mutual information . </S>",
    "<S> our results reveal that the mutual information for a chimera state lies between the values for synchronized and desynchronized states , which extends in a natural way the definition of chimera states to quantum mechanics .    </S>",
