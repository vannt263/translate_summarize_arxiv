    "<S> we investigate multipartite entanglement in rings of arbitrary spins with antiferromagnetic interactions between nearest neighbors . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , we show that the non - degenerate ground state of rings formed by an even number ( @xmath0 ) of spins is @xmath0-partite entangled , and exchange energy can thus be used as a multipartite - entanglement witness . </S>",
    "<S> we develop a general approach to compute the energy minima corresponding to biseparable states , and provide numerical results for a representative set of systems . despite its global character </S>",
    "<S> , exchange energy also allows a spin - selective characterization of entanglement . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , in the presence of a magnetic defect , one can derive separability criteria for each individual spin , and use exchange energy for detecting entanglement between this and all the other spins .    </S>",
    "<S> entanglement is a peculiar feature of composite quantum systems and has been recognized as a key resource in many fields of quantum technology @xcite . in particular , the generation and detection of multipartite entanglement ( me ) has been recently obtained in diverse physical systems @xcite . besides their fundamental interest , multipartite entangled states have important applications in quantum computation @xcite and in quantum metrology , where they allow one to reach high sensitivities , beyond the standard quantum limit @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> more generally , me can also be found in the ground and thermal states of prototypical many - body systems , such as spin systems with isotropic , @xmath1 and noncollinear ising interactions @xcite .    a practical tool for detecting ( multipartite ) entanglement is represented by entanglement witnesses @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> such operators are defined such that their expectation value can exceed a given threshold only in the presence of some specific form of entanglement @xcite . as the knowledge of the state </S>",
    "<S> is not required in this approach , entanglement witnesses are especially convenient with condensed matter systems , where such knowledge is in general unattainable , due to the large number of degrees of freedom @xcite . in spin systems , </S>",
    "<S> routinely measured thermodynamic quantities - such as the magnetic susceptibility @xcite , inelastic neutron scattering @xcite , and exchange energy @xcite - correspond to witnesses for spin - pair entanglement . </S>",
    "<S> thus , the experimental detection of entanglement is allowed even in systems where local constituents can not be locally addressed . along the same lines , </S>",
    "<S> spin squeezing inequalities allow the detection of multipartite entanglement in the vicinity of specific states , through the measurement of collective spin operators @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> exchange energy also represents a witness for block @xcite and multipartite entanglement @xcite . </S>",
