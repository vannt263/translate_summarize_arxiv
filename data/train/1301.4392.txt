    "<S> we study the photocurrent response of topological insulator surface states to circularly polarized light for arbitrary oblique incidence . </S>",
    "<S> we describe the surface states within a dirac model , including several perturbations such as hexagonal warping , nonlinear corrections to the mode velocity , and applied magnetic fields . </S>",
    "<S> we find that the photogalvanic current is strongly suppressed for the usual orbital coupling , prompting us to include the weaker zeeman coupling . </S>",
    "<S> we find that the helicity - independent photocurrent dominates over the helicity - dependent contributions .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction._topological insulators ( ti ) recently emerged as a central theme in condensed matter physics.@xcite the intense interest in this new state of matter is rooted in their unique properties.@xcite in addition to a band gap , tis have conducting surface states with remarkable properties . </S>",
    "<S> they are protected against backscattering by time - reversal symmetry , and are helical : each surface - momentum state possesses a unique spin direction . </S>",
    "<S> the unique properties of the surface are responsible for their exotic electromagnetic properties,@xcite and might be used to realize topological superconducting phases hosting majorana modes when brought into contact with s - wave superconductors.@xcite bismuth - based compounds were among the first materials predicted to be three - dimensional tis,@xcite a prediction verified experimentally by angle - resolved photoemission@xcite and scanning tunneling spectroscopy.@xcite    the surface states also exhibit exotic optical properties . gapped surface states are predicted to cause giant kerr and faraday rotations of polarized light.@xcite the helical nature of surface states is expected to make their photocurrent response to electromagnetic radiation rather unique.@xcite as illustrated in fig .  </S>",
    "<S> [ fig : schematic](a ) , due to spin - selection rules , one might expect that circularly polarized light excites the surface states anisotropically around the fermi surface , thus inducing a @xmath0 electric current . </S>",
    "<S> such photocurrents constitute an interesting probe of surface states in tis ( see , e.g. , ref . ) . </S>",
    "<S> an important motivation for investigating the photoresponse of tis is that helicity - dependent currents are expected to emerge solely due to the surface , as they require the breaking of inversion symmetry precluding a second - order photogalvanic effect.@xcite also , bulk electronic states in tis consist of kramers pairs , which rules out a helicity dependence of the photocurrent as well.@xcite    here we investigate ti surface photocurrents within a minimal model of the surface states motivated by @xmath1 , and obtain rather surprising results . the incident light couples to the surface electrons in two ways : through orbital minimal coupling ( peierls substitution ) , @xmath2 , and through the zeeman energy . </S>",
    "<S> usually , the orbital effect is expected to dominate strongly , and the zeeman coupling is neglected . here , however , we find that the orbital component of the photocurrent vanishes for the simplest model of a perfect dirac cone , as all surface - electron spins lie in the plane of their motion , and light excites carriers isotropically around the dirac cone </S>",
    "<S> . the orbital coupling can induce photocurrents only when perturbations of the ideal dirac cone are included : hexagonal warping of the fermi surface;@xcite an external magnetic field ; and a momentum - dependent correction to the fermi velocity.@xcite since these perturbations are quite small , we also include the zeeman coupling to the incident light in our analysis . surprisingly , the zeeman coupling is responsible for the dominant contribution to the surface photocurrent response , which we find to be helicity independent , linear in the zeeman coupling , and to flow against the direction of propagation of the light . </S>",
    "<S> the helicity - dependent photocurrent , suggested by the simple mechanism illustrated in fig .  [ fig : schematic](a ) , is found to be very small , i.e. , quadratic in the zeeman coupling .    _ </S>",

    "<S> conclusion._motivated by recent experiments , we studied photocurrents in topological insulators . focusing on the photocurrent response of the topological surface states , we find that the dominant photogalvanic current induced by obliquely incident circularly polarized light is helicity independent and in the plane of incidence of the light . this contribution is the result of an interference effect between the orbital and the zeeman coupling of the light to the surface electrons . </S>",
    "<S> the helicity - dependent photocurrent is found to be very small . </S>",
    "<S> although pure orbital coupling is the dominant excitation process , it does not induce a net photogalvanic charge current originating from the surface states unless when including both band curvature and an in - plane magnetic field . </S>",
    "<S> our results suggest that an understanding of the experiments may require one to extend the theory to include the bulk states , the photon drag effect , or thermoelectric effects originating from inhomogeneous laser excitation .    _ </S>",
