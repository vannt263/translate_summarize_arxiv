    "<S> random diffeomorphisms with bounded absolutely continuous noise are known to possess a finite number of stationary measures . </S>",
    "<S> we discuss dependence of stationary measures on an auxiliary parameter , thus describing bifurcations of families of random diffeomorphisms . </S>",
    "<S> a bifurcation theory is developed under mild regularity assumptions on the diffeomorphisms and the noise distribution ( e.g. smooth diffeomorphisms with uniformly distributed additive noise are included ) . </S>",
    "<S> we distinguish bifurcations where the density function of a stationary measure varies discontinuously or where the support of a stationary measure varies discontinuously .    </S>",
    "<S> we establish that generic random diffeomorphisms are stable . </S>",
    "<S> densities of stable stationary measures are shown to be smooth and to depend smoothly on an auxiliary parameter , except at bifurcation values . </S>",
    "<S> the bifurcation theory explains the occurrence of transients and intermittency as the main bifurcation phenomena in random diffeomorphisms . </S>",
    "<S> quantitative descriptions by means of average escape times from sets as functions of the parameter are provided . </S>",
    "<S> further quantitative properties are described through the speed of decay of correlations as function of the parameter .    </S>",
    "<S> random endomorphisms are studied in one dimension ; we show that stable one dimensional random endomorphisms occur open and dense and that in one parameter families bifurcations are typically isolated . </S>",
    "<S> we classify codimension one bifurcations for one dimensional random endomorphisms ; we distinguish three possible kinds , the random saddle node , the random homoclinic and the random boundary bifurcation . </S>",
    "<S> the theory is illustrated on families of random circle diffeomorphisms and random unimodal maps . </S>"
