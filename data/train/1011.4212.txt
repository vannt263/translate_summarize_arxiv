    "<S> 3d - mhd numerical simulations of bipolar , hypersonic , weakly magnetized jets and synthetic synchrotron observations are presented to study the structure and evolution of magnetic fields in fr  ii radio sources . </S>",
    "<S> the magnetic field setup in the jet is initially random . </S>",
    "<S> the power of the jets as well as the observational viewing angle are investigated . </S>",
    "<S> we find that synthetic polarization maps agree with observations and show that magnetic fields inside the sources are shaped by the jets backflow . </S>",
    "<S> polarimetry statistics correlates with time , the viewing angle and the jet - to - ambient density contrast . </S>",
    "<S> the magnetic structure inside thin elongated sources is more uniform than for ones with fatter cocoons . </S>",
    "<S> jets increase the magnetic energy in cocoons , in proportion to the jet velocity . </S>",
    "<S> both , filaments in synthetic emission maps and 3d magnetic power spectra suggest that turbulence develops in evolved sources . </S>"
