    "<S> we give the basic definition of algebraic entropy for lattice equations . </S>",
    "<S> the entropy is a canonical measure of the complexity of the dynamics they define . </S>",
    "<S> its vanishing is a signal of integrability , and can be used as a powerful integrability detector . </S>",
    "<S> it is also conjectured to take remarkable values ( algebraic integers ) .    </S>",
    "<S> the analysis of discrete dynamical systems , in particular the measure of their complexity , and possibly the detection of their integrability is a huge subject , originating in the work of poincar . </S>",
    "<S> it contains the study of the dynamics of rational maps , already a vast topic of research . </S>",
    "<S> it also contains the study of lattice equations , which are to maps what partial differential equations are to ordinary differential equations . </S>",
    "<S> our purpose here is to extend the notion of algebraic entropy , already widely used for maps  @xcite  @xcite , and recognized as an unmatched integrability detector  @xcite , to lattice equations as in  @xcite , thus introducing a measure of complexity for higher dimensional discrete dynamics .    </S>",
