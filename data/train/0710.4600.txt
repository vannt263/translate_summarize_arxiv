    "the rigidity theorem of the four - dimensional black hole space - times states that asymptotically flat , stationary , analytic spacetimes assure the existence of a axial killing vector field  @xcite .",
    "recently , the rigidity theorem was generalized to higher - dimensional space - times  @xcite . in particular , the rigidity theorem in five - dimensions guarantees at least one axial killing vector field .",
    "as conjectured by reall  @xcite , there may exist black hole solutions admitting only two commuting killing vector fields , although all of five - dimensional stationary black hole solutions found so far have three mutually commuting killing vector fields .",
    "hence , as this stage , it is natural to concentrate on asymptotically flat black hole solutions to the five - dimensional vacuum einstein equations admitting three commuting killing vector fields .",
    "one of authors and ida  @xcite showed that the only black hole solution with a regular event horizon homeomorphic to @xmath8 is the five - dimensional myers - perry black hole solution in this class .",
    "however , it is impossible to generalize this theorem to the solutions with horizon topology @xmath0 since there are two black ring solutions with different shapes for the same mass and the same angular momenta  @xcite .",
