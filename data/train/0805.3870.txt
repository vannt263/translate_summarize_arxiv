    "in summary , we have analyzed the stability of a dipolar bosonic condensate mixed with non - interacting fermions in a pancake trap at @xmath153 .",
    "we found that the fermions help stabilize the condensate for a significant range of boson - boson and boson - fermion interaction strengths .",
    "we then investigated the transition of the system from a gaussian - like to the density - wave ground state as a function of number of bosons , strength of the contact interaction , and trap aspect ratio .",
    "our central result is the use of a variational ansatz to show that while in a purely bosonic system the density - wave state is always unstable it can be stabilized by the admixture of even a small boson - fermion interaction .",
    "in particular , this study leads us to the conclusion that a pancake - shaped @xmath81rb-@xmath82k mixture , which has a large boson - fermion @xmath1-wave scattering length , should be absolutely stable in the dipole dominated regime . by tuning the @xmath1-wave scattering length it is possible to reach a situation characterized by the appearance of a roton instability in the excitation spectrum , leading to the existence of a stable density - wave state .",
    "however , due to the small dipole moment of rubidium atoms the transition to this stable density - wave regime needs a substantial number of atoms , of the order of @xmath154 .",
