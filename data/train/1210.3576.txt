    "<S> the field theory of the semimetal - superconductor quantum phase transition for graphene and surface states of topological insulators is presented . </S>",
    "<S> the lagrangian possesses the global @xmath0 symmetry , with the self - interacting complex bosonic order - parameter and the massless dirac fermions coupled through a yukawa term . </S>",
    "<S> the same theory also governs the quantum critical behavior of graphene near the transition towards the bond - density - wave ( kekule ) insulator . </S>",
    "<S> the local @xmath0 gauged version of the theory which describes the quantum semimetal - superconductor transition in the ultimate critical regime is also considered . due to </S>",
    "<S> the yukawa coupling the transitions are found to be always continuous , both with and without the fluctuating gauge - field . </S>",
    "<S> the critical behavior is addressed within the dimensional regularization near four space - time dimensions , and the calculation of various universal quantities , including critical exponents and the universal mass - ratio is reported .    </S>",
    "<S> dirac quasiparticles represent low - energy excitations in various low - dimensional condensed - matter systems , such as graphene and topological insulators ( tis ) . in graphene , </S>",
    "<S> pseudorelativistic dirac quasiparticles emerge from hopping of the electrons on the underlying honeycomb lattice,@xcite while on a surface of a ti , they result from an odd number of band inversions in the bulk of the system leading to a topologically non - trivial @xmath1 invariant.@xcite in both cases , the linearly dispersing dirac quasiparticles give rise to a semimetallic ground state , stable against weak electron - electron interactions.@xcite    when the repulsive interactions are sufficiently strong , however , a plethora of insulating phases can in principle be realized in graphene.@xcite furthermore , dirac fermions in graphene can also condense into _ four _ different gapped superconducting states , if the net interaction acquires an attractive component.@xcite the simplest of them , which will be the subject of the present study , is the uniform , spin - singlet @xmath2-wave pairing , favored by a sufficiently strong on - site attractive interaction.@xcite two of the remaining pairing gaps are spatially inhomogeneous spin triplets , which break the translational symmetry of the honeycomb lattice into kekule patterns.@xcite they are favored by the sufficiently strong nearest - neighbor attraction . </S>",
    "<S> finally , yet another triplet pairing with an @xmath3-wave symmetry can be stabilized by a strong second - neighbor attraction.@xcite on the other hand , due to their reduced number of fermionic components , the massless dirac fermions residing on the surface of tis can acquire a superconducting gap only by pairing into an @xmath2-wave superconducting state . </S>",
    "<S> possible inhomogeneous @xcite and chiral @xmath4 superconducting states in doped graphene,@xcite as well as proposed realizations of majorana fermions in graphene and tis , @xcite make the study of superconducting instabilities of dirac fermions in low - dimensional condensed - matter systems theoretically and experimentally interesting and timely . </S>",
