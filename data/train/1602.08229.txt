    "spiral arms of external galaxies are usually approximated by logarithmic spirals .",
    "this is a simple but reasonable assumption and is also applicable to our milky way galaxy . that the pitch angle of one spiral is constant is one of the properties of a logarithmic spiral .",
    "usually , the spiral arms of one galaxy are roughly fitted by logarithmic spirals with the same pitch ( e.g. , @xcite ) , or more accurately , different spiral arms are fitted by different pitches ( e.g. , ) . and sometimes even one spiral arm is fitted by varying the pitch angle ( e.g. , the sagittarius arm in @xcite ) .",
    "in fact , @xcite recently showed that in some external galaxies , the pitch angle in the same spiral arm varies , and the variance is as large as that among different arms within the same galaxy .",
    "so a more accurate assumption may be that the pitch angle of one segment of the arm within one galaxy is constant .",
