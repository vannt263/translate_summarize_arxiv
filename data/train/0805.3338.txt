    "recently , superconductivity has been discovered in a number of noncentrosymmetric compounds .",
    "physical properties of these materials vary considerably , from cept@xmath0si ( ref . ) , in which strong electron correlations are responsible for a heavy - fermion behavior , and the superconductivity is likely anisotropic with gap nodes , to li@xmath1pd@xmath0b and li@xmath1pt@xmath0b ( ref . ) , which show rather conventional features , described by the bardeen - cooper - schrieffer ( bcs ) theory of phonon - mediated pairing .",
    "the absence of inversion symmetry in the crystal lattice brings about important qualitative changes , both in normal and superconducting properties , compared with the centrosymmetric case .",
    "these differences are highlighted , in particular , by different responses to an external magnetic field .",
    "distinctive features of noncentrosymmetric superconductors include a strongly anisotropic spin susceptibility with a large residual component,@xcite magnetoelectric effect,@xcite and novel nonuniform ( `` helical '' ) superconducting states.@xcite    in this paper , we extend the classic theory of the upper critical field , @xmath2 , in bcs superconductors , which was developed in refs . and , see also ref . , to the noncentrosymmetric case .",
    "the spin - orbit ( so ) coupling of electrons with a noncentrosymmetric crystal lattice considerably changes the nature of single - electron states , lifting spin degeneracy of the energy bands .",
    "this modifies the zeeman coupling of band electrons with magnetic field , which in turn affects the paramagnetic pair breaking .",
    "the magnetic phase diagram of noncentrosymmetric superconductors has been previously studied in a two - dimensional case,@xcite in which the orbital effects are not important . in ref .",
    ", the upper critical field for a three - dimensional rashba superconductor was calculated in the absence of impurities .",
    "the effects of disorder on @xmath3 in the ginzburg - landau regime near the zero - field critical temperature have been investigated in ref . for an arbitrary pairing symmetry , but without paramagnetic effects . in this paper , we include both orbital and paramagnetic mechanisms of pair breaking , as well as scalar disorder , at arbitrary temperatures .",
    "we consider a `` minimal '' model of a three - dimensional weak - coupling superconductor of cubic symmetry , with an isotropic parabolic band split in two by the so coupling .",
    "we assume that the ratio of the so band splitting to the fermi energy is small ( which is the case in most known noncentrosymmetric materials ) , and neglect the impurity - induced mixing of the singlet and triplet components of the gap function .",

    "<S> we calculate the upper critical field in superconductors without inversion symmetry at arbitrary temperatures , in the presence of scalar impurities . </S>",
    "<S> both orbital and spin ( paramagnetic ) mechanisms of pair breaking are considered . </S>",
    "<S> the superconducting phase transition at nonzero field occurs into a helical vortex state , in which the order parameter is modulated along the applied field ( in a cubic crystal ) . </S>",
    "<S> the helical state is stable with respect to disorder . </S>",
    "<S> however , if the difference between the densities of states in the electron bands split by spin - orbit coupling is neglected , then the order parameter modulation is present only at low temperatures , and only if the system is sufficiently clean and paramagnetically limited . </S>",
    "<S> this state resembles the larkin - ovchinnikov - fulde - ferrell state in centrosymmetric superconductors . </S>"
