    "<S> low energy physics of quasi - one - dimensional ultracold atomic gases is often described by a gapless luttinger liquid ( ll ) . </S>",
    "<S> it is nowadays routine to manipulate these systems by changing their parameters in time but , no matter how slow the manipulation is , it must excite a gapless system . </S>",
    "<S> we study a smooth change of parameters of the ll ( a smooth `` quench '' ) with a variable quench time and find that the excitation energy decays with an inverse power of the quench time . </S>",
    "<S> this universal exponent is @xmath0 at zero temperature , and @xmath1 for slow enough quenches at finite temperature . </S>",
    "<S> the smooth quench does not excite beyond the range of validity of the low energy ll description . </S>"
