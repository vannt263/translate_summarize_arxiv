    "displaced - beam scintillometers are useful to make measurements of turbulent heat fluxes since their footprint is much larger than other measurement devices such as sonic anemometers .",
    "their source measurements are the index of refraction structure parameter @xmath1 and the turbulence inner scale length @xmath0 , which are used to infer the turbulent heat fluxes @xcite .",
    "this inference follows from equations from the monin - obukhov similarity hypothesis , which is a model for turbulent fluxes of heat and momentum in the atmospheric surface layer @xcite .",
    "studying the sensitivity of the derived turbulent heat flux to the uncertainties in the source measurements is important , and several studies have been published exploring the case of measurements over flat terrain @xcite .",
    "there is debate over whether the monin - obukhov similarity hypothesis can be applied over variable terrain since the canonical model is for flat terrain , however many scintillometer field campaigns take place over variable terrain @xcite . the turbulent fluxes are derived through equations which extend the monin - obukhov equations from flat terrain to variable terrain via the application of an effective beam height @xcite . the effective beam height is calculated assuming that the monin - obukhov surface layer profiles in height are valid over the slowly varying terrain .",
    "the research question to be asked is , if one can safely extend the monin - obukhov equations from flat terrain to variable terrain , then in what manner is the error propagated from the uncertainty in topography to the derived turbulent heat flux ?    through the assumptions of the monin - obukhov similarity hypothesis ,",

    "using the effective beam height extension of the monin - obukhov similarity equations to variable terrain , we have solved for how uncertainty in topographic heights propagates to displaced - beam scintillometer measurements of turbulent heat fluxes over any field site .",
    "we have found that uncertainty is concentrated in areas around topographic protuberances , as well as near the center of the beam path as seen in figures [ zg ] and [ shsz ] .",
    "the local sensitivity can easily approach values of @xmath72 in unstable conditions as seen in figure [ shsz ] , but the average sensitivity over the beam path never exceeds @xmath73 as seen in figure [ shszint ] .",
    "these results carry important ramifications in the selection of beam paths , in the calculation of uncertainty , and in the type of topographic data used",
    ". it may be that for many scintillometer beam paths , in order to achieve reasonable uncertainty we must use high precision lidar topographic data in order to reduce what is likely the greatest contributor to overall uncertainty .",
    "it is interesting that the average value of the sensitivity function @xmath43 over the beam path reduces to the identical sensitivity function @xmath45 for diplaced - beam scintillometers over flat terrain as seen in figure [ shszint ] and in .",
    "we have essentially expanded the flat terrain sensitivity function first explored in from one dimension to two , and then we averaged through one dimension to arrive back at the original one - dimensional sensitivity function .",
    "future work should perhaps focus on applying this type of sensitivity analysis on large aperture scintillometers using the businger - dyer relation to obtain path averaged @xmath6 , @xmath7 and @xmath3 measurements .",

    "<S> displaced - beam scintillometer measurements of the turbulence inner - scale length @xmath0 and refractive index structure function @xmath1 resolve area - average turbulent fluxes of heat and momentum through the monin - obukhov similarity equations . </S>",
    "<S> sensitivity studies have been produced for the use of displaced - beam scintillometers over flat terrain . </S>",
    "<S> many real field sites feature variable topography . </S>",
    "<S> we develop here an analysis of the sensitivity of displaced - beam scintillometer derived sensible heat fluxes to uncertainties in spacially distributed topographic measurements . </S>",
    "<S> sensitivity is shown to be concentrated in areas near the center of the beam and where the underlying topography is closest to the beam height . </S>",
    "<S> uncertainty may be decreased by taking precise topographic measurements in these areas .    </S>",
