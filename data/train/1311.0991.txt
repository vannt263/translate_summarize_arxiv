    "<S> we present rigorous topological order which emerges in a one - dimensional spin - orbital model due to the ring topology . </S>",
    "<S> although an exact solution of a spin - orbital ring with su(2 ) spin and xy orbital interactions separates spins from orbitals by means of a unitary transformation , the spins are not independent when the ring is closed , but form two half - rings carrying opposite pseudomomenta . we show that an inverse transformation back to the physical degrees of freedom entangles the spin half - rings with the orbitals once again . </S>",
    "<S> this surprising correlation arises on changing the topology from an open to a closed chain , which reduces the degeneracy of the ground - state manifold , leaving in it only the states in which pseudomomenta compensate each other .    </S>",
    "<S> spin - orbital physics is one of the foundation stones in the theory of frustrated magnetism @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> when degenerate @xmath0 orbitals in a transition - metal oxide are partly filled , electrons localize due to large on - site coulomb interaction and superexchange between magnetic ions includes both spin and orbital degrees of freedom that are strongly interrelated @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the orbital degeneracy leads in many cases to a dramatic increase of quantum fluctuation @xcite , which may trigger exotic order @xcite or may stabilize a spin - liquid @xcite when different states compete near a quantum critical point . while spin - orbital separation is possible in one - dimensional ( 1d ) systems @xcite , as observed recently in sr@xmath1cuo@xmath2 @xcite , spins and orbitals </S>",
    "<S> are usually entangled strongly , as in the archetypal kugel - khomskii model @xcite . in the @xmath3 su(2)@xmath4su(2 ) </S>",
    "<S> chain @xcite , both ground state @xcite and excited states @xcite are entangled , similar to the @xmath5 su(2)@xmath4su(2 ) chain which plays a prominent role in perovskite vanadates @xcite . only in exceptional cases </S>",
    "<S> can such 1d models be solved exactly , for example at the su(4 ) point @xcite or for a valence - bond state @xcite of alternating spin and orbital singlets @xcite , but even in these situations the spins and orbitals can not be separated from each other .    in real materials </S>",
    "<S> the symmetry between spin and orbital interactions is absent . </S>",
    "<S> orbital interactions generically have lower symmetry than spin ones @xcite , being usually ising- or xy - like @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the xy case is quantum and in general the orbitals can not be separated from the spins @xcite . in this context </S>",
    "<S> the 1d spin - orbital su(2)@xmath4xy model introduced by kumar @xcite is surprising  by a change of basis , the @xmath3 spins decouple from the orbitals in an _ open _ chain . </S>",
    "<S> the orbital interactions remain formally unchanged but the spin ones are gauged away . </S>",

    "<S> frustrated spin systems are at the forefront of modern condensed matter theory and experiment @xcite , in large part for the investigation of topology in many - body physics . </S>",
    "<S> a particular manifestation is the topological spin liquid ( tsl ) @xcite , a category including resonating valence - bond ( rvb ) states @xcite and states hosting excitations with non - abelian fractional quantum statistics , long sought in quantum information and topological quantum computation @xcite ( topological protection against decoherence ) . </S>",
    "<S> one experimental example of topological order is the fractional quantum hall effect , where the excitations are usually abelian @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the search for realistic tsl models gained momentum after the demonstration @xcite of a z@xmath1 tsl ground state for the @xmath3 kagome antiferromagnet . </S>",
    "<S> recent progress has been due largely to advanced numerical methods , including extension of the quasi-1d density matrix renormalization group ( dmrg ) approach @xcite to 2d tsls @xcite and the development of intrinsically high - dimensional techniques such as the projected entangled - pair states ( peps ) ansatz @xcite and its extension to `` simplex '' lattice units @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> peps have been used in tsls for : ( i ) very efficient representation of the rvb state @xcite , ( ii ) classifying the topologically distinct ground states of the kagome antiferromagnet @xcite , and ( iii ) demonstrating a tsl in the antiferromagnetic @xmath8-@xmath9 model on the square lattice @xcite . despite these recent breakthroughs , however , </S>",
