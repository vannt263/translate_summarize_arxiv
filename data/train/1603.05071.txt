    "<S> adiabatic evolution is a powerful technique in quantum information and computation . however , its performance is limited by the adiabatic theorem of quantum mechanics . in this scenario , </S>",
    "<S> shortcuts to adiabaticity , such as provided by the superadiabatic theory , constitute a valuable tool to speed up the adiabatic quantum behavior . in this dissertation </S>",
    "<S> we introduce two different models to perform universal superadiabatic quantum computing , which are based on the use of shortcuts to adiabaticity by counter - diabatic hamiltonians . </S>",
    "<S> the first model is based on the use of superadiabatic quantum teleportation , introduced in this dissertation , as a primitive to quantum computing . </S>",
    "<S> thus , we provide the counter - diabatic driving for arbitrary @xmath0-qubit gates . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , our approach maps the counter - diabatic hamiltonian for an arbitrary @xmath0-qubit _ gate _ teleportation into the implementation of a rotated counter - diabatic hamiltonian for an @xmath0-qubit _ state _ teleportation . in the second model we use the concept of controlled superadiabatic evolutions to show how we can implement arbitrary @xmath0-controlled quantum gates . </S>",
    "<S> remarkably , this task can be performed by simple time - independent counter - diabatic hamiltonians . </S>",
    "<S> these two models can be used to design different sets of universal quantum gates . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the use of the quantum speed limit suggests that the superadiabatic time evolution is compatible with arbitrarily small time intervals , where this arbitrariness is constrained to the energetic cost necessary to perform the superadiabatic evolution . </S>"
