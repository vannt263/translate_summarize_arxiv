    "heteroclinic objects ( connections , cycles , and networks ) exist in a robust way provided that flow - invariant spaces exist where each heteroclinic connection is of saddle - sink type .",
    "the existence of flow - invariant spaces is a natural feature of symmetric problems , in which case flow - invariant spaces arise in the form of fixed - point subspaces .",
    "recent results about how symmetry can be used to construct heteroclinic cycles can be found in ashwin and postlethwaite @xcite and in field @xcite .",
    "flow - invariant spaces also occur naturally in applications where the state variables are constrained by the nature of the problem . in this context , the flow - invariant spaces are not fixed - point spaces .",
    "we address the stability of what we call _ quasi - simple _ cycles : robust heteroclinic cycles whose connecting orbits are one - dimensional and contained in flow - invariant spaces of equal dimension .",
    "these flow - invariant spaces are not necessarily fixed - point spaces so that quasi - simple cycles may also be heteroclinic cycles arising in a non - symmetric setting .    in the context of symmetry , krupa and melbourne @xcite",
    "have defined a heteroclinic cycle in @xmath6 to be simple if the connecting orbits between consecutive equilibria lie in a 2-dimensional fixed - point subspace .",
    "simple cycles in @xmath6 are grouped into one of three types depending on symmetry related properties : @xmath7 , @xmath8 and @xmath9 .",
    "we are not concerned with type @xmath7 cycles .",
    "more recently , podvigina @xcite studies simple cycles in dimension higher than @xmath10 and groups the cycles into two types : @xmath7 , as before , and @xmath1 .",
    "the set of type @xmath1 cycles contains all cycles of types @xmath8 and @xmath9 .",
    "heteroclinic cycles also appear naturally in non - symmetric problems . in evolutionary game theory , see hofbauer and sigmund @xcite , the state variables are probability vectors so that the state space is a @xmath11-dimensional simplex . under the classic replicator dynamics ,",
    "flow - invariant spaces appear naturally on the boundary of the simplex .",
    "the classic example of a heteroclinic cycle in this context is that which can be found in the dynamics of the rock - scissors - paper ( rsp ) game .",
    "this game as been used to study problems in population dynamics as well as economics .",

    "the study of the stability of heteroclinic objects goes back to the work of krupa and melbourne @xcite , and melbourne @xcite .",
    "the stability of heteroclinic cycles and networks is obtained from the stability of individual connections and can be quantified by the stability index introduced by podvigina and ashwin @xcite .",
    "the stability index has been defined generically for an invariant set but , to the best of our knowledge , has only been calculated for some types of simple cycle in @xcite and @xcite , and used by @xcite in the context of networks . in the present article",
    ", we extend the calculations to quasi - simple cycles , a much wider collection of heteroclinic cycles .",
    "these include all simple cycles of type @xmath1 , various non - simple cycles arising from a straightforward generalisation of a construction method in @xcite , and cycles with connections contained in flow - invariant spaces which are not vector subspaces , as those arising from population dynamics .",
    "podvigina @xcite establishes results concerning the fragmentary asymptotic stability of cycles of type @xmath1 .",
    "fragmentary asymptotic stability is a weak notion of stability : a fragmentarily asymptotically stable cycle attracts a positive measure set in a small neighbourhood of the cycle .",
    "our method , to obtain results in a far more general context . ] extends to the study of essential asymptotic stability of such cycles , a stronger stability concept , as well as stability of non - simple cycles or cycles arising in a non - symmetric context .",
    "our main contribution consists in providing a method that , under one mild assumption , provides an expression for the values of the local stability index at a point of a connection . of course , in the context of simple cycles in dimension @xmath10 , our method recovers results previously obtained by other authors .",
    "our assumption a guarantees basic transition matrices exist for representing the dynamics between incoming cross - sections at consecutive equilibria and this is a crucial element in the calculation of local stability indices .",
    "we note that , for the purpose of studying the stability of heteroclinic cycles , all our results hold as long as a representation using basic transition matrices exists , whether or not the assumption is satisfied .",
    "we thus confirm the views of @xcite in that ` transition matrices can be used to study the stability of simple cycles in higher - dimensional systems ' while contradicting their expectation that ` we expect such a classification to be so complex that the results can hardly be enlightening ' .",
    "our results are constructive in the sense that we define a function whose images provide the stability index .",

    "<S> the stability of heteroclinic cycles may be obtained from the value of the local stability index along each connection of the cycle . </S>",
    "<S> we establish a way of calculating the local stability index for _ quasi - simple _ cycles : cycles whose connections are @xmath0-dimensional and contained in flow - invariant spaces of equal dimension . </S>",
    "<S> these heteroclinic cycles exist both in symmetric and non - symmetric contexts . </S>",
    "<S> we make one assumption on the dynamics along the connections to ensure that the transition matrices have a convenient form . </S>",
    "<S> our method applies to all simple heteroclinic cycles of type @xmath1 and to various heteroclinic cycles arising in population dynamics , namely non - simple heteroclinic cycles , as well as to cycles that are part of a heteroclinic network . </S>",
    "<S> we illustrate our results with two types of quasi - simple ( non - simple ) cycles , including a cycle present in a heteroclinic network of the rock - scissors - paper game .    </S>",
