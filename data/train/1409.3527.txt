    "<S> the derivation of a quantum markovian model for an opto - mechanical system consisting of a quantum mechanical mirror interacting with quantum optical input fields via radiation pressure is difficult problem which ultimately involves the scattering process of quantum stochastic calculus . </S>",
    "<S> we show that while the scattering process may be approximated in a singular limit by regular processes using different schemes , however the limit model is highly sensitive to how the approximation scheme is interpreted mathematically . </S>",
    "<S> we find two main types of stochastic limits of regular models , and illustrate the origin of this difference at the level of one particle scattering . as an alternative modelling scheme , </S>",
    "<S> we consider models of mirrors as non - trivial dielectric medium with a boundary that is itself quantized . rather than treating the plane waves for the electromagnetic field , we take the actual physical modes and quantize these . the input - output formalism </S>",
    "<S> is then obtained in the far zone where the plane wave approximation is valid . </S>",
    "<S> several examples are considered , and the quantum stochastic model is derived . </S>",
    "<S> we also consider the quantum trajectories problem for continual measurement of the reflect output fields , and derive the stochastic master equations for homodyning and photon counting detection to estimate the mirror observables . </S>"
