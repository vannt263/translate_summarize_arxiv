    "<S> we present a comprehensive study of the silicate features at 9.7 and 18 of a sample of almost 800 active galactic nuclei ( agn ) with available spectra from the _ spitzer _ infrared spectrograph ( irs ) . </S>",
    "<S> we measure the strength of the silicate feature at 9.7 , s@xmath0 , before and after subtracting the host galaxy emission from the irs spectra . </S>",
    "<S> the numbers of type 1 and 2 agn with the feature in emission increase by 20 and 50% , respectively , once the host galaxy is removed , while 35% of objects with the feature originally in absorption exhibit it in even deeper absorption . </S>",
    "<S> the peak of s@xmath0 , @xmath1 , has a bimodal distribution when the feature is in emission , with about 65% of the cases showing @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> silicates can appear in emission in objects with mid - infrared ( mir ) luminosity spanning over six orders of magnitude . </S>",
    "<S> the derived distributions of the strength of the silicate features at 9.7 and 18 provide a solid test bed for modeling the dust distribution in agn . </S>",
    "<S> clumpiness is needed in order to produce absorption features in unobscured agn and can also cause the silicates to be in absorption at 9.7 and in emission at 18 in type 1 sources . </S>",
    "<S> we find the ` cosmic ' silicates of ossenkopf et al . to be more consistent with the observations than draine s ` astronomical ' silicates . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we discuss the possibility of a foreground absorber to explain the deep silicate absorption features in the mir spectra of some type 2 agn . </S>"
