    "<S> the resonator - induced phase ( rip ) gate is a multi - qubit entangling gate that allows a high degree of flexibility in qubit frequencies , making it attractive for quantum operations in large - scale architectures . </S>",
    "<S> we experimentally realize the rip gate with four superconducting qubits in a three - dimensional ( 3d ) circuit - quantum electrodynamics architecture , demonstrating high - fidelity controlled - z ( cz ) gates between all possible pairs of qubits from two different 4-qubit devices in pair subspaces . </S>",
    "<S> these qubits are arranged within a wide range of frequency detunings , up to as large as 1.8 ghz . </S>",
    "<S> we further show a dynamical multi - qubit refocusing scheme in order to isolate out 2-qubit interactions , and combine them to generate a four - qubit greenberger - horne - zeilinger state .                as recent progress in superconducting quantum processors </S>",
    "<S> has marched towards more complex networks of qubits  @xcite , it becomes increasingly crucial to develop robust protocols for multi - qubit control . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , there has been a considerable amount of work aimed at improving single-  @xcite and two - qubit  @xcite controls for superconducting transmon devices . </S>",
    "<S> although the fidelity of single - qubit gates ( @xmath0 ) has already been pushed above fault - tolerant thresholds for error correction codes such as the surface code  @xcite , the study of two - qubit gates in multi - qubit systems is still an area of great exploration .    </S>",
    "<S> currently , many two - qubit gates for superconducting qubits require specific arrangements of qubit frequencies to perform optimally . </S>",
    "<S> for example , the dynamically - tuned controlled - z ( cz ) gate  @xcite functions through magnetic flux - tuning two qubits into a specific resonance condition involving higher energy levels , which will not work if any other existing energy levels intervene between the qubits . </S>",
    "<S> a similar limitation arises with the all - microwave cross - resonance ( cr ) gate  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the cr gate works for qubits within a narrow window of detunings defined by the anharmonicity of the qubit  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this restriction becomes accentuated in larger networks of qubits where all qubit frequencies must be arranged within a small frequency window  @xcite .     </S>",

    "<S> the rip gate is a cz gate that exploits strong coupling between qubits and a resonator in a circuit quantum electrodynamics ( cqed ) system . </S>",
    "<S> it is realized by applying a detuned pulsed microwave drive to a shared bus cavity , without a strong requirement on the qubit frequencies . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , it is insensitive to phase fluctuations of the drive , depending only on drive amplitude and detuning .    in this letter </S>",
