    "<S> we show theoretically that a weakly interacting gas of spin - polarized exciton - polaritons in a semiconductor microcavity supports propagation of spin waves . </S>",
    "<S> the spin waves are characterised by a parabolic dispersion at small wavevectors which is governed by the polariton - polariton interaction constant . </S>",
    "<S> due to spin - anisotropy of polariton - polariton interactions the dispersion of spin waves depends on the orientation of the total polariton spin . for the same reason , the frequency of homogeneous spin precession / polariton spin resonance depends on their polarization degree .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction_. spin waves are weakly damped harmonic oscillations of spin polarization . predicted to appear in fermi liquids over 50 years ago  @xcite and discovered in the end of 1960s in metals  @xcite are among the most fascinating manifestations of collective effects in interacting system . </S>",
    "<S> later it was understood theoretically that the spin waves can exist in a non - degenerate electron gas  @xcite as well as in atomic gases  bashkin81,ll1982 , and the spin waves were indeed observed in a number of interacting gases such as hydrogen and helium  physrevlett.52.1508,nacher1984,physrevlett.52.1810 , see ref .  </S>",

    "<S> recently , semiconductor microcavities with quantum wells sandwiched between highly reflective mirrors have attracted a lot of interest in the solid state and photonics communities  @xcite . in these artificial structures </S>",
    "<S> the strong coupling is achieved between excitons , being material excitations in quantum wells , and photons confined between the mirrors  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> resulting mixed light - matter particles , exciton - polaritons , demonstrate the bose - einstein statistics and may condense at critical temperatures ranging from tens kelvin  @xcite till several hundreds kelvin  @xcite , which exceeds by many orders of magnitude the bose - einstein condensation temperature in atomic gases . </S>",
    "<S> high transition temperatures and the strong coupling with light makes semiconductor microcavities perfectly suited for benchtop studies of collective effects of bosons .    in typical gaas based microcavities , exciton - polaritons may have two spin projections onto the structure growth axis , @xmath1 , corresponding to right- and left- circular polarizations of photons ( and spin moment of excitons ) forming polaritons . owing to the composite nature of exciton - polaritons , </S>",
    "<S> the interactions between them are strongly spin - dependent  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> a number of prominent spin - related phenomena both in interacting and in noninteracting polariton systems have already been predicted and observed in the microcavities , such as , e.g. , polarization multistability  physrevlett.98.236401,paraiso:2010fk and optical spin hall effect  kavokin05prl , leyder:2007ve , see refs .  </S>",
