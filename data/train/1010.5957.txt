    "we have demonstrated that the mean - field repulsive nonlinearity suppresses the 3d quantum collapse induced by the central attractive potential , @xmath207 , which can be realized in the ultracold gas of dipolar molecules attracted by the central charge .",
    "the dipole - dipole interactions were also taken into account , resulting ( in the framework of the mean - field approximation ) in a redefinition of the scattering length which accounts for the contact repulsion .",
    "the nonlinearity creates the gs ( ground state ) in place of the collapse regime . for @xmath127 ,",
    "when the respective 3d schrdinger equation does not lead to the collapse , the inclusion of the harmonic trap gives rise to the tristability .",
    "the cubic repulsion is not strong enough to prevent the 2d collapse , but the quintic term is sufficient for this purpose .",
    "it also gives rise to the gs which replaces the quantum collapse in the 2d space , and to similar modes carrying the angular momentum ( which feature the amplitude diverging , rather than vanishing , at @xmath4 , while the total norm of the vortical mode converges ) .",
    "a difference from the 3d case is that , in the presence of the harmonic trap , one of the three confined modes supported by the weakly repulsive central potential with @xmath208 , in the combination withe quintic nonlinearity , is unstable , transforming itself into a breather .",
    "a counter - intuitive finding is that the 2d self - trapped mode exists even in the case of the weakly repulsive potential , while the harmonic trap is absent .",
    "this work suggests continuations in several directions .",
    "if the orientation of the dipoles in the 3d space is fixed by an external uniform field , the central charge induces an axisymmetric potential , @xmath209 , and it may be interesting to study the possibility of the replacement of the corresponding anisotropic collapse ( cf .",

    "<S> the quantum - mechanical collapse ( alias _ fall onto the center _ of particles attracted by potential @xmath0 ) is a well - known issue in the quantum theory . </S>",
    "<S> it is closely related to the _ quantum anomaly _ , i.e. , breaking of the scaling invariance of the respective hamiltonian by the quantization . </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate that the mean - field repulsive nonlinearity prevents the collapse and thus puts forward a solution to the quantum - anomaly problem different from that previously developed in the framework of the linear quantum - field theory . </S>",
    "<S> this solution may be realized in the 3d or 2d gas of dipolar bosons attracted by a central charge , and in the 2d gas of magnetic dipoles attracted by a current filament . in the 3d setting </S>",
    "<S> , the dipole - dipole interactions are also taken into regard , in the mean - field approximation , resulting in a redifinition of the scattering length which accounts for the contact repulsion between the bosons . in lieu of the collapse </S>",
    "<S> , the cubic nonlinearity creates a 3d ground state ( gs ) , which does not exist in the respective linear schrdinger equation . </S>",
    "<S> the addition of the harmonic trap gives rise to a tristability , in the case when the schrdinger equation still does not lead to the collapse . in the 2d setting , the cubic nonlinearity is not strong enough to prevent the collapse ; however , the quintic term does it , creating the gs , as well as its counterparts carrying the angular momentum ( vorticity ) . </S>",
    "<S> counter - intuitively , such self - trapped 2d modes exist even in the case of a weakly _ repulsive _ potential @xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> the 2d vortical modes avoid the phase singularity at the pivot ( @xmath2 ) by having the amplitude diverging at @xmath3 , instead of the usual situation with the amplitude of the vortical mode vanishing at @xmath4 ( the norm of the mode converges despite of the singularity of the amplitude at @xmath4 ) . in the presence of the harmonic trap , the 2d quintic model with a weakly repulsive central potential @xmath1 </S>",
    "<S> gives rise to three confined modes , the middle one being unstable , spontaneously developing into a breather . in both the 3d and 2d cases , </S>",
    "<S> the gs wave functions are found in a numerical form , and also in the form of an analytical approximation , which is asymptotically exact in the limit of the large norm . </S>"
