    "several unique physical phenomena were observed in graphene or its derivatives at high magnetic fields @xcite .",
    "these include shubnikov - de haas oscillations indicating zero quasiparticle rest mass @xcite , room - temperature quantum hall effect with a nonstandard ( half - odd integer ) sequence of landau levels @xcite , signatures of a  fractal energy spectrum known as hofstadter s butterfly @xcite , and many others .",

    "<S> we discuss , by means of mode - matching analysis for the dirac equation , how splittings of the landau - level ( ll ) degeneracies associated with spin , valley , and layer degrees of freedom affect the ballistic conductance of graphene bilayer . </S>",
    "<S> the results show that for wide samples ( @xmath0 ) the landauer - bttiker conductance reaches the maximum @xmath1 at the resonance via each ll , with the prefactor varying from @xmath2 if all three degeneracies are preserved , to @xmath3 if all the degeneracies are split . in the absence of bias between the layers , the degeneracies associated with spin and layer degrees of freedom </S>",
    "<S> may be split by manipulating the doping and magnetic field ; the conductance at the zeroth ll is twice as large , while the conductance at any other ll equals to the corresponding conductance of graphene monolayer . </S>",
    "<S> the presence of bias potential allows one also to split the valley degeneracy . </S>",
    "<S> our results show that the charge transfer at each ll has pseudodiffusive character , with the second and third cumulant quantified by @xmath4 and @xmath5 ( respectively ) . in case </S>",
    "<S> the electrochemical potential is allowed to slowly fluctuate in a  finite vicinity of ll , the resulting charge - transfer characteristics are still quantum - limited , with @xmath6 and @xmath7 in the limit of large fluctuations . </S>",
    "<S> analogously , the above values of @xmath8 and @xmath9 are predicted to be approached in the limit of high source - drain voltage difference applied . </S>",
    "<S> the possible effects of indirect interlayer hopping integrals are also briefly discussed .    </S>",
