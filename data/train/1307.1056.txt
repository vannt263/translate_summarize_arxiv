    "<S> we consider conservation laws on moving hypersurfaces . in this work </S>",
    "<S> the velocity of the surface is prescribed . </S>",
    "<S> but one may think of the velocity to be given by pdes in the bulk phase . </S>",
    "<S> we prove existence and uniqueness for a scalar conservation law on the moving surface . </S>",
    "<S> this is done via a parabolic regularization of the hyperbolic pde . </S>",
    "<S> we then prove suitable estimates for the solution of the regularized pde , that are independent of the regularization parameter . </S>",
    "<S> we introduce the concept of an entropy solution for a scalar conservation law on a moving hypersurface . </S>",
    "<S> we also present some numerical experiments . as in the euclidean case </S>",
    "<S> we expect discontinuous solutions , in particular shocks . </S>",
    "<S> it turns out that in addition to the `` euclidean shocks '' geometrically induced shocks may appear .        0.0em </S>"
