    "<S> in this paper i describe a new optimal krylov subspace solver for shifted unitary matrices called the shifted unitary orthogonal method ( suom ) . this algorithm is used as a benchmark against any improvement like the two - grid algorithm . </S>",
    "<S> i use the latter to show that the overlap operator can be inverted by successive inversions of the truncated overlap operator . </S>",
    "<S> this strategy results in large gains compared to suom .    </S>",
    "<S> it is well - known that overlap fermions @xcite lead to much more expensive computations than standard fermions , i.e. wilson or kogut - sussking fermions . </S>",
    "<S> this is obvious since for every application of the overlap operator an extra linear system solving is needed . for the time being , it seems that to get chiral symmetry at finite lattice spacing one should wait for a petaflops computer being built .    </S>",
    "<S> however , algorithmic research is far from exhausted . in this paper </S>",
