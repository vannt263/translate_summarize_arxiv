    "<S> we report amplification of electromagnetic waves by a single artificial atom in open 1d space . our three - level artificial atom  a superconducting quantum circuit  coupled to a transmission line </S>",
    "<S> presents an analog of a natural atom in open space . </S>",
    "<S> the system is the most fundamental quantum amplifier whose gain is limited by a spontaneous emission mechanism . </S>",
    "<S> the noise performance is determined by the quantum noise revealed in the spectrum of spontaneous emission , also characterized in our experiments .    </S>",
    "<S> the quantum amplifiers are actively used devices and most of them rely on natural intra - atomic or molecular transitions with almost untunable transition frequencies @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> demonstration of amplification on a single atom or molecule in open space is possible @xcite , however , extremely difficult due to another common characteristic of natural atoms ( molecules , quantum dots ) : they are relatively weakly coupled to the spatial electromagnetic waves in real experiments @xcite , in spite of theoretical feasibility of perfect coupling by careful matching of the spacial modes to the atom @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> an alternative approach is coupling of the atoms to a field of a high quality resonator @xcite , which has been successfully used to demonstrate lasing action on single natural @xcite and artificial @xcite atoms . in the resonators , </S>",
    "<S> the atom is coupled to a single mode . on the other hand , </S>",
    "<S> an elementary ( ultimate ) quantum amplifier avoids this limitation and can be realized on a single atom strongly coupled to a continuum of electromagnetic modes of open space . </S>",
    "<S> the matching problem of spacial modes of electromagnetic waves can be solved by reducing space dimensionality to a 1d @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> recently , the highly efficient coupling of an artificial atom to an open 1d transmission line has been achieved experimentally @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate amplification on a single three - level artificial atom coupled to a 1d transmission line . </S>",
    "<S> the atom is a fully controllable and tunable quantum system , with all its basic characteristics , such as energy splitting and coupling to the line , designed in accordance with our requirements . </S>",
    "<S> our demonstration opens the perspective of developing a new type of on - chip quantum amplifiers and other quantum devices , capable of both reproducing the known quantum - optical phenomena and realizing the novel ones , which will use the tunability , controllability and strong coupling .    </S>",
