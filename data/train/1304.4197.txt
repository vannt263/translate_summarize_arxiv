    "<S> we consider how nonlinear interaction effects can manifest themselves and even be enhanced in a strongly driven optomechanical system . using a keldysh green s function approach , </S>",
    "<S> we calculate modifications to the cavity density of states due to both linear and nonlinear optomechanical interactions , showing that strong modifications can arise even for a weak nonlinear interaction . </S>",
    "<S> we show how this quantity can be directly probed in an optomechanically - induced transparency type experiment . </S>",
    "<S> we also show how the enhanced interaction can lead to nonclassical behaviour , as evidenced by the behaviour of @xmath0 correlation functions .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction _ the field of cavity optomechanics involves understanding and exploiting the quantum interaction between a mechanical resonator and photons in a driven electromagnetic cavity . </S>",
    "<S> it holds immense promise for both fundamental studies of large - scale quantum phenomena as well as applications to quantum information processing and ultra - sensitive detection , and has seen remarkable progress in the past five years . </S>",
    "<S> highlights include the use of radiation pressure forces to cool a mechanical resonator to close to its motional ground state @xcite and experiments where the mechanical motion causes squeezing of the light leaving the cavity @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> as remarkable as this progress has been , it has relied on strongly driving the optomechanical cavity to enhance the basic dispersive coupling between photons and mechanical position . </S>",
    "<S> while the resulting interaction can be made larger than even the dissipative rates in the system @xcite , it is purely bilinear in photon and phonon operators . as a result </S>",
    "<S> , it can not convert gaussian state inputs into non - classical states or give rise to true photon - photon interactions . </S>",
    "<S> recent theoretical work has addressed physics of the nonlinear interaction in weakly driven systems @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> unfortunately , one finds that effects are suppressed by the small parameter @xmath1 .    in this paper , we now consider nonlinear interaction effects in an optomechanical system that ( unlike refs .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite ) is also subject to a strong laser drive ; we consider effects of this driving beyond simple linear - response . </S>",
    "<S> we find somewhat surprisingly that one can use the strong drive to enhance the underlying single - photon interaction . </S>",
    "<S> using non - equilibrium many - body perturbation theory ( based on the keldysh technique ( see , e.g. , @xcite ) ) , we calculate how these effects modify the cavity density of states , and hence the cavity s response to an additional weak probe laser . </S>",
    "<S> this response is exactly the quantity measured in so - called optomechanically - induced transparency ( omit ) experiments @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> we find striking modifications of the omit spectrum , effects which can be attributed to the nonlinear interaction causing a hybridization between one and two polariton states ( with the polaritons being joint mechanical - photonic excitations ) . </S>",
    "<S> we also find the possibility of enhanced polariton - polariton interactions , which lead in turn to non - classical correlations ( as measured by a @xmath0 correlation function ) .    _ </S>",
