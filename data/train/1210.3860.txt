    "<S> we investigate gravitational radiation in the linear approximation within the framework of the recent nonlocal generalization of einstein s theory of gravitation . in this theory </S>",
    "<S> , nonlocality can simulate dark matter ; in fact , in the newtonian regime , we recover the phenomenological tohline - kuhn approach to modified gravity . to account for the observational data regarding the rotation curves of spiral galaxies , nonlocality is associated with a characteristic length scale of order @xmath0  =  10 kpc . </S>",
    "<S> it follows that in nonlocal gravity , the treatment of extremely low - frequency ( @xmath1 hz ) gravitational waves with wavelengths of order @xmath0 would be quite different than in general relativity . however , for radiation of frequency @xmath2 hz , which is the frequency range that is the focus of current observational searches , the corresponding wavelengths are very small compared to @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> we find that in this frequency regime the nonlocal deviations from general relativity essentially average out and can be safely neglected in practice . </S>"
