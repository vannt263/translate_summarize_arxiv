    "for more than a decade , we have been witnessing the rise of a plethora of ever new two - dimensional ( 2d ) materials displaying their unique electronic properties , which has initiated major activities in those systems . leading",
    "the pack was , of course , monolayer and bilayer graphene displaying the behavior of `` dirac fermions '' of the charge carriers with `` dirac points '' where the two energy bands meet @xcite with linear dispersions in the vicinity that forms the characteristic `` dirac cones '' @xcite .",
    "their many exotic physical properties , in particular , in a strong magnetic field , have been well documented , and range from the magnetic field effects in the extreme quantum limit @xcite to hofstadter butterflies @xcite .",
    "these were then followed by other graphenelike systems , such as silicene and germanene @xcite , the 2d version of black phosphorus ( bp ) @xcite , and , most recently , the planar electron systems in zno heterojunctions @xcite .",
    "interestingly , an anisotropic two - dimensional system can undergo a transition between an insulating state with gapped spectrum and a semimetal state with two dirac cones separated in the momentum space .",
    "the possibility of such a quantum phase transition has been considered theoretically in honeycomb lattice models @xcite and few - layer black phosphorus where the band inversion can be induced by an external perpendicular electric field @xcite or by doping @xcite .",
    "the gapless spectrum at the phase transition point may arise in the @xmath0 nanostructures @xcite .",
    "experimentally , merging or creation of dirac points has been observed in systems of ultracold atoms @xcite , photonic crystals @xcite , microwave analog of graphene @xcite , and , more recently , in a potassium - doped few - layer bp @xcite .",
    "various properties of a system undergoing this phase transition have been reported in the literature , which include the landau levels and the hofstadter spectrum @xcite , the hall conductivity @xcite , effects of disorder @xcite , the quantum critical behavior @xcite , and the transport characteristics @xcite . in this paper",

    "<S> we have studied the dynamical polarization and collective excitations in an anisotropic two - dimensional system undergoing a quantum phase transition with merging of two dirac points . </S>",
    "<S> analytical results for the one - loop polarization function are obtained at the finite momentum , frequency , and chemical potential . </S>",
    "<S> the evolution of the plasmon dispersion across the phase transition is then analyzed within the random phase approximation . </S>",
    "<S> we derive analytically the long - wavelength dispersion of the undamped anisotropic collective mode and find that it evolves smoothly at the critical merging point . </S>",
    "<S> the effects of the van hove singularity on the plasmon excitations are explored in detail . </S>"
