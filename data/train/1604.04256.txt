    "<S> the maximum achievable rate or mutual information of multidimensional rotationally invariant distributions in the presence of additive white gaussian noise is analyzed . </S>",
    "<S> a simple expression for the special case of multisphere distributions is derived . </S>",
    "<S> such distributions consist of points in a multidimensional euclidean space that are uniformly distributed over several multidimensional concentric hyperspheres . for the @xmath0-dimensional case , </S>",
    "<S> such distributions have been previously considered in order to reduce the computational complexity of finding a bound on the channel capacity of fiber - optic channels . </S>",
    "<S> these distributions take advantage of the statistical rotational invariance of the noise and nonlinear distortions in fiber - optic channels . using the derived mutual information expression , @xmath0- and @xmath1-dimensional multisphere distributions </S>",
    "<S> are compared for fiber - optic dual - polarization channels dominated by linear noise . at high signal - to - noise ratios , </S>",
    "<S> @xmath1-dimensional multisphere distributions offer higher achievable rates than @xmath0-dimensional ones transmitted on each of the two polarizations of the optical carrier . </S>",
    "<S> such @xmath1-dimensional multisphere distributions are also shown to be statistically invariant under @xmath1-dimensional nonlinear transmission in fibers .    </S>",
    "<S> coherent detection , fiber - optic communication , multidimensional constellation , mutual information , polarization , ring constellation , spherical distribution . </S>"
