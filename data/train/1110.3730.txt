    "<S> we propose a method of simulating efficiently many - body interacting fermion lattice models in trapped ions , including highly nonlinear interactions in arbitrary spatial dimensions and for arbitrarily distant couplings . </S>",
    "<S> we map products of fermionic operators onto nonlocal spin operators and decompose the resulting dynamics in efficient steps with trotter methods , yielding an overall protocol that employs only polynomial resources </S>",
    "<S> . the proposed scheme can be relevant in a variety of fields as condensed - matter or high - energy physics , where quantum simulations may solve problems intractable for classical computers .    </S>",
    "<S> quantum simulations promise to revolutionize computing technologies and scientific research by allowing us to solve problems that are otherwise intractable with classical computers @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> several physical systems have been proposed in a quantum simulator : spin models @xcite , quantum phase transitions @xcite , quantum chemistry @xcite , anyonic statistics  @xcite , many - body systems with rydberg atoms  @xcite , and relativistic systems @xcite . trapped </S>",
    "<S> ions offer one of the most promising platforms for quantum simulators  @xcite , due to their high controllability , efficient initialization and measurement  @xcite . in the near future </S>",
    "<S> , experiments in quantum simulations should be able to solve problems intractable for classical computers , turning this technology into a remarkable tool for scientists and engineers .    </S>",
    "<S> the numerical simulation of fermionic systems is , in general , a hard problem due to the huge increase of the hilbert space dimension with the number of modes  @xcite . using customized numerical methods as quantum monte carlo is not always possible due to the well - known sign problem  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> quantum simulations appear as a tool that will allow us to compute the time evolution of free and interacting fermion lattice theories with minimal experimental resources . </S>",
    "<S> this will be helpful in performing a wide range of condensed matter calculations , including those related with many - body interactions as the kondo  @xcite , fermi - hubbard  @xcite , or frhlich  @xcite hamiltonians . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , quantum simulations will allow us to reproduce the complete dynamics of these systems , avoiding mean field approximations as hartree - fock to simplify nonlinear interactions  @xcite .    in this letter , </S>",
    "<S> we propose a method of realizing the quantum simulation of many - body fermionic lattice models for @xmath0 fermionic modes in trapped ions . </S>",
    "<S> our method can be described in three steps . </S>",
    "<S> firstly , we map the set of @xmath0 fermionic modes , via the jordan - wigner transformation  @xcite , to a set of @xmath0 nonlocal spin operators . </S>",
    "<S> the second step consists in decomposing the total unitary evolution via trotter expansion  @xcite in terms of a product of exponentials associated to each nonlocal spin operator appearing in the hamiltonian . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we implement each of these exponentials in polynomial time on a set of @xmath0 two - level trapped ions with a reduced number of laser pulses  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> these three steps yield an efficient protocol that employs only polynomial resources . </S>",
    "<S> our method can simulate highly nonlinear and long - range interactions in arbitrary spatial dimensions , applying the jordan - wigner transformation without the usual restriction of a reduced number of neighbors , and without the need of auxiliary virtual majorana fermions  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this is due to the fact that the dynamics associated with the nonlocal spin operators , containing a large number of pauli matrices , can still be efficiently implemented . </S>",
    "<S> the proposed protocol opens the possibility to simulate a wide range of interesting condensed - matter and high - energy physics fermionic systems for a large number of particles . </S>",
    "<S> this includes the calculation of time evolutions and ground state computations , e.g. through adiabatic protocols  @xcite . for a number of particles above @xmath1 , which is foreseeable in the near future </S>",
    "<S> , one could already simulate fermionic systems that are intractable for classical computers .    </S>",
