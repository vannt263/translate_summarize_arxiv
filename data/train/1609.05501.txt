    "measurements naturally provide some information about the system involved . in quantum physics",
    ", no information can be gained without disturbance of the system  @xcite .",
    "the ultimate form of such a disturbance takes place in projective measurements , when the observation of a particular outcome @xmath6 leads the system into the state @xmath7 ( conditional state preparation ) .",
    "the mathematical form of the noise and disturbance relation is recently found for general fuzzy observables  @xcite .",
    "the effect of system disturbance becomes more visible in sequential measurements .",
    "for instance , a high repetition rate of the same projective measurement results in the quantum zeno effect  @xcite , in which the system state dynamics is frozen .",
    "non - projective sequential measurements at finite frequency may also lead to a perfect freezing  @xcite .",
    "however , the accelerated decay is more ubiquitous in case of a slow repetition rate of measurements ( anti - zeno effect )  @xcite .",
    "since the conditional output state is non - linearly related with the input density operator , other non - trivial dynamics is possible including the emerging chaotic behavior  @xcite .",
    "statistics of general sequential measurements may exhibit the properties of undecidability  @xcite , universality with respect to the construction of a joint observable  @xcite , informational completeness for state tomography  @xcite .",
    "moreover , sequential measurements find applications in estimation of quantum system parameters  @xcite , process tomography  @xcite , one - way quantum computing  @xcite , channel decoding  @xcite , detection  @xcite and generation  @xcite of non - classical correlations , and maintenance of quantum coherence in the presence of noise  @xcite or acceleration of decoherence  @xcite , quantum control of dynamical transitions between qubit levels  @xcite .",
