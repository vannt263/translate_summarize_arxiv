    "the response of a system to a perturbation lies at the heart of important concepts such as linear response theory  @xcite , characterizing whether a system is chaotic or not  @xcite , state engineering  @xcite , and adiabatic transport  @xcite .",
    "this paper considers the quantum mechanical response of an initial state to a time - dependent variation of the system hamiltonian . in the context of cold atom systems",
    "@xcite , two limiting cases have received considerable attention , the regime where the system hamiltonian is changed adiabatically and the regime where the system hamiltonian is quenched , i.e. , changed significantly over a time scale that is short compared to the intrinsic time scales of the system ( essentially instantaneously ) .",
    "the time dynamics of small cold atom systems has specifically attracted a great deal of attention recently , either because the few - body dynamics is interesting in its own right or as a model for understanding intricate many - body dynamics  @xcite . here",
    ", we consider an `` in between '' case in which the system hamiltonian is changed continuously on a time scale that is comparable to the intrinsic time scale of the system hamiltonian at time @xmath0 , where @xmath0 is the time at which the time variation of the hamiltonian is turned on . starting with an eigenstate at @xmath0 , we focus on the regime where the long time dynamics exhibits oscillations .",
    "reference  @xcite discussed an intriguing analogy between the dynamics of an @xmath1-atom system with vanishing or infinitely large two - body zero - range interactions under time - dependent external harmonic confinement and the static three - body efimov solution  @xcite .",
    "the former exhibits , for a properly chosen time variation of the trap , log - periodic expansion dynamics  @xcite while the latter is , for infinitely large @xmath2-wave scattering length , characterized by log - periodic energy spacings  @xcite .",
    "it was shown  @xcite that the log - periodic time evolution can be traced back to the scale invariance of the @xmath1-body hamiltonian . specifically , by changing the angular trap frequency with time , the harmonic oscillator length can be effectively removed from the problem , leaving a scale - invariant space - time hamiltonian that is governed by intriguing long - term dynamics . the present paper investigates how the dynamics changes when the two - body interactions define a length scale . how does the time evolution change when the interaction strength increases from zero to infinity ?",

    "<S> the response of a cold atom gas with contact interactions to a smoothly varying external harmonic confinement in the non - adiabatic regime is studied . </S>",
    "<S> the time variation of the angular frequency is varied such that the system is , for vanishing or infinitely strong contact interactions , scale invariant . </S>",
    "<S> the time evolution of the system with broken scale invariance ( i.e. , the time evolution of the system with finite interaction strength ) , is contrasted with that for a scale invariant system , which exhibits efimovian - like expansion dynamics that is characterized by log - periodic oscillations with unique period and amplitude . </S>",
    "<S> it is found that the breaking of the scale invariance by the finiteness of the interactions leads to a time dependence of the oscillation period and amplitude . </S>",
    "<S> it is argued , based on analytical considerations for atomic gases of arbitrary size and numerical results for two one - dimensional particles , that the oscillation period approaches that of the scale - invariant system at large times . </S>",
    "<S> the role of the time - dependent contact in the expansion dynamics is analyzed . </S>"
