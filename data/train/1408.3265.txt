    "suppressed noise in two - mode multi - particle systems known as `` spin squeezing '' introduced by kitagawa and ueda @xcite is an essential tool in quantum metrology protocols @xcite .",
    "the interferometric schemes utilizing this effect cover broad area of possible physical systems , ranging from collective spins of neutral atoms interacting by collisions @xcite , atoms interacting with light by faraday rotation and ac - stark shift @xcite , atoms interacting by rydberg blockade @xcite , polarized light @xcite , to bose - einstein condensates ( bec ) in double - well potentials ( bosonic josephson junctions ) @xcite .",
    "typically , the preparation of spin squeezed states is based on nonlinear inter - particle interactions . in terms of the collective `` spin '' operators @xmath0 ,",
    "the procedures have been classified as `` one - axis twisting '' ( oat ) with a term @xmath1 , and `` two - axis counter - twisting '' ( tact ) with a term @xmath2 , the tact being shown to be more efficient to produce highly squeezed states @xcite .",
    "recently , a scheme has been proposed to combine a sequence of oat and spin rotations to an effectively tact procedure @xcite .",
    "efficient preparation of spin - squeezed states has become an objective of various optimized procedures @xcite . here",
    "i show that any quadratic interaction in the collective spin can be described by means of a twisting tensor , encompassing the oat and tact as special cases .",

    "the maximum squeezing rate only depends on the difference between the maximum and minimum eigenvalues of the twisting tensor . for certain times ,",
    "deviations from the optimum squeezing rate can be compensated by suitable rotations .",
    "the results are applicable for optimizing strategies of interferometric measurements with various nonlinear media .",

    "the aim of this paper was to offer a unified tensor approach to all quadratic squeezing schemes that have so far been treated separately , such as one - axis twisting or two - axis counter - twisting .",
    "the main results are derivation of a closed set of equations governing the first and second moments in gaussian approximation , and showing the role of eigenvalues of the twisting tensor . at the early stages of squeezing ,",
    "the most relevant parameter is the difference between the maximum and minimum eigenvalues of the twisting tensor which determines the squeezing rate . for longer times , most efficient",
    "squeezing is achieved if the middle eigenvalue halves the interval between the extreme ones ( tact ) . in other cases , for certain times the imbalance of the middle eigenvalue",
    "can be compensated by suitable rotation .",
    "the approach is suitable for two - mode systems with quadratic nonlinearities , such as two - mode optical resonators with kerr media , bec in structured traps , collective atomic spins , etc . apart from covering various squeezing scenarios important mostly for quantum metrology and interferometry",
    ", it is also relevant for the lmg model studied as a paradigm of quantum phase transitions .",
    "further generalization to cover losses and decoherence @xcite and various squeezing optimization strategies will be the subject of a forthcoming work .",

    "<S> a unified tensor description of quadratic spin squeezing interactions is proposed , covering the single- and two - axis twisting as special cases of a general scheme . </S>",
    "<S> a closed set of equations of motion of the first moments and variances is derived in gaussian approximation and their solutions are discussed from the prospect of fastest squeezing generation . </S>",
    "<S> it turns out that the optimum rate of squeezing generation is governed by the difference between the largest and the smallest eigenvalues of the twisting tensor . </S>",
    "<S> a cascaded optical interferometer with kerr nonlinear media is proposed as one of possible realizations of the general scheme . </S>"
