    "one of the most challenging problems in quantum many - body systems , especially in low - dimensions , is related to the evaluation of the correlation functions .",
    "though many field theoretical schemes or numerical techniques have been developed and achieved remarkable success , the exact evaluation is in general still very difficult even in models which are _ exactly solvable _ by the bethe ansatz methods .",
    "the spin-1/2 heisenberg xxz chain is one of the simplest but non - trivial solvable models , and has served as a testing ground for many theoretical approaches . concerning the correlation function of this model , jimbo _ et al .",
    "@xcite derived a multiple integral representation of arbitrary correlators in the off - critical xxz antiferromagnet at zero temperature and zero magnetic field .",
    "their method utilizing the representation theory of the quantum affine algebra @xmath0 has been extended to the xxx @xcite , the massless xxz @xcite antiferromagnets .",
    "alternative approaches combining the algebraic bethe ansatz and the solution of the quantum inverse scattering problem for local spin operators were proposed by kitanine _",
    "_ in @xcite , and were applied to derive a multiple integral representation for both the critical and off - critical regimes of the xxz model in _ finite _ magnetic field .",
    "moreover they have obtained a new multiple integral representation which is more appropriate for the two - point correlators @xcite , and have generalized further to the time - dependent longitudinal correlation function @xcite ( see @xcite for a recent review ) . on the other hand",
    ", a finite temperature generalization was achieved more recently by ghmann _",
    "by utilizing the quantum transfer matrix formalism for thermodynamics @xcite ( see also @xcite for recent progress in this direction ) .",
    "motivated by these seminal works , in this paper , we generalize the result @xcite to the time - dependent longitudinal correlation function at finite temperature . by combining a lattice path integral formulation with the solution of the quantum inverse scattering problem",

    "in this paper , we have derived a multiple integral representation for the time - dependent longitudinal correlation function @xmath286 at any finite temperature and finite magnetic field .",
    "the formula reproduces the known results in the following three limits : ( i ) static limit , ( ii ) low - temperature limit and ( iii ) xy limit",
    ". it will be very interesting to consider the following problems which still remain open .",
    "first , it is well - known that the long - distance asymptotics of correlation functions at the low - energy region ( namely @xmath218 or @xmath287 ) in the critical regime can be derived by a field theoretical argument ( see @xcite for example ) .",
    "in contrast , our multiple integral representation is valid for any finite temperature and interaction strength .",
    "the exact computations of the asymptotic behavior from beyond the field theoretical predictions are of importance .",
    "second , how do we evaluate and extract the long - time asymptotics of the correlation function @xmath208 at _ infinite _ temperature ? in this case the auxiliary function @xmath84 in becomes quite simple : @xmath288 .",
    "this problem is of interest in relation to the issue of spin - diffusion in the spin-1/2 heisenberg xxz chain ( see , for example , @xcite ) .",

    "finally we would like to comment on the form factor expansion .",
    "in fact , the multiple integral representation for @xmath218 ( see ) is directly connected to the form factor expansion @xcite . on the other hand , for the finite temperature case , it is not clear whether has a connection with the form factor expansion , since is derived by the quantum transfer matrix acting not on the quantum space but on the auxiliary space .",
