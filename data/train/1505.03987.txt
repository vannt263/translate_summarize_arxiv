    "<S> the equations of secular evolution for dust grains in mean motion resonances with a planet are solved for stationary points . </S>",
    "<S> this is done including both poynting  </S>",
    "<S> robertson effect and stellar wind . </S>",
    "<S> the solutions are stationary in semimajor axis , eccentricity , and resonant angle , but allow the pericentre to advance . </S>",
    "<S> the semimajor axis of stationary solutions can be slightly shifted from the exact resonant value . </S>",
    "<S> the periodicity of the stationary solutions in a reference frame orbiting with the planet is analytically proved . </S>",
    "<S> the existence of periodic solutions in mean motion resonances means that analytical theory enables for dust particles also infinitely long capture times . </S>",
    "<S> the stationary solutions are periodic motions to which the eccentricity asymptotically approaches and around which the libration occurs .    using numerical integration of equation of motion </S>",
    "<S> are successfully found initial conditions corresponding to the stationary solutions . </S>",
    "<S> numerically and analytically determined shifts of the semimajor axis form the exact resonance for the stationary solutions are in excellent agreement .    </S>",
    "<S> the stationary solutions can be plotted by locations of pericenters in the reference frame orbiting with the planet . </S>",
    "<S> the pericenters are distributed in the space according to properties of dust particles .    </S>",
