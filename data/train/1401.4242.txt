    "<S> we develop a unified treatment of pumping and nonequilibrium thermodynamics . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the pumping current generated through an adiabatic mechanical operation in equilibrium can be expressed in terms of the stationary distribution of the corresponding driven nonequilibrium system . </S>",
    "<S> we also show that the total transfer in pumping can be evaluated from the work imported to the driven counterpart . </S>",
    "<S> these findings lead us to a unified viewpoint for pumping and nonequilibrium thermodynamics .    for centuries </S>",
    "<S> , heat pumping has been considered an important topic . </S>",
    "<S> the carnot engine showed the direct relation between mechanical work and pumping of heat . </S>",
    "<S> pumping induced by electric current known as the peltier effect , was explained by the linear response theory as an example of the reciprocal relation . in molecular scales , </S>",
    "<S> the possibility of realizing heat pumps with thermal ratchets is suggested in @xcite    apart from heat pumps , ion pumps or the directive transport of biomolecules are theoretically intensively studied . </S>",
    "<S> these are modeled using flashing ratchets @xcite as stochastic pumps in molecular scales . </S>",
    "<S> the mechanism of pumping in flashing ratchets is related to geometric effects in the parameter space @xcite . the same property in heat pumps </S>",
    "<S> has also been discussed @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> these studies suggest that the universal characteristics of pumps exist in various designs .    in this paper , we develop a unified viewpoint on pumping and nonequilibrium thermodynamics , from which one can derive the universal characteristics of pumps as well as examine efficient protocols for pumping . by pumping , we mean an equilibrium process in which the parameters of the system are varied through an external agent according to a fixed protocol in order to invoke the desired type of current through the system . for each setup of pumping </S>",
    "<S> , we introduce a corresponding `` _ driven counterpart _ '' , i.e. , a nonequilibrium system in which the current flows spontaneously owing to an applied driving field . </S>",
    "<S> we then show that the pumping current is expressed in terms of the stationary probability distribution of the driven system and that it is well evaluated from the work imported to the driven counterpart operated using the same protocol . </S>"
