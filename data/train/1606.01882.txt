    "<S> various types of stabilizing controls lead to a deterministic difference equation with the following property : once the initial value is positive , the solution tends to the unique positive equilibrium . </S>",
    "<S> introducing additive perturbations can change this picture : we give examples of difference equations experiencing additive perturbations which have solutions staying around zero rather than tending to the unique positive equilibrium . </S>",
    "<S> when perturbations are stochastic with a bounded support , we give an upper estimate for the probability that the solution can stay around zero . applying extra conditions on the behavior of the map function @xmath0 at zero or on the amplitudes of stochastic perturbations , </S>",
    "<S> we prove that the solution tends to the unique positive equilibrium almost surely . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , this holds either for all amplitudes when the right derivative of the map @xmath0 at zero exceeds one or , independently of the behavior of @xmath0 at zero , when the amplitudes are not square summable .    * </S>",
