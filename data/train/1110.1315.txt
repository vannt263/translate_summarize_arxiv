    "<S> in this paper we consider a random copolymer near a selective interface separating two solvents . </S>",
    "<S> the configurations of the copolymer are directed paths that can make i.i.d .  </S>",
    "<S> excursions of finite length above and below the interface . the excursion length distribution is assumed to have a tail that is logarithmically equivalent to a power law with exponent @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> the monomers carry i.i.d .  </S>",
    "<S> real - valued types whose distribution is assumed to have zero mean , unit variance , and a finite moment generating function . </S>",
    "<S> the interaction hamiltonian rewards matches and penalizes mismatches of the monomer types and the solvents , and depends on two parameters : the interaction strength @xmath1 and the interaction bias @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> we are interested in the behavior of the copolymer in the limit as its length tends to infinity .    </S>",
    "<S> the quenched free energy per monomer @xmath3 has a phase transition along a quenched critical curve @xmath4 separating a localized phase , where the copolymer stays close to the interface , from a delocalized phase , where the copolymer wanders away from the interface . </S>",
    "<S> we derive _ variational formulas _ for both these quantities . </S>",
    "<S> we compare these variational formulas with their analogues for the annealed free energy per monomer @xmath5 and the annealed critical curve @xmath6 , both of which are explicitly computable . </S>",

    "<S> an estimate of the total number of times the copolymer visits the interface in the interior of the quenched delocalized phase . * an identification of the asymptotic frequency at which the copolymer visits the interface in the quenched localized phase .    </S>",
