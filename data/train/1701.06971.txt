    "<S> we study the dynamics of a pulse - gated semiconductor double quantum dot qubit . in our experiments , </S>",
    "<S> the qubit coherence times are relatively long , but the visibility of the quantum oscillations is low . we show that these observations are consistent with a theory that incorporates decoherence arising from charge noise that gives rise to detuning fluctuations of the double dot . because effects from charge noise are largest near the singlet - triplet avoided level crossing , the visibility of the oscillations are low when the singlet - triplet avoided level crossing occurs in the vicinity of the charge degeneracy point crossed during the manipulation , but there is only modest dephasing at the large detuning value at which the quantum phase accumulates . </S>",
    "<S> this theory agrees well with experimental data and predicts that the visibility can be increased greatly by appropriate tuning of the interdot tunneling rate .    </S>",
    "<S> = 1    _ introduction . _ </S>",
    "<S> electrically - gated solid - state qubits fabricated using quantum dots in semiconductors are attractive because of the similarity of the technology to that used in current classical electronic devices , with the great potential advantages of scalability and relative ease of qubit manipulation  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> quantum dot qubits in gallium arsenide ( gaas ) heterostructures  @xcite display fast dephasing ( on nanosecond time scales ) due to the strong hyperfine interaction between electron and nuclear spins  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> electrons in silicon quantum dots have weaker coupling to nuclear spins  @xcite , and measured qubit coherence times are indeed longer , on the order of @xmath0  @xcite for natural silicon and even longer for isotopically enriched silicon@xcite . integrating a micromagnet into a double quantum dot device enables the establishment of a large magnetic field difference between the dots that does not depend on the presence of nuclear spins  @xcite , enabling fast spin manipulations without introducing a magnetic source of decoherence .    in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we study landau - zener - stckelberg ( lzs ) oscillations that are performed by pulsing through an @xmath1 anticrossing in a double quantum dot fabricated in a silicon / silicon - germanium ( si / sige ) heterostructure with an integrated micromagnet . </S>",
    "<S> lzs oscillations were demonstrated first in gaas devices @xcite . in the gaas experiments , </S>",
    "<S> the coherence time of the lzs oscillations is short , @xmath210 ns , with an oscillation visibility of @xmath230%  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> we report lzs experiments performed in a si / sige heterostructure for a variety of ramp rates and find that the decoherence times are indeed much longer , @xmath3 , but that the visibility of the qubit oscillations is only @xmath430% . </S>",
    "<S> we then demonstrate that these observations can be understood as a consequence of the presence of charge noise . </S>",
    "<S> dephasing from charge noise has been argued previously to be important for lzs experiments  @xcite , and numerical simulations have yielded strong evidence that charge noise effects are substantial  @xcite . here </S>",
    "<S> we argue that because the energy splitting at the relevant anticrossing is much smaller than the temperature , excitations across the energy gap play a critical role . </S>",
    "<S> the effects of charge noise are substantial only near the charge transition and are much smaller at large detunings where the spin rotations are performed , so the measured spin coherence times can be long even though the visibility is low . our theoretical treatment yields substantial analytic insight into the processes limiting the oscillation visibility . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the visibility can be increased substantially by changing the dot parameters , specifically , by increasing the interdot tunnel coupling .    _ </S>",

    "<S> conclusions . </S>",
    "<S>  _ we have shown that charge noise , which induces a fluctuating detuning on a double quantum dot , can give rise to low visibility of lzs oscillations even when the decoherence time is very long . </S>",
    "<S> the key physics is that decoherence processes are greatly enhanced at the @xmath8 anticrossing , which decreases the visibility , but are suppressed at large detuning , leading to a long decoherence time . </S>",
    "<S> the numerical results agree well with the experimental data using fitting parameters that agree with the estimations from experiment . </S>",
    "<S> the mechanism may also apply to other types of qubit that exhibit low visibility and long decoherence time@xcite . </S>",
