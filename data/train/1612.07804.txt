    "there is now solid evidence that the formation of high - mass stars ( @xmath9 ) involves accretion through circumstellar disks @xcite .",
    "this observational evidence supports recent theoretical work which suggests that the radiation pressure barrier problem can be overcome when considering more complex than spherically symmetric infall geometries @xcite .",
    "however , the classical accretion disk scenario , which assumes the monolithic collapse of a core to a single star and which is commonly applied to low - mass star formation , fails to explain the high multiplicity fraction that has been observed for high - mass stars .",
    "multiplicity studies on main - sequence stars found that @xmath10% of all o - type stars ( @xmath11 ) are close multiple systems , while this fraction rapidly drops to 20% for stellar masses of @xmath12 @xcite . at the same time , the number of companions per system also increases with stellar mass .",
    "for instance , the five o-/early b - type stars in the orion trapezium cluster have on average 2.5 known companions , which is about @xmath13 higher than what has been found for low - mass stars @xcite .",
    "different mechanisms have been proposed in order to explain these extraordinary characteristics .",
    "for instance , these high - mass multiples might form via fragmentation of self - gravitating massive disks @xcite , disk - assisted capture @xcite , or through failed mergers in stellar collisions @xcite .",
    "testing these scenarios requires the detection of high - mass `` protobinaries '' that are still in their formation phase .",
