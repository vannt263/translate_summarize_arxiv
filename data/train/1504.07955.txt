    "<S> it is shown that a charge - neutral spin-1 exciton , realizable only in planar systems like graphene , can effectively be used for controlled spin transport in such media . </S>",
    "<S> the excitonic bound state is destabilized by quantum fluctuations , characterized by a threshold for excitation and melts in a smooth manner under thermal fluctuations . </S>",
    "<S> this planar exciton differs from the conventional ones , as it owes its existence to the topological chern - simons ( cs ) term . </S>",
    "<S> the parity and time - reversal violating cs term can arise from quantum effects in systems with parity - breaking mass - gap . </S>",
    "<S> the spinning exciton naturally couples to magnetic field , leading to the possibility of controlled spin transport . being neutral , it is immune to adverse effects , afflicting spin transport by charged fermions .    </S>",
    "<S> # 1#2#3#4#1 * # 2 * , # 3 ( # 4 )    lower dimensional systems exhibit a host of unique features @xcite , absent in their higher - dimensional counterparts . </S>",
    "<S> realization of majorana fermion @xcite , thin film topological insulators ( tis ) @xcite , charge density wave @xcite , helical transport @xcite and charge - spin separation @xcite characterize one - dimensional systems . </S>",
    "<S> planar ( 2 + 1 dimensional ) materials have led to physical realization of large spin - orbit coupling @xcite , spin - momentum locking @xcite and low - energy dirac fermions @xcite etc . </S>",
    "<S> 2 + 1 dimensional systems are endowed with non - trivial inherent topology , which manifests in quantum hall effect @xcite and boundary conductivity in tis @xcite , having symmetry protected topological order @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> [ [ section ] ]    the characteristic non - trivial feature of the planar world , absent in their even - dimensional counterparts , is the manifestation of the topological chern - simons ( cs ) term ,    _ </S>",
    "<S> cs=^a__a_.[e1 ] in the gauge sector , due to which , propagating photons can have gauge - invariant mass @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> interacting particles can acquire additional spin @xcite , leading to a change in their statistics @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the cs term arises due to quantum effects , in theories with parity - breaking massive fermions @xcite and bosons @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the advent of planar materials like graphene @xcite and tis @xcite have led to physical realization of low - energy ` relativistic ' planar systems . </S>",
