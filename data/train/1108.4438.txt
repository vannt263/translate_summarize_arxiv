    "<S> we demonstrate quantitative magnetic field mapping with nanoscale resolution , by applying a lock - in technique on the electron spin resonance frequency of a single nitrogen - vacancy defect placed at the apex of an atomic force microscope tip . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , we report an all - optical magnetic imaging technique which is sensitive to large off - axis magnetic fields , thus extending the operation range of diamond - based magnetometry . </S>",
    "<S> both techniques are illustrated by using a magnetic hard disk as a test sample . </S>",
    "<S> owing to the non - perturbing and quantitative nature of the magnetic probe , this work should open up numerous perspectives in nanomagnetism and spintronics .    </S>",
    "<S> the ability to map magnetic field distributions with high sensitivity and nanoscale resolution is of crucial importance for fundamental studies ranging from material science to biology , as well as for the development of new applications in spintronics and quantum technology  @xcite . in that context </S>",
    "<S> , an ideal scanning probe magnetometer should provide quantitative magnetic field mapping at the nanoscale under ambient conditions . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , the magnetic sensor should not introduce a significant magnetic perturbation of the probed sample . </S>",
    "<S> + over the last decades , different roads have been taken towards ultra - sensitive detection of magnetic fields including superconducting quantum interference devices ( squids )  @xcite , semiconductor - based hall probes  @xcite and optical magnetometers  @xcite . even though extremely high sensitivity has been achieved with these devices , </S>",
    "<S> their spatial resolution remains limited at the micron - scale . </S>",
    "<S> prominent approaches to reach nanoscale resolution are scanning - tunneling microscopy  @xcite , mechanical detection of magnetic resonance  @xcite , nanosquids  @xcite , x - ray microscopy  @xcite and magnetic force microscopy ( mfm )  @xcite . since the latter technique operates under ambient conditions without any specific sample preparation , it is now routinely used for mapping magnetic field gradients around magnetic nanostructures . however , besides introducing an inevitable perturbation of the studied magnetic sample owing to the intrinsic magnetic nature of the probe  @xcite , mfm does not provide quantitative information about the magnetic field distribution . </S>",
    "<S> + here we follow a recently proposed approach to magnetic sensing based on optically detected electron spin resonance ( esr )  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> it was shown that this method applied to a single nitrogen - vacancy ( nv ) defect in diamond could provide an unprecedented combination of spatial resolution and magnetic sensitivity under ambient conditions  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the principle of the measurement is similar to the one used in optical magnetometers based on the precession of spin - polarized atomic gases  @xcite . </S>",
