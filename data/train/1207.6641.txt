    "supersymmetry ( susy ) is one of the most popular extensions of the standard model ( sm ) @xcite .",
    "the main advantage with respect to non - susy models is the technical solution that it provides to the hierarchy problem @xcite .",
    "in addition , other theoretical and phenomenological issues can be addressed in the context of supersymmetric models .",
    "for example , questions such as the radiative origin of the electroweak symmetry breaking @xcite and the unification of the gauge couplings at high energies @xcite .",
    "however , despite these appealing theoretical motivations , no experimental evidence of supersymmetry has been found so far at the large hadron collider ( lhc ) @xcite .",
    "this might be telling us that we are adopting a wrong search strategy .",
    "in fact , most searches are based on the assumption of conserved r - parity @xcite , thus including a lower cut on the amount of missing transverse energy .",
    "this should encourage the search for non - minimal supersymmetric scenarios with a departure from the usual signatures , such as those with r - parity violation @xcite .",
    "in the minimal supersymmetric standard model ( mssm ) there are renormalizable terms , perfectly allowed under all symmetries of the theory , that break either baryon or lepton number @xcite .",
    "these new interactions , that involve sm particles and their superpartners , are highly constrained by the non - observation of baryon or lepton number violating processes .",
    "in fact , if baryon and lepton number breaking interactions were present at the same time , they would induce proton decay . for these reasons ,",
    "these dangerous terms are usually forbidden by an _",
    "ad - hoc _ additional symmetry , such as r - parity .",
    "however , in order to stabilize the proton it is sufficient to forbid one of these terms , namely the baryon number violating one . in this case , the lepton number violating interactions would contribute to the generation of majorana neutrino masses @xcite , an open issue not addressed in the mssm , while problems due to proton decay are evaded .",
    "this well motivated framework is one of the few scenarios where the mechanism behind neutrino mass generation can be directly tested in accelerator experiments , see for example @xcite .",
    "furthermore , the presence of r - parity violating couplings opens up good perspectives for a richer phenomenology .",
    "although the smallness of the r - parity violating couplings implies that the standard decay chains are not affected , the decay of the lsp changes dramatically the final signatures at the lhc @xcite .",
    "in fact , the lsp decay reduces the amount of missing transverse energy in the final state .",
    "instead , one expects events with large lepton and/or jet multiplicity .",
    "this has been recently used by different authors @xcite to relax the stringent bounds on the squark and gluino masses , otherwise pushed to values clearly above the tev .",
