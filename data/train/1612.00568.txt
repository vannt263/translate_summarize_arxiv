    "<S> many realizations of solid - state qubits involve couplings to leakage states lying outside the computational subspace , posing a threat to high - fidelity quantum gate operations . </S>",
    "<S> mitigating leakage errors is especially challenging when the coupling strength is unknown , e.g. , when it is caused by noise . here we show that simple pulse sequences can be used to strongly suppress leakage errors for a qubit embedded in a three - level system . as an example </S>",
    "<S> , we apply our scheme to the recently proposed charge quadrupole ( cq ) qubit for quantum dots . </S>",
    "<S> these results provide a solution to a key challenge for fault - tolerant quantum computing with solid - state elements .    </S>",
    "<S> recent advances in semiconducting quantum dots make them promising candidates for universal quantum computing  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , performing gate operations with high enough fidelity to support fault - tolerant error correction remains a key challenge  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> two ways that a qubit can fail to have high fidelity are ( i ) the qubit could decay or dephase , and ( ii ) quantum information could leak out of the qubit s logical subspace into other quantum states in the physical system  @xcite . while several recent proposals in quantum dots have focused on suppressing dephasing from environmental noise  @xcite , relatively little effort has gone into suppressing leakage .    several approaches for reducing leakage errors have been developed for superconducting qubits , including analytic pulse shaping  @xcite , optimal quantum control  @xcite and concatenated bang - bang pulses  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , suppressing leakage errors below the fault - tolerant threshold under experimental conditions for quantum dot qubits is challenging , because of the need to apply smoothly varying control pulses  @xcite and because the qubits experience fluctuations that vary in time and strength  @xcite . for semiconducting systems , </S>",
    "<S> these constraints preclude using analytically derived pulse shapes , since these require knowing the noise strength as an input parameter  @xcite , or quantum control strategies that rely on optimizing an unrealistically large number of control parameters  @xcite .    in this work </S>",
    "<S> , we develop a simple and experimentally feasible protocol based on composite pulses that suppresses leakage errors in a three - level quantum system where the leakage state is coupled to one of the logical states with an unknown but static coupling strength ( referred to as the quasistatic noise approximation  @xcite ) . whereas the leakage error scales quadratically with noise amplitude in conventional pulsed - gate schemes for this model , our protocol significantly improves the gate fidelities by eliminating computational errors in the logical subspace up to fourth order in the noise amplitude and leakage errors up to sixth order .    in a basis comprised of two logical states and one leakage state , </S>",

    "<S> , we have developed special pulse sequences for a three - level system , comprised of two logical states and a leakage state , which suppress the leakage probability and enable arbitrary , high - fidelity , single - qubit rotations . </S>",
    "<S> we consider a general model , in which only one of the logical states is coupled to the leakage state . </S>",
    "<S> a crucial feature of this method is that it requires no knowledge of the coupling to the leakage state . </S>",
    "<S> the method is perfectly suited for the quantum dot charge quadrupole ( cq ) qubit , whose leakage coupling is caused by charge noise . taking the cq as our model system </S>",
    "<S> we have shown that computational errors in the gate operation arise at fourth order in the noise amplitude , while leakage errors arise at sixth order . </S>",
    "<S> this represents a substantial improvement over conventional pulse schemes , for which errors arise at second order in the noise amplitude . to understand why our pulse sequence works , we have introduced an intuitive geometrical mapping of the three - level system , in which the logical and leakage states are represented on two coupled bloch spheres . in this way </S>",
