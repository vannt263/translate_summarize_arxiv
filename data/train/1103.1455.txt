    "<S> in this paper an approach is outlined . with this approach </S>",
    "<S> some explicit algorithms can be applied to solve the initial value problem of @xmath0dimensional damped oscillators . </S>",
    "<S> this approach is based upon following structure : for any non - conservative classical mechanical system and arbitrary initial conditions , there exists a conservative system ; both systems share one and only one common phase curve ; and , the value of the hamiltonian of the conservative system is , up to an additive constant , equal to the total energy of the non - conservative system on the aforementioned phase curve , the constant depending on the initial conditions . </S>",
    "<S> a key way applying explicit symplectic algorithms to damping oscillators is that by the newton - laplace principle the nonconservative force can be reasonably assumed to be equal to a function of a component of generalized coordinates @xmath1 along a phase curve , such that the damping force can be represented as a function analogous to an elastic restoring force numerically in advance . </S>",
    "<S> two numerical examples are given to demonstrate the good characteristics of the algorithms .    </S>",
    "<S> hamiltonian , dissipation , non - conservative system , damping , explicit symplectic algorithm </S>"
