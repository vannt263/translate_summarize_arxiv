    "<S> the resulting asymmetry of the transport characteristic gives rise to a ratchet effect , i.e. , the rectification of a dc current for an applied ac voltage . in the case of strong repulsive interactions , the ratchet current grows in a wide voltage interval with decreasing ac voltage . in the regime of weak </S>",
    "<S> interaction the current - voltage curve exhibits oscillatory behavior . </S>",
    "<S> our results apply to single - band quantum wires and to tunneling between quantum hall edges </S>",
