    "<S> we analyze the quantum quench dynamics in the formation of a phase - biased superconducting nanojunction . </S>",
    "<S> we find that in the absence of an external relaxation mechanism and for very general conditions the system gets trapped in a metastable state , corresponding to a non - equilibrium population of the andreev bound states . </S>",
    "<S> the use of the time - dependent full counting statistics ( fcs ) analysis allows us to extract information on the asymptotic population of even and odd many - body states , demonstrating that a _ universal _ </S>",
    "<S> behavior , dependent only on the andreev states energy , is reached in the quantum point contact limit . </S>",
    "<S> these results shed light on recent experimental observations on quasiparticle trapping in superconducting atomic contacts .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction : _ superconducting nanodevices are of central interest as building blocks of future quantum information processors . </S>",
    "<S> while traditionally based on josephson junctions architectures @xcite , superconducting quantum dots and quantum point contacts are now being explored for quantum information applications like generation of electron entanglement through cooper pair splitting @xcite and the so - called andreev qubits @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> similar hybrid systems can host majorana - like excitations whose search and potential applications are generating a huge research activity in the condensed matter community @xcite . within this context , </S>",
    "<S> studies of the transient response of superconducting nanodevices are of basic as well as practical interest @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this is connected with the unexplained evidence of residual non - equilibrium quasiparticles which undermines the quantum coherence in these devices @xcite . in the case of superconducting atomic contacts ( sacs ) </S>",
    "<S> this phenomenon manifests in the presence of long lived trapped quasiparticle states within their andreev bound states ( abs ) @xcite .    in the present work we address these questions by analyzing the quench dynamics in the formation of a phase - biased superconducting single channel contact . </S>",
