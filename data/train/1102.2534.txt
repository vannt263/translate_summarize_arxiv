    "<S> consider a measurable space with a finite vector measure . </S>",
    "<S> this measure defines a mapping of the @xmath0-field into a euclidean space . according to lyapunov s convexity theorem , the range of this mapping is compact and , if the measure is atomless , this range is convex . </S>",
    "<S> similar ranges are also defined for measurable subsets of the space . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the union of the ranges of all subsets having the same given vector measure is also compact and , if the measure is atomless , it is convex . </S>",
    "<S> we further provide a geometrically constructed convex compactum in the euclidean space that contains this union . </S>",
    "<S> the equality of these two sets , that holds for two - dimensional measures , can be violated in higher dimensions . </S>"
