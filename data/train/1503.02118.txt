    "coherent quantum feedback control builds on the idea of constructing a feedback loop from the interconnection of quantum systems through field coupling @xcite .",
    "this technique avoids loss of quantum information in conversion to classical signals which occurs during measurement , as a direct consequence of the projection postulate in quantum mechanics @xcite .",
    "the coherent quantum control approach aims at developing systematic methods to design measurement - free interconnections of quantum systems modelled by quantum stochastic differential equations ( qsdes ) ; see for example @xcite .",
    "quantum - optical components , such as optical cavities , beam splitters and phase shifters , make it possible to implement quantum feedback systems governed by linear qsdes @xcite , provided the latter represent open quantum harmonic oscillators @xcite .",
    "this important class of linear qsdes models the heisenberg evolution of pairs of conjugate operators in a multi - mode quantum harmonic oscillator that is coupled to external bosonic fields .",
    "the condition of physical realizability ( pr ) of a linear qsde as an open quantum harmonic oscillator is organised as a set of constraints on the coefficients of the qsde @xcite or , alternatively , on the quantum system transfer matrix @xcite in the frequency domain .",
    "coherent quantum feedback control problems , such as stabilization and robust controller design , are of particular interest in linear quantum control theory @xcite",
    ". these problems are amenable to transfer matrix design methods @xcite .",
    "there are classical approaches to control synthesis problems for linear multivariable systems based on the transfer matrix of the system @xcite .",
    "in particular , one of the important strategies in controller design for such systems is the stable factorization approach @xcite .",
    "the central idea of the factorization approach is to represent the transfer matrix of a system as a ratio of stable rational matrices .",
    "this idea gives rise to a methodology which leads to the solution of several important control problems ; see @xcite",
    ". one of the fundamental results used in the factorization approach to classical control is the parameterization of all stabilizing controllers , which is known as the youla - kuera parameterization .",
    "the youla - kuera parameterization was developed originally in the frequency domain for finite - dimensional linear time - invariant systems using transfer function methods , see @xcite , and generalized to infinite - dimensional systems afterwards @xcite .",
    "the state space representation of all stabilizing controllers has also been addressed for finite - dimensional , linear time - invariant @xcite and time - varying @xcite systems .",
    "furthermore , the approach was shown to be applicable to a class of nonlinear systems @xcite . in the present paper",
    ", we employ the stable factorization approach in order to develop a quantum counterpart of the classical youla - kuera parameterization for describing a set of linear coherent quantum controllers that stabilize a linear quantum system .",
    "in particular , we address the problem of coherent quantum stabilizability of a given linear quantum plant .",
    "the class of stabilizing controllers is parameterized in the frequency domain .",
    "this approach allows weighted @xmath0 and @xmath1 coherent quantum control problems to be formulated for linear quantum systems in the frequency domain . in this way",
    ", the weighted @xmath0 and @xmath1 control problems are reduced to constrained optimization problems with respect to the youla - kuera parameter with convex cost functionals .",
    "moreover , these problems are organised as a constrained version of the model matching problem @xcite .",
    "finally , a projected gradient descent scheme is proposed to solve the weighted @xmath0 coherent quantum control problem in the frequency domain .",

    "<S> this paper is concerned with application of the classical youla - kuera parameterization to finding a set of linear coherent quantum controllers that stabilize a linear quantum plant . </S>",
    "<S> the plant and controller are assumed to represent open quantum harmonic oscillators modelled by linear quantum stochastic differential equations . </S>",
    "<S> the interconnections between the plant and the controller are assumed to be established through quantum bosonic fields . in this framework , </S>",
    "<S> conditions for the stabilization of a given linear quantum plant via linear coherent quantum feedback are addressed using a stable factorization approach . </S>",
    "<S> the class of stabilizing quantum controllers is parameterized in the frequency domain . </S>",
    "<S> also , this approach is used in order to formulate coherent quantum weighted @xmath0 and @xmath1 control problems for linear quantum systems in the frequency domain . </S>",
    "<S> finally , a projected gradient descent scheme is proposed to solve the coherent quantum weighted @xmath0 control problem . </S>"
