    "<S> the paper provides a rigorous analysis of the dispersion spectrum of sh ( shear horizontal ) elastic waves in periodically stratified solids . </S>",
    "<S> the problem consists of an ordinary differential wave equation with periodic coefficients , which involves two free parameters @xmath0 ( the frequency ) and @xmath1 ( the wavenumber in the direction orthogonal to the axis of periodicity ) . </S>",
    "<S> solutions of this equation satisfy a quasi - periodic boundary condition which yields the floquet parameter @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> the resulting dispersion surface @xmath3 may be characterized through its cuts at constant values of @xmath4 and @xmath0 that define the passband ( real @xmath2 ) and stopband areas , the floquet branches and the isofrequency curves , respectively . </S>",
    "<S> the paper combines complementary approaches based on eigenvalue problems and on the monodromy matrix @xmath5 . </S>",
    "<S> the pivotal object is the lyapunov function @xmath6 which is generalized as a function of two variables . </S>",
    "<S> its analytical properties , asymptotics and bounds are examined and an explicit form of its derivatives obtained . attention is given to the special case of a zero - width stopband . </S>",
    "<S> these ingredients are used to analyze the cuts of the surface @xmath7 the derivatives of the functions @xmath8 at fixed @xmath2 and @xmath9 at fixed @xmath1 and of the function @xmath10 at fixed @xmath0 are described in detail . </S>",
    "<S> the curves @xmath8 at fixed @xmath2 are shown to be monotonic for real @xmath11 while they may be looped for complex @xmath2 ( i.e. in the stopband areas ) . </S>",
    "<S> the convexity of the closed ( first ) real isofrequency curve @xmath10 is proved thus ruling out low - frequency caustics of group velocity . </S>",
    "<S> the results are relevant to the broad area of applicability of ordinary differential equation for scalar waves in 1d phononic ( solid or fluid ) and photonic crystals . </S>"
