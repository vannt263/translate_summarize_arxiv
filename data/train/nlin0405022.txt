    "<S> the interaction of polymers with turbulent shear flows is examined . </S>",
    "<S> we focus on the structure of the elastic stress tensor , which is proportional to the polymer conformation tensor . </S>",
    "<S> we examine this object in turbulent flows of increasing complexity . </S>",
    "<S> first is isotropic turbulence , then anisotropic ( but homogenous ) shear turbulence and finally wall bounded turbulence . </S>",
    "<S> the main result of this paper is that for all these flows the polymer stress tensor attains a universal structure in the limit of large deborah number @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> we present analytic results for the suppression of the coil - stretch transition at large deborah numbers . above the transition the turbulent velocity fluctuations </S>",
    "<S> are strongly correlated with the polymer s elongation : there appear high - quality  hydro - elastic \" waves in which turbulent kinetic energy turns into polymer potential energy and vice versa . </S>",
    "<S> these waves determine the trace of the elastic stress tensor but practically do not modify its universal structure . </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate that the influence of the polymers on the balance of energy and momentum can be accurately described by an effective polymer viscosity that is proportional to to the cross - stream component of the elastic stress tensor . </S>",
    "<S> this component is smaller than the stream - wise component by a factor proportional to @xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> finally we tie our results to wall bounded turbulence and clarify some puzzling facts observed in the problem of drag reduction by polymers . </S>"
