    "<S> the dissipative dicke model exhibits a fascinating out - of - equilibrium many - body phase transition as a function of a coupling between a driven photonic cavity and numerous two - level atoms . </S>",
    "<S> we study the effect of a time - dependent parametric modulation of this coupling , and discover a rich phase diagram as a function of the modulation strength . </S>",
    "<S> we find that in addition to the established normal and super - radiant phases , a new phase with pulsed superradiance which we term dynamical normal phase appears when the system is parametrically driven . </S>",
    "<S> employing different methods , we characterize the different phases and the transitions between them . </S>",
    "<S> specific heed is paid to the role of dissipation in determining the phase boundaries . </S>",
    "<S> our analysis paves the road for the experimental study of dynamically stabilized phases of interacting light and matter .    </S>",
    "<S> experimental progress in control and manipulation of light - matter quantum systems has generated a growing interest in many - body phenomena out of equilibrium  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> well established examples of such systems include ultracold atomic or ionic quantum gases in high finesse optical cavities  @xcite , semiconductor microcavities in the strong coupling regime  @xcite , and superconducting qubits in microwave resonators  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the engineered interplay between light and matter in these systems has led to the observation of a host of fascinating collective phases and quantum phase transitions including superfluidity in polaritons  @xcite and the super - radiant dicke phase transition in a bose - einstein condensate ( bec ) coupled to an optical cavity  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> a defining feature of many of these systems is that they are inherently driven and subject to dissipation . </S>",
    "<S> driven dissipative systems are usually treated within a rotating frame formalism . this effectively renders the problem time - independent with the important feature that the asymptotic steady states are necessarily out of equilibrium . </S>",
    "<S> however , parametric driving of the system often does not allow the usual rotating frame simplifications . </S>",
    "<S> consequently , the resulting interplay between interactions , dissipation , and parametric driving , could lead to novel and exotic steady - state physics that has no counterpart in the undriven case . </S>",
    "<S> parametric driving is increasingly used as an experimental tool in diverse contexts , e.g.  , in the generation of floquet topological insulators  @xcite , improved measurement fidelity with squeezed quantum states  @xcite and unconventional phenomena in cavity qed  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> a prime example of a system exhibiting light - matter collective phenomenon is the dicke model  @xcite . here </S>",
    "<S> , a bosonic / cavity mode is coupled to a large number of two - level atoms . </S>",
    "<S> it exhibits a @xmath0 symmetry breaking quantum phase transition from a normal phase ( np ) , where all the atoms are in their ground state and the cavity is empty , to a super - radiant phase ( sp ) , where the atoms are excited and the cavity is in a coherent state . </S>",
    "<S> this model has recently been realized by coupling the external degree of freedom of a bec to a quantized mode of a laser - driven optical cavity , and the theoretically predicted non - equilibrium phase transition has been observed  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , the inevitable photon leakage out of the cavity as well as dissipation of the bec has been shown to lead to a considerable modification of the critical exponents of the transition  @xcite .     a sketch of a parametrically driven dicke model . </S>",
