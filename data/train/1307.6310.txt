    "the non - diffusive thermoelastic martensitic transition ( mt ) in ferromagnetic shape memory alloy ( fsma ) is a notable example of magneto - structural instability among metallic alloys .",
    "fsmas are bi - ferroic materials which combine ferroelasticity and ferromagnetism through the mt , and this magnetoelastic coupling helps to manipulate the ferroelastic domains of fsma by magnetic field leading to numerous functional properties .",
    "@xcite the electronic and magnetic ground states of fsmas are often found to be quite intriguing due to the complex interplay between these two ferroic properties .",
    "the present paper deals with a cu - mn - al based fsma with particular focus on the magnetic ground state of the sample . localized nature of mn moment is responsible for the magnetism in this system  @xcite , and the magnetic coupling is mediated through rudermann - kittel - kasuya - yosida ( rkky ) type exchange interaction between mn atoms .",
    "@xcite stoichiometric cu@xmath3mnal is ferromagnetic in nature although its elements are all non - ferromagnetic .",
    "@xcite however , cu@xmath3mnal does not undergo martensitic type structural instability .",

    "@xcite development of a glassy magnetic state out of a long range magnetically ordered state is referred as a reentrant spin glass ( rsg ) transition .",
    "@xcite rsg state occurs due to the competition between fm and afm interactions arising from site or bond randomness  @xcite , although the mean interaction has to have a nonzero fm character for the realization of an rsg phase .",
    "the magnetic phase below the spin freezing transition ( here occurring at @xmath8 @xmath19 ) is a mixed phase with the coexistence of fm and spin - glass orderings .",
    "in contrast , usual csg have spin freezing directly from a disordered paramagnetic phase and it is generally not associated with any net fm moment . the characteristic parameters ( such as @xmath30 , @xmath66 , @xmath47 etc . ) obtained from @xmath29 dependent @xmath16 measurement and their subsequent fitting to the relevant models do not match with that of csg .",
    "they are rather close to the values reported for cluster glass or rsg systems .",
    "the magnetic phase in cu@xmath0mn@xmath1al@xmath2 is substantially different from the conventional rsg systems such as fe - au or fe - mn alloys .",
