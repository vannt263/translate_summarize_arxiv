    "<S> we generalize the elliott - yafet ( ey ) theory of spin relaxation in metals with inversion symmetry for the case of large spin - orbit coupling ( soc ) . </S>",
    "<S> the ey theory treats the soc to the lowest order but this approach breaks down for metals of heavy elements ( such as e.g. caesium or gold ) , where the soc energy is comparable to the relevant band - band separation energies . </S>",
    "<S> the generalized theory is presented for a four - band model system without band dispersion , where analytic formulae are attainable for arbitrary soc for the relation between the momentum- and spin - relaxation rates . as an extended description , </S>",
    "<S> we also consider an empirical pseudopotential approximation where soc is deduced from the band potential ( apart from an empirical scaling constant ) and the spin - relaxation rate can be obtained numerically . </S>",
    "<S> both approaches recover the usual ey theory for weak soc and give that the spin - relaxation rate approaches the momentum - relaxation rate in the limit of strong soc . </S>",
    "<S> we argue that this limit is realized in gold by analyzing spin relaxation data . </S>",
    "<S> a calculation of the @xmath0-factor shows that the empirical elliott - relation , which links the @xmath0-factor and spin - relaxation rate , is retained even for strong soc .    </S>",
    "<S> the prospect of using electron spins as information carriers , ( a field known as spintronics ) renewed the interest in the fundamental desciption of spin relaxation in metals and semiconductors . </S>",
    "<S> spintronics devices rely on the controlled creation and readout of a non - equilibrium net spin population . </S>",
    "<S> spin relaxation in turn characterizes how rapidly the non - equilibrium spin population decays , knowledge and theoretical description of spin relaxation is therefore of central importance .    </S>",
    "<S> the first experiment in the field dates back to 1955 ( ref . ) , which reported the first electron spin resonance in metals . the first proper theoretical description of spin relaxation in metals with inversion symmetry was provided by elliott @xcite , which was later generalized to lower temperatures and for various relaxation mechanisms by yafet @xcite . </S>",
