    "<S> the objectives of the present work are twofold . </S>",
    "<S> the first is to address and resolve some of the differences present in independent , chiral - effective - field - theory ( @xmath0eft ) derivations up to one loop , recently appeared in the literature , of the nuclear charge and current operators . </S>",
    "<S> the second objective is to provide a complete set of @xmath0eft predictions for the structure functions and tensor polarization of the deuteron , for the charge and magnetic form factors of @xmath1he and @xmath1h , and for the charge and magnetic radii of these few - nucleon systems . </S>",
    "<S> the calculations use wave functions derived from high - order chiral two- and three - nucleon potentials and monte carlo methods to evaluate the relevant matrix elements . </S>",
    "<S> predictions based on conventional potentials in combination with @xmath0eft charge and current operators are also presented . </S>",
    "<S> there is excellent agreement between theory and experiment for all these observables for momentum transfers up to @xmath22.5 @xmath3 ; for a subset of them , this agreement extends to momentum transfers as high as @xmath46 @xmath3 . </S>",
    "<S> a complete analysis of the results is provided . </S>"
