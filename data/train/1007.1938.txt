    "<S> homogeneous rotation symmetric boolean functions have been extensively studied in recent years because of their applications in cryptography . </S>",
    "<S> little is known about the basic question of when two such functions are affine equivalent . </S>",
    "<S> the simplest case of quadratic rotation symmetric functions which are generated by cyclic permutations of the variables in a single monomial was only settled in 2009 . </S>",
    "<S> this paper studies the much more complicated cubic case for such functions . </S>",
    "<S> a new concept of _ patterns _ is introduced , by means of which the structure of the smallest group @xmath0 , whose action on the set of all such cubic functions in @xmath1 variables gives the affine equivalence classes for these functions under permutation of the variables , is determined . </S>",
    "<S> we conjecture that the equivalence classes are the same if all nonsingular affine transformations , not just permutations , are allowed . </S>",
    "<S> our method gives much more information about the equivalence classes ; for example , in this paper we give a complete description of the equivalence classes when @xmath1 is a prime or a power of @xmath2 .    </S>",
