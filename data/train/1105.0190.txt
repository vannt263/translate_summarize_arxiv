    "precoding and power control are well studied strategies that support high spectral efficiency in wireless network with multiple antenna transceivers , when channel state information ( csi ) is available at the transmitters .",
    "several system - wide objective functions have been considered in the literature for precoding and power control optimization of broadcast channels ( bcs ) and interference channels ( ics ) .",
    "some of these problems are convex , for example power minimization @xcite or sinr balancing for the multiple - input single - output ( miso ) bc @xcite , and thus solvable with standard techniques in reasonable ( polynomial ) time . however ,",
    "in general , the problems at hand are non - convex .",
    "unlike convex problems , non - convex problems typically do not afford efficient ( i.e. , polynomial - time ) algorithms that are able to achieve global optimality @xcite .",
    "for example , it is known that the weighted sum - rate maximization ( wsrm ) in parallel ic channels , where interference from other users is treated as noise ( a non - convex problem ) is np - hard @xcite ( this result extends also to bc as a special case ) .    in this paper",

    ", we propose a novel bb framework for global optimization of a problem formulation that includes , for instance , miso bc and ic wsrm with general convex power constraint .",

    "<S> utility ( e.g. , sum - rate ) maximization for multiantenna broadcast and interference channels ( with one antenna at the receivers ) is known to be in general a non - convex problem , if one limits the scope to linear ( beamforming ) strategies at transmitter and receivers . in this paper , it is shown that , under some standard assumptions , most notably that the utility function is decreasing with the interference levels at the receivers , a global optimal solution can be found with reduced complexity via a suitably designed branch - and - bound method . although infeasible for real - time implementation , this procedure enables a non - heuristic and systematic assessment of suboptimal techniques . </S>",
    "<S> in addition to the global optimal scheme , a real - time suboptimal algorithm , which generalizes the well - known distributed pricing techniques , is also proposed . </S>",
    "<S> finally , numerical results are provided that compare global optimal solutions with suboptimal ( pricing ) techniques for sum - rate maximization problems , affording insight into issues such as the robustness against bad initializations in real - time suboptimal strategies .    </S>",
