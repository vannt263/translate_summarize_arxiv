    "<S> weyl s formulation of quantum mechanics opened the possibility of studying the dynamics of quantum systems both in infinite - dimensional and finite - dimensional systems . based on weyl s approach , generalized by schwinger </S>",
    "<S> , a self - consistent theoretical framework describing physical systems characterised by a finite - dimensional space of states has been created . the used mathematical formalism is further developed by adding finite - dimensional versions of some notions and results from the continuous case . </S>",
    "<S> discrete versions of the continuous gaussian functions have been defined by using the jacobi theta functions . </S>",
    "<S> we continue the investigation of the properties of these finite gaussians by following the analogy with the continuous case . </S>",
    "<S> we study the uncertainty relation of finite gaussian states , the form of the associated wigner quasi - distribution and the evolution under free - particle and quantum harmonic oscillator hamiltonians . in all cases , </S>",
    "<S> a particular emphasis is put on the recovery of the known continuous - limit results when the dimension @xmath0 of the system increases . </S>"
