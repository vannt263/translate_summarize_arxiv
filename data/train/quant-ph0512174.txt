    "<S> we describe how to implement quantum logic operations in a silicon - based quantum computer with phosphorus atoms serving as qubits . </S>",
    "<S> the information is stored in the states of nuclear spins and the conditional logic operations are implemented through the electron spins using nuclear - electron hyperfine and electron - electron exchange interactions . </S>",
    "<S> the electrons in our computer should stay coherent only during implementation of one control - not gate . </S>",
    "<S> the exchange interaction is constant and selective excitations are provided by a magnetic field gradient . </S>",
    "<S> the quantum logic operations are implemented by rectangular radio - frequency pulses . </S>",
    "<S> this architecture is scalable and does not require manufacturing nanoscale electronic gates . as shown in this paper parameters of a quantum protocol can be derived analytically even for a computer with a large number of qubits using our perturbation approach . </S>",
    "<S> we present the protocol for initialization of the nuclear spins and the protocol for creation of entanglement . </S>",
    "<S> all analytical results are tested numerically using a two - qubit system . </S>"
