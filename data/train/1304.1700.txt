    "<S> we study charge ordering driven by coulomb interactions on triangular lattices relevant to the wigner - mott transition in two dimensions . </S>",
    "<S> dynamical mean - field theory reveals the pinball liquid phase , a charge ordered metallic phase containing quasi - localized ( pins ) coexisting with itinerant ( balls ) electrons . </S>",
    "<S> based on an effective periodic anderson model for this phase , we find an antiferromagnetic kondo coupling between pins and balls and strong quasiparticle renormalization . </S>",
    "<S> non - fermi liquid behavior can occur in such charge ordered systems due to spin - flip scattering of itinerant electrons off the pins in analogy with heavy fermion compounds .    </S>",
    "<S> the interplay between charge ordering ( co ) and the mott metal - insulator transition ( mit ) has common manifestations in narrow - band systems such as the transition metal oxides @xcite ( cuprates , manganites , nickelates , cobaltates ) , low - dimensional organic conductors @xcite as well as transition - metal dichalcogenides @xcite . on one hand , </S>",
    "<S> charge ordering phenomena are favored by the presence of electronic correlations , as these slow down the electronic motion and reduce the tendency to charge delocalization . </S>",
    "<S> conversely , the reconstruction of the electronic spectrum upon entering a phase with broken symmetry can enhance the effects of correlations , extending the realm of mott physics away from the usual integer fillings @xcite . a fundamental relation between charge ordering and the mott mit </S>",
    "<S> has also been pointed out recently in the two - dimensional electron gas ( 2deg ) , leading to the concept of the wigner - mott transition @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> close to the wigner crystal phase induced by the long ranged coulomb repulsion at low density , the electrons form an effectively narrow - band system at half - filling ( one electron per site of the wigner crystal @xcite ) , whose physical properties resemble those of a mott insulator . correspondingly , it has been shown that the wigner crystal melting shares several common features with the mott transition @xcite .    in the present letter </S>",
    "<S> , we explore the interplay of charge order and electronic correlations starting from the extended hubbard model on the triangular lattice , which frustrates the insulating co found in bipartite lattices . </S>",
    "<S> we focus on the _ pinball liquid _ ( pl ) phase @xcite , i.e. a charge ordered metallic phase where quasi - localized electrons ( pins ) coexist with itinerant electrons ( balls ) over a broad range of concentrations . applying dynamical mean field theory ( dmft ) </S>",
    "<S> we find that the electron dynamics become heavily renormalized for sufficiently large carrier concentration . </S>",
    "<S> the origin of this renormalization is elucidated via a mapping to a periodic anderson model ( pam ) . </S>",
    "<S> the strong analogies with heavy fermions @xcite indicate that non - fermi liquid behavior can arise in correlated co systems on frustrated lattices in a broad temperature range above the kondo breakdown temperature scale .    </S>",
