    "<S> the grbner walk is an algorithm for conversion between grbner bases for different term orders . </S>",
    "<S> it is based on the polyhedral geometry of the grbner fan and involves tracking a line between cones representing the initial and target term order . </S>",
    "<S> an important parameter is explicit numerical perturbation of this line . </S>",
    "<S> this usually involves both time and space demanding arithmetic of integers much larger than the input numbers . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we show how the explicit line may be replaced by a formal line using robbiano s characterization of group orders on @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> this gives rise to the generic grbner walk involving only grbner basis conversion over facets and computations with marked polynomials . </S>",
    "<S> the infinite precision integer arithmetic is replaced by term order comparisons between ( small ) integral vectors . </S>",
    "<S> this makes it possible to compute with infinitesimal numbers and perturbations in a consistent way without introducing unnecessary long integers . </S>",
    "<S> the proposed technique is closely related to the lexicographic ( symbolic ) perturbation method used in optimization and computational geometry . </S>",
    "<S> we report on computations with toric ideals , where a version of our algorithm in certain cases computes test sets for hard integer knapsack problems significantly faster than the buchberger algorithm . </S>"
