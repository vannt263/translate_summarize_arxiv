    "<S> we propose an all - electrical experimental setup to detect and manipulate the amplitude of odd - frequency pairing in a double quantum dot . </S>",
    "<S> odd - frequency pair amplitude is induced from the breakdown of orbital symmetry when cooper pairs are injected in the double dot with electrons in different dots . when the dot levels are aligned with the fermi energy , i.e. , on resonance , nonlocal andreev processes are directly connected to the presence of odd - frequency pairing . </S>",
    "<S> therefore , their amplitude can be manipulated by tuning the level positions . </S>",
    "<S> detection of nonlocal andreev processes by conductance measurements contributes a direct proof of the existence of odd - frequency pair amplitude and is available using current experimental techniques .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction. _ the symmetry analysis of cooper pairs is a key element in the study of superconductivity . for example </S>",
    "<S> , cooper pairs at conventional bcs superconductors form a spin - singlet even - parity state , where the electrons have opposite spins and are coupled in momentum space by the isotropic @xmath0-wave channel . </S>",
    "<S> a current trend in the study of superconductivity is to _ engineer _ unconventional superconductors by breaking down symmetries of a bcs superconductor . </S>",
    "<S> consequently , a new type of pairing emerges which is odd in frequency , i.e. , odd under an exchange of the time coordinates @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> plenty of theoretical studies suggest ubiquitous presence of odd - frequency superconductivity at inhomogeneous superconducting systems @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> unfortunately , experimental evidence for odd - frequency pair amplitude is very limited . </S>",
    "<S> odd - frequency spin - triplet @xmath0-wave superconductivity can explain the long - range proximity effect @xcite , the intrinsic paramagnetic meissner effect @xcite , and the subgap structure @xcite observed in ferromagnet - superconductor hybrids . </S>",
    "<S> however , odd - frequency pairs are mixed with conventional ones and their amplitude is not tunable but accidentally determined by the configuration of magnetic moments realized at the junction . to unambiguously establish the presence of odd - frequency pairing , new proposals that filter odd - frequency pairs and allow to control their amplitude </S>",
