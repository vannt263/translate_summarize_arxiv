    "<S> we describe a scalable experimental protocol for estimating the average error of individual quantum computational gates . </S>",
    "<S> this protocol consists of interleaving random clifford gates between the gate of interest and provides an estimate as well as theoretical bounds for the average error of the gate under test , so long as the average noise variation over all clifford gates is small . </S>",
    "<S> this technique takes into account both state preparation and measurement errors and is scalable in the number of qubits . </S>",
    "<S> we apply this protocol to a superconducting qubit system and find a bounded average error of @xmath0 $ ] for the single - qubit gates @xmath1 and @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> these bounded values provide better estimates of the average error than those extracted via quantum process tomography .    </S>",
    "<S> determining how well an operation is implemented on a quantum device is of fundamental importance in quantum information theory . </S>",
    "<S> such a characterization allows a direct comparison between different architectures for computation as well as an understanding of the performance of the building blocks of a quantum computer . </S>",
    "<S> the standard method for characterizing a quantum operation is quantum process tomography ( qpt ) @xcite which is subject to two significant drawbacks : first , it is not scalable in the number of sub - systems ( qubits ) comprising the system ; and second if state - preparation and measurement ( spam ) errors are present , then these errors will contribute to those of the gate being characterized , hence giving an unfaithful estimation of the actual error . in many cases , one does not require the complete knowledge that qpt aims to provide . as a result , various methods for partially characterizing a quantum gate have been developed @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> ideally such a method should be scalable in the number of qubits , @xmath3 , comprising the system as well as provide a faithful measure of the noise that is independent of spam errors .    </S>",
    "<S> one particular method for partial noise characterization is  randomized benchmarking \" ( rb ) @xcite , with ref </S>",
    "<S> .  @xcite providing the first scalable rb protocol that satisfies all of the above criteria . </S>",
    "<S> the general idea of rb is to implement random sequences of gates that compose to the identity operation , and measure the fidelity of each sequence . averaging over different realizations results in a fidelity decay versus the sequence length , from which the average error over the full gate set is estimated via fitting the curve to a derived model . </S>",
