    "<S> disordered and frustrated graphical systems are ubiquitous in physics , biology , and information science . for models on complete graphs or random graphs , deep understanding has been achieved through the mean - field replica and cavity methods . </S>",
    "<S> but finite - dimensional ` real ' systems persist to be very challenging because of the abundance of short loops and strong local correlations . </S>",
    "<S> a statistical mechanics theory is constructed in this paper for finite - dimensional models based on the mathematical framework of partition function expansion and the concept of region - graphs . </S>",
    "<S> rigorous expressions for the free energy and grand free energy are derived . </S>",
    "<S> message - passing equations on the region - graph , such as belief - propagation and survey - propagation , are also derived rigorously .    </S>",
    "<S> graphical models are used to describe systems composed of elements which lack translational degrees of freedom but have changeable internal states . </S>",
    "<S> disordered and frustrated graphical models are ubiquitous in physics ( spin glasses ) , biology ( gene regulation and neuron networks ) , and information science ( error - correcting codes , constraint satisfaction problems ) . revealing and understanding the rich and fascinating properties of these systems has been a major field of statistical physics for many years @xcite . on the theoretical side , a deep understanding of the equilibrium behaviors of models defined on complete graphs ( e.g. , the sherrington - kirkpatrick model ) or </S>",
    "<S> finite - connectivity random graphs ( e.g. , the viana - bray model ) has been achieved by the mean - field replica and cavity methods @xcite .    due to the finite - dimensional nature , real - world graphical model systems ( e.g. , the edwards - anderson ( ea ) model @xcite ) usually contain extremely many short loops with strong interaction strengths , which cause strong local correlations within groups of elements . </S>",
    "<S> the abundance of short loops makes theoretical study of finite - dimensional graphical models very difficult , and so far progress has mainly been achieved through extensive numerical simulations ( see , e.g. , @xcite and references therein ) . </S>",
    "<S> the high - temperature equilibrium properties of finite - dimensional models can be studied by the cluster variation method @xcite and its recent extension , the region - graph method @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> but for the most interesting low - temperature regime , a powerful and general theoretical framework is still lacking . </S>",
    "<S> recently , rizzo and co - workers @xcite made an initial step in combining the cluster variation method with the replica method to study the low - temperature behavior of the ea model , but several technical difficulties of this approach still need to be remedied ( such as the issue of non - positive - definite message functionals ) .    </S>",
    "<S> in this letter , a statistical mechanics theory for finite - dimensional graphical models is rigorously constructed based on the mathematical framework of partition function expansion @xcite and the region - graph concept of yedidia and co - workers @xcite . in this theoretical framework , </S>",
    "<S> the sets of elements that form local loops are regarded as regions , and the possible strong local correlations within the regions are explicitly taken into account . </S>",
    "<S> free energy expressions and region - graph message - passing equations at different hierarchies of the free energy landscape are derived without using any approximations . </S>",
    "<S> the theoretical framework directly corresponds to a distributed algorithm for single graphical systems . </S>",
    "<S> this work may find broad applications in finite - dimensional spin - glass systems and in real - world information systems ( such as image processing ) .    </S>",
