    "nonlocally coupled oscillator systems can exhibit a remarkable class of patterns called _ chimera _ , in which identical oscillators separate sharply into two domains , one coherent and phase locked , the other incoherent and drifting  @xcite .",
    "these two groups of the oscillators together maintain stable organized patterns in the system .",
    "the existence of such patterns was first noticed and explained in a ring of phase oscillators  @xcite , and studied in further detail  @xcite .",
    "furthermore , it was found that the chimera states also appear in rotating spiral waves in two - dimensional spatially extended systems  @xcite .",
    "recently , an interesting pattern similar to the chimera states studied in refs .",
    "@xcite was also found in nonlocally coupled hodgkin - huxley equations with excitatory and inhibitory synaptic coupling , where such patterns spontaneously appears as a result of the instability of the uniform state , in contrast to the original chimera that appears when the uniform state is stable  @xcite .    in this paper ,",
    "we present another simple example of the chimera state associated with the ising walls in one - dimensional spatially extended systems , which is exhibited by a _ nonlocally _ coupled complex ginzburg - landau equation ( cgle ) _ with _ a parametric forcing .",
    "recently , a _ nonlocally _ coupled cgle _ without _ a forcing has been studied intensively  @xcite , and a _ locally _ coupled cgle _ with _ a forcing has also been investigated widely  @xcite . to our knowledge , battogtokh considered the _ nonlocally _ coupled cgle _ with _ the forcing for the first time , and demonstrated various interesting phenomena , e.g. , nonequilibrium ising - bloch transitions  @xcite . here , we will focus only on the chimera ising wall , which is the simplest one among them , but its theoretical analysis has not yet been carried out .",

    "<S> nonlocally coupled oscillator systems can exhibit an exotic spatiotemporal structure called _ chimera _ , where the system splits into two groups of oscillators with sharp boundaries , one of which is phase - locked and the other is phase - randomized . </S>",
    "<S> two examples of the chimera states are known : the first one appears in a ring of phase oscillators , and the second one is associated with the two - dimensional rotating spiral waves . in this article , we report yet another example of the chimera state that is associated with the so - called ising walls in one - dimensional spatially extended systems , which is exhibited by a nonlocally coupled complex ginzburg - landau equation with external forcing . </S>"
