    "<S> we study a complex plasma under microgravity conditions that is first stabilized with an oscillating electric field . </S>",
    "<S> once the stabilization is stopped , the so - called heartbeat instability develops . </S>",
    "<S> we study how the kinetic energy spectrum changes during and after the onset of the instability and compare with the double cascade predicted by kraichnan and leith for two - dimensional turbulence . </S>",
    "<S> the onset of the instability manifests clearly in the ratio of the reduced rates of cascade of energy and enstrophy and in the power - law exponents of the energy spectra .    [ </S>",
    "<S> [ introduction ] ] introduction + + + + + + + + + + + +    turbulence is often cited as one of the main challenges of modern theoretical physics @xcite , despite being a long - standing subject of study . </S>",
    "<S> many turbulent systems are more complex than navier - stokes flow in a fluid - involving , for instance , complicated ways of energy injection , transfer and dissipation @xcite . turbulent pulsations or its analogues occur in systems as diverse as soap films @xcite , insect flight @xcite , lattices of anharmonic oscillators @xcite , bose - einstein condensates @xcite , and microswimmers @xcite . the development or , inversely , the decay of turbulence is of special interest and can be studied , for instance , in flames @xcite or behind grids @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> in general , when energy is put into a two - dimensional turbulent system , the energy spectrum splits into the inverse energy and direct enstrophy ranges  </S>",
    "<S> the so - called double cascade develops @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> its presence has been supported by computer simulations , but not unambiguously @xcite . only a few numerical simulations @xcite and experiments @xcite were able to simultaneously observe both cascades , which is challenging due to the large range of scales necessary to cover both the inverse and direct cascade @xcite . recently , it was suggested that the inverse cascade might not be robust , but depend on friction @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the evolution of the spectrum during the onset of two - dimensional forced turbulence was investigated in @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> a recent topic of interest is the transition of weak wave turbulence to wave turbulence with intermittent collapses @xcite and intermittency , i.e. strong non - gaussian velocity fluctuations , in wave turbulence @xcite , and so - called janus spectra which differ in streamwise and transverse direction @xcite .    in this paper , we present the first study of developing turbulence in dusty / complex plasmas . </S>",
    "<S> complex plasmas consist of microparticles embedded in a low - temperature plasma . </S>",
    "<S> the microparticles acquire high charges and interact with each other . </S>",
    "<S> they can be visualized individually and thus enable observations on the kinetic level of , for instance , vortices @xcite , tunneling @xcite , and channeling @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> gravity is a major force acting on the microparticles in ground - based experiments . under its influence , </S>",
    "<S> the particles are located close to the sheath region of the plasma , where strong electric fields compensate for gravity , and strong ion fluxes are present . </S>",
    "<S> it is desirable to perform experiments with microparticles under microgravity conditions . </S>",
    "<S> then , the microparticles are suspended in the more homogeneous plasma bulk with a weak electric field @xcite , and the strong ion flux effects such as wake formation @xcite are avoided . </S>",
    "<S> the data presented in this paper were measured using the pk-3 plus laboratory @xcite in the microgravity environment on board the international space station . </S>",
    "<S> here , large , symmetric microparticle clouds form in the plasma bulk which typically contain a small central , particle - free void caused by the interplay between the ion drag and electric forces acting on the microparticles @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> several authors have recently begun to study turbulence in complex plasmas @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> using complex plasmas to study turbulence has the advantage that the particles that carry the interaction can be visualized individually , in contrast to traditional experiments on turbulence @xcite , in which the use of tracer particles might not be reliable @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> turbulence in complex plasmas occurs at low reynolds numbers , which is common in viscoelastic fluids @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , complex plasmas are usually highly dissipative . </S>",
    "<S> therefore , it is advantageous to study forced turbulence . </S>",
    "<S> a good mechanism to induce turbulent pulsations is the self - sustained heartbeat oscillation @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this type of instability is characterized by a regularly pulsating ( auto - oscillating ) microparticle cloud @xcite . the heartbeat - induced auto - oscillations pump energy into the microparticle cloud and are able to induce turbulence and effectively channel the flow in two dimensions @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in the experiment presented in this letter , we use a developing heartbeat instability and auto - oscillations to study the onset of turbulence and , specifically , the development of the kinetic energy spectrum . </S>",
    "<S> in addition to the channeling due to the heartbeat , we limit our analysis to particles that move within a plane for 0.2  s or longer , thereby excluding particles with a significant transverse velocity component .    </S>",

    "<S> [ [ energy - spectra ] ] energy spectra + + + + + + + + + + + + + +    next , we are going to explore the evolution of the energy spectra of the microparticles . to calculate the spectra </S>",
    "<S> , we follow the method presented in @xcite : we calculate velocity maps , i.e. , the average horizontal or vertical velocity of the particles as a function of position . </S>",
    "<S> the energy is then calculated from the squares of the fourier transformed velocity maps by associating every energy value with the corresponding wave vector modulus @xmath21 . </S>",
    "<S> as we are interested in the transition process , we calculate the velocity maps averaging over a number of frames . </S>",
    "<S> a small number of frames is desirable to increase the resolution during the transition , but introduces the problem of `` holes '' in the velocity maps . </S>",
    "<S> these holes are positions in space for which no average velocity data is available , as no particles were present or detected at these positions in the frame range used to calculate the map . </S>",
    "<S> the presence of the holes / gaps in the data can potentially have a significant influence on calculated energy spectra @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> the size and number of holes depends on the radius over which mean velocities are calculated and on the number of frames involved in averaging . </S>",
    "<S> we test the influence on the spectra by first calculating the spectrum from a map without holes . </S>",
    "<S> then , we artificially remove the data at the position of the holes in a different velocity map and recalculate the energy spectrum . </S>",
    "<S> we repeat this method using various averaging radii and frame ranges to find the optimal parameters . according to this analysis , we determine an averaging radius of 5  pixels and a sliding window of 5  s width[multiblock footnote omitted ] as optimal choice to calculate velocity maps . </S>",
