    "in this paper , we calculate the damping at finite temperatures of collisionless condensate collective modes due to interactions with a static thermal cloud .",
    "our treatment starts with the general theory developed in ref .",
    "@xcite and is an extension of the static hartree - fock - bogoliubov popov ( hfb - popov ) approximation  @xcite to include the effect of the lack of diffusive equilibrium between the condensate and non - condensate .",
    "the static thermal cloud acts as a reservoir that can exchange particles with the condensate so as to bring the condensate back into equilibrium , resulting in a damping of the collective oscillations of the condensate .",
    "we do not expect , in general , that the collisional damping we find from a static thermal cloud will be much modified when we treat the non - condensate dynamically . because we are treating the non - condensate statically , landau and beliaev damping",
    "are not contained within in our description .",
    "our present work is the natural extension of a recent study  @xcite of the damping of collisionless condensate collective modes at finite temperatures .",
    "this was based on a simplified model treating the time - dependent gross - pitaevskii equation for the condensate within the thomas - fermi approximation ( tfa ) .",
    "we described the damping in terms of the density @xmath1 and velocity @xmath2 fluctuations of the condensate oscillations . at @xmath3 , where the non - condensate component is absent , stringari  @xcite showed that a simple analytic solution of the condensate collective modes could be obtained within the thomas - fermi approximation . in ref .",
    "@xcite , we formally generalized these `` stringari '' solutions to finite temperatures by assuming that the mean field of the static thermal cloud has a negligible effect on the condensate oscillations .",
    "we then calculated the damping due to the lack of equilibrium between the condensate and non - condensate to first order in perturbation theory , using the stringari modes as a zeroth - order solution . in ref .",
    "@xcite we compared this damping directly to landau damping and found it gives a @xmath4 to @xmath5 correction to the total damping for typical experimental parameters .    the main focus of the present paper is to generalize our earlier work  @xcite to include the same inter - component collisional damping mechanism directly in the bogoliubov equations , still treating the non - condensate statically .",

    "<S> we calculate the damping of condensate collective excitations at finite temperatures arising from the lack of equilibrium between the condensate and thermal atoms . since a static thermal cloud already produces damping in our model , we ignore the non - condensate dynamics . </S>",
    "<S> we derive a set of generalized bogoliubov equations for finite temperatures that contain an explicit damping term due to collisional exchange of atoms between the two components . </S>",
    "<S> we have numerically solved these bogoliubov equations to obtain the temperature dependence of the damping of the condensate modes in a harmonic trap . </S>",
    "<S> we compare these results with our recent work based on the thomas - fermi approximation .    </S>"
