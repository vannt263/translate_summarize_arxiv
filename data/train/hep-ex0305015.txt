    "the statistical error entering the @xmath77 function is taken from the model prediction .",
    "this approach is completely equivalent to using the log - likelihood function , and it has been verified that both methods lead to the same results .",
    "each data point gets a varying weight depending on the model parameter , but independent of fluctuations in the data . for the present data this leads to more symmetric situations and an unbiased evaluation . in general , the resulting @xmath77 curves show a stronger sensitivity to details of the ci model , e.g. interference patterns , as can be seen in figure  [ clcurves ] .",
    "the widths of the distributions are often , but not always , wider than in the previous case . the problem to extract limits from these distributions persists .",
    "the final limits presented here have been determined by applying a frequentist approach .",
    "starting from a specific model with a scale parameter @xmath188 the cross section @xmath74 is calculated and then smeared according to the statistical error given by the predicted number of events .",
    "distortions due to all uncorrelated and correlated systematic uncertainties ( except for the parton distributions ) are included , assuming gaussian behaviour of the errors .",
    "the monte carlo experiment is then analysed in the same way as the data , i.e. with statistical errors from the prediction and including all systematics , resulting in a fitted value @xmath189 .",
    "this procedure is repeated numerous times and the fit results are recorded in a probability distribution .",
    "the scale parameter is then varied and the 95% confidence lower limit @xmath190 ( @xmath191 ) is defined as that value @xmath188 where 95% of the monte carlo experiments produce values of @xmath189 which are larger ( smaller ) than the parameter @xmath9 actually obtained from the data .",
