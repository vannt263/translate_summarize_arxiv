    "<S> the magnetic rare earth ions of the spin - ice compounds like dy@xmath1ti@xmath1o@xmath2form a lattice of corner - sharing tetrahedra . the strong influence of crystal fields forces the magnetic moments to point along the axis connecting the centers of adjacent tetrahedra giving rise to a description in terms of effective ising spins . in zero magnetic field , </S>",
    "<S> the ground state is characterized by a 2-in/2-out spin state configuration , i.e. , there are two spins for each tetrahedron that point inwards and two that point outwards . the ground state , however , is not unique as there is a multitude of possible realizations of this 2-in/2-out arrangement giving rise to a finite zero - point entropy </S>",
    "<S> . given the resemblance to the proton configurations of water - ice , this peculiar magnetism is known as spin - ice @xcite . flipping a single </S>",
    "<S> spin breaks the 2-in/2-out ice rule for two adjacent tetrahedra : the resulting magnetic dipole excitation decomposes into an emergent magnetic monopole and anti - monopole pair that interact via a long - range magnetic coulomb law @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> spin - ice thus offers a rare example of fractionalized excitations in a three - dimensional material .    </S>",
    "<S> recently , it has been pointed out by d. khomskii @xcite that each magnetic monopole is also accompanied by an electric dipole . </S>",
    "<S> the presence of a magnetic monopole on a tetrahedron results in an electric dipole moment either due to a spontaneous charge redistribution or due to a magnetoelastic coupling . </S>",
    "<S> this dipole moment always points along the corner of the tetrahedron that is occupied by the spin which is singled out by the 1-in/3-out or the 1-out/3-in configuration , see fig .  </S>",

    "<S> the dynamics of monopoles within the material thus leads to characteristic fluctuations of the electric polarization that , in principle , can be investigated with the help of the dielectric function @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> this is remarkable as it would provide means to probe explicitly the magnetic monopole excitations in spin - ice . whereas the magnetic response @xmath5 is sensitive to both , magnetic monopoles that break the ice rules as well as collective spin - fluctuations that leave them intact , the magnetic part of the dielectric response is mainly susceptible to the former . </S>",
