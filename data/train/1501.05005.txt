    "<S> we consider the evolution of variance of entropy ( varentropy ) in the course of a polar transform operation on binary data elements ( bdes ) . </S>",
    "<S> a bde is a pair @xmath0 consisting of a binary random variable @xmath1 and an arbitrary side information random variable @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> the varentropy of @xmath0 is defined as the variance of the random variable @xmath3 . </S>",
    "<S> a polar transform of order two is a certain mapping that takes two independent bdes and produces two new bdes that are correlated with each other . </S>",
    "<S> it is shown that the sum of the varentropies at the output of the polar transform is less than or equal to the sum of the varentropies at the input , with equality if and only if at least one of the inputs has zero varentropy . </S>",
    "<S> this result is extended to polar transforms of higher orders and it is shown that the varentropy decreases to zero asymptotically when the bdes at the input are independent and identically distributed .    polar coding , varentropy , dispersion . </S>"
