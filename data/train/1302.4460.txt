    "<S> we theoretically predict a non - equilibrium phase transition in quantum spin systems induced by laser , which provides a purely quantum - mechanical way for a coherent control of magnetization . </S>",
    "<S> namely , when a circularly polarized laser is applied to a spin system , the magnetic component of laser induces a magnetization normal to the plane of polarization , leading to an ultrafast phase transition . </S>",
    "<S> we first demonstrate this numerically for an @xmath0 antiferromagnetic heisenberg spin chain , where the topologically - ordered haldane state gives way to a new state with an emerging magnetization . </S>",
    "<S> we then elucidate its physical mechanism by applying a many - body floquet picture . </S>",
    "<S> the theory also indicates that the phenomenon should occur in general quantum spin systems with a magnetic anisotropy . </S>",
    "<S> the required laser frequency is in the thz range , and the intensity within a prospective experimental feasibility .    </S>",
    "<S> there is a growing fascination with physics of non - equilibrium systems , which is becoming an important topic in condensed - matter and other fields of physics . for electron systems , a host of novel phenomena induced in non - equilibrium situations , such as photo - induced mott transitions  @xcite </S>",
    "<S> , photo - induced topological transitions  @xcite , etc , have been fathomed . </S>",
    "<S> it is a hot topic in cold atoms as well , where quantum simulation is being realized  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> now we pose a question : `` can we propose novel non - equilibrium phenomena for quantum spin systems ? '' </S>",
    "<S> spin systems , a basis for the physics of magnets , are many - body in nature and have various applications in engineering and , more recently , in spintronics . </S>",
    "<S> this paper proposes a novel , non - equilibrium way to _ </S>",
    "<S> coherently _ control many - body states in _ spin systems _ with laser . </S>",
    "<S> this is in contrast to the control of single quantum states , e.g. , qubits , which is becoming important in the field of quantum computation  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> instead , we want to develop a method to control the collective phenomena , e.g. , phase transitions , with laser .    </S>",
    "<S> interaction between matter and laser , the most powerful tool for coherent control , has been mainly used in electron and atomic systems . </S>",
    "<S> so the first question one might ask is `` can spin systems be manipulated by laser as well ? '' the second question , no less important , is `` can it be done coherently on a many - body state ? '' </S>",
    "<S> control of spin systems by lasers is a hot topic due to recent experimental advances  @xcite . _ </S>",
    "<S> the key of our study is to use the magnetic component of circularly polarized lasers with photon energy far below the electron energy scale . </S>",
    "<S> _ it was demonstrated recently that magnetic fields from lasers in the thz regime can directly access to spin dynamics without disturbing the charge degrees of freedom  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the merit is that incoherent processes resulting from charge excitations can be prevented . while the setup of ref </S>",
    "<S> .   was within the linear response regime , here we extend the avenue to the non - perturbative regime by proposing that one can realize a  </S>",
    "<S> laser - induced phase transition \" with perfect quantum coherence . </S>",
    "<S> we show that , with circularly polarized laser , we can induce net magnetization in a quantum antiferromagnet . </S>",
    "<S> first , it is demonstrated numerically with the infinite time - evolving block decimation ( itebd )  @xcite for one - dimensional spin chains . </S>",
    "<S> the motion of the induced magnetization can be controlled as desired by shaping the laser pulse , hence provides a new technique for coherent spin manipulation .    </S>",
    "<S> the dynamical phase transition requires a theoretical treatment which goes beyond the linear response theory . </S>",
    "<S> floquet theory is becoming a standard theoretical picture for studying quantum systems under periodic driving  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> namely , the time - periodicity enables us to cast a time dependent problem into a static effective model governed by the floquet hamiltonian . </S>",
    "<S> this has proved to be a powerful method in the theory of  floquet topological insulators \"  @xcite , and has also been applied to many - body problems such as the photo - induced mott transition  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> we usually have to deal with a hamiltonian in an infinite - dimensional floquet matrix form as a penalty for going from the time - dependent problem to a time - independent one . </S>",
    "<S> we find here , however , a dramatic reduction of the dimension for the floquet hamiltonian due to a symmetry by applying the many - body floquet method to spin systems in circularly polarized laser . the reduced model , which we call the `` irreducible floquet model '' , turns out to be a spin system with a static slanted magnetic field . </S>",
    "<S> the emergence of the laser - induced magnetization can be understood with this static model , which we confirm by comparing exact diagonalization results with itebd . since the above discussion is applicable also for more than one dimension </S>",
    "<S> , the induction of magnetization is independent of the dimension .    </S>",
    "<S> while the magnetization can be induced in any dimensions , one - dimensional antiferromagnet has , quantum mechanically , a special interest . </S>",
    "<S> the system is known to be gapless if the size of each spin , @xmath1 , is a half - odd - integer , and gapped if @xmath1 is an integer . </S>",
    "<S> this is the celebrated haldane s conjecture  @xcite , now established by intensive analytic , numerical , and experimental studies . </S>",
    "<S> specifically , the ground state ( gs ) of @xmath0 spin chain , known as the haldane phase , is topologically protected by a symmetry  @xcite , and is characterized by a `` string order parameter . ''  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite this enables us to demonstrate that the laser - induced non - equilibrium phenomenon we propose here is indeed a phase transition from a state having a topological order into a state with finite magnetization which replaces the topological order . as a further indication that the present transition is genuinely a non - equilibrium process , </S>",
    "<S> we show that the necessary strength of the magnetic field can be smaller than the static excitation ( haldane ) gap , which is not the case with conventional magnetization processes in static magnetic fields .    </S>",
