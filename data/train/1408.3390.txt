    "the discovery of supermassive black holes at the center of galaxies , specially the one corresponding to sgra * in the milky way @xcite , has led to a growing interest in the study of strong deflection gravitational lensing . within this context ,",
    "an important feature is that the observation of optical effects due to the supermassive galactic black hole , including direct imaging , seems to be possible in the near future @xcite .",
    "an astrophysical object with a photon sphere makes light rays passing close to it to have a large deviation , resulting in two infinite sets of the denominated relativistic images @xcite . in this case",
    ", an analytical treatment can be performed by using the strong deflection limit , which was introduced for the schwarzschild geometry @xcite , extended to the reissner ",
    "nordstrm spacetime @xcite , and to any spherically symmetric object @xcite . using this method , which consists in a logarithmic approximation of the deflection angle for light rays deflecting close to the photon sphere ,",
    "it is possible to obtain the positions , the magnifications , and the time delays of the relativistic images .",
    "many works considering strong deflection lenses with spherical symmetry , most of them analytical and the others numerical , can be found in the literature @xcite .",
    "the lensing effects of rotating black holes were also analyzed in several articles @xcite ; a related interesting aspect is that the apparent shapes ( or shadows ) of rotating black holes have a deformation produced by the spin @xcite . for recent reviews about strong deflection lensing",
