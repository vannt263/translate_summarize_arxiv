    "<S> we explain how and why all thermodynamic properties of spin systems can be computed in one and two dimensions in the whole range of temperatures overcoming the divergence towards zero temperature of the standard high temperature series expansions ( hte ) . </S>",
    "<S> the method relies on an approximation of the entropy versus energy ( microcanonical potential function ) on the whole range of energies . </S>",
    "<S> the success is related to the intrinsic physical constraints on the entropy function and a careful treatment of the boundary behaviors . </S>",
    "<S> this method is benchmarked against two one - dimensional solvable models : the ising model in longitudinal field and the xy model in a transverse field . with ten terms in the hte </S>",
    "<S> , we find a spin susceptibility within a few % of the exact results in the whole range of temperature . </S>",

    "<S> recent years have seen an outburst of magnetic materials that might be realistic candidates for the long searched spin liquids @xcite . in this quickly maturing field , it is now highly desirable to compare the experimental properties of these new states of matter with theory . </S>",
    "<S> modelization of the magnetic interactions in a mott insulator is a challenge . </S>",

    "<S> in fact hte diverge at low temperature and increasing the length of the series and/or using pad approximants do not help much to reach useful information at temperatures lower than the main interaction . </S>",
    "<S> but , as was noticed at the early years of this quest @xcite , the interesting physics in frustrated systems appear in a range of temperatures at least an order of magnitude lower than the main coupling , and specially for competing interactions where the temperature range available with the raw hte is quite insufficient .    </S>",
    "<S> many mathematical methods have been tried to reach low temperature properties of magnets . </S>",
