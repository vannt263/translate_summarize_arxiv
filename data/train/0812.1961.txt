    "<S> after proper rescaling and under some technical assumptions , the smallest eigenvalue of a sample covariance matrix with aspect ratio bounded away from @xmath0 converges to the tracy  widom distribution . </S>",
    "<S> this complements the results on the largest eigenvalue , due to soshnikov and pch .    </S>",
    "<S> it has been long conjectured that some of the asymptotic statistical properties that are known for eigenvalues of large matrices with gaussian entries should be valid , in particular , for more general random matrices with independent entries . </S>",
    "<S> this is part of a phenomenon called ` universality ' in the physical literature ; see for example conjecture  1.2.1 , conjecture  1.2.2 , and various remarks scattered in mehta s book @xcite </S>",
    "<S> .    in particular , the local statistics of the eigenvalues at the edge of the spectrum should be the same as in the gaussian case ( precise definitions are provided below . )    the first rigorous results of this kind are due to soshnikov . in @xcite , he established a universality result at the edge for large hermitian matrices with independent entries ; we formulate his result as theorem  [ th : herm ] below . </S>",
    "<S> universality at the edge for hermitian random matrices with independent entries was further studied by ruzmaikina @xcite and khorunzhiy and vengerovsky @xcite .    in the subsequent work @xcite , </S>",
    "<S> soshnikov extended his method to the largest eigenvalues of the sample covariance matrices @xmath1 , under some restrictions on the dimensions of the matrices @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> these restrictions were later disposed of by pch @xcite ; see theorem  [ th : covm ] below .    in the hermitian case , the largest and the smallest eigenvalues are identically distributed ; soshnikov s result encompasses both the largest and the smallest eigenvalue . </S>",
    "<S> the state of affairs is different for sample covariance matrices , the smallest eigenvalue of which is much smaller in absolute value than the largest one . </S>",
    "<S> therefore soshnikov s approach does not seem to be applicable to the smallest eigenvalue of a sample covariance matrix ; we discuss this further below .    in this paper , we suggest a different approach , and apply it to prove a universality result for the smallest eigenvalue of a sample covariance matrix ; see theorem  [ th : cov1 ] below . </S>",
    "<S> we also apply it to give another proof of the results of soshnikov and pch , theorems  [ th : herm ] and [ th : covm ] .    in the special case of gaussian matrices , </S>",
    "<S> alternative approaches are available , and most of the results are known . </S>",
    "<S> the asymptotic distribution of the extreme eigenvalues of gaussian hermitian matrices has been first studied by bronk @xcite in the 1960-s , and more recently by bowick and brzin , moore , forrester , and finally by tracy and widom , who have established the conclusion of theorem  [ th : herm ] in the gaussian case . </S>",
    "<S> parallel results for gaussian sample covariance matrices have been proved by johansson , johnstone , and soshnikov , and borodin and forrester . </S>",
    "<S> we defer the precise references to section  [ s : rem ] .    </S>",
    "<S> in fact , our argument ( as well as those of soshnikov and pch ) involves reduction to the gaussian case . </S>",
    "<S> we discuss this in detail in section  [ s : rem ] . </S>"
