    "the analytical and numerical results presented here demonstrate that it is possible to eliminate leakage in physical qubits by using a second quadrature control approximately equal to the derivative of the first .",
    "the predicted single - qubit error rates are extremely low , even when we include decoherence . while in this paper this procedure is only applied to leakage , it will also find application to other systems , for example turning off unwanted coupling in multi - qubit and qubit - oscillator systems for single and two qubit operations .",

    "<S> in realizations of quantum computing , a two - level system ( qubit ) is often singled out from the many levels of an anharmonic oscillator . in these cases , </S>",
    "<S> simple qubit control fails on short time scales because of coupling to leakage levels . </S>",
    "<S> we provide an easy to implement analytic formula that inhibits this leakage from any single - control analog or pixelated pulse . </S>",
    "<S> it is based on adding a second control that is proportional to the time - derivative of the first . for realistic parameters of superconducting qubits </S>",
    "<S> , this strategy reduces the error by an order of magnitude relative to the state of the art , all based on smooth and feasible pulse shapes . </S>",
    "<S> these results show that even weak anharmonicity is sufficient and in general not a limiting factor for implementing quantum gates .    </S>",
    "<S> quantum information processing devices are paving new inroads into the understanding of coherent processes and their applicability to high - complexity computation . </S>",
    "<S> the fundamental building blocks for these devices are quantum bits ( qubits ) , which are quantum two - level systems . naturally occurring qubits such as spin @xmath0 particles and photons are difficult to both isolate and control . </S>",
    "<S> fortunately , effective qubits can also be manufactured from an anharmonic multi - level system . </S>",
    "<S> these systems can be understood as a generalized atom , and all share the common problem of leakage out of the qubit subspace if the control bandwidth is comparable to the anharmonicity . </S>",
    "<S> examples of such systems are superconducting qubits [ 1 - 15 ] , optical lattices @xcite , and quantum dots @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> in superconducting qubits @xcite , researchers have demonstrated single qubit gates @xcite , two qubit gates @xcite , and the use of a quantum bus @xcite , suggesting the possibility of a scalable technology . </S>",
    "<S> decoherence has dropped dramatically @xcite from the original designs @xcite , due to both device and operation improvement @xcite . to date </S>",
    "<S> , the single - qubit accuracy of such systems has been in the range of 98% @xcite- 99.3% @xcite , which is a major achievement , yet not sufficient for quantum computing .    for large scale computation </S>",
    "<S> , gate errors are required below a fault - tolerance threshold , conservatively estimated at @xmath1 @xcite . in superconducting qubits such as the phase @xcite and the transmon @xcite qubits , </S>",
    "<S> long pulses are limited by decoherence and short pulses by excitations out of the qubit space @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> thus , one prevalent approach to achieve high fidelity gates is to increase the coherence time ; alternatively , the approach we will take is to reduce the gate time by using optimized control pulses that avoid leakage .    thus far , trying to minimize gate times through better control has been met with limited success . </S>",
    "<S> little improvement has been achieved beyond what is possible with a gaussian waveform @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> optimal control theory with only amplitude modulation has required pulses which have sharp features @xcite . for example , the pulses in @xcite require 3 pulse steps and 2 waiting periods , which requires currently impractical pulse shaping hardware . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , these pulses are limited to a minimal time , given by the inverse of the anharmonicity .    </S>",
    "<S> we propose to solve this problem using two quadrature controls for effective phase modulation , which allows us to achieve _ instantaneous _ cancellation of leakage using slowly varying controls . </S>",
    "<S> we present an analytical approach to accomplish this called derivative removal by adiabatic gate ( drag ) . </S>",
    "<S> then , we map the full potential of the technique using numerical refinement and consider the case when decoherence is included . </S>"
