    "<S> we study the possibility of revealing a weak coherent force by using a pendular mirror as a probe , and coupling this to a radiation field , which acts as the meter , in a cavityless configuration . </S>",
    "<S> we determine the sensitivity of such a scheme and show that the use of an entangled meter state greatly improves the ultimate detection limit . </S>",
    "<S> we also compare this scheme with that involving an optical cavity .    </S>",
    "<S> optomechanical systems play a crucial role in a variety of precision measurement like gravitational wave detection @xcite and atomic force microscope @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> they are based on the interaction between a movable mirror , a _ probe _ experiencing tiny forces , and a radiation field , a _ meter </S>",
    "<S> _ reading out the mirror s position .    in these applications </S>",
    "<S> one needs very high resolution measurements and a good control of the various noise sources , because one has to detect the effect of very weak forces . in the simpler setup @xcite , </S>",
    "<S> optomechanical systems are usually intended as a fabry - perot cavity with a movable end mirror coupled to the external force and to the radiation probe . as shown by the pioneering work of braginsky @xcite , even though all classical noise sources had been minimized , the detection of weak forces in such optomechanical systems would ultimately be determined by quantum fluctuations and the heisenberg uncertainty principle . in particular , quantum noise has two fundamental sources , the photon shot noise of the laser beam , and the fluctuations of the mirror position due to radiation pressure . </S>",
    "<S> the two quantum noises determine the so - called _ standard quantum limit _ </S>",
