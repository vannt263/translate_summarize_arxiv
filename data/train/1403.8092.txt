    "<S> we show that dirac fermions moving in two spatial dimensions with a generalized dispersion @xmath0 , subject to an external magnetic field and coupled to a complex scalar field carrying a vortex defect with winding number @xmath1 acquire @xmath2 zero modes . </S>",
    "<S> this is the same as in the absence of the magnetic field . </S>",
    "<S> our proof is based on selection rules in the landau level basis that dictate the existence and the number of the zero modes . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the result is insensitive to the choice of geometry and is naturally extended to general field profiles , where we also derive a generalization of the aharonov - casher theorem . </S>",
    "<S> experimental consequences of our results are briefly discussed .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction_.topological defects such as kinks in one spatial dimension and vortices and domain walls in higher dimensions frequently arise in physical systems as excitations in a background quantum field or an ordered state of matter . </S>",
    "<S> it was understood long ago that fermions moving in the background of a vortex defect can acquire fractional quantum numbers relative to the smooth vacuum background  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this fractionalization is mediated by zero - energy bound states of fermions to the vortex . at a domain wall , </S>",
    "<S> bound states turn into mid - gap propagating modes along the wall that endow it with special transport properties  @xcite . in either case </S>",
    "<S> the mid - gap spectrum and , in particular , the number of zero modes are central to understanding the physical properties of the combined system of fermions and defects  @xcite . formally , the number of zero - energy states is given by the index of the dirac hamiltonian of the fermion - vortex system . </S>",

    "<S> index theorems relate this number to the topological information encoded in the scalar field  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> an important system where such configurations arise is the single- or multi - layer graphene with a spectral gap due to a u(1 ) order parameter , such as kekul bond density  @xcite , antiferromagnetic  @xcite , superconducting  @xcite , and quantum anomalous hall  @xcite states . </S>",
    "<S> the effective hamiltonian of fermions in a system of @xmath8 bernal - stacked layers of graphene is a generalized dirac form where the spinor structure comes from the sublattices of the honeycomb lattice and the spectrum has band touchings with energy @xmath0 . </S>",
