    "during the last few years applications of gauge / gravity duality @xcite to hadronic physics attracted a lot of attention , and various holographic dual models of qcd were proposed in the literature ( see , e.g. , @xcite-@xcite ) .",
    "these models were able to incorporate such essential properties of qcd as confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking , and also to reproduce many of the static hadronic observables ( decay constants , masses ) , with values rather close to the experimental ones .    in our recent papers",
    "@xcite we developed a formalism that allows to systematically study the meson form factors within the holographic `` hard - wall '' approach of refs .",
    "we applied it first to form factors and wave functions of vector mesons @xcite and then @xcite to the pion electromagnetic form factor . in ref .",
    "@xcite , we extended the holographic dual model of qcd to incorporate the anomalous @xmath1 form factor .    in the present paper ,",
    "we consider a holographic model of qcd proposed by hirn and sanz @xcite , with @xmath2 yang - mills ( ym ) gauge fields living in the background of sliced five - dimensional ( 5d ) ads space . unlike the approach of refs .",
    "@xcite , this model does not require the existence of an additional degree of freedom dual to the chiral condensate of four - dimensional ( 4d ) qcd , which spontaneously breaks the chiral symmetry via the higgs - like mechanism . instead , the chiral symmetry breaking down to its vector subgroup @xmath3 occurs due to the boundary conditions ( b.c . ) imposed on the infrared ( ir ) brane .    at the same time",
    ", the global @xmath4 symmetry of qcd is generated from the requirement that the fields vanish on the ultraviolet ( uv ) boundary .",
    "the chiral field @xmath5 , the phase of which describes the nambu - goldstone bosons , appears in this model as a product of wilson lines connecting ir and uv branes by the fifth components of left and right gauge fields .    since the model of ref .",
    "@xcite incorporates ym fields only , it has a single parameter , the cutoff scale @xmath6 , that determines the size of masses @xmath7 , etc . , of vector and axial - vector mesons , and also the pion decay constant @xmath8 . still , many features of this construction are similar to those discussed in the papers @xcite",

    "we start by outlining , in section ii , the basics of the hard - wall model of hirn and sanz . in particular",

    "<S> we study pion in the holographic model of hirn and sanz which contains two yang - mills fields defined in the background of the sliced ads space . </S>",
    "<S> the infrared boundary conditions imposed on these fields generate the spontaneous breaking of the chiral symmetry down to its vector subgroup . within the framework of this model , </S>",
    "<S> we get an analytic expression for the pion form factor and a compact result for its radius . </S>",
    "<S> we also extend the holographic model to include chern - simons term which is required to reproduce the appropriate axial anomaly of qcd . as a result , we calculate the anomalous form factor of the pion and predict its @xmath0-slope for the kinematics when one of the photons is almost on - shell . </S>",
    "<S> we also observe that the anomalous form factor with one real and one virtual photon is given by the same analytic expression as the electromagnetic form factor of a charged pion . </S>",
    "<S> one of the advantages of the present model is that it does not require an infrared boundary counterterm to correctly reproduce the anomaly of qcd . </S>"
