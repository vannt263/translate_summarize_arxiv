    "<S> graphene has raised high expectations as a low - loss plasmonic material in which the plasmon properties can be controlled via electrostatic doping . here , </S>",
    "<S> we analyze realistic configurations , which produce inhomogeneous doping , in contrast to what has been so far assumed in the study of plasmons in nanostructured graphene . </S>",
    "<S> specifically , we investigate backgated ribbons , co - planar ribbon pairs placed at opposite potentials , and individual ribbons subject to a uniform electric field . </S>",
    "<S> plasmons in backgated ribbons and ribbon pairs are similar to those of uniformly doped ribbons , provided the fermi energy is appropriately scaled to compensate for finite - size effects such as the divergence of the carrier density at the edges . </S>",
    "<S> in contrast , the plasmons of a ribbon exposed to a uniform field exhibit distinct dispersion and spatial profiles that considerably differ from uniformly doped ribbons . </S>",
    "<S> our results provide a road map to understand graphene plasmons under realistic electrostatic doping conditions .    </S>",
    "<S> plasmons@xcite the collective oscillations of conduction electrons in metals are capable of confining electromagnetic energy down to deep sub - wavelength regions . </S>",
    "<S> they can also enhance the intensity of an incident light wave by several orders of magnitude . </S>",
    "<S> these phenomena are the main reason why the field of plasmonics is finding a wide range of applications that include single - molecule sensing,@xcite nonlinear optics,@xcite and optical trapping of nanometer - sized objects.@xcite    recently , confined plasmons have been observed and spatially mapped in doped graphene.@xcite the level of doping in this material can be adjusted by exposing it to the electric fields produced by neighboring gates . </S>",
    "<S> electrostatic doping has actually been used to demonstrate plasmon - frequency tunability@xcite and induced optical modulations in the thz@xcite and infrared@xcite response of graphene .    </S>",
    "<S> the two - dimensional ( 2d ) band structure of pristine graphene consists of two cones filled with valence electrons and two empty inverted cones joining the former at the so - called dirac points , which mark the fermi level . </S>",
    "<S> extra electrons or holes added to this structure form a 2d electron or hole gas that can sustain surface plasmons.@xcite compared to noble - metal plasmons , graphene modes are believed to be long - lived excitations.@xcite but most importantly , their frequency can be controlled via the above - mentioned electrostatic doping.@xcite for example , in homogeneous suspended graphene , a perpendicular dc electric field @xmath0 applied to one side of the carbon sheet is completely screened by an induced surface charge density @xmath1 , and this in turn situates the fermi level at an energy @xmath2 relative to the dirac points.@xcite here , @xmath3 m/s is the fermi velocity of graphene .     as a function of width - to - distance </S>",

    "<S> conclusions.- _ we have shown that the plasmons of doped graphene ribbons are highly sensitive to the inhomogeneities of the doping charge density produced by realistic electrostatic landscapes . </S>",
    "<S> the doping profile can be engineered by adjusting the configuration of the gates relative to the graphene . </S>",
    "<S> we find an interesting scenario when a uniform external electric field is used to dope the graphene , leading to plasmons with very different characteristics ( e.g. , induced charges piling up near the center of the ribbon ) compared to those of uniformly doped graphene ( in which plasmons pile up at the edges ) . </S>",
    "<S> this configuration can be used to avert losses associated with nonlocal effects at the edges , which are expected to be significant.@xcite the present study can be straightforwardly extended to other configurations , such as finite graphene nanoislands exposed to either backgates or side gates . </S>",
    "<S> electrostatic charge accumulation at sharp edges can offer an additional handle to manipulate plasmon modes . </S>",
    "<S> in addition to the possibilities explored in this paper , one can use biased tips to produce localized disk - like doping areas at designated positions targeted by simply moving the tips above a graphene flake . in conclusion , </S>",
    "<S> the design of electrostatic landscapes becomes a useful tool to engineer graphene plasmons .    </S>",
