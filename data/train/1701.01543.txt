    "graphene nanoribbons are known as excellent counterpart of graphene due to the possibility of exhibiting accessible and modeled energy gaps@xcite . in particular , ribbons with armchair edge belong to different semiconducting families , with a gap size depending essentially on the nanoribbon width .",
    "zigzag ribbons , otherwise , show metallic behavior due to the presence of edge localized states at the fermi energy@xcite .",
    "the presence of roughness , mainly at the edges of the ribbons , can be responsible for important transport suppressions and formation of conductance gaps , leading to some restrictions for the use of graphene in ballistic devices@xcite .",
    "differently from what could be naively expected , some particular deformations can however strength transport properties as it happens in the case of strained - fold graphene systems .",
    "when graphene samples are deposited on @xmath0 or hexagonal @xmath1 substrates , deformations such bubbles and wrinkles may accidentally arise mainly due to the difference in the thermal expansion coefficients of the constituent materials@xcite .",
    "deformations can then be produced , leading to the formation of homogeneous pseudo - magnetic fields@xcite .",
    "other routes are based on patterning not the graphene itself but the supporting substrates that induce different strain profiles @xcite .",
    "physical scenarios have been explored where strain induced pseudo - landau levels are observed @xcite . in addition , controlled creation of periodic ripples in suspended graphene sheets may also be achieved by thermally generated strains@xcite .",
    "alternatively , strained folds can be generated by pressuring a gas inside a sealed container with a slit covered by graphene @xcite or by the presence of a gate voltage , below or on top of graphene on an extended trench to induce the deformation @xcite .",
    "a large quantity of theoretical works have discussed the coupling between mechanical deformations and electronic responses for particular deformations in graphene @xcite .",
    "experimentally , laser ablation , stm and afm have been used for manipulation and detection of these deformations@xcite .",
    "recently the electronic transport along fold - like deformed areas have been explored in zigzag nanoribbons@xcite . when a gaussian - like deformation parallel to the zigzag direction is considered , strain - induced pseudomagnetic fields are formed exhibiting stripped spatial distribution .",
    "it was shown that folded zigzag gnrs behave as natural waveguides for electronic transport and provide a splitting of an incident current , which results in valley - polarized currents moving along different parts of the structure@xcite .",
    "the spatial separation of valley currents is preserved when disorder at the edge are included in the model , giving rise to novel quasi - ballistic transport characteristics . on the other hand , folded armchair nanoribbons do not generate pseudomagnetic fields like the zigzag gnrs",

    "<S> strain fold - like deformations on armchair graphene nanoribbons ( agnrs ) can be properly engineered in experimental setups , and could lead to a new controlling tool for gaps and transport properties . here </S>",
    "<S> , we analyze the electronic properties of folded agnrs relating the electronic responses and the mechanical deformation . </S>",
    "<S> an important and universal parameter for the gap engineering is the ribbon percent width variation , i.e. , the difference between the deformed and undeformed ribbon widths . </S>",
    "<S> agnrs bandgap can be tuned mechanically in a well defined bounded range of energy values , eventually leading to a metallic system . </S>",
    "<S> this characteristic provides a new controllable degree of freedom that allows manipulation of electronic currents . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the numerical results are analytically predicted by solving the dirac equation for the strained system . </S>"
