    "in the past few years there has been significant progress in the study of the chiral - continuum extrapolation problem for lattice qcd with different types of fermions ( see , for example , refs .",
    "extrapolation of lattice qcd simulation results to the physical ( light ) quark masses is a nontrivial task due to the nonanalytic variation of hadronic properties with quark masses which is a consequence of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking . to perform an extrapolation to the continuum limit we need to account for direct chiral , taste ( in the staggered case ) and rotational symmetry breaking by finite lattice spacing .    at present ,",

    "@xcite it follows that the simulations with ginsparg - wilson fermions may be about ten to one hundred times more expensive than with improved staggered fermions at comparable masses .    however , the price of these advantages of staggered fermions is that they contain an internal flaw . in those simulations , to reduce the number of taste degrees of freedom , the fourth - root trick is employed . in the continuum limit",
