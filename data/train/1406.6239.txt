    "optical vortices , phase singularities of the electromagnetic field , show interesting classical and quantum properties with a variety of applications @xcite .",
    "they are observed as dark spots in bright background . for the vortex of topological charge or order @xmath0 ,",
    "the azimuthal phase varies as @xmath1 in a full rotation around the dark spot @xcite .",
    "the sense of rotation of phase provides the sign of its charge @xcite . the topological charge @xmath0 can be considered as an important parameter for such beams .",
    "one of the main characteristics of these beams is that they carry an orbital angular momentum ( oam ) of @xmath2 per photon @xcite .",
    "this particular property has successfully been utilized for particle manipulation @xcite and quantum information @xcite .",
    "vortex beams have been experimentally realized not only in optics but also with electron beams @xcite .",
    "these beams form an infinite dimensional basis for applications such as quantum computation and cryptography @xcite . moreover ,",
    "increase in information entropy with the order of vortices can be utilised to encode more amount of information in these structures @xcite . here , we present a new aspect i.e. inseparability of position and momentum akin to quantum entanglement , for these fascinating optical structures .    non - quantum entanglement has been discussed earlier also @xcite , however , there has not been a rigorous experiment showing the violation of bell s inequality involving inseparability of continuous variables for a classical system . here , we present our experiment using the theoretical results @xcite to show that inseparable position and momentum variables of a phase singular beam do violate bell s inequality . a brief theoretical discussion on the bell s inequality violation for optical vortices has been given in section [ sec : theory ] , experiments performed in section [ sec : experiment ] and results in section [ sec : result ] .",
