    "the dissipationless flow of a current through a superconductor  normal - metal  superconductor ( sns ) junction is a fundamental demonstration of the `` proximity effect '' : a normal metal borrows characteristic properties from a nearby superconductor .",
    "the energy gap @xmath3 in the bulk induces a suppression of the density of states inside the normal metal near the fermi level , depending on the phase difference @xmath8 between the superconductors .",
    "the resulting @xmath8-dependence of the free energy @xmath9 implies the flow of a current @xmath10 in equilibrium .",
    "in contrast to the original josephson effect in tunnel junctions , the separation of the superconductors in an sns junction can be much greater than the superconducting coherence length .",
    "recent experiments on mesoscopic josephson junctions @xcite have revived theoretical interest in this subject@xcite , which goes back to work by kulik @xcite and aslamasov , larkin , and ovchinnikov @xcite .",

    "<S> the classical motion in the quantum dot is assumed to be chaotic on time scales greater than the ergodic time @xmath0 , which itself is much smaller than the mean dwell time @xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> the excitation spectrum of the josephson junction has a gap @xmath2 , which can be less than the gap @xmath3 in the bulk superconductors . </S>",
    "<S> the average supercurrent is computed in the ergodic regime @xmath4 , using random - matrix theory , and in the non - ergodic regime @xmath5 , using a semiclassical relation between the supercurrent and dwell - time distribution . </S>",
    "<S> in contrast to conventional josephson junctions , raising the temperature above the excitation gap does not necessarily lead to an exponential suppression of the supercurrent . </S>",
    "<S> instead , we find a temperature regime between @xmath2 and @xmath3 where the supercurrent decreases logarithmically with temperature . </S>",
