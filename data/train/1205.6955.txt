    "<S> we propose optical phonon lasing for a double quantum dot ( dqd ) fabricated in a semiconductor substrate . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the dqd is weakly coupled to only two lo phonon modes that act as a natural cavity . </S>",
    "<S> the lasing occurs for pumping the dqd via electronic tunneling at rates much higher than the phonon decay rate , whereas an antibunching of phonon emission is observed in the opposite regime of slow tunneling . </S>",
    "<S> both effects disappear with an effective thermalization induced by the franck - condon effect in a dqd fabricated in a carbon nanotube with a strong electron - phonon coupling .    </S>",
    "<S> electrically tunable two - level systems are ideal candidates to study the interaction between fermions and bosons under nonequilibrium conditions . </S>",
    "<S> single - qubit lasers or nanomechanical resonators @xcite are examples where concepts from quantum optics , such as the microlaser @xcite , have been successfully transferred to and combined with artificial solid - state architectures . </S>",
    "<S> semiconductor double quantum dots ( dqds ) play a similar role as model systems with the coupling between electrons and the surrounding substrate leading to , e.g. , tunable spontaneous phonon emission and dicke - type interference effects @xcite .    in this letter </S>",
    "<S> , we propose optical phonon lasing in a dqd without the requirement of an additional cavity or resonator . </S>",
    "<S> we start from the observation that a dqd effectively couples to only two lo phonon modes that work as a natural cavity . </S>",
    "<S> the pumping to the upper level is realized by an electric current through the dqd under a finite bias . </S>",
    "<S> the amplified lo phonons occasionally escape from this cavity by decaying into the so - called `` daughter phonons '' @xcite that can be observed externally . </S>",
    "<S> the phonon lasing is possible when the pumping rate is much higher than the phonon decay rate @xmath0 .    </S>",
    "<S> we also observe the phonon antibunching in the same system when the pumping rate is lower than @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> we emphasize that the phonon statistics can be changed by electrically tuning the tunnel coupling between dqds and leads @xcite . </S>",
