    "<S> we investigate the large deviation properties of the maximum likelihood estimators for the ornstein - uhlenbeck process with shift . </S>",
    "<S> we propose a new approach to establish large deviation principles which allows us , via a suitable transformation , to circumvent the classical non - steepness problem . </S>",
    "<S> we estimate simultaneously the drift and shift parameters . on the one hand , we prove a large deviation principle for the maximum likelihood estimates of the drift and shift parameters . surprisingly , we find that the drift estimator shares the same large deviation principle as the one previously established for the ornstein - uhlenbeck process without shift . </S>",
    "<S> sharp large deviation principles are also provided . on the other hand , we show that the maximum likelihood estimator of the shift parameter satisfies a large deviation principle with a very unusual implicit rate function .    </S>",
