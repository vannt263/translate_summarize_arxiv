    "the different possible equations of state ( eos ) of dense matter inside neutron stars ( nss ) are often given as tabulated data which are fed into the solution of the hydrostatic equilibrium equations via an interpolation technique .",
    "while this method works well in the case of general relativity ( gr ) for which the hydrostatic equilibrium is described by the tov equations @xcite , it leads to problems in modified theories of gravity , e.g.  @xmath0 theories @xcite in which the equations describing the hydrostatic equilibrium contain second derivatives of @xmath1 ( see e.g.  @xcite ) where @xmath2 is the pressure and @xmath3 is the density . in such cases",
    "the order of the polynomial used in interpolation technique may effect the results if it is not sufficiently high and the linear interpolation technique will certainly fail .",
    "it is then necessary to employ analytical representations of the eos that are sufficiently many times differentiable . in this work ,",
    "we provide an accurate unified analytical representation for 22 eos .",
