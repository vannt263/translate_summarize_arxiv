    "removing any edge divides the tree into two connected parts , thereby inducing a split ( bipartition ) on the set of leaf labels .",
    "each edge induces a different split , and the tree can be reconstructed from these splits .",

    "phylogenetic tree represents both clustering ( splits ) and proximity ( distance ) information .",
    "however only very special collections of splits , and only very special metrics , can be represented faithfully on a tree .",
    "that is an advantage for inference if trees are appropriate representations .",
    "it is a disadvantage of they are not .",
