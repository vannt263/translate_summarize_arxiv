    "<S> the serpens cloud has received considerable attention in the last years , in particular the small region known as the serpens cloud core where a plethora of star formation related phenomena are found . </S>",
    "<S> this review summarizes our current observational knowledge of the cloud , with emphasis on the core . </S>",
    "<S> recent results are converging to a distance for the cloud of @xmath0 pc , an issue which has been controversial over the years . </S>",
    "<S> we present the gas and dust properties of the cloud core and describe its structure and appearance at different wavelengths . </S>",
    "<S> the core contains a dense , very young , low mass stellar cluster with more than 300 objects in all evolutionary phases , from collapsing gaseous condensations to pre - main sequence stars . </S>",
    "<S> we describe the behaviour and spatial distribution of the different stellar populations ( mm cores , classes 0 , i and ii sources ) . </S>",
    "<S> the spatial concentration and the fraction number of class 0/class i / class ii sources is considerably larger in the serpens core than in any other low mass star formation region , e.g. taurus , ophiuchus or chamaeleon , as also stated in different works . </S>",
    "<S> appropriate references for coordinates and fluxes of all serpens objects are given . </S>",
    "<S> however , we provide for the first time a unified list of all near - ir sources which have up to now been identified as members of the serpens core cluster ; this list includes some members identified in this review . a cross - reference table of the near - ir objects with optical , mid - ir , submillimeter , radio continuum and x - ray surces </S>",
    "<S> is also provided . </S>",
    "<S> a simple analysis has allowed us to identify a sample of @xmath1 brown dwarf candidates among the 252 near - ir objects ; some of them show near - ir excesses and , therefore , they constitute an attractive sample to study very young substellar objects . </S>",
    "<S> the review also refers to the outflows associated with the young sources . </S>",
    "<S> a section is dedicated to the relatively small amount of works carried out towards serpens regions outside the core . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , we refer to iso and to recent spitzer data . </S>",
    "<S> these results reveal new centers of active star formation in the serpens cloud and the presence of new young clusters , which deserve follow - up observations and studies to determine their characteristics and nature in detail . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we give a short , non - exhaustive list of individually interesting serpens objects . </S>"
