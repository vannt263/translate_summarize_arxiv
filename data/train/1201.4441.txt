    "<S> faithfully storing an unknown quantum light state is essential to advanced quantum communication and distributed quantum computation applications . </S>",
    "<S> the required quantum memory must have high fidelity to improve the performance of a quantum network . here </S>",
    "<S> we report the reversible transfer of photonic polarization states into collective atomic excitation in a compact solid - state device . </S>",
    "<S> the quantum memory is based on an atomic frequency comb ( afc ) in rare - earth ion doped crystals . </S>",
    "<S> we obtain up to @xmath0 process fidelity for the storage and retrieval process of single - photon - level coherent pulse . </S>",
    "<S> this reliable quantum memory is a crucial step toward quantum networks based on solid - state devices .    </S>",
    "<S> quantum communication channels are naturally carried by photons . to realize a quantum network , we must coherently transfer quantum information between the stationary qubits and the flying photon qubits @xcite . despite some remarkable efforts in quantum networks based on cold atoms @xcite , single atom in a cavity @xcite bose - einstein condensate @xcite , atomic vapors @xcite and </S>",
    "<S> trapped ions @xcite , there are strong motivations for using more practical systems , e.g. solid - state devices . </S>",
    "<S> rare - earth ( re ) ion - doped solids provide a particular electronic structure that can be seen as a frozen gas of atoms , they have excellent coherence properties for optical and spin transitions . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , they have already shown excellent capability to store light for extended periods @xcite with high efficiency @xcite and a large bandwidth @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> recent achievements include the storage of photonic time - bin entanglement generated through spontaneous parametric down conversion ( spdc ) @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> logical qubits with photons can be encoded in several ways , for example via polarization , time - bin , path , phase or photon - number encodings . </S>",
    "<S> the polarization degree of freedom is particularly useful because it allows a single - photon qubit to be transmitted in a single spatial and temporal mode . </S>",
    "<S> polarized photons are more easily transportable qubits and are more robust against decoherence . </S>",
    "<S> note that many quantum light sources generate entangled photons with information encoded in their polarization degree , such as the eight - photon entangled state generated by spdc @xcite and the on - demand entangled photon pairs generated by biexciton decay in quantum dots @xcite . realizing the quantum interface between quantum memory and other quantum light source will also require the capability of the quantum memory to store the polarization information for light . </S>",
    "<S> therefore , the ability to store polarization information with high fidelity is of particular interests . </S>",
    "<S> one approach to realizing a polarization - qubit memory is to first transform the polarization encoding into path encoding and then store it into two distant ensembles . </S>",
    "<S> this approach has been implemented in both atomic ensembles @xcite and atomic vapors @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the abovementioned realizations suffer from several practical drawbacks due to the extra step of spatially splitting the input state . </S>",
    "<S> phase locking of the two optical paths @xcite may be required , and imperfect spatial mode matching may limit the memory fidelity . </S>",
    "<S> another approach is using uniformly absorbed samples for two orthogonal polarizations , which requires no spatially splitting of the input photon . </S>",
    "<S> this approach has been implemented with two spatially overlapped atomic ensembles @xcite , bec @xcite and with a single - atom in a cavity @xcite . up to now , </S>",
    "<S> the best fidelity performance achieved with single - photon - level input is 0.95 @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> all of these experiments are based on the raman process or electromagnetically induced transparency ( eit ) . </S>",
    "<S> these processes require a strong control or read light pulse during the memory sequence , which introduces unavoidable noise into the retrieval signal .    </S>",
    "<S> afc is an alternative protocol for realizing quantum memory without a strong control light during the memory sequence . </S>",
    "<S> afc quantum memory with re doped solids has shown an excellent capability to store quantum light @xcite . because of the strongly polarization - dependent absorption of ions in crystal </S>",
