    "nanoelectromechanical systems ( nems ) have become a promising playground for probing the quantum behavior of mesoscopic objects , theoretically as well as experimentally @xcite .",
    "the diverse reasons to study nems are their vast number of ( possible ) applications as for instance the measurement of mass , force and position @xcite with high precision .",
    "nanomechanical systems being at the boarder of classical to quantum are also being studied from a very fundamental point of view .",
    "this includes the observation , measurement and control of quantum states of a mesoscopic mechanical continuous variable system such as a harmonic oscillator .",
    "superconducting qubits electrically coupled to the mechanical system have been successfully used to characterize the mechanical resonator s quantum state @xcite .    making quantum effects visible in nanomechanical systems",
    "calls for ultralow temperatures and low dissipation .",
    "the goal of observing the quantum mechanical ground state of a harmonic oscillator requires temperatures @xmath8 .",
    "recently , this goal has been achieved by using a microwave - frequency mechanical oscillator , with a frequency of @xmath9 which allowed cooling to the ground state with conventional cryogenic refrigeration @xcite .",
    "further proposals of cooling a nanomechanical resonator coupled to an optical cavity have been proposed @xcite and experimentally implemented @xcite .",
    "the theoretical treatment of nems widely uses a markovian master equation approach @xcite with a few exceptions , for instance , the work by wabnig _",
    "_ @xcite and rastelli _ et al . _",
    "@xcite where a keldysh perturbation theory has been employed . here",
    ", we also make use of the keldysh technique because it allows us to treat the non - equilibrium system fully quantum mechanically and , furthermore , to carefully investigate the non - markovian regime where @xmath10 .",
    "since we are interested in the quantum nature of the oscillator , it is important that the temperature @xmath7 and the applied bias @xmath1 of the tunnel junction are not much larger than the eigenfrequency @xmath2 of the oscillator .",
    "otherwise , the oscillator would be heated and low energy properties inaccessible .",

    "<S> we propose and analyze different schemes to probe the quantum nature of nanoelectromechanical systems ( nems ) by a tunnel junction detector . </S>",
    "<S> using the keldysh technique , we are able to investigate the dynamics of the combined system for an arbitrary ratio of @xmath0 , where @xmath1 is the applied bias of the tunnel junction and @xmath2 the eigenfrequency of the oscillator . in this sense , we go beyond the markov approximation of previous works where these parameters were restricted to the regime @xmath3 . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , we also go beyond the born approximation because we calculate the finite frequency current noise of the tunnel junction up to fourth order in the tunneling amplitudes .    </S>",
    "<S> interestingly , we discover different ways to probe both position and momentum properties of nems . on the one hand , for a non - stationary oscillator </S>",
    "<S> , we find a complex finite frequency noise of the tunnel junction . by analyzing the real and the imaginary part of this noise separately , </S>",
    "<S> we conclude that a simple tunnel junction detector can probe both position- and momentum - based observables of the non - stationary oscillator . on the other hand , for a stationary oscillator , </S>",
    "<S> a more complicated setup based on an aharonov - bohm - loop tunnel junction detector is needed . </S>",
    "<S> it still allows us to extract position and momentum information of the oscillator . for this type of detector , </S>",
    "<S> we analyze for the first time what happens if the energy scales @xmath4 , @xmath5 , and @xmath6 take arbitrary values with respect to each other where @xmath7 is the temperature of an external heat bath . under these circumstances , we show that it is possible to uniquely identify the quantum state of the oscillator by a finite frequency noise measurement . </S>"
