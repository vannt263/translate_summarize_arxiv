    "secular resonances occur when there is a commensurability between the proper frequency of precession of the argument of pericenter @xmath31 or of the longitude of the node @xmath11 of a given asteroid and a planet .",
    "if the commensurability is just between one frequency of the asteroid and one frequency of the planet , such as in the case of the @xmath36 secular resonance , the resonance is linear .",
    "higher order resonances involving more complex combinations of asteroidal and planetary frequencies , such as for instance the @xmath37 resonance , are called non - linear secular resonances .",
