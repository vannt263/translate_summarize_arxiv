    "<S> frames , or lattices consisting of mass points connected by rigid bonds or central - force springs , are important model constructs that have applications in such diverse fields as structural engineering , architecture , and materials science . </S>",
    "<S> the difference between the number of bonds and the number of degrees of freedom of these lattices determines the number of their zero - frequency  floppy modes \" . </S>",
    "<S> when these are balanced , the system is on the verge of mechanical instability and is termed isostatic . </S>",
    "<S> it has recently been shown that certain extended isostatic lattices exhibit floppy modes localized at their boundary . </S>",
    "<S> these boundary modes are insensitive to local perturbations , and appear to have a topological origin , reminiscent of the protected electronic boundary modes that occur in the quantum hall effect and in topological insulators . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we establish the connection between the topological mechanical modes and the topological band theory of electronic systems , and we predict the existence of new topological bulk mechanical phases with distinct boundary modes . </S>",
    "<S> we introduce model systems in one and two dimensions that exemplify this phenomenon .    </S>",
    "<S> isostatic lattices provide a useful reference point for understanding the properties of a wide range of systems on the verge of mechanical instability , including network glasses @xcite , randomly diluted lattices near the rigidity percolation threshold @xcite , randomly packed particles near their jamming threshold @xcite , and biopolymer networks @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> there are many periodic lattices , including the square and kagome lattices in @xmath0 dimensions and the cubic and pyrochlore lattices in @xmath1 , that are locally isostatic with coordination number @xmath2 for every site under periodic boundary conditions . </S>",
    "<S> these lattices , which are the subject of this paper , have a surprisingly rich range of elastic responses and phonon structures @xcite that exhibit different behaviors as bending forces or additional bonds are added .    the analysis of such systems dates to an 1864 paper by james clerk maxwell @xcite that argued that a lattice with @xmath3 mass points and @xmath4 bonds has @xmath5 zero modes . </S>",
    "<S> maxwell s count is incomplete , though , because @xmath6 can exceed @xmath7 if there are @xmath8 states of self - stress , where springs can be under tension or compression with no net forces on the masses . </S>",
    "<S> this occurs , for example , when masses are connected by straight lines of bonds under periodic boundary conditions . </S>",
    "<S> a more general maxwell relation @xcite , @xmath9 is valid for infinitesimal distortions .    in a locally isostatic system with periodic boundary conditions , @xmath10 . </S>",
    "<S> the square and kagome lattices have one state of self - stress per straight line of bonds and associated zero modes along lines in momentum space . cutting a section of @xmath11 sites from these lattices </S>",
    "<S> removes states of self - stress and @xmath12 bonds and necessarily leads to @xmath12 zero modes , which are essentially identical to the bulk zero modes . </S>",
    "<S> recently sun _ et al . _ </S>",
    "<S> @xcite studied a twisted kagome lattice in which states of self - stress are removed by rotating adjacent site - sharing triangles in opposite directions . </S>",
    "<S> this simple modification converts lines of zero modes in the untwisted lattice to gapped modes of nonzero frequency ( except for @xmath13 ) and localizes the required zero modes in the cut lattice to its surfaces .    </S>",
    "<S> these boundary zero modes are robust and insensitive to local perturbations . </S>",
    "<S> boundary modes also occur in electronic systems , such as the quantum hall effect @xcite and topological insulators @xcite . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we establish the connection between these two phenomena . </S>",
    "<S> our analysis allows us to predict the existence of new topologically distinct bulk mechanical phases and to characterize the protected modes that occur on their boundary . </S>",
    "<S> we introduce a 1d model that illustrates this phenomenon in its simplest form and maps directly to the su - schrieffer - heeger ( ssh ) model @xcite for the electronic excitations of polyacetylene ( @xmath14 ) , a linear polymer with alternating single and double bonds between carbon atoms as show in figure [ fig1 ] that has toplogically protected electron states at free ends and at interfaces . </S>",
    "<S> we then prove an index theorem that generalizes equation ( [ eq1 ] ) and relates the local count of zero modes on the boundary to the topological structure of the bulk . </S>",
    "<S> we introduce a deformed version of the kagome lattice model that exhibits distinct topological phases . </S>",
    "<S> the predictions of an index theorem are verified explicitly by solving for the boundary modes in this model . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we show that some of the distinctive features of the topological phases can be understood within a continuum elastic theory . </S>"
