    "<S> the complex x - ray morphology of the 300 kpc long x - ray jet in pks  1127 - 145 ( z=1.18 quasar ) is clearly discerned in a @xmath0100 ksec _ chandra _ observation . </S>",
    "<S> the jet x - ray surface brightness gradually decreases by an order of magnitude going out from the core . </S>",
    "<S> the x - ray spectrum of the inner jet is relatively flat with @xmath1 and steep in the outer jet with @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> the x - ray and radio jet intensity profiles are strikingly different , with the radio emission peaking strongly at the two outer knots while the x - ray emission is strongest in the inner jet region . </S>",
    "<S> we discuss the constraints implied by these data on the x - ray emission models and conclude that `` one - zone '' models fail and that at least a two - component model is needed to explain the jet s broadband emission . </S>",
    "<S> we propose that the x - ray emission originates in the jet proper while the bulk of the radio emission comes from a surrounding jet sheath . </S>",
    "<S> we also consider intermittent jet activity as a possible cause of the observed jet morphology . </S>"
