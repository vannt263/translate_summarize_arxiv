    "<S> we present a set of 26 finite quandles that distinguish ( up to reversal and mirror image ) by number of colorings , all of the 2977 prime oriented knots with up to 12 crossings . </S>",
    "<S> we also show that 1058 of these knots can be distinguished from their mirror images by the number of colorings by quandles from a certain set of 23 finite quandles . </S>",
    "<S> we study the colorings of these 2977 knots by all of the 431 connected quandles of order at most 35 found by l. vendramin . among other things , </S>",
    "<S> we collect information about quandles that have the same number of colorings for all of the 2977 knots . </S>",
    "<S> for example , we prove that if @xmath0 is a simple quandle of prime power order then @xmath0 and the dual quandle @xmath1 of @xmath0 have the same number of colorings for all knots and conjecture that this holds for all alexander quandles @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> we study a knot invariant based on a quandle homomorphism @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> we also apply the quandle colorings we have computed to obtain some new results for the bridge index , the nakanishi index , the tunnel number , and the unknotting number . in an appendix we discuss various properties of the quandles in vendramin s list . </S>",
