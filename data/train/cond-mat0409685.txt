    "<S> transmission of a scalar field through a random medium , represented by a system of randomly distributed dielectric cylinders , is calculated numerically . </S>",
    "<S> the system is mapped to the problem of electronic transport in disordered two - dimensional systems . </S>",
    "<S> universality of the statistical distribution of transmission parameters is analyzed in the metallic and localized regimes . in the metallic regime , the universality of transmission statistics in all transparent channels is observed . in the band gaps , </S>",
    "<S> we distinguish a disorder induced ( anderson ) localization from tunneling through the system , due to a gap in the density of states . </S>",
    "<S> we also show that absorption causes a rapid decrease of the mean conductance , but , contrary to the case of the localized regime , the conductance is self - averaged with a gaussian distribution . </S>"
