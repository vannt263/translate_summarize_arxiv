    "<S> this report presents a new , algorithmic approach to the distributions of the distance between two points distributed uniformly at random in various polygons , based on the extended kinematic measure ( km ) from integral geometry . </S>",
    "<S> we first obtain such random point distance distributions ( pdds ) associated with arbitrary triangles ( i.e. , triangle - pdds ) , including the pdd within a triangle , and that between two triangles sharing either a common side or a common vertex . for each case </S>",
    "<S> , we provide an algorithmic procedure showing the mathematical derivation process , based on which either the closed - form expressions or the algorithmic results can be obtained . </S>",
    "<S> the obtained triangle - pdds can be utilized for modeling and analyzing the wireless communication networks associated with triangle geometries , such as sensor networks with triangle - shaped clusters and triangle - shaped cellular systems with highly directional antennas . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , based on the obtained triangle - pdds , we then show how to obtain the pdds associated with arbitrary polygons through the decomposition and recursion approach , since any polygons can be triangulated , and any geometry shapes can be approximated by polygons with a needed precision . finally , we give the pdds associated with ring geometries . </S>",
    "<S> the results shown in this report can enrich and expand the theory and application of the probabilistic distance models for the analysis of wireless communication networks .    </S>",
    "<S> distance distributions ; kinematic measure ; triangles ; polygons ; ring geometries ; wireless communication networks </S>"
