    "a scheme for analysis of thomson scattering from plasmas based on the average - atom model , a quantum - mechanical version of the  generalized thomas - fermi theory \" of feynman , metropolis and teller @xcite is presented .",
    "given the plasma composition @xmath194 , density @xmath14 and temperature @xmath89 , the model gives , in addition to the equation of state of the plasma , all parameters needed for a complete description of the thomson scattering process .",
    "in particular , the average - atom code predicts wave functions for bound and continuum electrons , densities of bound , screening and free electrons , and the chemical potential .",
    "predictions of the present average - atom model disagree with those in ref .",
    "@xcite where a similar model with different boundary conditions is used . in particular , in ref .",
    "@xcite , @xmath195 electrons were bound in metallic density al for temperatures between 2 and 10  ev , leading to substantial bound - state contributions to the dynamic structure function . in the present model",
    "the @xmath195 subshell of metallic density al is vacant in the temperature range @xmath196  ev and the corresponding bound - state features are absent .",
    "elastic scattering from bound and screening electrons is treated following the model proposed by @xcite which makes use of formulas for the static ion - ion structure function @xmath68 given by @xcite .",
    "modifications suggested by @xcite to account for different electron and ion temperatures are also included .",
    "treatment of the ion - ion structure function appears to be the weakest aspect of the present analysis .",
    "the dynamic structure function for scattering from free electrons depends sensitively on the free - electron dielectric function @xmath67 .",
    "again , we follow the model proposed in ref .  @xcite and evaluate the dielectric function in the random - phase approximation .",
    "the rpa dielectric function includes features such as plasmon resonant peaks that show up in experimental intensity profiles and can be used in connection with the principle of detailed balance to determine electron temperatures .",
    "bound - state features are included in the present scheme , inasmuch as the average - atom model provides bound - state and continuum wave functions .",
    "coulomb - field effects are automatically included in calculations carried out using average - atom continuum states rather than plane waves to describe the final state electron . in conclusion",
    ", the average - atom model provides a simple and consistent point of departure for theoretical analysis of thomson scattering from plasmas .",

    "<S> the average - atom model is applied to study thomson scattering of x - rays from warm - dense matter with emphasis on scattering by bound electrons . </S>",
    "<S> parameters needed to evaluate the dynamic structure function ( chemical potential , average ionic charge , free electron density , bound and continuum wave functions and occupation numbers ) are obtained from the average - atom model . </S>",
    "<S> the resulting analysis provides a relatively simple diagnostic for use in connection with x - ray scattering measurements . </S>",
    "<S> applications are given to dense hydrogen , beryllium , aluminum and titanium plasmas . </S>",
    "<S> in the case of titanium , bound states are predicted to modify the spectrum significantly .    </S>"
