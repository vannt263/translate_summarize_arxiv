    "<S> quark gluon plasma ( qgp ) state is expected to be formed in the relativistic heavy ion collisions . in order to identify the plasma or a de - confined state </S>",
    "<S> , one needs to study the physical processes in quark matter , that can distinctly and conclusively identify this state of matter , such as parton energy loss leading to jet suppression mechanism . in this context , electromagnetic processes such as photons and dilepton emission are also considered as important signals . </S>",
    "<S> photons and dileptons are emitted at various stages during plasma evolution , for an overview one may see @xcite and the references therein . in depth </S>",
    "<S> study of photon emission processes in quark - gluon plasma were reported @xcite including processes also from hot hadron gas @xcite . in the formalism of hard thermal loops @xcite ( htl ) </S>",
    "<S> effective theory , the processes of bremsstrahlung @xcite and a crossed process of off - shell annihilation called @xmath13 @xcite contribute to photon emission at the two loop level . </S>",
    "<S> these two processes contribute at the leading order @xmath14 owing to the collinear singularity that is regularized by the effective thermal masses . </S>",
    "<S> higher loop multiple scatterings having a ladder topology also contribute at the same order as the one and two loop processes @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> these higher loop rescatterings , each giving finite decoherent correction to the two loop processes , need to be resummed . </S>",
    "<S> this resummation effectively implements the landau - pomarenchk - migdal ( lpm ) effects @xcite arising due to rescattering of quarks in the medium during the photon formation time . </S>",
    "<S> this results in an integral equation of a transverse vector function for the real photons . </S>",
    "<S> the photon emission rates are suppressed owing to the lpm effects @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the lpm modification of the photon spectrum is important at very low photon energies or at high photon energies . </S>",
    "<S> for example , the bremsstrahlung radiation is strongly suppressed by almost @xmath15 at very low @xmath16 values , whereas the photon emission from @xmath17 falls strongly for higher @xmath16 values @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> thus the rescattering corrections modify the two loop contributions for bremsstrahlung and @xmath3 processes at opposite ends of the photon spectrum for real photons . </S>"
