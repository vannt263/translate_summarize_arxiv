    "metals become superconducting , when their electrons form singlet cooper pairs via an attractive interaction . on the other hand , electrons in metals",
    "can also undergo another type of singlet formation , namely to form a kondo screening cloud , when magnetic impurities are present . the fascinating interplay of cooper pair and kondo singlet formation @xcite",
    "can be microscopically observed when magnetic atoms or molecules are adsorbed on superconducting surfaces .",
    "the ground state of such a combined system has been predicted to be either a kondo screened singlet state ( @xmath0 ) , if the kondo scale @xmath1 is much larger than the superconducting pairing energy @xmath2 , or an unscreened multiplet state ( @xmath3 ) for @xmath4 .",
    "characteristic features include subgap states with bound state energies @xmath5 - often called shiba states .",
    "for the singlet ground state with screened impurity spin , the bound states are @xmath3 excitations at @xmath6 , where the kondo singlet is broken , and for the multiplet ground state the bound states with @xmath7 are singlet excitations including kondo screening .",
    "when the energy scale for kondo singlet formation becomes smaller and comparable with the cooper pairing energy , @xmath8 , the bound state energies at @xmath5 go to @xmath9 , and the @xmath3 state becomes the ground state . at this point",
    "a quantum phase transition ( qpt ) occurs .",

    "manganese - phthalocyanine ( mnpc ) molecules on a pb(111 ) substrate form a moire - like superstructure .",
    "tunneling spectroscopy on different mn sites reveals two kondo screening channels and pairs of bound states of varying energy @xmath5 .",

    "<S> magnetic molecules adsorbed on a superconductor give rise to a local competition of cooper pair and kondo singlet formation inducing subgap bound states . for manganese - phthalocyanine molecules on a pb(111 ) substrate , scanning tunneling spectroscopy resolves pairs of subgap bound states and two kondo screening channels . </S>",
    "<S> we show in a combined approach of scaling and numerical renormalization group calculations that the intriguing relation between kondo screening and superconducting pairing is solely determined by the hybridization strength with the substrate . </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate that an effective one - channel anderson impurity model with a sizable particle - hole asymmetry captures universal and non - universal observations in the system quantitatively . </S>",
    "<S> the model parameters and disentanglement of the two screening channels are elucidated by scaling arguments . </S>"
