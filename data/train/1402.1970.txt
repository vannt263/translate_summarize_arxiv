    "<S> a few years ago we identified a recursion that works directly with the gaps among the generators in each stage of eratosthenes sieve . </S>",
    "<S> this recursion provides explicit enumerations of sequences of gaps among the generators , which are known as constellations .    </S>",
    "<S> as the recursion proceeds , adjacent gaps within longer constellations are added together to produce shorter constellations of the same sum . </S>",
    "<S> these additions or closures correspond to removing composite numbers that are divisible by the prime for that stage of eratosthenes sieve . </S>",
    "<S> although we do nt know where in the cycle of gaps a closure will occur , we can enumerate exactly how many copies of various constellations will survive each stage .    in this paper , we broaden our study of these systems of constellations of a fixed sum . by generalizing our methods , </S>",
    "<S> we are able to demonstrate that for every even number @xmath0 the gap @xmath1 occurs infinitely often through the stages of eratosthenes sieve . moreover , </S>",
    "<S> we show that asymptotically the ratio of the number of gaps @xmath1 to the number of gaps @xmath2 at each stage of eratosthenes sieve converges to the estimates made for gaps among primes by hardy and littlewood in conjecture b of their 1923 paper . </S>"
