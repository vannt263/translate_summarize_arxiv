    "it is a remarkable fact  @xcite that , under the conventional parameter renormalization in gauge theory , a simple one - parameter evolution of bare gauge and bare fermion fields by flow equations in  refs .",
    "@xcite renders composite operators of these fields renormalized .",
    "see also ref .",
    "@xcite for an earlier study .",
    "because of this renormalization property , the flow can be employed in lattice gauge theory for instance to set a mass scale , to define a non - perturbative running coupling , and to define the topological charge . refs .",
    "@xcite are reviews and ref .",
    "@xcite provides a pedestrian proof of the above renormalization property and an extensive list of recent related works .",
    "although any composite operator of fields evolved by the flow ( which we term `` flowed fields '' in what follows ) is always a renormalized one , how to relate it to desired renormalized quantities in the original gauge theory is a different issue .",
    "one possible rather versatile method to do this is the small flow - time expansion  @xcite .",
    "the flow is parametrized by the flow - time  @xmath2 and the small flow - time expansion expresses for  @xmath3 a composite operator of flowed fields by an asymptotic series of local operators . since the flow - time  @xmath4 possesses the mass dimension  @xmath5 , the expansion is a series of local operators of increasing mass dimensions .",
    "on the basis of this small flow - time expansion , representations of renormalized noether currents , such as the energy  momentum tensor  @xcite and the flavor non - singlet axial vector current  @xcite , in terms of flowed fields have been constructed . since composite operators of flowed fields",
    "are renormalized ones and independent of the regularization , those representations are thought to be useful in lattice gauge theory .",
    "the validity of the representation of the energy ",
    "momentum tensor has been numerically examined in  refs .",
