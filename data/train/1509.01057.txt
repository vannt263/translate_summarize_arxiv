    "<S> a semiconductor quantum dot can generate highly coherent and indistinguishable single photons . </S>",
    "<S> however , intrinsic semiconductor dephasing mechanisms can reduce the visibility of two - photon interference . for an electron in a quantum dot , a fundamental dephasing process is the hyperfine interaction with the nuclear spin bath . here </S>",
    "<S> we directly probe the consequence of the fluctuating nuclear spins on the elastic and inelastic scattered photon spectra from a resident electron in a single dot . </S>",
    "<S> we find the nuclear spin fluctuations lead to detuned raman scattered photons which are distinguishable from both the elastic and incoherent components of the resonance fluorescence . </S>",
    "<S> this significantly reduces two - photon interference visibility . however , we demonstrate successful screening of the nuclear spin noise which enables the generation of coherent single photons that exhibit high visibility two - photon interference .            </S>",
    "<S> indistinguishable single photons are an essential resource for quantum photonic logic gates and networking  @xcite . among the various approaches to generate identical light quanta , resonance fluorescence ( rf ) from a semiconductor quantum dot ( qd )  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite is one of the most promising for practical technological implementation . </S>",
    "<S> the rf spectrum is composed of elastic and inelastic scattered light  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the elastic or rayleigh scattered light , first measured in homodyne absorption experiments on a qd  @xcite , has the first order coherence properties of the laser but the second order coherence properties of the emitter  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this light , which can be imprinted with an arbitrary phase or temporal profile  @xcite , is fundamentally indistinguishable . </S>",
    "<S> rf can therefore relax the requirement for a perfectly stable , transform - limited optical transition that is difficult to realize in the solid - state . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , compared to non - resonant excitation followed by spontaneous emission , rf helps overcome the relatively slow spectral fluctuations caused by charge noise in the qd environment  @xcite .        </S>",
    "<S> in addition to the elastic and incoherent components of rf , near resonant excitation can lead to raman scattering into another ground state . </S>",
    "<S> raman scattering from a qd with a resident electron or hole can have several attractive features . </S>",
    "<S> first , below saturation , the coherence of the raman photons is determined by the ground state dephasing rather than that of the excited state  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> second , raman photons are highly tunable : their energy is determined by the detuning of the driving field rather than the fundamental optical transition energy  @xcite . finally , due to clean selection rules in a qd , the polarization of the raman scattered photons can be linked to the spin of the final state  @xcite , enabling spin - photon entanglement  @xcite and raising the prospect for quantum networks  @xcite . typically , spin - flip raman photons are generated using an in - plane external magnetic field ( @xmath0 ) to mix the spin states  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> a iii - v qd also contains an intrinsic effective magnetic field , the overhauser field ( @xmath1 ) , due to coupling of the qd s constituent atoms nuclear spins with the electron spin via the contact part of the hyperfine interaction . </S>",
