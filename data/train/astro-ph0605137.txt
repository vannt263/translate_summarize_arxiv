    "abundance studies of heavy elements and isotopes in galactic halo stars are providing important clues and insights into the nature of , and sites for , nucleosynthesis early in the history of the galaxy ( sneden & cowan 2003 ; cowan & thielemann 2004 ; cowan & sneden 2006 ) .",
    "observations of heavy neutron - capture elements ( _ i.e. _ , those produced in the slow ( @xmath0 ) or rapid ( @xmath1)-process ) in both old ( low iron , or metallicity ) stars and younger ( more metal - rich ) stars are also providing clear indications of the nature of the galactic chemical evolution .",
    "in addition the detection of the long - lived radioactive @xmath2-capture elements , such as thorium and uranium , are allowing direct age determinations for the oldest stars in the galaxy - thus , providing lower limits on both the age of the galaxy and the universe .",
    "all of these abundance determinations and studies depend critically upon atomic data , particularly transition probabilities . during the past few years",
    "there have been dramatic improvements in the experimental determinations of these transition probabilities for a number of rare earth elements : these include improved laboratory values for the elements ce ( palmeri  2000 ) , nd ( den hartog  2003 ) , ho ( lawler  2004 ) , pt ( den hartog  2005 ) , and sm ( lawler  2006 ) .",
    "new values for the element gd have also recently been obtained ( den hartog  2006 ) , but gd abundances in the current paper do not reflect these new data .",
    "these improved data sets have been employed to determine elemental abundances in three metal - poor galactic halo stars .",
    "in addition new ( refined ) solar photospheric abundances have been obtained for nd , ho and sm - this was not possible , however , for pt .",

    "it is clear in both the top and bottom panels of figure [ fig2 ] that the abundances of the stable elements ba and above are consistent with the scaled solar system elemental @xmath1-process distribution .",
    "this agreement has been seen in other @xmath1-process - rich stars and strongly suggests that the @xmath1-process is robust over the history of the galaxy .",
    "it further demonstrates that early in the history of the galaxy , all of the @xmath2-capture elements were synthesized in the @xmath1-process , and not the @xmath0-process .",
    "additional comparisons in the figure , however , suggest the lighter elements do not fall on this same curve that fits the heavier @xmath2-capture elements .",

    "recent analyses of this element indicates its abundance is tied to the iron level in metal - poor halo stars ( cowan  2005 ) , and thus suggests ge synthesis in some type of charged - particle reactions , or other primary process , in massive stars and supernovae in the early galaxy ( see discussion in cowan & sneden 2006 ) .",
