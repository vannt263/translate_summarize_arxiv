    "we derived a formula of the superconformal index for @xmath0 superconformal large @xmath1 quiver chern - simons theories . in the case of vector - like models",
    ", we confirmed the factorization of the index into the neutral , the positive , and the negative parts .",
    "for chiral theories , the factorization was confirmed only in the sector of diagonal monopole operators .",
    "we did not take account of non - diagonal monopole operators on the assumption that they are non - bps and do not contribute to the index . in this paper",
    ", we have not proved this on the gauge theory side .",
    "it would be important to analyze the contribution of non - diagonal operators systematically for understanding of the relation between non - diagonal monopole operators and wrapped m2-branes in ads@xmath9/cft@xmath10 .",
    "we applied the formula to some chern - simons theories which have been proposed as dual theories to m - theory in homogeneous spaces , @xmath2 , @xmath3 , @xmath4 , @xmath5 , and @xmath6 .",
    "the actions of these theories do not have full global symmetries which exist on the gravity side as isometries of the internal spaces .",
    "we computed the index for each theory up to the order of @xmath42 , and confirmed that it is invariant under the weyl group of the full symmetry expected from @xmath199 if we include the contribution of diagonal monopole operators .",
    "in other wards , the index is a linear combination of characters of the full symmetry .",
    "this result strongly suggests that the global symmetry of the chern - simons theory is enhanced to the full symmetry non - perturbatively .",
    "we emphasize that we only confirm the invariance of the index under the weyl group , and it does not guarantee the enhancement of the continuous symmetry . to obtain more convincing evidences of the symmetry enhancement",
    ", we need to compare the index to the result of kaluza - klein analysis on the gravity side .",
    "such an analysis is important especially for inhomogeneous sasaki - einstein spaces in which we have no symmetry enhancement in general .",
