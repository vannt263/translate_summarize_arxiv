    "<S> it is well known that the classical gauss sum , normalized by the square - root number of terms , takes only finitely many values . </S>",
    "<S> if one restricts the range of summation to a subinterval , a much richer structure emerges . </S>",
    "<S> we prove a limit law for the value distribution of such incomplete gauss sums . </S>",
    "<S> the limit distribution is given by the distribution of a certain family of periodic functions . </S>",
    "<S> our results complement oskolkov s pointwise bounds for incomplete gauss sums as well as the limit theorems for quadratic weyl sums ( theta sums ) due to jurkat and van horne and the second author . </S>"
