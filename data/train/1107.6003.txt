    "observations of type ia supernovae ( ea ) are a key measurement in determining the standard cosmological model . their empirical luminosity - distance calibration based on relations between a  peak luminosity and both light - curve width and optical colors",
    "@xcite provides evidence for the accelerated expansion of the universe and the existence of dark energy @xcite . according to the current theory , the progenitor of a a  is a carbon - oxygen white dwarf that approaches the chandrasekhar limit , resulting in a thermonuclear explosion @xcite .",
    "however , the exact mechanism by which the progenitor accumulates this mass remains uncertain .",
    "investigations of the physical properties of a  host galaxies can provide insight into the environment in which these progenitor systems form .",
    "furthermore , although ea  are remarkably standarizable , correcting for light - curve width and color still results in a scatter in peak brightness of @xmath3 mag @xcite .",
    "studying how variations in a  luminosities depend on the environment of the progenitor will help reveal the origin of this scatter .    over the years , several correlations between ea  and the properties of their progenitors and environments have been discovered .",
    "for example , intrinsically brighter ea  tend to occur in galaxies with younger stellar populations while fainter ones often occur in passively evolving galaxies @xcite .",
    "studies have also shown that per unit stellar mass , the rate of occurrence of ea  within a galaxy declines with decreasing sfr @xcite .",
    "in addition , properties of the progenitors themselves can directly influence light - curve properties of ea .",
    "theoretical models generally agree that the metallicity of the white dwarf progenitor affects the amount of radioactive @xmath4ni produced in the thermonuclear explosion , the decay of which powers the light curve of the  @xcite . assuming that global metallicity correlates with progenitor metallicity , @xcite presented qualitative evidence suggesting that it is more likely that progenitor age , rather than metallicity , is primarily responsible for the variability in a  peak luminosity .",
    "the true source of this variability has yet to be determined definitively .",
    "more recently , @xcite found that early - type host galaxy metallicity is correlated with residuals on the   hubble diagram around the best - fit cosmology .",
    "the galaxy mass - metallicity relationship @xcite has led several authors to investigate whether mass is a proxy for this metallicity trend with hubble residual ( hr ) .",

    ", we fit for a linear dependence of hr with age and mass using the package linmix @xcite , as was used to determine the significance of trends with hr by @xcite .",
    "linmix is a bayesian approach to linear regression using a markov chain monte carlo ( mcmc ) analysis , assuming that the measurement errors are gaussian .",
    "we make the assumption that our errors on the host properties are gaussian and input into linmix the average of the upper and lower @xmath62 uncertainties as the error in the dependent variable .",
    "when fitting , we do not add the intrinsic uncertainty ( 0.14 mag for ) in quadrature to the hr errors that is added by others when fitting for trends of host properties with hr @xcite .",
    "this intrinsic uncertainty arises from the fit to the hubble diagram and is the amount of scatter that must be added to the distance modulus such that the reduced @xmath16 of the best - fit cosmology is close to unity .",
    "this is done in an attempt to account for unknown effects on a  luminosity by factors not accounted for in the light - curve correction process , e.g. the properties of the host galaxy .",
    "the effect of host galaxy properties on a  is precisely the purpose of our study , so including the intrinsic scatter has the effect of weakening the strength of the measured correlations .",
