    "<S> we give evidence of a clear structural signature of the glass transition , in terms of a static correlation length with the same dependence on the system size which is typical of critical phenomena . </S>",
    "<S> our approach is to introduce an external , static perturbation to extract the structural information from the system s response . in particular , we consider the transformation behavior of the local minima of the underlying potential energy landscape ( inherent structures ) , under a static deformation . </S>",
    "<S> the finite - size scaling analysis of our numerical results indicate that the correlation length diverges at a temperature @xmath0 , below the temperatures where the system can be equilibrated . </S>",
    "<S> our numerical results are consistent with random first order theory , which predicts such a divergence with a critical exponent @xmath1 at the kauzmann temperature , where the extrapolated configurational entropy vanishes .    </S>",
    "<S> is there a static , structural origin of the dramatic slowing down of dynamics in supercooled liquids ? </S>",
    "<S> the physical mechanism of the dramatic slowing down and corresponding increase in viscosity upon approaching the glass transition has been vehemently debated for more than 40 years @xcite . in critical phenomena , the slowing down of the system s dynamics is intimately related to a nearby phase transition , where a diverging length scale typically reflects the onset of long - range correlation . </S>",
    "<S> several recent theories of the glass transition are built in this spirit @xcite and are supported from results on dielectric susceptibility @xcite and specific heat @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the few experimental studies , however , show only a moderate cooperativity length near the glass transition of some four to five particle diameters @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> specifically designed simulations @xcite have recently indicated a growing amorphous order in the range of four particle diameters . </S>",
    "<S> the results confirm , to some extent , the predictions of random first order theory ( rfot ) @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , no evidence for critical behavior with a diverging length scale could be found there . </S>",
    "<S> a growing correlation length is also indicated by a very recent numerical study of different local quantities @xcite and it is interpreted as the hint of a critical behavior , whose evidence , nevertheless , is still very elusive . </S>",
    "<S> the heterogeneous dynamics of glass forming liquids allow one to define a dynamical correlation length @xcite which is indeed growing as the glass transition is approached , but it remains unclear how , or whether at all , this dynamical length has a static , structural origin as is the case in phase transitions . </S>",
    "<S> a promising , alternative approach to supercooled liquids is based on inherent structures , i.e.  the local minima of the underlying potential energy landscape @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> although several qualitative changes in the system dynamics can be related to changes in their inherent structures @xcite , no length scale as significantly increasing as the dynamical correlation length has emerged so far @xcite .    following a nonequilibrium thermodynamic theory of glasses @xcite , </S>",
    "<S> here we use small , static deformations to perturb the inherent structure configurations of supercooled liquids approaching the glass transition @xcite . with this procedure , </S>",
    "<S> large correlated regions emerge at low temperatures and allow us to detect a static correlation length that shows critical behavior upon approaching the glass transition . _ </S>",

    "<S> _ discussion . </S>",
    "<S> _  bringing together concepts from inherent structure formalism of supercooled liquids and from deformations of amorphous solids offers a new perspective on the long - standing glass problem . in this spirit , we have employed a new method in order to elucidate growing static correlations in supercooled liquids . </S>",
    "<S> we found direct evidence for the critical behavior of the static correlation length close to the glass transition , which has been speculated by several recent theories @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , our estimates of the transition temperature , critical exponents , as well as the link with relaxational dynamics are in quantitative agreement with the predictions from rfot @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however further studies are needed in order to determine the numerical values more precisely . </S>",
    "<S> our results have important consequences for flow - induced rearrangements in colloidal glasses @xcite and elastic response of amorphous solids @xcite , where similar cooperative behavior has been observed recently .    </S>",
    "<S> our study has been performed at constant density , where the critical temperature @xmath92 extracted from @xmath51 is apparently below the lowest temperature at which we have been able to equilibrate the system . </S>",
    "<S> however , the critical point would not necessarily be located at the density chosen here and only a systematic investigation in the @xmath93-plane will shed further light on its existence , nature , and location . </S>",
    "<S> the long - standing problem of a true thermodynamic phase transition underlying the slowing down of the dynamics at the glass transition can now be addressed from a different perspective , knowing that a direct structural signature exists . </S>"
