    "<S> transport measurements are powerful tools to probe electronic properties of solid - state materials . to access properties of local electronic states in nanostructures , such as local density of states , electronic distribution and so on , micro - probes utilizing artificial nanostructures have been invented to perform measurements in addition to those with conventional macroscopic electronic reservoirs . </S>",
    "<S> here we demonstrate a new kind of micro - probe : a fast single - lead quantum dot probe , which utilizes a quantum dot coupled only to the target structure through a tunneling barrier and fast charge readout by rf reflectometry . </S>",
    "<S> the probe can directly access the local electronic states with wide bandwidth . </S>",
    "<S> the probe can also access more electronic states , not just those around the fermi level , and the operations are robust against bias voltages and temperatures .    </S>",
    "<S> new kinds of structures based on solid - state nanostructures have been proposed to realize functional devices . </S>",
    "<S> for example , spintronics devices utilizing spin effects  @xcite and quantum information - processing devices utilizing quantum effects in nanostructures  @xcite have been proposed and studied intensively . in these new devices , </S>",
    "<S> local electronic states play important roles and their understanding on a microscopic basis is crucial .    </S>",
    "<S> transport measurements are one of the most powerful tools to probe electronic properties of nanostructures . in conventional transport measurements , macroscopic probes , electronic reservoirs that contain huge ensembles of electrons , </S>",
    "<S> are coupled to the target system and the flow of electrons is measured to probe electronic properties  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> using such macroscopic electronic reservoirs brings some constraints in the measurement of nanostructures : direct access to small local regions is not easy , the transport is limited to only around the fermi level , the measurement is greatly affected by change of the electronic distribution in the reservoir , for example caused by bias voltages or electron temperatures , and the measurement is usually slow because of the large geometric capacitances of the leads . </S>",
    "<S> to overcome these restrictions is a strong challenge in transport measurements .    </S>",
    "<S> one possible solution to this challenge requires microscopic probes utilizing nanostructures instead of macroscopic reservoirs . the use of semiconductor quantum dots ( qds ) in such probes has been demonstrated . </S>",
    "<S> qds have well defined inner quantum levels that can be controlled by applying voltages on gate electrodes  @xcite . by measuring the transport through these artificial quantum levels , </S>",
    "<S> we directly access local electronic states . </S>",
    "<S> this can not be realized with conventional macroscopic probes . </S>",
    "<S> for example , measurements of local electronic states , energy relaxation and heat transport in quantum hall edge states have been demonstrated by utilizing qds  @xcite .    in this paper , we realize a new kind of qd probe : a fast single - lead quantum dot ( slqd ) probe . </S>",
    "<S> an slqd is a qd , which couples to a target system through a single tunneling barrier  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> we can probe more states , not only those around the fermi level , with robustness against change of the electronic distribution because the slqd is fully isolated from electronic reservoirs . </S>",
    "<S> also , we can improve the operation time of the probe because the geometric capacitance of the slqd is small and by utilizing rf reflectometry techniques  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> first , we realized the fast slqd probe and evaluated its operation speed . </S>",
    "<S> second , we applied the probe to detect local electronic states in another qd , which is used as a controllable target nanostructure in this experiment . by measuring tunneling events of electrons between the probe slqd and the target qd , we confirmed the operation of the new probe . </S>",
    "<S> additionally , we demonstrated its key features . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we show that this probe can conduct fast real - time measurements of local electronic states . </S>"
