    "the @xmath0 heisenberg model on a square lattice is the well  known basic model in the quantum theory of magnetism which became especially attractive after the discovery of high  temperature superconductors @xcite .",
    "much more materials which can be viewed as realizations of the 2d @xmath0 heisenberg model are known at present time .",
    "many analytical and numerical studies analyzing thermodynamics , spin correlations and their dynamics for the 2d @xmath0 heisenberg model were performed during the last fifteen years .",
    "one of the ( approximate ) analytical approaches is based on the 2d jordan  wigner fermionization .",
    "an extension of the 1d jordan  wigner transformation for higher dimensions was suggested in the late 80s @xcite ( see also @xcite ) .",
    "such kind of approximate treatment was applied to the square ",
    "lattice quantum spin models in several papers @xcite ( for a brief review see @xcite ) .",
    "recently , the 2d jordan  wigner mapping has been used for the calculation of the magnetization curves of several 2d spin systems @xcite .",
    "the reported studies @xcite deal with the @xmath0 isotropic heisenberg or the @xmath0 isotropic @xmath1 models and the effects of anisotropic @xmath1 exchange interaction were not considered .",
    "another intriguing problem in the theory of magnetism is the influence of the spatial anisotropy on the properties of quantum spin models between the limiting cases of a system of noninteracting chains and of a spatially isotropic 2d system .",
    "the issue of disappearing of long  range order in 1d limit although discussed by several authors ( see , for example , the papers @xcite on the crossover from one to two dimensions for the @xmath0 heisenberg model ) remains ambiguous .    in this paper",
    "we consider the @xmath0 anisotropic @xmath1 model on a spatially anisotropic square lattice using the 2d jordan  wigner fermionization approach in order 1 ) to examine the effects of exchange interaction anisotropy and the effects of spatial anisotropy on the ground  state and thermodynamic properties of the spin model and 2 ) to gain an understanding how well this technique works comparing the approximate results with the exact ones ( 1d limit , the onsager solution for the square  lattice ising model ) . to examine the quality of the 2d jordan  wigner fermionization scheme in the cases for which exact results are not available we perform linear spin  wave theory calculations and finite  size scaling analysis of exact diagonalization data .",

    "to summarize , we have extended the 2d jordan  wigner fermionization approach for the _ anisotropic _ square  lattice @xmath0",
    "@xmath1 model . in our study of the ground ",
    "state properties we have concentrated on the effects of i ) exchange interaction anisotropy and ii ) spatial anisotropy .",
    "we have analyzed 1 ) the quantum phase transition driven by the exchange interaction anisotropy and 2 ) appearance / disappearance of the long  range order in quasi1d limit .",
    "we have discussed these issues using different approaches : 2d jordan  wigner fermionization , linear spin  wave theory and exact diagonalization data supplemented by finite ",
    "size scaling .",
    "the study of the anisotropy driven changes extends the the earlier papers @xcite suggesting the first ",
    "order quantum phase transition scenario .",
    "the study of the 2d  to1d crossover constitutes the @xmath1 counterpart of the problem intensively discussed within the context of the heisenberg model ( @xcite and references therein ) . in our study of the temperature effects we have discussed to what extent the jordan  wigner fermions can reproduce the peculiarities of the specific heat for the square ",
    "lattice ising and isotropic @xmath1 models as well as the relation to the mermin  wagner theorem .",
    "we have found that the 2d jordan  wigner fermionization for the @xmath1 hamiltonian works well in quasi1d limit ( especially as @xmath168 ) for calculating both the ground  state and thermodynamic quantities .",
