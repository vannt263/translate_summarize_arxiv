    "initial follow - up observations identified many of the sub - mjy sources with blue , often morphologically disturbed , galaxies @xcite .",
    "optical spectroscopy has revealed a large fraction of galaxies with spectra indicative of star formation ( @xmath6 ) , similar to nearby galaxies detected by the infrared astronomical satellite ( iras ) , and extending to @xmath7 @xcite .",
    "satisfactory models of the faint radio source counts require the inclusion of an evolving population of such star - forming galaxies @xcite in addition to the agn population .",
    "interest in the sub - mjy population has increased significantly in recent years .",

    "secondly , there is some overlap between the still mysterious sub - millimeter galaxy population , possibly dominated by dusty star - forming galaxies at high redshifts , and very faint ( s@xmath9jy ) radio galaxies @xcite .",
    "the radio emission of these sub - mm sources is often the best available clue in refining the source position to allow optical identification @xcite .",
    "it also provides an initial , if coarse , redshift estimate @xcite , which may be the only such estimate , since optical and near - infrared fluxes are often too faint for detection .    despite the potential of the faint radio population for cosmological studies ,",
