    "to the attempts at discrediting the usefulness of the l  t model ( or more general ones ) for cosmology , one can give a philosophical answer : objects existing in nature do not fulfil mathematical assumptions with perfect precision .",
    "assumptions such as spherical symmetry , axial symmetry , isolated body , free fall , ideal gas , incompressible fluid , are in reality fulfilled only up to some degree of approximation",
    ". why should the universe be an exception and arise in an exactly simultaneous big bang , when the theory allows the bb to be extended in ( comoving ) time ?",
    "anticipating more general solutions of einstein s equations , one should even expect the most general bb time to be a function of all three spatial variables , possibly limited in generality by the constraint equations .",
