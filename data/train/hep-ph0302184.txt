    "hard partons produced in nucleus - nucleus collisions at rhic and lhc propagate through highly excited matter before hadronizing in the vacuum . the resulting medium - dependence of parton fragmentation",
    "is expected to affect hadronic observables .",
    "this is of two - fold interest .",
    "first , it provides a novel test of the space - time evolution of the perturbative parton shower .",
    "second , the modification of hadronic observables due to the spatially extended , hot and dense qcd matter allows to characterize the properties of the transient state produced in the collision .    gluon emission off highly virtual hard partons is an essential component in the standard description of parton fragmentation in elementary processes .",
    "this effect degrades the energy of the leading parton .",
    "recently , it has been proposed  @xcite that in the presence of a spatially extended medium , the additional medium - induced energy degradation of the leading parton can be described by a probability @xmath3 , the so - called quenching weight , which is obtained from a probabilistic iteration of the medium - modified elementary splitting processes @xmath4 and @xmath5 . the main purpose of the present work is to calculate and compare this quenching weight for different approximations of the medium - modified splitting process , to make the results for @xmath3 available as a numerical subroutine , and to illustrate the use of this subroutine with some applications .",
    "we start from recent calculations  @xcite of the modification of the elementary splitting processes @xmath4 and @xmath5 due to multiple scattering .",
    "these results go under the name medium - induced gluon radiation .",

    "<S> we calculate the probability ( `` quenching weight '' ) that a hard parton radiates an _ additional _ energy fraction @xmath0 due to scattering in spatially extended qcd matter . </S>",
    "<S> this study is based on an exact treatment of finite in - medium path length , it includes the case of a dynamically expanding medium , and it extends to the angular dependence of the medium - induced gluon radiation pattern . </S>",
    "<S> all calculations are done in the multiple soft scattering approximation ( baier - dokshitzer - mueller - peign - schiff  zakharov `` bdmps - z''-formalism ) and in the single hard scattering approximation ( @xmath1 opacity approximation ) . by comparison , we establish a simple relation between transport coefficient , debye screening mass and opacity , for which both approximations lead to comparable results . </S>",
    "<S> together with this paper , a cpu - inexpensive numerical subroutine for calculating quenching weights is provided electronically . to illustrate its applications </S>",
    "<S> , we discuss the suppression of hadronic transverse momentum spectra in nucleus - nucleus collisions . </S>",
    "<S> remarkably , the kinematic constraint resulting from finite in - medium path length reduces significantly the @xmath2-dependence of the nuclear modification factor , thus leading to consistency with the data measured at the relativistic heavy ion collider ( rhic ) .    </S>",
