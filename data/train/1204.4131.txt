    "<S> * interfacing single photons and electrons is a crucial ingredient for sharing quantum information between remote solid - state qubits @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> semiconductor nanowires offer the unique possibility to combine optical quantum dots with avalanche photodiodes , thus enabling the conversion of an incoming single photon into a macroscopic current for efficient electrical detection . </S>",
    "<S> currently , millions of excitation events are required to perform electrical read - out of an exciton qubit state @xcite . here </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate multiplication of carriers from only a single exciton generated in a quantum dot after tunneling into a nanowire avalanche photodiode . due to the large amplification of both electrons and holes ( @xmath0 ) , </S>",
    "<S> we reduce by four orders of magnitude the number of excitation events required to electrically detect a single exciton generated in a quantum dot . </S>",
    "<S> this work represents a significant step towards single - shot electrical read - out and offers a new functionality for on - chip quantum information circuits . </S>",
    "<S> *    semiconductor quantum dots have been proposed as fundamental elements of a quantum information processor @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> quantum bits ( qubits ) can be stored in either the spin state of a single electron @xcite , the spin state of a single hole @xcite , the dark exciton state @xcite or the bright exciton state @xcite . among these , </S>",
    "<S> exciton qubits are particularly attractive for long distance quantum communication through the exciton - to - photon and photon - to - exciton transfer of quantum information @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> coherent optical control of a single exciton qubit in quantum dots has been demonstrated with both optical @xcite and electrical @xcite read - out . </S>",
    "<S> however , current read - out schemes demand millions of excitation events in order to produce a measurable signal . to reduce the number of excitation events towards single - shot measurements , </S>",
    "<S> an internal pre - amplification of the electrical signal would be desirable . </S>",
    "<S> avalanche photodiodes ( apd ) ensure this high internal gain and generate a macroscopic current in response to a single photon absorbed @xcite . as a result , </S>",
    "<S> single photons can be efficiently detected @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the avalanche process has been shown at the nano - scale using semiconductor nanowires @xcite and carbon nanotubes @xcite , with sensitivity limited to , at best , approximately 100 photons . </S>",
    "<S> importantly , semiconductor nanowires grown via bottom - up techniques offer an unprecedented material freedom in growing advanced heterostructures @xcite due to reduced strain achieved in the growth of highly mismatched materials @xcite , and can additionally include hetero - structured @xcite or electrically defined @xcite quantum dots for quantum information processing .    </S>",
