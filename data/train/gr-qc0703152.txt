    "we will now consider the three most commonly used models used to characterise the toa of pulses from pulsars in binary systems .",
    "the blandford and teukolsky model @xcite ( bt ) makes no assumptions about the correct theory of gravity .",
    "instead , it assumes a simple keplerian orbit with slow precession , into which additional relativistic effects have been added .",
    "other phenomena can be taken into account through time derivatives of the four main orbital elements , excluding @xmath38 .",
    "the bt model has been used to fit data for 53 of the binary pulsars with @xmath52hz , and is the most common model used .",
    "exceptionally , one of these systems is modelled using the tempo  model bt2p which accommodates three orbits , the first of which can be relativistic , but the second and third are keplerian .",
    "the system is a multiple system , described in ref .",
    "@xcite , in which three , or possibly four , planets orbit the pulsar .",
    "although these additional orbits complicate the above equations , the standard bt model is sufficient for our purposes .",
    "the second most common model used in fitting radio observations of binaries is the low eccentricity model ( called ell1 in tempo ) developed in ref .",
    "it is used as a fit for pulsars in very low eccentricity orbits ( @xmath53 ) and is the appropriate model for 38 of our target pulsars with @xmath54hz .",
    "an almost circular orbit makes @xmath38 and @xmath39 nearly degenerate , so these parameters , along with @xmath41 , are replaced with the non - covariant parameters of the time of the ascending node of the orbit ( @xmath55 ) and the first and second laplace - lagrange parameters @xmath56 and @xmath57 .",
    "the time delays for this model are defined in @xcite and tempo .",
    "the third most common model is that of damour and deruelle ( dd ) @xcite . this model uses a method for solving the relativistic two - body problem to post - newtonian order and is valid under very general assumptions about the nature of gravity in strong field regimes .",
