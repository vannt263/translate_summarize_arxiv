    "quantum monte carlo ( qmc ) methods are among the most established and powerful tools to solve the quantum many - body problem of correlated electrons . in particular ,",
    "the auxiliary - field qmc method @xcite and the stochastic series expansion ( sse ) representation @xcite are widely used to simulate lattice models , whereas more recent continuous - time ( ctqmc ) methods @xcite are predominantly applied as impurity solvers in dynamical mean - field theory ( dmft ) @xcite .",
    "recently , progress has been made in the development of new methods to simulate fermionic lattice models @xcite , the solution of the fermionic sign problem for specific models @xcite , and the calculation of novel observables such as the entanglement entropy @xcite and the fidelity susceptibility @xcite .    for a large class of qmc methods ( e.g. , sse and ctqmc ) the partition function",
    "is calculated stochastically in a series expansion and operators that are sampled can be measured directly from the monte carlo configurations . in this article , we consider the continuous - time interaction expansion ( ct - int ) method @xcite . in ct - int ,",
    "the configurations are sets of interaction vertices and expectation values are usually calculated from the single - particle green s function using wick s theorem @xcite .",
    "however , it can be advantageous to exploit the information contained in the distribution of vertices , an important example being the fidelity susceptibility @xcite .",
    "the action - based formulation of the ct - int method in particular allows efficient simulations of fermion - boson lattice models @xcite , and has been successfully applied to electron - phonon problems @xcite .",
    "if the action is quadratic in the bosonic fields the latter can be integrated out exactly @xcite , resulting in a fermionic action with retarded interactions .",
    "remarkably , autocorrelations  which can be prohibitively strong in cases where the bosons are sampled explicitly @xcite  are significantly reduced in the fermionic representation .",
    "an apparent disadvantage of the fermionic approach is the loss of access to bosonic observables .",
    "however , as shown here , the latter can be systematically calculated from fermionic correlation functions using sum rules derived from generating functionals .",
    "information about the bosonic fields is also encoded in the distribution of vertices . for a local fermion - boson interaction ( e.g. , the holstein model @xcite ) the bosonic contributions to the total energy as well as the local bosonic propagator",
    "can be calculated efficiently from the vertex distribution . moreover , with the help of auxiliary ising fields @xcite originally introduced to avoid the sign problem @xcite , even nonlocal correlation functions such as the full bosonic propagator become accessible .",
    "similar techniques have been applied to solve fermion - boson problems with dmft and the hybridization expansion ( ct - hyb ) method @xcite to understand dynamical screening effects @xcite , and in extended dmft calculations @xcite .",
    "finally , we derive an estimator for the fidelity susceptibility applicable to retarded boson - mediated interactions .",
    "the improved estimators are applied to one - dimensional holstein models @xcite .",
    "these fundamental models for electron - phonon interaction constitute a significant numerical challenge due to the infinite phonon hilbert space , and the different time scales for the fermion and boson dynamics . in the half - filled case considered here ,",
    "they describe a quantum phase transition from a metallic phase to a peierls insulator with long - range charge - density - wave order @xcite .",
    "we study the renormalization of the phonon spectrum in the adiabatic regime , and consider the phonon kinetic energy and the fidelity susceptibility as diagnostics for the detection of the critical point .",

    "the peierls quantum phase transition in half - filled spinful holstein and holstein - hubbard models has been studied with a number of numerical techniques , see ref .",
    "@xcite for a review . while early qmc results @xcite suggested the absence of a metallic phase , more recent work has established a phase transition at a nonzero critical value @xmath196 @xcite in accordance with functional renormalization group results @xcite",
    ". however , the exact determination of the phase boundary , as well as the characterization of the metallic phase in terms of luttinger liquid parameters remain open problems @xcite .",
    "the difficulties are associated with the berezinskii - kosterlitz - thouless ( bkt ) nature of the transition  so that gaps are exponentially small near @xmath196and a small but nonzero spin gap caused by attractive backscattering which is hard to resolve numerically @xcite . in particular , the spin gap renders the previously used charge susceptibility @xcite essentially useless for detecting long - range charge order @xcite .",
    "in contrast , no such complications are encountered for the spinless holstein model .",

    "the ct - int quantum monte carlo method is particularly useful to simulate fermion - boson models because the bosons can be integrated out . while advantageous for simulations , this integration makes it nontrivial to calculate expectation values of bosonic variables . here , we obtained estimators for arbitrary bosonic correlation functions using generating functionals . as a concrete example , we derived sum rules for the total energy and the phonon propagator of the holstein model . moreover , we showed that several observables of interest can be measured directly from the vertex distribution instead of using wick s theorem . additionally , we generalized the qmc estimator for the fidelity susceptibility @xcite to retarded boson - mediated interactions .",
    "a comparison of different observables and simulation parameters showed that statistical errors are of the same order for the vertex estimators and the estimators based on wick s theorem .",
    "the vertex estimators are easy to implement , more efficient , and often avoid systematic errors from numerical integration .",
    "our results are general and can be applied to a variety of other lattice fermion - bosons models .",
    "for example , the possibility to calculate the total energy provides access to the specific heat . moreover , the calculation of the charge susceptibility from the auxiliary ising spins may be advantageous to detect charge order in higher dimensions or in hubbard - type models .",
    "these methodological developments were applied to one - dimensional spinless and spinful holstein models describing charge carriers coupled to quantum phonons .",
    "the phonon kinetic energy was found to exhibit a minimum in the vicinity of the critical point of the peierls metal - insulator transition .",
    "the minimum was related to the renormalization of the phonon mode . for intermediate phonon frequencies ,",
    "its position is consistent with estimates of the critical point .",
    "the phonon spectral function calculated from the phonon momentum correlator revealed the hardening of the phonon mode in the peierls phase , which allowed us to demonstrate that the peierls transition is of the soft - mode type .",
    "finally , we also presented results for the fidelity susceptibility .",
    "for both the spinless and the spinful holstein model , the latter exhibits a broad maximum at intermediate coupling and significant finite - size effects . while the fidelity susceptibility does not provide more accurate critical values in the one - dimensional case considered , the qualitatively similar behavior observed for the spinless and the spinful model may be regarded as additional evidence for an extended metallic phase in the spinful holstein model .",

    "<S> we extend the continuous - time interaction - expansion quantum monte carlo method with respect to measuring observables for fermion - boson lattice models . using generating functionals , </S>",
    "<S> we express expectation values involving boson operators , which are not directly accessible because simulations are done in terms of a purely fermionic action , as integrals over fermionic correlation functions . </S>",
    "<S> we also demonstrate that certain observables can be inferred directly from the vertex distribution , and present efficient estimators for the total energy and the phonon propagator of the holstein model . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , we generalize the covariance estimator of the fidelity susceptibility to retarded interactions . </S>",
    "<S> the new estimators are applied to half - filled spinless and spinful holstein models in one dimension . </S>",
    "<S> the observed renormalization of the phonon mode across the peierls transition in the spinless model suggests a soft - mode transition in the adiabatic regime . </S>",
    "<S> the critical point is associated with a minimum in the phonon kinetic energy and a maximum in the fidelity susceptibility . </S>"
