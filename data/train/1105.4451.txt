    "<S> quasi - two - dimensional superconductors with tunable spin - orbit coupling are very interesting systems with properties that are also potentially useful for applications . in this letter we demonstrate that these systems exhibit undamped collective spin oscillations that can be excited by the application of a supercurrent . </S>",
    "<S> we propose to use these collective excitations to realize persistent spin oscillators operating in the frequency range of @xmath0 .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction . </S>",
    "<S>  _ spin - orbit - coupled two - dimensional ( 2d ) electron gases ( egs ) are the focus of great interest in the field of semiconductor spintronics  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this interest has been largely fueled by the hope to realize the visionary datta - das  spin transistor \"  @xcite in which the on / off state is achieved by purely - electrical control of the electron s spin in a spin - orbit - coupled semiconductor channel placed between ferromagnetic leads . </S>",
    "<S> research in spin - orbit - coupled 2degs has been recently revitalized by theoretical  @xcite and experimental  @xcite studies of the spin hall effect , in which a current traversing the sample generates a spin - current in the orthogonal direction .    </S>",
    "<S> the study of the interplay between spin - orbit coupling ( soc ) and superconductivity in 2d systems , stemming from the seminal works of edelstein  @xcite and gorkov and rashba  @xcite , has also gained impetus  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> there is a large variety of systems in which soc and superconductivity coexist : two examples of great current interest are i ) 2degs in inas or gaas semiconductor heterostructures that are proximized by ordinary s - wave superconducting leads  @xcite  a class of systems which plays a key role in the quest for majorana fermions  @xcite  and ii ) 2degs that form at interfaces between complex oxides  @xcite , such as @xmath1 and @xmath2 , which display tunable soc  @xcite and superconductivity  @xcite .    motivated by this body of experimental and theoretical literature </S>",
    "<S> , we investigate the collective spin dynamics of an archetypical 2deg model hamiltonian with rashba soc and s - wave pairing  @xcite , in the presence of _ repulsive _ electron - electron ( e - e ) interactions . in the absence of superconductivity </S>",
    "<S> a rashba 2deg exhibits spin oscillations , which , at long wavelength and for weak repulsive interactions , have a frequency @xmath3 , @xmath4 being the strength of soc and @xmath5 the 2d fermi wavenumber in the absence of soc . </S>",
    "<S> these oscillations , however , are damped and quickly decay due to the emission of ( double ) electron - hole pairs , which , in the normal phase , are present at arbitrary low energies . in this letter </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate that in a gorkov - rashba superconductor ( grsc ) , collective spin oscillations continue to exist in a wide range of parameters , and are undamped because they lie inside the superconducting gap where no other excitation exists .     </S>",
