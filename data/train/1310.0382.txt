    "<S> we study persistent currents for interacting one - dimensional bosons on a tight ring trap , subjected to a rotating barrier potential , which induces an artificial @xmath0 gauge field . </S>",
    "<S> we show that , at intermediate interactions , the persistent current response is maximal , due to a subtle interplay of effects due to the barrier , the interaction and quantum fluctuations . </S>",
    "<S> these results are relevant for ongoing experiments with ultracold atomic gases on mesoscopic rings .    </S>",
    "<S> a quantum fluid confined on a ring and subjected to a @xmath0 gauge potential displays a periodicity in the particle current as a function of the flux of the corresponding classical gauge field . </S>",
    "<S> this persistent current phenomenon is a manifestation of the aharonov - bohm effect , and reflects the macroscopic coherence of the many - body wave function along the ring . </S>",
    "<S> such currents were observed more than 50 years ago in bulk superconductors  @xcite and , more recently , in normal metallic rings , overcoming the challenges of the decoherence induced by inelastic scattering  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the most recent developments in the manipulation of ultracold atoms on ring traps  @xcite have disclosed a novel platform for the study of persistent currents , which can be induced by the application of a rotating localized barrier or , alternatively , by inducing suitable artificial gauge fields  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> tunable localized barriers in toroidal bose - einstein condensates have been realized , using well - focused , repulsively tuned laser beams  @xcite . also , recently the engineering of an atomic superconducting quantum interference device was demonstrated  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the unprecedented variety of interaction and barrier strength regimes paves the way to applications such as high - precision measurements , atom interferometry and quantum information , _ </S>",
    "<S> e.g. _ , by the construction of macroscopic superposition of current states and flux qubits  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> the scenario becomes particularly intriguing if the transverse section of the ring is sufficiently thin to effectively confine the system in one dimension ( 1d ) : the rich interplay between interactions , quantum fluctuations , and statistics acquires a role of primary relevance . </S>",
    "<S> in absence of any obstacle along the ring , the persistent currents display an ideal sawtooth behavior as a function of the flux , _ i.e. _ , perfect superfluidity for any interaction strength at zero temperature  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> diamagnetic or paramagnetic response depending on the population parity is expected  @xcite for fermions but not for bosons . </S>",
    "<S> if a localized barrier is added , persistent currents are smeared  their shape taking a sinusoidal form in the case of large - barrier or small - tunneling limit  as obtained for thin superconducting rings from a luttinger - liquid approach  @xcite . beyond these limiting regimes </S>",
    "<S> the physics of bosonic persistent current remains unexplored .    </S>",
    "<S> the aim of the present work is to provide a complete characterization of persistent currents for 1d bosons , in all interaction and barrier strength regimes . by combining analytical as well as numerical techniques suited for the 1d problem , </S>",
    "<S> we show that the current amplitude is a nonmonotonic function of the interaction strength and displays a pronounced maximum in all regimes of barrier height . </S>",
    "<S> the presence of an optimal regime illustrates the highly nontrivial combination of correlations , quantum fluctuations and barrier effects . </S>",
    "<S> our results demonstrate that , in a large range of interaction strengths , unwanted impurities or imperfections on the ring only weakly affect the system properties . for the application to quantum state manipulation </S>",
    "<S> , the regimes of choice should be either very weak or very strong interactions , where the response to a localized external probe is stronger . </S>",
    "<S> our predictions are readily amenable to experimental testing with quasi-1d ultracold atomic gases confined in mesoscopic uniform and lattice rings . </S>",
