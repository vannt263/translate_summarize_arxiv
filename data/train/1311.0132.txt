    "<S> in this paper we study families of lagrangian tori that appear in a neighborhood of a resonance of a near - integrable hamiltonian system . </S>",
    "<S> such families disappear in the `` integrable '' limit @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> dynamics on these tori is oscillatory in the direction of the resonance phases and rotating with respect to the other ( non - resonant ) phases .    </S>",
    "<S> we also show that , if multiplicity of the resonance equals one , generically these tori occupy a set of large relative measure in the resonant domains in the sense that the relative measure of the remaining `` chaotic '' set is of order @xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> therefore for small @xmath2 a random initial condition in a @xmath3-neighborhood of a single resonance occurs inside this set ( and therefore generates a quasi - periodic motion ) with a probability much larger than in the `` chaotic '' set .    </S>",
    "<S> we present results of numerical simulations and discuss the form of projection of such tori to the action space . </S>"
