    "two - photon bunching was first observed by hanbury brown and twiss in 1956 @xcite , in which randomly emitted photons by thermal light source were found to have the tendency to come in bunches rather than randomly .",
    "lots of attentions were drawn to this bunching effect shortly after it was reported .",
    "some researchers repeated hanbury brown and twiss s experiments and got negative results @xcite .",
    "it was later understood that the negative results were due to the response time of the detection system is much longer than the coherence time of the measured light beams @xcite .",
    "classical theory was first employed to interpret the bunching effect @xcite .",
    "then quantum theory was also employed to interpret the same effect @xcite .",
    "it is now well accepted that the two - photon bunching effect of thermal light can be explained by both quantum and classical theories @xcite .",
    "however , the full quantum explanation of the bunching effect given by glauber greatly deepens our understanding of optical coherence .",
    "the hanbury brown and twiss s experiments @xcite and glauber s quantum optical coherence theory @xcite are usually thought as the cornerstones of modern quantum optics @xcite .",
    "the experimental setup employed by hanbury brown and twiss @xcite , which is known as hanbury brown - twiss ( hbt ) interferometer , plays an important role in measuring the second - order coherence of light and photon statistics in quantum optics @xcite .",
    "the second - order coherence of light can be described by the normalized second - order coherence function introduced by glauber @xcite . for a light beam in a hbt interferometer",
