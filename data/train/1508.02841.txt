    "<S> we consider the berkson model of logistic regression with gaussian and homoscedastic error in regressor . </S>",
    "<S> the measurement error variance can be either known or unknown . </S>",
    "<S> we deal with both functional and structural cases . </S>",
    "<S> sufficient conditions for identifiability of regression coefficients are presented .    </S>",
    "<S> conditions for identifiability of the model are studied . in the case where the error variance is known , </S>",
    "<S> the regression parameters are identifiable if the distribution of the observed regressor is not concentrated at a single point . in the case where the error variance is not known , </S>",
    "<S> the regression parameters are identifiable if the distribution of the observed regressor is not concentrated at three ( or less ) points .    </S>",
    "<S> the key analytic tools are relations between the smoothed logistic distribution function and its derivatives .    </S>",
