    "<S> we present a formalism to derive entanglement criteria beyond the gaussian regime that can be readily tested by only homodyne detection . </S>",
    "<S> the measured observable is the einstein - podolsky - rosen ( epr ) correlation . </S>",
    "<S> its arbitrary functional form enables us to detect non - gaussian entanglement even when an entanglement test based on second - order moments fails . </S>",
    "<S> we illustrate the power of our experimentally friendly criteria for a broad class of non - gaussian states under realistic conditions . </S>",
    "<S> we also show rigorously that quantum teleportation for continuous variables employs a specific functional form of epr correlation .    </S>",
    "<S> quantum entanglement plays a key role in making quantum - mechanical predictions distinct from their classical counterparts . </S>",
    "<S> its characterization and detection have been a topic of crucial importance that has attracted a great deal of effort , both theoretically and experimentally . despite remarkable progress </S>",

    "<S> gaussian entangled states have been the subject of study , but now considerable attention has been directed to non - gaussian states . </S>",
    "<S> this may be attributed to the fact that non - gaussian entangled states not only provide practical advantages over their gaussian counterparts , e.g. in quantum teleportation @xcite and dense coding @xcite , but also turn out to be essential , e.g. for universal quantum computation @xcite and nonlocality test @xcite .    </S>",

    "<S> some entanglement criteria have also been proposed @xcite . </S>",

    "<S> some criteria are at least theoretically easier to assess but they do not seem useful for a large class of non - gaussian states . therefore , new entanglement criteria beyond the gaussian regime must be developed , desirably in an experimentally friendly form , which is the objective of the current work .    </S>",
