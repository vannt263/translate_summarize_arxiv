    "<S> the shockley equation ( se ) , originally derived to describe a p  </S>",
    "<S> n junction , was frequently used in the past to simulate current  voltage ( j / v ) characteristics of organic solar cells ( osc ) . in order to gain a more detailed understanding of recombination losses </S>",
    "<S> , we determined the se parameters , i.e. the ideality factor and the dark saturation current , from temperature dependent static j / v - measurements on poly(3-hexylthiophene-2,5-diyl)(p3ht):[6,6]-phenyl - c@xmath0 butyric acid methyl ester ( pcbm ) bulk heterojunction solar cells . as we show here , </S>",
    "<S> these parameters are directly related to charge carrier recombination and become also accessible by transient photovoltage and photocurrent methods in the case of field - independent charge carrier generation . </S>",
    "<S> although determined in very different ways , both se parameters were found to be identical . </S>",
    "<S> the good agreement of static and transient approaches over a wide temperature range demonstrates the validity of the shockley model for osc based on material systems satisfying the requirement of field - independent polaron - pair dissociation . in particular , we were able to reproduce the photocurrent at various light intensities and temperatures from the respective nongeminate recombination rates . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , the temperature dependence of the dark saturation current @xmath1 allowed determining the effective band gap of the photoactive blend perfectly agreeing with the literature values of the energy onset of the photocurrent due to charge transfer absorption . </S>",
    "<S> we also present a consistent model directly relating the ideality factor to recombination of free with trapped charge carriers in an exponential density of tail states . </S>",
    "<S> we verify this finding by data from thermally stimulated current measurements . </S>"
