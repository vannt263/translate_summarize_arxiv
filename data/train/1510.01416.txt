    "<S> the mode - locking regions of a dynamical system are the subsets of the parameter space of the system within which there exists an attracting periodic solution . for piecewise - linear continuous maps , </S>",
    "<S> these regions have a curious chain structure with points of zero width called shrinking points . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we perform a local analysis about an arbitrary shrinking point . </S>",
    "<S> this is achieved by studying the symbolic itineraries of periodic solutions in nearby mode - locking regions and performing an asymptotic analysis on one - dimensional slow manifolds in order to build a comprehensive theoretical framework for the local dynamics . </S>",
    "<S> we obtain leading - order quantitative descriptions for the shape of nearby mode - locking regions , the location of nearby shrinking points , and the key properties of these shrinking points . </S>",
    "<S> we apply the results to the three - dimensional border - collision normal form , nonsmooth neimark - sacker - like bifurcations , and grazing - sliding bifurcations in a model of a dry friction oscillator .    </S>",
