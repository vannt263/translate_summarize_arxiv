    "<S> we study the nonlinear driven dissipative quantum dynamics of an array of optomechanical systems . at each site of such an array , </S>",
    "<S> a localized mechanical mode interacts with a laser - driven cavity mode via radiation pressure , and both photons and phonons can hop between neighboring sites . </S>",
    "<S> the competition between coherent interaction and dissipation gives rise to a rich phase diagram characterizing the optical and mechanical many - body states . </S>",
    "<S> for weak intercellular coupling , the mechanical motion at different sites is incoherent due to the influence of quantum noise . </S>",
    "<S> when increasing the coupling strength , however , we observe a transition towards a regime of phase - coherent mechanical oscillations . </S>",
    "<S> we employ a gutzwiller ansatz as well as semiclassical langevin equations on finite lattices , and we propose a realistic experimental implementation in optomechanical crystals .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction . - </S>",
    "<S> _ recent experimental progress has brought optomechanical systems into the quantum regime : a single mechanical mode interacting with a laser - driven cavity field has been cooled to the ground state @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> several of these setups , in particular optomechanical crystals , offer the potential to be scaled up to form optomechanical arrays . </S>",
    "<S> applications of such arrays for quantum information processing @xcite have been proposed . </S>",
    "<S> given these developments , one is led to explore quantum many - body effects in optomechanical arrays . in this work , we analyze the nonlinear photon and phonon dynamics in a homogeneous two - dimensional optomechanical array . </S>",
    "<S> in contrast to earlier works @xcite , here we study the array s quantum dynamics beyond a quadratic hamiltonian . to tackle the non - equilibrium many - body problem of this nonlinear dissipative system , we employ a mean - field approach for the collective dynamics . </S>",
    "<S> first , we discuss photon statistics in the array , in particular how the photon blockade effect @xcite is altered in the presence of intercellular coupling . </S>",
    "<S> the main part of the article focuses on the transition of the collective mechanical motion from an incoherent state ( due to quantum noise ) to an ordered state with phase - coherent mechanical oscillations . </S>",
    "<S> for these dynamics , the dissipative effects induced by the optical modes play a crucial role . on the one hand </S>",
    "<S> , they allow the mechanical modes to settle into self - induced oscillations @xcite once the optomechanical amplification rate exceeds the intrinsic mechanical damping . on the other hand , the fundamental quantum noise ( e.g. cavity shot noise ) diffuses the mechanical phases and prevents the mechanical modes from synchronizing . </S>",
    "<S> this interplay leads to an elaborate phase diagram characterizing the transition . </S>",
    "<S> we develop a semiclassical model to describe the effective dynamics of the mechanical phases and to study the system on finite lattices . </S>",
    "<S> while true long - range order is prohibited for a two - dimensional system with continuous symmetry , at least for equilibrium systems , a beresinskii - kosterlitz - thouless transition towards a state with quasi - long - range order is possible . </S>",
    "<S> the ordered mechanical phase thus resembles the superfluid phase in two - dimensional cold atomic gases @xcite or josephson junction arrays @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> notably , optomechanical arrays combine the tunability of optical systems with the robustness and durability of an integrated solid - state device . </S>",
    "<S> other driven dissipative systems that have been studied with regard to phase transitions recently include cold atomic gases @xcite , nonlinear cavity arrays @xcite and optical fibers @xcite . in a very recent work and along the lines of @xcite , the preparation of long - range order for photonic modes </S>",
