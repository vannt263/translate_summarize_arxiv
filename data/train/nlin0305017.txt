    "it is generally believed that turbulence promotes mixing ( see , e.g. , @xcite )",
    ". however , laboratory experiments and observations in the atmospheric turbulence show formation of long - living inhomogeneities in concentration distribution of small inertial particles and droplets in turbulent fluid flows ( see , e.g. , @xcite ) .",
    "the origin of these inhomogeneities is not always clear but there influence on the mixing can be hardly overestimated .",
    "the goal of this study is to analyze the particle - air interaction leading to the formation of strong inhomogeneities of particle distribution , referred to as _",
    "particle clustering_(see @xcite , and references therein ) .",
    "particle clustering is a consequence of a spontaneous breakdown of their homogeneous space distribution . as a result of the nonlinear stage of clustering",
    ", the local density of particles may rise by orders of magnitude and strongly increase the probability of particle - particle collisions .",
    "it was suggested in @xcite that the main reason for the particle clustering is their inertia : the particles inside the turbulent eddies are carried out to the boundary regions between the eddies by the inertial forces .",
    "this mechanism of the preferential concentration acts in all scales of turbulence , increasing toward small scales .",
    "later , this was contested in refs .",
    "@xcite using the so - called `` kraichnan model '' @xcite of turbulent advection by the delta - correlated in time random velocity field , whereby the clustering instability did not occur .",
    "however , it was shown in ref .  @xcite that accounting for a finiteness of correlation time of the fluid velocity field results in the clustering instability of heavy particles .",
    "the theory of the clustering instability of the inertial particles advected by a turbulent velocity field was developed in ref .",
    "@xcite and applied to the dynamics of aerosols in the turbulent atmosphere .    in the present study we extended the theory of particle clustering to the case when the particle stokes time is larger than the kolmogorov time scale , but is much smaller than the correlation time at the integral scale of turbulence .",

    "<S> we analyzed formation of small - scale inhomogeneities of particle spatial distribution ( particle clustering ) in a turbulent flow . </S>",
    "<S> the particle clustering is a consequence of a spontaneous breakdown of their homogeneous space distribution , and is caused by a combined effect of the particle inertia and a finite correlation time of the turbulent velocity field . </S>",
    "<S> theory of the particle clustering is extended to the case when the particle stokes time is larger than the kolmogorov time scale , but is much smaller than the correlation time at the integral scale of turbulence . </S>",
    "<S> the criterion of the clustering instability is obtained . </S>",
    "<S> applications of the analyzed effects to the dynamics of inertial particles in industrial turbulent flows are discussed . </S>"
