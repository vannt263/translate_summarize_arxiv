    "this electromagnetic environment is a source of decoherence and plays a central role for single - electron effects @xcite .",

    "in conclusion , we have presented a fully quantum mechanical derivation of the effect of an electromagnetic environment on current and voltage fluctuations in a mesoscopic conductor , going beyond an earlier study at zero temperature @xcite .",
    "the results agree with those obtained from the cascaded langevin approach @xcite , thereby providing the required microscopic justification .    from an experimental point of view",
    ", the nonlinear feedback from the environment is an obstacle that stands in the way of a measurement of the transport properties of the mesoscopic system . to remove the feedback it is not sufficient to reduce the impedance of the environment .",
    "one also needs to eliminate the mixing in of environmental thermal fluctuations .",
    "this can be done by ensuring that the environment is at a lower temperature than the conductor , but this might not be a viable approach for low - temperature measurements .",
    "we have proposed here an alternative method , which is to ensure that the measured variable changes sign under time reversal . in practice",
    "this could be realized by measuring the hall voltage over a macroscopic conductor in series with the mesoscopic system .",
    "the field theory developed here also provides for a systematic way to incorporate the effects of the coulomb blockade which arise if the high - frequency impedance of the environment is not small compared to the resistance quantum .",

    "<S> a theory is presented for low - frequency current and voltage correlators of a mesoscopic conductor embedded in a macroscopic electromagnetic environment . </S>",
    "<S> this keldysh field theory evaluated at its saddle - point provides the microscopic justification for our earlier phenomenological calculation ( using the cascaded langevin approach ) . </S>",
    "<S> the nonlinear feedback from the environment mixes correlators of different orders , which explains the unexpected temperature dependence of the third moment of tunneling noise observed in a recent experiment . at non - zero temperature , </S>",
    "<S> current and voltage correlators of order three and higher are no longer linearly related . </S>",
    "<S> we show that a hall bar measures voltage correlators in the longitudinal voltage and current correlators in the hall voltage . </S>",
    "<S> we go beyond the saddle - point approximation to consider the environmental coulomb blockade . </S>",
