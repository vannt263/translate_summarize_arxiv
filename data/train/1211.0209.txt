    "in recent years the interest in correlated quantum many - body systems has increased steadily due to the observation of correlation effects such as liquid and crystal formation , superfluidity of bose systems or bound states .",
    "examples of systems include nuclear matter @xcite , solid state systems @xcite and dense astrophysical , laboratory dusty plasmas or the quark gluon plasma , for a recent overview see @xcite .",
    "a quantity sensitive to spatial correlations is the pair distribution function ( pdf ) @xmath0the probability to find an arbitrary pair of particles at a distance @xmath1",
    ".    of particular current interest is the short - time behavior of correlated systems following external perturbation such as excitation by intense radiation , e.g. , @xcite . in quantum systems such as atoms or solids",
    "this is often connected with rapid electron thermalization coupled to the dynamics of electronic correlations .",
    "time and space - resolved measurement techniques detecting chemical reaction products , nuclear collision fragments or electrons and ions produced by laser ionization , e.g. , @xcite , are becoming increasingly powerful .",
    "this brings the direct measurement of time - dependent pair correlations described by the nonequilibrium generalization of the pdf , @xmath2 , within reach and increases the need for theoretical and computational tools that are able to predict @xmath2 .    from the theory side pdfs",
    "are routinely computed for nonideal classical systems using , e.g. , monte carlo or molecular dynamics simulations , e.g. , @xcite .",
    "extensions to quantum systems in equilibrium are available , e.g. , in the frame of quantum monte carlo methods .",
    "however , there is still a high demand for accurate nonequilibrium simulations .",
    "theoretical access to nonequilibrium pdfs is straightforwardly reached within density operator theory , e.g. , @xcite .",
    "an alternative approach to quantum many - body systems out of equilibrium is based on nonequilibrium green s functions ( negf ) .",
    "apart from exact time - dependent numerical calculations , e.g. , @xcite , which are limited to a few particles , numerical results for the two - time negf are expected to be the most accurate ones for strongly correlated quantum systems in nonequilibrium .",
    "this concerns , in particular , full two - time calculations , i.e. direct solutions of the keldysh / kadanoff - baym equations ( kbe ) which , after the pioneering work of danielewicz @xcite , has now become routine @xcite .",
    "they yield the single - particle green s functions and all one - particle observables , including spectral function , as a function of time .",

    "in this paper we presented an approach to the pair distribution function of a quantum many - body system in the frame of the nonequilibrium real - time green s functions .",
    "this problem is complicated due to the fact that the standard approach used in negfs uses a formal decoupling of the martin - schwinger hierarchy on the level of the first equation : the two - particle green s function is eliminated by introduction of the single - particle selfenergy , cf .",
    "( [ eq : selfenergy ] ) . with this elimination also",
    "direct access to the pair correlations is lost .",
    "thus one has to reconstruct the pair correlations form the single - particle green s function within a chosen approximation for the selfenergy .    to solve this reconstruction problem",
    "we started the analysis from the equation of motion of the two - particle green s function  the bethe - salpeter equation and simplified it systematically .",
    "we concentrated on the case of the static second born approximation because for it a large number of numerical solutions of the kbe exist , for which it would be desirable to evaluate the pdf .",
    "it was shown that in second born approximation a closed expression for the nonequilibrium pdf can be derived which can be straightforwardly evaluated .",
    "the result involves combinations of four single - particle green s functions and is computationally expensive .",
    "numerical results will be presented in a forthcoming paper .",

    "<S> the pair distribution function ( pdf ) is a key quantity for the analysis of correlation effects of a quantum system both in equilibrium and far from equilibrium . </S>",
    "<S> we derive an expression for the pdf in terms of the single - particle green s functions  the solutions of the keldysh / kadanoff - baym equations in the two - time plane  for a one- or two - component system . </S>",
    "<S> the result includes initial correlations and generalizes previous density matrix expressions from single - time quantum kinetic theory . </S>",
    "<S> explicit expressions for the pdf are obtained in second born approximation . </S>"
