    "<S> surface superconductivity in rhombohedral graphite is a robust phenomenon which can exist even when higher order hoppings between the layers lift the topological protection of the surface flat band and introduce a quadratic dispersion of electrons with a heavy effective mass . </S>",
    "<S> we show that for weak pairing interaction , the flat band character of the surface superconductivity transforms into a bcs - like relation with high critical temperature characterized by a higher coupling constant due to a much larger density of states than in the bulk . </S>",
    "<S> our results offer an explanation for the recent findings of graphite superconductivity with an unusually high transition temperature .    </S>",
    "<S> a low critical temperature of conventional superconductors results from a constant density of states ( dos ) due to a linear - in - momentum electronic spectrum near the fermi energy . with a higher - order dispersion , </S>",
    "<S> the relation between the critical temperature and the coupling constant becomes stronger , boosting the superconductivity . </S>",
    "<S> the extreme case would be a completely dispersionless energy spectrum , a flat band , which has been predicted in many condensed matter systems , see e.g. refs .  . </S>",
    "<S> in some cases the flat bands are protected by topology in momentum space ; they emerge in gapless topological matter@xcite . </S>",
    "<S> a singular dos associated with the dispersionless spectrum was recently shown@xcite to essentially enhance the transition temperature opening a new route to room - temperature superconductivity . </S>",
    "<S> the problem is to find the metal with such a higher - order dispersion around the fermi sea </S>",
    "<S> . refs .   and </S>",
    "<S> have shown that within the nearest - neighbour approximation , rhombohedral graphite ( rhg ) has topologically protected surface states with a flat band at the fermi energy , and these surface states support high - temperature superconductivity where the superconducting order parameter is concentrated around the surfaces . a flat band forms out of a low dispersive band that appears on the surface of a multilayered rhombohedral graphene structure with a large number of layers . </S>",
    "<S> the corresponding critical temperature depends linearly on the pairing interaction strength and can be thus considerably higher than the usual exponentially small critical temperature in the bulk . </S>",
    "<S> flat - band superconductors can carry quite high surface supercurrent with the critical value proportional to the critical temperature @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> experimental evidence of a high - temperature superconductivity in graphite in the form of a small meissner effect and of a sharp drop in resistance appeared in the literature during past years @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> recently , these findings have been ratified by observations of zero resistance in graphitic samples up to 175 k @xcite and indications of even room - temperature superconductivity in specially prepared graphite samples @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the enhanced superconducting density has been also reported on twin boundaries in ba(fe@xmath0co@xmath1)@xmath2as@xmath2 @xcite . in this letter </S>",
    "<S> , we argue that the high - temperature superconductivity in graphite can be related to surface superconductivity that may form either on the outer surfaces of the sample or on twin boundaries of or on grain boundaries between inclusions of rhg . </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate that the surface superconductivity is a robust phenomenon which survives even when the topological protection of the flat band itself is lifted . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , the next - nearest neighbour hoppings in rhg can break the exact topological protection and , therefore , the flat - band mechanism of superconductivity could be destroyed . </S>",
    "<S> we study these higher - order interactions @xcite and show that , though breaking the flat - band scenario at sufficiently low values of the coupling energy , they provide another mechanism of surface superconductivity which is of the bcs type but still has a much larger coupling constant than the usual superconductivity in bulk , thus favoring high - temperature superconductivity . </S>",
    "<S> the enhanced coupling constant comes from a high dos associated with a heavy effective mass of surface quasiparticles emerging on the background of the pre - existing flat band . </S>",
    "<S> our results help to identify the regime of parameters where extremely high - temperature surface superconductivity may be found .        </S>",
