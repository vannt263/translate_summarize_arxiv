    "<S> we study the asymptotic behavior of the eigenvalues of gaussian perturbations of large hermitian random matrices for which the limiting eigenvalue density vanishes at a singular interior point or vanishes faster than a square root at a singular edge point . </S>",
    "<S> first , we show that the singular behavior propagates macroscopically for sufficiently small gaussian perturbations , and we describe the macroscopic eigenvalue behavior for gaussian perturbations of critical size . secondly , for sufficiently small gaussian perturbations of unitary invariant random matrices , we prove that the microscopic eigenvalue correlations near the singular point are described by the same limiting kernel as in the unperturbed case . </S>",
    "<S> we also interpret our results in terms of nonintersecting brownian paths with random starting positions , and we establish multi - time generalizations of the microscopic results . </S>"
