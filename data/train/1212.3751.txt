    "<S> it is found that the surface part of a heavy nucleus contributes dominantly to its symmetry energy compared to its inner part . </S>",
    "<S> the symmetry energy coefficient @xmath0 is then directly extracted and the ratio of the surface symmetry coefficient to the volume symmetry coefficient @xmath1 is estimated . meanwhile , with the help of experimental alpha decay energies , a macroscopic method is developed to determine the symmetry energy coefficient of heavy nuclei . </S>",
    "<S> the resultant @xmath0 is used to analyze the density dependence of the symmetry energy coefficient of nuclear matter around the saturation density , and furthermore , the neutron skin thickness of @xmath2 is deduced which is consistent with the pygmy dipole resonance analysis . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , it is shown that the ratio @xmath1 obtained from the macroscopic method is in agreement with that from the skyrme energy density functional . </S>",
    "<S> thus the two completely different approaches may validate each other to achieve more compelling results .    </S>",
