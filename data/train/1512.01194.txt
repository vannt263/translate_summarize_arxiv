    "<S> ( the only one is mass . ) </S>",
    "<S> however , adiabatic invariants ( ai ) can be found . </S>",
    "<S> when the kdv expansion parameters are zero , exact invariants are recovered . </S>",
    "<S> existence of adiabatic invariants results from general theory of near - identity transformations ( nit ) which allow us to transform higher order nonintegrable equations to asymptotically equivalent ( when small parameters tend to zero ) integrable form . </S>",
    "<S> here we present the direct method of calculations of adiabatic invariants . </S>",
    "<S> it does not need a transformation to a moving reference frame nor performing a near - identity transformation . </S>",
    "<S> numerical tests show that deviations of ai from almost constant values are indeed small .    </S>",
