    "the support theorem for diffusion processes defined by stochastic differential equations has been a much studied topic for probabilists and analysts since the seminal work of stroock and varadhan @xcite .",
    "the typical approach to a support theorem in the norm of uniform convergence consists of two steps .",
    "one step is to establish a limit theorem for sdes , meaning that the solution of an sde can be approximated by a sequence of solutions of odes , obtained by regularizing the brownian paths @xcite ; the other is to prove a denjoy - type approximate continuity theorem , stating that the solution of an sde is approximately continuous at points in a dense set of the cameron ",
    "martin space .",
    "millet and sanz - sol @xcite proposed a simple approach to characterizing in hlder spaces the support of diffusions described by general sdes , obtained by approximating brownian motions with linear adapted interpolations , and proved the two inclusions through approximation results .    in this work",
    "we are concerned with the support problem of diffusions constrained in a domain @xmath0 with normal reflection boundary .",
    "such diffusions have been constructed by anderson and orey @xcite if @xmath0 has smooth boundary and by tanaka @xcite if @xmath0 is convex .",
    "correspondingly the support theorem has been established by doss and priouret @xcite if @xmath0 has smooth boundary , and a limit theorem has been proved by pettersson @xcite when @xmath0 is a convex domain and the diffusion coefficient is constant . recently in @xcite , a support theorem was proved for stochastic variational inequalities ; this means , in particular , that the support theorem holds true for diffusions normally reflected in convex domains .",
    "however , normally reflected diffusions have been constructed for domains much wider than convex domains and smooth domains ( see lions and sznitman  @xcite and saisho @xcite ) , so a natural ( and application - motivated ) question is whether or not the support theorem continues to hold true for such diffusions .",
    "the first step in this respect was taken by evans and stroock @xcite who proved , under the set of conditions given by lions and sznitman , that a weak limit theorem holds . very recently",
    "this result was improved by aida and sasaki @xcite , and independently by zhang @xcite , who used an adapted version of the wong  zakai approximations rather than the usual one , by removing the admissibility condition from the set of conditions and proving that the convergence takes place , in fact , in @xmath1 ( and they obtained the convergence speed ) .",
    "roughly speaking , they proved a strong limit theorem for the reflected diffusions studied by saisho in @xcite . to date , this was the widest , well - studied situation .",
    "on the other hand , however , approximate continuity has not yet been touched in such situations .",
    "our first result fills this gap , and it , together with the wong  zakai convergence result in @xcite and @xcite , will yield the support theorem in the locally uniform convergence topology for normally reflected sdes in domains , satisfying the conditions of lions and sznitman @xcite , except the admissibility . the second contribution of this paper is to present a characterization of the support for reflected diffusions in hlder spaces in domains satisfying the conditions in @xcite , by extending the idea of millet and sanz - sol @xcite to sdes with normal reflections .",
