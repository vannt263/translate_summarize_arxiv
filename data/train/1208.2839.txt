    "<S> caldern - zygmund decompositions of functions have been used to prove weak - type ( 1,1 ) boundedness of singular integral operators . in many examples , </S>",
    "<S> the decomposition is done with respect to a family of balls that corresponds to some family of dilations . </S>",
    "<S> we study singular integral operators @xmath0 that require more particular families of balls , providing new spaces of homogeneous type . </S>",
    "<S> rotations play a decisive role in the construction of these balls . </S>",
    "<S> boundedness of @xmath0 can then be shown via caldern - zygmund decompositions with respect to these spaces of homogeneous type . </S>",
    "<S> we prove weak - type ( 1,1 ) and @xmath1 estimates for operators @xmath0 acting on @xmath2 , where @xmath3 is a homogeneous lie group . </S>",
    "<S> our results apply to the setting where the underlying group is the heisenberg group and the rotations are symplectic automorphisms . </S>",
