    "<S> the surface states of weyl semi - metals ( sm s ) consist of disjointed fermi - arcs . this unusual </S>",
    "<S> surface - fermiology provides a fingerprint of the topological features of the bulk weyl - phase . using a combination of semiclassical and numerical methods , </S>",
    "<S> we show that , in contrast to naive expectation , there are closed magnetic orbits involving the open- surface fermi - arcs . below a critical field strength that depends on sample thickness , </S>",
    "<S> these orbits produce periodic quantum oscillations of the density of states in a magnetic field , enabling a variety of experimental probes of the unconventional fermi - arc surface states . </S>",
    "<S> the orbits are also essential for reproducing the bulk chiral anomaly in a finite slab . </S>",
    "<S> these results are then extended to the closely related and recently discovered 3d dirac sm materials , including cd@xmath0as@xmath1 and na@xmath0bi , which are doubled copies of weyl semi - metals protected by crystal symmetry . </S>",
    "<S> despite the fact that the protecting crystal symmetry is broken by a surface , we show that dirac materials can still host unconventional surface - states , which can be detected in quantum oscillations experiments .    </S>",
    "<S> weyl semi - metals ( sms ) are three - dimensional materials for which the bulk band - gap closes at an even - number of discrete points ( weyl nodes ) in the brillouin zone@xcite . near the weyl nodes , </S>",
    "<S> electrons have relativistic dispersion @xmath2 . </S>",
    "<S> each weyl node acts as a monopole or anti - monopole of berry - curvature , ( i.e. a closed surface in momentum space surrounding a node will be pierced by @xmath3 berry flux ) and is associated with a positive or negative chirality respectively . </S>",
    "<S> consequently , 2d cross sections of the brillouin zone change chern number by @xmath4 across each weyl node , implying that a generic surface will exhibit surface states whose  fermi - surfaces \" consist of a set of open line - segments@xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> these fermi - arcs connect pairs of bulk weyl nodes with opposite chiralities and can not be removed without annihilating the bulk weyl nodes . </S>",
    "<S> such unusual surface - fermiology would be impossible in a purely two - dimensional system , whose fermi - surface is necessariliy smooth , and can not abrubtly terminate at a point within the brillouin zone . </S>",
    "<S> consequently , the fermi - arcs serve as a surface fingerprint of the topological character of the bulk band - structure , and it is interesting to ask how they might be experimentally observed .    traditionally , the most powerful methods of mapping out a material s fermi - surface rely on periodic - in-@xmath5 quantum oscillations of the density of states in a magnetic field , @xmath6 . </S>",
    "<S> such quantum oscillations require closed magnetic orbits for electrons at the fermi - surface , which naively can not arise from disjointed fermi - arcs . </S>",
    "<S> this raises the interesting question : do fermi - arcs lead to quantum oscillations ?    </S>",
    "<S> [ fig : semiclassicaltrajectory ]    we answer this question in the affirmative by noting that , in a slab of weyl semi - metal of finite - thickness , closed magnetic orbits can be obtained by traversing the fermi - arc on the top surface and returning along the corresponding arc on the bottom surface . </S>",
    "<S> using semiclassical methods , we find that such closed orbits can indeed lead to periodic - in-@xmath5 quantum oscillations of the density of states . </S>",
    "<S> since the orbits involved require an electron to traverse the bulk in order to connect top and bottom surfaces , the oscillations only occur up to a critical field strength which depends on slab thickness . for larger fields , </S>",
    "<S> the orbits no longer produce periodic quantum oscillations , but are important for understanding the fate of the bulk chiral landau - levels ( lls ) and associated chiral anomaly in finite systems . </S>",
    "<S> the semiclassical results are then validated by direct numerical simulation of the spectrum of a weyl sm slab in a field .    </S>",
    "<S> weyl sms are theoretically predicted to occur in strongly spin - orbit coupled systems like the iridates , certain spinels and topological insulator - ferromagnet multilayers@xcite , yet , despite promising indications@xcite , there is currently no clearcut experimental candidate . </S>",
    "<S> however , two recently discovered materials cd@xmath0as@xmath1@xcite and na@xmath0bi@xcite are thought to be 3d dirac sms , close cousins of weyl sms . </S>",
    "<S> the bulk band - structure of these dirac sms consists of two superimposed copies of weyl sms with opposite chiralities . </S>",
    "<S> ordinarily , when weyl nodes of opposite chirality are not separated in momentum , they can mix and gap each other out . </S>",
