    "modulational instabilities ( mis ) have a time - honored history in nonlinear wave equations . their occurrences span areas ranging from fluid dynamics @xcite ( where they are usually referred to as the benjamin - feir instability ) and nonlinear optics @xcite to plasma physics @xcite .",
    "while earlier manifestations of such instabilities were studied in continuum systems @xcite , in the last decade the role of the mi in the dynamics of discrete systems has emerged . in particular , the mi was analyzed in the context of the discrete nonlinear schrdinger equation @xcite , a ubiquitous nonlinear - lattice dynamical model @xcite .",
    "more recently , it was studied in the context of weakly interacting trapped bose - einstein condensates ( becs ) , where the analytical predictions based on the discrete model @xcite were found to be in agreement with the experiment @xcite ( see also the recent works @xcite , and recent reviews in @xcite ) .",
    "additionally , the development of discrete nonlinear optics  ( based on nonlinear waveguide arrays ) has recently provided the first experimental observation of the mi in the latter class of systems @xcite .",
    "an extension of these recent works , which is relevant to both becs in the presence of an optical - lattice potential @xcite and nonlinear optics in photorefractive crystals @xcite , is the case of multi - component discrete fields .",
    "these can correspond to a mixture of two different atomic species , or different spin states of the same atom , in becs @xcite , or to light waves carried by different polarizations or different wavelengths in optical systems @xcite .",

    "in this work , we have examined the extension of the modulational instability ( mi )  concept to the case of multiple - component discrete fields .",
    "we have shown , in a systematic way , how to formulate the linear mi equations and how to extract the mi criteria .",
    "we have also followed the dynamical evolution of the instability by means of direct simulations , and have identified the effects of the linear and nonlinear couplings on the range of modulationally unstable wavenumbers . in particular , we have demonstrated that the joint action of the two couplings may give rise to noteworthy features , such as opening of new mi  bands on the wavenumber scale .",
    "additionally , the identification of a pair of conjugate uniform solutions in the two - component model has prompted us to examine domain - wall ( dw)solutions between such states .",
    "we were able to demonstrate that the dws can be linearly stable , provided that the inter - site coupling in the lattice is sufficiently weak .    from our results",
    ", it is clear that multi - component lattice models have a rich phenomenology , which is a natural addition to that of single - component ones",
    ". it would be interesting to observe the predicted features in experimental settings , including weakly coupled becs and photonic - crystal nonlinear media .",
