    "<S> consider informative selection of a sample from a finite population . </S>",
    "<S> responses are realized as independent and identically distributed ( i.i.d . ) </S>",
    "<S> random variables with a probability density function ( p.d.f . ) @xmath0 , referred to as the superpopulation model . </S>",
    "<S> the selection is informative in the sense that the sample responses , given that they were selected , are not i.i.d . </S>",
    "<S> @xmath0 . in general </S>",
    "<S> , the informative selection mechanism may induce dependence among the selected observations . the impact of such dependence on the empirical cumulative distribution function ( c.d.f . ) </S>",
    "<S> is studied . an asymptotic framework and weak conditions on the informative selection mechanism </S>",
    "<S> are developed under which the ( unweighted ) empirical c.d.f . converges uniformly , in @xmath1 and almost surely , to a weighted version of the superpopulation c.d.f . </S>",
    "<S> this yields an analogue of the glivenko  </S>",
    "<S> cantelli theorem . </S>",
    "<S> a series of examples , motivated by real problems in surveys and other observational studies , shows that the conditions are verifiable for specified designs .    , </S>"
