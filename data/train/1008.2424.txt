    "bars are a common self - gravitating structure present in disc galaxies . about 50% of such galaxies",
    "are stongly or weakly barred , including our milky way @xcite .",
    "the effect of a weak bar is usually represented as a potential in cylindrical coordinates in the form @xmath0 @xcite . in the case of strong bars , the only exact , self - consistent models known are those of freeman @xcite , although they present some unrealistic features for bars . in studies of orbits involving strong bars ,",
    "they are often modelled as homogeneous ellipsoids @xcite or inhomogeneous prolate spheroids @xcite .",
    "these mass distributions have a finite extent .",
    "long & murali @xcite discuss a simple method to generate analytical potential - density pairs for barred systems that extend to all space .    in this paper",
    "we construct analytical potential - density pairs for infinitesimally thin and ` softened ' bars @xcite that can be expressed solely in terms of elementary functions .",
    "the starting point is an identity that relates multipolar solutions of the einstein equations to newtonian potentials of bars with densities proportional to legendre polynomials .",
    "these bars can then be superposed to generate other bars with a desired density profile .",

    "we presented analytical potential - density pairs for infinitesimally thin and softened bars constructed from an identity that relates multipolar solutions of the einstein equations to newtonian potentials of bars with densities proportional to legendre polynomials .",
    "the main advantage of these models is that all potential density - pairs can be explicitly expressed in terms of elementary functions , and bars with a desired density profile can be constructed from the set of densities ( [ eq_lambda ] ) .    as an application of the barred potentials , we calculated the equilibrium points for the motion of test particles in the gravitational field of three models of rotating bars and analysed their stability .",
    "the results suggest some conclusions .",
    "the equilibrium point @xmath51 has the tendency to be more stable in bar model 2 , and more unstable in bar model 3 .",
    "the stability diagrams for the equilibrium points along the @xmath50-axis have the same qualitative behaviour for the three models of bars .",
    "on the other hand , the properties of the equilibrium points along the @xmath41-axis seem to be quite sensitive to the particular model of bar used . in the case of bar model 2 , the points are always unstable , whereas for bar models 1 and 3 , there even exists the possibility of two pairs of equilibrium points , one being stable and the other unstable .",
    "it is known that the equilibrium points on the @xmath41-axis of a homogeneous ellipsoid are always unstable @xcite . from our three models",
    "the bar model 2 has the nearest shape of an ellipsoid , thus it is not surprising that it exhibits similar properties .",
    "it seems that barred mass distributions with less mass concentrated around the centre of the bar tend to stabilize the equilibrium points along the @xmath41-axis .",
    "our results are in qualitative agreement with those obtained by michalodimitrakis @xcite , who compared the stability properties of equilibrium points for a homogeneous ellipsoid and for a homogeneous parallelepiped .",
