    "<S> graph dynamical systems ( ) can be used to describe a wide range of distributed , nonlinear phenomena . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we characterize _ cycle equivalence _ of a class of finite called sequential dynamical systems ( ) . in general , </S>",
    "<S> two finite are cycle equivalent if their periodic orbits are isomorphic as directed graphs . </S>",
    "<S> sequential dynamical systems may be thought of as generalized cellular automata , and use an _ update order _ to construct the dynamical system map . </S>",
    "<S> the main result of this paper is a characterization of cycle equivalence in terms of shifts and reflections of the update order . </S>",
    "<S> we construct two graphs @xmath0 and @xmath1 whose components describe update orders that give rise to cycle equivalent . </S>",
    "<S> the number of components in @xmath0 and @xmath1 is an upper bound for the number of cycle equivalence classes one can obtain , and we enumerate these quantities through a recursion relation for several graph classes . </S>",
    "<S> the components of these graphs encode dynamical neutrality , the component sizes represent periodic orbit structural stability , and the number of components can be viewed as a system complexity measure . </S>"
