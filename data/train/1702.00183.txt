    "<S> we study the mass discrepancy - acceleration relation ( mdar ) of 57 elliptical galaxies by their einstein rings from the sloan lens acs survey ( slacs ) . </S>",
    "<S> this is the first time that the mdar is discussed with data from gravitational lensing , a relativistic effect . </S>",
    "<S> the mass discrepancy between the lensing mass and the baryonic mass derived from population synthesis is larger when the acceleration of the elliptical galaxy lenses is smaller . </S>",
    "<S> the mdar is also related to surface mass density discrepancy . at the einstein ring , </S>",
    "<S> these lenses belong to high - surface - mass density galaxies . </S>",
    "<S> similarly , we find that the discrepancy between the lensing and stellar surface mass density is small . </S>",
    "<S> it is consistent with the recent discovery of dynamical surface mass density discrepancy in disk galaxies where the discrepancy is smaller when surface density is larger . </S>",
    "<S> we also find relativistic modified newtonian dynamics ( mond ) can naturally explain the mdar and surface mass density discrepancy in 57 einstein rings </S>",
    "<S> . moreover , the lensing mass , the dynamical mass and the stellar mass of these galaxies are consistent with each other in relativistic mond . </S>"
