    "<S> we investigate the implementation of binary projective measurements with linear optics . </S>",
    "<S> this problem can be viewed as a single - shot discrimination of two orthogonal pure quantum states . </S>",
    "<S> we show that any two orthogonal states can be perfectly discriminated using only linear optics , photon counting , coherent ancillary states , and feedforward . </S>",
    "<S> the statement holds in the asymptotic limit of large number of these physical resources .    </S>",
    "<S> projection measurements play an essential role in photonic quantum - information protocols . in these applications , </S>",
    "<S> generally , a projection onto superposition states or entangled states of optical fields is required . </S>",
    "<S> physically , it is a highly nontrivial problem how to implement such a measurement .    one plausible approach is to use linear optics and classical feedforward associated with a partial measurement . </S>",
