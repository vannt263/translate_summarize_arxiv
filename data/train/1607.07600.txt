    "<S> in this paper , we introduce the fundamental notion of a markov basis , which is one of the first connections between commutative algebra and statistics . the notion of a markov basis </S>",
    "<S> is first introduced by diaconis and sturmfels ( @xcite ) for conditional testing problems on contingency tables by markov chain monte carlo methods . in this method , we make use of a connected markov chain over the given conditional sample space to estimate the @xmath0 values numerically for various conditional tests . a markov basis plays an importance role in this arguments , because it guarantees the connectivity of the chain , which is needed for unbiasedness of the estimate , for arbitrary conditional sample space . as another important point , a markov basis is characterized as generators of the well - specified toric ideals of polynomial rings . </S>",
    "<S> this connection between commutative algebra and statistics is the main result of @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> after this first paper , a markov basis is studied intensively by many researchers both in commutative algebra and statistics , which yields an attractive field called _ computational algebraic statistics_. in this paper , we give a review of the markov chain monte carlo methods for contingency tables and markov bases , with some fundamental examples . </S>",
    "<S> we also give some computational examples by algebraic software macaulay2 ( @xcite ) and statistical software ` r ` . </S>",
    "<S> readers can also find theoretical details of the problems considered in this paper and various results on the structure and examples of markov bases in @xcite . </S>"
