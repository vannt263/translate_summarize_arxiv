    "we have calculated the non - linear current through a molecule with two non - degenerate electron - accepting orbitals coupled asymmetrically to an internal vibration in the limit of weak tunneling to the electrodes .",
    "we found that due to the interplay of coulomb blockade and non - equilibrium vibration - assisted tunneling ndc effects become amplified and pervasive in comparison with a one - orbital model .",
    "the only resonances where we consistently find current steps correspond to an electron tunneling off the molecule starting from the charged state coupled strongly to the vibration . at all other resonance lines the @xmath146 may become negative depending on the electron - vibration couplings and applied voltages .",
    "a weak and strong ndc effect may be distinguished , which require the larger of the two electron - vibration couplings to be moderate and strong respectively .",
    "+ the weak ndc effect is found at resonance lines where an electron can tunnel onto the molecule resulting in the charged state coupled _ stronger _ to the vibration .",
    "this effect only occurs when two ( or more ) orbitals are competing in the transport .",
    "the current steps and drops occur at bias positions with an _ opposite _ gate - voltage dependence and give rise to current _",
    "oscillations_. we proved that this type of ndc is robust against strong relaxation of the vibrational distribution on the molecule due to a dissipative environment .",
    "any ndc at other resonances conditions is a proof of a non - equilibrium vibrational distribution on the molecule .",
    "the current oscillations may even become amplified by strong relaxation depending on the applied voltages .",
    "+   strong ndc effects appear at the first few resonance lines associated with the state _ weakly _ coupled to the vibration .",
    "this is a non - equilibrium effect which is typically weakened by relaxation processes .",
    "cascades of single - electron tunneling processes involving the vibrational excitations of the weakly coupled state provide a _ feedback _ which rapidly populates the strongly coupled state .",
    "the latter thus acts as a blocking state which is almost fully occupied . the few , strong ndc lines correspond to the activation of the feedback mechanism and can have the _ same _ gate voltage dependence as the current steps , in contrast to the weak ndc above . in a one - orbital model",
    "the feedback mechanism is also active but produces only a weak effect due to the absence of a competing orbital .",
    "an anomalous _ current peak _ of width @xmath7 appears when the feedback mechanism becomes effective only sufficiently deep _ inside _ a resonance .",
    "the peak signals the crossover of the vibrational distribution from equilibrium to non - equilibrium .",
    "+ interestingly , the blocking state can be the vibrational ground state of either charged state whichever couples stronger to the vibration .",
    "when the electronic excited orbital couples most strongly the ndc signals a voltage - controlled _ population inversion _ between the charged states induced by the vibration - assisted tunneling .",
    "also , new resonances appear associated with an electron entering the molecule and _ absorbing _ vibrational energy stored on it ( despite the low temperature ) where the current is _ suppressed_. + the ndc effects are due to asymmetry of the orbital energies and couplings to the internal vibration which are _ intrinsic _ properties of the molecule",

    "<S> we calculate the non - linear tunneling current through a molecule with _ two _ electron - accepting orbitals which interact with an _ intramolecular _ vibration . </S>",
    "<S> we investigate the interplay between coulomb blockade and non - equilibrium vibration - assisted tunneling under the following assumptions : ( i ) the coulomb charging effect restricts the number of extra electrons to one . </S>",
    "<S> ( ii ) the orbitals are non - degenerate and couple asymmetrically to the vibration . </S>",
    "<S> ( iii ) the tunneling induces a non - equilibrium vibrational distribution ; we compare with the opposite limit of strong relaxation of the vibration due to some dissipative environment . </S>",
    "<S> we find that a non - equilibrium _ feedback _ mechanism in the tunneling transitions generates strong negative differential conductance ( ndc ) in the model with two competing orbitals , whereas in a one - orbital model it leads only to weak ndc . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , we find another mechanism leading to weak ndc over a broader range of applied voltages . </S>",
    "<S> this pervasive effect is completely robust against strong relaxation of the vibrational energy . </S>",
    "<S> importantly , the modulation of the electronic transport is based on an _ </S>",
    "<S> intra_molecular asymmetry . </S>",
    "<S> we show that one can infer a non - equilibrium vibrational distribution when finding ndc under distinct gate- and bias - voltage conditions . </S>",
    "<S> in contrast , we demonstrate that any ndc effect in the one - orbital case is completely suppressed in the strong relaxation limit .    optical spectroscopy of vibrational modes has provided detailed information about the structure of molecules  @xcite . in a similar fashion </S>",
    "<S> , tunneling - current spectroscopy  @xcite may reveal microscopic details of single - molecule devices  @xcite . at low temperature electrons tunneling onto a molecular device </S>",
    "<S> excite _ discrete _ vibrations by spending some of their excess energy provided by the bias voltage . </S>",
    "<S> the resulting changes in the current with bias voltage are determined by the effective potentials for the nuclear vibration in different _ charge _ states , in contrast to optical spectroscopy . </S>",
    "<S> this provides information on how the molecule is situated in a nanojunction ( center of mass modes between the electrodes )  @xcite and the role of different parts of the molecule in the transport ( internal modes )  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> it is also of interest to _ control _ the electronic response of molecular devices by designing their mechanical properties through chemical synthesis . in this respect , </S>",
