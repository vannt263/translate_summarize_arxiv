    "the swimming behavior of motile microorganisms is strongly influenced by the presence of nearby surfaces .",
    "bacteria tend to swim in circles when they swim near a rigid wall  @xcite , and hydrodynamic effects influence the accumulation of spermatozoa  @xcite and the swimming behavior of bacteria near rigid walls  @xcite",
    ". swimmers also encounter deformable surfaces , such as the air - water interface  @xcite , or the soft mucus - lined tissues in the mammalian female reproductive tract  @xcite .",
    "the deformability of these interfaces helps determine swimming behavior ; for example , fish spermatozoa close to an air - water interface have been observed to swim faster than they do at a liquid - water interface  @xcite . at larger scales ,",
    "the deformation of the air - water interface is thought to be crucial for the motility of water snails  @xcite .",
    "phenomena such as these have motivated analytical  @xcite and numerical  @xcite calculations of the swimming behavior of idealized model swimmers at zero reynolds number ( see the references in lauga and powers  @xcite ) .",
    "perhaps the simplest geometry is a two - dimensional sheet of infinite extent subject to traveling waves of undulation ; analytic calculations show that for a deformation of fixed wave speed , wavelength , and amplitude , the presence of a nearby wall makes the swimmer go faster  @xcite than it would in the absence of confinement  @xcite .",
    "more recent analytic calculations have explored the effect of walls on propulsive force on an infinite sheet  @xcite .",
    "another model is a point - like swimmer such as a force dipole .",
    "a deformable interface near such a swimmer exhibiting a reciprocal swimming stroke can lead to net propulsion  @xcite since the interface breaks the symmetry required for the application of the scallop theorem  @xcite .",
    "finally , numerical calculations for a swimming sheet in a viscoelastic medium near an elastic membrane have shown that nearby elastic structures can enhance both the swimming speed and efficiency  @xcite .",
    "likewise , numerical calculations of a model dipole swimmer in an elastic tube also show an increase in swimming speed due to the elasticity of the walls  @xcite . in this article",
    "we calculate the swimming speed for a swimming sheet near an elastic membrane in the limit of small - amplitude waves .",

    "to summarize , we have studied the problem of a swimming sheet near a deformable membrane with bending rigidity and a constant surface tension .",
    "our main results are the swimming speed of the sheet as well as the deformation and swimming speed of the membrane .",
    "a distinctive feature of our problem that does not occur in the problem of a sheet swimming in unbounded fluid is that fluid is dragged along with or propelled backwards past the swimmer .",
    "future work should lift some of the simplifying assumptions we have made , and examine finite - length swimmers with large amplitude deformations near flexible surfaces .",

    "<S> microorganisms are rarely found in nature swimming freely in an unbounded fluid . </S>",
    "<S> instead , they typically encounter other organisms , hard walls , or deformable boundaries such as free interfaces or membranes . </S>",
    "<S> hydrodynamic interactions between the swimmer and nearby objects lead to many interesting phenomena , such as changes in swimming speed , tendencies to accumulate or turn , and coordinated flagellar beating . inspired by this class of problems , we investigate locomotion of microorganisms near deformable boundaries . </S>",
    "<S> we calculate the speed of an infinitely long swimmer close to a flexible surface separating two fluids ; we also calculate the deformation and swimming speed of the flexible surface . </S>",
    "<S> when the viscosities on either side of the flexible interface differ , we find that fluid is pumped along or against the swimming direction , depending on which viscosity is greater . </S>"
