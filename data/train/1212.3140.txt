    "<S> the electron - nuclei ( hyperfine ) interaction is central to spin qubits in solid state systems . it can be a severe decoherence source but also allows dynamic access to the nuclear spin states . </S>",
    "<S> we study a double quantum dot exposed to an on - chip single - domain nanomagnet and show that its inhomogeneous magnetic field crucially modifies the complex nuclear spin dynamics such that the overhauser field tends to compensate external magnetic fields . </S>",
    "<S> this turns out to be beneficial for polarizing the nuclear spin ensemble . </S>",

    "<S> double quantum dots ( dqds ) , each electron interacts with nuclear spins , which fluctuate thermally even at cryogenic temperatures . </S>",

    "<S> nuclear spin manipulation has also motivated theoretical proposals @xcite and experimental realizations @xcite of nuclear spins as quantum memory .    here , we present a novel nuclear spin manipulation technique . </S>",
