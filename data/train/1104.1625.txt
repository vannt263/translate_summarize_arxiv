    "<S> the magnetization dynamics of ferromagnets are often formulated in terms of the landau - lifshitz - gilbert ( llg ) equation . </S>",
    "<S> the reactive part of this equation describes the response of the magnetization in terms of effective fields , whereas the dissipative part is parameterized by the gilbert damping tensor . </S>",
    "<S> we formulate a scattering theory for the magnetization dynamics and map this description on the linearized llg equation by attaching electric contacts to the ferromagnet . </S>",
    "<S> the reactive part can then be expressed in terms of the static scattering matrix . </S>",
    "<S> the dissipative contribution to the low - frequency magnetization dynamics can be described as an adiabatic energy pumping process to the electronic subsystem by the time - dependent magnetization . </S>",
    "<S> the gilbert damping tensor depends on the time derivative of the scattering matrix as a function of the magnetization direction . by the fluctuation - dissipation theorem , </S>",
    "<S> the fluctuations of the effective fields can also be formulated in terms of the quasistatic scattering matrix . </S>",
    "<S> the theory is formulated for general magnetization textures and worked out for monodomain precessions and domain wall motions . </S>",
    "<S> we prove that the gilbert damping from scattering theory is identical to the result obtained by the kubo formalism . </S>"
