    "<S> one topic of particular interest is the mechanism of electroweak - symmetry breaking . </S>",
    "<S> first it is necessary to establish the existence or non - existence of a light physical higgs boson , and if such a particle exists it is necessary to compare its properties to the predictions of the standard model ( sm ) . because the massive vector bosons obtain their masses via the symmetry - breaking mechanism , a study of the higgs sector is related to the study of the properties of massive gauge bosons . </S>",
    "<S> this talk is primarily concerned with the latter , particularly through the study of the process @xmath0 .    in order to compare experiment with the predictions of the sm </S>",
    "<S> it is necessary to parameterize deviations from the sm in a convenient manner . </S>",
    "<S> the parameterization should be sufficiently general , yet manageable . </S>",
    "<S> an especially powerful tool is the effective theory . in general </S>",
    "<S> one assumes that the effects of new physics characterized by a high - energy scale may be observed at low energies only through quantum corrections . </S>",
    "<S> these quantum corrections may be described by effective operators which are constructed from the fields of the low - energy theory ; the coefficients of these operators may be measured via experiment , but a theoretical determination is only possible once the complete theory is known .    because the existence or non - existence of a light physical higgs boson has not yet been established the inclusion or exclusion of the higgs field in the construction of the effective lagrangian is open to debate . for this reason </S>",
