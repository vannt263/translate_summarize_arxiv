    "<S> we show that highly twisted minimal strips can undergo a non - singular transition , unlike the singular transitions seen in the mbius strip and the catenoid . </S>",
    "<S> if the strip is non - orientable this transition is topologically frustrated , and the resulting surface contains a helical defect . through a controlled analytic approximation </S>",
    "<S> the system can be mapped onto a scalar @xmath0 theory on a non - orientable line bundle over the circle , where the defect becomes a topologically protected kink soliton or domain wall , thus establishing their existence in minimal surfaces . </S>",
    "<S> experimental studies of soap films confirm these results and demonstrate how the position of the defect can be controlled through boundary deformation .    </S>",
    "<S> minimal surfaces , critical points of the area functional , are geometric motifs that appear across physics . from their early identification in soap films  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite , they have since been identified in a variety of places in condensed matter physics  @xcite including as the boundary in bicontinuous phases  @xcite , smectic liquid crystals  @xcite as well as in other areas such as monopoles and twistor theory  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the simplest minimal surface is the plane , which is also the only stable complete minimal surface  @xcite , and thus any other soap film surface must become unstable if the boundary is deformed beyond a critical conformation . </S>",
    "<S> studies of these instabilities in soap film annuli have typically focused on the catenoid  @xcite and , more recently , the mbius strip  @xcite . in both of these cases , </S>",
    "<S> as the boundary wire is varied one sees an instability that leads to a singularity and subsequent topological change in the surface  to two discs in the case of the catenoid and a single disc in the case of the mbius strip . </S>",
    "<S> more generally , these instabilities illustrate how surface tension serves to control morphology , and morphological changes , in a simple but generic system .    here </S>",
    "<S> we discuss instabilities in highly twisted minimal strips . </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate that the collapsing instabilities that one sees for the catenoid and mbius strip do not extend to an arbitrary number of twists . </S>",
    "<S> instead , one observes buckling instabilities that lead to non - singular transitions in the surface , similar in nature to the non - singular ` headphone ' transitions studied by courant  @xcite , and the creation of ribbon - like structures , analogous to those seen in the helicoid  @xcite . in geometric terms , the change in the morphology of the strip is from twist to writhe . </S>",
    "<S> we show that if the strip is non - orientable the instability is topologically frustrated and the resulting ribbon contains a topological defect or domain wall , which we show in a series of experiments , demonstrating the topological robustness of the effect . </S>",
    "<S> the simplest examples of domain walls are found in one - dimensional systems with two groundstates such as polyacetylene  @xcite and its mechanical analogues  @xcite , where the continuum limit is a @xmath0 field theory  @xcite . by mapping the structure of the non - orientable strip onto such a theory </S>",
