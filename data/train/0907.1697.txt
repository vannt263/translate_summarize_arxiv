    "<S> the information about sizes and nuclear density distributions in unstable ( radioactive ) nuclei is usually extracted from the data on interaction of radioactive nuclear beams with a nuclear target . </S>",
    "<S> we show that in the case of nucleus - nucleus collisions the values of the parameters depend rather strongly on the considered theoretical approach and on the assumption about the parametrization of the nuclear density distribution . </S>",
    "<S> the obtained values of root - mean - square radii ( @xmath0 ) for stable nuclei with atomic weights a = 12@xmath140 vary by approximately 0.1 fm when calculated in the optical approximation , in the rigid target approximation , and using the exact expression of glauber theory . </S>",
    "<S> we present several examples of @xmath0 radii calculations using these three theoretical approaches and compare these results with the data obtained from electron - nucleus scattering .    </S>",
