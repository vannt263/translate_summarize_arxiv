    "<S> we study numerically a stochastic differential equation describing an interface driven along the hard direction of an anisotropic random medium . </S>",
    "<S> the interface is subject to a homogeneous driving force , random pinning forces and the surface tension . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , a nonlinear term due to the anisotropy of the medium is included . </S>",
    "<S> the critical exponents characterizing the depinning transition are determined numerically for a one - dimensional interface . </S>",
    "<S> the results are the same , within errors , as those of the `` directed percolation depinning '' ( dpd ) model . </S>",
    "<S> we therefore expect that the critical exponents of the stochastic differential equation are exactly given by the exponents obtained by a mapping of the dpd model to directed percolation . </S>",
    "<S> we find that a moving interface near the depinning transition is not self - affine and shows a behavior similar to the dpd model .    </S>",
