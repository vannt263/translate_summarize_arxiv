    "<S> we apply reservoir engineering to construct a thermal environment with controllable temperature in an ultracold atomic rydberg system . </S>",
    "<S> a boltzmann distribution of the system s eigenstates is produced by optically driving a small environment of ultracold atoms , which is coupled to a photonic continuum through spontaneous emission . </S>",
    "<S> this technique provides a useful tool for quantum simulation of both equilibrium dynamics and dynamics coupled to a thermal environment . </S>",
    "<S> additionally , we demonstrate that pure eigenstates , such as bell states , can be prepared in the rydberg atomic system using this method .    _ introduction._solving the dynamics of open quantum systems is one important task addressed by quantum simulators @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the dynamics generated by the environment - couplings of the target system must be reproduced in the simulator  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> coherent  @xcite and incoherent approaches  @xcite have been developed for this _ reservoir engineering _ problem , for particular environments or degrees of freedom in the system . </S>",
    "<S> reservoir engineering  @xcite involves design of the environment or its coupling to the system , which can be applied in a variety of systems for entanglement generation and protection  @xcite , dissipative computation  @xcite and open quantum system simulation  @xcite . here , we consider reservoir engineering of an extreme nature : using optical control </S>",
    "<S> , we transform a typically non - thermal environment into a thermal environment with controllable temperature . </S>",
    "<S> access to an arbitrary - temperature thermal environment provides two - fold benefits . </S>",
    "<S> firstly , a system with a fixed - temperature environment can simulate more general non - equilibrium dynamics . </S>",
    "<S> secondly , thermal state preparation allows the study of interacting systems initially at equilibrium , which has motivated the design of digital algorithms  @xcite .    in this letter </S>",
    "<S> we show that a driven dissipative atomic environment , as shown in figure  [ fig : sketch_setup](a ) , provides a highly - tunable environment . in our setup , the steady - state populations of the system eigenstates can be precisely controlled by means of currently - achievable frequencies and intensities of the two lasers driving the environment atoms . </S>",
