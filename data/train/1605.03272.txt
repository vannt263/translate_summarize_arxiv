    "<S> we calculate the resonant inelastic x - ray scattering ( rixs ) response of the kitaev honeycomb model , an exactly solvable quantum - spin - liquid model with fractionalized majorana and flux excitations . </S>",
    "<S> we find that the fundamental rixs channels , the spin - conserving ( sc ) and the non - spin - conserving ( nsc ) ones , do not interfere and give completely different responses . </S>",
    "<S> sc - rixs picks up exclusively the majorana sector with a pronounced momentum dispersion , whereas nsc - rixs also creates immobile fluxes , thereby rendering the response only weakly momentum dependent , as in the spin structure factor measured by inelastic neutron scattering . </S>",
    "<S> rixs can therefore pick up the fractionalized excitations of the kitaev spin liquid separately , making it a sensitive probe to detect spin - liquid character in potential material incarnations of the kitaev honeycomb model .    </S>",
    "<S> quantum spins in a solid can , instead of ordering in a definite pattern , form a fluid type of ground state : a quantum spin liquid ( qsl ) @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> theory predicts a remarkable set of collective phenomena to occur in such qsls , including topological ground - state degeneracy , long - range entanglement , and fractionalized excitations . beyond their clear theoretical appeal </S>",
    "<S> , these exotic properties also find applications in the field of topological quantum computing @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> the kitaev honeycomb model is an exactly solvable yet realistic spin model with a qsl ground state @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> neighboring @xmath0 spins @xmath1 at the sites @xmath2 of the honeycomb lattice are coupled via different spin components along the three bonds connected to any given site . </S>",
    "<S> the hamiltonian is then @xmath3 where @xmath4 are the coupling constants for the three types of bonds @xmath5 , @xmath6 , and @xmath7 [ see fig .  [ fig-1](a ) ] . </S>",
    "<S> depending on @xmath4 , the model has two distinct phases . in the gapped ( gapless ) </S>",
    "<S> phase , the ground state is a gapped ( gapless ) qsl . </S>",
    "<S> the spins fractionalize into two types of elementary excitations in both phases : majorana fermions and emergent gauge fluxes .    from an experimental standpoint , finding a physical realization of the kitaev honeycomb model </S>",
    "<S> has proven to be a challenging task . </S>",
    "<S> so far , three types of honeycomb systems have been proposed as candidate incarnations of @xmath8 : the iridates @xmath9-a@xmath10iro@xmath11 with a @xmath12 na or li @xcite , the ruthenate @xmath9-rucl@xmath11 @xcite , and ultracold atoms in optical lattices @xcite . in the iridates and the ruthenate , </S>",
    "<S> however , a potential spin - liquid phase at low temperatures is preempted by magnetic order due to residual magnetic interactions beyond @xmath8 @xcite . nevertheless , </S>",
    "<S> since these interactions are typically small , the higher - energy excitations above the energy scale setting the magnetic order are expected to be governed by @xmath8 @xcite .    given that qsls are inherently defined in terms of a property that they do not have ( i.e. , magnetic order ) , their experimental identification and characterization are far from obvious @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> one potential hallmark of qsls is the presence of fractionalized magnetic excitations . for the kitaev spin liquid </S>",
    "<S> , it has been proposed that signatures of these excitations can be observed by inelastic neutron scattering ( ins ) @xcite and by raman scattering ( rs ) with visible light @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> however , both of these methods have important limitations . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , neither of them can directly probe the highly dispersive gapless majorana excitations . </S>",
    "<S> ins displays an overall energy gap and shows little momentum dispersion because it creates two immobile flux excitations that dominate the response . </S>",
    "<S> rs creates two majorana excitations only and measures their density of states , but it is an inherently zero - momentum probe and does not provide any information on their dispersion .     </S>",
