    "<S> we study the many - body localization transition in one - dimensional hubbard chains using exact diagonalization and quantum chaos indicators . </S>",
    "<S> we also study dynamics in the delocalized ( ergodic ) and localized phases and discuss thermalization and eigenstate thermalization , or the lack thereof , in such systems . </S>",
    "<S> consistently within the indicators and observables studied , we find that ergodicity is very robust against disorder , namely , even in the presence of weak hubbard interactions the disorder strength needed for the system to localize is large . we show that this robustness might be hidden by finite size effects in experiments with ultracold fermions .    [ [ introduction . ] ] introduction . </S>",
    "<S> + + + + + + + + + + + + +    over the years , substantial attention has been devoted to understanding the dynamical properties of disordered systems . </S>",
    "<S> interest on this topic goes back to a seminal paper by anderson in 1958 , who showed that sufficiently strong quenched disorder can produce localization of noninteracting particles , precluding transport in the thermodynamic limit  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> destructive interference is at the heart of this phenomenon . </S>",
    "<S> it is more prominent in lower dimensions , and , as a result , any nonzero disorder strength leads to localization in one and two dimensions  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> a fundamental aspect of anderson localization is that it occurs not only in the ground state but also in ( highly ) excited states .    </S>",
    "<S> because of the possibility of localization occurring in interacting systems , a phenomenon termed many - body localization ( mbl ) , disordered systems in the presence of interactions have received a lot of attention in recent years . early perturbative arguments @xcite and numerical simulations in the presence of strong interactions @xcite have triggered much research on this topic </S>",
    "<S> @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the mbl transition has also started to be explored in experiments with ultracold atoms @xcite and ions @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> the contrast between the properties of many - body eigenstates of interacting systems in the presence and absence of mbl makes apparent how remarkable mbl is . in generic isolated systems , </S>",
    "<S> interactions make it possible for the system to act as its own `` effective bath '' . </S>",
    "<S> if taken out of equilibrium , such systems evolve in time in such a way that observables equilibrate and can be described by traditional ensembles of statistical mechanics ( i.e. , they thermalize ) . </S>",
    "<S> this is just one of the manifestations of a phenomenon known as eigenstate thermalization @xcite , which , in short , means that the expectation value of an observable in an eigenstate of a many - body interacting system is the same as that in thermal equilibrium ( with the same mean energy as the eigenstate energy ) . </S>",
    "<S> eigenstate thermalization has been shown to occur in several many - body quantum systems @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> it is known not to occur only in integrable and mbl systems , i.e. , the latter two classes of systems generally do not exhibit thermalization even if they are thermodynamically large @xcite .    as a matter of fact , it was the latter property of mbl systems that was used in the experiments of ref .  </S>",
    "<S> @xcite to distinguish between the delocalized ( ergodic ) regime and the mbl one , for spinful fermions in the presence of a quasi - periodic potential . </S>",
    "<S> motivated by those experiments , in this work we study the mbl transition in hubbard chains with disorder . </S>",
    "<S> we contrast the predictions of quantum chaos indicators for the transition to those from thermalization and eigenstate thermalization . </S>",

    "<S> our main result from the analysis of quantum chaos indicators is that ergodicity is very robust against disorder . even for on - site interactions as weak as @xmath36 , we find that the disorder strength required to localize the system is or the order of twice the single - particle bandwidth . </S>",
    "<S> we studied the dynamics of those systems starting from a state of the form @xmath45 . </S>",
    "<S> we find that various experimentally relevant observables equilibrate in time scales @xmath76 , whenever the system is not close to the mbl transition . </S>",
    "<S> close to the mbl transition , equilibration times become orders of magnitude longer and might be difficult to reach experimentally . </S>",
    "<S> we have also studied the differences between observables after equilibration and the predictions of the microcanonical ensemble finding that , for the small lattice sizes that we are able to study ( @xmath77 sites ) , they increase smoothly as one increases disorder and can be large even far from the mbl transition . </S>",

    "<S> large system sizes , or a careful finite size scaling analysis , are needed . </S>",
