    "<S> we present an impedance engineered josephson parametric amplifier capable of providing bandwidth beyond the traditional gain - bandwidth product . </S>",
    "<S> we achieve this by introducing a positive linear slope in the imaginary component of the input impedance seen by the josephson oscillator using a @xmath0 transformer . </S>",
    "<S> our theoretical model predicts an extremely flat gain profile with a bandwidth enhancement proportional to the square root of amplitude gain . </S>",
    "<S> we experimentally demonstrate a nearly flat 20 db gain over a 640 mhz band , along with a mean 1-db compression point of -110 dbm and near quantum - limited noise . </S>",
    "<S> the results are in good agreement with our theoretical model .    </S>",
    "<S> josephson parametric amplifiers ( jpas ) have become a crucial component of superconducting qubit @xcite measurement circuitry , enabling recent studies of quantum jumps @xcite , generation and detection of squeezed microwave field @xcite , quantum feedback @xcite , real - time tracking of qubit state evolution @xcite and quantum error detection @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> although jpas based on josephson junctions embedded in a resonator @xcite regularly achieve 20 db power gain and quantum - limited noise , typical bandwidth is restricted to 10 - 50 mhz @xcite , making them suitable for single qubit measurements only . the rapid progress towards multi - qubit architectures @xcite for fault - tolerant quantum computing @xcite demands an amplifier with much larger bandwidth to enable simultaneous readout of multiple qubits with minimal resources .    </S>",
    "<S> there have been several attempts in this direction in recent years . </S>",
    "<S> one such attempt used a broadband impedance transformer @xcite to lower the quality factor of a lumped - element josephson oscillator which is the main component of a parametric amplifier . </S>",
    "<S> while the observed large bandwidth was qualitatively explained by a model consisting of a negative resistance @xcite coupled to a frequency dependent impedance , no clear prescription on the design principle was provided . </S>",
    "<S> a different approach using josephson non - linear transmission lines @xcite was recently demonstrated @xcite with nearly 4 ghz of bandwidth . </S>",
    "<S> however , this design requires fabrication of about 2000 nearly identical blocks of oscillator stages , demanding fairly sophisticated fabrication facilities . </S>",
