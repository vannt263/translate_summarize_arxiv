    "<S> scattering between different bands by impurities has a pair - breaking effect and introduces states inside the gap . </S>",
    "<S> we studied the statistics of disorder - induced subgap states in @xmath0 superconductors due to collective effects of impurities . numerically solving the two - band bogolyubov equations </S>",

    "<S> we located the mobility edge separating the localized and delocalized states for the 3d case and the crossover between the weak and strong localization regimes for the 2d case . </S>",

    "<S> a standard analytical approach to describe collective effects of impurities in superconductors is the self - consistent t - matrix approximation ( sctm ) . for the born limit </S>",
    "<S> , it was elaborated in the famous paper by abrikosov and gorkov@xcite and was later generalized for strong scattering @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> most theoretical works on collective effects of impurities in superconductors are based on this approximation , see , e.g. , the review [ ] . while giving a qualitative description of the impurity band , the sctm approximation has serious deficiencies . for small impurity concentrations </S>",

    "<S> another deficiency of the sctm description is that it ignores localization properties of the states . </S>",
