    "<S> a two - component analysis of spectra to @xmath0 gev / c for identified pions and protons from 200 gev au - au collisions is presented . </S>",
    "<S> the method is similar to an analysis of the @xmath1 dependence of @xmath2 spectra from p - p collisions at 200 gev , but applied to au - au centrality dependence . </S>",
    "<S> the soft - component reference is a lvy distribution on transverse mass @xmath3 . </S>",
    "<S> the hard - component reference is a gaussian on @xmath4 with exponential ( @xmath2 power - law ) tail . </S>",
    "<S> deviations of data from the reference are described by hard - component ratio @xmath5 which generalizes nuclear modification factor @xmath6 . </S>",
    "<S> the analysis suggests that centrality evolution of pion and proton spectra is dominated by changes in parton fragmentation . </S>",
    "<S> the structure of @xmath5 suggests that parton energy loss produces a negative boost @xmath7 of a large fraction ( but not all ) of the minimum - bias fragment distribution , and that lower - energy partons suffer relatively less energy loss , possibly due to color screening . </S>",
    "<S> the analysis also suggests that the anomalous @xmath8 ratio may be due to differences in the parton energy - loss process experienced by the two hadron species . </S>",
    "<S> this analysis provides no evidence for radial flow . </S>"
