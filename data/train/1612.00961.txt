    "<S> the shear modulus of jammed frictional granular materials with the harmonic repulsive interaction under an oscillatory shear is numerically investigated . </S>",
    "<S> it is confirmed that the storage modulus , the real part of the shear modulus , for frictional grains with sufficiently small strain amplitude @xmath0 discontinuously emerges at the jamming transition point . </S>",
    "<S> the storage modulus for small @xmath0 differs from that of frictionless grains even in the zero friction limit , while they are almost identical with each other for sufficiently large @xmath0 , where the transition becomes continuous . </S>",
    "<S> the stress - strain curve exhibits a hysteresis loop even for a small strain , which connects a linear region for sufficiently small strain to another linear region for larger strain . </S>",
    "<S> we propose a new scaling law to interpolate between the states of small and large @xmath0 .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction. _ when the packing fraction @xmath1 exceeds a critical value @xmath2 , amorphous materials consisting of repulsive particles such as granular materials , colloidal suspensions , foams , and emulsions turn into jammed solids which have rigidity . </S>",
    "<S> such a transition , known as the jamming transition , has been the subject of extensive studies over the last two decades @xcite . for frictionless grains , </S>",
    "<S> the pressure increases continuously from @xmath2 , while the coordination number @xmath3 exhibits a discontinuous transition at @xmath2 in the hard core limit @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> an assembly of frictionless grains under a simple shear exhibits a rheological continuous transition : the viscosity diverges as @xmath1 approaches @xmath2 below , while the yield stress increases continuously above @xmath2 @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the jamming transition is also characterized by the appearance of rigidity under an oscillatory strain above @xmath2 . for sufficiently small strain , </S>",
    "<S> the elastic modulus of frictionless grains is independent of the strain and the critical exponents for the jamming transition depend on the type of the local interaction @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> we call this regime the linear response regime . for large strain </S>",
    "<S> , recent studies @xcite have revealed that the real part of the shear modulus , the storage modulus @xmath4 , of frictionless particles decreases with increasing strain as a result of nonlinear response because of slip avalanches @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the present authors have proposed a scaling law of @xmath4 to interpolate between the linear and the nonlinear responses @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> note that the storage modulus as the ratio to the stress to the strain can be used even in the nonlinear response regime .    </S>",
    "<S> most of previous studies , however , assume that grains are frictionless , though it is impossible to remove contact friction in experiments of dry granular particles and the friction causes drastic changes in rheology such as a discontinuous shear - thickening with a hysteresis loop near @xmath2 @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> somfai et al . </S>",
    "<S> @xcite have numerically investigated the elastic moduli of a frictional system with the aid of the density of state , and found that @xmath5 with the bulk modulus @xmath6 in the linear response regime is proportional to @xmath7 , the excess coordination number relative to the isostatic value . </S>",
    "<S> they have also found that @xmath7 discontinuously appears at @xmath2 for frictional grains @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this suggests that frictional grains with the harmonic repulsive interaction exhibit a discontinuous change of @xmath4 at @xmath2 even in the frictionless limit contrast to a continuous change for frictionless grains @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> the difference of the linear elasticity between the frictionless limit and the frictionless case casts doubt on the expectation that the scaling laws for the elasticity of frictionless grains can be confirmed in experiments of grains with sufficiently small friction coefficient . the accessible shear strain in the experiment @xcite </S>",
