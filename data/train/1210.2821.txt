    "networks of identical nonlinear units which are coupled by their dynamic variables can synchronize to a common chaotic trajectory @xcite .",
    "this phenomenon is of fundamental interest in nonlinear dynamics , with applications in neural networks , coupled lasers , electronic networks and secure communication @xcite . for many applications ,",
    "the coupling signals are transmitted with a time delay which is much larger than the internal time scales of the individual units .",
    "a network of identical units with identical delay times can synchronize to a common chaotic trajectory without any time shift between these units ( zero lag synchronization ) @xcite .",
    "this can only occur for so - called weak chaos @xcite , i.e. , if the largest of the network decays with the inverse delay time .    a chaotic system with time delayed couplings ( including self - feedback for a single unit ) has special mathematical properties .",
    "the system becomes infinite dimensional and has a continuous spectrum of .",
    "the dimension of the chaotic attractor increases proportional to the delay time @xcite . in this contribution",
    "we investigate the attractor dimension close to the transition to chaos synchronization .",
    "the dimension of a chaotic attractor is not uniquely defined @xcite .",
    "we consider two attractor dimensions : the dimension @xcite , @xmath0 , which is determined from the spectrum of the , and the correlation dimension , @xmath1 @xcite .",
    "the dimension is conjectured to be identical to the information dimension , @xmath2 .",
    "this is known as the so - called conjecture @xcite .",
    "the information dimension in turn is an upper bound for the correlation dimension , @xmath3 @xcite . in this article",
    "we study these two attractor dimensions , @xmath0 and @xmath1 , for networks of identical nonlinear units coupled by their time - delayed variables .",
    "the delay time is much larger than the internal time scales , and chaos is generated by the coupling . increasing the coupling strength , the system has a transition to complete synchronization @xcite .",
    "we show that the dimension is discontinuous at this transition , it jumps from a high value for the unsynchronized to a low value for the synchronized chaotic attractor .",
    "this is a general results which holds for any networks with time - delayed couplings .",
    "we numerically calculate the dimension for networks of iterated maps and for two coupled semiconductor lasers . for networks of bernoulli maps we are also able to obtain analytical results . as a cross check the correlation dimension of the systems",
    "is computed as well and ( at least ) for networks of bernoulli maps it displays a clear discontinuity at the synchronization transition in agreement with the jump in the dimension . finally we calculate the jump in the dimension as a function of the size of the network .",

    "<S> the attractor dimension at the transition to complete synchronization in a network of chaotic units with time - delayed couplings is investigated . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , we determine the kaplan - yorke dimension from the spectrum of lyapunov exponents for iterated maps and for two coupled semiconductor lasers . </S>",
    "<S> we argue that the kaplan - yorke dimension must be discontinuous at the transition and compare it to the correlation dimension . for a system of bernoulli maps </S>",
    "<S> we indeed find a jump in the correlation dimension . </S>",
    "<S> the magnitude of the discontinuity in the kaplan - yorke dimension is calculated for networks of bernoulli units as a function of the network size . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore the scaling of the kaplan - yorke dimension as well as of the kolmogorov entropy with system size and time delay is investigated . </S>"
