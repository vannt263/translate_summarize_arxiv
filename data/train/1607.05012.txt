    "ce - based heavy fermion systems , whose electronic ground state properties are determined by strongly competing ruderman - kittel - kasuya - yosida ( rkky ) and kondo interactions , present very interesting physics @xcite .",
    "while rkky interaction tends to establish a long range order , kondo interaction causes screening of @xmath8 moments ( below a characteristic kondo temperature @xmath9 ) leading to a paramagnetic ground state and a quantum critical behavior is realized when the strength of these interactions become comparable , i.e. at the boundary between the magnetically ordered and paramagnetic states .",
    "a wide range of intriguing physical properties is seen in the proximity of a quantum critical point ( qcp ) @xcite .",
    "one can achieve qcp by tuning the electronic ground state of antiferromagnetically ordered systems using an external pressure , magnetic field or chemical doping and because of this ce - compounds are ideal for the study of the physics of quantum critical phenomena . for example , a pressure induced superconductivity is observed in the antiferromagnet cecoge@xmath0 at a critical pressure of 5.5  gpa and a partial substitution of ge by si leads to a non - fermi liquid behavior and quantum criticality in ceco(ge@xmath10si@xmath11)@xmath12 @xcite .",
