    "<S> we investigate properties of a topological mott insulator in one dimension by examining the bulk topological invariant and the entanglement spectrum of a correlated electron model . </S>",
    "<S> we clarify how gapless edge states in a non - interacting topological band insulator evolve into spinon edge states in a topological mott insulator . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , we propose a topological mott transition , which is a new type of topological phase transition and never observed in free fermion systems . </S>",
    "<S> this unconventional transition occurs in spin liquid phases in the mott insulator and is accompanied by zeros of the single - electron green s function and a gap closing in the spin excitation spectrum .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction- _ for many years , characterization of quantum phases has been done in terms of spontaneous broken symmetry and the corresponding order parameter , which is well known as the ginzburg - landau paradigm . </S>",
    "<S> recently , however , it has become clear that topological phases of matter are out of this framework . </S>",
    "<S> these topological states are gapped in the bulk and differ from trivial ones in the topology of their electronic states @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the big difference between them is the existence of gapless edge modes which are a source of a variety of intriguing physics . </S>",
    "<S> for instance , gapless edge modes play an essential role for the quantization of the hall conductivity @xcite , topological magnetoelectric effects in three - dimensional topological insulators in bismuth based compounds @xcite , realization of majorana fermions @xcite which are useful for quantum computation , _ </S>",
    "<S> etc_. in many cases , such topological phases have been regarded as free fermion systems .    </S>",
    "<S> recently , realizations of topological phases in @xmath0- and @xmath1- electron systems have been proposed @xcite , highlighting the importance of topological aspects of strongly correlated systems . </S>",
    "<S> indeed , this issue has been extensively studied so far @xcite , and an interaction - induced topological phase is proposed within a hartree - fock approximation @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore , it has been reported that correlation effects reduce the number of nontrivial phases ; two distinct topological phases of free fermion systems can be adiabatically connected if the intermediate states are correlated @xcite .    in spite of these extensive studies , </S>",
    "<S> there are still important issues to be solved concerning mott physics , which leads to a topological spin liquid in the strong interaction region . </S>",
    "<S> ( i ) one of them is how the gapless edge modes , which are a source of exotic and rich physics , are affected by correlation effects . </S>",
    "<S> it is proposed that edge states composed only of spinons appear in topological mott insulators @xcite . however , </S>",
    "<S> this has been demonstrated only for a particular model , and it is desirable to establish gapless edge spinons in topological mott insulators from a more general point of view . </S>",
    "<S> ( ii ) even for bulk systems , there is an important issue to be elucidated , i.e. , properties of a topological phase transition . from a mathematical view point </S>",
    "<S> , one can expect an unconventional topological phase transition without gap closing in the single - electron exitation spectrum ; a topological transition accompanied by zeros of the single - electron green s function is also possible since at the transition point , the green s function should be just singular@xcite . unfortunately , however , mott transitions reported so far in correlated topological band insulators are of first order , and the unconventional topological transition has not been reported yet . </S>",
    "<S> therefore , it is particularly interesting and important to explore whether unconventional topological phase transitions can really occur in correlated systems , and if they occur how the system behaves near such transitions .    in this article </S>",
    "<S> , focusing on one - dimensional ( 1d ) systems , we explore possibilities of these exotic phenomena with two theoretical probes for the topological structure , the winding number and the entanglement spectrum . our analysis reveals : ( i ) correlation effects change gapless edge modes in the single - electron excitations to those in the spinon excitations ( edge - mott states ) , and ( ii ) an unconventional transition occurs from a trivial band insulator to a topological mott insulator , which is accompanied by a gap closing in the collective spin excitations rather than in the single particle excitations . </S>",
    "<S> this transition is characterized by zeros of the green s function .    _ </S>",
