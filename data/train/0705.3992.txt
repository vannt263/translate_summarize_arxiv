    "for example , some finite geometry ldpc codes require a redundant parity check matrix to achieve good decoding performance with bp . recent works of schwartz and vardy@xcite , abdel - ghaffar and weber@xcite , hollmann and tolhuizen@xcite indicate that the stopping set weight distribution of a given matrix can be improved by appending linearly dependent rows to the original matrix .",
    "recent developments described in studies of the average weight distributions of ldpc codes , such as litsyn and shevelev@xcite@xcite , burshtein and miller@xcite richardson and urbanke@xcite , imply that _ ensemble analysis _ is a powerful method for investigating typical properties of codes and matrices , properties are not easy to obtain from an instance .",

    "the average stopping set weight distributions presented in @xcite and @xcite are a useful decoding performance measure ( for bp on bec ) of a given ensemble of parity check matrices .",

    "bec is not only of practical interest , but also can be considered as a good starting point for theoretical studies of performance analysis of bp for more general channels , such as binary input symmetric output channels@xcite .    in this paper , redundant random matrix ensembles ( abbreviated as _ redundant random ensembles _ ) are defined and their ss weight distributions are analyzed .",
    "the redundant random ensemble consists of a set of binary matrices with linearly dependent rows .",

    "<S> in this paper , redundant random matrix ensembles ( abbreviated as _ redundant random ensembles _ ) are defined and their stopping set ( ss ) weight distributions are analyzed . </S>",
    "<S> a redundant random ensemble consists of a set of binary matrices with linearly dependent rows . </S>",
    "<S> these linearly dependent rows ( redundant rows ) significantly reduce the number of stopping sets of small size . </S>",
    "<S> an upper and lower bound on the average ss weight distribution of the redundant random ensembles are shown . from these bounds , </S>",
    "<S> the trade - off between the number of redundant rows ( corresponding to decoding complexity of bp on bec ) and the critical exponent of the asymptotic growth rate of ss weight distribution ( corresponding to decoding performance ) can be derived . </S>",
    "<S> it is shown that , in some cases , a dense matrix with linearly dependent rows yields asymptotically ( _ i.e. _ , in the regime of small erasure probability ) better performance than regular ldpc matrices with comparable parameters .      </S>",
