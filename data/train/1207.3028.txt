    "<S> magnetic impurities affect the transport properties of the helical edge states of quantum spin hall insulators by causing single - electron backscattering . </S>",
    "<S> we study such a system in the presence of a rashba spin - orbit interaction induced by an external electric field , showing that this can be used to control the kondo temperature , as well as the correction to the conductance due to the impurity . </S>",
    "<S> surprisingly , for a strongly anisotropic electron - impurity spin exchange , kondo screening may get obstructed by the presence of a non - collinear spin interaction mediated by the rashba coupling . </S>",
    "<S> this challenges the expectation that the kondo effect is stable against time - reversal invariant perturbations .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction . _ </S>",
    "<S> the discovery that hgte quantum wells support a quantum spin hall ( qsh ) state @xcite has set off an avalanche of studies addressing the properties of this novel phase of matter @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> a key issue has been to determine the conditions for stability of the current - carrying states at the edge of the sample as this is the feature that most directly impacts prospects for future applications in electronics / spintronics . in the simplest picture of a qsh system </S>",
    "<S> the edge states are _ helical _ , with counter - propagating electrons carrying opposite spins . by time - reversal invariance electron transport </S>",
    "<S> then becomes ballistic , provided that the electron - electron ( e - e ) interaction is sufficiently well - screened so that higher - order scattering processes do not come into play @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> the picture gets an added twist when including effects from magnetic impurities , contributed by dopant ions or electrons trapped by potential inhomogeneities . </S>",
    "<S> since an edge electron can backscatter from an impurity via spin exchange , time - reversal invariance no longer protects the helical states from mixing . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , correlated two - electron @xcite and inelastic single - electron processes @xcite must now also be accounted for . as a result , at high temperatures @xmath0 electron scattering off </S>",
    "<S> the impurity leads to a @xmath1 correction of the conductance at low frequencies @xmath2 @xcite , which , however , vanishes in the dc limit @xmath3  @xcite . at low @xmath0 , for weak e - e interactions , </S>",
    "<S> the quantized edge conductance @xmath4 is restored as @xmath5 with power laws distinctive of a helical edge liquid . for strong interactions </S>",
    "<S> the edge liquid freezes into an insulator at @xmath6 , with thermally induced transport via tunneling of fractionalized charge excitations through the impurity @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> a more complete description of edge transport in a qsh system must include also the presence of a rashba spin - orbit interaction . </S>",
    "<S> this interaction , which can be tuned by an external gate voltage , is a built - in feature of a quantum well @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in fact , hgte quantum wells exhibit some of the largest known rashba couplings of any semiconductor heterostructures @xcite . as a consequence , </S>",
    "<S> spin is no longer conserved , contrary to what is assumed in the minimal model of a qsh system @xcite . however , since the rashba interaction preserves time - reversal invariance , kramers theorem guarantees that the edge states are still connected via a time - reversal transformation ( `` kramers pair '' ) @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> provided that the rashba interaction is spatially uniform and the e - e interaction is not too strong , this ensures the robustness of the helical edge liquid @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> what is the physics with both kondo _ and _ rashba interactions present ? in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we address this question with a renormalization group ( rg ) analysis as well as a linear - response and rate - equation approach . specifically , we predict that the kondo temperature @xmath7 @xmath8 which sets the scale below which the electrons screen the impurity @xmath8 can be controlled by varying the strength of the rashba interaction . </S>",
    "<S> surprisingly , for a strongly anisotropic kondo exchange , a non - collinear spin interaction mediated by the rashba coupling becomes relevant ( in the sense of rg ) and competes with the kondo screening . </S>",
    "<S> this challenges the expectation that the kondo effect is stable against time - reversal invariant perturbations @xcite . </S>",

    "<S> _ we have studied the combined effect of a kondo and a rashba interaction at the edge of a quantum spin hall system . </S>",
    "<S> the interplay between an anisotropic kondo exchange and the rashba interaction is found to result in a non - collinear electron - impurity spin interaction . </S>",
    "<S> a perturbative rg analysis indicates that this interaction may block the kondo effect when the electron - electron interaction is weakly screened . </S>",
    "<S> we conjecture that this surprising result @xmath8 challenging a time - honored expectation that the kondo effect is blind to time - reversal invariant perturbations @xcite @xmath8 is due to the breakdown of single - particle physics in one dimension . </S>",
    "<S> it remains a challenge to unravel the microscopic scenario behind this intriguing phenomenon . in the second part of our work we derived expressions showing how charge transport at the edge is influenced by the simultaneous presence of a magnetic impurity and a rashba interaction . </S>",
    "<S> a case study suggests that the predicted current - voltage characteristics should indeed be accessible in an experiment . </S>",
    "<S> most interestingly , its manifest dependence on the gate - controllable rashba coupling breaks a new path for charge control in a helical electron system .    _ </S>",
