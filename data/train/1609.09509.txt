    "<S> the simultaneous control of optical and mechanical waves has enabled a range of fundamental and technological breakthroughs , from the demonstration of ultra - stable frequency reference devices to the exploration of the quantum - classical boundaries in laser - cooling experiments . </S>",
    "<S> more recently , such an opto - mechanical interaction has been observed in integrated nano - waveguides and microcavities in the brillouin regime , where short - wavelength mechanical modes scatters light at several ghz . </S>",
    "<S> here we engineer coupled optical microcavities spectra to enable a low threshold excitation of mechanical travelling - wave modes through backward stimulated brillouin scattering . </S>",
    "<S> exploring the backward scattering we propose microcavity designs supporting super high frequency modes ( @xmath0  ghz ) an large optomechanical coupling rates ( @xmath1  khz ) .    </S>",
    "<S> brillouin scattering occurs due to the interaction of optical and mechanical waves and it leads to the inelastic scattering of pump photons to doppler red - shifted ( stokes ) or blue - shifted ( anti - stokes ) photons . in optical waveguides and microcavities </S>",
    "<S> this interaction occurs due to a combination of the photo - elastic effect  @xcite , induced by strain , and moving - boundary effect caused by the mechanical mode distortion of the optical boundaries  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> these two scattering processes are strongly influenced by optical and mechanical properties of the confining structure and can be tailored for various applications . </S>",
    "<S> for instance , the generation of anti - stokes photons , which is accompanied by destruction of phonon quanta , can be used to cool down mechanical modes in optical cavities  @xcite ; whereas the generation of stokes photons , which create phonons ( heating ) , may foster the development of high - coherence lasers , ultra - stable radio frequency ( rf ) synthesizers  @xcite , and broadband tuning of rf filters  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> such confinement - enhanced optomechanical interaction has been observed as stimulated raman - like  @xcite and brillouin scattering in a range of photonic structures  @xcite  where both energy and momentum conservation are directly fulfilled . in microcavities , however , the short roundtrip length and narrow linewidth further constrain the conservation laws , requiring both pump and scattered waves to be resonant with the optical cavity modes in order to ensure efficient brillouin scattering . </S>",
    "<S> these constraints have limited the current cavity demonstrations of brillouin scattering either to mm - scale cavities  @xcite , whose optical free - spectral range matches the mechanical resonant frequency ; or heavily multimode micro - cavities , whose distinct transverse optical modes  @xcite frequency difference accidentally matches the mechanical frequency , both at the cost of reduced optomechanical coupling .    here </S>",
