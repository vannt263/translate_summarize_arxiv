    "topological defects are nontrivial collective excitations that appear in a wide variety of systems in different physical branches including classical fluids , cosmology  @xcite , condensed matter  @xcite , optics  @xcite , cold atoms  @xcite , etc . despite the fact that they do not correspond to the ground states of the systems , these topological defects can be stable and live for a long time under certain physical conditions , which may have important applications for information processing . because of the tunability of the interaction coupling constants and the absence of disorder , ultracold atomic gases provide an ideal playground for the observation of these excitations .",
    "since the first realization of bose - einstein condensate with alkali atoms , various topological defects have been experimentally observed and/or theoretically investigated , such as scalar solitons  burger1999,denschlag2000,khaykovich2002 , vector solitons  busch2001,becker2008,liu2009,hamner2011 , domain walls  @xcite , vortices  @xcite , skyrmions  khawaja2001,leslie2009 , etc .",

    "we have investigated the main features of moving magnetic solitons in rabi - coupled binary bose - einstein condensates .",
    "two types of magnetic solitons have been identified and characterized : ( i ) @xmath0 solitons , which are connected to the unmagnetized static son - stephanov domain wall and exhibit a @xmath0 relative phase jump ; ( ii ) @xmath1 soliton , which are connected to @xmath0 solitons at a critical velocity , where the density of one component vanishes , and which do not exhibit a net jump of the relative phase .",
    "the complete phase diagram , the energy and the magnetic properties of these solitons are obtained in uniform matter , and their dynamical evolution is calculated in a 1d and 2d harmonic trap . a peculiar feature emerging from our calculations",
    "is that @xmath0 solitons evolve into @xmath233 solitons ( and vice versa ) during their oscillatory motion in a harmonic trap .",
    "we expect these novel examples of solitons can be observed experimentally in the near future . to observe them in ultracold atoms one can , for example , use a mixture of the @xmath272 and @xmath273 hyperfine components of the @xmath274 states of sodium , where @xmath275  @xcite and the exact miscibility of the atomic states can be reached  @xcite . for typical experimental parameters ,",
    "the chemical potential is @xmath276hz , and thus the critical rabi coupling is estimated as @xmath277hz .",
    "therefore , a weak rabi coupling ( the order of @xmath278hz ) is required to observe these magnetic solitons , a condition which can be achieved with current experimental techniques .",
    "although our discussion of magnetic solitons has been focused on the context of the binary bose - einstein condensates , similar physics can be easily generalized to and investigated in other physical systems which are governed by coupled gpes , such as fiber optics  @xcite , exciton - polaritons  @xcite , etc .",

    "<S> we study magnetic solitons , solitary waves of spin polarization ( i.e , magnetization ) , in binary bose - einstein condensates in the presence of rabi coupling . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the system exhibits two types of magnetic solitons , called @xmath0 and @xmath1 solitons , characterized by a different behavior of the relative phase between the two spin components . </S>",
    "<S> @xmath0 solitons exhibit a @xmath0 jump of the relative phase , independent of their velocity , the static domain wall explored by son and stephanov being an example of such @xmath2 solitons with vanishing velocity and magnetization . </S>",
    "<S> @xmath1 solitons do not instead exhibit any asymptotic jump in the relative phase . </S>",
    "<S> systematic results are provided for both types of solitons in uniform matter . </S>",
    "<S> numerical calculations in the presence of a one dimensional harmonic trap reveal that a @xmath0 soliton evolves in time into a @xmath1 soliton , and vice versa , oscillating around the center of the trap . </S>",
    "<S> results for the effective mass , the landau critical velocity and the role of the transverse confinement are also discussed . </S>"
