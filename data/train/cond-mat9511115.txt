    "<S> we study the conservation laws of both the classical and the quantummechanical continuum @xmath0 type systems . for the classical case </S>",
    "<S> , we introduce new integrals of motion along the recent ideas of shastry and sutherland ( ss ) , supplementing the usual integrals of motion constructed much earlier by moser . </S>",
    "<S> we show by explicit construction that one set of integrals can be related algebraically to the other . </S>",
    "<S> the difference of these two sets of integrals then gives rise to yet another complete set of integrals of motion . </S>",
    "<S> for the quantum case , we first need to resum the integrals proposed by calogero , marchioro and ragnisco . </S>",
    "<S> we give a diagrammatic construction scheme for these new integrals , which are the quantum analogues of the classical traces . </S>",
    "<S> again we show that there is a relationship between these new integrals and the quantum integrals of ss by explicit construction . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we go to the asymptotic or low - density limit and derive recursion relations of the two sets of asymptotic integrals . </S>"
