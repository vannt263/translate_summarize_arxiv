    "the gibbs distribution function has several successful applications in various domains of physics .",
    "it is a consequence of statistical mechanics which satisfies all postulates of equilibrium thermodynamics  @xcite .",
    "recently , however , a number of experimental data have appeared , where the asymptotic distribution differs from the gibbs distribution  @xcite .",
    "these deviations point out that either the equilibrium assumption fails for high energies or there is a need to construct the equilibrium statistical mechanics with non - gibbs distribution function .",
    "the tsallis statistics explores this second alternative on the base of a non - extensive entropy  @xcite .",
    "there has been an increasing interest in studying the non - extensive tsallis statistics .",
    "unfortunately , the generalized tsallis statistical mechanics have problems with the zeroth law of thermodynamics .",
    "several authors  @xcite have attempted to get around this difficulty , but remain unpersuasive .",
    "further attempts to solve this problem were undertaken by introducing an extensive representation for the non - extensive tsallis entropy  @xcite .",
    "as it will be shown in this work , this idea leads to a transformation of tsallis statistics into the rnyi statistics , but does not solve the problem of second law of thermodynamics for tsallis statistics : the conventional correspondence between temperature and heat is less obvious . in this paper",
    "we point out that the statistical mechanics with extensive rnyi entropy fulfill all requirements of an equilibrium thermodynamics and still have its canonical distribution function in a form with power - law asymptotics",
    ". it might be , on the other hand , less preferable regarding the stability property ( h theorem based on a generalized entropy ) @xcite .",
    "this paper is organized as follows . in sections",
    "ii and iii we discuss the microcanonical and canonical ensembles in the incomplete non - extensive statistics and in the rnyi statistics .",
    "the transformation rules from wang formalism to rnyi statistics are derived in section iv . in section v the properties of thermodynamical averages and the zeroth law of thermodynamics are discussed . finally , in section vi we apply these results for the classical ideal gas of massive particles .",

    "in conclusion we summarize the main idea of this paper . two power - law tailed representations for non - gibbs canonical distribution functions @xmath160 were obtained , the wang and rnyi statistics .",
    "the equilibrium distribution function @xmath70 both for wang and rnyi statistics satisfies the fundamental equation of thermodynamics .",
    "the transformation between them preserves the first and second laws of thermodynamics .",
    "the same is true for the legendre transformation structure and for the thermodynamical relations for intensive quantities , like pressure and chemical potential in the microcanonical and in the canonical ensemble .    as a general rule",
    "the averages associated with rnyi statistics coincide with those for wang statistics , but with a renormalized temperature and entropy .",
    "this makes the physical interpretation of these quantities more uncertain than it was in the gibbs case .",
    "the physical temperature in wang statistics reflects the transformation formula between wang and rnyi statistics .",
    "the non - extensive entropy of wang formalism changes , however , an important property of temperature , the satisfaction of the zeroth law of thermodynamics at dynamical and statistical independence request . the extensive rnyi statistics , on the contrary , passes even this test fulfilling all the requirements of equilibrium thermodynamics .",
    "the canonical distribution function also in this case is power - law tailed .",
    "we have studied the rnyi statistics for classical ideal gas of massive nucleons with a finite number of particles .",
    "the equation of state ( eos ) satisfies the ideal gas law ( @xmath161 ) familiar from gibbs statistics in this case , but the distribution function differs from the maxwell - boltzmann one : it has a power - law tail .",
