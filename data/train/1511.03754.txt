    "<S> we investigate the ( 0001 ) surface of single crystal quartz with a submonolayer of rb adsorbates . </S>",
    "<S> using rydberg atom electromagnetically induced transparency , we investigate the electric fields resulting from rb adsorbed on the quartz surface , and measure the activation energy of the rb adsorbates . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the adsorbed rb induces a negative electron affinity ( nea ) on the quartz surface . </S>",
    "<S> the nea surface allows low energy electrons to bind to the surface and cancel the electric field from the rb adsorbates . </S>",
    "<S> our results are important for integrating rydberg atoms into hybrid quantum systems and the fundamental study of atom - surface interactions , as well as applications for electrons bound to a 2d surface .    </S>",
    "<S> due to recent technological advances in fabrication and trapping , hybrid quantum systems ( hqs ) consisting of atoms and surfaces , as well as electrons and surfaces , are fast emerging as ideal platforms for a diverse range of studies in quantum control , quantum simulation and computing , strongly correlated systems and microscopic probes of surfaces . </S>",
    "<S> miniaturization of chip surfaces is necessary to achieve large platform scalability , but decoherence and noise emerge as serious challenges as feature sizes shrink @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> mitigating the noise is a fundamental and necessary step in realizing the full potential of hqss for quantum technologies .    </S>",
    "<S> combining ultracold rydberg atoms with surfaces for hqs is attractive because rydberg atoms can have large sizes , significant electric dipole moments and strong interactions . </S>",
    "<S> there have recently been a host of theoretical proposals for utilizing rydberg atoms near surfaces @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> progress on the experimental front has been hampered by uncertainties in characterizing interactions of atoms with surfaces , although some recent work in this regards are noteworthy @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> to take full advantage of rydberg atom hqss , a more complete understanding of surfaces is needed . </S>",
    "<S> one problem is that rydberg atoms incident upon metal surfaces can be ionized @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> a second major hurdle is the background electric fields ( e - fields ) caused by adsorbates @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> rydberg states are sensitive to adsorbate e - fields because they are highly polarizable @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> adsorbate e - fields have caused problems for other experiments as well , including casimir - polder measurements @xcite , and surface ion traps @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> a possible solution is to minimize the e - fields by canceling them out .    </S>",
    "<S> a convenient surface for applications in hqss is quartz because of its extensive use in the semiconductor and optics industries . despite numerous theoretical and experimental studies of bulk @xmath0 @xcite , </S>",
    "<S> the surface properties are not well understood . </S>",
    "<S> recent theoretical work has focused on surface reconstruction and the adsorption of water and graphene @xcite . </S>",
