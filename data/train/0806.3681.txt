    "_ sensor networks , whose nodes sample a physical field , like air temperature , light intensity , pollution levels or rain falls , typically represent an example of quasi - equally spaced sampling @xcite . indeed , often sensors are not regularly deployed in the area of interest due to terrain conditions and deployment practicality and , thus , the physical field is not regularly sampled in the space domain .",

    "<S> we study a class of random matrices that appear in several communication and signal processing applications , and whose asymptotic eigenvalue distribution is closely related to the reconstruction error of an irregularly sampled bandlimited signal . </S>",
    "<S> we focus on the case where the random variables characterizing these matrices are @xmath0-dimensional vectors , independent , and quasi - equally spaced , i.e. , they have an arbitrary distribution and their averages are vertices of a @xmath0-dimensional grid . although a closed form expression of the eigenvalue distribution is still unknown , under these conditions we are able ( _ i _ ) to derive the distribution moments as the matrix size grows to infinity , while its aspect ratio is kept constant , and ( _ ii _ ) to show that the eigenvalue distribution tends to the marenko - pastur law as @xmath1 </S>",
    "<S> . these results can find application in several fields , as an example we show how they can be used for the estimation of the mean square error provided by linear reconstruction techniques .    * edics : * dsp - reco signal reconstruction , dsp - samp sampling , spc - perf performance analysis and bounds . </S>"
