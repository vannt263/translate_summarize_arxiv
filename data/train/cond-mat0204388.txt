    "since it is often impossible to isolate a quantum system completely from its environment , the study of open quantum dynamical systems is necessary in many circumstances .",
    "techniques used to treat open quantum systems have been developed extensively and used in numerous applications .",
    "@xcite projection operator techniques @xcite and influence functional methods @xcite have been actively used in chemical physics in studies of electron , proton , and exciton transfer processes in the condensed phase and in biological systems .",
    "@xcite    in this paper , we investigate some of the circumstances under which an open quantum system can be described using mixed quantum - classical dynamics .",
    "quantum - classical dynamics is used to study condensed phase many - body systems @xcite , especially in the context of methods for treating non - adiabatic dynamics @xcite . in the studies presented here ,",
    "we consider a composite quantum mechanical system @xmath2 comprising two coupled subsystems @xmath0 and @xmath1 ; subsystem @xmath1 is assumed to be in direct contact with a thermal quantum mechanical bath @xmath3 .",
    "systems with this structure can arise in condensed phase dynamics where certain quantum degrees of freedom ( @xmath0 ) , for example , those associated with protons or electrons , may interact directly with neighboring solvent molecules ( @xmath1 ) , which in turn interact with the rest of the solvent ( @xmath3 ) .",
    "it is interesting to determine if the composite system may be treated as mixed quantum - classical system where the dynamics of subsystem @xmath1 , which is in direct contact with the heat bath , is classical in character while subsystem @xmath0 retains its quantum nature .",
    "some aspects of the dynamics of such quantum - classical composite systems have been investigated .",
    "@xcite    in order to investigate this problem we study a simple model system where subsystem @xmath0 depends on spin degrees of freedom and subsystem @xmath1 is a single harmonic oscillator bilinearly coupled both to subsystem @xmath0 and the bath . the bath is a collection of independent harmonic oscillators . while this is a highly simplified model of the realistic systems discussed above , it does capture some essential features of real coupled systems and is amenable to detailed analysis . due to its interaction with the bath , the dynamics of subsystem @xmath1 is dissipative and executes brownian motion .",
    "the brownian motion of a quantum particle governed by different potential functions and immersed in a thermal harmonic oscillator bath has been studied extensively by influence functional methods@xcite .",
    "the character of its dynamics is determined by the system - bath coupling and the spectral density and temperature of the bath . in the composite system we study , the dynamics of the @xmath1 subsystem oscillator",
    "is also influenced by the quantum dynamics of subsystem @xmath0 .",
    "the dynamics of subsystem @xmath0 is more complicated .",
    "it is also dissipative but its energy must be transmitted through subsystem @xmath1 to the bath .",
    "our results on the applicability of mixed quantum - classical dynamics are based on the nature of decoherence@xcite in the coupled system : when one subsystem behaves quantum mechanically and the other classically , there must be a mechanism making the former decohere slowly and the latter quickly .",

    "<S> the conditions under which an open quantum mechanical system may be described by mixed quantum - classical dynamics are investigated . </S>",
    "<S> decoherence is studied using influence functional methods in a model composite quantum system comprising two coupled systems , @xmath0 and @xmath1 , interacting with a harmonic bath with ohmic and super - ohmic spectral densities . </S>",
    "<S> subsystem @xmath0 is directly coupled to subsystem @xmath1 , while @xmath1 is coupled directly to the bath . </S>",
    "<S> calculations are presented for a model where subsystem @xmath0 is taken to be a two - level system which is bilinearly coupled to a single harmonic oscillator @xmath1 subsystem . </S>",
    "<S> the loss of quantum coherence in each subsystem is discussed in the extreme non - adiabatic regime where the dynamics of subsystem @xmath0 is essentially frozen . </S>",
    "<S> subsystem @xmath1 is shown to lose its coherence rapidly , while subsystem @xmath0 maintains coherence for longer time periods since @xmath1 modulates the influence of the bath on @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> thus , one may identify situations where the coupled @xmath2 system evolution effectively obeys mixed quantum - classical dynamics .    2 </S>"
