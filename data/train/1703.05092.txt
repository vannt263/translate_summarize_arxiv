    "different simple compact models using an energy independent transmission probability have been reported in the literature for sb - fets @xcite . in these models ,",

    "our model does not take into consideration the electron band  to  band tunneling and multiple reflections between the sbs located at the source and drain , which would lead to a slight increase of the total electron transmission probability to tunnel through the channel .",
    "also , the electron reflections from the sbs are not taken into account in the charge calculations , which would cause a small increase of the tube potential @xmath5 at a large drain  source voltage .",
    "we have developed an analytical model for the ballistic mosfet - like one - dimensional transistors with sb contacts .",
    "the model is free of numerical integration , therefore , it significantly decreases the evaluation times and eases the implementation of the model in verilog - a . the developed semi  empirical formulation of the subthreshold current allows to accurately describe the experimental data .",
    "the implementation of the analytical expressions in verilog - a , which is supported by almost all commercially available circuit simulators , provides fast , accurate spice - level design and verification of analog , radio frequency ( rf ) , and mixed - signal circuits facilitating accuracy / performance tradeoff .",
    "the total current is differentiable throughout all regions of operation .",
    "we have introduced the piece - wise approximation for fermi  dirac distribution function and modified the transmission probability using simple elementary functions , which allow to simplify the current calculations .",
    "our model can be successfully used for the analysis of experimental data as well as for performance predictions for different sb height , characteristic length , and either electron effective mass or band gap of channel material for quasi-1d sb - fets based on both semiconductor nanowires and cnts as needed for device optimization and variability studies .",
