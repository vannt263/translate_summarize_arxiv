    "<S> the influence of a small perturbation on a cavity mode plays an important role in fields like optical sensing , cavity quantum electrodynamics and cavity optomechanics . </S>",
    "<S> typically , the resulting cavity frequency shift directly relates to the polarizability of the perturbation . </S>",
    "<S> here we demonstrate that particles perturbing a radiating cavity can induce strong frequency shifts that are opposite to , and even exceed , the effects based on the particles polarizability . </S>",
    "<S> a full electrodynamic theory reveals that these anomalous results rely on a non - trivial phase relation between cavity and nanoparticle radiation , allowing back - action via the radiation continuum . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , an intuitive model based on coupled mode theory is presented that relates the phenomenon to retardation . </S>",
    "<S> because of the ubiquity of dissipation , we expect these findings to benefit the understanding and engineering of a wide class of systems .    </S>",
