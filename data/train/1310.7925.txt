    "<S> [ [ introduction . ] ] introduction . </S>",
    "<S> + + + + + + + + + + + + +    the study of two one dimensional gapless systems connected by some sort of interaction has become paradigmatic in modern quantum physics . </S>",
    "<S> it plays a particularly important role in the context of local quenches , transport through quantum dots , and the dynamics of magnetic impurities .    </S>",
    "<S> an essential feature of these systems is the existence of crossover scales , which play a role similar to the kondo temperature in the kondo problem  @xcite , and qualitatively separate weak and strong coupling regimes . </S>",
    "<S> these scales make the methods of conformal field theory inapplicable , and exact results are very scarce . at the same time </S>",
    "<S> , the presence of the crossover indicates very rich physics .    </S>",
    "<S> a case in point is the so - called kane - fisher problem  @xcite , where a single impurity in a luttinger liquid has the dramatic effect of decoupling the two sides ( repulsive case ) or disappearing ( attractive case ) as energy is swept across the crossover scale @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> this problem appears in various other guises , in particular in experiments where a fractional quantum hall fluid is pinched by a gate voltage  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> a quantity of crucial interest is then the entanglement entropy @xmath1 of a region ( of length @xmath2 ) bounded by the impurity with the rest of the system . in the case where the impurity ( defect ) is marginal  @xcite , or for some classes of strongly disordered critical points  @xcite </S>",
