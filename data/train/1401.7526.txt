    "<S> quantum state tomography suffers from the measurement effort increasing exponentially with the number of qubits . here </S>",
    "<S> , we demonstrate permutationally invariant tomography for which , contrary to conventional tomography , all resources scale polynomially with the number of qubits both in terms of the measurement effort as well as the computational power needed to process and store the recorded data . </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate the benefits of combining permutationally invariant tomography with compressed sensing by studying the influence of the pump power on the noise present in a six - qubit symmetric dicke state , a case where full tomography is possible only for very high pump powers .    </S>",
    "<S> _ _ introduction.__the number of controllable qubits in quantum experiments is steadily growing @xcite . yet , to fully characterize a multiqubit state via quantum state tomography ( qst ) , the measurement effort scales exponentially with the number of qubits . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , the amount of data to be saved and the resources to process them scale exponentially , too . </S>",
    "<S> thus , the limit of conventional qst will soon be reached . </S>",
    "<S> the question arises : how much information about a quantum state can be inferred without all the measurements a full qst would require . </S>",
    "<S> protocols have been proposed which need significantly fewer measurement settings if one has additional knowledge about a state , e.g. , that it is of low rank , a matrix product state or a permutationally invariant ( pi ) state @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> some of these approaches only require a polynomially increasing number of measurements and even offer scalable post - processing algorithms @xcite . yet , it is important to test the different approaches and evaluate their results for various quantum states . </S>",
    "<S> here we implement and compare four different qst schemes in a six - photon experiment . in detail , we perform the largest qst of a photonic multiqubit state so far . </S>",
    "<S> we use these data as a reference for a detailed evaluation of different tomography schemes , which enable the state determination with significantly fewer measurements . </S>",
    "<S> the recently proposed , scalable pi analysis is implemented here and thus enables us , for the first time , to also perform the numerical evaluation with polynomial resources only . </S>",
    "<S> we evaluate the convergence of compressed sensing ( cs ) schemes and show that the combination of pi and cs can further reduce the measurement effort , without sacrificing performance . </S>",
