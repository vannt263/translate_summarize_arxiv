    "<S> the peaceman  rachford scheme is a commonly used splitting method for discretizing semilinear evolution equations , where the vector fields are given by the sum of one linear and one nonlinear dissipative operator . </S>",
    "<S> typical examples of such equations are reaction - diffusion systems and the damped wave equation . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> we conduct a convergence analysis for the peaceman  </S>",
    "<S> rachford scheme in the setting of dissipative evolution equations on hilbert spaces . </S>",
    "<S> we do not assume lipschitz continuity of the nonlinearity , as previously done in the literature . </S>",
    "<S> first or second order convergence is derived , depending on the regularity of the solution , and a shortened proof for @xmath0-convergence is given when only a mild solution exits . </S>",
    "<S> the analysis is also extended to the lie scheme in a banach space framework . </S>",
    "<S> the convergence results are illustrated by numerical experiments for caginalp s solidification model and the gray  </S>",
    "<S> scott pattern formation problem .    </S>",
