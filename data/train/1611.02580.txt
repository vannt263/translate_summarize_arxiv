    "<S> the question of what determines the diffusion coefficient of a protein , or a macromolecular complex in general , is addressed by using a simple generic model of an asymmetric dumbbell that is made of two hydrodynamically coupled subunits . </S>",
    "<S> it is shown that equilibrium fluctuations can lead to an interplay between the internal and the external degrees of freedom and give rise to negative contributions to the overall diffusion coefficient . these fluctuation  </S>",
    "<S> induced contributions are controlled by the strength of the interactions between the subunits and their geometric characteristics . </S>",
    "<S> our findings provide a significant step toward understanding the diffusion properties of proteins , which play a crucial role in their function and intracellular organization .    </S>",
    "<S> _ introduction_. proteins and enzymes are able to perform highly precise functions in conditions dominated by thermal fluctuations and viscous hydrodynamics @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> understanding their transport and diffusion properties therefore constitutes a key problem in biophysics , which has been studied using a variety of techniques including dynamic light scattering @xcite , fluorescence correlation spectroscopy @xcite , and the recently developed stimulated emission depletion microscopy @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , single - molecule techniques were developed to elucidate other mechanical aspects of complex biological processes . </S>",
    "<S> recently , a more intriguing fundamental question , namely the effect of the chemical activity of enzymes on their center of mass diffusion has been addressed : enhanced diffusion has been reported for enzymes in dilute conditions when they are catalytically active @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this feature appears to be generic as it is observed for a wide class of enzymes , and thus opens the way to theoretical investigations of possible mechanisms that could explain it . in particular </S>",
    "<S> , the case where the enzyme catalyzes an exothermic reaction has been discussed , and different scenarios have been proposed and compared . </S>",
    "<S> the question of the hydrodynamic coupling of enzyme molecules to their environment has also been investigated theoretically , building on theoretical models that have been developed to describe molecular swimmers . despite these developments , </S>",
    "<S> the problem of enhanced diffusion of chemically active proteins has remained elusive .     </S>",
