    "<S> _ kepler _ will monitor enough stars that it is likely to detect single transits of planets with periods longer than the mission lifetime . </S>",
    "<S> we show that by combining the _ kepler _ photometry of such transits with precise radial velocity ( rv ) observations taken over @xmath03 months , and assuming circular orbits , it is possible to estimate the periods of these transiting planets to better than 20% ( for planets with radii greater than that of neptune ) and the masses to within a factor of 2 ( for planet masses @xmath1 ) . </S>",
    "<S> we also explore the effects of eccentricity on our estimates of these uncertainties . </S>"
