    "<S> we develop an instantonic calculus to derive an analytical expression for the thermally - assisted tunneling decay rate of a metastable state in a fully connected quantum spin model . </S>",
    "<S> the tunneling decay problem can be mapped onto the kramers escape problem of a classical random dynamical field . this dynamical field is simulated efficiently by path integral quantum monte carlo ( qmc ) . </S>",
    "<S> we show analytically that the exponential scaling with the number of spins of the thermally - assisted quantum tunneling rate and the escape rate of the qmc process are identical . </S>",
    "<S> we relate this effect to the existence of a dominant instantonic tunneling path . </S>",
    "<S> the instanton trajectory is described by nonlinear dynamical mean - field theory equations for a single - site magnetization vector , which we solve exactly . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we derive scaling relations for the spiky \" barrier shape when the spin tunneling and qmc rates scale polynomially with the number of spins @xmath0 while a purely classical over - the - barrier activation rate scales exponentially with @xmath0 . </S>"
