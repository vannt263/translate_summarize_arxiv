    "it has been proposed @xcite that the low - temperature dynamics of antiferromagnetic rings could be characterized by the coherent tunneling of the nel vector @xmath58 between the @xmath59 and @xmath60 directions .",
    "this phenomenon is the af counterpart of the tunneling of the magnetization ( qtm ) observed in single - molecule magnets @xcite .",
    "interestingly , while for the latter the tunneling time is so long that the dynamics becomes overdamped due to dissipation into the nuclear - spins subsystem , the time scale of nvt in af rings could be much shorter than the decoherence time . within the semiclassical framework",
    ", the condition for the occurrence of nel vector tunneling is that the action @xmath61 be much larger than unity .",
    "hence , nvt is expected to occur in long af rings with large anisotropy . while qtm can be probed by macroscopic magnetization measurements ,",
    "nvt is elusive and a direct experimental demonstration of its occurrence is lacking .",
    "nel vector tunneling has been recently shown to occur in a fe@xmath62 ring @xcite , but only indirectly through powder ins and bulk thermodynamic measurements .",
    "+ the possible occurrence of the nvt scenario in the low-@xmath12 dynamics of af rings can be assessed by extracting dynamical correlations functions with the method presented in the paper . indeed , in the case of nvt the",

    "by exploiting the cr@xmath1 prototype af ring as a benchmark , we have shown that the quantum spin dynamics of mnms can be fully unraveled by four - dimensional inelastic neutron scattering .",
    "the possibility to extract all two - spin dynamical correlation functions opens remarkable perspectives in the understanding of crucial but elusive quantum phenomena in several classes of magnetic molecules .",
    "in particular , supramolecular complexes containing linked molecular nanomagnets have been demonstrated to display entanglement @xcite and to be excellent candidates for implementing quantum - computation @xcite and quantum - simulation algorithms @xcite .",
    "the present method could be applied to reach a profound knowledge of the structure of the eigenstates of these complexes and of the entanglement between different molecular qubits .",
    "+ another emerging field is that of frustrated mnms , whose magnetic correlations and spin dynamics are particularly complex . in fact , their energy spectrum has a structure with large degeneracies which can not be rationalized in terms of simple paradigms ( e.g. , giant spins or rotational bands ) .",
    "however , a deep understanding of the spin dynamics of these systems is often hindered by the very large dimension of the associated hilbert space .",
    "the approach described here can be exploited to determine the full pattern of dynamical spin correlations in some of these systems .",

    "<S> molecular nanomagnets are among the first examples of spin systems of finite size and have been test - beds for addressing a range of elusive but important phenomena in quantum dynamics . </S>",
    "<S> in fact , for short - enough timescales the spin wavefunctions evolve coherently according to the an appropriate cluster spin - hamiltonian , whose structure can be tailored at the synthetic level to meet specific requirements . </S>",
    "<S> unfortunately , to this point it has been impossible to determine the spin dynamics directly . if the molecule is sufficiently simple , the spin motion can be indirectly assessed by an approximate model hamiltonian fitted to experimental measurements of various types . </S>",
    "<S> + here we show that recently - developed instrumentation yields the four - dimensional inelastic - neutron scattering function @xmath0 in vast portions of reciprocal space and enables the spin dynamics to be determined with no need of any model hamiltonian . </S>",
    "<S> we exploit the cr@xmath1 antiferromagnetic ring as a benchmark to demonstrate the potential of this new approach . for the first time we extract a model - free picture of the quantum dynamics of a molecular nanomagnet . </S>",
    "<S> this allows us , for example , to examine how a quantum fluctuation propagates along the ring and to directly test the degree of validity of the nel - vector - tunneling description of the spin dynamics .    </S>",
    "<S> mesoscopic systems can exhibit typical quantum dynamical phenomena , for instance by being able to tunnel through an energy barrier or by displaying long - lived coherent oscillations associated with superposition of states . </S>",
    "<S> this has attracted considerable interest for addressing fundamental issues and for the possible applications in quantum - information processing . </S>",
    "<S> molecular nanomagnets ( mnms ) are spin clusters where the topology of magnetic interactions can be tailored precisely at the synthetic level . </S>",
    "<S> they are metal - organic molecules containing a small number of magnetic ions whose spins are strongly coupled by exchange interactions . </S>",
    "<S> shells of organic ligands provide magnetic separation between adjacent magnetic cores , which behave as identical and independent zero - dimensional units@xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the magnetic dynamics are characterized by strong quantum fluctuations and this makes mnms of great interest in quantum magnetism as model systems to investigate a range of phenomena , such as quantum - tunnelling of the magnetization @xcite , nel - vector tunnelling ( nvt)@xcite , quantum information processing@xcite , quantum entanglement @xcite or decoherence @xcite . besides their fundamental interest , mnms are also the focus of intense research for the potential technological applications as classical or quantum bits@xcite and as magnetocaloric refrigerants@xcite . + </S>",
    "<S> a crucial aspect of the research on mnms is the understanding of their low - temperature spin dynamics , especially of those aspects which are a direct manifestation of quantum mechanics like the tunneling of the nel vector in antiferromagnetic rings . </S>",
    "<S> the most powerful technique to investigate the spin dynamics is inelastic neutron scattering ( ins ) . </S>",
    "<S> ins measurements on mnms are normally performed on polycrystalline samples . for these the cross - section </S>",
    "<S> is averaged over all molecular orientations , and only depends on the modulus of momentum transfer ( @xmath2 ) . </S>",
    "<S> the resulting ins spectrum is composed of peaks whose energies yield the eigenvalues and whose areas contain information on the corresponding wavefunctions . </S>",
    "<S> the dynamics is then extrapolated by fitting experimental spectra to a suitable spin hamiltonian @xmath3 and by performing calculations within the framework set by this model . </S>",
    "<S> however , this indirect approach does not work in many interesting cases when the hilbert space dimension is too large to diagonalise @xmath3 or when a large number of model parameters makes the fit non - univocal . even by using single - crystal samples , only limited and partially - integrated information on the @xmath4-dependence of the scattering function @xmath5 </S>",
    "<S> can be obtained by traditional time - of - flight neutron spectrometers , i.e. , carrying unitary detectors fitted on debye - scherrer rings . </S>",
    "<S> the implementation of large arrays of position sensitive detectors in cold - neutron time of flight spectrometers@xcite , together with the advances in software @xcite , has recently opened unprecedented possibilities in inelastic neutron scattering experiments on molecular nanomagnets allowing to determine the four - dimensional scattering function @xmath5 in a vast portion of the reciprocal space . </S>",
    "<S> this provides a much more selective characterization of the mnms when different candidate models can only be discriminated by the vectorial @xmath4-dependence of @xmath5 @xcite . </S>",
