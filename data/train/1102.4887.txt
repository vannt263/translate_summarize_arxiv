    "at low temperatures electronic correlations can cause anomalies at the semimetal - semiconductor ( sm - sc ) transition .",
    "@xcite half a century ago , mott  @xcite argued that in a sm with a very low carrier density the coulomb attraction between electrons and holes should lead to the spontaneous formation of electron - hole bound states ( excitons ) , and the system would become insulating .",
    "shortly afterwards , knox  @xcite noticed that a sc is unstable against the spontaneous formation of excitons if the exciton binding energy overcomes the gap energy separating valence and conduction band .",
    "both arguments suggest a new distorted phase , an exciton condensate known as the excitonic insulator ( ei ) , to be the crystal ground state .",
    "the sm - ei transition is mathematically similar to the bcs theory of superconductivity , while the sc - ei transition can be treated as a bose - einstein condensation ( bec ) of preformed excitons .",
    "hence , the ei is discussed in view of a bcs - bec crossover scenario in a solid .",
