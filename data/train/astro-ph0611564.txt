    "the cosmic microwave background ( cmb ) is expected to be linearly polarized by thomson scattering at the last scattering surface and after re - ionization .",
    "the detection of the cmb polarization has been reported by the degree angular scale interferometer ( dasi ) @xcite and recently by the wilkinson microwave anisotropy probe ( wmap ) satellite @xcite .",
    "measurements of the cmb temperature anisotropy with interferometers are made in the very small array ( vsa ) , the cosmic background imager ( cbi ) and many other experiments .",
    "the cmb polarization measurements with interferometers are on - going and planned in the experiments such as dasi , cbi and the millimeter - wave bolometric interferometer ( mbi ) @xcite . with many desirable features of an interferometer ,",
    "interferometers are more and more employed in cmb polarization experiments .",
    "the cmb polarization can be decomposed into gradient - like e mode and curl - like b mode @xcite .",
    "b mode polarization is not induced by scalar density perturbation but only tensor perturbation , while e mode polarization is induced by both @xcite .",
    "since tensor - to - scalar ratio is much smaller than unity in most inflationary models , b mode polarization is expected to be much smaller than e mode polarization .",
    "though there is complication by gravitational lensing @xcite , measurement of b mode polarization makes it possible probing the universe on the energy scale at inflationary period @xcite .",
    "the 1@xmath0 error on the parameter estimation can be forecast from the fisher matrix @xcite .",
    "it will be shown that in interferometric measurement of stokes parameter @xmath2 or @xmath3 , the 1@xmath0 error on e and b power spectra estimation varies with rotation of its polarizer frame .",
    "we will show that certain rotation of the polarizer frame from the associated baseline minimize the 1@xmath0 error on either e or b mode power spectra estimation . for a feedhorn array ( n@xmath12 ) , it is not always possible to realize the specific rotation of the polarizer frame from all the associated baselines .",
    "we will show that forming certain linear combinations of polarimetric visibilities is identical with physically rotating the polarizer frame to the optimal orientation , thereby enabling the b mode measurement optimization for a feedhorn array ( n@xmath12 ) .",

    "<S> the sensitivity of interferometers with linear polarizers to the cmb e and b mode are variant under the rotation of the polarizer frame , while interferometer with circular polarizers are equally sensitive to e and b mode . </S>",
    "<S> we present analytically and numerically that the diagonal elements of window functions for cmb e / b power spectra are maximized in interferometric measurement of linear polarization , when the polarizer frame is in certain rotation from the associated baseline . </S>",
    "<S> we also present the simulated observation to show that the 1@xmath0 errors on e / b mode power spectrum estimation are variant under the polarizer frame rotation in the case of linear polarizers , while they are invariant in the case of circular polarizers . </S>",
    "<S> simulation of the configuration similar to the dasi shows that minimum 1@xmath0 error on b mode in interferometer measurement with linear polarizers is 26 per cent of that in interferometric measurement with circular polarizers . </S>",
    "<S> the simulation also shows that the e / b mixing in interferometer measurement with linear polarizers can be as low as 23 per cent of that in interferometric measurement with circular polarizers . </S>",
    "<S> it is not always possible to physically align the polarizer frame with all the associated baselines in the case of an interferometer array ( n@xmath12 ) . </S>",
    "<S> there exist certain linear combinations of visibilities , which are equivalent to visibilities of the optimal polarizer frame rotation . </S>",
    "<S> we present the linear combinations , which enables b mode optimization for an interferometer array ( n@xmath12 ) .    </S>",
