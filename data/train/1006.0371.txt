    "<S> consider a measurable space with an atomless finite vector measure . </S>",
    "<S> this measure defines a mapping of the @xmath0-field into an euclidean space . according to the lyapunov convexity theorem </S>",
    "<S> , the range of this mapping is a convex compactum . </S>",
    "<S> similar ranges are also defined for measurable subsets of the space . </S>",
    "<S> two subsets with the same vector measure may have different ranges . </S>",
    "<S> we investigate the question whether , among all the subsets having the same given vector measure , there always exists a set with the maximal range of the vector measure . </S>",
    "<S> the answer to this question is positive for two - dimensional vector measures and negative for higher dimensions . </S>",
    "<S> we use the existence of maximal ranges to strengthen the dvoretzky - wald - wolfowitz purification theorem for the case of two measures . </S>"
