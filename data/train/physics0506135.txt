    "<S> radio frequency waves do not penetrate into a plasma and are damped within it . </S>",
    "<S> the electric field of the wave and plasma current are concentrated near the plasma boundary in a skin layer . </S>",
    "<S> electrons can transport the plasma current away from the skin layer due to their thermal motion . as a result , </S>",
    "<S> the width of the skin layer increases when electron temperature effects are taken into account . </S>",
    "<S> this phenomenon is called anomalous skin effect . </S>",
    "<S> the anomalous penetration of the rf electric field occurs not only for transversely propagating to the plasma boundary wave ( inductively coupled plasmas ) but also for the wave propagating along the plasma boundary ( capacitively coupled plasmas ) . </S>",
    "<S> such anomalous penetration of the rf field modifies the structure of the capacitive sheath . </S>",
    "<S> recent advances in the nonlinear , nonlocal theory of the capacitive sheath are reported . </S>",
    "<S> it is shown that separating the electric field profile into exponential and non - exponential parts yields an efficient qualitative and quantitative description of the anomalous skin effect in both inductively and capacitively coupled plasma . </S>"
