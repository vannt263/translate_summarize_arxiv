    "it has been well known for some time that the effects of the fermionic quantum fluctuations in space ",
    "time uniform abelian gauge fields can be effectively integrated out , resulting in a one  loop effective action @xcite@xcite .",

    "all the results can be extended to the case of the quantum fluctuations of scalar particles .",

    "<S> in strong ( quasi)abelian fields , even at the one  loop level of the coupling constant , quantum fluctuations of fermions induce an effective lagrangian density whose imaginary ( absorptive ) part is purely nonperturbative and known to be responsible for the fermion  </S>",
    "<S> antifermion pair creation . on the other hand , </S>",
    "<S> the induced real ( dispersive ) part has perturbative and nonperturbative contributions . in the one  loop case , </S>",
    "<S> we argue how to separate the two contributions from each other for any strength of the field . </S>",
    "<S> we show numerically that the nonperturbative contributions are in general comparable with or larger than the induced perturbative ones . </S>",
    "<S> we arrive at qualitatively similar conclusions also for the induced energy density . </S>",
    "<S> further , we investigate numerically the quasianalytic continuation of the perturbative results into the nonperturbative sector , by employing ( modified ) borel  pad . </S>",
    "<S> it turns out that in the case at hand , we have to integrate over renormalon singularities , but there is no renormalon ambiguity involved . </S>",
