    "<S> transitional pipe flow is modeled as a one - dimensional excitable and bistable medium . </S>",
    "<S> models are presented in two variables , turbulence intensity and mean shear , that evolve according to established properties of transitional turbulence . </S>",
    "<S> a continuous model captures the essence of the puff - slug transition as a change from excitability to bistability . a discrete model , that additionally incorporates turbulence locally as a chaotic repeller , reproduces almost all large - scale features of transitional pipe flow . </S>",
    "<S> in particular it captures metastable localized puffs , puff splitting , slugs , a continuous transition to sustained turbulence via spatiotemporal intermittency ( directed percolation ) , and a subsequent increase in turbulence fraction towards uniform , featureless turbulence .    </S>",
    "<S> the transition to turbulence in pipe flow has been the subject of study for over 100 years  @xcite , both because of its fundamental role in fluid mechanics and because of the detrimental consequences of turbulent transition in many practical situations . </S>",
    "<S> there are at least two features of the problem that make it fascinating , but also difficult to analyze . </S>",
    "<S> the first is that when turbulence appears , it appears abruptly  @xcite , and not through a sequence of transitions each increasing the dynamical complexity of the flow . </S>",
    "<S> turbulence is triggered by finite - sized disturbances to linearly stable laminar flow  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this hysteretic , or subcritical , aspect of the problem limits the applicability of linear and weakly nonlinear theories . </S>",
    "<S> the second complicating feature is the intermittent form turbulence takes in the transitional regime near the minimum reynolds number ( non - dimensional flow rate ) for which turbulence is observed  @xcite . in sufficiently long pipes , localized patches of turbulence ( puffs ) may persist for extremely long times before abruptly reverting to laminar flow @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in other cases , turbulent patches may spread by contaminating nearby laminar flow ( puff splitting and slugs )  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> while minimal models have been very useful in understanding generic features of intermittency in subcritical shear flows @xcite , such models do not capture the puffs , puff splitting , and slugs that are essential to the character of pipe flow . in this paper </S>",
    "<S> i argue that transitional pipe flow should be viewed in the context of excitable and bistable media . with this perspective </S>",
    "<S> i present models , based on the interaction between turbulence and the mean shear , that both capture and organize most large - scale features of transitional pipe flow .     </S>",

    "<S> puffs , puff splitting , and slugs  without appealing in detail to the underlying structures within shear turbulence . </S>",
    "<S> this approach is similar to that expounded by pomeau  @xcite , and considered elsewhere  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the important insight here is the close connection between subcritical shear flows and excitable systems . </S>",
    "<S> the view is that a great many features of intermittent pipe flow can be understood as a generic consequence of the transition from excitability to bistability where the turbulent branch is itself locally a chaotic repeller . </S>",
    "<S> i have introduced particular model equations to express these ideas in simple form . </S>",
    "<S> while phenomena have been demonstrated with specific parameters , the phenomena are robust . </S>",
    "<S> the challenge for future work is to obtain more quantitatively accurate models , perhaps utilizing full simulations of pipe flow , since ultimately the fluid mechanics of shear turbulence ( streaks and streamwise vortices ) is important for the details of the process . </S>",
    "<S> more challenging is to extend this effort to other subcritical shear flows , such a plane channel , plane couette , and boundary - layer flows . </S>",
