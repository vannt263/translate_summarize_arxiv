    "in a quantum spin - hall insulator ( qshi ) @xcite , the only coducting channels are the edge states that propagate in both directions with opposite spins , carrying no spin polarization in equilibrium .",
    "if , however , the equilibrium between the right- and left - movers is broken by a bias voltage , a qshi edge can acquire spin polarization , acting as a quantum spintronic device @xcite .",
    "this way of producing edge spin polarization does not work for superconducting source and drain reservoirs , as they support electric current at perfect equilibrium between the right- and left - movers . at the same time , the ability to generate spin polarization in superconducting qshi systems is crucial for many their potential applications , e.g. , as hosts of majorana zero modes @xcite  non - abelian anyons required for topological quantum computing @xcite .",
    "possible ways to achieve the required spin polarization are to use ferromagnetic insulators or the zeeman effect of an external magnetic field .",
    "unfortunately , combining qshis , superconductors and ferromagnetic insulators with controllable magnetization turns out to tremendously difficult .",
    "exploiting the zeeman effect is also not always an option because of the material limitations on the values of the carrier g - factor . besides",
    ", it is impossible to completely suppress the orbital magnetic - field effect .",
    "all this makes the search for alternative sources of spin polarization in superconducting qshis an important outstanding problem .",
    "the aim of this work is to point out a very efficient mechanism of spin polarization based on the magnetoelectric ( edelstein ) effect . here",

    "<S> hybrid structures of quantum spin - hall insulators ( qshis ) and superconductors present a unique opportunity to access dissipationless topological states of matter , which , however , is frequently hindered by the lack of control over the spin polarization in qshis . </S>",
    "<S> we propose a very efficient spin - polarization mechanism based on the magnetoelectric ( edelstein ) effect in superconducting qshi structures . </S>",
    "<S> it acts akin to the zeeman splitting in an external magnetic field , but with an effective g - factor of order of 1000 . </S>",
    "<S> we show that the edelstein spin splitting triggers @xmath0 josephson transitions with a superharmonic @xmath1-periodic current - phase relationship at the transition . </S>",
    "<S> this manifests itself as a crossover from @xmath2 - to @xmath3 - periodic magnetic oscillations of the josephson current in a superconducting loop ( @xmath4 is the magnetic flux quantum ) . </S>",
    "<S> such controllable @xmath0 transitions offer new perspectives for dissipationless spintronic devices and engineering flux qubits . </S>"
