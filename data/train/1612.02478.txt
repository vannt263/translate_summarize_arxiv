    "absence of spontaneous magnetization in the two - dimensional quantum heisenberg model was proven by mermin and wagner in their seminal article @xcite . their result revealed some fundamental properties of two - dimensional systems with continuous symmetry .",
    "it was subsequently extended and generalized in several directions . in @xcite ,",
    "absence of continuous symmetry breaking in extremal gibbs states was established for a large class of models .",
    "fisher and jasnow @xcite first explained why there is nt any long - range order in some two - dimensional models with continuous symmetries ; the two - point correlation functions of the heisenberg model were shown to decay at least logarithmically .",
    "their decay is , however , expected to be power - law .",
    "this was first proven by mcbryan and spencer @xcite for the classical rotor model in a short and lucid article .",
    "their proof is based on complex rotations and extends to a large family of classical spin systems .",
    "shlosman obtained similar results with a different method @xcite .",
    "power - law decay was established for some quantum systems in @xcite ; the proofs in these papers involve the fourier transform of correlations and the bogolubov inequality , and they are limited to regular two - dimensional lattices .",
    "a more general result was obtained by koma and tasaki , using complex rotations @xcite .",
    "their proof was simplified in @xcite .    in the work presented in @xcite specific models",
    "are considered , and proofs rely on explicit settings .",
    "the method of proof in @xcite is however robust , and it is not hard to convince oneself that it can be expected to apply to a much broader class of models . our goal in the present article is to propose a general setting accommodating many models of interest and prove a general result concerning the decay of correlations . as a consequence ,",
