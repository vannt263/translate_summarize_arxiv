    "<S> we study the spin correlations of a few fermions in a quasi one - dimensional trap . </S>",
    "<S> exact diagonalization calculations demonstrate that repulsive interactions between the two species drives ferromagnetic correlations . </S>",
    "<S> the ejection probability of an atom provides an experimental probe of the spin correlations . with more than five atoms trapped , </S>",
    "<S> the system approaches the itinerant stoner limit . </S>",
    "<S> losses to feshbach molecules are suppressed by the discretization of energy levels when fewer than seven atoms are trapped .    </S>",
    "<S> recent experimental advances allow investigators to confine up to twenty atoms in a single trap and address their quantum state  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this precise control enabled the heidelberg group to confirm the fundamental physics of short range repulsion  @xcite . here </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate that just such repulsive interactions acting between a few fermions allows us to construct a hamiltonian analogous to the stoner model  @xcite and offers experimentalists an opportunity to observe the emergent ferromagnetic correlations without losses to feshbach molecules .    </S>",
    "<S> the itinerant ferromagnet predicted by the stoner hamiltonian has never been cleanly realized and studied in the solid state . </S>",
    "<S> however , it was proposed  @xcite that a ferromagnetic phase could be created with a fermionic cold atom gas . </S>",
    "<S> an experiment by the mit group displayed signatures compatible with ferromagnetism  @xcite , but the observations were later explained by a loss mechanism  @xcite . to circumvent losses </S>",
    "<S> it has been suggested that magnetic correlations could be explored in systems with a mass imbalance  @xcite , two - dimensional geometry  @xcite , spin spirals  @xcite , or flux lattices  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> here we demonstrate how a quasi one - dimensional system containing only a few fermions could avoid the problems associated with losses and display ferromagnetic correlations . </S>",
