    "<S> we study the properties of spin systems realized by cold polar molecules interacting via dipole - dipole interactions in two dimensions . using a spin wave theory , that allows for the full treatment of the characteristic long - distance tail of the dipolar interaction , </S>",
    "<S> we find several anomalous features in the ground state correlations and the spin wave excitation spectrum , which are absent in their counterparts with short - range interaction . </S>",
    "<S> the most striking consequence is the existence of true long - range order at finite temperature for a two dimensional phase with a broken @xmath0 symmetry .    </S>",
    "<S> the foundation for understanding the behavior and properties of quantum matter is based on models with short - range interactions . </S>",
    "<S> experimental progress in cooling polar molecules @xcite and atomic gases with large magnetic dipole moments @xcite has however increased the interest in systems with strong dipole - dipole interactions . </S>",
    "<S> while many properties of quantum systems with dipole - dipole interactions derive from our understanding of systems with short - range interactions , the dipole - dipole interaction can give rise to phenomena not present in their short - range counterparts . </S>",
    "<S> prominent examples are the description of dipolar bose - einstein condensates , where the contribution of the dipolar interaction can not be included in the @xmath1-wave scattering length @xcite , and the absence of a first order phase transition with a jump in the density @xcite . in this letter , we demonstrate anomalous behavior in two dimensional spin systems with dipolar interactions realized by polar molecules in optical lattices .    </S>",
    "<S> a remarkable property of cold polar molecules confined into two dimensions is the potential formation of a crystalline phase for strong dipole - dipole interactions @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in contrast to a wigner crystal with coulomb interactions @xcite , the crystalline phase exhibits the conventional behavior expected for a crystal realized with a short - range repulsion and the characteristic @xmath2 behavior of the dipole interaction can be truncated at distances involving several interparticle separations . </S>",
    "<S> several strongly correlated phases have been predicted , which behave in analogy to systems with interactions extending over a finite range , such as a haldane phase @xcite , supersolids @xcite , pair supersolids in bilayer systems @xcite , valence bond solids @xcite , as well as @xmath3-wave superfluidity @xcite , and self - assembled structures in multilayer setups @xcite . on the other hand , </S>",
    "<S> it has recently been demonstrated that polar molecules in optical lattices are also suitable for emulating quantum phases of two dimensional spin models @xcite .    here , we demonstrate that such spin models with dipole - dipole interactions exhibit several anomalous features , which are not present in their short - range counterparts . </S>",
    "<S> the analysis is based on analytical spin wave theory , which allows for the full treatment of the @xmath2 tail of the dipole - dipole interactions . </S>",
    "<S> we find that the excitation spectrum exhibits anomalous behavior at low momenta , which gives rise to unconventional dynamic properties of the spin wave excitations . </S>",
    "<S> remarkably , we derive from this anomalous behavior the existence of a long - range ordered ferromagnetic phase at finite temperatures ; this finding is consistent with the well - known mermin - wagner theorem as the latter does not exclude order for interactions with a @xmath4 tail , where @xmath5 @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we show that the dipole - dipole interaction gives rise to algebraic correlations even in gapped ground states , in agreement with recent predictions @xcite .    </S>",
