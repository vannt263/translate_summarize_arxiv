    "<S> this article describes a method to compute successive convex approximations of the convex hull of a set of points in @xmath0 that are the solutions to a system of polynomial equations over the reals . </S>",
    "<S> the method relies on sums of squares of polynomials and the dual theory of moment matrices . </S>",
    "<S> the main feature of the technique is that all computations are done modulo the ideal generated by the polynomials defining the set to the convexified . </S>",
    "<S> this work was motivated by questions raised by lovsz concerning extensions of the theta body of a graph to arbitrary real algebraic varieties , and hence the relaxations described here are called theta bodies . </S>",
    "<S> the convexification process can be seen as an incarnation of lasserre s hierarchy of convex relaxations of a semialgebraic set in @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> when the defining ideal is real radical the results become especially nice . </S>",
    "<S> we provide several examples of the method and discuss convergence issues . finite convergence , especially after the first step of the method , can be described explicitly for finite point sets . </S>"
