    "<S> out of thermal equilibrium , an environment imposes effective mechanical forces on microscopical nanofabricated devices , chemical or biological systems . </S>",
    "<S> here we address the question of how to calculate these forces together with the response of the system from the first principles . </S>",
    "<S> we show that an ideal gas - like environment , even near thermal equilibrium , can enforce a specific steady state on the system by creating effective potentials in otherwise homogeneous space . </S>",
    "<S> an example of stable and unstable rectifications of thermal fluctuations is presented using modified feynman - smoluchowski ratchet with two degrees of freedom . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , the stability of a steady configuration depends on its chiral symmetry . </S>",
    "<S> the transition rate probabilities and the corresponding kinetic equations are derived for a complex mechanical system with arbitrary degrees of freedom . </S>",
    "<S> this work therefore extends the applicability of mechanical systems as a toy model playground of statistical physics for multiple degrees of freedom active and living matter .    </S>",
