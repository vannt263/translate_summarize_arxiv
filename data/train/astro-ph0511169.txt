    "<S> here we focus on the optical spectroscopy program for 53 galaxies in the sample . </S>",
    "<S> we report 35 spectroscopic redshifts , based on observations with the very large telescope ( vlt ) , the new technology telescope ( ntt ) and the australian national university s 2.3 m telescope ; they include five radio galaxies with @xmath0 . </S>",
    "<S> spectroscopic redshifts for the remaining 18 galaxies could not be confirmed : three are occulted by galactic stars , eight show continuum emission but no discernible spectral lines , whilst the remaining seven galaxies are undetected in medium - deep vlt integrations . </S>",
    "<S> the latter are either at very high redshift ( @xmath1 ) or heavily obscured by dust . </S>",
    "<S> a discussion of the efficiency of the uss technique is presented . </S>",
    "<S> based on the similar space density of @xmath0 radio galaxies in our sample compared with other uss - selected samples , we argue that uss selection at 8431400mhz is an efficient and reliable technique for finding distant radio galaxies .    </S>",
