    "since the creation of a single vortex in bose - einstein condensates in harmonic traps @xcite , experimental techniques have rapidly created arrays of several hundred vortices @xcite . in these experiments ,",
    "the condensate rotates at angular velocities @xmath2 that typically approach the high - rotation limit @xmath3 , where the centrifugal effect from the rotation would cancel the radial confinement of the trap @xcite .    for @xmath4 in a pure harmonic radial trap , the combined effective radial trap vanishes and",
    "the thomas - fermi ( tf ) radius of the condensate diverges .",
    "moreover , as ho @xcite pointed out , the single - particle hamiltonian in a rapidly rotating harmonic trap is analogous to that of a charged particle in a uniform magnetic field , and all particles should condense into the lowest landau level ( lll ) .",
    "this insight has stimulated several experimental @xcite and theoretical @xcite works .",
    "an additional radial trap potential , stronger than harmonic , opens new possibilities in the study of rapidly rotating bose - einstein condensates , because it can provide confinement even for @xmath5 .",
    "a quartic potential is a simple choice @xcite that has recently been realized experimentally with a blue - detuned laser directed along the axial direction of an elongated condensate @xcite .",
    "not only does the combined potential overcome the limit on the angular velocity @xmath2 , but it also enriches the physics of rapidly rotating condensates with the possibility of the interesting new vortex configurations .    in such a combined potential , no quantized vortices appear in the condensate at sufficiently slow rotations @xmath6 . with increasing @xmath2 @xmath7 , the condensate passes through a sequence of states with many vortices that eventually form a large vortex lattice .",
    "these states are essentially the same as those in a pure harmonic trap .",
    "the new vortex phases begin to appear as the condensate continues to expand radially for @xmath8 .",
    "specifically it develops a central hole at a critical angular velocity @xmath0 that depends on the interaction strength and the trap potential . for larger values of @xmath9 ,",
    "the condensate forms an annulus , whose mean radius expands and whose width continues to shrink with increasing @xmath2 .",
    "finally a `` giant '' vortex state with pure irrotational flow is expected to appear at @xmath1 , when all the singly quantized vortices disappear from the annulus .",
    "previous theoretical work has either relied on numerical methods @xcite or dealt only with weak interactions and small anharmonicities @xcite .",
    "in contrast , we here use a time - dependent variational analysis to study the behavior of an annular condensate with a single ring of vortices in the annulus in the tf limit .",
    "as @xmath2 increases beyond @xmath0 , the width of the annular condensate becomes increasingly narrow , and above some value of angular speed @xmath2 ( smaller than @xmath1 ) the annular condensate can support only a one - dimensional array of vortices arranged in a single ring @xcite .",
    "the transition to a giant vortex state is investigated by comparing the energy of these two equilibrium states ( a state with a single ring of vortices and a giant vortex state with pure irrotational flow ) and also by studying the dynamical stability of small - amplitude normal mode excitations of the vortex array .",
