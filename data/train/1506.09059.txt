    "a number of experimental observations show that more complex diffusion processes in which the mean - square displacement is not proportional to the time @xmath0 take place in various systems . a broad family of processes described by certain deviations from the classical brownian linear time dependence of the centered second moment",
    "is called anomalous diffusion .",
    "anomalous diffusion in one dimension is characterized by the occurrence of a mean square displacement of the form @xmath1 which deviates from the linear brownian dependence on time @xcite .",
    "( [ eq : anom - diff ] ) introduce the anomalous diffusion coefficient @xmath2 .",
    "such a deviation from classical diffusive behavior can be observed in many systems @xcite and leads to many interesting physical properties @xcite .",
    "applications of anomalous diffusion have been found in physics , chemistry and biology @xcite . in general ,",
    "anomalous diffusion occurs in complex structures exhibiting the presence of long - range correlations or memory effects @xcite . in the physics of complex systems , anomalous transport properties and their description",
    "have attracted considerable interest starting with the pioneering papers of montroll and his collaborators @xcite .",
    "an important subclass of anomalous diffusion processes constitute subdiffusion processes , characterized by the sublinear dependence with the power - law exponent in the range @xmath3 . in this situation no finite mean jump time @xmath4 exists @xcite .",
    "subdiffusion processes have been reported in condensed matter systems @xcite , ecology @xcite , and biology @xcite .",
    "continuous time random walks ( ctrws ) with on - site waiting - time distributions falling slowly as @xmath5 and lacking the first moment predicts a subdiffusive behavior and is a powerful tool to describe systems which display subdiffusion @xcite .",
    "starting from the generalized master equation or from the ctrw the fractional fokker - planck equation can be rigorously derived @xcite .",
    "fractional fokker - planck equation provides a useful approach for the description of transport dynamics in complex systems which are governed by anomalous diffusion @xcite and nonexponential relaxation patterns @xcite .",
    "it has been used to model dynamics of protein systems and for reactions occurring in disordered media @xcite .",
    "description equivalent to a fractional fokker - planck equation consist of a markovian dynamics governed by an ordinary langevin equation but proceeding in an auxiliary , operational time instead of the physical time @xcite .",
    "this markovian process is subordinated to the process defining the physical time ; the subordinator introduces memory effects @xcite .",
    "other approaches for the theoretical description of the subdiffusion use the generalized langevin equation @xcite , fractional brownian motion @xcite , or the langevin equation with multiplicative noise @xcite .",
    "the traditional ctrw provides a homogeneous description of the medium .",
    "more complex situation is the diffusion in nonhomogeneous media , for example diffusion on fractals and multifractals @xcite .",
    "nonhomogeneous systems exhibit not only subdiffusion related to traps , but also enhanced diffusion can occur : for example , transport of interacting particles in a weakly disordered media is superdiffusive due to the disorder and subdiffusive without the disorder @xcite .",

    "<S> the corresponding probability density satisfies the time - fractional fokker - planck equations . in the homogeneous systems </S>",

    "<S> we consider nonhomogeneous systems and show that in such systems the power spectral density can have power - law behavior with the exponent equal to or larger than 1 in a wide range of intermediate frequencies . </S>"
