    "<S> we investigate the spin dynamics of half - solitons in polariton bose - einstein condensates . </S>",
    "<S> half - solitons , which behave as magnetic monopoles , can be accelerated in the presence of the effective magnetic field of the microcavity . </S>",
    "<S> we study the generation of dc magnetic currents in a gas of half - solitons . at low densities , </S>",
    "<S> the current is suppressed due to the dipolar oscillations . at moderate densities , </S>",
    "<S> a magnetic current is recovered as a consequence of the collisions between the carriers . </S>",
    "<S> we show a deviation from ohm s law due to the competition between magnetic dipoles and monopoles .    since the original idea of dirac @xcite , </S>",
    "<S> magnetic monopoles have been one of the most important physical questions in quantum mechanics . </S>",
    "<S> in fact ,  real \" elementary magnetic charges have not been observed up to now , despite long efforts to detect them @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> recently , magnetically frustrated materials , or spin ices @xcite , offered the possibility of investigating magnetic charge transport . besides the substantial experimental evidence to support the existence of spin - ice magnetic monopoles @xcite , </S>",
    "<S> the measurement of the charge and current of magnetic monopoles has become possible @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> in fact , signatures of magnetic monopoles are present in other systems , such as nanowires @xcite and spinor bose - einstein condensates @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> physically , these monopoles are elementary excitations in the system , or quasiparticles , a concept that is widely used in solid state physics to describe the behavior of carriers in the band structure @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> modern electronics , for example , is successfully described in their terms . </S>",
    "<S> quasiparticles differ from  real \" particles in the sense that they can not exist outside the underlying medium .    </S>",
    "<S> an interesting example of quasiparticles are the so called half - solitons ( hs ) in spinor bose - einstein condensates ( becs ) . </S>",
    "<S> half - solitons are stable localized excitations of spinor condensates with spin - anisotropic interactions @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> recently , some of us have experimentally demonstrated that they behave like effective magnetic charges , being accelerated along applied effective magnetic fields @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> electricity , which is the basis of the modern world , is a current of electric charges in applied electric fields . by analogy </S>",
    "<S> , the motion of magnetic charges in magnetic field has been generally referred to as  magnetricity \" @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> therefore , the idea of using hs to envisage magnetricity appears both natural and important . </S>",
    "<S> in particular , exciton - polariton condensates in semiconductor microcavities have been pointed out as extremely promising platforms to investigate magnetricity@xcite .    in this work </S>",
    "<S> , we present a theoretical study of polaritonic magnetricity : the collective motion of magnetic monopoles in the presence of an effective magnetic field . </S>",
    "<S> we show that at very low densities , the conductivity is suppressed due to dipole oscillations . at higher densities , </S>",
    "<S> when collisions between hs are more likely , the magnetic conductivity is optimal . </S>",
    "<S> for very dense gases , the conductivity decreases as a consequence of the collision time shortening . </S>",
    "<S> we also predict a deviation from the magnetic ohm s law @xmath0 for moderate magnitudes of the applied field @xmath1 . to confirm our predictions based on a kinetic model , we perform numerical simulations of realistic experimental configurations , where the dc conductivity can be effectively measured . </S>",
    "<S> finally , we estimate the mobility of magnetic charges to 10@xmath2 @xmath3/vs , an order of magnitude larger than the record value of the electronic mobility in graphene @xcite .    </S>",
