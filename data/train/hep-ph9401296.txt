    "high - energy collisions involving hadronic final states typically create a large number of particles .",
    "a convenient kinematic quantity for the analysis of many - particle events is the rapidity variable @xmath2 where @xmath3 is the energy of a final - state particle and @xmath4 its longitudinal momentum with respect to a reference axis .",

    ", the distribution of particles in the rapidity variable often contains gaps .",
    "that is , absence of particles in a rapidity interval .",
    "the existence of a gap may reflect the underlying exchange mechanism .",
    "for instance , a gap in diffractive scattering experiments can be interpreted as the exchange of a pomeron @xcite .",
    "the possibility of using rapidity gaps as triggering signals in high - mass scale physics has motivated recent interest in the study of jet events containing gaps @xcite .    in hadron - hadron collisions ,",
    "the analysis of gap physics is complicated by the presence of spectator partons .",
    "these partons can interact with each other during the collision , creating additional particles which may contaminate and spoil a potential rapidity gap .",
    "the survival probability @xcite of a rapidity gap is an important subject of study for unraveling of high - mass scale physics .    in comparison ,",
    "lepton - initiated collisions offer a cleaner environment , free of the uncertainties of survival probability considerations .",
    "the study of rapidity - gap events in lepton colliders can thus offer important complementary information to the study of gap events in hadron - hadron machines .",
