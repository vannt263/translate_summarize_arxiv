    "there are numerous scattering media that are either of natural origin - such as biological tissue , fog , cloud , and teeth - or man - made - such as paint , diffuser glass , and phosphor inclusion in white leds .",
    "these materials multiply scatter waves in all directions , due to the random spatial variations of the refractive indices .",
    "random interference occurs between the multiply scattered waves that is well - known as speckles  @xcite . upon averaging over realizations of scatterers ,",
    "the speckles average out and the resulting average energy density is well described by diffusion theory  @xcite .",
    "recently , novel wave - shaping methods such as feedback - based wavefront shaping @xcite , time reversal@xcite , phase conjugation@xcite , and transmission matrix - based control @xcite have demonstrated the control of transmission and reflection through scattering media .",
    "these wave - shaping methods remarkably enhance the intensity in a single speckle as well as the total reflected and transmitted intensity .",
    "the wave - shaping methods have led the way for exciting applications such as non - invasive biomedical imaging  @xcite , advanced optics  @xcite , and cryptography and secure communication@xcite .    although these methods are useful tools in controlling the intensity at the interfaces of a scattering medium , the energy density distribution of the shaped waves inside the scattering medium is still unknown . in the case of light ,",
    "the knowledge of the energy density distribution is important for applications such as enhanced energy conversion in white leds  @xcite , efficient light harvesting in solar cells  @xcite , and controlled illumination in biomedical imaging  @xcite . to date , only numerical calculations of scalar waves  @xcite and a single - realization elastic wave experiment  @xcite have addressed the energy density distribution of shaped waves inside two - dimensional ( 2d ) scattering media . however , none of these studies provide an analytical model for the energy density distribution . for high - transmission channels , davy _",
    "@xcite described the energy density using a parabolic function . in ref .",
    "@xcite , our team reported the first measurement of the total energy density of light inside a three - dimensional ( 3d ) scattering medium and reported an enhanced total energy density for shaped waves .",
    "the results were interpreted with a heuristic model that the total energy density of shaped waves can be described with only the fundamental eigenfunction of the diffusion equation , which agreed well with experimental observation .",
    "however , the exact distribution of the spatial energy density for shaped waves still remains unknown .    in this paper",
    ", we study the energy density of shaped waves inside a scattering medium by using the eigenfunctions of the diffusion equation to reconstruct the energy density distribution . here",
