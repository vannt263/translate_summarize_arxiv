    "<S> we correlate the _ rosat _ @xmath0kev all - sky survey with the leiden / dwingeloo survey , looking for soft x - ray signatures of prominent high - velocity - cloud ( hvc ) complexes . </S>",
    "<S> we study the transfer of @xmath0kev photons through the interstellar medium in order to distinguish variations in the soft x - ray background ( sxrb ) intensity caused by photoelectric absorption effects from those due to excess x - ray emission . </S>",
    "<S> the x - ray data are modelled as a combination of emission from the local hot bubble ( lhb ) and emission from a distant plasma in the galactic halo and extragalactic sources . </S>",
    "<S> the x - ray radiation intensity of the galactic halo and extragalactic x - ray background is modulated by the photoelectric absorption of the intervening galactic interstellar matter . </S>",
    "<S> we show that large- and small - scale intensity variations of the @xmath0kev sxrb are caused by photoelectric absorption which is predominantly traced by the total @xmath1 distribution . </S>",
    "<S> the extensive coverage of the two surveys supports evidence for a hot , x - ray emitting corona . </S>",
    "<S> we show that this leads to a good representation of the sxrb observations . for four large areas on the sky , we search for regions where the modelled and observed x - ray emission differ . </S>",
    "<S> we find that there is excess x - ray emission towards regions near hvc complexes c , d , and gcn . </S>",
