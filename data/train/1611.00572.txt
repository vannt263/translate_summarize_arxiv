    "we studied the spectral singularity of a semi - infinite coupled resonator system .",
    "the system is a coupled resonator array with a gain ( lossy ) resonator at the end .",
    "this system can be described by a one - dimensional tight - binding chain .",
    "based on the exact solution , the critical dynamical behavior associated with the spectral singularities was demonstrated .",
    "the dissipative resonator absorbs the incoming wave with a matched wave vector .",
    "when the resonator loss is replaced by a gain , the coupled resonator system remains at the spectral singularity .",
    "however , the dynamics is a persistent wave emission instead of a perfect absorption .",
    "we found that the wave emission can be characterized by a time dependent gaussian error function .",
    "the emission wave amplitude probability linearly depends on the fwhm of the incident gaussian wave , and quadratically depends on the resonator gain .",
    "combination of the scattering systems at spectral singularities can form a finite @xmath0-symmetric system at exceptional point .",
    "thus , we linked the spectral singularity to the exceptional point .",
    "the coalescence states are the absorption and emission solutions at the spectral singularities in the scattering system .",
    "the occurrence of two coalescent states indicates that the @xmath0-symmetric system has a @xmath1 jordan block and that the probability increases quadratically . calculating the time evolution of gaussian wave packet with energy on resonance of the coalesced states , we revealed a stepped progressive ascent of probability . for a long time scale ,",
    "the probability increases quadratically , which reflects the exceptional point ; for a short time scale , the plane wave emission and absorption phenomena reflect the dynamics at the spectral singularities .",
    "the dynamical features near the spectral singularities were investigated , the dynamical differences in the wave packet profiles and probabilities induced by slight deviation from the critical gain ( loss ) was shown , the effect bringing by the deviation is significant ( tiny ) at large critical gain ( loss ) .",
