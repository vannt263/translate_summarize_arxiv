    "<S> we investigate a formulation of poincar invariant quantum mechanics where the dynamical input is euclidean invariant green functions or their generating functional . </S>",
    "<S> we argue that within this framework it is possible to calculate scattering observables , binding energies , and perform finite poincar transformations without using any analytic continuation . </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate , using a toy model , how matrix elements of @xmath0 in normalizable states can be used to compute transition matrix elements for energies up to 2 gev . </S>",
    "<S> we discuss some open problems .    </S>",
    "<S> we investigate the possibility of formulating poincar invariant quantum models of few - body systems where the dynamical input is given by a set of euclidean - invariant green functions . </S>",
    "<S> this is an alternative to the direct construction of poincar lie algebras on few - body hilbert spaces . in the proposed framework </S>",
