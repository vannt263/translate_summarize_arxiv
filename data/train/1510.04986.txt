    "<S> a quantum system interacting with its environment is subject to dephasing which ultimately destroys the information it holds . using a superconducting qubit </S>",
    "<S> , we experimentally show that this dephasing has both dynamic and geometric origins . </S>",
    "<S> it is found that geometric dephasing , which is present even in the adiabatic limit and when no geometric phase is acquired , can either reduce or restore coherence depending on the orientation of the path the qubit traces out in its projective hilbert space . </S>",
    "<S> it accompanies the evolution of any system in hilbert space subjected to noise .    the information stored in a quantum bit is ultimately lost when the interaction with its environment causes randomization of its quantum phase in a process known as dephasing @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> part of this dephasing is of geometric origin @xcite and is related to a type of geometric phase known as berry phase @xcite , which is accumulated when a quantum system is adiabatically steered along a closed contour in the parameter space of its hamiltonian . </S>",
    "<S> this phase underlines the dynamics and thermodynamics of a broad spectrum of quantum systems as measured in spins @xcite , systems dominated by spin - orbit - coupling @xcite , experiments exhibiting aharonov - bohm phases @xcite , as well as atomic @xcite and optical setups @xcite . here </S>",
    "<S> , we experimentally confirm the existence of geometric dephasing using a superconducting quantum system @xcite exposed to artificial noise . </S>",
    "<S> it is found that geometric dephasing can either reduce or restore coherence depending on the orientation of the path a qubit traces out in its projective hilbert space . </S>",
    "<S> this asymmetric decoherence mechanism is expected to play a role in numerous stochastic systems exhibiting geometric phases @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> the geometric phase roots in the structure of hilbert space and is  unlike the dynamic phase  not related to the duration of a quantum process . </S>",
    "<S> when it comes to quantum dissipative systems , ubiquitous in our physical world , the nature of the geometric phase may be put to question : noise may screen out geometric effects and the condition for adiabaticity is not self - evident . </S>",
    "<S> the former effect is frequently modeled by adding non - geometric dissipative rates to the equations of motion for the density matrix , see e.g. ref .  . </S>",
    "<S> the latter concern is alleviated in ref .  </S>",
    "<S> : naively one may expect that adiabaticity implies that the rate of change involved is smaller than the excitation gap . </S>",
    "<S> it turns out though that a gapless spectrum , which may be the result of coupling the system to a dissipative reservoir , does not necessarily annul the berry phase @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> moreover , dephasing and level broadening due to classical low frequency noise randomizing the berry phase are addressed in refs .  ; </S>",
    "<S> corrections to the berry phase , due to both slow , classical noise and high frequency quantum noise have been discussed in ref .  . </S>",
    "<S> in that work , the concept of geometric dephasing has been introduced . </S>",
    "<S> indeed , decoherence in quantum systems stems not only from the stochastic evolution of the dynamic phase of the system s wave function ( dynamic dephasing ) , but also from geometric effects .    while the effect of classical noise @xcite and of a quantum bath @xcite on the berry phase have been studied in experiments before , the present work reports on an explicit measurement of geometric dephasing . </S>",
