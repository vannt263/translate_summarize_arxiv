    "<S> the continuous - time random walk ( ctrw ) is a pure - jump stochastic process with several applications in physics , but also in insurance , finance and economics . </S>",
    "<S> a definition is given for a class of stochastic integrals driven by a ctrw , that includes the it and stratonovich cases . </S>",
    "<S> an uncoupled ctrw with zero - mean jumps is a martingale . </S>",
    "<S> it is proved that , as a consequence of the martingale transform theorem , if the ctrw is a martingale , the it integral is a martingale too . </S>",
    "<S> it is shown how the definition of the stochastic integrals can be used to easily compute them by monte carlo simulation . </S>",
    "<S> the relations between a ctrw , its quadratic variation , its stratonovich integral and its it integral are highlighted by numerical calculations when the jumps in space of the ctrw have a symmetric lvy @xmath0-stable distribution and its waiting times have a one - parameter mittag - leffler distribution . </S>",
    "<S> remarkably these distributions have fat tails and an unbounded quadratic variation . in the diffusive limit of vanishing scale parameters , </S>",
    "<S> the probability density of this kind of ctrw satisfies the space - time fractional diffusion equation ( fde ) or more in general the fractional fokker - planck equation , that generalize the standard diffusion equation solved by the probability density of the wiener process , and thus provides a phenomenologic model of anomalous diffusion . </S>",
    "<S> we also provide an analytic expression for the quadratic variation of the stochastic process described by the fde , and check it by monte carlo . </S>"
