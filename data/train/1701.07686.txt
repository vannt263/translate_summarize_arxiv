    "ultrafast electron diffraction has developed into a powerful technique for studying structural dynamics@xcite .",
    "pumping samples with femtosecond laser pulses and probing them with high energy electron bunches can easily lead to sample damage , which is particularly true for biological molecules@xcite .",
    "this means that diffraction patterns preferably have to be captured with a single electron bunch : single - shot electron diffraction .",
    "this requires electron bunches with @xmath11 electrons@xcite per pulse which are prone to space charge explosions , resulting in loss of temporal resolution and degradation of transverse beam quality .",
    "the effect of space charge forces may be mitigated by using an ultracold electron source since it allows for larger source sizes and thus lower bunch densities for the same beam quality@xcite .    to obtain high quality diffraction images the relative energy spread of the electron bunch needs to be much smaller than unity and the transverse coherence length larger than the lattice spacing of the structure under investigation .",
    "previous work demonstrated that the ultracold electron source is capable of producing high quality diffraction images@xcite , even with electron bunches created by femtosecond photoionization@xcite .",
    "the transverse beam quality of the ultracold electron source has been investigated extensively previously@xcite .",
    "however , the longitudinal electron beam characteristics have not been investigated in great detail .",
    "recently it was shown that the ultracold electron source can be used to create ultracold electron bunches with a root - mean - square ( rms ) pulse length of 250 ps@xcite . in this paper",
    "we present pulse length measurements with sub - ps resolution of ultracold electron bunches with an rms pulse duration of @xmath12 ps containing @xmath13 electrons per pulse . up to @xmath14 electrons can be extracted in a single shot from the ultracold electron source@xcite but then strong space charge effects come into play . these space charge effects can be minimized by shaping the initial electron distribution  @xcite . in this paper",
    "we stick to maximally @xmath8 electrons per bunch , thus avoiding the complication of space charge effects .    for ued",
    ", the electron pulse length has to be shorter than the shortest timescale associated with the process under investigation .",
    "this means that often the electron bunch length should preferably be much shorter than one picosecond .",
    "this can be achieved by compressing longer electron pulses using a resonant rf cavity@xcite in tm@xmath15 mode , but for this to work the electron pulse should not be longer than a few @xmath16  ps@xcite for a cavity operated at @xmath17ghz . in this paper",

    "an rf cavity has been used to measure the pulse length of electron bunches produced by femtosecond laser photoionization of a laser - cooled and trapped ultracold atomic gas .",
    "pulse lengths have been measured in three photoionization regimes : direct photoionization using only the femtosecond laser , two - step just - above - threshold photoionization , and two - step just - below - threshold photoionization .",
    "both direct and just - above - threshold ionization produce ultrashort electron pulses with rms pulse durations ranging from @xmath16 to @xmath119  ps .",
    "direct ionization produces few ( @xmath197 ) and hot ( @xmath198k ) electrons while just - above - threshold ionization produces many ( @xmath199 ) and ultracold ( @xmath200k ) electrons with a normalized transverse emittance @xmath201  nm@xmath10rad .",
    "the measured pulse lengths can be explained by the energy spread associated with the length of the ionization volume .",
    "these bunches are sufficiently short to be compressed to sub - ps pulse lengths using established rf compression techniques .",
    "just - below - threshold ionization produces a rydberg gas which ionizes on @xmath187s timescales .",
    "the method described here can be used to investigate the effect of space charge forces which is the main limitation of the achievable temporal resolution in single - shot ued .",
    "measuring the pulse length in the longitudinal waist should allow the direct measurement of the influence of space charge forces on the longitudinal phase space distribution .",

    "<S> we present measurements of the pulse length of ultracold electron bunches generated by near - threshold two - photon photoionization of a laser - cooled gas . </S>",
    "<S> the pulse length has been measured using a resonant @xmath0  ghz deflecting cavity in tm@xmath1 mode . </S>",
    "<S> we have measured the pulse length in three ionization regimes . </S>",
    "<S> the first is direct two - photon photoionization using only a @xmath2  nm femtosecond laser pulse , which results in short ( @xmath3ps ) but hot ( @xmath4k ) electron bunches . </S>",
    "<S> the second regime is just - above - threshold femtosecond photoionization employing the combination of a continuous - wave @xmath5  nm excitation laser and a tunable @xmath2  nm femtosecond ionization laser which results in _ both _ ultracold ( @xmath6k ) _ and _ ultrafast ( @xmath7ps ) electron bunches . </S>",
    "<S> these pulses typically contain @xmath8 electrons and have an rms normalized transverse beam emittance of @xmath9nm@xmath10rad . </S>",
    "<S> the measured pulse lengths are limited by the energy spread associated with the longitudinal size of the ionization volume , as expected . </S>",
