    "<S> we study theoretically the phonon - induced relaxation ( @xmath0 ) and decoherence times ( @xmath1 ) of singlet - triplet qubits in lateral gaas double quantum dots ( dqds ) . </S>",
    "<S> when the dqd is biased , pauli exclusion enables strong dephasing via two - phonon processes . </S>",
    "<S> this mechanism requires neither hyperfine nor spin - orbit interaction and yields @xmath2 , in contrast to previous calculations of phonon - limited lifetimes . </S>",
    "<S> when the dqd is unbiased , we find @xmath3 and much longer lifetimes than in the biased dqd . for typical setups , the decoherence and relaxation rates due to one - phonon processes are proportional to the temperature @xmath4 , whereas the rates due to two - phonon processes reveal a transition from @xmath5 to higher powers as @xmath4 is decreased . </S>",
    "<S> remarkably , both @xmath0 and @xmath1 exhibit a maximum when the external magnetic field is applied along a certain axis within the plane of the two - dimensional electron gas . </S>",
    "<S> we compare our results with recent experiments and analyze the dependence of @xmath0 and @xmath1 on system properties such as the detuning , the spin - orbit parameters , the hyperfine coupling , and the orientation of the dqd and the applied magnetic field with respect to the main crystallographic axes . </S>"
