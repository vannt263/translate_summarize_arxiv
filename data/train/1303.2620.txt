    "<S> we perform measurements of phase - slip - induced switching current events on different types of superconducting weak links and systematically study statistical properties of the switching current distributions . </S>",
    "<S> we employ two types of devices in which a weak link is formed either by a superconducting nanowire or by a graphene flake subject to proximity effect . </S>",
    "<S> we demonstrate that , independently on the nature of the weak link , higher moments of the distribution take universal values </S>",
    "<S> . in particular , the third moment ( skewness ) of the distribution is close to @xmath0 both in thermal and quantum regimes . </S>",
    "<S> the fourth moment ( kurtosis ) also takes a universal value close to @xmath1 . </S>",
    "<S> the discovered universality of skewness and kurtosis is confirmed by an analytical model . </S>",
    "<S> our numerical analysis shows that introduction of extraneous noise into the system leads to significant deviations from the universal values . </S>",
    "<S> we suggest to use the discovered universality of higher moments as a robust tool for checking against undesirable effects on noise in various types of measurements .    _ </S>",
    "<S> introduction_.the field of quantum noise has recently seen rapid development caused both by its growing significance in many areas of condensed matter physics as well as by a constant improvement in the capabilities of high precision measurements  @xcite . perhaps the most intensively studied question to date </S>",
    "<S> is related to the statistics of charge transport in mesoscopic conductors . in such systems probability distribution of current fluctuations , </S>",
    "<S> the so - called full counting statistics , was rigorously derived for various normal and superconducting circuits  @xcite and tested in the state - of - the - art measurements of the third moment ( skewness ) of current fluctuations  @xcite . given that charge is a quantum mechanical conjugate variable to the phase , it is of fundamental interest to study corresponding statistics of phase fluctuations . </S>",
    "<S> superconducting nanowires and related proximity devices offer a natural platform for this purpose , which we explore in the present work .    </S>",
    "<S> the macroscopic quantum tunneling of the phase across a current - biased josephson junction  @xcite or a superconducting nanowire  @xcite is arguably the most profound and well - known manifestation of quantum fluctuations at the macroscopic level . </S>",
    "<S> this phenomenon is observed by registering phase slip events  @xcite , which proliferate at currents close to the critical and drive transitions between supercurrent - carrying and dissipative branches of current - voltage characteristics  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> macroscopic quantum tunneling is usually described in the framework that treats quantum or thermally activated transitions of the phase between neighboring minima of a tilted washboard potential in the presence of a dissipative environment  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> complimentary approaches employ an effective action for bcs superconductors  @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> unlike experiments associated with the charge transfer where measurements of each moment of the full counting statistics is beyond current experimental capabilities , experiments on switching current allow one to reconstruct full distribution of superconducting phase fluctuations since a single phase slip is sufficient to drive the system into resistive state  @xcite by creating a hot spot  @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> thus there exists a one - to - one correspondence between phase fluctuations - _ phase slips _ - and switching events .    in this letter </S>",
    "<S> we report a systematic study of higher moments of the switching current distribution as a function of temperature and other parameters of our devices . </S>",
    "<S> the higher moments under investigation include skewness @xmath2 that quantifies an asymmetry of the distribution , and kurtosis @xmath3 that is a measure of its peakedness ( for definitions see below ) . </S>",
    "<S> we present evidence , both experimental and theoretical , that these higher moments are in fact universal constants : @xmath4 and @xmath5 . </S>",
    "<S> surprisingly , the observed crossover from a classical escape mechanism ( i.e. , the thermal activation ) to a quantum one ( i.e. , quantum tunneling from a metastable energy minimum ) does not lead to any noticeable changes in these moments . </S>",
    "<S> we evince this universality using two types of samples , namely graphene junctions under the proximity effect as well as ultra - thin superconducting nanowires </S>",
