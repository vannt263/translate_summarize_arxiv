    "by giving an analytic expression for the meridional overturning strength @xmath23 as a function of the meridional pressure difference @xmath24 , we discuss the scaling of the two main quantities of the conceptual model introduced by _",
    "g99_. the model exhibits two scaling regimes which both correspond to unphysical situations .",
    "linear scaling occurs in a situation where the eddy - induced return flow is much stronger than the northern downwelling .",
    "this corresponds to a circulation which is localized entirely in the so and in which all downward volume transport is due to the eddy - induced return flow .",
    "this situation is inconsistent with the physical assumption of an interhemispheric overturning cell underlying the model and the isopycnal nature of the return flow .",
    "the second scaling regime corresponds to a purely northern cell where the upwelling takes place entirely in low latitudes , described by an advection - diffusion balance . in this case",
    "the overturning is proportional to the square root of the pressure as reported by _",
    "bryan _ [ 1987 ] .",

    "<S> we present an analysis of the model by _ gnanadesikan _ [ 1999 ] for the pycnocline depth in the ocean . </S>",
    "<S> an analytic solution for the overturning strength as a function of the meridional pressure difference is derived and used to discuss their mutual scaling . </S>",
    "<S> we show that scaling occurs only in two unphysical regimes of the model . in the absence of the southern ocean ( so ) processes , i.e. for a northern overturning cell , the volume transport is proportional to the square root of the pressure difference . </S>",
    "<S> linear scaling is seen when the overturning is restricted entirely to the so , i.e. when no northern downwelling exists . for comparison , </S>",
    "<S> we present simulations with the coupled climate model climber-3@xmath0 which show linear scaling over a large regime of pressure differences in the north atlantic ( na ) . </S>",
    "<S> we conclude that the pycnocline model is not able to reproduce the linear scaling between its two central variables , pressure and volume transport . </S>"
