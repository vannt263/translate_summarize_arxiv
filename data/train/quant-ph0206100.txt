    "<S> quantum algorithms are well - suited to calculate estimates of the energy spectra for spin lattice systems . these algorithms are based on the efficient calculation of discrete fourier components of the density of states . </S>",
    "<S> the efficiency of these algorithms in calculating the free energy per spin of general spin lattices to bounded error is examined . </S>",
    "<S> we find that the number of fourier components required to bound the error in the free energy due to the broadening of the density of states scales polynomially with the number of spins in the lattice . </S>",
    "<S> however , the precision with which the fourier components must be calculated is found to be an exponential function of the system size . </S>"
