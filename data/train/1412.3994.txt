    "already decades ago the potential use of molecules as components of electronic circuitry was proposed@xcite thereby introducing the field of molecular electronics .",
    "since then , the goal of the field has been to create high - speed processing molecular devices with miniature size.@xcite in that respect it is important to investigate the properties of transport through single molecules in the presence of external fields.@xcite single - molecule magnets ( smms ) are a class of molecules with a permanent magnetization . due to both classical@xcite and quantum@xcite characteristics of smms ,",
    "their application in molecular electronics became a topic of intense research considering their potential usage in creation of memory devices.@xcite    time - dependent transport through molecular junctions has been theoretically studied using different techniques , such as nonequilibrium green s functions technique,@xcite time - dependent density functional theory,@xcite reduced density matrix approach,@xcite etc .",
    "time - dependent periodic fields in electrical contacts cause photon - assisted tunneling,@xcite a phenomenon based on the fact that by applying an external harmonic field with frequency @xmath0 to the contact , the conduction electrons interact with the ac field and consequently participate in the inelastic tunneling processes by absorbing or emitting an amount of energy @xmath1 , where @xmath2 .",
    "theoretically , photon - assisted tunneling through atoms and molecules was investigated in numerous works.@xcite some experimental studies addressed photon - assisted tunneling through atomic - sized@xcite and molecular@xcite junctions in the presence of laser fields . in junctions with time - dependent ac bias",
    "the presence of displacement currents is inevitable due to the charge accumulation in the scattering region.@xcite this problem can be solved either implicitly by including the coulomb interaction in the hamiltonian of the system,@xcite or explicitly by adding the displacement current to the conduction current,@xcite thus providing the conservation of the total ac current .",
    "spin transport through magnetic nanostructures can be used to manipulate the state of the magnetization via spin - transfer torques ( stts).@xcite the concept of stt is based on the transfer of spin angular momenta from the conduction electrons to a local magnetization in the scattering region generating a torque as a back - action of the spin transport and thus changing the state of the magnetic nanostructure.@xcite hence , current - induced magnetization reversal became an active topic in recent years.@xcite the measurement and control of the magnetization of smms@xcite employing spin transport may bring important applications in spintronics .    in this work",

    "<S> we study the ac - charge and -spin transport through an orbital of a single molecule magnet precessing in a constant magnetic field . </S>",
    "<S> we assume that the source and drain contacts have time - dependent chemical potentials . </S>",
    "<S> we employ the keldysh green s functions method to calculate the spin and charge currents to linear order in the time - dependent potentials . </S>",
    "<S> the molecular and electronic spins are coupled via exchange interaction . </S>",
    "<S> the time - dependent molecular spin drives inelastic transitions between the zeeman split orbital energy levels resulting in a rich structure in the transport characteristics . </S>",
    "<S> the time - dependent voltages allow to reveal the internal precession time scale ( the larmor frequency ) by a dc - conductance measurement if the ac - frequency matches the larmor frequency . </S>",
    "<S> furthermore we show that the setup can be used to generate dc - spin currents with arbitrary magnetization direction by controlling the relative phases between the larmor precession and the ac - voltage . </S>"
