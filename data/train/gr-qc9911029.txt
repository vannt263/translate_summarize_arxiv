    "<S> stochastic description of inflationary spacetimes emulates the growth of vacuum fluctuations by an effective stochastic `` noise field '' which drives the dynamics of the volume - smoothed inflaton . </S>",
    "<S> we investigate statistical properties of this field and find its correlator to be a function of distance measured in units of the smoothing length . </S>",
    "<S> our results apply for a wide class of smoothing window functions and are different from previous calculations by starobinsky and others who used a sharp momentum cutoff . </S>",
    "<S> we also discuss the applicability of some approximate noise descriptions to simulations of stochastic inflation . </S>"
