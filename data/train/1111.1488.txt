    "<S> the consistency of the interaction with the quark model predictions is also discussed .    </S>",
    "<S> introduction relativistic mean field ( rmf ) theory has been successfully applied to both finite nuclei and nuclear matter in order to describe their bulk properties @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> starting from an effective lagrangian in which nucleon and meson fields are coupled in a covariant manner , single - particle dirac equations for nucleons are derived within the mean field approximation . in this model , </S>",
    "<S> nucleons are treated as dirac particles moving independently in the mean field generated by the mesons . </S>",
    "<S> the spin - orbit interaction with the correct sign and magnitude naturally arises from the relativistic treatment of nucleons . </S>",
    "<S> the success of the model affirms the meson exchange picture of a nucleon - nucleon interaction in nuclei .    </S>",
    "<S> recently , another class of relativistic model , that is , the relativistic point coupling ( rpc ) model @xcite has also been widely employed @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this model consists of skyrme - type zero - range interactions and has been found to be as capable as meson exchange rmf models of reproducing the properties of finite nuclei and nuclear matter @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this model has several advantages compared to the meson exchange models . </S>",
    "<S> first , there is no need to solve the klein - gordon equations for mesons since the mesonic degrees of freedom are all implicit in the rpc model . </S>",
    "<S> secondly , the fock terms can easily be introduced by using the fierz transformation because of its zero - range nature @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> lastly , it is much easier to apply the model to beyond - mean - field methods such as the generator coordinate method ( gcm ) , and angular momentum and particle number projections @xcite .    </S>",
    "<S> a zero - range type interaction is suitable also for the three - dimensional ( 3d ) mesh method for mean - field calculations in the coordinate space representation @xcite . with this method </S>",
    "<S> , an arbitrary deformation of nuclei can be efficiently described , and the method has been widely used in non - relativistic skyrme hartree - fock ( shf ) calculations together with the imaginary time technique @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> an extension of this method to the relativistic approach is not trivial , however . </S>",
    "<S> that is , a naive imaginary time evolution breaks down in relativistic systems due to the presence of the dirac sea @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> this is not a numerical , but rather a fundamental problem related to the variational principle . </S>",
    "<S> such phenomena have been well - known under the name of `` variational collapse '' in the field of relativistic quantum chemistry @xcite . for this reason , </S>",
    "<S> a 3d mesh calculation has not yet been carried out neither with rmf nor rpc . </S>",
    "<S> recently , a few prescriptions to avoid the variational collapse have been tested in the nuclear physics context @xcite . </S>",
    "<S> the prescriptions were found to work well , at least for simple spherical systems , and a 3d mesh calculation may now be almost ready to perform .    in this paper , we extend the relativistic point coupling model to hypernuclei . </S>",

    "<S> this is a straightforward extension of the relativistic point coupling model for nucleons , which has a similar structure as the skyrme interaction . to this end </S>",
    "<S> , we added effective contact @xmath1-@xmath0 interactions , corresponding to the @xmath0-@xmath2 and @xmath0-@xmath3 couplings , to the model lagrangian . </S>",
    "<S> in addition , we introduced the zero - range @xmath1-@xmath0 tensor coupling as well to mimic the tensor coupling between @xmath0 and @xmath3 meson , following the quark model suggestion .    </S>",
    "<S> we fitted the coupling constants in the strange sector to the experimental data of lambda binding energies . </S>",
    "<S> the four parameter sets , pcy - s1 , pcy - s2 , pcy - s3 , and pcy - s4 were proposed , which well reproduce the experimental data through the whole mass region . </S>",
    "<S> the resulting spin - orbit splittings in pcy - s1 , pcy - s2 , and pcy - s4 are smaller than that of nucleon by roughly a factor of 10 in their absolute values , although pcy - s3 yields too large spin - orbit splittings . for pcy - s1 and pcy - s2 their signs are opposite to that of nucleon in some nuclei in the heavier region . on the other hand , for pcy - s4 </S>",
    "<S> obtained without taking into account the vector derivative term , the sign of the spin - orbit splitting is the same as that for nucleons . </S>",
    "<S> high - precision @xmath107-ray experiments for @xmath0 single - particle energies are awaited in order to see whether the spin - orbit splitting of heavy hypernuclei is normal or inverted .    </S>",
    "<S> we have confirmed that the tensor coupling , which is ignored in the @xmath1-@xmath1 interaction , is quite important to reproduce the small spin - orbit splittings of @xmath0 particle . without the tensor coupling , the scalar and the vector couplings of @xmath0 to nucleon </S>",
    "<S> are forced to be unnaturally weak ( pcy - s2 ) . </S>",
    "<S> the tensor coupling suppresses the spin - orbit splittings , keeping the scalar and the vector couplings consistent with the naive quark counting . </S>",
    "<S> those good consistency with the quark model found in our interaction can be a useful guide in further extending the point coupling model to multi-@xmath0 or @xmath108 hypernuclei .    </S>",
    "<S> we conclude that the point coupling model is capable of describing single-@xmath0 hypernuclei as well as normal nuclei </S>",
    "<S> . the model can be an appropriate tool for relativistic calculations for hypernuclei on three - dimensional mesh due to its numerical simplicity . </S>",
    "<S> there has been no relativistic calculation performed on 3d mesh because of the variational difficulty . </S>",
    "<S> thus the primary future work is to develop an efficient calculation technique for relativistic calculations on 3d mesh that overcomes the `` variational collapse '' . </S>",
