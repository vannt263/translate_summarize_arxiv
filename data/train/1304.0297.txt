    "entanglement has proven to be `` the characteristic trait of quantum mechanics '' as first coined by schrdinger @xcite .",
    "it forms the foundations of quantum information theory and quantum computing .",
    "further , in interferometry entanglement enables measurement precision to surpass the standard quantum limit @xcite .",
    "this is particularly important in atom interferometry @xcite as atom flux is generally limited .",
    "however , the most important foundational trait of entanglement comes with its role in the einstein - podolsky - rosen paradox ( epr ) @xcite .",
    "this requires the underlying quantum correlations to be stronger than those satisfying the simpler inseparability criteria .",
    "the resulting epr - entanglement criterion confronts the heisenberg uncertainty relation and puts us into the context of epr arguments that question the completeness of quantum mechanics and open the door to alternative descriptions of these correlations via local hidden variable theories @xcite .",
    "the epr paradox for continuous - variable quadrature observables @xcite ( which are analogous to the position and momentum observables originally discussed by epr ) has been demonstrated in optical parametric down - conversion @xcite and most recently attempts have been made to demonstrate @xcite the paradox with ensembles of massive particles generated by spin - changing collisions in a spinor bose - einstein condensate ( bec ) @xcite .    in this paper , we seek to provide a theoretical treatment of the recent experiment by gross _",

    "the authors observed that the resulting state was inseparable , though a measurement of a stronger epr entanglement criterion was inconclusive .",
