    "in summary we have studied heat conduction across a @xmath10 quantum - mechanical harmonic chain , with arbitrary distribution of masses and inter - particle spring constants , that is connected to two other ordered @xmath10 harmonic crystals which have different mass densities and elastic constants . for this model",
    "we use two different approaches to demonstrate the relation @xmath167 between the energy transmittance @xmath1 , which occurs in the landauer formula for heat current , and the transmission coefficient @xmath2 related to passage of plane waves across the system . in the first approach we use the fact that the green s function occuring in the expression for @xmath4 has a simple representation in terms of product of @xmath168 matrices .",
    "the plane wave solutions are then obtained by directly solving the equations of motion and a representation of @xmath3 is obtained , again in terms of the product of matrices .",
    "the connection between @xmath4 and @xmath3 is then directly obtained .",
    "this approach can be extended to the case of regular lattices using , for example , the techniques used in @xcite for the representation of the green s functions using matrix products .    in the second approach",
    "it is not necessary to find the explicit form of the green s function .",
    "one notes that the required plane wave scattering states can be obtained by using the lippmann - schwinger approach to evolve initial states which are eigenmodes of either one of the reservoirs and are initially localized within the reservoirs .",
    "the lippmann - schwinger approach then directly gives @xmath3 in terms of the green s function .",
