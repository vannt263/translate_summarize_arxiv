    "within the last decades , model predictive control ( mpc ) has grown mature for both linear and nonlinear systems , see , e.g. , @xcite or @xcite .",
    "although analytically and numerically challenging , the method itself is attractive due to its simplicity : in a first step , a new measurement of the current system state is obtained which is thereafter used to compute an optimal control over a finite optimization horizon . in the third and last step , a portion of this control is applied to the process and the entire problem is shifted forward in time rendering the scheme to be iteratively applicable .",

    "<S> : the stability analysis of model predictive control schemes without terminal constraints and/or costs has attracted considerable attention during the last years . </S>",
    "<S> we pursue a recently proposed approach which can be used to determine a suitable optimization horizon length for nonlinear control systems governed by ordinary differential equations . in this context </S>",
    "<S> , we firstly show how the essential growth assumption involved in this methodology can be derived and demonstrate this technique by means of a numerical example . </S>",
    "<S> secondly , inspired by corresponding results , we develop an algorithm which allows to reduce the required optimization horizon length while maintaining asymptotic stability or a desired performance bound . </S>",
    "<S> last , this basic algorithm is further elaborated in order to enhance its robustness properties .    </S>",
    "<S> lyapunov stability ; predictive control ; state feedback ; suboptimal control </S>"
