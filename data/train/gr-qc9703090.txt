    "in general relativity , the local dynamics of the gravitational field is governed by a constraint rather than by a hamiltonian @xcite .",
    "accordingly , in non - perturbative quantum gravity the quantum dynamics is governed by a constraint operator @xcite , usually denoted the `` hamiltonian constraint operator '' ( hco ) or the wheeler - dewitt operator .",
    "the construction and the analysis of this operator is a central problem in quantum gravity . for a long time ,",
    "only formal or seriously ill - defined versions of the hco were known .",
    "concrete computations were thus impossible beyond semiclassical or minisuperspace approximations , and a predictive theory of the non - perturbative quantum dynamics of gravity was consequently lacking .",
    "the situation is perhaps improving with the development of the loop version of non - perturbative quantum gravity @xcite , derived from the introduction of the sen - ashtekar variables @xcite ( now evolved into the real barbero variables @xcite ) .",
    "this approach , denoted `` loop quantum gravity '' , has provided a rigorous framework for describing quantum geometry ( for recent reviews , and complete references see @xcite ) . as far as kinematics",
    "is concerned , the approach has yielded striking predictions : in particular , geometric operators corresponding to volumes and areas have been shown to have computable discrete spectra .",
    "this result was first obtained by rovelli and smolin in @xcite and later re - derived and sharpened with various alternative techniques ( @xcite ; see @xcite and references therein ) ; its physical significance was first asserted in @xcite .",
    "as far as dynamics is concerned , an extensive array of investigations of the hco , starting from the pioneering work of jacobson and smolin @xcite and continuing in @xcite , has recently culminated with the construction , due to thiemann , of a mathematically well - defined operator @xcite .",
    "this construction is already being used in various directions of research , for instance in the search for exact solutions to the constraints @xcite , or as a key ingredient of a spacetime formulation of the theory @xcite . in this last context ,",
    "the matrix elements of the hco play a role analogous to the coefficients of the vertices in the feymann diagrams .",
    "modifications of the operator have been considered in @xcite .",
    "thiemann s operator , however , is defined in a rather involved manner : its matrix elements and explicit action are far from easy to evaluate .",
    "this difficulty obstructs the research directions mentioned and renders the analysis of the operator quite difficult . in this work , we address this difficulty directly by studying thiemann s operator in detail , and showing how its matrix elements can be computed explicitly .    to perform the computation , we take advantage of two key techniques . first , we work in the spin network basis , introduced in quantum gravity in @xcite , and fully developed in @xcite ( see @xcite for an interesting historical reconstruction of these developments ) .",
    "second , we use the technique developed in @xcite , based on the ( kauffman - lins ) tangle theoretical version of recoupling theory @xcite ( whose utility for quantum gravity was first suggested in @xcite ) .",
    "since thiemann s operator is gauge invariant but is defined in terms of gauge non - invariant operators , we extend here these techniques to gauge non - invariant states @xcite .",
    "furthermore , we take full advantage of the complete equivalence between the `` loop representation '' @xcite and the `` connection representation '' @xcite of loop quantum gravity ( proven in detail in @xcite ; see also @xcite ) .",
    "it is this equivalence that allows us , for example , to use graphical calculus in a rigorous way in dealing with states that are functions of generalized connections .",
    "we study here only the euclidean term of the hco , but we expect that the techniques we use can be extended to the lorentzian term as well . also , we restrict our attention to trivalent vertices : extension to any valence is straightforward .",
    "thiemann defines two versions of the hco : symmetric and non - symmetric . here",
    ", we consider both operators , and show that they exhaust all `` natural '' orderings of the constraint .    we do not discuss the physical correctness of thiemann s operator .",
    "in particular , we do not address the objections that have been raised against it , concerning for instance the state dependence of the regularization , the strong commutativity ( which shows consistency , but seems to fail to reproduce the classical constraint algebra @xcite ) or the possible need for adding terms to the operator suggested for instance in @xcite .",
    "rather , we simply study the operator as defined by thiemann , and show how its matrix elements can be computed , using recoupling theory .",
    "this analysis is likely to be needed before attempting a physical evaluation and , in any case , it is relevant even if modifications to the thiemann operator are to be made .    in",
